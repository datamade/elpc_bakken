Mission Canyon 8, 771 ’ 1WD 8, 763’ TVD -6,528’ SS
The top of the Mission Canyon was picked using gamma markers found on offset logs. The Mission Canyon came in 5’ high to prognosis. Rate of penetration ranged from 0.33 to 3.80 minutes per foot and averaged 0.60 minutes per foot. Samples from the upper Mission Canyon were majority cream gray limestone. Samples transitioned to gray brown dolomitic limestone in the last half of the Mission Canyon. Average porosity through the Mission Canyon was fair. Minimum oil shows throughout the Mission Canyon. Total gas ranged from 146 to 449 units and averaged 281 units.

Lodgepole

The top of the Lodgepole was picked using gamma markers found on offset logs. The Lodgepole came in 12’ high to prognosis. Drill rates in the Lodgepole to kick off point ranged from 0.50 to 1.53 minutes per foot, and averaged 0.63 minutes per foot. Samples in the upper Lodgepole were generally light gray limestone with overall poor porosity. Total gas ranged from 135 to 303 units and averaged 269 units. Kick off point of 9,680’ was reached at 5:30 pm on December 19, 2014.
Curve 9,680’ — 10,503 ’ MD
Directional tools for the build section were picked up at 9,680’ MD, at which point the curve was kicked off. The landing target was to be 11’ into the Three Forks formation, initially set at 10,155’ TVD. No trip gas was recorded at kick off point. Drilling rates in the Lodgepole below KOP ranged from 0.30 to 5.60 minutes per foot, averaging 1.66 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 135 to 516 units, averaging 248 units. Samples in the lower Lodgepole were majority light gray to tan gray limestone with overall poor to fair porosity. At 10,040’ MD a trip was made due to build rates. The motor and bit were replaced. Once back on bottom trip gas spiked to 2251 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the curve to correlate to the Kaldahl 34-12H and Kaldahl 34-12TFH wells. Aﬁer the LP4 marker the target was lowered to 10,150’ TVD.
