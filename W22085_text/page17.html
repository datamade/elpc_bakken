To understand the basis for Samson’s position, a brief discussion of some general principals of oil and gas law may be instructive here.
Oil or gas in place is real estate and the ownership thereof may be severed from the
ownership of the surface. Beulah Coal Mining Co v. Heihn, 180 NW. 787, 789 (ND. 1920). Mineral interests may be separated from the surface rights and may exist distinct from the ownership of the surface. Slaaten v. Cliﬂ's Drilling C0., 748 F.2d 1275, 1277 (8th Cir. 1984)
