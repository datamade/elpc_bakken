Re: WPX Energy Williston LLC - Victor Elk Well Pad 32-29HB. 32-29HW. and 32-29HA and Access Road Crosses Section Line - Sections 5. T149N. R93W & 32. T1 SON. R93W. Dunn County. North Dakota

Dear Mr. Holweger:
The BIA has jurisdiction over lands within the exterior boundaries of the Fort Berthold Indian Reservation and supports WPX Energy Williston LLC‘s proposed Victor Elk well pad and access road which straddles the section line between Sections 5. TI 49N. R93W & 32, T1 SON. R93W. Based on topography in the Drags Wolf Bay area, access roads do not generally follow section lines. Access to this area must be topographically feasible and generally follow ridges and highland areas, not only to make access economically feasible, but also to keep construction out of low—lying and environmentally sensitive areas near the bay. As a result. no roads are currently proposed. or likely to be proposed in the future across the section line between Sections
5 and 32.
During the summer of 20] 2 an environmental on site was conducted and pad location alternatives were discussed. As a result. this Pad and Access Road location is the only feasible location for an important Bakken drilling development pad. The well pad proposed to be constructed will support the drilling of 3 wells in a 1280 acre drilling spacing unit. As a result of the environmental onsite. the Bureau of Indian Affairs conducted an Environmental Assessment which outlines mitigation measures for this well pad. A Finding of No Signiﬁcant Impact was issued on April 9, 2013 and the Grant of Easement for Right-Of-Way ( FBOGIOO829) was
approved on March 5. 2014.
This section line well pad and access road is consistent with set asides for rural transportation and utilities within the exterior boundaries of the Fort Berthold Reservation. The Bakken/Three Forks oil reserves within this two (2) section spacing unit will be stranded without the use of this section line set aside. Tribal, Individual Indian. and private mineral owners. as well as the State of North Dakota. will greatly beneﬁt from these valuable resources.
