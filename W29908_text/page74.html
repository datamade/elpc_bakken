Base of Last Salt 8,564’ MD 8,554 ’ T VD -6,319’ SS
The base of the last salt was picked by a decrease in rate of penetration, gamma markers, and change in sample from salt to limestone. The BLS came in 7’ high to
prognosis; Samples below the last salt were limestone with moderate amounts of anhydrite.

Mission Canyon

The top of the Mission Canyon was picked using gamma markers found on offset logs. The Mission Canyon came in 8’ high to prognosis. Rate of penetration ranged from 0.47 to 3.2 minutes per foot and averaged 0.92 minutes per foot. Samples from the upper Mission Canyon were majority light gray limestone. Samples transitioned to gray brown dolomitic limestone in the last half of the Mission Canyon. Average porosity through the Mission Canyon was fair. Minimum oil shows throughout the Mission Canyon. Total gas ranged from 180 to 314 units and averaged 235 units.

The top of the Lodgepole was picked using gamma markers found on offset logs. The Lodgepole came in 14’ high to prognosis.
