COMPLETION

The planned top of the 4.5” 1 1.6” production liner will be 50-100 ft above the kick off point. This liner will be cemented throughout the lateral and a wet shoe sub will be set at the toe of the liner. The frac string will be 12.75# P-l 10 with TSHP threads and stung into a PBR run with the liner hanger prior to the fracturing treatment. Current plans are for 28 stages using a plug and perf approach.
. ABNORMAL CONDITIONS
No abnormal temperatures or pressures are anticipated. Hydrogen sulﬁde (1128) may be encountered in the Mission Canyon Formation at a TVD of approximately 8988'. Safety equipment will be in operation at approximately 8488'.
A Hydrogen Sulﬁde (HzS) Contingency Plan is being prepared and will be submitted under separate cover for review and approval. As a safety precaution, the rig sub-structure will remain open during drilling operations to allow natural convection to dissipate any accumulations of hydrogen sulﬁde gas. Should H23 concentrations exceed 20 ppm, a “bug blower” fan will be activated at that position in order to dissipate the concentration of H23 gas to acceptable levels.
Maximum anticipated bottom hole pressure equals approximately 5740psi (calculated at 0.55 psi/foot at Middle Bakken target depth, 10,437’) and maximum anticipated surface pressure equals approximately 3444psi (bottom hole pressure minus the pressure of a partially evacuated hole calculated at 0.22 psi/foot at Three Forks target depth). The intermediate design casing will be run and cemented before drilling the target lateral.
Reservoir temperature is anticipated to be 244°F.
