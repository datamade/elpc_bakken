FALSE BAKKEN 1 0,4 78’ MD 10,281 ’ TVD -8,207’ SS
At 10,478’ MD the False Bakken came in at 10,281’ TVD, 9’ low to prognosis. The gamma reached 144.65 API units while in the False Bakken. The samples for the False Bakken
consisted of cream gray to gray tan, with blocky to platy limestone. The samples had weak milky yellow cuts with a peak of 180 units of gas seen in this interval.
SCALLION 10,485 ’ MD 10,284’ TVD -8,210’ SS
The Scallion was the next formation encountered. The scallion came in at 10,485’ MD and 10,284’ TVD, 9’ low to prognosis. The gamma cooled back down to 53.8 API units in the Scallion. The Scallion consisted of mostly cream buff Limestone with slow dull milky yellow cuts. Gas peaked at 236 units of gas along with fair milky cuts.
UPPER BAKKEN 1 0, 526’ MD 10,296 ’ TVD -8,222 ’ SS
The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,526’ MD, and 10,296’ TVD, this was 8’ low to the prognosis. The gamma reached 689.39 units at this point. Background gas averaged 277.9
units, peaking 679 units. Samples in the Upper Bakken were black, carbonaceous blocky shale, with instant milky yellow cuts.
MIDDLE BAKKEN 10,633’ MD 10,31 7’ TVD -8,243 ’ SS
The Middle Bakken was encountered at a MD of 10,633’ MD and 10,317’ TVD, 13’ low to the prognosis. The Middle Bakken was picked based on the change in samples and a decrease in gamma from 689.39 units down to 53.39 units API. The Middle Bakken samples consisted mostly of a light to dark cream gray tan platy siltstone. Gas peaked at 5450 units of gas along with fair milky cuts.
