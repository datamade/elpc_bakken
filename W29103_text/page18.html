Waypoint 4 redirected the wellbore back up between 9,348’ TVD and 9,3 58’ TVD at 14,000’ MD. Drilling from 12,736’ MD to 14,863’ MD, the wellbore maintained a position within the target window below the Upper Gamma Marker. Samples consisted of a sub rounded to rounded cream to light gray dolomite with oil intermittently observed in samples slightly diminishing as the mud weight increased. Gamma ranged tightly between 58 API and 70 API while gas averaged 1,000 units while going through the gas buster. Flares ranged from three to ten feet high and was observed sporadically. ROP averaged 275 feet per minute while rotating.
The Upper gamma marker was again observed at 14,863’ MD (TVD 9,348’) yielding a dip of 89.94 degrees. Waypoint 6 extended out to TD dropping by one foot to accommodate for the said dip.
From 15,000’ MD to a TD of 16,547 ’ MD, gamma ranged tightly between 65 and 80 API and drilled at an average of 275’/hr. Gas slowly dropped to average 700 units while open to the gas buster due to an increase in mud weight. Samples consisted of a
rounded partially unconsolidated sucrose like dolomite. The Upper Gamma Marker was observed one last time at 16,395’ MD (TVD 9,351) to conﬁrm a dip of 89.94 degrees.
