GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY
The Golden well is a Three Forks well located in Section 31 T152N R90W in horizontal well targeting the Three Forks l"d bench in McLean County, North Dakota. It is a single Three Forks well with a Pilot hole drilling into the Duperow. The Golden was spud on 19 August, 2014 and reached a total depth of MD 27,141 on October 9, 2014; 9 5/8” surface casing was set to 1,957’ MD. 7” casing was set at 10,591’ MD. Matt Buckalew, Tom Abar, Jim Campbell, Chad Olson and Trevor Meyers of Neset Consulting Service Inc. provided mud logging and geological services for this well. Lagged samples were caught and saved for archive at 30’, 50’ intervals. Sample quality was good in the vertical and curve. At times samples were poor in the lateral due to lube sweeps. A set of boxed samples will be sent to the state of North Dakota and the other to Fargo Data Solutions, Inc. The formation tops for the Kibbey through the Lodgepole were picked off of Gamma and samples. MWD information was provided by Scientiﬁc MWD personnel.

A typical sample was; LIMESTONE: Light gray, light brown, packstone, argillaceous, moderate induration, ﬁiable, anhydrite, white, light gray, amorphous, mineral ﬂuorescence only, no cut. No signiﬁcant hydrocarbon shows were observed.
