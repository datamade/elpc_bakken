The curve section was landed in the Middle Bakken at an 82.50o angle at 9,639‘ MD/9,328.95’ TVD on October 8, 2014 at 6:20 AM CDT. The ﬁnal curve TD depth of 9,639’ MD was conﬁrmed when the bottoms up sample at this depth contained bluish gray Middle Bakken dolomite with trace amounts of very dark brown/gray to dull black
shale.

Horizontal Section

After the intermediate 7” casing shoe was set to 9,638’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 9:35 PM CDT on October 9, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 9,629’ MD/-7,374’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned from the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near survey depth of 10,274’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 9,752’ MD/-7,385’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,600’ — 1 1,300’ MD averaged 90.3 degrees, and from 11,300’ — 12,200’ averaged 90.15 degrees. The formation ﬂattened to approximately 90 degrees from 12,200’ — 13,700’, then angled up again at an average of 90.15 degrees from 13,700’ MD to TD of 16,415’ MD.
