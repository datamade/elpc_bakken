Day 2

Drilling continued with slides done for the planned directional turn which was completed aﬁer reaching an azimuth of 180 degrees. Slides were also done throughout the day to continue to work on bringing the wellbore up in TVD back into the lower portion of the Middle Bakken target window. Just as the wellbore would reach about one to two feet below the base of the target window it would take a a deﬂection and begin dropping in TVD again. Aﬁer seeing this directional worked on keeping the wellbore at or slightly above 91 degrees to break through the hard streak. Once through the hard streak the wellbore continued to track the lower portion of the target window. At around a MD of 13,767’ the wellbore began to climb in TVD rapidly and the formation began to down dip at 89.66 degrees. Slides were done as the wellbore exited the top of the target window and the transition from the C to A faces in gamma was seen. After seeing the slides the wellbore was rolled over and dropped back into the target window seeing the same repeat in gamma as before and began to drop through the target window with directional working on slowing the drop in TVD. Penetration rates throughout the day averaged around 250 — 280 ﬁ/hr while rotating and around 80 — 100 ﬁ/hr while sliding. Background gases averaged around 400 — 600 units with connection gases reaching as high as 2,542 units. The samples seen throughout the upper portion of the Middle Bakken exhibited a light to medium tan/gray tan dolomite with an even to spotty yellow green ﬂuorescence with a slow faint streaming to cloudy yellow green cut.
