GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation Cherry State #31-16HU was drilled as a single lateral horizontal with the primary objective being in the Middle Bakken formation. This well is located in the NW NE Section 16 T149N R99W of McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Cherry State #31-16HU was spud on February 24, 2015 and reached a total depth for the vertical & curve at 11,319’ MD on March 1, 2015, ﬁve days from spud. Total depth of the South Lateral was reached at a MD of 21,215’ on March 8, 2015, twelve days from spud. This well was drilled using an invert based mud system for the vertical & curve and Brine for the lateral. Shawn Morrow and Freddy Roberts of Neset Consulting Service, Inc. provided wellsite mudlogging services, beginning at a depth of 9,200’ in the Charles Formation, through total depth in the Middle Bakken target. A NCS Total Gas detector was utilized for monitoring gas. Thirty foot lagged samples in the vertical and curve and one hundred foot lagged samples in the lateral were caught and saved to be archived at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota,
Vertical 9,200’ —— 10,475’ MD
Mudlogging began in the Charles Formation at a measured depth of 9,200’. Penetration rates throughout the Charles averaged around 200 - 250 ﬁ/hr through the salt benches and around 80 — 100 ﬁ/hr throughout the limestone and anhydrite benches. Background gases in the Charles averaged around 50 — 100 units with connection gases reaching as high as 202 units. The samples throughout the Amsden exhibited alternating benches of anhydrite and limestone with occasional shale and dolomite present with the salt benches showing primarily a clear angular salt. The Charles was evaluated while drilling ahead.
Base Last Salt 9,433 ’ MD 9,412 ’ T VD - 7,269’ SS

The Base of the Last salt was encountered at a measured depth of 9,433’, true vertical depth of 9,4 12’, two feet low to prognosis. Penetration rates through the last salt averaged around 180 - 200 ﬁ/hr with 25K weight on bit and penetration rates through the remainder of the Charles averaged around 80 - 100 ﬁ/hr with 35K weight on bit. The samples throughout the lower portion of the Charles exhibited primarily anhydrite and limestone with traces of cream tan dolomite and gray shale present. The remainder of the Charles was drilled out and the Ratcliffe and Midale formations were encountered. Penetration rates through the Ratcliffe and Midale averaged around 60 - 80 ﬁ/hr with the samples showing primarily a light to medium gray tan limestone with layers of anhydrite
and occasional cream tan dolomite present. The Ratcliffe and Midale formations were evaluated while drilling ahead.
