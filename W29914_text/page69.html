Upper and middle sections of the Middle Bakken showed a fairly calcareous, subarenaceous, partially translucent, medium to light gray, tan to beige and off white siltstone which frequently alternated between partially argillaceous ﬂakes and subarenaceous grains, to almost entirely silt- and very fine sand-sized grains. There appeared to be signiﬁcant bioturbation or other agitation in this zone. The lower section of the Middle Bakken, beginning about 16 feet below the Upper Bakken and extending to the Lower Bakken Shale, showed a fairly to moderately argillaceous, fairly to moderately ﬂaky, medium gray and tan fairly calcareous siltstone.
