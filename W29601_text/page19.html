Pipeline Notiﬁcation
This well will be connected to ONEOK’s low pressure gathering system in Dunn County, North Dakota. Oxy’s acreage that comprises the spacing units (SU) for the Kenneth Stroh 5-1H-14397 is dedicated to ONEOK. Under terms of the contract, ONEOK is obligated to install the facilities that are required to connect these wells to its gathering system. Oxy provides (on a monthly basis) to ONEOK a drilling, completion and estimated ﬁrst production date for all wells that are scheduled to be drilled in the foreseeable ﬁlture. In addition, Oxy and ONEOK have conference calls, usually bi-weekly, to discuss connection status of wells and changes to Oxy’s drilling and completion schedules.
Gathering System
ONEOK’s gathering system in this area is a dynamic system that has multiple compressor stations with common suction lines. Gas produced from this pad will have a primary ﬂow path to ONEOK’s Little Knife compressor station and secondary ﬂow paths to ONEOK’s Killdeer and Lodgepole compressor stations. The actual ﬂow of the gas will be based on compression operating parameters and gathering system pressures. Gas gathered on this part of ONEOK’s
gathering system is processed at ONEOK’s Grassland Processing Plant located in Section 36, T146N, R105W, McKenzie County, North Dakota.
In addition to ONEOK, Hiland Partners owns and operates a low pressure gathering line less than one-quarter of a mile from the ONEOK tie-in point for this pad. Oxy owns and operates an oil gathering system that will be connected to the tank facilities at this pad location which has an extra line that could be used to gather gas to increase capacity in the vicinity of this well. PetroHunt has a low pressure gathering system approximately four miles west of the K Stroh l Pad location. Hiland’s, Oxy’s and Petro-Hunt’s lines, along with ONEOK’S, are shown on the attached map.
