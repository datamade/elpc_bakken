Three Forks (Pronghorn Member)

The top of the Pronghorn member of the Three Forks formation came in at 10,570’ TVD. The top of the formation was called where the gamma cooled down to 80 api. Remnants of the Lower Bakken Shale were exhibited through a warmer gamma marker at the base of the Middle Bakken/top of the Pronghorn formation The Pronghorn member is in the upper portion of the Three Forks formation and was the primary interest of this well. Initially the formation consisted of light to medium brown, gray brown, medium tan, gray tan, argillaceous, micro to very ﬁnely crystalline dolomite.
Based off of the top of the Pronghorn formation the target would be 10,580’ TVD so the directional team tried to land it a bit lower than the previous target of 10,57 5’ TVD. The curve was landed at 10,919’ MD/10,579’ TVD at 89.71 degrees at 5:00 PM MDT on August 18“, 2014. At this time a trip to surface was made to run and cement intermediate casing.
