Three Forks 11,078’MD 10,910’ TVD 8,801 ’ SS
At 10,910’ TVD the Three Forks was encountered. This top was picked from both lithology and gamma changes. The upper portion of the Three Forks exhibited dolomite that was light gray, gray tan, tan, slightly argillaceous, limestone in part, microcrystalline, moderate intergranular porosity, abundant silt, with scattered yellow ﬂuorescence, fast streaming yellow-green milky cut. Lower into the zone we encountered a light gray limy dolomite that had slightly larger crystals which slowed ROP and made sliding more difﬁcult. There was also a noticeable increase in ﬁne grained quartz siltstone. Fluorescence in this area was more abundant, even bright yellow and bright green. Cuts from this area in zone were thick and bright, oﬁen streaming from the reservoir rock.
