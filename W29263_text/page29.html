Upper Bakken Shale

The Upper Bakken Shale (UBS) was encountered at 9,463’ MD/-7,441’ SS, which was initially determined from an increase in ROP when transitioning out of harder limestone into softer shale, and ﬁnally determined when gamma values dramatically increased as the gamma tool entered the UBS, with the gamma tool being 48 feet behind the drill bit. Here shale samples were generally dull black to very dark gray and very dark brown, blocky, ﬁssile, carbonaceous, spotted dark yellow to dark green to black ﬂuorescence, with instant to fast blooming milky cuts.

The curve section was landed in the Middle Bakken at an 80.00° angle at 9,553' MD/9,366.70’ TVD on October 27, 2014 at 12:30 PM CDT. The ﬁnal curve TD depth of 9,553’ MD was conﬁrmed when the bottoms up sample at this depth centained light bluish gray Middle Bakken dolomite with trace amounts of very dark brown/gray to dull

After the intermediate 7” casing shoe was set to 9,550’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 4:30 AM CDT on October 29, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was oﬂicially determined at 9,543’ MD/-7,461’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned ﬁ‘om the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near survey depth of 10,650’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 9,588’ MD/—7,47 1’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,600’ — 11,500’ MD averaged 90.3 degrees, and ﬂour 11,500’ ~ 12,200’ averaged 89.9 degrees. The formation dip angle from 12,200’ — 15,250’ then angled up again at an average of 90.2 degrees, and then slightly angled down with an average angle of 89.9 to 90 degrees ﬁom 15,250’ MD to TD of 16,640’ MD.
