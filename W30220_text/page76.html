GAMIWA
Gamma readings throughout Lateral were consistent in magnitude and range compared to the readings encountered during the curve. Gamma readings in the Middle Bakken formation ranged between 56 and 101 API and averaged 81 API during the lateral

SAMPLES & GAS Typical samples in the Middle Bakken formation were described as:

SANDSTONE: Gray brown to brown, light gray to light gray brown, light gray tan to cream tan, very ﬁne to ﬁne, subrounded to subangular, moderately sorted, argillaceous, fair calcareous to dolomitic cementation, gray to dark gray, fair induration,
fair intergranular porosity, light brown oil stain, even bright yellow green ﬂuorescence, fair milky cut
The oil shows varied from weak to fair milky cuts, with even dull to bright yellow green ﬂuorescence throughout Lateral. Gas ranged from 17 — 4,097 units with an average of 1874 units throughout the lateral.
Conclusion
The Eckert Foundation 152-102-22-1 5-1H Lateral reached a total depth of 20,712’ MD on April 1, 2014 at 12:15 PM MST. The Gamma ranged from 45 - 101 API with average of 79 API. Gas averaged 1874 units throughout the lateral, The lateral section was drilled 100% in the Middle Bakken formation with poor to moderate intergranular porosity seen throughout the lateral, light to fair brown oil staining in samples, and weak to fair milky cuts and even dull to bright yellow green ﬂuorescence. The indications of good gases, and fair oil shows in the lithology while drilling indicate this well will be a good oil producer. The Eckert Foundation 152-102-22-15—1H will be awaiting completion to go online producing oil,
