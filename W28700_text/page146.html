Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H
Prepare for Frac:
19. Determine which company will be providing the frac stack. NU 5M 7-1/16” x 10M 4-1/16" ﬂange that will seat directly on extended neck hanger — OR rig up other equipment so wellhead is now rated to 10M. 20. Discuss timing of frac job.
21. NU 10M 4—1/16” manual wheel frac valve and flow tee with a night cap. This equipment is necessary to pressure test the frac string.
22. Pressure Test if Frac String mnPressure up on annulus to 4,000 psi. Have tapped bull plug available to install in backside valve. Install with 5,000 psi gauge to ensure seal assembly is holding. Tubing head is rated to 5,000 psi if seal assembly does not hold.
23. When pressure testing the annulus, have the 4 1/2” casing open at surface to see if there is any communication.
24. Pressure Test if no Frac String—Check with --Maximum pressure the 7" 32# P110 casing should be exposed to is 10,000 psi (1.24 SF)
25. Rig down casing crew. (If Frac string was run)
26. The wing valves on the “B” section are 5M and are now the backside valves for the 4 1/2” frac string.
27. Move excess 4 1/2” casing to side of location. Make a note of how much casing is left on location.
28. Ensure the flow tee is rigged up with three (3) 10M 1-13/16” wing valves. Two (2) wing valves on one side of the flow tee and one (1) wing valve on the other side.
29. RU wireline truck.
30. MU Baker Oil Tools wireline set and wireline retrievable 10,000 psi bridge plug—set below liner hanger (3.77” OD but verify) --on Penkota wireline with CCL and #10 setting tool. Drift on 13.5# pipe is 3.795”.
Run 3.77” gauge ring. 31. Correlate to CBL and set plug in 1st full joint of casing below hanger. Depth is approximately _-ft. Deviation at -ft is 0.xx°.
32. TOOH with wireline tools but do not release truck.
33. Tie into wing valve with pressure truck. Will be pressure testing casing or frac string to 8,500 psi while holding 2,500 psi on backside of frac string to allow for tubing movement. Pressure on backside will increase as pressure is applied to the frac string.
34. Pressure up on 4 1/2" casing to 5,000 psi. Hold for 5 minutes. Check backside gauge. Continue increasing pressure to 8,500 psi. Hold for 20 minutes. Have chart recorder available for test.
35. After successful pressure test, RU wireline and retrieve bridge plug from liner.
36. RD wireline.
37. Close frac valve and secure well.
38. Rig down and release drilling rig.
