Bakken shale. The contact point of the top of the clean gamma is used for the set line to determine the target. The set line is 4’ above the base of the target window, which will be clean gamma and the top 6’ of the window will be the dirtier gamma which is ratty throughout. The lateral began with the wellbore at the top of the target window. Formation was expected to updip slightly while making the azimuth turn, then begin to drop aﬁer an azimuth of 180 was achieved. Overall formation dip was expected to be 89.7 degrees.

After drilling out intermediate casing the wellbore was allowed to drop in true vertical depth in order to enter the target window. After the wellbore encountered the base of the target window, sliding brought the inclination closer to 90 degrees in hopes of leveling off in the target window. At this time the azimuth turn was still being made, requiring a higher than average slide to rotate ratio. At 10,943’ measured depth a trip was made due to a suspected torqued scribe line. By that time the inclination had reached 94 degrees. The wellbore reached to about 2 feet below the lower Bakken shale before being turned down. No black shale was seen in samples. In the interval ﬁ’om 10,488’ to 10,943’ the rate of penetration ranged from 0.17 to 2.97 minutes per foot and averaged 0.81 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 22 to 528 units and averaged 213 units. Trip gas at 10,943’ reached 1252 units. Gamma ranged from 50 to 153 API and averaged 82 API. After picking up a new motor and bit, sliding succeeded in bringing the wellbore lower in true vertical depth in order to reencounter the target window. The wellbore drilled under the 4’ set line until 12,480’, at which point gamma indicates the top of the Three Forks Aquitard was encountered. Sliding quickly brought the wellbore higher in true vertical depth and out of the Aquitard. The wellbore continued to drill under the set line until 14,300’.
