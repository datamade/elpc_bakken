Aﬂer the intermediate casing shoe was set to 9,695’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 10:40 AM CST on December 7, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 9,669’ MD/-7,399’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned from the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled oﬂ‘ within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near measured depth of 10,000’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 10,007’ MD/-7,410’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,700’ — 10,000’ MD averaged 90.1 degrees, and from 10,000’ — 14,610’ MD ranged from 89,75 to 8990 degrees.

The Upper Bakken Shale (UBS) was encountered at 9,558’ MD/-7,381’ SS, which was initially determined from an increase in ROP when transitioning out of harder limestone into soﬁer shale, and ﬁnally determined when gamma values dramatically increased as the gamma tool entered the UBS, with the gamma tool being 39 feet behind the drill bit. Here shale samples were generally dull black to very dark gray and very dark brown, blocky, ﬁssile, carbonaceous, spotted dark yellow to dark gray to black ﬂuorescence, with instant to fast blooming milky cuts.
