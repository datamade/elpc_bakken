Seven inch casing was run and set at 10,514 feet measured depth and cemented into place. Bit number six, a Hughes 4062, directional BHA, and four inch drill pipe were picked up. On the morning of the 15TH of October, 2014, the ﬂoat and cement were drilled, and a casing pressure test was successﬁrlly completed. The shoe and ten feet of open formation were drilled, and a formational integrity test was successﬁilly performed. The lateral section of the wellbore began drilling ahead at 9:00 AM CDT.
The Sanish ﬁeld targeting methodology was used for the duration of the lateral wellbore. A nine foot targeting window was used, with the top of the window initially set at 10,243 feet true vertical depth at 10,385 feet measured depth, and projected at an anticipated dip rate of 90.45 degrees. The target line was set six feet below the top, with the base of the window an additional three feet below the target. The observed dip rate of the lateral rolled, averaging between 90.4 and 90.7 degrees between multiple B to C facies gamma contacts.
The ﬁrst day of lateral drilling, the 15TH of October, 2014, began at 9:00 AM CDT, with drill rates while rotating averaging between 100 and 130 feet per hour, and 50 feet per hour while sliding. Background gas averaged 440 units. The ﬁrst surveys out of intermediate casing saw inclinations of 87.82, well below the EOC projection of 9023 degrees. The wellbore dropped into the C facies following 10,510 feet measured depth, and out the bottom of the target window following 10,550 feet measured depth. Multiple upward slides, as well as slides to ﬁnish the planned turn were implemented over the ﬁrst day of drilling. Samples consisted primarily of light to dark gray, gray brown, cream gray, siltstone, which was pyritic, argillaceous, moderately to heavily dolomitic, and moderate dark gray striations, with fair induration.
