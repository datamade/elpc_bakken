Geologic Summary

Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation’s Dishman #13-19-2H is a horizontal well targeting the Middle Bakken formation, to be drilled in an east southeast direction at a vertical section azimuth of 102.48 degrees for approximately 7,102 feet vertical section. The well is located in the NW ‘A, SW ‘/4, Section 4, Township 153N, Range 92W of Mountrail County, northwest of New Town, North Dakota.
This well was spud in on October I“, 2014 at 1:30 AM CDT. Well site geological services began at 8,300 feet measured depth on October 8TH, 2014 shortly before 3:00 AM CDT. A thirteen and one half inch wellbore was drilled to 2,126 feet measured depth, and nine and ﬁve eighths inch casing was run and set. The remaining vertical wellbore and curve was drilled, and seven inch intermediate casing was ran and set at 10,514 feet measured depth. One six inch bit was used while drilling lateral number one. Total gas hotwire monitoring system was used for the duration of logging services.

Began logging in the Charles Formation
Logging services began at 8,300 feet measured depth in the Charles formation at 3:00 AM CDT on October 8”, 2014. Charles consisted primarily of anhydrite, limestone, and interlaminated dolomite. Drill rates ranged from 05-15 minutes per foot. Background gas averaged 30 units. A trip was performed within the Charles at 8,462 feet measured depth at 1:00 PM CDT on October 8”, 2014 due to slow drill rates. Bit number three, a Smith MDSi616 was picked up and tripped in, drilling resumed shortly after 10:00 PM CDT. Base of the last salt was picked at 8,578 feet measured depth, 8,577 feet true vertical depth, with a drill break of .5 to 1 minute per foot. Samples following consisted primarily of beds of anhydrite, limestone, and minor dolomite, with interbedded shale through the Mission Canyon.
