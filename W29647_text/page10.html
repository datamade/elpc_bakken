Allocation Procedure

SM Energy Company will use the following procedure to allocate production between the Beverly 2-16HN, Faye 2-16HS, Riley 28—16HS, and Virgil 23-16HN, at the Alvin 4-15 CTB:

Each well has an individual flowline, individual oil, water, and gas flow meters, and tanks. All four wells will have 6’ x 20’ vertical treaters rated to 125 psi.

Oil - Oil will be sold to AP! standards at LACT units on the CTB facility location. Daily volumes will be measured by guided wave radar tank gaugers and also by dedicated Coriolis meters placed downstream from each well’s oil shipping tank but before being sold and commingled at the LACT point. Any differences in the sum of oil measured at the Coriolis meters and the LACT meter reading will be measured each day and back allocated to the appropriate well. Oil custody transfer will be received by Summit Midstream Partners.

Gas —— Produced gas from each treater will flow into the heater treater separation vessel and gas will come off of the top of each treater past an orifice plate meter that measures the total gas produced for each well. From here the gas that is going to sales passes through an ABB TotalFlow orifice meter (or equivalent) and on to OneOk’s gas gathering system. If line pressure or other issues prevent gas from being sent to sales, the gas will be sent down a flare line to a Steffes flare/combustor combination. This location will have a vapor recovery unit and thus tank vapor gas will be metered through a dedicated gas sales meter downstream of the VRU and be sent into the sales line.
