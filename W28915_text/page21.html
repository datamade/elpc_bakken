Build Curve

The EN-VP&R-154-94—2536H-3 build curve began on November 4, 2014 at a measured depth of 10,180' in the mid portion of the Lodgepole formation and was drilled with an average drill rate of 68 ft/hr while sliding and rotating. The Lodgepole consisted of soft to ﬁrm limestone, that is also light gray, gray, and light gray brown in color. Landing estimations ﬂuctuated as high as 10,789' TVD and as low as 10,797' TVD throughout the Lodgepole formation with the original projected TVD set at 10,786' TVD. The ideal landing was set at 10, 791 ’ TVD once recording the top ofthe Three Forksformaz‘iorz at 10, 759' TVD.
The Upper Bakken Shale formation was encountered at the base of the Lodgepole formation at a MD of 10,715‘ and a TVD of 10,616'. The lithology of shale was black in color and very carbonaceous with fair yellow oil shows. The top of the Bakken shale was 6' low from projection which projected the estimated landing to 10,792' TVD. After 38‘ MD and 24' TVD the base of the Bakken Shale was reached and the top of the Middle Bakken was encountered at a MD of 10,753' and TVD of 10,640', projecting landing at 10,793‘ TVD. Lithology moved from carbonaceous shale to light brown to brown to light brown gray sandstone. Shows maintained fair quality with yellow cuts. Drill rates decreased slightly and averaged 50—60 ﬁ/hr while rotating and sliding. Continued to drill the Middle Bakken formation on the way to the Lower Bakken Shale which was encountered at a measured depth of 10,869' MD and a TVD of 10,702', trending along with projection. The lithology of the Lower Bakken Shale was similar to the upper shale and consisted of black carbonaceous shale, fairly brittle, with interbedded pyrite. Oil shows continued to be good quality and background gas ranged 150 to 700 units. Entered the lower shale at approximately 63 degree inclination and exited at 70 degrees inclination in the Three Forks formation. The Three Forks formation was recorded aﬁer a gamma count decrease below 150 units at 11,012 ' MD and 10,759' TVD, conﬁrming the ﬁnal landing at 10,791' TVD which targeted 32' into the formation. Lithology moved to a light gray to light blue gray to tan sandstone, which is fairly calcareous, argillaceous in parts, with good oil shows present. The EN-VP&R-154-94—2536H-3 build curve was landed at 11,200' MD and 10,787’ TVD on November 5, 2014 at 2:40 pm. CST. The rig proceeded to ream the curve wellbore and trip out
to lay down build curve tools before picking up 7" casing.
