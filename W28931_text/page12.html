NOTES ON 9-5/8” SURFACE CASING DESIGN:

The key point in designing surface casing is to extend the shoe of the surface casing beyond the top of the Pierre Shale. This ensures that all potable or usable water zones are covered. At this point in the well design, the drilling ﬂuid is changed to oil base mud to contend with salt sections that occur at approximately 6,800’ TVD and 9,150’ TVD. The 36 ppf J55 casing accomplishes these criteria easily. A higher burst rating is not necessary based on industry standards throughout the Williston Basin.
In the event the surface casing burst rating was increased, the normally pressured and sub normally pressured zones all the way thru the Madison Group at approximately 8,500’ TVD has the potential to break down. Therefore the increased pressure rating of surface casing is not warranted. It is not feasible to set the surface casing point at a greater depth because the shut in surface pressures would not appreciably increase. The Dakota formation (5,100’ TVD) is frequently weak. Typically, we drill the intermediate section with 10.5 ppg OBM to the 7” casing point. In the event we lost circulation in the exposed intermediate interval, the wellbore will not be entirely evacuated and the surface casing can be shut in without compromising the pressure rating of the 36 ppf J -55.
