DETAILS OF WORK Hess used the following process of soil stabilization using commercial grade Portland cement on the above referenced well between February 2nd, 2015 and February 3rd, 2015.
Drilling location sub-grade was established using standard methods. Once ﬁnish grade was established, a milling machine was used to combine commercial grade Portland cement with sub-grade. The milling machine pulverized and mixed the soil and cement together at a depth of 8". Water was added to milling process, as necessary, to meet
optimum moisture content of soil.
Once milling process was complete, a smooth drum roller compacted milled material into place. A motor grader then established ﬁnal surface grade.
Affected area was then left untouched for approximately 24 hours to allow for curing process of treated area.
