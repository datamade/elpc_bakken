Mission Canyon 8,015 ’ MD 8,011 ’ T VD -6, 007’ SS
The Mission Canyon formation top was picked at 8,015’ MD/-6,007’ SS, and was picked from lithology change and a temporary increase in ROP when transitioning from the Charles formation limestone into Mission Canyon limestone. ROP in the Mission Canyon ranged from 0.20 to 5.30 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranging from 22 to 193 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were light to medium gray and dark gray in part, medium to light brown and dark brown in part, packstone to wackestone, moderately argillaceous, moderately carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair intergranular porosity, light to dark green and dark yellow and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with moderately fast streaming cuts.

Lodgepole

The Lodgepole formation top was picked at 8,610’ MD/—6,602’ SS, where samples changed from a light to medium brown and gray limestone to a medium to dark gray limestone. ROP in the Lodgepole ranged from 0.30 to 4.90 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, with gas measurements ranging from 8 to 407 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone which were medium gray to dark and light gray, brown to light brown in part, argillaceous, slightly carbonaceous, wackestone, silty, slightly carbonaceous, were moderately well indurated, with fair to good intergranular porosity and light green to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with fast streaming cuts.
Original Curve Section 8,900’ — 9,914 ’ MD
The curve section of Parshall 97-3332H began 290’ MD into the Lodgepole formation at a KOP of 8,900’ MD. Directional tools were picked up on December 14, 2014, and the curve was started at 9:00 PM CST on December 14, 2014. Drilling rates in the curve ranged from 0.10 to 8.90 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, and gas measurements ranged from 16 to 1,004 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the curve to correlate Parshall 97-3332H with Parshall 98-33H.
