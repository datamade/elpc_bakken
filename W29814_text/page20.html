The wellbore ended low to the 8 foot target window that had been most recently projected at 9042". The target and formation had risen 60 feet over the course of the entire lateral. The wellbore is estimated to have occupied the 8 foot target window for 93% of the lateral.
The lithology of the cuttings recovered indicated a heavily clastic, detrital sand/siltstone, darker in color and bioturbated in the lower portions of the middle Bakken. The upper extent of the member was represented by a tan and occasionally translucent dolomite that lacked any signiﬁcant amounts of sedimentary material. Notable to strong shows of hydrocarbons were identiﬁed throughout the entire lateral. The strongest of the shows were produced while drilling in the top half of the target window, within the more pure dolomite, and closer to the upper Bakken shale.
