Lower Bakken Dolomite 11,320’ MD 11,116’ T VD -8, 797’ SS
At 1 1,1 16’ TVD the Lower Bakken Dolomite was encountered. This top was picked from lithology, gamma, and ROP changes. The Lower Bakken Dolomite exhibited cuttings that were dark gray to medium gray, dark cream, dark gray staining in part, soft to gummy, microcrystalline, sub-rounded to sub-angular, carbonaceous, slightly argillaceous, calcareous, moderate intimation, good to moderate intercrystalline porosity, scattered dull yellow green ﬂuorescence, with a slow light cloudy cut.

At 11,150’ TVD the Three Forks formation was encountered. This formation was picked from lithology, gamma, and ROP changes. fair intercrystalline porosity, yellow to dull green ﬂuorescence, with a delayed thin streaming cut.
Horizontal 11,510’ — 20,932’ MD
The Horizontal section was started using brine water, in the Three Forks Formation at 8:20 AM CDT on December 11th, 2014. Drilling times ranged from 0.1 to 12.2 min/ft. Gas averaged 35 units with values as high as 443 units. Gamma was monitored and correlated to calculate formation dips throughout the Lateral.
The ﬁrst section of the Lateral experienced a formation dip of 89.5 to 89.68 degrees. The well bore was drilled just above the “Top Window” marker for the majority of this section. In this section we were able to come down in zone and see gamma from
our entire drilling window TVD. This provided a base line gamma signature to gee-steer the remainder of the Lateral.

In the second section of the Lateral the wellbore tracked very well above the “Top Window” marker at a calculated formation dip of 89.58 degrees. At 16,000’ MD the well bore dropped below the marker and remained in between the “Top Window” marker and the “E” marker illustrated on the Well Tracker. At 16,832’ MD, the assembly was unable to slide for direction/inclination, so the decision was made to trip out of hole to pick up an agitator and shock sub. At 17,000’ MD, a hard streak that exhibited moderate amounts
of pyrite was encountered. The lowest part of the well was encountered in this section at 17,450’ MD/11,221.74’ TVD.
