All in SE/SE Sec 17-T158N-R95W, Williams County
All future infill wells (Middle Bakken and Three Forks) in the DSU defined by T158N, R95W, Sections 17 and 8

The above wells have the same royalty interest owners and may or may not have different working interest owners. Prior to commingling, the oil, water and gas streams for each well will be metered using continuous measurement, located at their respective wellsite.
General
Each well will flow into a dedicated three phase separator/treater on the wellsite location where the oil, gas and water will be separated and continuous measured before being commingled and delivered to a
central facility. The continuous measurement of oil, gas and water will be used to allocate production volumes back to the individual well as follows;
a) An allocation factor will be calculated for a given well by dividing the individual wells metered production by the sum of all the individual wells metered production that flow into the commingled facility.
b) The allocated production will be calculated for each well by multiplying its allocation factor by the total oil, gas or water stream leaving the facility.
The metering device used to measure the oil, gas and water volumes at the dedicated three phase separator/treater are as follow;

OIL —The oil metering device on each well will be a Coriolis-style meter using electronic flow measurement (EFM) and appropriate industry and manufacture standards. Each oil meter will be equipped with l’proving loop” connections. Initially, oil sales will be measured through trucked oil load tickets. However, it is the intent of the operator to install a LACT meter at a future date.

GAS ~—The gas metering device on each well will be an orifice plate meter using EFM and appropriate industry and manufacture standards. Gas sales will also be measured via an orifice plate meter using EFM and appropriate industry and manufacture standards.

WATER — The water metering device on each well will be a Coriolis-style meter using electronic flow measurement (EFM) and appropriate industry and manufacture standards. Each water meter will be equipped with "proving loop” connections. initially, water will be removed for disposal and measured through trucked water load tickets. However, it is the intent of the operator to install a SWD transmission system with metering capability at a future date.
