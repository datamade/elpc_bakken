Geologic Summary

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation, Sondrol 11—3-3H was drilled as a Bully well with primary interest in the Middle Bakken Formation. The well was drilled by Pioneer 74 ﬂex rig. The well is located in the NWNW quarter of section 3 T148N R99W in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Sondrol 11-3-3H was spudded on February 22, 2015 at 10:30 PM CST. The well reached a total depth of 20,668’ MD on March 18, 2015. Geological services began at 9,450’ MD in the Charles Formation on March 9, 2015 and continued to TD. Samples were caught by mudloggers throughout the well. All samples were washed, cataloged, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Society in Grand Forks, North Dakota. Total Gas LTD gas equipment was used to monitor gas throughout the well in correspondence with NOV RigSense EDR.
Vertical 9,450’ — 10,710’MD

Logging began in the Charles Formation at a measured depth of 9,450 feet at 9: 15

AM CST on March 9, 2015. Rate of penetration varied throughout the different rock types and formations but averaged 113 feet per hour throughout the vertical section. Gas averaged 18 units with a maximum reading of 847 units in the Charles Formation. The Pioneer 74 ﬂex rig reached kick off point at 9:00 AM CST on March 10, 2015 at 10,710 feet.

Base of Last Salt 9, 698’ MD 9, 685 ’ T VD -7,255 ’ SS
The Base of Last Salt Formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,698 feet, 20 feet high to the prognosis. It was picked at the base of a slight heat up in gamma at 9,698 feet MD Samples also changed from salt that was clear and translucent to off white anhydrite. The lithology of the Base of Last Salt Formation was mainly light gray to gray limestone and off white anhydrite with spotted yellow white ﬂuorescence and weak slow cuts. Rate of penetration was an average of 85 ft/hr. Gas was an average of 17 units with a maximum of 18 units through the lower Charles.
Mission Canyon 9, 938’ MD 9,925 ’ T VD -7,495’ SS

The Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,938

feet, 14 feet low to the prognosis. It was picked at a high to low gamma signature at 9,838 feet MD, Samples also changed from tan gray, light gray, limestone to cream tan, tan, cream, limestone. The lithology of the Mission Canyon Formation was limestone that ranged in color from cream tan to gray to light gray and cream gray that produced a spotted ﬂuorescence and weak cuts. Rate of penetration was an average of 113.2 ﬁz/hr. There was no measurable gas encountered in the Mission Canyon here.
