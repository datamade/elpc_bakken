GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

The Rennerfeldt 6-30H1 is a Three Forks well targeting the low gamma ray, high porosity zone above the Internal 1 Shale and is located on Section 19 T155N R98W in Williams County, North Dakota. The initial phase is a vertical and curve, landing 21.5 feet within the Three Forks Formation. The Rennerfeldt 6-301-11 spudded on December 16, 2014 and reached a total depth of21,690’ MD on January 11, 2015. The Bottom Hole Location is 239.39' FSL & 1,461 .79‘ FEL in Section 31 T155N R98W, Williams County, North Dakota.
Wellsite geological services began at 9,400’ in the Charles Formation, just above the Base of the Last Charles Salt at 2:36 pm am CST on December 20, 2014. Thirty foot lagged samples were collected during the straight hole and curve while one hundred foot lagged samples were caught during the lateral. All samples were washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey depository in Grand Forks, ND.

Mission Canyon

The top ofthe Mission Canyon was picked at 9,720’ MD and 9,719’ TVD, with no difference from the prognosis depth. The pick was based off of the gamma marker and lithology change, along with drilling breaks. Drill rates in the Mission Canyon averaged 0.96 minutes per foot with gas up to 104 units and an average of 39 units. Typical samples were medium gray to dark gray limestone, wackestone to packstone, moderately argillaceous, poorly to moderately indurated, poor intergranular porosity. Cuts and ﬂuorescence shows were sporadic and questionable due to the oil-based mud.
