1.0 INTRODUCTION
The Nelson 157-100-25A-36-3H is a down-dip south lateral Middle Bakken well with a
SHL located in SEC 25, T157N, R100W in Williams County, North Dakota. The primary
pay zone was 13'-23' under the top of the Middle Bakken. This pay zone was picked for
its production potential and quality of reservoir rock. The objective was to steer the well
within the defined pay zone and within the legal requirements of the state. Directional
drilling and MWD services were performed by Leam. Skylar Kjos, Patrick Haley and
Aclam Kpekpasse were the primary wellsite geologists, providing geo-steering and mud
logging from Columbine Logging, Inc.

2.0 SERVICES
2.1 Wellsite Geology
Geological consulting and mud logging started on 01/2/2015 for the vertical/curve
section and on 1/27/2015 for the lateral. Services provided included; morning reports,
noon reports, evening reports, sample examination, sample recording via pictures,
production of vertical and horizontal mudlogs, geosteering, sample collection and
bagging, sample mailing and a final end of well report.
2.1.1 Geosteering
Within the Middle Bakken, the primary objective was to drill below the packstone marker
at the top of our set window. The bottom of the cool gamma (the packstone marker) was
found to be 13' below the top of the Middle Bakken. Gamma patterns were compared
with the offset log and a Gamma Ray vs. TVD log was created while landing the curve to
in order to pick Formation Tops and land in the targeted zone. Steering in the lateral was
accomplished by calculating dip from relevant gamma markers, as well as by using
lithology, total gas and ROP to determine our position within the formation.
2.1.2 Gamma and Surveys
Gamma and survey MWD services were provided by Leam. Proposed Azimuth of the
well was 181.71°. Surveys were taken every 30' through the curve from 9,732' MD
through 10,511' MD. Surveys were taken every 90' through the lateral from 10,611' MD
to TD at 20,551' MD The survey tie in used was at 2,099' MD, 2,098.9' TVD. Gamma
was logged from 2,099' MD to 20,500' MD at TD. The survey tool was 51' back of bit
and the gamma tool was 43' back of bit. Gamma within the target window ranged from
50-190 api. Marking the top of the target window was a hot (high 160s) gamma marker,
and marking the bottom of the target window was a cool (low 70's) gamma marker.
These markers were encountered multiple times within the lateral to help confirm dip.
2.2 Mud Logging
2.2.1 Sample Examination
Samples were collected every 30 ft in the straight hole and build section, and every 30 ft
while drilling the lateral. Descriptions included; mineralogy, color, firmness, argillaceous
content, structure, texture, allochems, porosity, oil stain, and hydrocarbon fluorescence.
Carbonate identification was determined with 10% dilute HCl-, alizarin red and
calcimeter. Hydrocarbon fluorescence was determined using a fluoroscope with a UV
lamp.
2.2.2 Gas Detection
Gas was logged using a Bloodhound total gas/chromatograph system. The gas detection
system uses an infra-red detector to measure total gas and the chromatograph separates
and measures gases C1, C2, C3, iC4 and nC4. Gas was recorded in units where 1 unit
equals 100 ppm. The gas detection system measured gases: C1, C2, C3, IC4, NC4, H2S,
O2 and CO2.
The Bloodhound Gas Detection and Chromatograph system use digital signal processing
techniques and non-dispersive infrared and chemical sensors for gas detection. The
system uses a proprietary chromatograph, which has the capability to detect from 0 to
10,000 gas units. This translates as 0 to 100% typical naturally-occurring hydrocarbon
gas mixtures. Calibration is performed using National Institute of Standards and
Technology (NIST) traceable calibration gases. Lab calibration points include 0%, 2.5%,
and 100% pure methane. Complete immunity to saturation or damage in the presence of
4
high concentrations of both light and heavy hydrocarbon gases precludes the necessity of
constant re-calibration or zero referencing. This allows the Bloodhound to react to
hydrocarbon based gases from zero to 100% in concentration without dilution.
Lag time was approximated from a calculation of annular velocity based on: pump
output, open-hole diameter, cased hole diameter, collar diameter, drill pipe diameter and
bottom whole assembly. Connection gases were monitored to confirm lag time
calculations and thereby adjust lag time when needed.

3.4 Gas and Oil Shows
The vertical-build section was drilled with oil-based mud (OBM) and the lateral was
drilled with brine/production water. The OBM contributed a background gas of 30-50
units and saturated cuttings with oil, masking formation oil shows. Gas shows were
consistently between 500-2000 units in the lateral within the target zone.
3.4.1 Oil Shows
OBM was used in the vertical, masking any oil shows. In the lateral part of the well, the
oil shows in the dolomitic siltstone of the Middle Bakken ranged from: NFSOC (no
fluorescence, stain, odor or cut) to abundant yellow fluorescence, streaming bright white
cut, no stain. Most samples were far too contaminated with lube to record an oil show
indicative of the actual formation in the area being drilled. These sample oil shows were
recorded as NFSOC.

7.0 SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION
The Nelson 157-100-25A-36-3H is a down-dip south lateral Middle Bakken well with a
SHL located in SEC 25, T157N, R100W in Williams County, North Dakota. The primary
pay zone was 13'-23' under the top of the Middle Bakken. This pay zone was picked for
its production potential and quality of reservoir rock. The objective was to steer the well
within the defined pay zone and within the legal requirements of the state. The majority
of the well was drilled within the siltstone within our target zone. In-zone percentage and
footage are 77.7% and 7,780' respectively. The gamma within the target window ranged
from 50-180 api. Marking the top of the target window was a hot (high 170s) gamma
marker, and marking the bottom of the target window was a cool (high 70's) gamma
marker.
The targeted pay zone for this well was 13' to 23' below the top of the Middle Bakken.
The wellbore started in the top half of the window and moved down through section for
the first 600', before being steered through the target zone and encountering the hot (165
api) gamma marker at the top of the window from around 11,700'-12,250' MD. The well
bore was steered back to target zone center again before again rising out the top of the
target zone between 13,200' MD due to steepening dips before being steered back into
zone at 14,478' MD. The wellbore drifted out the base of the target zone from 16,295'-
16,720' MD before being steered up and back into zone. After steering the well up to the
target zone top between 17,247'-17,422' MD to confirm dip with the hot gamma marker
at the top of the window, the remainder of the wellbore was drilled in zone to TD at
20,551' MD.
The formation had an average dip of 89.56° and an overall dip of 89.6°. The well was
mostly drilled near the center of our set window, venturing out the target top four times
and the target base once. The objective while drilling the lateral was to keep the wellbore
in the warm gamma marker, between 13' and 23' below the top of the Middle Bakken.
Currently the well is awaiting completion.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
