GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources, Inc drilled the North-bound Syracuse 7-23H1 in Sections 26, 23, and 14
of Township 152N, Range 99W into the Mississippian/Devonian age Middle Bakken. The Well
spud using Cyclone 4 on December 28, 2014 at a surface location of 280' FNL, and 583' FWL.
The surface was pre-drilled with surface casing set at 2312'MD.
Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and chromatography data
were monitored and recorded using a Continental Laboratories total gas detector and
chromatograph Varian AGD 01. Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the
aid of lagged total gas data, lagged geological samples, a Pason EDR Workstation, and gamma
ray logging coupled with directional drilling survey data Leam provided Directional Drilling,
and Measurement While Drilling (MWD) services. NorAm Wellsite Services provided both Mud
Logging and Geosteering Services.
NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8900' MD on December 31, 2014, just
above the Top Charles Salts, and for the remainder of the 8 3/4" vertical portion, which was
drilled to Kick-off Point (KOP) at 10760'MD. There were no trips in the vertical.
The Base Last Salt top came in 8' higher than prognosis. The landing target of 11250'TVD (20'
into Middle Bakken) was raised 8' to 11242'TVD. Once the gamma probe on MWD tool was
added for the curve assembly, and the markers within the Lodgepole were lined up, the target
was then raised another 5' to 11237'TVD. The Upper Bakken Shale came in 14' high to
prognosis, as well as the Middle Bakken Member.
The curve was drilled in 16 hours on January 2-3, 2015. The wellbore entered into the Middle
Bakken top at 11372'MD, 11216'TVD, and landed at 11508'MD, 11230.0'TVD, 19' into the
Middle Bakken. 7 Inch Intermediate casing operations immediately followed, before the rig was
skid over to the Chicago 7-26H.
The Lateral resumed drilling on January 24, 2015 at 15:45 hours. 2961' into the lateral at
14469'MD was the 1st trip for a new BHA on January 25, 2014 at 22:00 hours due to low ROP.
Drilling resumed the next morning January 26, 2015 at 13:12 hours, after circulating the trip gas
out. Gamma Ray was re-logged from 13374'-14469'MD. 110' of new hole was drilled before
MWD Tool failed at 14579'MD. Pipe was then tripped out to KOP, to wireline the MWD Tool.
Drilling resumed early January 27, 2015 at 00:40 hours, after re-logging Gamma Ray from
14500'-14579'MD. Gas throughout the well averaged 1500 units background while venting
through the gas buster, with a 4-6' intermittent flare. Samples in the upper portion of the target
zone were evenly oil stained, with moderate to fast streaming and diffuse cuts. At 17:40 hours on
January 29, 2015, the Syracuse 7-23H reached TD of 21542'MD. Projection to bit is 21542'MD,
11282.55'TVD, 90.9 INC, and 359.8 AZM. Overall dip was an 89.7 with the wellbore in zone
95% of the time. Bottom hole Location is 264.5'FNL & 637'FWL, Section 14, T152N, R99W,
McKenzie Co., North Dakota.

Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
