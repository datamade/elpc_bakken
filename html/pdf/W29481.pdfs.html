GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
The Continental Resources, Inc. Barney 7-29H, surface hole location 300' FNL, 1555' FWL,
S29-T155N-R98W, is located approximately 5 miles south of Epping, North Dakota, and
terminates near the southern boundary of the Brooklyn Field, approximately 18 miles west of
the Nesson Anticline. Barney 7-29H is the 7th southerly lateral originating along the northern
boundary of Section 29 and terminating near the southern boundary of Section 32. The well
was planned for one 9,709' long southerly transect in the Bakken Formation Middle Member.
The initial structural interpretation assumed a consistent down dip of approximately 89.65°,
with the terminus of the lateral bore expected to be 60' lower than the landing point of the
curve. The intent was to remain in a 14' thick zone in the Middle Member. The Target Zone is
strati graphically situated between 14' and 28' beneath the base of the Upper Shale. The
drilling plan included a vertical hole to be drilled to a kick-off point of MD 10643' in the
Lodgepole Formation, then begin a 13.00°/100' build to a landing point of MD 11333' TVD
11084'. The lateral was planned to start with a 3.00°/100' turn holding an 89.65° inclination to
MD 12260', then holding 180.00° azimuth and 89.65° inclination to TD at MD 21,042'.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Nabors Rig 784 spudded the Barney 7-29H December 5, 2014. Prior to the Nabors rig moving
onto the hole, a spudding rig drilled a 13 1/2" hole with fresh water to 2245'. The surface hole
was isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J55 STC casing and cemented to surface. Diesel invert mud was
used while drilling from the surface casing shoe to intermediate casing point at MD 11349'.
Two 8 ¾" PDC bits and two 1.5 degree mud motors were used to drill from surface casing to
kick off point at MD 10630'. Directional tools employing a 2.38° bend assembly were used to
land the build curve at MD 11369' TVD 11081', 3' into the Target Zone with a projected
inclination of 89.20°. 7", 32# P110 IC, LTC intermediate casing was then run to 11349' and
cemented.
Lateral
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
with 9.9 to 10.0# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. One 6" PDC bit
and 1.5 degree steerable motor assembly was used to drill the lateral section, even with a
Lower Shale strike and subsequent sidetrack.

LITHOLOGY AND HYDROCARBON SHOWS
Vertical and Curve
Geologic evaluation began at 9450' in the lower Charles Formation. The Base of the Last
Charles Salt was encountered at TVD 9493' (SS TVD -7205). No significant hydrocarbon
shows were observed while drilling through the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at TVD 9724' (SS TVD -7435).
No hydrocarbon shows were observed while drilling through the Mission Canyon Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole "X" Marker was observed at TVD 10347' (SS TVD -8058). The well
was kicked­off at MD 10630'. Four gamma markers were selected and used to calculate the
curve landing point while drilling through the Lodgepole. No significant shows were observed
while drilling through the Lodgepole.
The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11165' TVD 11045' (SS TVD -
8756), 7' structurally low to offset well Barney 4-29H1, 3' high to Barney 1-29H and 26' low to
Barney 3-29H3.
Samples contained carbonaceous shale, black to very dark gray brown,
blocky, brittle, non-calcareous and non-fluorescent with slow milky to slowly streaming pale
yellow cut.
Total gas increased rapidly from 300 units to a maximum of 2929 units
approximately 3 vertical feet above the top of the Upper Shale (possibly denoting micro
fractures) and ranged from 189u to 2417u while the shale was penetrated.
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was encountered at MD 11225', TVD 11064'
(SS TVD -8775), 8' structurally low to offset well Barney 4-29H1, 3' high to Barney 1-29H and
24' low to Barney 3-29H3. Samples were varied and observed to consist of argillaceous to
siltaceous dolomitic mudstone, slightly arenaceous, soft to firm, microcrystalline to very fine
sucrosic, spotty medium to dark brown oil stain, spotty to even faint pale yellow fluorescence
and immediate milky to slowly streaming pale yellow green cut. Total gas was unstable while
drilling through the Middle Member and ranged from 456 units to 1952 units.
Lateral Sample Shows
Samples observed in the Primary Target Zone of Barney 7-29H were argillaceous dolomite,
friable to firm, microcrystalline to micro-sucrosic, slightly siltaceous, slightly to moderately
calcareous, poor to fair inter-crystalline porosity, faint dull yellow fluorescence and faint slow to
slowly streaming milky yellow green cut. Samples from the base of the Target Zone contained
argillaceous dolomite, friable to firm, microcrystalline, slightly siltaceous, slightly calcareous,
poor inter-crystalline porosity, faint, dull, scattered yellow mineral fluorescence and slow
diffuse yellow green cut. Above the Target Zone, samples were argillaceous to siltaceous
dolomite, friable to sub-hard, microcrystalline, slightly calcareous, trace to fair inter-crystalline

TARGET ZONE IDENTIFICATION IN THE LATERAL
The Target Zone consists of a 14' thick segment of the Middle Member of the Bakken FM
beginning 14' beneath the base of the Upper Shale. Gamma API counts from 70 to 125
reflected the thinly bedded nature of the horizons in the target zone. Lithology in the target
was observed to be argillaceous dolomite with a subtle lithological change at the upper
boundary of the zone to slightly more siltaceous argillaceous dolomite. This was accompanied
by a sharp rise in gamma count from 80 to 125 when drilling through the top of the Target
Zone. Identification of the lower boundary of the zone was also subtle. The base of the zone
was evidenced by API gamma counts rising from 80 to 115, with a move in AGI counts to 125
when drilling deeper into the base. This gamma pattern was very similar to the pattern seen
while drilling 2' to 4' below the top of the Target Zone. Lithology both in and below zone
consisted of argillaceous dolomite, medium to light gray, slightly calcareous with poor inter-

LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of Barney 7-29H was
replaced with 9.9 to 10.0# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. The
curve was landed 3' below the top of the Target Zone with a projected inclination of 89.20° and
7" intermediate casing was set in the Bakken FM Middle Member at MD 11349' TVD 11081'.
The initial formation dip was calculated to be 89.69° when the top of the Target Zone was
breached at MD 12427' with the API count increasing from 85 to 125. 5' above the top, a large
gamma marker denoted by a rapid API increase from 50 to 140 was observed. The gamma
profile employed for this lateral was derived from Barney 6-29H1 and displayed a similar
magnitude marker near the base of the Target Zone. In an attempt to locate this marker,
which was later found to be much less distinct, the Lower Shale was encountered at MD
14080'. A decision was made to pull back to MD 12974' for sidetrack #1 and time drilled to MD
13006'.
Additional formation dips of 89.61°, 89.56° and 89.63° were calculated along the
length of the lateral by periodically allowing the wellbore to drill above the target zone where
somewhat consistent gamma signatures were observed. Also noted was a low gamma count
spike to 70-79 API which consistently occurred 3'-4' below the top of zone. A formation dip of
89.57° was calculated for the entire lateral. The 6" bore exposed 9641 linear feet of hole
through the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation. One 6" PDC bit and one 1.5 degree
steerable motor assembly was used to drill the lateral section, even with the shale strike and
subsequent sidetrack. Rotational drilling (including sidetrack) of 9686' (90.2%) and directional
slides of 1050' (9.8%) comprised drilling of the lateral. The wellbore remained in the target
zone approximately 8319' (86%), below the target zone 187' (2%) and above the target zone
1135' (12%).

CONCLUSIONS
Oil shows observed while drilling the Barney 7-29H were weak as were gas shows. Oil shows
in samples were consistent although weak throughout the lateral. Subsurface structural
control, utilizing data from Barney 6-29H1 and Barney 1-29H, was inadequate and did not
mirror Barney 7-29H. Similar samples and gamma signatures from above, below and in the
Target Zone made geological interpretation difficult. Steering to correct for inclination and/or
azimuth was responsive throughout the lateral. Average rotational ROP of 167'/Hr., slide ROP
of 25'/Hr. along with zero trips out of hole resulted in the lateral to be drilled in five days and
8:40 hours including the sidetrack. Barney 7-29H will be completed with a multi-stage fracture
stimulation after which its production potential will be revealed. Samples and surveys have
been delivered to the North Dakota Industrial Commission.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
