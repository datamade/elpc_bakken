Introduction
Continental Resources, Inc. successfully drilled the Bud 2-30H horizontal well at
an average Azimuth of 359.56º and an average Inclination of 89.8°, targeting the Middle
Bakken Formation from a surface hole location Section 31, T153N, R99W, (2160' FNL
& 756' FWL) in Williams County, North Dakota, drilling north to the northwest quarter of
the northwest quarter of Section 19, T153N, R99W, with a spacing unit consisting of
1280 acres. The well is located in the Crazy Man Creek Field in the West Central region
of the Williston Basin, North Dakota. The main well used for correlation was the
Alfsvaag 1-31H drilled by CRI in Section 31, T153N, R99W, Williams County, North
Dakota. The other nearby offset well was the Cornette POW 5300 34-26 drilled by
Oasis in Section 26, T153N, R99W, Williams County, North Dakota. The Bud 3-30H1
drilled by CRI in Section 31, T153N, R99W, Williams County, North Dakota, was also
used for an offset for the vertical and the curve sections of the Bud 2-30H.
Operations Summary
Vertical Section
The Bud 2-30H was spud on August 16, 2014 and the surface casing was
drilled out on September 8, 2014. Brara Geologic Consulting arrived on site
August 31, 2014 and rigged up gas and geological equipment and began
analysis on the Bud 3-30H1. After the vertical and curve were completed the rig
skid over for the vertical, curve and lateral of the Bud 2-30H. A Mudlogging
Systems, Inc. Redbox gas detection system was used for analyzing and
recording total gas and chromatograph data. The Bud 2-30H had preset 9 5/8"
surface casing at 1965' MD. An invert mud system was used to drill out from
surface throughout the landing of the curve.
The first BHA used included (Bit #1) an 8 ¾'' Security MM65D, and a
Hunting 7/8, 3.5 Stage, 1.5° Fixed Bent Housing mud motor and a Scientific
MWD tool, which was used to drill out from surface casing at 1965' MD to 8923'
MD. This assembly drilled 6958' in 46.83 bit hours. A trip was made at 8923' MD
for a motor failure. The second BHA used included (Bit #2) an 8 ¾'' Security
MM65D, and a Hunting 7/8, 3.5 Stage, 1.5° Fixed Bent Housing mud motor and a

Closing Summary
The Bud 2-30H was drilled from North to South in the Middle Bakken Dolomite
Member with the target consisting of ten vertical feet from nine feet below the Upper
Bakken Shale to 42' feet above the Lower Bakken Shale. The well bore is estimated to
have been in the target zone 87% of the lateral. The overall dip was 90.1°. Ninety foot
(90') surveys were taken throughout the turn in the lateral to total depth (TD).
Formation dip ran flat at a 90.2° between the intermediate casing shoe and
approximately 14000' MD, where the formation started to descend at an approximate
dip rate of 89.9°, for approximately 1800' MD. The formation then dropped to an 89.3° at
approximately 16000' MD. At 17000' MD the formation began to descend again at an
average dip rate of 89.6° to 19000' MD. From 19000' MD to 22300' MD the formation
ran at an approximate dip rate of 90.2°. At 22300' MD the formation began to descend
at 89.8° for the remainder of the lateral to total depth (TD) at 23017' MD. The majority of
the lateral was drilled in the middle to lower section of the target zone for the Bud 2-
30H.
Nearly all samples exhibited good even-scattered moderately yellow/green
fluorescence when examined under a fluoroscope. Oil cuts were commonly immediate,
diffuse/flash milky blue in color, leaving residual rings on the porcelain thumb dish. A
majority of samples were contaminated with petroleum-based lubricant, which was used
to achieve adequate bit weight to drill and to slide. This contamination masked some
fluorescence and oil cuts in some of the samples.
Throughout the Bud 2-30H the gas readings were considered good-fair,
averaging around 2500 to 4000 units. The most consistent gamma marker for this
lateral was found one foot above the bottom of the target zone and was approximately
one to two feet in thickness. This hotter gamma marker read about 120 (API). After total
depth was reached at 23017' MD on September 27, 2014, Brara Geologic Consulting
remained onsite for monitoring gas for the short trip and through running and setting the
production liner.

1.) The Continental Resources, Inc. Bud 2-30H was a successfully drilled two-run
lateral well in the Middle Bakken Dolomite Formation from a surface location in
Section 31, T153N, R99W (1610' FNL & 670' FWL) to a total depth (TD) of
23017' MD, 10761' TVD, (-8817' SS), with a bottom hole location in Section 19,
7

T153N, R99W (236' FSL & 657' FEL) in Williams County, North Dakota. The well
was drilled from the intermediate casing shoe to TD with one sidetrack at 15887'
MD due to an Upper Bakken Shale Strike at 16380' MD. Total depth was
reached at 09:00 hours on September 27, 2014. The Bud 2-30H required
nineteen total days of drilling and setting intermediate casing.
2.) Ultraviolet sample shows in this lateral would be rated as fair to good with
moderately fast to instant milky cuts with residual rings. Total gas shows and
overall porosity would also be rated as fair. Wellbore placement was within the
target zone for approximately 87% of the total 11925' of lateral section, which
should allow for excellent production opportunities.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
