2. Well Summary
The CCU Golden Creek 44-23MBH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Middle
Bakken Formation in Dunn County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled South to North, crossing
from the SHL at 410' FSL & 253' FEL of section 23, T147N, R95W, through into section 14, T147N,
R95W and followed an approximate azimuth of 1.42 degrees until reaching total depth at 227' FNL & 9'
FEL of section 14, T147N, R95W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately a 20 feet thick zone
(~10' ­30' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale). The overall dip rate of this lateral was
approximately 0.60' per 100' or 89.68 degree down dip.

5. Lithology
Samples: See mud log and horizontal logs for full sample descriptions. All samples were taken at
30' lagged intervals from the shakers, cleaned, labeled, and bagged for shipment to the state of North
Dakota. The samples collected with the best quality were within the first half of the lateral just outside
the casing shoe. After this, samples were returned ground up into fine sand and were extremely fine
grained at the tail end of the lateral.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
