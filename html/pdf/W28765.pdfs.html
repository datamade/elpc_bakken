GENERAL:
Newfield Production Company's double section Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H
was "spudded" as a Middle Bakken Formation Double lateral well on September 18th, 2014. The
subject well is being drilled in the Westburg field, located about 10 miles North West of Keene
in McKenzie County, North Dakota. Surface location of the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-
3H is 408' FNL, 869' FEL, NENE Section 21-T153N-R96W. The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-
21-28-3H was planned with the ultimate goal of oil production in the dolomite porosity of the
Middle Bakken formation. The horizontal well-path was planned to follow structure with an
estimated 90° average formation dip throughout the lateral to total depth at 21,735.07' MD. The
well bore was planned with one 38 degree turn and finished with drilling to the South following
an azimuth along 180.0°.
ENGINEERING:
The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H was drilled with Patterson UTI 346. Ground
level of the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H was 2,057' above mean sea level with the
Kelly bushing located at 2,082' above mean sea level, 25' above ground level.
The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H was drilled with fresh water from surface to
1,687' MD. A switch was then made and oil base mud, and was used from 1,687' MD to
10,531'MD. Once horizontal, the well was switched over to salt brine from 10,531' MD to total
depth at 20,410' MD.
The main lateral of Newfield Production Company's Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-
3H began on October 26th, 2014. It was drilled from 10,531' MD (10,181' TVD) to a TD at
20,410' MD (10,264.97'TVD, est). At TD a 5" liner was run in the vertical and 4.5" liner was
ran through the lateral and fracture stimulation was planned to enhance the well.
The final lateral profile for the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H showed a
net drop of 83' TVD over a span of 9750' of vertical section drilled bringing the average dip for
the whole lateral to approximately 89.52 deg. The bottom hole location for the Sand Creek
Federal 153-96-21-28-3H is 256' FNL, 1721' FEL of NENE Sec 28 T153N R96W.
GEOLOGICAL EVALUATION:
Geologic supervision of the Newfield Production Company's Sand Creek Federal 153-
96-21-28-3H well was provided by Geo-Link Geological Services with two-man geo-steering
and geological consulting service. Geologic evaluation began at a measured depth of 6,900' in
the Minnelusa Formation and continued until TD at the end of the lateral. An MSI Total Gas
System model TGC "Red Box" was used to record gas levels at the rigs shakers. The MSI
model TGC was integrated into Pason's EDR (Electronic Data Recorder) system. Also integrated
into Pason's EDR system was gamma data provided by Baker Hughes. Pason's EDR provided
drill rate, total gas, gamma and circulation data for viewing on location. Lagged samples for the
well were caught by the rig crews in 30' intervals from 6,900' through 10,531' and then 100'

The Charles Salt came in at 7,846' MD 7,831' TVD -5,749' sub-sea-level. The Charles
Salt consisted predominately of salts, limestone, and anhydrite. Salts from the Charles formation
were typically clear to translucent to translucent red in color, hard, massive, cubic and euhedral
crystalline structure, vitreous to sub glassy textures. Lithology below the salts in the Charles
formation consisted of limestone and anhydrites. Limestone's above the Ratcliffe interval within
the Charles formation were typically light gray to medium gray to bluish gray in color, firm to
brittle to hard, predominantly coarse to poor crystalline, wackestone, argillaceous, with poor
visible fracture porosity, no stain, no fluorescence, no odor, or cut fluorescence. No oil or gas
shows were observed while drilling the Charles Formation. No problems were encountered while
drilling the Charles Formation. The Base of the last Charles Salt was observed at 8,576' MD,
8,561' TVD and -6,479' sub-sea level.
The Mission Canyon formation top came in at 8,768' MD, 8,753' TVD and -6,671' sub-
sea level. The Mission Canyon formation consisted of gray limestones, moderately hard to hard
in hardness, cryptocrystalline wackestone to packstone in structure. Common shale inter-bedding
was observed. Poor to moderate oil show accompanied it in samples. Occasional lost circulation
intervals are found in the Mission Canyon. No other problems were encountered while drilling
the Mission Canyon formation.
The Lodgepole Formation top was recorded and observed at 9,378' MD, 9,363' TVD and
-7,281' sub-sea level. The Lodgepole Formation consisted of medium gray to gray to dark brown
to dark gray brown limestone, hard and microcrystalline to cryptocrystalline packstone and
wackestone in structure. No problems were encountered while drilling the Lodgepole Formation.
The False Bakken top was recorded and observed at 10,263' MD, 10,133' TVD and -
8,051' sub-sea level. The False Bakken formation consisted of dark brown to dark gray limey
shale's. The shale's of the False Bakken were typically firm to brittle, sub-platy in structure, and
silty to earthy in texture. A 4123 unit gas show was encountered while drilling the False Bakken
and had an oil show consisting of scattered faint yellow fluorescence with fast streaming blue
white cut fluorescence. No problems were encountered while drilling the False Bakken
formation.
The Upper Bakken Shale formation top was recorded and observed at 10,294' MD,
10,144' TVD and -8,062' sub-sea level. The Upper Bakken Shale's consisted of very dark gray
to black shale's, firm to soft to occasionally brittle in hardness, sub-blocky ­ blocky in structure,
carbonaceous to earthy in texture. Oil show was noted with drill cuttings of the Upper Bakken
Shale exhibiting a flash blue white cut fluorescence. A background gas increase was observed
while drilling the Upper Bakken Shale with a peak gas recorded of 2,141 units. No problems
were encountered while drilling the Upper Bakken Shale.

The Middle Bakken dolomite top was recorded and observed at 10,381' MD, 10,167'
TVD and -8,085' sub-sea level. The Middle Bakken consisted of light gray to gray to tan to gray
brown dolomite. The Middle Bakken dolomite was typically soft to firm and occasionally brittle
in hardness, very fine crystalline packestone in structure, with crystalline to sub sucrosic
textures. Drill cuttings of the Middle Bakken showed good oil shows with even dull green to
yellow green fluorescence, faint to very strong hydrocarbon odors, moderately bright milky
bluish white flash cut with secondary multi-streaming cut fluorescence, and good visible
porosity. Gas levels while drilling the middle Bakken formation ranged from 1000 units to 2000
units recorded by the ML TGC gas detector. Gas was run through the gas buster for the entire
lateral. An intermittent 2-15' flare was present for the entire lateral while drilling the Sand Creek
Federal 153-96-21-28-3H. The lateral was drilled 100% in the Middle Bakken, 47% in zone and
reached total depth of 20,410' MD on 10/29/2014 @ 04:50 hrs. Average dip for the lateral was
89.85°.
SUMMARY:
Newfield Production Company's Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-3H was drilled
successfully as a double lateral into the Middle Bakken Formation. Sand Creek Federal 153-96-
21-28-3H took 21 days to drill from "Spud" to TD.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
