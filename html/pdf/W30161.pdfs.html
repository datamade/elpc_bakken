GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources' Chicago 5-26H was the first of four wells to be drilled on a pad in
NWNE Section 26, T152N, R99W, McKenzie County, North Dakota. The 9 5/8-inch surface
casing had been set at 2253 feet, and H&P Rig 640 moved onto location and spudded at 12:30pm
on March 9, 2015. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision and mudlogging
operations near the top of the Charles formation on March 12 at 8850 feet. Total gas was
recorded using the MP2300, and chromatography was evaluated on the Varian CP4900 Micro-
GC Gas Analyzer. A trip was made for motor failure at 6992 feet, and again at 10150 feet. A
trip for bit failure was made at 10282 feet with possible junk in the hole. The fourth bit run
reached Kick-off Point at 10638 feet. Formation tops were all within five feet of prognosis, and
a revision to the Middle Bakken Landing Point Target of 11186' TVD was not necessary.
The curve section was drilled on March 16 with a Phoenix 2.38 degree fixed motor, and
the rate of build was projected at 12 degrees per 100 feet, with a turn midway through the curve
from 123° to 180° azimuth. The False Bakken marker appeared at 11304' MD (11140' TVD),
then there was an irregular gamma spike, and the top of the Upper Bakken Shale was 11340' MD
(11152' TVD). The Landing Point Target was revised four feet lower to 11190' TVD, and the
Middle Bakken came in at 11400' MD (11170' TVD). The curve was drilled in 15.5 hours from
KOP to a measured depth of 11610 feet (11189' TVD) with a vertical section of 642 feet. Gas
readings increased significantly at the False Bakken marker, from 700 units to a maximum of
4916 units. Flow was diverted through the gas buster with a steady 6 to 8 foot flare, and mud
weights fluctuated between 10.0 and 10.2 ppg. A wiper trip through the Charles Salts to 8790
feet, then washed and reamed to bottom, circulated out 4266 units of gas. Intermediate casing
was run by the rig crew to a measured depth of 11594 feet on March 18.
The lateral was drilled out on March 19 with a 6-inch PDC bit and 1.5 degree fixed
Phoenix motor turning at 0.80 rev/gal. The drilling fluid was a saltwater brine at 9.6 ppg. The
first survey beyond the shoe showed an inclination of 93.1 degrees, and efforts to level-off the
wellbore were successful with dogleg severity less than 5 degrees per 100 feet. The clean
gamma marker below the Upper Bakken Shale was thin and inconsistent, so the top of the ten-
foot-TVD Target Interval was placed at a high-gamma signature 17 feet below the Shale.
Penetration rates of 450 feet per hour were not uncommon, and over 3000 feet was drilled in the
first 24 hours. A trip was made for motor failure at 14611 feet, and a Baker motor and bit were
run in the hole. Initial formation dip ranged from 89.8 to 90 degrees, steepening to 89.6 degrees
from 15800 to 17000 feet. A hard streak at 17300 feet caused a bit deflection from 89.3° to 93.2
degrees, which nearly resulted in a shale strike before wellbore inclination turned around at
17950 feet. A trip was made at 20504 feet for another motor failure, and 3901 units of trip gas
was circulated out through the gas buster with a four to eight foot flare. Total depth of 20992
feet was reached at 06:10 on March 25, 2015. Bottom hole location was 246' FSL and 1999'
FEL, Sec 35, T152N, R99W. Vertical section was 10013 feet, TVD was 11194 feet, and subsea
depth was ­8940 feet.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
