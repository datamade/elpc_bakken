INTRODUCTION
The Hess Bakken Investments II, LLC GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 (SE SE Section 31,
T158N, R97W), is located roughly 12 miles south of Wildrose, North Dakota in Williams
County. GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 is a double-section horizontal Middle Bakken well located
on a four well pad; it consists of one 9,789'-long lateral drilled toward the north (359.42°).
Drilling of the vertical and curve portions of the well were completed by H&P #309. H&P #293
was moved onto the pad to complete the lateral portion of the wells on this pad. During drilling
of the lateral portion of GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 directional drilling and geo-steering
techniques were employed to maintain maximum possible exposure to the target reservoir.
ENGINEERING
Lateral Operations
Lateral operations for GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 began on December 22, 2104. Drilling
began at a measured depth of 10,650' and was drilled with two bottom hole assemblies totaling
83.4 drilling hours. Salt water drilling fluid fluctuated in density between 9.8-9.9 ppg throughout
the lateral. The lateral was completed at a total depth (TD) of 19,820' MD on December 27,
2014 with a closure azimuth of approximately 359.42° and a closure distance of approximately
9,789'. The bottom-hole location (BHL) is estimated to be 9,788.44' north and 98.30' west of the
surface location or approximately 312.27' FNL and 538.30' FEL, NE NE Sec. 30, T158N,
R97W in Williams County, North Dakota.

Oil and Gas Show
Total gas was recorded as units (100 units = 1% methane) and chromatography of C1 ­ C4
components were recorded in parts-per-million. Gas shows were encountered throughout the
entire lateral. The largest gas show encountered measured 1,971 units, and was dominantly
methane (C1). This large gas show occurred when mud weight was 9.85 ppg at 19,057' MD, and
was seen as the wellbore traversed up to the base of the ideal geologic window. Background gas
varied throughout the lateral, ranging from roughly 75 units to 450 units in some portions of the
lateral. In general, the highest gas shows were observed when the wellbore was at the base of the
ideal geologic window. Gas shows also increased when the wellbore was less than 10' below the
base of the ideal geologic window, as seen in Figure 2. There was no flare or live oil observed
during the drilling of the GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 lateral.

Geo-steering
Baker Hughes provided equipment and personnel for MWD and directional services for the
lateral part of the well. Directional drillers, MWD field engineers, Sunburst Consulting, Inc.
well-site geologists and the Hess operations geologist collaborated throughout the project to
make decisions regarding steering and exploration of formations for production of hydrocarbons.
The 10' thick target zone within the Middle Bakken was established by the Hess operations
geologist based on core analysis, electric logs from offsetting wells, and seismic data of the area.
The upper 5'-6' of the targeted pay zone was characterized by cooler gamma signatures (40-70
API) that are associated with the presence of a limestone layer. The center of the targeted
geologic window was characterized by a warm gamma spike (95-105 API). The bottom half of
the targeted pay zone was characterized by warmer, more ambiguous gamma signatures (70-90
API). Overall gross apparent dip is calculated at 0.95° up (Figure 3). GN-Halseth-158-97-
3130H-1 reached TD (19,820' MD) while staying within the ideal geologic window for roughly
12% of the well due to misinterpretation of gamma ray signatures. GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1
remained in the drilling target window provided by the Hess operations geologist for
approximately 77% of the well.

SUMMARY
1)
The Hess GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 lateral was successfully drilled as a Middle
Bakken lateral at New Home field in 5 drilling days, reaching a TD of 19,820' on
December 27, 2014.
2)
Samples from this Middle Bakken lateral consisted mostly of very fine-grained silty
sandstone, varying in color from off white to light-medium gray to light brown, with
dolomite and calcite cement and traces of disseminated pyrite and trace to occasional oil
staining.
3)
The highest gas shows were observed in a 5' zone that included the bottom 2' of the
targeted geologic window and roughly 3' below the geologic window. Background gas
values varied throughout the lateral ranging from 30-250 units. No flare was observed
while drilling GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1.
4)
Overall apparent dip of GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-1 was calculated at approximately
0.95° up. The steering team successfully opened ~9,789' of wellbore to the potentially
productive Middle Bakken reservoir rock while staying within the ideal geologic window
for 12% of the lateral.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
