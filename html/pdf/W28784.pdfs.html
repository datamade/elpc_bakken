2. Well Summary
The Hammerhead 41-26TFH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Three Forks in
McKenzie County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled north to south, crossing from the SHL at
240' FNL & 660' FEL of section 26, T153N, R97W, into section 35, T153N, R97W. The well followed
an approximate azimuth of 179.43 degrees until reaching total depth of 20516'MD. BHL is located at 234'
FSL & 587' FEL of section 35, T153N, R97W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately a 28' thick
zone located 14' below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. The overall dip rate of the lateral was
approximately -0.24' per 100' or 89.86 degree dip. The well experienced several unexpected dip changes
beginning at ~12200'MD where an up dip (90.1)was encountered, followed by a rapid change to a down
dip at ~13000'MD (89.65). A second up dip was observed from ~15000' to ~17300' (90.12-90.0) this
preceded a sudden down dip until TD (89.85-89.80).

5. Lithology
Samples: See mud log and horizontal logs for full sample descriptions. All samples were taken at
30' lagged intervals from the shakers, cleaned, labeled, and bagged for shipment to the state of North
Dakota. The samples collected with the best quality were within the first 3000' of the lateral. As the
lateral continued samples became very fine and were occasionally contaminated by mud additives (after
17000'MD) which caused them to be less distinguishable. Additives may have contributed to improved
fluorescence and cut at certain points in the well.

argillaceous, even faint white/yellow fluorescence, slow fair diffused cut with thin streams (cuts and

5.2
Target Zone: Three Forks (Approximately 14'-28' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken
Shale)
Near the top of target zone samples were light brown, tan and cream, some near white. Large amounts
of pale green shale were seen in the upper portion and near the bottom, although lesser than observed in
other Three Forks wells. Near center they samples were sandier and poorly sorted with layers of
sandstone within the samples. A heavy pyrite streak lies 1-2' above center but may not have been
present as it was only witnessed in our initial drilling in that area. (Also this well contained less of the
green shale than was common in Three forks wells to the south.) 4-6' below center the samples began
to turn tan to light brown and white. This represented the best drilling in the lateral. Shales were also
present as very light green to pale grey/blue. Sample quality overall was mostly poor and became
indistinguishable after 20000'MD.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
