2. Well Summary
The Ora 32-24MBH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Middle Bakken Formation
in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled North to South, crossing from the SHL at
2317' FNL & 2345' FEL of section 24, T148N, R98W, through section 25, T148N, R98W, and into
section 36, T148N, R98W and followed an approximate azimuth of 187.19 degrees until reaching total
depth BHL at 212' FSL & 3612' FWL of section 36, T148N, R98W. The drilling target pay zone was
approximately a 20 feet thick zone (~9' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale). The overall dip rate
of this lateral was approximately 0' per 100' or 90 degree dip.

3. Vertical & Build Curve Well Section

hole and began wireline logging, paying particular attention to the Tyler Sand interval; from this point
the curve/build section was initiated. The build section was initiated with a planned curve of 9

(see chart below) and the landing was adjusted by 30'. Landing was made at 12218' MD, 11267.21'

4. Gas and Oil Shows
4.1. Uphole and Build Shows Table:
The first noteworthy gas show appeared in the Lodgepole. Upon tripping for a new BHA while
in the curve, we observed a trip gas of 898 units. The gases in the Lodgepole averaged 70-150 unit range
with connections up to 775 units, until seeing a formation gas of 704 units in the Lower Lodgepole,
followed by 840 unit formation gas in the Upper Bakken Shale. The gases again settled into the 500-700
unit ranges into the Middle Bakken. We TD'd the curve at 12218' MD, and conducted a short trip and
upon returning to the bottom, the background gas climbed up to 840 units.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
