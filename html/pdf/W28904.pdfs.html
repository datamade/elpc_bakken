INTRODUCTION
Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 3T [Section 33, T156N R101W]
is located approximately 8 miles north of Williston in Williams County, North Dakota. The
Harbour 5601 42-33 3T is a horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin consisting of
one ~9,419 long lateral drilled towards the south (358.15º). This well is an expansion of the
Tyrone Field. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,120' measured depth (MD). Plans
called for a 12º/100' build rate in the curve to ensure landing within the Three Forks sucrosic
dolomite porosity. To isolate the targeted reservoir, intermediate casing was set after drilling to
11,005' MD. After intermediate casing had been set, directional drilling and geo-steering
techniques were employed to maintain maximum exposure to the target reservoir.
OFFSET WELLS
Similar offset wells were used as primary controls while drilling the Harbour 5601 42-33 3T.
The offsets utilized were the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 2B [SESW Section 33,
T156N, R101W], located 33 feet west of the subject well, the Andre 5601 42-33 2B [SESW
Section 33, T156N, R101W], located 99 feet east of the subject well, and the Oasis Petroleum,
Holmes 5501 12-5 5T [Lot 3 Section 5, T155N-R101W], located 1.03 miles west of the subject
well (Figure 2). The above wells were selected based on their proximity to the subject well. Data
including rate of penetration (ROP), lagged samples and measurement-while-drilling (MWD)
gamma ray data were used to determine marker and formation tops during the vertical hole.
MWD gamma ray data was also utilized during the curve build and lateral sections. During both
the vertical and curve-build sections, tops were compared to primary offset wells to ensure
successful landing within the target zone. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing target was
calculated by assuming comparable formation thicknesses to the offset wells.
Target was
adjusted as necessary until a successful landing was achieved.

ENGINEERING
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 3T was reentered on September
24, 2014 by Nabors #110. Drilling progressed from that point until kick-off point (KOP), at
10,120' MD. AT 10,573' MD while drilling the curve the ROP slowed significantly enough to
warrant a trip for the mud motor. Drilling proceeded from this point until curve landing was
reached. The curve was landed with a projected inclination of 90.0°. The original wellbore
successfully penetrated the Three Forks at 10,850' MD (10,632' TVD, -8,437' MSL). Diesel
invert drilling fluid used during both the vertical and curve ranged in value from 9.5+-10.5+
parts per gallon (ppg). The 7" intermediate casing consisted of 90 joints of 32# P-110 and 161
joints of 29# P-110 set to 11,005' MD. Casing operations were performed by DC Powertongs.
Cementing operations were performed by Schlumberger. After intermediate casing procedures
were completed, 4" drill pipe was picked up, diesel invert drilling fluid was replaced with salt
water drilling fluid. TD was reached at 05:20 CDT on November 14, 2014 at a depth of 20,430'
MD (10,549.94' TVD) with an inclination of 91.30º, closure angle of 358.15º and closure
distance of 10,025.23'. The bottom hole location (BHL) is estimated to be 10,019.98' N &
324.44' W of surface location or approximately 254.18 FNL & 1,877.56' FWL, NE NW Sec. 28,
T156N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Two wellsite geologists Sunburst Consulting Inc. employees conducted the geologic analysis for
Harbour 5601 42-33 3T. Sunburst installed and monitored a digital gas detector and
chromatograph interfaced with a Rig Watch Electronic Data Recording System (EDR). The EDR
provided depth, drilling rate and pump stroke data to the M-Controller gas-monitoring software
which returned gas data in the form of total gas units and hydrocarbon constituent (methane,
ethane, propane, butane) concentrations expressed in parts-per-million. Gas samples were pulled
through ¼" polyflow tubing after agitation in Sunburst's gas trap installed in the possum belly.
Rig crews caught lagged samples under the supervision of wellsite geologists in 30' intervals
from 8,120' MD to 20,430' MD. Wet and dry cuttings were examined under a binocular
microscope. Cuttings were also evaluated for hydrocarbon "cut" by immersion in Carbo-Sol
(trichloroethylene) and inspected under a UV fluoroscope. One set of dry cuttings was collected
and sent to the North Dakota Geological Survey and Core Library. Complete lithologic
descriptions are provided in a lithology document as an appendix in the full report.
Lithology
Sample analysis began at 8,120' MD in interbedded sandstone, shale and siltstone lithologies
with a persistent limestone bed located in the roughly the middle of the unit. One prospective pay
zone was recorded while drilling the subject well, the Mission Canyon.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississipian-Madison Group) is typically comprised of light to
medium brown fossiliferous limestone and dolomite.
The initial contact with the Mission
Canyon was at 9,294' MD 9,293' TVD (-7,098') MSL, 1' high to the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour
5601 42-33 2B offset. Traces of algal laminations were recorded. Samples exhibited pinpoint
porosity.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,670' MD, 10,566' TVD (-8,371') MSL, 2' low to
the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 2B offset. Representative samples of the Middle
Bakken consist of fine grained light gray brown calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular
porosity and trace brown oil stain. Good hydrocarbon shows and heavy gases (C1 ­C3) were
recorded through this interval.
The Three Forks Formation (Devonian) top was encountered at 10,850' MD 10,632' TVD,
(-8,437') MSL, 1' low to the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 2B. The formation is
comprised of interbedded dolomites, shales and claystones. Representative samples consist of
light pink to tan dolomite and pale green to blue green shale with intercrystalline porosity and
traces of light brown spotty oil stain. Hydrocarbons show and heavy gases (C1 ­C4) were
recorded through this interval.

Hydrocarbon Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 ­C4) are expressed in parts-per million (ppm) concentrations.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was utilized in the vertical and curve. Salt water drilling fluid was
employed during the lateral. Background gases recorded throughout the vertical were ~10-20
units, reaching a peak value of 35 units while drilling in the Upper Mission Canyon. While
drilling the curve background and peak gas shows began to increase when drilling in the Upper
Bakken Shale down to the Lower Bakken Shale, reaching a peak value of 1,181 units when
drilling in the Upper Bakken Shale. While drilling the curve, peak values ranged from 10 units
in the Lodgepole to 1,181units in the Upper Bakken Shale. Extreme cold temperatures caused
many issues with the poly flow gas lines and gas trap components freezing up throughout the
lateral. After running new lines and establishing a regiment of regular trap defrosting, gas flow
was restored to expected levels. Gas shows reached a peak value of 1,949 units at 15,891' MD.
Gas shows were of the highest values while drilling above the upper half of the target zone. It
was decided to circulate through the gas buster at approximately 16,950' MD, and an intermittent
3' flare was observed throughout the remainder of the lateral. Numerous gas shows above 1,500
units were observed throughout the second half of the lateral. Oil shows were described as trace
brown spotty oil stain throughout the target zone along the length of the lateral. When immersed
in Carbo-Sol sample cuts typically displayed moderate diffuse-streaming blue white
fluorescence.
Geo-steering
RPM Consulting provided directional drilling services and well site supervision. Gyrodata was
contracted for MWD and directional services. Oasis geologist in Houston, directional drillers and
Sunburst geologists worked closely throughout the well to make steering decisions to maximize
the amount of borehole exposure to the pay zone. The Three Forks member of the Harbour 5601
42-33 3T is estimated at ~13' thick. The top of the 5' target interval chosen for this well is
located 7' above the Claystone and was logged initially at 10,638' TVD. This target interval was
also chosen to minimize chances of contacting the Pronghorn siltstone member. Numerous
interbedded shale layers provided helpful geosteering markers throughout the remainder of the
lateral, but a strategy of contacting the Claystone every few thousand feet was employed to
ensure position in zone, as the interbedded shale layers were observed to be intermittent at times.
Stratigraphic location within the target zone was based on gamma ray values, sample
observations, gas shows, and drill rates. API gamma counts throughout target ranged from the
mid 20's to the mid 110's. The overall formation dip of 0.51° up resulted in a structural position
at TD that was ~87' higher in TVD than initial contact with the target zone.
SUMMARY
1) The Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 3T is a continuation
of exploratory efforts in Williams County, North Dakota targeting the Three Forks
Formation.
2) The well was reentered on 24 September, 2014. The vertical hole was drilled to a kick-
off point of 10,120' on 27 September, 2014. Intermediate casing was set at 11,005' MD
in preparation for lateral operations. Lateral drilling operations concluded on 14
November, 2014.
3) The lateral was drilled in 119.5 hours with one 6" PDC bit.
4) A diesel invert mud program used through the vertical and curve ranged from (9.5+-
10.5+ ppg). The salt water drilling fluid ranged from (9.3 - 9.45 ppg), to maintain
hydrostatic balance.
5) Samples from the Three Forks target porosity zone consisted of interbedded light pink to
tan dolomite and pale green to blue green shale with intercrystalline porosity and a trace,
light-brown oil stain.
6) Hydrocarbon shows in the Three Forks were trace. Gas shows reached a peak value of
1,949 units at 15,891' MD.
7) Geo-steering was accomplished via sample evaluation and gamma markers. Dip
projections were successfully utilized to maintain contact between the well bore and the
targeted porosity zone, with 78% exposure to the Three Forks.
8) The Harbour 5601 42-33 3T awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate
production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
