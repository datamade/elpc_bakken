GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
The objective of the Miles 4-6H1 oil well borehole was to drill a single, South-bearing horizontal
lateral, across the first bench of the Three Forks Formation. This is the second of four wells to
be drilled on this pad. The well's surface location is 355' from the North line (FNL) & 1135'
from the West line (FWL), Section 6, T 150 N, R 96 W, McKenzie County, North Dakota. The
engineering oil-well plan sets the seven-inch intermediate casing at 11,243' MD, 915' FNL &
1292' FWL, Section 6, T 150 N, R 96 W. The location at total depth (TD) is 230' FSL & 1295'
FWL, Section 7, T150N, R96W.
Vertical and Build Sections
The surface hole was pre-drilled and cased to 2075' MD prior to the rig move. At 18:00 on
October 17, 2014, Cyclone Rig #34 drilled out from the 9 5/8-inch surface casing, float and shoe
at 2075 feet measure depth (MD). The vertical and curve portions of the wellbore were drilled
with diesel-based, oil invert mud (OBM). The operations company-men, Derek Tangsrud and
Keith Sproales provided supervision across the oil well drilling operations.
NorAm Wellsite personnel were previously rigged up for the Miles 3-6H well and started
monitoring total gas from out of surface casing on October 17, 2014. After a bit trip at 8352'
MD, the mud logging operations commenced at 8430' MD on October 20, 2014 just above the
top of the Charles Formation. The mud logging software utilized the vertical strip log and
horizontal log versions from WellSight Systems, Incorporated. The borehole's geologic
information came from; the time lagged drill cuttings, time lagged total gas unit values data, rate
of drilling penetration data and real time down hole API gamma ray data. The drilling rate of
penetration (ROP) data was imported from the Pason monitoring system (EDR) through
NorAm's MiniPac 2300 total gas detector and the Varian 614 Chromates, gas detection system.
The total gas unit values were determined from NorAm's MiniPac 2300 hotwire total gas
detector, which is calibrated at the standard of 100 units equals 1% methane in atm.
As per the North Dakota Oil Commissions requirements, the mud loggers caught the borehole's
30-foot sample drill cuttings intervals for geology samples. The geology samples were then
cleaned, dried and inventoried in order for library inspection.
Mudlogging and sample collection began at 8430' MD on October 20, 2014 at the top of the
Charles Formation. The top of the Charles was picked at 8498' MD, 8496' TVD (14' high to the
prognosis). The Base of the Last Charles Salt came in at 9149' MD, 9147' TVD (23' high to the
prognosis). The Mission Canyon came in at 9362' MD, 9360' TVD (20' high to the prognosis).
The Lodgepole came in at 9939' MD, 9937' TVD (31' high to the prognosis). The vertical
section of the borehole was completed when the Kick-Off-Point (KOP) was reached at 10,430'
MD at 20:30 on October 21, 2014.
Kick Off Point for the build section commenced at 10,430' MD using a 8 ¾ inch bit with a 5.0
stage mud motor with a 1.5 degree bend. The well plan was designed to require motor yields and
dogleg of 13 degrees per 100 feet. Based on the MS Guidance Services design plan, from the
KOP at 10,430' MD to the landing point at 11,243' MD, the bit was aimed to 176 azimuth to
avoid other proposed wells on the same pad. Drilling proceeded effectively without event.

The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,828' MD, 10,772' TVD. Thickness to target was

at 10,864' MD, 10,793' TVD. The Lower Bakken Shale came in at 10,950' MD, 10,833' TVD
and the Three Forks top was drilled at 11,039' MD, 10,864' TVD. Crews tripped for a new bit at
11,052' due to slow penetration rate then resumed drilling the curve on October 23, 2014. The
Three Forks target top was confirmed by gamma at 11,179' MD, 10,889' TVD.
The build section was reached at a total depth of 11,243' MD, 10,893' TVD at 16:50 October
23rd approximately 4 feet vertically into the Three Forks dolomite. The 7" intermediate casing
was set at 11,227' MD. The vertical to horizontal wellbore landing TD, was completed at an
inclination of 90.5 degrees, inside Continentals' predetermined target zone stratum. The location
of the 7 inch casing point was 915' FNL & 1292' FWL, Section 6, T 150 N, R 96 W.
Crews landed the curve at 11,243' MD at 16:50 on October 23, 2014. After a short trip, the crew
tripped out of the hole laying down 5" drill pipe. Crews set intermediate casing to 11,227' MD at
18:00 on October 24th then circulated waiting on cementers. Cementers mistakenly pumped the
tail cement first and the cement had to be circulated out of the hole. Cementing was completed
at 09:00 on the 25th. Crews then prepared the rig for the drilling of the lateral portion of the well.
Lateral Horizontal Section
Drilling ahead commenced on the South-bearing horizontal borehole on October 26, 2014 at
00:20 using a 6 inch SEC SK711M bit. After drilling out from the float and casing shoe, the
target zone's Formation gas, monitored as background and connection total gas unit values,
remained up averaging 1500 units while venting through the gas buster. Gas readings increased
at approximately 16,000' MD from apparent Formation gas with frequent increases over 3000
units through the gas buster. Formation gas remained up for the remainder of drilling with some
increases to 10,000 units.
Miles 4-6H1's, predetermined drill target zone was estimated at 12 feet thick. During drilling
the target appeared to be only 10' thick with the internal 1 shale directly beneath the base of the
target zone. The top of the target zone is approximately 25 feet into the Three Forks Formation.
The Formation target zone stratigraphic inclination or apparent dip is determined from the MWD
Api gamma markers that were repeat-ably intercepted along the course length of the horizontal
lateral.
Drilling proceeded to 15,750' MD with the Formation remaining relatively flat. From
approximately 15,750' to 17500' the Formation dip changed from 89.98 degrees to
approximately 90.2 degrees. The Formation came up from 17,600' to 19,300' at 90.6 degrees.
The bit proceeded to climb in Formation from 18,400' to 18,743'and slides were ineffective to
halt the ascent. At 18,743' the bit was approximately 5' TVD above the top of the target zone
heading up at 92.4 inclination with Formation dip of 90.6 degrees.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
