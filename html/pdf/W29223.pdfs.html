Geologic Summary
Overview:
P Jackman 156-100-2-18-19-15H3 was spud on November 3, 2014 at
surface location 666' FNL and 1537' FEL, in Section 18, T156N, R100W, Williams
County, North Dakota. The well was designed to have a single lateral starting in
section 18 and moving into, and finishing in section 19. The southern leg was drilled
to a bottom hole location of 251.06' FSL and 1414.72' FEL in Section 19, T156N,
R100W.
The design of this well was to core 180' of formation, then drill one lateral
from one curve. During drilling operation we encountered one point in which we had
to sidetrack, in the horizontal section. The Horizontal drilling for the south leg began
on January 23, 2014 and finished when we approached the south hard line in
Section 19 at 19739' MD, on January 27, 2015.
Vertical core, and Build:
Mud logging began near the Charles Salt formation at 8,500' MD. We had
no significant gas kicks or oil shows through the Mission Canyon (9014' MD) and
Lodgepole (9644' MD) Formations. At a depth of 10209' MD we reached our
core point. Once at the core point we cored to 10484' MD, a total of 275'. We
recovered 278.9' of core from our drilling. After finishing our coring we drilled a rat
hole for wireline logs that ended at 10707' MD in the Nisku formation. After the
coring process was finished we set a cement plug and began the build section for
the well.
Due to the fluxuation in isopach thicknesses, our first marker top is the False
Bakken (10306' MD, 1010251.47' TVD) with the second being the Scallion,
(10310' MD, 10254.1' TVD). Using these two markers, the top of the Bakken
4
Shale (10321' MD, 10261.33' TVD) and the top of the Middle Bakken (10346' MD,
10276.91' TVD) we drilled through the Lower Bakken shale (10431' MD, 10321.4'
TVD) to our target formation, the Three Forks (10482' MD, 10341.28' TVD). We
landed the well at 88.6 degrees approximately 14' into the Three Forks formation.
Southern Leg:
On January 23, 2015, we drilled out of 7" production casing shoe to begin
the lateral. This lateral was drilled to the south with a TD azimuth of 179.4°. This
was the point of our first sidetrack, due to a shale strike at 15984' MD, 10379.07'
TVD. The open hole sidetrack was begun at 15705' MD to TD. Upon completion
the lateral extended to 19739' MD and ended as it neared the South hard line of
Section 19, T156N, R100W. A total of 9084' horizontal hole was drilled in the
Three Forks.
Once the lateral was started, we drilled ahead throughout the target zone
starting at the bottom and making our way up to the upper and middle of the target
zone. Once to our target TVD we used gamma signatures to drill in and use as
way points, with only momentarily drilling in the TF3. Within our target zone our gas
shows averaged approximately 368 units running throughout.
The Three Forks went down approximately 59' over the course of the
southern lateral. Dip averaged approximately .38 down dip.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
