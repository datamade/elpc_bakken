GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
XTO Energy, Inc.'s Eide 31X-29D (250' FNL; 2419' FEL NWNE Sec 29, T158N, R95W, Williams Co.,
ND) is located approximately 6 miles northwest of Tioga, in Williams Co. ND. The well is east of the
Nesson anticline and part of a 4 well pad program designed to develop oil production in a unit
composed of parts of Sections 29 and 32. The Eide 31X-29D well was drilled to 1,913' and 9-
5/8"surface casing set to a depth of 1,908' MD, then drilled to 9940' and 7" intermediate casing was set
to a depth of 9,914'. The lateral was then drilled to 20,234' and a production run. The well is to be a
Middle Bakken lateral. The intent was to remain in the 16' thick Target Zone of the Middle Bakken,
stratigraphically situated from 21' to 37' beneath the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. Through
geosteering and directional drilling techniques the wellbore was drilled to maximize its extent in the
target zone to the Total Depth of 20,234'.
EQUIPMENT AND SAMPLING
Gas detection and wellsite geological supervision commenced at 6,500', above the Tyler Formation.
Geologic analysis for Eide 31X-29D was provided by a Decollement Consulting geology team.
Information was networked through a Pason electronic data recorder system. This equipment provided
depth, ROPs, pump strokes, total gas units, and other drilling parameters to monitors across the drilling
location and internet.
Sample collection began above the Tyler Formation and continued with 30' interval samples until total
depth was reached. Rock cuttings were analyzed in wet and dry conditions under a binocular
microscope and also with a fluoroscope to analyze fluorescence and solvent cuts. Detailed lithologic
descriptions are included in the lithology portion to this report. Sample quality was good for most of the
well. Cuttings will be sent to the North Dakota Geologic Survey and to XTO Energy, Inc.
OFFSET DATA
The primary offset wells used for structural correlation during curve operations were the XTO Energy,
Inc. Eide 31X-29G, and the Petro-Hunt Pederson AI 1 wells. These wells were useful in the vertical
hole and during curve drilling operations while landing the well. The TVD log of the Middle Bakken from
the Eide 31-29G well was used as the main reference during the horizontal geosteering of the well.
DRILLING THE VERTICAL AND CURVE HOLE SECTIONS
The Eide 31X-29D spudded October 12, 2014 and reached a surface casing depth of 1913' and was
cased and cemented to 1908'. The intermediate and curve hole section was then drilled to 9940' and 7"
casing was run to 9914' and cemented in preparation for the drilling of the lateral in the Middle Bakken.
Gas detection and wellsite geological supervision commenced at 6,500', several hundred feet above
the Tyler Formation. Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in part-per-million
concentrations. Sampled gas was pulled from Pason's agitator in the possum belly and analyzed by
Pason's Gas Analyzer to deliver the compositional data.
A successful Middle Bakken landing was accomplished by comparing gamma signatures from the
offsets to the MWD TVD gamma ray log produced during the drilling of the Eide 31X-29G. The landing
target true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to ensure accurate landing of the curve within
the Middle Bakken as near the target window as possible.
5
Logging began in the Minnekahta Formation, a soft, medium red siltstone with some shale stringers.
At MD 6,780' (TVD 6,777') the Tyler Formation came in, starting as interbedded sandstone, siltstones
and shales and ending as a series of interbedded limestones and shales. From MD 6,970' to 7,050'
three black, carboniferous, non to slightly calcareous shales were present, but no show was
encountered. The Kibbey Lime was encountered at MD 7,387' (TVD 7,384') and was a hard, very light
grey limestone (mudstone) and then the lithology turned into a medium reddish brown siltstone.
The Charles Formation was drilled into at MD 7,546' (TVD 7,543') and was a series of salts,
anhydrites, limestones and shales with no shows present. The Base of the Last Salt of the Charles
Formation was encountered at a depth of 8,134' MD (TVD 8,131'). At MD 8,178' (TVD 8,175') the
Ratcliffe was entered, and was a light to medium grey algal limestone (mudstone) was drilled. Some
stringers of anhydrite were also noted. The Mission Canyon formation (a gray dense to finely
crystalline mudstone) was then drilled into at MD 8,335' (TVD 8,331').
The Lodgepole formation came in at 8,946' MD (8,942 TVD). Samples showed a medium to dark grey
mudstone. Drilling then continued through the Lodgepole limestone to the KOP at 9,169' MD. The BHA
was changed out to a curve building assembly, the BHA was then tripped in and the curve was drilled
to 9,940' MD. Background gas varied from 45 units to 116 units during the curve. C1 through C4 gases
were also present in proportionate quantities. Sliding continued through the Lodgepole formation to the
top of the Upper Bakken Shale, which gamma showed coming in at MD 9,798' (TVD 9,565'), a 13'
zone of black carbonaceous shale with some pyrite. The highest gas reading recorded was 287 units.
The Middle Bakken Dolomite was encountered at MD 9,737' (TVD 9,578'), with gamma and samples
confirming the light gray, very silty dolomite. Some dull yellow fluorescence was observed that had a
good flush cut when exposed to solvent. Total gas units ranged from 81 to 272 units with proportionate
C1 through C4's.
GEOLOGIC INTERPRETATION OF THE MIDDLE BAKKEN TARGET SECTION
For geosteering and evaluation purposes a TVD log of the well's target zone was created from the
MWD data recorded during the drilling of the Eide 31X-29G and then used as a reference during the
drilling of the lateral by the wellsite team. See figure 1.
Figure 1. TVD gamma log of the Eide 31X-29G well.

During the lateral there were some continuing similarities in the samples and gamma readings as the
wellbore traversed the stratigraphic section of the target zone. The drilled cuttings observed were
mostly composed of calcareous, very silty and sandy dolomites ranging from mudstone to wackestone
in the Dunham classification. The colors were mostly light grey and very light brown. The cuttings
ranged from soft to firm throughout the target section and were clean to very slightly argillaceous. Minor
constituents were mostly of anhydrite and pyrite seen mainly as the well path went through the upper
portion of the target zone. The most distinctive changes when drilling through the section were noticed
in the color of the uppermost portion of the target zone when drilling the low gamma clean dolomitic
limestone known locally as the "packstone". It was off white to a very light brown and contained
significant amounts of pyrite in some samples.
The hydrocarbon shows seen during the lateral consisted of a barely perceptible, very dull yellow
fluorescence that varied from 10 to 70 percent of the cuttings, with the solvent cuts ranging from fair to
good flush cuts.
DRILLING AND GEOSTEERING OF THE MIDDLE BAKKEN LATERAL
The geosteering of the lateral was done by the XTO Operations Geologist with input from the wellsite
geologic team, with a goal of maximizing the wellbore contact with the best reservoir rock available
while minimizing dogleg severity and drilling as fast as conditions permitted.
