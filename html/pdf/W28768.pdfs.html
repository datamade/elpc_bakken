INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 8T [Lot 3, Section 3,
T153N, R93W] is located approximately 13 miles north of the town of New Town in
Mountrail County, ND. It is one of three wells on the Langved 5393 12-3 pad. The Langved
5393 12-3 8T is a horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin consisting of one
9,599' lateral drilled toward the south. Geologically, the Williston Basin is an intracratonic,
structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the Canadian Shield. Within
the basin, the Three Forks Formation (Late Devonian) deposited dolomitic mudstone
interbedded with light green shale and claystone. Advances in drilling techniques and well
stimulation have made multiple zones viable as an oil and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found `Structure'
section appended to this report. Offset well control is essential in curve building operations,
in order to successfully land within a targeted zone of the Three Forks Formation. Formation
thicknesses expressed by gamma ray signatures in these wells were compared to gamma data
of offset wells in order to successfully land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth
(TVD) was periodically updated to ensure accurate landing of the curve. The offset wells
used for the Langved 5393 12-3 8T were Langved 5393 11-3H, Langved 5393 12-3 6T3,
Flavin 5393 14-3T.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 12-3 8T was reentered on October 21, 2014 by Xtreme 21. Drilling fluid
was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. One trip
was made during the vertical at 8,195' due to low ROP. The curve was landed at 11,075' MD
(10,718' TVD) with an inclination of 89.44°. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing
was set and cemented to 11,061' MD. To start the lateral, a 6 Varel PDC bit, MWD tool, and
a 1.5° fixed mud motor were used. A trip was required at 19,535' for a motor failure and the
bit was replaced with another Varel PDC bit. The BHA accomplishments are tabulated in an
appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached total depth (TD) at 20,660' MD at
0001 hrs CDT on November 6, 2014, generating an exposure of 9,599 linear feet of 6 hole
through the Three Forks First Bench target interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies
10,061.81' south & 526.04' east of surface location or approximately 232.32' FSL and
2,437.60' FEL, SE SW Sec. 24, T153N, R100W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 7,980' MD where the observed lithology consists of primarily shale
with thin interbedded sandstone units. The main zone of interest drilled at the subject well
was the Three Forks First Bench.
The Mission Canyon Formation was drilled at 9,127' TVD (-6,869') and was a prospective
area for hydrocarbon shows. Samples consisted of light to medium gray brown limestone,
and trace dead oil stain.
The Three Forks First Bench member of the Three Forks Formation (Late Devonian) was
entered at 10,669' TVD (-8,411'), 3' high to the Langved 5393 12-3 6T3. Representative
samples consist of a tan to cream dolomitic mudstone with interbedded light green shale and
intercrystalline porosity and a trace light-brown oil stain. Samples may exhibit a faint, pale-
yellow, good, streaming to diffuse cut fluorescence. Strong hydrocarbon show and heavy
gases (C1-C4) were observed in this interval.
Figure 2. Cuttings of tan dolomite and light green shale from the Three Forks First Bench.
Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 15-6,236 units
above a background of ~32 units against an average mud weight of 10.1lb/gal diesel-invert.
Methane and Ethane (C1-C2) were observed throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than C2
were not observed. After kicking off the curve, shows were similar with respect to the
vertical. Shows remained low from kick-off through entry of the False Bakken interval of the
lower Lodgepole. Gas shows were consistently high from this interval through curve landing
in the targeted Three Forks pay zone.
Shows from the False Bakken through curve landing ranged from 300-5,000 units over a
background of 400 units against a 10.2 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight.
The lateral began with gas shows ranging from 150-1000 units with an average background
of 200-300 units. At ~ 12,300' MD shows increased greatly and ranged from 2,016-3,513
units over a background of 1,400 units against a 10 lb/gal salt-water mud weight.
Background gas continued to rise for the remainder of the lateral, which shows of over 6,000
units. A 1-3' flare was observed while drilling the lateral. Trace amounts of light brown free
oil were observed over the shakers.
GEOSTEERING
The ideal target zone of the Langved 5393 12-3 8T was identified by evaluating gamma ray
data from the nearby Langved 5393 6T3 (Figure 3). For geosteering purposes, the ideal target
is set from the top of the Claystone 1 marker to 15' above the Claystone 1 Marker.
Figure 3. Langved 5393 12-3 8T TVD gamma ray log. Ideal target zone highlighted in blue.
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 10,701' TVD at 89.8°. The Base of Last Salt
top indicated that the target was within a few feet of prognosis. No changes were made for a
change in target. The curve was landed at a TVD of 10,718' at 89.3°, about 48' TVD below
the top of the Three Forks, 14' below the ideal target zone.
The most consistent marker used to steer was the Claystone 1 marker, a high-gamma marker
(~100 API) at the bottom of the zone which is easily identified in samples. Many of the
gamma markers in the upper Three Forks are known to be unreliable and often lead to
miscorrelations. Steering decisions are based off both samples and occasionally tapping the
Claystone 1 marker near the bottom of the zone.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 8T was drilled by Xtreme
21 from reentry to TD in 16 days. The Langved reached a total depth of 20,660' MD on
November 6, 2014. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination
obtained during the Langved 5393 12-3 8T indicate a hydrocarbon rich system in the Three
Forks First Bench Target. Significant shows were recorded throughout the lateral. Samples
from the target zone consist of cream to tan, dolomite and pale green shale with
intercrystalline porosity and a trace, light-brown oil stain that yielded a faint, pale-yellow,
good, streaming to diffuse cut fluorescence. Lateral operations were successful in exposing
9,599' of potentially productive Three Forks First Bench target zone rock to the wellbore.
The well was within the targeted zone for 98% of the 9,599' of lateral.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
