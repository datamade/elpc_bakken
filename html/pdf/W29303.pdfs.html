GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
INTRODUCTION
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Frisco 2-31H1 as a two-section lateral targeting the first
bench of the interbedded silty, argillaceous dolomite and shale of the Upper Devonian Three
Forks formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota within the Grass Bluff Field. This is the
second vertical and curve drilled of a two well pad, and the first lateral to be completed. Below
the Pronghorn Member and conformably overlying the Birdbear Formation, the Three Forks
carbonate sequence was deposited in a shallow epeiric sea paleoenvironment during supra tidal
conditions. The surface hole location is in the Southeast one-quarter of the Southeast one-quarter
of Section 31, Township 152 North, Range 102 West (395' FSL & 898' FEL). The well then
traverses North to a final bottom hole location 230' from the North section line and 1322' from
the East section line (230' FNL & 1322' FEL) of Section 30, Township 152 North, Range 102
West.
PLAN
The vertical portion of the Frisco 2-31H1 was planned to be drilled to a kick-off point of
10591'MD, within the Mississippian Lodgepole Formation. Build rates of 12 degrees per 100'
were required to land the curve with the bit inclined approximately 90 degrees within the
targeted interval, 16' below the top of the Three Forks (6' below the base of the Pronghorn),
according to prognosis. Intermediate casing would then be set and cemented at 11270'MD
(10845'TVD), prior to drilling the lateral portion of the well. The horizontal well would be
drilled to a planned total depth of 20729'MD, within the Northeast one-quarter of the Northeast
one-quarter of Section 30, Township 152 North, Range 102 West (200' FNL & 1320' FEL).
OFFSET WELLS
The primary offset used was the vertical Iverson 1-31H, situated Northeast of the Frisco 2-31H1
surface hole location. The Iverson 1-31H gamma ray type log data in addition to several other
offset wells (Frisco 3-31H, Frisco 1-31H, Miramar 2-31H, Miramar 3-31H1) were used to
facilitate formation thicknesses in the area as well as lithology, estimated zones of porosity, and
an accurate projection of a curve landing target depth throughout the vertical and build sections
of the Frisco 2-31H1. These data, in conjunction with Mission Canyon structural isopachs
(thought to be conformable to the Three Forks Formation), were utilized to predict the local
structure to be encountered during the lateral section of the Frisco 2-31H1 and to aid in
geosteering decisions. The Frisco 1-31H was a north-bearing lateral well drilled parallel to the
Frisco 2-31H1 within the Middle Bakken pool. Its completed geosteering cross section,
interpreted with the use of gamma ray logging and borehole data during the lateral portion of the
Frisco 1-31H, was used as an additional type log for geosteering purposes.

ENGINEERING OPERATIONS
Vertical
Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on December 1, 2014. 36
weight P110 9.625" diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2175' prior to the arrival
of H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8550' MD on December
3, 2014. Vertical operations were completed with 2 8.75" bits at a total depth of 10220'MD on
December 6, 2014. Diesel based drilling mud weighing 9.6-9.9 ppg was used throughout the
vertical section.
Curve Build
The build section began early on the morning of December 6, 2014 at 10220'MD with a fixed
2.38 degree mud motor and 8.75" PDC bit. The curve was drilled to a measured depth of 11320';
a total of 1100' in 15.4 hours. Necessary build rates were achieved and the bit was landed at
10830' TVD, 22' into the targeted first bench of the Upper Devonian Three Forks formation. 32
weight LTC 7" diameter intermediate casing was installed and cemented in place at 11308'
(11308' MD & 10830' TVD) on the morning of December 8, 2014.
Lateral
The active drilling fluid system was switched to saltwater based brine for the lateral portion of
the Frisco 2-31H1, with a mud weight 9.8-10.2 ppg. Lateral drilling resumed along a general 0-
degree North azimuth on December 9, 2014, after drilling out of the 7" intermediate casing shoe
with a 6" Smith PDC bit and a fixed 1.5 degree mud motor at 11308'MD, 10830'TVD. The north
bearing Frisco 2-31H1 lateral was then drilled to a depth of 17039'MD on December 12, 2014
for a total of 5731' drilled in 49.58 circulating hours, at which time the decision was made to
come out of the hole to replace the bottom hole assembly due to an increasingly consistent low
penetration rate (<75'/hour). At this time MWD tools were reprogrammed and a new 6" bit and
1.5 degree fixed bend mud motor were subsequently tripped into the hole. Total depth was
reached on December 15, 2014 with a final projection to bit at 20595'MD, 10843.3'TVD,
concluding the 7 day, 9287'MD Frisco 2-31H1 lateral well. The bottom hole location (BHL) is
approximately 230' from the North line (FNL) and 1322' from the East line (FEL) of Section 30,
Township 152 North, Range 102 West.

GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Methods
NorAm Wellsite Services provided wellsite geologic supervision for the Frisco 2-31H1. A two-
person team of geologists provided geosteering services while logging drill cuttings and
monitoring gas from 8550'MD until the well was drilled to completion and production liner was
set. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and chromatography
data were monitored and recorded using a NorAm Wellsite Services Advanced Gas Detector
(AGD 08), including a Continental Laboratories MP2300 total gas detector and Varian
chromatograph. Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the aid of lagged
total gas data, lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and gamma ray logging
coupled with directional drilling survey data from Phoenix Technology Services. Rock cuttings
were collected by H&P 320 rig crews in 30' intervals throughout the vertical and build sections,
and 100' intervals throughout the lateral, under the supervision of the wellsite geologists. The
cuttings were then examined both wet and dried using a 10x-25x zoom binocular stereo
microscope with 10x oculars. Examination of hydrocarbon cut fluorescence was conducted until
the completion of the lateral well, and was described as a slow, weak, yellow green diffuse cut
for the majority of the lateral, although many sections of the targeted zone showed no
hydrocarbon cut fluorescence. These data, in conjunction with the previously mentioned
methods, were utilized to provide Phoenix directional drillers with optimal TVD targets,
ensuring a wellbore within the 14' targeted Upper Devonian Three Forks drilling interval.

Formation (Big Snowy Group) at 8550'MD. A complete listing of sample descriptions is

The Mission Canyon, also of the Madison Group, was intercepted at a true vertical depth of
9473' (-7233'). This formation records a regressive sequence, and historically has produced more

Conformably overlying the Bakken Formation, the Mississippian Lodgepole was intercepted at a
true vertical depth of 9990' (-7751'). This unit typically consists of argillaceous limestones
deposited in marine to restricted shelf environments. Locally, the Lodgepole exhibits potential
fracture porosity and a general absence of oil staining. Samples are described as:
LIMESTONE: medium to gray to gray brown, trace off white to light gray, trace dark gray,
mottled in part, very firm to hard, brittle in part, microcrystalline, lithographic to slightly earthy,
silty, argillaceous
LIMESTONE: light gray to medium gray, firm to hard, microcrystalline, slightly earthy, silty,
slightly argillaceous, slightly carbonaceous
The Mississippian to Devonian Bakken Formation, although not the Frisco 2-31H1 targeted
interval, has shown to be an excellent source rock for hydrocarbons within the Williston Basin.
This unit is thought to record an offshore marine environment, although marine swamp
paleoenvironment models have also been considered.
The organic-rich Upper Shale member of the Bakken Formation was intercepted at a true vertical
depth of 10763.5' (-8524.5'). This unit is characterized by a black, sub blocky to sub platy,
petroliferous shale. No visible porosity was observed, but samples showed even live oil staining.
Increased gas shows and a high gamma ray signature are also of note.
The Lower Mississippian-Upper Devonian Middle Bakken Member was intercepted at a true
vertical depth of 10776.5' (-8537.5'). Samples were described as:
SILTSTONE: grey to grey brown, slightly firm to friable, argillaceous, calcareous to dolomitic,
poor intergranular porosity
The Devonian Lower Bakken Shale was intercepted at a true vertical depth of 10802.4' (-
8563.4'). Samples in this member are similar to the Upper Bakken Shale Member, with earthy
porosity, poor permeability, and light brown live even oil staining, also accompanied by a high
gamma ray signature and increase in gas shows.

Hydrocarbon Shows
Please see the Total Gas Data section for hydrocarbon shows throughout the vertical and build
section of the Frisco 2-31H1.
Hydrocarbon shows steadily increased throughout the duration of the lateral portion of the Frisco
2-31H1. Out of the 7" intermediate casing shoe, background gas averaged around 75 units and
eventually increased to a maximum background gas of 850 units at the well's total depth. The
largest gas show observed within the targeted interval, 16'-30' into the first bench of the Three
Forks Formation, was 1155 units at 20264'MD (~1' above the top of target zone). Also of note, a
trip gas of 1995 units was observed at 17040'MD, following the trip for bottom hole assembly at
17039'MD.

GEOSTEERING
Directional and MWD equipment and personnel were provided by Phoenix Technology Services
for the duration of the Frisco 2-31H1. NorAm Wellsite Services wellsite geologists worked
together with Phoenix crews and the Continental Resources, Inc. operations geologist to make
decisions ensuring maximum wellbore exposure within the targeted zone.
The potential pay zone for the Frisco 2-31H1 was determined by evaluating electric logs from
offset wells in the area, including neutron density logs from the Iverson 1-31H. It was
determined the ideal target would be approximately 14' thick, and begin 16' into the first bench
of the Three Forks Formation, and extend to 2' above the Internal 1 Shale Member. The 90
degree curve landing target was projected to be 20' below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale, in
the upper-middle portion of the 14' target zone.
As stated previously, data from the Frisco 1-31H and Frisco 3-31H, whose surface hole locations
are situated West and East, respectively, of the Frisco 2-31H1 surface hole location, were also
used as offsets. The base of the last Charles Salt and upper Lodgepole markers and formation
thicknesses suggested the landing TVD for the build section be set at 10835'TVD (20' into the
Three Forks, Bench 1), in accordance with prognosis. This landing target held until the Upper
and Middle Bakken were intercepted 4' higher than expected, followed by the Three Forks being
intercepted 7' high. The landing target was adjusted accordingly to 10828'TVD, and the bit was
landed at 10830' TVD, 22' into the first bench of the Upper Devonian Three Forks formation.
The gamma signature for the target zone (see figure below) was defined by two hotter peaks
(~100-130 API), 5' and 8', respectively, into zone, accompanied by a basal dolomite bed
exhibiting clean gamma (~60-80 API). To avoid the Pronghorn Member above and the Internal 1
Shale Member below, it was necessary to keep the wellbore within the 14' targeted zone. The 2
hot gamma markers in the upper-middle portion of zone as well as the clean gamma marker at
the base of zone were used to interpret geologic structure throughout the Frisco 2-31H1 lateral.

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
Based on Mission Canyon structural isopachs, thought to be conformable to the Three Forks
Formation, the lateral portion of the Frisco 2-31H1 was projected to show structure down-
dipping ~89.7 degrees out of the intermediate casing shoe until ~13500'MD, after which point
structure was expected to gradually flatten out and turn slightly upward (~90.1 degrees) toward
the end of the lateral (~18000'MD). An overall dip around 89.9 degrees was anticipated. These
projections also took data from the Frisco 1-31H (north bearing horizontal Middle Bakken well
west of Frisco 2-31H1) and the Iverson 1-31H (vertical well northeast of Frisco 2-31H1 surface
hole location) into account.
After drilling out of the 7" intermediate casing shoe, formation generally followed Mission
Canyon structural isopachs in the area, dipping an average of 89.7 degrees, until formation
turned over to ~90.4 around 16000'MD. Structure exhibited a general upward trend (averaging
90.3 degrees) until 18500'MD, where it flattened out to ~90 degrees. Formation held close to flat
for ~1800', briefly rolling over (~89.8 degrees) until the well's total depth (20595'MD,
10843.3'TVD). The overall apparent formation dip was calculated at 89.8 degrees, with a TVD
gain of -18' over the course of the lateral well.

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
SUMMARY
1. The Frisco 2-31H1 is a Three Forks horizontal well spudded with H&P320 on December 1,
2014, and was successfully drilled in the Grass Bluff Field in 7 days, reaching a total depth of
20595'MD at 17:50 CST on December 15, 2014.
2. The wellbore was within the targeted zone 88% of the 9287', 7 day lateral well, exposing a
total of 8183' of open hole within the prospective pay zone.
3. Diesel based invert drilling fluid during the vertical and curve build sections, and salt water
based brine during the lateral, maintained stable hole conditions and permitted adequate analysis
of gas concentrations.
4. Samples throughout the course of the lateral varied from a grey, cream, and tan (top of target
zone down to gamma marker 2) to a cream, light pink, and translucent (clean dolomite bed just
above internal 1 shale), microcrystalline, silty, argillaceous Dolomite with disseminated pyrite
throughout and poor intercrystalline porosity, interbedded with light blue grey to blue green, sub
blocky, sub platy, dolomitic Shale. Pyrite was most abundant closest to the Pronghorn Member
of the upper Three Forks.
5. Background gas values consistently increased throughout the lateral, starting around 50 units
(average connection gas 80 units) out of the intermediate casing shoe, eventually averaging 850
units (average connection gas 900 units) by the well's total depth. The highest gas show observed
was 1155 units at 20264'MD, ~1' above the top of the 14' targeted interval.
6. Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data,
lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with
directional drilling survey data from Phoenix Technology Services. The overall apparent dip of
the Frisco 2-31H1 was approximately 89.8 degrees.
7. The Frisco 2-31H1 is currently awaiting completion operations.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
