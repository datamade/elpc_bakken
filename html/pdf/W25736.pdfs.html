WELLSYNOPSIS
Well Plan:
The Snow 149-93-07A-12H was spudded on September 27th, 2014 with a surface
location of SENE Sec 7 T149N, R93W, 1933' FNL & 818 FEL, McKenzie County, North
Dakota. Conductor pipe was set, surface hole drilled, and 9 5/8 surface casing set to
2171 . Precision 560 moved onto the hole October 25th, 2014 and drilled out of cement at
05:00 hours on October 27th, 2014. The well was operated by Enerplus Resources
(USA) Corp. with a target in the C Zone of the Middle Bakken.
The well plan called for a target TVD of 10831 at 88° with a KOP of 10340 MD. This
allowed for an 11.5 degree build, with a plan nudge to 315 deg. azimuth. The first logged
tops were slightly low to the prognosis: (Kibbey Lime 8420 TVD) (Charles 8580 TVD),
(Base of Charles Salt was at 9125 TVD). (Mission Canyon, 9287 TVD) and (Lodgepole
9922 TVD) the original landing at 10831 w/ 88 degrees remained. The target landing
was adjusted to 10834 TVD based on the Upper Bakken Shale (10799 TVD). The
Bakken A Zone was called at (10814 TVD) landing was then changed to 10835 TVD.
Using these tops the landing was projected to be 5 feet into the Middle Bakken C . The
Landing Point was at 11188 MD 10838 TVD at 02:49 hours on November 4th, 2014.
The plan was to drill across Sections 7 and 12. Offsets used in drilling were the Rain
149-93-07A-12H TF Sec. 7, T 149N, R 93W that was drilled on this very pad.
Core Section:
No cores were planned or taken

Vertical Section: After drilling out of surface casing at 05:00 hours, October 25th, 2014, Bit #1 was tripped
at 8580 in the Charles and decision was made to swap out motors as well. Bit #2
reached the KOP of 10340 at 01:10 hours on October 31st, 2014.
Build Section:
The Snow well was kicked off at 10340 MD 18:16 hours on October 31st, 2014. Bit #3
drilled to 10503 MD and due to poor build rates decision was made to TOOH and swap
out for a bigger bend motor at 22:42 on 10/31/14. Run 0400 drilled to 10653 MD and
decision was made due to poor build rates to trip for new assembly and putting a tri cone
button bit. Run 0500 was then drilled to 10843 MD and was able to catch up on our build
rates back to 11.5 Degree builds to continue, the decision then again was to Trip out for a
another curve assembly to land the curve. Run 0600 landed 4 feet deeper TVD than
desired at 11188 MD 10837.95 TVD 88.37 INC and 309.56 AZ. The Curve ended on
November 4th at 02:49.
Lateral Leg:
Drilling of the lateral section started @ 20:05 on November 6th, 2014. Drilling
commenced using a 6 PDC bit and 1.5° mud motor with oil base invert mud, casing was
set at 11183 . This was nine feet below the C Zone Top. Penetration rates while sliding
for azimuth turn and inclination changes were slow, in the 12-20 foot/hour range. Rotating
penetration rates were in the 80 to 100 foot/hour range until slowing near 17300 . The
decision was made to trip for a new assembly at 17656 MD. There were strong gas
increases at 15000 and again at 17500 while the well bore was in or near the B/C
Contact or the Clean B Section. After 15000 the connection gases increased from 600
/800 units up to 3000 plus units, while background increased 400 to 600 units. There

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
