Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Pipeline Notification
These wells will be connected or are connected to ONEOK's low pressure gathering system in
Dunn County, North Dakota. Oxy's acreage that comprises the spacing unit (SU) for the State
Dvorak A 6-16-21H-142-96 is dedicated to ONEOK. Under terms of the contract, ONEOK is
obligated to install the facilities that are required to connect these wells to its gathering system.
Oxy provides (on a monthly basis) to ONEOK a drilling, completion and estimated first
production date for all wells that are scheduled to be drilled in the foreseeable future.
In
addition, Oxy and ONEOK have conference calls, usually bi-weekly, to discuss connection
status of wells and changes to Oxy's drilling and completion schedules.
Gathering System
ONEOK's gathering system in this area is a dynamic system that has multiple compressor
stations with common suction lines. Gas produced from this pad will have a primary flow path
to ONEOK's Lodgepole compressor station and secondary flow paths to ONEOK's Killdeer and
Little Knife compressor stations. The actual flow of the gas will be based on compression
operating parameters and gathering system pressures. Gas gathered on this part of ONEOK's
gathering system is processed at ONEOK's Grassland Processing Plant located in Section 36,
T146N, R105W, McKenzie County, North Dakota.
In addition to ONEOK, Hiland has a low pressure gathering line approximately 500' north of the
State Dvorak NENW 16 Pad location. Hiland's lines, along with ONEOK's, are shown on the
attached map.
The production facilities for the State Dvorak A 6-16-21H-142-96 will be located less than 50'
from ONEOK's existing 8.625" outside diameter (OD) low pressure gathering line.
The
connection to ONEOK will require less than 50' of 4.50" OD low pressure gathering line and
related facilities.
Existing Lines

then connects to a 16.0" OD gathering line. The 16" OD gathering line extends approximately
24 miles and then connects to the inlet station piping at ONEOK's Lodgepole compressor
station. This will be the primary flow path of gas delivered to ONEOK from this pad. As noted
under "Gathering System", above, the gas will have secondary flow paths due to the dynamics of
ONEOK's gathering system. The table below shows the capacities and current flows of the

ONEOK is in the process of adding compressors at its Lost Bridge compressor station in Section
14, T147N, R96W, Dunn County and installing a 16" OD low pressure gathering line that will
start in T145N, R94W, Dunn County (Dunn Center) and end at the inlet of the Lost Bridge
compressor station. Construction of these facilities will redirect gas that currently flows on the
primary and secondary flow paths of the State Dvorak NENW 16 Pad to flow on the new
facilities. Although this does not directly increase the capacity of the primary and secondary
flow paths of the State Dvorak NENW 16 Pad, it redirects gas that currently flows on the same
pipelines to a different compressor station, in essence reducing volumes transported on the flow
paths for this well. ONEOK is also planning on constructing a new discharge line from its Lost
Bridge compressor station that will deliver some of the gas currently processed at ONEOK's
Grasslands Processing Plant to ONEOK's Garden Creek Processing Plant(s).
ONEOK has announced plans to construct the Bear Creek natural gas processing plant and
related natural gas and natural gas liquids infrastructure in Dunn County, North Dakota. The
Bear Creek natural gas processing plant and related facilities are expected to be operation during
the second quarter of 2016. In addition, ONEOK Demicks Lake natural gas processing plant,
located in McKenzie County, North Dakota, is expected to be operational during the third quarter
of 2016.
Flowback Strategy
After the fracture treatment, this well will be flowed through a choke to test production tanks.
Choke size will depend on final shut in pressure post frac and well pressure prior to opening the
choke. The choke will be adjusted up or down based on flowing pressure, liquid rates and sand
4
returns, to prevent clogging manifold chokes. During flowback, the water chlorides and sand
content will be monitored. When the water chlorides are greater than 100,000 ppm and the
produced fluids contain no sand, the well will be turned to production facilities.
Since the
pipeline is already at the location of the pad and Oxy will have its production facilities
constructed before flowback starts, it is expected that gas sales will start as soon as the well start
flowing through the production facilities, unless there are capacity issues on ONEOK's system at
that time. Based on current information, it is our belief the system can take this gas upon
completion of this well.
This well will initially flow 445 barrels of oil and 200 thousand cubic feet of gas per day based
on Oxy's production type curve of this area. It is anticipated this well will decline sharply after
initial production as do the majority of horizontal wells in the Williston Basin.
