INTRODUCTION
The QEP Energy Company's P. Levang 3-14-23BH [NW NW, Section 14, T150N,
R95W] is located approximately 29 miles to the east of Watford City, North Dakota. This
well is permitted as a 1,280 acre spacing unit, and is proposed as a double section 10,000'
lateral targeting the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation within Grail Field of the
Williston Basin. The lateral will be drilled to the south east within section 14. This well
will be the third vertical hole to be drilled on the P. Levang 4-14x Pad.
The primary offset well used for adjusting the curve landing was QEP's P. Levang 4-14-
23H drilled 37 feet to the south. The other offset wells used for correlation were the QEP
Energy Company's Jones 16-14H, located 1.1 miles to the southeast, and QEP Energy
Company's Jones 4-15-22H located approximately 1.2 mile to the west southwest. All
three offset wells drilled horizontally into the Three Forks, providing good data for
isopach thickness and structural dip estimations.
ENGINEERING
The P. Levang 3-14-23BH was spud on October 26, 2014 by Unit Drilling Rig #121,
where a 13 ½" surface hole was drilled with fresh water to 2,108' then isolated with 9
5/8" casing and cemented to surface. The drilling fluid was changed to diesel invert for
the vertical and curve build. A vertical hole was drilled to kick off point at 10,130' MD.
Halliburton provided the directional drilling and MWD personnel and tools, including
gamma ray and surveys. The curve assembly was picked up at the kick off point, and the
curve was completed in 18.5 drilling hours at 11,870' MD, on November 13, 2014. Upon
completing the curve, 7" 32# HCL-80 intermediate casing was set and cemented from
10,870' MD to surface.
The lateral section of the P. Levang 3-14-23BH was proposed to drill 10,000' in the
Middle Bakken, horizontally from north to south. A total of one BHA was used to drill
the entire lateral. The P. Levang 3-14-23BH lateral was completed on November 11,
2014 at 13:50 CT which reached a total depth of 20,986' MD and 10,670' TVD in 13
drilling days. The bottom hole location is 9,981' S & 1,436.73' E of surface location or
approximately 250.52' FSL & 1,960.72' FWL, SE SW Sec. 23, T150N, R95W. Overall
the lateral was drilled 100% within the target window
GEOLOGY
Methods
Geologic analysis for P. Levang 3-14-23BH was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc.
Information was networked through Pason's electronic data recorder system. This
information network provided depth, drilling rate, pump strokes and total gas units
throughout the well site. Gas data was gathered using Pason's gas detection system and
disseminated across the location. Total gas was measured in units, using a 10,000 unit
scale and logged without the use of a chromatograph. Beginning at 6,500' MD, within the
Spearfish Formation, rig crews caught lagged samples at the direction of the well site
geologists. Samples were collected at 30', and two sets of dry cuttings were collected for
QEP Energy Company, one of which was sent to the North Dakota Geological Survey.
Rock cuttings were analyzed in wet and dry conditions under a 20x60 power binocular
microscope.
Prospective Intervals
The intervals which showed good potential for oil and gas are noted below, based on
sample analysis, gas shows, oil staining, and drilling parameters.
The Mission Canyon Formation was drilled for 626' TVD. The upper section varied from
wackestone to mudstone and contained dark brown algal laminations with moderate
amounts of anhydrite and dolomite made up the uppermost 70' of the formation. The
middle to lower sections primarily consisted of argillaceous limestone described as
moderately soft mudstone, light gray to tan in color with an earthy texture. Traces of dark
brown oil staining were found in sample cuttings throughout the Mission Canyon.
Background gas throughout the formation varied from 150 to 250 units with connection
gas peaks up to 564 units. Increased porosity became evident at 9,300' MD where a
single gas show reached 1,215 units, subsequent background gas increased throughout
the lower portion of the Mission Canyon.
The False Bakken Member of the Lodgepole Formation is 8' TVD in thickness, and
consists of a dark brown to black shale that had an earthy texture and an abundance of
disseminated pyrite. Upon entering the False Bakken background gas increased 200 units
reaching 528 units at its peak.
The Scallion Member (Figure 2) located at the base of the Lodgepole Formation is 9'
TVD in thickness, the composition of this member is made up of, moderately hard and
platy lime wackestone with waxy texture, traces of nodular pyrite and light brown oil
staining also present throughout. At 10,682' MD a gas show of 4,736 units suggests
possible fractures within the member.
Figure 2. Example of Scallion limestone.
The Upper Bakken Shale (Figure 3) is made up of 23' TVD of soft to friable sub-blocky
and petroliferous black shale with consistent distribution of disseminated pyrite within
the rock. The Upper Bakken Shale high gamma signature and abundant oil shows are
distinguishing characteristics. Immediately upon entering the Upper Shale a gas show of
5,328 units occurred. Background gas throughout the Upper Shale remained between
1,500 to 2,000 units.
Figure 3. Pyritic & petroliferous shale of the Upper Bakken.
At the top of the Middle Bakken, is a thin layer of dark gray to dark brown carbonaceous
siltstone with veins of interbedded and disseminated pyrite. Below the siltstone facies
lays the limestone interval referred to as the "Shoal" by QEP's geology team. The Shoal
(Figure 4) consists of hard lime packstone with an abundance of fossil fragments and very
well oil stained.
Figure 4. Middle Bakken oolitic lime packstone referred to as the "Shoal".
The third facies encountered within the Middle Bakken is silty sandstone and the primary
target rock for laterals within this member. The target zone (Figure 5) within this Middle
Bakken lateral was set at 20' thick. The top of the target window starts at the top of the
"Shoal", or approximately 10' below the Middle Bakken top. Samples collected
throughout the lateral consisted of silty sandstone containing pyrite and calcite often
fluctuating from light gray to dark gray, varying from friable and poorly consolidated to
moderately hard and well cemented, typically including calcareous cement. Color and
pyritic density variations of the rocks help to determine stratigraphic positioning
throughout the lateral. Often higher concentrations of pyrite as well as darker colored
rocks where present in the lower portion of the target zone, while lighter and more
calcareous samples were found near the upper portion of the target zone.
Background gas throughout the first half of the lateral consistently read from 1,000 to
1,500 units. At approximately 16,600' MD background gas readings began to drop off
and would remain low for the duration of the well. After troubleshooting Pason gas
detection, the cause of low gas was undetermined.
Figure 5. Silty sandstone in the Middle Bakken Target Zone.
GEO-STEERING
Curve/Landing
The kick off point to start the curve build was reached at 10,130' MD on November 11,
2014 while drilling in the Lodgepole Formation. The landing was proposed to be 10' to
20' TVD into the Middle Bakken. Due to predicted formation tops close relativity to the
prognosis the landing was not adjusted during the curve. The curve landed 10,870' MD
and 10,608' TVD in the Middle Bakken, within the Target Zone, on November 13, 2014
at 11:10 CT.
Lateral
Upon completing the curve, 7" 32# HCL-80 intermediate casing was set and cemented
from 10,870' MD to surface.
The lateral section of the P. Levang 3-14-23BH was proposed to drill 10,000' in the
Middle Bakken, horizontally from north to south. A total of one BHA was used to drill
the entire lateral. The P. Levang 3-14-23BH lateral was completed on December 11, 2014
at 13:50 CT which reached a total depth of 20,986' MD and 10,670' TVD in 13 drilling
days. The bottom hole location is 9,981' S & 1,436.73' E of surface location or
approximately 250.52' FSL & 1,960.72' FWL, SE SW Sec. 23, T150N, R95W. Overall
the lateral was drilled 100% within the target window.
The lateral section of the P. Levang 3-14-23BH was geo-steered by the QEP Geology
team. Dip expectations were provided by QEP along with structure maps and nearby
offset wells, all of which suggested an approximate 0.5° regional structural dip. The
target zone defined by QEP was a 20' thick target window beginning at the top of the top
of the "Shoal". Attempts were made to correlate repeating gamma signatures for dip
calculations. The most prominent marker used for geo-steering was the "Hard Streak"
located in the lower portion of the target window. Contact with the "Hard Streak" was
encountered often throughout the lateral section providing a strong anchor point for geo-
steering decision making. Initially a 0.7° structural dip was assumed from surface to
bottom hole, however the structure was slightly flatter than expectations. The overall
structure was very close to QEP's pre-drill estimation, only slight adjustments were made
throughout. The gross overall dip for the P. Levang 3-14-23BH is 0.39° downward.

SUMMARY
The formations tops encountered during the vertical and curve portion of the P. Levang
3-14-23BH were close to expectations therefore no changes were made to the landing
point. The P. Levang 3-14-23BH was the third well on this pad to be completed, and the
second Middle Bakken well. The P. Levang 3-14-23BH vertical and curve reached its
Intermediate Casing Point at 10,870' MD and 10,608' TVD on the 13rd of November
2014. The overall gas units as noted were significantly higher in the first half of the well,
with background gas dropping off in the latter half. Throughout the target zone, spotty
brown to light green oil staining was present within the rock cuttings, as well as
noticeable oil indications over the shakers. The lateral was drilled from Intermediate
Casing Point to Total Depth in 6 drilling days for a total of 13 drilling days from spud to
finish and reached TD at 20,986' MD on the 11th of November, 2014. The P. Levang 3-
14-23BH was successfully geo-steered approximately 100% within the designated target
zone.

NOTES ON 9-5/8" SURFACE CASING DESIGN:
The key point in designing surface casing is to extend the shoe of the surface casing beyond the top of
the Pierre Shale. This ensures that all potable or usable water zones are covered. At this point in the well
design, the drilling fluid is changed to oil base mud to contend with salt sections that occur at
approximately 6,550' TVD and 8,950' TVD. The 36 ppf J55 casing accomplishes these criteria easily.
A higher burst rating is not necessary based on industry standards throughout the Williston Basin.
In the event the surface casing burst rating was increased, the normally pressured and sub normally
pressured zones all the way thru the Madison Group at approximately 8,250' TVD has the potential to
break down. Therefore the increased pressure rating of surface casing is not warranted. It is not feasible
to set the surface casing point at a greater depth because the shut in surface pressures would not
appreciably increase. The Dakota formation (4,900' TVD) is frequently weak. Typically, we drill the
intermediate section with 10.5 ppg OBM to the 7" casing point. In the event we lost circulation in the
exposed intermediate interval, the wellbore will not be entirely evacuated and the surface casing can be
shut in without compromising the pressure rating of the 36 ppf J-55.
