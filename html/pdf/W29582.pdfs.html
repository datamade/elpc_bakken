Well Evaluation
Satter 21X-1C [Lot 3 Section 1, T150N, R98W] is located approximately 5 miles
northeast of Watford City in McKenzie County, ND. The Satter 21X-1C is a horizontal
Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled toward
the south. The vertical hole will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Middle
Bakken. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques were then used to maintain
maximum exposure to the target zone.

GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation in order to
log the Base Last Salt for landing target purposes.
The Middle Bakken was logged at 11,006' TVD (-8,924' MSL). It consists primarily of
silty sandstone that is medium grey to light grey with occasional off white clasts and
contains trace to rare amounts of spotty, medium brown oil stain. The Middle Bakken
was the target reservoir for the lateral portion of the well.
Gas Monitoring
A fracture gas of 1,046u was recorded while drilling the curve at 10,718' MD. The
highest gas observed while drilling the vertical portion of the well was 51u at 9,748' MD.
The highest gas value recorded for the well was a down-time gas of 1,756u at 14,785'.
No flare was observed as there was no circulation through the gas buster. Background gas
fluctuated between 50 and 300u throughout the lateral. The entire ideal target zone was
observed to contain low to moderate gas concentrations and consistent oil staining. The
highest gas concentrations were seen in the lower 7-10 feet of the ideal target zone
(Figure 2).

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 25' interval, the top of which was initially set at
11,016' TVD, 30' below the top of the Upper Bakken Shale (Figure 1). The superposition
of a cool gamma marker reading 60-70 API just above a warm gamma marker reading
85-100 API ~12' into the Middle Bakken target zone proved to be the most useful for
geo-steering. Offset wells indicated that the Middle Bakken would be flat from landing to
TD. The final gross overall change in TVD from landing to the end of the lateral was
estimated at 13' over 9,514' (0.08° up dip, Figure 3). The bottom-hole location is
estimated to be 9,992.20' south & 307.71' east of surface location or approximately
251.64' FSL & 2,370.21' FEL, SW SE Sec. 12, T150N, R98W. Geo-steering techniques
exposed 100% of uncased borehole to the 25' Middle Bakken ideal target and the well
reached a TD of 20,770' on December 15, 2014.

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Satter 21X-1C was spud on October 27, then Trinidad 226 moved
onto the location November 26 re-entering surface on November 27 and officially
reached TD in 22 total drilling days on December 15, 2014. Samples from the Middle
Bakken silty sandstone target contained oil staining that was described as trace to rare
with visible intergranular porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP
were successfully utilized to expose 100% of the lateral borehole to the ideal Middle
Bakken target. The Satter 21X-1C awaits completion operations to maximize its
production potential.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
