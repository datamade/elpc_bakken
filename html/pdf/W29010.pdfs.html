Well Evaluation
Nelson 24X-11F [SE SW Section 11, T151N, R98W] is located approximately 8 miles
northeast of Watford City in McKenzie County, ND. The Nelson 24X-11F is a horizontal
Three Forks well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled towards the
north. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Three Forks.
Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will then be used to maintain maximum
exposure to the target zone.

GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation in order to
log the Base Last Salt for landing target purposes.
The Three Forks Formation was logged at 11,150' TVD (-9,065' MSL). It consists
primarily of dolomite with laminated intervals of pale green shale and contains trace to
rare amounts of spotty, medium brown oil stain. The Three Forks was the target reservoir
for the lateral portion of the well.
Gas Monitoring
During the vertical a possible fracture gas of 159u was recorded at 10,378' MD which
was the highest gas observed while drilling the vertical portion of the well. The highest
gas value recorded for the well was 3,538u. No flare was observed during the course of
the well as there was no circulation through the gas buster. Background gas fluctuated
between 50-500u throughout the lateral section. The entire ideal target zone was observed
to contain relatively low to moderate gas concentrations and consistent oil staining. The
highest gas concentrations were seen in the upper 7 feet of the ideal target zone (Figure
3).

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 15' interval, the top of which was initially set at
11,163' TVD, 48' below the top of the Lower Bakken Shale (Figure 1). The
superposition of a warm gamma marker reading 100-120 API, 1-2' above a cool gamma
marker reading 55-65 API at the center of the Three Forks zone; proved to be the most
useful for geo-steering. Offset wells indicated that the Three Forks would be up-dipping
approximately 0.06° from landing to TD. Overall, the formation dropped approximately
18' over 9,766' from the beginning to the end of the lateral (0.11° down, Figure 3). The
bottom-hole location is estimated to be 9,885.51' north & 858.35' east of surface location
or approx. 253.73' FNL & 2,588.35' FWL, NE NW Sec. 2, T151N, R98W. Geo-steering
techniques exposed 88% of uncased borehole to the 15' Three Forks ideal target and the
well reached a TD of 21,055' on September 5, 2014.

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Nelson 24X-11F was spud on August 15 and officially reached TD
in 22 total drilling days on September 5, 2014. Samples from the Three Forks dolomite
target contained oil staining that was described as trace to rare with visible
intercrystalline porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were
successfully utilized to expose 88% of the lateral borehole to the ideal Three Forks target.
The Nelson 24X-11F awaits completion operations to maximize its production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
