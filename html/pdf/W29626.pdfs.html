INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Osen 6192 43-21T [SW SE, Section 21, T161N,
R92W] is located approximately 13 miles north of Powers Lake in Burke County, ND. It shares a
pad with the Kukuk 6192 43-21B well. The Osen 6192 43-21T is a horizontal Three Forks well
within the Williston Basin consisting of one 10,150' lateral drilled toward the south.
Geologically, the Williston Basin is an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at
the western edge of the Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Three Forks Formation (Late
Devonian) deposited dolomitic mudstone interbedded with light colored shale and claystone.
Advances in drilling techniques and well stimulation have made multiple zones viable as an oil
and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found `Structure' section
appended to this report. Offset well control is essential in curve building operations, in order to
successfully land within a targeted zone of the Three Forks Formation. Formation thicknesses
expressed by gamma ray signatures and were compared to gamma data of offset wells in order to
successfully land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically
updated to ensure accurate landing of the curve. The offset wells used for the Osen 6192 43-21T
were the Gustafson 29-32-161-92H, Woll 6093 12-1T, Domalakes 6092 44-16H.
ENGINEERING
The Osen 6192 43-21T was spud on December 2, 2014 by Xtreme 21. Drilling fluid was diesel
invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt-water mud for the lateral. The curve was landed at,
8,990 MD (8,648' TVD) with an inclination of 90°. One trip was made during the curve at MD
8,615 for poor build rates. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to
8,891' MD. To start the lateral, a 6 Varel PDC bit, MWD tool, and a 1.5° fixed mud motor were
used. Due to a bad scribe on the motor and an imminent collusion with the Pronghorn, the
original well bore was abandoned and a sidetrack was initiated at MD 9,140' with a Baker
Hughes bit being used. This bit was replaced by a Reed bit at 13,424' MD for trouble with
sliding. At MD 17,377' a trip was made for an MWD failure and bit #7 was reran. The BHA
accomplishments are tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached
total depth (TD) at 19,041 MD at 2220 CDT on January 13, 2015, generating an exposure of
10,150 linear feet of 6 hole through the Three Forks First Bench target interval. The bottom
hole location (BHL) lies 10,609.68' south & 20.07' east of surface location or approximately
236.76' FSL and 1,814.93' FEL, SW SE Sec. 33, T161N, R92W.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water mud
was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 15-123 units above a
background of ~7 units against an average mud weight of 9.5 lb/gal diesel-invert. Methane and
Ethane (C1-C2) were observed throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than C2 were not observed.
After kicking off the curve, shows were similar with respect to the vertical. Shows remained low
from kick-off through entry of the Upper Bakken Shale interval of the Bakken Formation, where
shows of 246 and 367 units were seen. Gas shows were consistently high in both shale intervals
low in the Middle Bakken. Gas shows in the Three Forks started low but would reach over 2,000
units when close to the lower claystone facies of the first bench and drop off drastically when in
the claystone facies.
GEOSTEERING
The ideal target zone of the Osen 6192 43-21T was identified by evaluating gamma ray data
from the nearby Saratoga 12-1-161-92H (Figure 3). For geosteering purposes, the ideal target is
set with the top of the target at the 6 marker and the bottom of the target 2' into the top of the
claystone.
Figure 3. Type TVD log from Saratoga 12-1-161-92H. Ideal target zone highlighted in green. (Curtis
Johnson, Oasis Geologist)
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 8,642' TVD at 90.0°. The Base of Last Salt top
indicated that the target was 12' low of prognosis. The decision was made to move the target 8'
lower. The curve was landed at a TVD of 8,648' at 90.4°, about 12' TVD below the top of the
Three Forks, at the very top of the ideal target zone.
The most consistent marker used to steer was the Claystone 1 marker, a high-gamma marker
(160-200 API) at the bottom of the zone, which is easily identified in samples, with a drop of
gas, and a drilling breaks. Many of the gamma markers in the upper Three Forks are known to be
unreliable and often lead to miscorrelations. Steering decisions are based off both samples and
occasionally tapping the Claystone 1 marker near the bottom of the zone.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Osen 6192 43-21T was drilled by Xtreme 21 from
spud to TD in 20 days. The Osen reached a total depth of 19,041' MD on January 13, 2015.
Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination obtained during the
Osen 6192 43-21T indicate a hydrocarbon rich system in the Three Forks First Bench Target.
Significant shows were recorded throughout the lateral, particularly above the claystone facies.
Samples from the target zone consist of an off white to cream with occasional light brown
dolomite with interbedded mint green to light blue green shale and intercrystalline porosity and
a trace light-brown oil stain. Lateral operations were successful in exposing 10,150' of
potentially productive Three Forks First Bench target zone rock to the wellbore. The well was
within the targeted zone for 94% of the 10,150' of lateral.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
