GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
The Continental Resources, Inc. Barney 6-29H1, surface hole location 300' FNL, 1600' FWL,
S29-T155N-R98W, is located approximately 5 miles south of Epping, North Dakota, and
terminates near the southern boundary of the Brooklyn Field, approximately 18 miles west of
the Nesson Anticline. Barney 6-29H1 is the 6th southerly lateral originating along the northern
boundary of Section 29 and terminating near the southern boundary of Section 32. The well
was planned for one 9,997' long southerly transect in the First Bench of the Three Forks
Formation.
The initial structural interpretation assumed a consistent down dip of
approximately 89.66°, with the terminus of the lateral bore expected to be 60' lower than the
landing point of the curve. The intent was to remain in an 8' thick zone in the Three Forks
Formation. The Target Zone is strati graphically situated between 17' and 25' beneath the
base of the Lower Bakken Shale. The drilling plan included a vertical hole to be drilled to a
kick-off point of MD 10713' in the Lodgepole Formation, then begin a 13.00°/100' build to a
landing point of MD 11403' TVD 11154'. The lateral was planned to start with a 3.00°/100' turn
holding an 89.66° inclination to MD 13094', then holding 180.00° azimuth and 89.66°
inclination to TD at MD 21,399'.

OFFSET INFORMATION
Offset well data for developing a prognosis of formation tops, and also in determining a
structure model for the Target Zone for Barney 6-29H1 was obtained from vertical and curve
data from three nearby, previously drilled Continental Resources, Inc. wells. These wells are
located in the same sections as Barney 6-29H1: Sections 29 and 32 of the Brooklyn Field
T155N-R98W.
Barney 4-29H1 is a Three Forks well with a surface hole location
approximately 100` north and 630' west of Barney 6-29H1, Barney 7-29H, a Middle Bakken
well adjacent to, and approximately 45' west of Barney 6-29H1 and Barney 1-29H, a Middle
Bakken well approximately 25' north and 1847' west of Barney 6-29H1. Barney 3-29H3 was
also used in landing Barney 6-29H1 in the Three Forks Formation.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Nabors Rig 784 spudded Barney 6-29H1 December 13, 2014. Prior to the Nabors rig moving
onto the hole, a spudder rig drilled a 13 1/2" hole with fresh water to MD 2264'. The surface
hole was isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J55 STC casing and cemented to surface. Diesel invert mud
was used while drilling from the surface casing shoe to the intermediate casing point at MD
11411'. Two 8 ¾" PDC bits and two 1.5 degree mud motors were used to drill from surface
casing to the kick off point of MD 10700'. A curve assembly employing a third 8 ¾" PDC bit
and 2.38 degree bend mud motor was used to drill and complete the curve. The curve was
landed at MD 11485' TVD 11152', at the top boundary of the Target Zone, with a projected
inclination of 86.20 degrees. 7", 32# P110 IC, LTC intermediate casing was then run to MD
11411' TVD 11144' and cemented.
Lateral
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
with 9.8 to 10.0# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations.
The 6"
horizontal lateral was then drilled at a planned 3.00º/100' azimuth to MD 12441', where a
decision was made to sidetrack when suitable inclination build rates were unable to be
achieved. The sidetrack was time drilled beginning at MD 11797' to MD 11812' and control
drilled to MD 11,827'. The azimuth turn was completed at MD 13520' and the lateral was
drilled to TD at MD 21274', TVD 11210.45' (-8921), 233' FSL & 2624' FWL S32-T155N-R98W
Williams County, North Dakota. Four MWD tools, three 6" PDC bits and three 1.83 degree
steerable motors were used to drill the lateral section.

TARGET ZONE IDENTIFICATION IN THE LATERAL
The initial Primary Target Zone of Barney 6-29H1 was an 8' thick segment of the Three Forks
Formation beginning 17' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale to one foot above the
Internal One. API gamma counts from 45 to 110 reflected the thinly bedded nature of horizons
in the Target Zone. Sharp, high gamma (135+ API) readings signified entering the Internal
One, and gamma of 125-130 API denoting the top of the Target Zone, greatly enhanced zone
identification. Lithology in the Target Zone was observed to be dolomite with a lithological
change to calcareous argillaceous dolomite with common gray to gray green, waxy, micro-
pyritic dolomitic shale inter beds at the upper boundary of the zone. Lithology below zone
consisted of argillaceous dolomite with cuttings being slightly more calcareous than cuttings
from the target zone and the presence of light gray, waxy, micro-pyritic dolomitic shale.
LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
9.8# to 10.0 # sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. Three 6" PDC bits,
three 1.83 degree steerable motors, and four MWD tools were used to drill the lateral section.
The first survey upon exiting the casing shoe revealed an inclination of 84.80 degrees, which
was significantly different from the projected inclination 86.20°. This may have been caused
by an additional 10' being drilled after the initial curve TD had been reached. A second survey
was taken after drilling ahead 35' and an inclination of 84.30° confirmed the need for
directional up slides. Also confirmed was the Target Zone Base at MD 11575'. Directional
slides were successful and the Target Zone was re-entered at MD 11940' and the initial
formation dip of 89.92° was calculated. The 3.00°/100' azimuth turn began at MD 11904' and
continued to MD 12441' when a decision was made to sidetrack when inclination builds could
not be maintained while executing the azimuth turn. Sidetrack trough drilling began at MD
11703' with time drilling initiated at MD 11797'.
Re-entry into the Target Zone was
encountered at MD 12330' resulting in a formation dip of 89.71°.
From this point, local
formation dips ranged 89.55° to 89.58° to MD 17022', where a dip reversal to 90.46° was
calculated. A down dip of 89.65° occurred at approximately MD 17569', with another dip
reversal of 90.98° at MD 17910', with a resumption of the down dip to TD at MD 21274'. A
formation dip of 89.74° was calculated for the entire lateral. The 6" bore exposed 9863 linear
feet of hole through the Three Forks Formation.
Rotational drilling of 9675' (91.1%) and
directional slides of 942' (8.9%) comprised drilling of the lateral. Average rotating ROP was
129'/Hr. and sliding ROP was 20'/Hr. The wellbore remained in the Target Zone approximately
5418' (54.9%), below the target zone 3404' (34.5%) and above the target zone 1041' (10.6%).

SUMMARY
Barney 6-29H1 was drilled in the Three Forks Formation and oil and gas shows observed
were fair. Oil shows in samples were consistent displaying rare to some medium brown stain
and faint to moderately bright, spotty to even, yellow, yellow gray and yellow green
fluorescence with cut from weak to blooming. Subsurface structural control utilizing MWD
gamma and data from Barney 1-29H was sufficient to allow drilling the hole with low potential
for stratigraphic positional misinterpretation. Steering to correct for inclination or azimuth was
responsive after the sidetrack to TD. Barney 6-29H1 will be completed with multi-stage
fracture stimulation after which its production potential will be revealed. Samples and surveys
have been delivered to the North Dakota Industrial Commission.

Filter Socks and Other Filter Media

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
