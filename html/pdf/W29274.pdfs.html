Synopsis
The Drummond 5501 42-21 4T [SE SW Sec. 21 T155N R101W] is located in the Missouri Ridge
Field in Williams County approximately eight miles North of Williston, North Dakota. The well
plan proposed a single lateral leg trending 1.07° south to be drilled from the SE SW corner of
Section 21 to the NE NW corner of Section 16. The lateral portion of the Drummond 5501 42-
21 4T targeted a zone in the dolomite sandstone of the Three Forks formation, approximately 6'
to 12' below the Pronghorn formation.
Control Wells
One complete well, the Drummond 5501 42-21 6T was used as the control offset for the
Drummond 5501 42-21 4T. Throughout the curve progression, gamma-ray data from the
Drummond 5501 42-21 6T was utilized to help determine proper landing depth for the
Drummond 5501 42-21 4T.
The Drummond 5501 42-21 6T [SE SW Sec. 21 T155N R101W] is located on the same pad as
the subject well.
A graph was constructed to determine which markers and formation tops could be accurately
used in landing future curves on the Drummond pad. Markers with more than 5 feet difference
between the offsets were watched but not utilized in decision for moving the TVD target of the
curve.

Geologic Assessment
Methods
Geologic supervision of Drummond 5501 42-21 4T was provided by two experienced RPM well
site geologists. Gas and chromatograph levels were measured using NOV Wellsite Gas Watch
real time gas detector and chromatograph system. The NOV Wellsite Gas Watch system uses
non-dispersive infrared and chemical sensor gas detection to evaluate gases liberated from the
formation by the drill bit and carried to the surface by the drilling fluid.
The NOV Wellsite Gas Watch was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder system Pason
provided rate of penetration, on-off bottom and pump strokes to the NOV Wellsite Gas Watch
and received total gas information from the NOV Wellsite Gas Watch for viewing on location
and remotely.
Under the direction of RPM well site geologist, rig crews were instructed to catch lagged drill
cutting samples at 30' intervals from 8,330 to the kick off point (KOP) at 10,280'. Samples were
continuously logged at 50' intervals during the curve and throughout the lateral section.
Sampled drill cuttings were examined wet and dry under a binocular microscope using plain
(broad spectrum) and transmitted light. Cuttings were evaluated for hydrocarbon "cut" by
immersion in carbo-sol (trichloroethylene) and inspection under a UV fluoroscope. 10%
hydrochloric acid and alizarin red were used to determine the calcareous and dolomitic content
of rocks and cementing.
Vertical Operations
Overview
The Drummond 5501 42-21 4T was spud on the 22nd of November 2014 using Patterson 330 as
the drilling rig. Prior to rig up of RPM mud logging services, a 9 5/8" hole was drilled with fresh
water to a depth of 2,330' and isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J-55 casing cemented from 2,309' to
surface. Bit #1 (8 ¾" Reed) was used to drill from 2,330' 8,361'. It drilled 6,031' in 37 hours,
averaging 163.0 ft/hr. Bit #2 (8 ¾" Reed) was used to drill from 8,361' to KOP (10,280'). It
drilled 1,919' in 25.5 hours, averaging 75.3 ft/hr.
RPM well site geologists arrived at site on 24 November 2014 and began logging the vertical
section at 8,330'.
Diesel invert drilling fluid with a mud weight of ranging from 9.3-10.4 ppg was used during
remainder of the vertical hole and in the curve builds sections.

Directional Operations
RPM provided equipment and personnel for directional services. Ryan Energy Technologies
(Ryan) provided equipment and personnel for MWD services. RPM geologists worked closely
with RPM directional and Ryan MWD teams to formulate steering decisions to maximize
exposure of borehole in the pay zone. RPM well site geologists closely observed MWD gamma-
ray information and penetration rates to aid in steering decisions and dip degree estimations.
Curve Build
Overview
Bit #3 (8 ¾" Security MMD55M) was used to drill the curve. It drilled from 10,280' to 11,160'
in 15.5 hours, averaging 56.8 ft/hr.

42-21 6T as well as data from another offset, the Glen 1-30 was compared to gamma-ray

entered into the table as they were observed to determine the target landing point. Based on the
marker pick for the Base Charles Salt, the target TVD for the curve was kept the same due to
very minor variation from the off-set well. The curve was completed at a measured depth of
11,160'and a TVD of 10,798.85' in the Three Forks 1st Bench.

Lithology
The top of the "False Bakken" was penetrated at 10,798 MD / 10,701' TVD (-8,462' MSL), 2'
high to the Drummond 5501 42-21 6T. Gas levels quickly peaked at 368 units and remained
high through the "False Bakken". Due to the low angle of penetration the "False Bakken" was
not largely observed in sample.
The Upper Shale of the Bakken formation [Mississippian / Devonian] was drilled at 10,814'
MD / 10,711' TVD (-8,472' MSL) with sample returns of typical black, carbonaceous and
petroliferous shale (Figure 4) characterized by gamma-ray values in excess of 250 API counts.
The Upper Shale was even to the Drummond 5501 42-21 6T. Gas levels remained fairly
consistent with average background gas of ~120 units. Samples were described as:
SHALE: very dark gray to brown, sub blocky to blocky, friable to firm, smooth hard,
waxy textures, calcareous, occasional disseminated pyrite, trace visible oil stain
Figure 4: Photograph sample from the Upper Bakken Shale.
The Middle Member of the Bakken formation [Mississippian / Devonian] was penetrated at
10,844' MD / 10,729' TVD (-8,490' MSL), even to the Drummond 5501 42-21 6T. The Middle
Member contained a tight grey siltstone with little or no oil stain or visible porosity along with a
liter brown silty sandstone, with apparent porosity and oil stain (Figure 5). Gas levels averaged
~150 units through the Middle Member of the Bakken formation. Samples from the Middle
Member were described as:
SILTY SANDSTONE: light to medium gray, brown, trace white, soft to friable, sub blocky
to blocky, calcite to cement, slightly dolomitic, trace disseminated pyrite, no visible
porosity, trace dark brown oil stain
Figure 5: Photograph representative of the siltstone and silty sandstone from the Middle Bakken
The Lower Shale of the Bakken formation [Devonian] was penetrated at 10,935' MD / 10,770'

SHALE: very dark gray to brown, sub blocky to blocky, friable to firm, smooth hard, waxy

DOLOMITE: mudstone, pale green, tan to cream, very fine crystalline, friable to firm, smooth

Lateral
Overview
Drilling fluid consisting of a closed system salt water brine (9.3 ­ 9.7ppg) was used while
drilling the lateral section. Two 6" PDC bits were used to drill the lateral.
Bit #4 (6" Smith Z613), a PDC bit in front of a 1.5° mud motor was used to drill the lateral from
11,160' to 16,607'. The average ROP for this bit was 93.1 ft/hr in 58.5 hours of drilling.
At approximately 16,600' measured depth the Pronghorn was encountered. The decision was
made to sidetrack. The first sidetrack attempt was made with Bit #4 at a measured depth of
16,630'. After the failure of the first sidetrack the bit was changed and the well was successfully
sidetracked from a measured depth of 15,733'.
Bit #5 (6" Smith 787718), a PDC bit in front of a 1.5° mud motor was used to drill the lateral
from 15,733' to 19,553'. This bit was used to sidetrack after a Pronghorn strike. The average
ROP for this bit was 52.3 ft/hr in 73 hours of drilling.
Bit #4 (6" Smith Z613) was rerun after Bit #5 was pulled. It was a PDC bit used in front of a 1.5°
mud motor to drill the lateral from 19,553' to 20,467'. The average ROP for this bit was 101.6
ft/hr in 9 hours of drilling.
Drilling operations were concluded at 15:00 hours on 23 December 2014 at a bottom hole
location of 9,888.53' N & 174.63' E of surface location or approximately 314.83' FNL &
2,247.63' FWL, NE NW Sec. 16, T155N, R101W.
Lithology
The Three Forks formation target zone is represented by an 8 foot thick target zone situated 9 to
17 feet below the base of the Pronghorn formation. The target zone was characterized by an
interval of cold gamma and consisted primarily of sandy dolomite. Every attempt was made to
keep the well bore in the target zone below this low gamma package for as much of the drilling
operations as possible.
The Three Forks formation seen in drilling samples consisted of two primary facies. Samples
observed while drilling the upper target zone (Figure 8) consisted of a cream to tan, occasionally
to light pink sandy dolomite with lenses of purple to gray sandy siltstone. Pale green siltstone
with common disseminated pyrite was present. Common to trace nodular & disseminated pyrite
was present. Samples in the upper target zone commonly exhibited a slow white diffuse cut with
trace white streaming cuts when submersed as dry samples in EnTron solvent.
DOLOMITE: mudstone, cream, tan, occasionally white, rarely light gray to gray, very fine
crystalline, friable, sub angular to sub rounded, sucrosic texture, well sorted, dolomitic cement,
very sandy, occasional pale green siltstone, trace nodular pyrite, occasional disseminated pyrite,
common oil stain, trace intergranular porosity, possible fracture porosity
Figure 8: Dolomite in the upper part of the target zone
Samples observed within the lower target zone were dominantly composed of a cream to off
white, light brown colored dolomitic cemented Silty Sandstone with trace to occasional pale
green siltstone which contained moderate amounts of disseminated pyrite and rare amounts of
nodular pyrite. Slow white diffuse cuts with fast white streaming cuts were observed in dry
sample when submersed in EnTron solvent.
Figure 9: Silty sandy dolomite from lower "Target Zone" of the Three Forks formation
Samples observed below the target zone were dominantly composed of a cream to off white,
light brown, trace dark gray colored Claystone with trace to occasional silty sandstone, pale
green siltstone which contained trace amounts of disseminated pyrite and rare amounts of
nodular pyrite.
Figure 10: Claystone from below the base of the target in the Three Forks formation
Gas and Hydrocarbon Shows
While drilling the curve through the Lodgepole formation, total gas levels were recorded with an
average background of 50 to 100 units. Total gas stayed consistent through penetrating the
Upper Bakken Shale marker. Background gas levels stayed consistent through Upper Shale of
the Bakken formation with a peak of 368 units. Background gas levels remained constant upon
penetration of the Middle Member of the Bakken formation without exhibiting significant peaks.
Background gas levels maintained an average of 100 to 200 units through the Middle Member of
the Bakken formation and the Three Forks formation until reaching intermediate casing point at
11,160' MD (~ 10,798' TVD). Gas levels in the lateral fluctuated around 1,000 units, with
connection peaks regularly exceeding 1,500 units. The NOV gas detector showed components of
methane and propane present in the entire lateral.
Hydrocarbon cuts using Entron solvent were moderate though the entire lateral. The target zone
and above was characterized by slow white to yellow diffuse cuts and common fast streaming
cuts. No correlation was found between the strength of the hydrocarbon cuts and the position of
the bit in the 1st Bench Three Forks.
Summary
1) The Drummond 5501 42-21 4T was spud 22 November 2014 approximately 8 miles north of
the town Williston in Williams County, North Dakota. The vertical hole was drilled to a total
depth of 10,280'.
2) A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.3 ­ 10.4 ppg) after surface casing and
through the curve build sections. Salt water brine (9.3 ­ 9.7 ppg) was used in the lateral. The
drilling mud was successful in maintaining stable hole conditions and minimizing washout
through the salt intervals.
3) Two 8 3/4" Reed PDC bits were used to drill the vertical hole from surface casing to a
vertical depth of 10,280' in a total of 62.5 hours. One 8 3/4" Security bit was used to drill
through the curve, landing at a measured depth of 11,160' in 15.5 hours. The entire 10,181'
lateral was drilled in 140.5 hours with two 6" PDC bits used in three separate BHAs.
4) The lateral was targeting an 8' thick section of the 1st Bench of the Three Forks situated 9'
below the Pronghorn.
5) Gas levels remained around 1000 units through most of the drilling operations, with regular
connection peaks of around 1,500 units.
6) The target zone of the Three Forks formation consisted primarily of a cream to light brown
sandy dolomite, with occasional pale green siltstone containing disseminated pyrite, trace lenses
of light red, gray sandy siltstone and trace to rare amounts of nodular pyrite. Moderate white
diffuse cuts with fast white streaming cuts were observed in dry sample when immersed in
EnTron solvent.
7) A strike on the Pronghorn occurred at approximately 16,600' MD. After a failed sidetrack
attempt at 16,330' MD the BHA was changed and the well was successfully sidetracked at
15,733' MD.
8) Drilling operations were concluded at 15:00 hours on 23 December 2014 at a bottom hole
location of 9,888.53' N & 174.63' E of surface location or approximately 314.83' FNL &
2,247.63' FWL, NE NW Sec. 16, T155N, R101W.
9) Exposure to the target window was approximately 70% of the lateral. Oasis Petroleum's
Drummond 5501 42-21 4T awaits completion operations to determine ultimate production
potential.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
