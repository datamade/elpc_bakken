GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Jamestown Federal 7-17H1 as a single lateral targeting
the upper first bench of the Late-Devonian Three Forks formation in McKenzie County, North
Dakota. The surface hole location is in the Southwest one-quarter of the Southeast one-quarter of
Section 8, Township 152 North, Range 99 West (200' FSL & 2,128' FWL). The well then
traverses South to a final bottom hole location 228.8' from the South section line and 2,678.7'
from the West section line of Section 20, Township 152 North, Range 99 West (211.3'FNL &
2,678.7'FWL).
Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on August 5, 2014. Nine and
five eights inch diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2,131' prior to the arrival of
H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8,800' MD on August 5,
2014. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and chromatography
data were monitored and recorded using the NorAm Wellsite Services Advanced Gas Dectector,
AGD 16 from 8,800' MD to 20,737' MD and AGD 13 from 20,737' MD to 21,651' MD.
Geosteering and borehole guidance was accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data,
lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with
directional drilling survey data. Phoenix Technology Services provided the Directional Drilling
(DD) and Measurement While Drilling (MWD) Services.
The build section began in the afternoon of August 11, 2014, at 10,546'MD. Data from the
Jamestown 3-17H, situated Southeast of the Jamestown Federal 7-17H1's surface hole location,
was used as an offset. Mission Canyon, Lodgepole, and Upper Bakken markers and formation
thicknesses suggested the landing TVD for the build section should be lowered from the
prognosed 11,189'TVD (14' in to the 1st bench of the Three Forks) to 11193'TVD. This landing
target was moved up 2' to 11,191' TVD due to correlation of markers throughout the Lodgepole
during the building of the curve. The bit was landed at 11190.7' TVD, 15.26' into the 1st bench
of the Three Forks. The 7" diameter intermediate casing was installed and cemented in place at
11,643' MD & 11,190.1' TVD on August 13, 2014. The well path then proceeded along a
general 180-degree South azimuth.
On August 18, 2014, the Lower Bakken Shale was encountered at 17,620' MD, 11,175.8' TVD,
and decision to sidetrack was ordered. A sidetrack point was chosen at 17,409' MD, and drilling
resumed on the Jamestown Federal 7-17H1 Sidetrack 1 on August 19, 2014 at 14:30 and
progressed to TD on August 22, 2014 at 17:50.
Overall dip for the Jamestown Federal 7-17H1 was projected to be 89.9º degrees. After drilling
out of 7" casing, formation followed the structure map with some changes. An 89.94º degree dip
was observed until 13,700' MD, where formation came up slightly at 90.32º before rolling over
and following a general 89.75 degree dip until 20,000' MD. The formation flattened then started
rolling up to an 90.14º dip until a total depth of 21,651' MD was achieved. Overall dip was
89.94º.

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
Gas shows were the highest at the top of the targeted zone, where background gas remained
about 450 total gas units and formation gas reached as high as 1195 total gas units. Samples
throughout the entirety of the lateral remained fairly consistent, showing a light to dark grey,
slight grey brown, occasionally cream to tan, very fine grained, microcrystalline, slightly
argillaceous, slight to moderately calcareous, Dolomite with poor intercrystalline porosity, and
an even, very dull green fluorescence with a white-blue green diffuse cut and the occasional dull
green to white-blue streaming cut. Please note, the fluorescence and cut, was possibly
contaminated by increasing amounts of torque lube in sample as drilling progressed passed
14,800' MD.
A total depth of 21,651' was reached at 17:50 CST on August 22, 2014. The bit was landed at
the top of the targeted zone, and was in zone 78% of the lateral portion of the well. NorAm
Wellsite Services was held in retainer for the lateral portion of the Jamestown Fed. 6-17H
production well.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
