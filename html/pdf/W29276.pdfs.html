Synopsis
The Drummond 5501 42-21 6T [SE SW Sec. 21 T155N R101W] is located in the Missouri Ridge
Field in Williams County approximately eight miles North of Williston, North Dakota. The well
plan proposed a single lateral leg trending 6.92° north to be drilled from the SE SW corner of
Section 21 to the NW NE corner of Section 16. The lateral portion of the Drummond 5501 42-
21 6T targeted a zone in the dolomite sandstone of the Three Forks formation, approximately 6
to 12 feet below the Pronghorn formation.
Control Wells
One complete well, the Twobins 5501 13-29 4T was used as the control offset for the Drummond
5501 42-21 6T. Throughout the curve progression, gamma-ray data from the Twobins 5501 13-
29 4T was utilized to help determine proper landing depth for the Drummond 5501 42-21 6T.
The Twobins 5501 13-29 4T [NW NE Sec. 29 T155N R101W] was drilled in the early fall of
2014 by the same rig and contractor team prior to the Drummond pad.
A graph was constructed to determine which markers and formation tops could be accurately
used in landing future curves on the Drummond pad. Markers with more than 5 feet difference
between the offsets were watched but not utilized in decision for moving the TVD target of the
curve.

Geologic Assessment
Methods
Geologic supervision of Drummond 5501 42-21 6T was provided by two experienced RPM well
site geologists. Gas and chromatograph levels were measured using NOV Wellsite Gas Watch
real time gas detector and chromatograph system. The NOV Wellsite Gas Watch system uses
non-dispersive infrared and chemical sensor gas detection to evaluate gases liberated from the
formation by the drill bit and carried to the surface by the drilling fluid.
The NOV Wellsite Gas Watch was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder system Pason
provided rate of penetration, on-off bottom and pump strokes to the NOV Wellsite Gas Watch
and received total gas information from the NOV Wellsite Gas Watch for viewing on location
and remotely.
Under the direction of RPM well site geologist, rig crews were instructed to catch lagged drill
cutting samples at 30' intervals from 8,340 to the kick off point (KOP) at 10,303'. Samples were
continuously logged at 50' intervals during the curve and throughout the lateral section.
Sampled drill cuttings were examined wet and dry under a binocular microscope using plain
(broad spectrum) and transmitted light. Cuttings were evaluated for hydrocarbon "cut" by
immersion in carbo-sol (trichloroethylene) and inspection under a UV fluoroscope. 10%
hydrochloric acid and alizarin red were used to determine the calcareous and dolomitic content
of rocks and cementing.
Vertical Operations
Overview
The Drummond 5501 42-21 6T was spud on 08 November 2014 using Patterson 330 drilling rig.
Prior to rig up of RPM mud logging services, a 9 5/8" hole was drilled with fresh water to a
depth of 2,366' and isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J-55 casing cemented to from 2,309' to surface. Bit
#1 & 2 (8 ¾") were used to drill from 2,366' to KOP (10,303').
RPM well site geologists arrived at site on the 8th of November 2014 and began logging the
vertical section at 8,340'.
Diesel invert drilling fluid with a mud weight of ranging from 9.6-10.4 ppg was used during
remainder of the vertical hole and in the curve builds sections.

Directional Operations
RPM provided equipment and personnel for directional services. Ryan Energy Technologies
(Ryan) provided equipment and personnel for MWD services. RPM geologists worked closely
with RPM directional and Ryan MWD teams to formulate steering decisions to maximize
exposure of borehole in the pay zone. RPM well site geologists closely observed MWD gamma-
ray information and penetration rates to aid in steering decisions and dip degree estimations.
Curve Build
Overview
Bit #3 (8 ¾" Security MMD55M) was used to start the curve. It drilled from 10,303' to 10,574'
in 17 hours, averaging 15.9 ft/hr.
Bit #4 (8 ¾" Smith MDi516) was used to drill the middle of the curve. It drilled from 10,574' to
10,834' in 5.5 hours, averaging 35.8 ft/hr.
Bit #5 (8 ¾" Smith F30T) was used to finish the curve. It drilled from 10,834' to 11,160' in 15.5
hours, averaging 21.0 ft/hr.
During the curve progression, isopach data of gamma-ray signatures from the Twobins 5501 13-
29 4T as well as data from another offset, the Glen 1-30 was compared to gamma-ray readings
from the MWD tool from the Base Charles Salt (Table 1). Prospective markers were entered
into the table as they were observed to determine the target landing point. Based on the marker
pick for the Base Charles Salt, the target TVD for the curve was kept the same due to very
minor variation from the off-set well. The curve was completed at a measured depth of
11,160'and a TVD of 10,794' in the 1st Bench of the Three Forks.

Lithology
The top of the "False Bakken" was penetrated at 10,775' MD / 10,703' TVD (-8,464')

The Upper Shale of the Bakken formation [Mississippian ­ Devonian] was drilled at 10,792'
MD / 10,711' TVD (-8,472') with sample returns of typical black, carbonaceous and
petroliferous shale (Figure 4) characterized by gamma-ray values in excess of 250 API counts.
Gas levels remained fairly consistent with average background gas of ~40 units. The Upper
Shale came in 6' low to the Twobins 5501 13-29 4T. Samples were described as:

Lateral
Overview
Drilling fluid consisting of a closed system salt water brine (9.45 ­ 9.6 ppg) was used while
drilling the lateral section. One 6" PDC bit was used to drill the lateral.
Bit #6 (6" Smith Z613), a PDC bit in front of a 1.5° mud motor, was used to drill the lateral from
11,160' to 20,830'. The average ROP for this bit was 92.5 ft/hr in 104.5 hours of use.
Drilling operations were concluded at 10:30 hours on 08 January 2015 at a bottom hole location
of 9,899.97' N & 1,223.31' E of surface location or approximately 319.75' FNL & 1,903.17' FEL,
NW NE Sec. 16 T155N, R101W.
Lithology
The Three Forks formation target zone is represented by an 8 foot thick interval situated 9 to 17
feet below the base of the Pronghorn. The target zone was characterized by an interval of cold
gamma and consisted primarily of sandy dolomite. Every attempt was made to keep the well
bore in the target zone below this low gamma package for as much of the drilling operations as
possible.
The Three Forks formation seen in drilling samples consisted of two primary facies. Samples
observed while drilling the upper target zone (Figure 8) consisted of a cream to tan, occasionally
to light pink sandy dolomite with lenses of purple to gray sandy siltstone. Pale green siltstone
with common disseminated pyrite was present. Common to trace nodular & disseminated pyrite
was present. Samples in the upper target zone commonly exhibited a slow white diffuse cut with
trace white streaming cuts when submersed as dry samples in EnTron solvent.
DOLOMITE: mudstone, cream, tan, occasionally white, rarely light gray to gray, very fine
crystalline, friable, sub angular to sub rounded, sucrosic texture, well sorted, dolomitic cement,
very sandy, occasional pale green siltstone, trace nodular pyrite, occasional disseminated pyrite,
common oil stain, trace intergranular porosity, possible fracture porosity
Figure 8: Dolomite in the upper part of the target zone
Samples observed within the lower target zone (Figure 9) were dominantly composed of a cream
to off white, light brown colored Silty Sandstone with trace to occasional pale green siltstone
which contained moderate amounts of disseminated pyrite and rare amounts of nodular pyrite.
Slow white diffuse cuts with fast white streaming cuts were observed in dry sample when
submersed in EnTron solvent.
Figure 9: Silty sandy dolomite from lower "Target Zone" of the Three Forks formation
Samples observed below the target zone (Figure 10) were dominantly composed of a cream to
off white, light brown, trace dark gray colored Claystone with trace to occasional silty sandstone,
pale green siltstone which contained trace amounts of disseminated pyrite and rare amounts of
nodular pyrite.
Figure 10: Claystone from below the base of the target in the Three Forks formation
Gas and Hydrocarbon Shows
While drilling the curve through the Lodgepole formation, total gas levels were recorded with an
average background of ~ 50 units. Total gas stayed consistent through penetrating the "Upper
Bakken Shale" marker. Background gas levels stayed consistent through Upper Shale of the
Bakken formation at ~60 units. Background gas levels dropped slightly upon penetration of the
Middle Member of the Bakken formation without exhibiting significant peaks. Background gas
levels maintained an average of ~55 units through the Lower Member of the Bakken formation
and the Three Forks formation until reaching intermediate casing point at 11,160' MD (~
10,794' TVD). In the lateral gas levels remained between 250 and 1,000' units regardless of
placement in zone.
Hydrocarbon cuts using Entron solvent were moderate though the entire lateral. The target zone
and above was characterized by slow white to yellow diffuse cuts and common fast streaming
cuts. No correlation was found between the strength of the hydrocarbon cuts and the position of
the bit in the 1st Bench of the Three Forks.
Summary
1) The Drummond 5501 42-21 6T was spud on the 8th of November 2014 north of the town
Williston in Williams County, North Dakota. The vertical hole was drilled to a total depth of
10,303'.
2) A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.6 ­ 10.2 ppg) after surface casing and
through the curve build sections. The drilling mud was successful in maintaining stable
hole conditions and minimizing washout through the salt intervals. Salt water brine (9.45 ­ 9.6
ppg) was used to maintain hydrostatic balance in the lateral to TD.
3) Two 8 3/4" PDC bits were used to drill the vertical hole from surface casing to a vertical
depth of 10,303' in a total of 99.5 hours. Three 8 3/4" bit was used to drill through the curve,
landing at a measured depth of 11,160' in 38 hours. The entire lateral was drilled in 104.5 hours
with one 6" PDC bit.
4) The lateral was targeting a 9' thick section of the middle member of the Three Forks situated
9 feet below the Pronghorn.
5) Gas levels of between 250 and 1000 units were maintained through most of the drilling
operations.
6) The target zone of the Three Forks formation consisted primarily of a cream to light brown
sandy dolomite, with occasional pale green siltstone containing disseminated pyrite, trace lenses
of light red, gray sandy siltstone and trace to rare amounts of nodular pyrite. Moderate white
diffuse cuts with fast white streaming cuts were observed in dry sample when immersed in
EnTron solvent.
7) Drilling operations were concluded at 10:30 hours on 08 January 2015 at a bottom hole
location of 9,899.97' N & 1,223.31' E of surface location or approximately 319.75' FNL &
1,903.17' FEL, NW NE Sec. 16 T155N, R101W.
8) Exposure to the target window as approximately 50% of the lateral with the other half of the
lateral spent mostly in the Claystone below the target window. Oasis Petroleum Drummond 5501
42-21 6T awaits completion operations to determine ultimate production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
