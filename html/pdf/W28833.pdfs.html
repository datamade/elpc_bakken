INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Waters 3B-28HS [NE NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] is
located approximately 10 miles northeast of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota.
The subject well was designed to drill south at a heading of 179.7°, from [250' FNL and
2,225' FWL] Section 28 to SE SW Section 33, T163N, R99W by the Nabors #687
drilling rig (Figure 1). The Waters 3B-28HS is intended to develop oil production from
the Bakken Formation in the Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Waters 3B-28HS offset control was provided by the SM Energy Company Waters 4-28H
[NW NW Section 28, T163N, R101W], the SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HN [NE
NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] and the SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS [NE NW
Section 28, T163N R99W] (Figure). The Waters 4-28H is located approximately 0.22
miles west of the Waters 3B-28HS, and was drilled in November of 2011. The Waters 4-
28H was drilled in the Three Forks Formation, and has produced over 140 thousand
barrels over the life of the well. The Waters 3-28HN is located approximately 100' east
of the Waters 3B-28HS, and was drilled in October of 2014. The Waters 3-28HN was
drilled in the Three Forks Formation and is currently awaiting completion. Waters 3-
28HS is located 150' east of the Waters 3B-28HS, and was drilled in September of 2014
within the Three Forks Formation and is also awaiting completion. Open-hole logs from
these wells were used in forecasting formation tops and the landing target for the Waters
3B-28HS. The Waters 3-28HS was used to provide the closest landing projection through
the Middle Bakken.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On November 4th, 2014, the Waters 3B-28HS was re-entered at 1,479' measured depth
(MD) by Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-
55). The straight hole was drilled to KOP (Kick off Point) at 7,644' MD with a Smith 8
¾" PDC dill bit and a Hunting NBR 1.50º mud motor. A curve assembly including a
NOV 8 ¾" PDC bit and a Sperry 2.25º motor was tripped in and drilled to a landing of
8,431' MD on November 8th, 2014. Diesel invert drilling fluid was used through the
vertical hole and curve build sections (1,479' to 8,431' MD) maintaining a 9.50-9.85 ppg
mud weight for stable hole conditions. Sperry-Halliburton provided night tour directional
drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the vertical hole
provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and lateral drilling.
Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided onsite geologic consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,411' MD. Drilling fluid was changed over to saltwater ranging between
9.65-9.80 ppg. Lateral operations began at 1145 hrs CDT, on November 9th, 2014. The
lateral BHA included a Varel 6" PDC bit, a Hunting 1.5° fixed mud motor, and MWD
survey and gamma tools. This BHA was used to drill the entire lateral section of the
Waters 3B-28HS..
The Waters 3B-28HS was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 18,030' MD, 8,235' true
vertical depth (TVD), on November 13th, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole
coordinates are 10,103.08' south and 56.01' east of surface location or approximately
215.16' FSL and 2,281.01' FWL SE SW Section 33, T163N, R99W. At TD the wellbore
has a closure distance of 10,103.23' at a closure direction of 179.68°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Waters 3B-28HS.
The geology team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and provided onsite geo-steering
consultation. Sample logging and gas monitoring began at a KOP of 7,644' MD. The
wellsite geologists caught drill cuttings at 30' lagged intervals through the curve and 100'
intervals through the lateral. Drill cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope
and complete lithologic descriptions are provided in the appendix of this report. Dried
cuttings were distributed to SM Energy Company and the North Dakota Geological
Survey. A Pason digital gas detector was interfaced with PASON, an electronic data
recorder (EDR). The PASON system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump rates to
the gas detector and distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total
gas was recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began just above the Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian,
Madison Group]. The Lodgepole was drilled starting at 7,696' TVD (-5,448' MSL). The
Lodgepole top came in even with the Waters 3-28HS and contact was indicated by an
increase in rate of penetration and an increase in gamma readings. Limestone and
argillaceous limestone compose the Lodgepole Formation and background gas readings
within this interval were between 30 and 40 units, with significant gas shows as high as
80 units.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was contacted at 8,100' TVD (-5,852'), which
was 3' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by
sample returns of dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale.
Gas shows were as high as 205 units. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to 691
API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was drilled starting at 8,112
TVD (-5,864'), and was 4' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The Middle Bakken top was
marked by an abrupt decrease in both gamma measurements and rate of penetration as the
wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. The silty sandstone was
logged as very light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite cemented and containing trace
amounts of disseminated pyrite (Figure 3). Additionally, trace poor intergranular
porosity and trace spotty light brown oil stain were present.
Figure 3) Calcite cemented silty sandstone from the

An approximately 13' thick interval of highly calcite cemented silty sandstone lies within
the Middle Bakken approximately 14' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. It is
described as very light to medium gray in color, and containing trace amounts of
disseminated pyrite (Figure 4). Also, trace poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty
light brown oil stain were present. Being locally distinct with it's significantly `cooler'
(40-50 API) gamma signature and gas shows the base of this facies was set as the top
marker for the Waters 3B-28HS drilling target.
The greatest hydrocarbon shows recorded in the Middle Bakken occurred within the
highly calcite cemented sandstone facie above the target interval, including shows as high
as 378 units at 11,432' MD, and background gas ranged from 6 to 90 units. Connection
gas shows within the low gamma interval reached 296 units at 11,532' MD.
Hydrocarbon shows within the target interval were lower than those recorded in the
overlying facies. Gas shows reached 238 units at 9,031' MD. Connection gases reached
129 units. Background gas ranged from 6 to 105 units.

GEO-STEERING
Figure 5) Type log of the Middle Bakken gamma signatures as recorded in the SM Energy Company
Waters 3-28HS MWD logs and used to provide log character, in comparison to MWD gamma ray
measurements observed while drilling the Waters 3B-28HS (Andrew Kunz, Wellsite Geologist, Sunburst
Consulting, Inc.).
The target interval within the Middle Bakken was 8' thick, beginning approximately 27'
below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD
gamma ray log of the offset control well, SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS (Figure
5). Gamma ray readings within the 8' target interval ranged from 74-103 API. Landing
depths were adjusted as formation markers were traversed. Calculations from markers
within the Lodgepole were confirmed within the Upper Bakken Shale Member and
provided a target landing depth of 8,141' TVD. The directional drillers were able to
precisely land at 8,141' TVD. MWD-gamma ray data correlated adequately with the
Waters 3-28HS, which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. The steering
team was successful in maintaining 96% of the exposed 9,619' wellbore within target
(Figure 6). Based upon intersections with the cool gamma marker at the upper target
boundary, an overall apparent dip was estimated at 0.55° down.

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Waters 3B-28HS was re-entered on November 4th, 2014 by
Nabors #687. It is a two-section Bakken horizontal well within the Ambrose Field in
Divide County, North Dakota. 96% of the lateral was drilled within the ideal 8' primary
target interval. A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.50-9.85 ppg) during the
vertical and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.65-9.80 ppg) during the lateral was
employed. The target zone was an 8' thick interval that lies beneath a highly calcareous
silty sandstone unit, approximately 27' below the top of the Middle Bakken. The silty
sandstone displayed poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil
staining. Background gas ranged from approximately 6 to 105 units. The Waters 3B-
28HS awaits completion to determine its full production potential.

detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray marker tops have been inserted in
the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined wet and dry under a stereo zoom Binocular microscope.
The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface casing to an intermediate casing point of 8,431' MD, and saltwater brine
was used throughout the lateral to a total depth of 18,030' MD. Samples were collected from 7,644' to 18,030' MD (TD).
Two sets of dried samples were boxed and distributed: one to SM Energy Company and one to the state of North Dakota.
