INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Waters 3B-28HN [NE NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] is
located approximately 10 miles east of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota. The
subject well was designed to drill north at a heading of 358.56°, from [250' FNL and
2,275' FWL] Section 28 to NE NW Section 16, T163N, R99W by the Nabors #687
drilling rig (Figure 1). The Waters 3B-28HN is intended to develop oil production from
the Bakken Formation in the Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Waters 3B-28HN offset control was provided by the SM Energy Company, Waters 4-28H
[NW NW Section 28, T163N, R99W], the SM Energy Company, Waters 3-28HN [NE
NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] and the SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS [NE NW
Section 28, T163N R99W] (Figure 2). The Waters 4-28H is located approximately 0.23
miles west of the Waters 3B-28HN, and was drilled in November of 2011. The Waters 4-
28H was drilled in the Three Forks Formation, and has produced over 136 thousand
barrels over the life of the well. The Waters 3-28HN is located 50' east of the Waters 3B-
28HN and was drilled in October of 2014. The Waters 3-28HN was drilled in the Three
Forks Formation and is currently awaiting completion. The Waters 3-28HS is located
100' east of the Waters 3B-28HN and was drilled in September of 2014 within the Three
Forks Formation and is also awaiting completion. Open-hole logs from these wells were
used in forecasting formation tops and the landing target for the Waters 3B-28HN. The
Waters 3-28HN was used to provide the closest landing projection for the Middle
Bakken.
Figure 2) Map showing the location of the SM Energy Company Waters 3B-28HN and
the location of the three wells used for offset control (NDIC Oil & Gas Division, Arc
IMS online).
ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On October 18th, 2014 the Waters 3B-28HN was re-entered at 1,478' measured depth
(MD) by Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-
55). The straight hole was drilled to 5,679' MD with an IDS 8 ¾" PDC dill bit and a
Hunting NBR 1.50º mud motor, at which point the assembly was tripped out for a low
penetration rate. A Hughes 8 ¾" PDC bit and an identical Hunting NBR mud motor
drilled the remaining vertical hole to a KOP (kick off point) of 7,685' MD. Three bottom
hole assemblies (BHA) were used during the curve operation. A HDBS PDC and a
Hunting NBR 2.38º motor drilled 129' to 7,814' MD and was changed out for a Hughes
VGD-20 tri-cone bit and a Sperry 2.25º motor due to low ROP and unacceptable build
rates. This assembly drill 357' to 8,171' MD where it was replaced with an identical
assembly due to low penetration rates. The final assembly drilled the curve to landing at
8,415' MD on October 24th, 2014. Diesel invert drilling fluid was used through the
vertical hole and curve build sections (1,478' to 8,415' MD) maintaining a 9.65-10.15
ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions. Sperry-Halliburton provided night tour
directional drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the
vertical hole provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and
lateral drilling. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided onsite geologic consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,393' MD. Drilling fluid was changed over to saltwater ranging between
9.70-9.80 ppg. Lateral operations began at 900 hrs CDT, on October 26th, 2014. The
lateral BHA included a Varel 6" PDC bit, a Hunting 1.5° fixed mud motor, and MWD
survey and gamma tools. This BHA was used to drill the entire lateral section of the
Waters 3B-28HN.
The Waters 3B-28HN was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 18,447' MD, 8,027' true
vertical depth (TVD), on November 1st, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole
coordinates are 10,599.52' north and 296.11' west of surface location or approximately
221.72' FNL and 1,978.89' FWL NE NW Section 16, T163N, R99W. At TD the
wellbore has a closure distance of 10,603.65' at a closure direction of 358.40°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Waters 3B-
28HN. The geology team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and provided onsite geo-
steering consultation. Sample logging and gas monitoring began at a KOP of 7,685' MD.
The wellsite geologists caught drill cuttings at 30' lagged intervals through the curve and
100' intervals through the lateral. Drill cuttings were examined under a binocular
microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are provided in the appendix of this
report. Dried cuttings were distributed to SM Energy Company and the North Dakota
Geological Survey. A Pason digital gas detector was interfaced with PASON, an
electronic data recorder (EDR). The PASON system provided hole depth, bit depth and
pump rates to the gas detector and distributed the detector's total gas readings across
location. Total gas was recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began in the Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison
Group]. The Lodgepole was drilled at 7,692' TVD (-5,444'). The Lodgepole top came in
9' high to the Waters 3-28HN and contact was indicated by an increase in rate of
penetration and an increase in gamma readings. Limestone and argillaceous limestone
compose the Lodgepole Formation and background gas readings within this interval were
between 83 to 133 units, with significant gas shows as high as 196 units.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was contacted at 8,098' TVD (-5,850'), which
was 2' high to the Waters 3-28HN. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by
sample returns of dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale.
Gas shows were as high as 235 units and connection gas shows were as high as 517 units
within in this interval. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to 695 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was drilled at 8,111' TVD (-
5,863'), and was 2' high to the Waters 3-28HN. The Middle Bakken top was marked by
an abrupt decrease in both gamma measurements and rate of penetration as the wellbore
passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. The silty sandstone was logged as very
light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite cemented and containing trace amounts of
disseminated pyrite (Figure 3). Additionally, trace poor intergranular porosity and a
trace spotty light brown oil stain were present.

An interval of highly calcite cemented silty sandstone lies approximately 16'-18' below
the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. Varying in thickness, this interval ranged from

gray in color and containing trace amounts of disseminated pyrite (Figure 4). Also, trace
poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty light brown oil stain were present. Being
locally distinct with it's significantly `cooler' (40-50 API) gamma signature and gas
shows the base of this facies was set as the target top for the Waters 3B-28HN.

The greatest hydrocarbon shows recorded in the Middle Bakken occurred in the highly
calcite cemented sandstone facies, including gas shows as high as 303 units at 15,915'
MD, and background gas ranged from 11 to 55 units. Connection gas shows within the
sandstone reached 341 units at 16,888' MD. Hydrocarbon shows within the target
interval were lower than in the overlying highly calcite cemented sandstone. Gas shows
reached 240 units at 15,984' MD and connection gases reached 181 units. Background

GEO-STEERING
Figure 5) Type log of the Middle Bakken gamma signatures as recorded in the SM Energy Company,

The target interval within the Middle Bakken was 10' thick, beginning approximately 22'
below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the gamma
ray log of the offset control well, SM Energy Company, Waters 3-28HN (Figure 5).
Gamma ray readings within the 10' target interval ranged from 74-103 API. Landing
depths were adjusted as formation markers were traversed. Calculations from markers
within the Lodgepole were confirmed within the Upper Bakken Shale Member and
provided a target landing depth of 8,147' TVD. The directional drillers were able to land
at 8,149' TVD, which was 2' low to the planned target depth. However, due to localize
thinning of the Middle Bakken, this ultimately yielded a landing depth 7'-8' below the
drilling target base. MWD gamma ray correlated adequately with the Waters 3-28HN,
which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. The steering team was
successful in maintaining 78% of the exposed 10,054' wellbore within target (Figures 6
& 7). Based upon intersections with the cool gamma marker at the upper target boundary,
an overall apparent dip was estimated at 0.59° up.

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Waters 3B-28HN was re-entered on October 18, 2014 by
Nabors #687. It is a two-section Bakken horizontal well within the Ambrose Field in
Divide County, North Dakota. 78% of the lateral was drilled within the ideal 10' primary
target interval. A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.65-10.15 ppg) during the
vertical and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.70-9.80 ppg) during the lateral was
employed. The target zone was a 10' thick interval that lies beneath a highly calcareous
silty sandstone unit, approximately 22' below the top of the Middle Bakken. The silty
sandstone displayed poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil
staining. Background gas ranged from approximately 19 to 61 units. The Waters 3B-
28HN awaits completion to determine its full production potential.

Waters 3B-28HN
Sunburst geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals through the curve and 100' intervals through the lateral. A
detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray marker tops have been inserted in
the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined wet and dry under a stereo zoom Binocular microscope.
Sample cuts were examined with Carbo-sol (trichloroethylene) under UV light. The drilling fluid was oil based invert from
surface casing to an intermediate casing point of 8,415' MD, and saltwater brine was used throughout the lateral to a total
depth of 18,447' MD. Samples were collected from 7,685' to 18,447' MD (TD). Two sets of dried samples were boxed and
distributed: one to SM Energy Company and one to the state of North Dakota.
