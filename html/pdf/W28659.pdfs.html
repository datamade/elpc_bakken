INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the November 02, 2014 to November 14, 2014 drilling of the Scorio 159-101-10-3H-1.
Surface location is 275' FSL x 2375' FEL; SWSE Sec. 10-T159N-R101W. The projected path is to the north
with a proposed Vertical Section Azimuth of 2.25 °. The Offset well used for correlation include the Scorio 159-
101-11-2H-1 and Scorio 159-101-12-1H-1. Straight hole, curve and lateral surveys were taken with Scientific
Drilling MWD.
Samples were collected and gas monitored from 6200' MD in the vertical hole. The kick off point for the build
section was at 8847' MD. The Upper Bakken Shale was encountered at 9352 MD, 64.44° Inc, 29.50° Azi,
9251.90' TVD, -7102.90' SS. The Middle Bakken was encountered at 9388 MD, 68.10° Inc, 29.19° Azi,
9266.37' TVD, -7117.37' SS. The build section was TD at 9572' MD, 9300.37' TVD, -7151.37' SS, with
intermediate casing set to 9560' MD; 9299.47' TVD (-7150.47' SS), 466.95' VS; 457.90' N; 239.64' E, Legal:
732.90' FSL x 2135.36' FEL, SWSE Sec. 10-T159N-R101W.
Hunt Oil Company requested the Hotwire be calibrated to (same as Bowman 1-16H) 1% Methane = 50u =
10,000 ppm v. normal Williston Basin calibration of 1% Methane = 100u. The chromatograph was left at 1%
test gas = 100u. Background gas prior to the Upper Bakken Shale (9352' MD) averaged 41u. Gas in the Upper
Bakken Shale with an average of 126u and a max of 439u. While drilling the vertical and curve section the
Invert "MegaDrill" mud weight was held between 9.6 and 10.0 lbs. Saltwater used as the drilling fluid in the
lateral with a mud weight held between 9.7 and 10.0 lbs. Background gas in the Middle Bakken formation
fluctuated throughout the well between 0-185u. Connection gas within the Middle Bakken fluctuated between
55-270u. One possible fracture gas was observed with 520u at 14701`MD. Flares were not observed in the
lateral.
The Hunt Exploration team identifies three main sequences within the Middle Bakken. The first is identified as
the "Hot Dolomite" (top of Middle Bakken to the gamma marker); the second interval (not always present) is
interpreted as a cleaner "shoal" deposit (below the gamma ray marker and above the third sequence). The
third sequence is identified as the "Mottled Dolomite" (base of the gamma ray marker to top of the Lower
Bakken Shale). The "Hot Dolomite" lithology was represented in this well as a Dolomitic-Limestone and
sandstone. The Dolomitic Limestone is mottled medium gray/medium brown/cream/tan, microcrystalline
Mudstone, firm, very argillaceous, very sandy composed of well sorted medium subangular Quartzose grains/

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media

Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
