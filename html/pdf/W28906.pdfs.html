GENERAL:
On September 6, 2014 Continental Resource's Margaurite 3-15H was "spudded" as a Middle
Bakken formation, 1280 spaced, lateral well. The subject well is being drilled in the Sanish field,
located about 10 miles north of New Town, in Mountrail County in North Dakota. New Town is
located in the northeastern portion of the Williston Basin. Surface location of the Margaurite 3-15H is
450' FNL & 1000' FEL, NENE of Section 15, T153N - R93W. The Margaurite 3-15H was planned
with an ultimate goal of oil production in the Middle Bakken member of the Bakken formation. The
horizontal well-path was planned to follow structure with an estimated 89.70° average formation dip to
total depth at 20,225' MD. The well bore was planned to be drilled to the south across section 15 and
section 22 following an azimuth of 181.56°. The Margaurite 3-15H well is located on the same pad as
the Margaurite 1-15H and Margaurite 2-15H1 wells.
OFFSET INFORMATION:
The main offset well for drilling Continental Resource's Margaurite 3-15H well was provided
from Whiting's ­ Curren 11-14H well, API# 33-061-00969. The Curren 11-14H well is located in
Section 14, T153N ­ R93W.
ENGINEERING:
The Margaurite 3-15H surface hole was pre-spudded with a smaller surface rig prior to Cyclone
10 moving on location. Pad height of the Margaurite 3-15H was 2,072' above mean sea level with the
Cyclone #10 kelly bushing located at 2,092' above mean sea level, 20' above ground level.
Margaurite 3-15H was drilled with fresh water from surface to 2,054' MD. From surface
casing, the Margaurite 3-15H was drilled with oil based mud. Oil based mud was used from 2,054'
MD to 10,765' MD. A switch was then made to brine mud from 10,765' MD to a total depth of
20,192' MD.
Initially, the Margaurite 3-15H was drilled as a 13 ½" hole to 2,054' MD, and 9 5/8" 36# J-55
casing was set to a measured depth of 2,054' through the Fox Hills formation into the Pierre formation.
The Margaurite 3-15H then progressed as an 8 ¾" hole from the upper part of the Pierre formation
until landing of the curve in the Middle Bakken member. Kick off point (KOP) was reached in the
Lodgepole formation on September 10, 2014, 0138 hrs at 9,971' MD (9,968.88' TVD). At KOP, a trip
was made to pick up a LEAM mud motor with a 2.5° fixed bend and a new PDC bit. Directional work
during the curve was performed by Nick Davis and Robert Horton from LEAM directional services.
The curve from vertical to horizontal began on September 10, 2014 at 1700 hrs and was drilled starting
from 9,971' MD (9,968.88' TVD) and landed horizontal (88.15°) at 10,765' MD (10,459.50' TVD).
The curve was drilled holding an average build rate of 11.10° per 100 ft and was landed on September
11, 2014 at 1033 hrs. No mechanical problems were encountered while drilling the curve.

GEOLOGICAL EVALUATION:
Geologic supervision of the Continental Resource's Margaurite 3-15H well was provided by
Geo-Link, Inc with a two-man mudlogging / geo-steering consulting service. Geologic
evaluation began just above the Charles formation and continued until TD at the end of the
lateral in the Middle Bakken member. MSI's Red Box (chromatograph) was used to record gas
levels at the drilling rig's shakers. The Red Box gas data was integrated into Pason's EDR
(Electronic Data Recorder) system. Also integrated into Pason's EDR system was gamma ray
data provided by Scientific MWD services. Pason's EDR provided drill rate, Red Box gas data,
gamma and circulation data for viewing on location and remote via Pason's Datahub. Lagged
samples for the well were caught in 30' intervals from 8,200' measured depth until total depth
was reached at 20,192' measured depth. Cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope
and UV fluoroscope. Cuttings were tested with hydrochloric acid and alizarin to distinguish
between carbonates. Acetone was used to examine oil cut from the samples. The samples were
logged in MPlot, MSI's mudlogging program. After sample evaluation, one set of dried
cuttings was saved for the State of North Dakota.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
