INTRODUCTION
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Skjermo 2-14-163-102 [NW NE Section 14,
T163N, R102W] is located approximately 5.5 miles northwest of the town of Fortuna in
Divide County, North Dakota. The subject well was designed to drill south at a heading
of 181.67°, from NW NE [250' FNL and 2,050' FEL] Section 14, T163N, R102W by the
Nabors #49 drilling rig (Figure 1). The Skjermo 2-14-163-102 is intended to develop oil
production from the Devonian Three Forks Formation in the Skjermo Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Skjermo 2-14-163-102 offset control was provided by the American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102 [NW NW Section 15, T163N, R102W], the Kodiak Oil &
Gas (USA) Inc., Drawbond 11-10 [NE SW Section 10, T163N, R102W], the Kodiak Oil
& Gas (USA) Inc., Nielsen 14-12 [SE SW Section 12, T163N, R102W], and the Sun
Exploration & Production Co., Wehrman 1 [NE NE Section 13, T163N, R102W] (Figure
2). The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102 is located 1.40 miles west
of the Skjermo 2-14-163-102, and was drilled in February of 2014. Data provided by the
North Dakota Industrial Commission states that production information is on confidential
status for the Ella 4-15-163-102. The Kodiak Oil & Gas (USA) Inc., Drawbond 11-10 is
located approximately 1.31 miles west-northwest of the Skjermo 2-14-163-102, and was
drilled in May of 2007 in the Red River pool, and has produced 74 barrels of oil. The
Kodiak Oil & Gas (USA) Inc., Nielsen 14-12 is located approximately 0.81 miles east-
northeast of the Skjermo 2-14-163-102, and was a dry well drilled in January 2006 in the
Red River Pool. The Sun Exploration & Production Co., Wehrman 1 is located 1.27 miles
east of the Skjermo 2-14-163-102, and is a dry well that was drilled in September 1984.
Open-hole logs from these wells were used in forecasting formation tops and the landing
target for the Skjermo 2-14-163-102. The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-
163-102 was used to provide log character for the Three Forks.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On July 25th, 2014 Nabors #49 spud the Skjermo 2-14-163-102. The surface hole was
drilled to 1,434' MD and isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55) to 1,434' MD. The
vertical BHA was removed at 6,171' MD due to low ROP. A new vertical BHA with a new
8 ¾" bit and motor was used to 7,380' MD, which was flat to the planned kick-off point
(KOP), and the rig crew picked up the curve BHA which included an 8 ¾" PDC bit and a
2.12° motor. The curve BHA was drilled to a depth of 7,720' MD where is was removed
due to poor build rates. A new curve BHA with an 8 ¾" PDC bit and a 2.38° motor was
then used to 7,739' MD where it was removed due to motor failure. A third curve BHA
with an 8 ¾" PDC bit and a 2.38° motor was drilled to a landing depth of 8,152' MD.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was used through the vertical hole and curve build sections
(1,434' to 8,152' MD) maintaining a 9.3-9.6 ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions.
Millennium Directional Service provided directional drilling and measurement-while
drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the vertical hole provided tie-in orientation of the
wellbore for the curve build and lateral. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided well site
geology consultation.
After curve landing, a 7" intermediate casing string (29# HCL-80, 32# HCL-80 and 32# P-
110) was set and cemented to 8,132' MD. Drilling fluid was changed out to saltwater
ranging between 9.1-9.4 ppg. The lateral BHA included a 6" PDC bit, a 1.5° fixed mud
motor, and MWD survey and gamma tools. Lateral operations began at 1405 hrs CDT, on
August 2nd, 2014. The lateral BHA was used from 8,152' to 17,262' MD, then removed
for low ROP and motor problems. A new BHA with 6" PDC bit and new 1.5° fixed mud
motor was used to 17,820' MD (TD).
The Skjermo 2-14-163-102 was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 17,820' MD, 7,933.02' true
vertical depth (TVD), at 1532 CDT August 8th, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole
coordinates are 10,080.35' south and 299.90' west of surface location or approximately
266.95' FSL and 2,349.90' FEL SW SE Section 23, T163N, R102W. Projected to TD, the
wellbore has a closure distance of 10,084.81' at a closure direction of 181.70°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Skjermo 2-14-163-
102. The geology well site team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and geo-steered.
Logging samples and gas monitoring started at 5,550' MD. The rig crews caught drill
cuttings in 30' lagged intervals per instruction from well site geologists. Drill cuttings
were examined under a trinocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are
provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were collected and sent to the North
Dakota Geological Survey.
A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. TGC (total gas with
chromatograph) gas detector was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder (EDR).
The Pason system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump rates to the gas detector and
distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total gas was recorded in units
(100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air). In addition to cuttings and gas measurements
geological data such as gamma and ROP was interpreted for the use of geo-steering
throughout the well.
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began in the Piper Formation [Jurassic] above the Spearfish
Formation [Triassic/Permian]. The Spearfish Formation was penetrated at 5,609' TVD
(-3,460), which is 20' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102.
The Spearfish is composed of medium red brown to orange siltstone and silty sandstone in
this area. Background gas (C1) ranged from 12 to 16 units with no gas shows.
The Kibbey Formation of the Big Snowy Group [Mississippian] was penetrated at 5,836'
TVD (-3,687'), which is 15' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-
163-102. The Kibbey consists of siltstone, silty sandstone and anhydrite. There was also a
layer of limestone ("Kibbey Lime") that was off white to cream colored mudstone, which
was encountered at 5,959' TVD (-5,959'). Background gas (C1) was 14 to 26 units with no
shows.
The Charles Formation [Mississippian] was penetrated at 6,080' TVD (-3,931') and is
19' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. The upper Poplar
interval of the Charles consists of salt, anhydrite, dolomitic limestone, argillaceous
limestone and limestone. Salt intervals were marked by an increase in rate of penetration
along with a decrease in weight on bit. Background gas (C1) within the Poplar was 13 to
29 units and no gas shows were recorded. The Ratcliffe interval of the lower Charles
Formation consists of limestone, argillaceous limestone, argillaceous dolomite and
anhydrite. The top of the Ratcliffe was penetrated at 6,446' TVD (-4,297') and is 7' low
to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. The Ratcliffe top is within
a limestone and dolomite facies below an anhydrite unit. Background gas within the
Ratcliffe were within 12 to 23 units. The Base Last Salt interval was not present in this
area. The Midale interval of the lower Charles Formation consists of dolomite and
limestone. The top of the Midale was penetrated at 6,634' TVD (-4,485') and is 23' low
to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. Gas shows within the
Midale reached as high as 43 units, and background gases were within 15 to 33 units.
The Mission Canyon Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] consists primarily of
argillaceous limestone and limestone. The Mission Canyon top was penetrated at 6,737'
TVD (-4,588'), and is 2' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-
102. The top of the Mission Canyon was marked by a transition from argillaceous
limestone to a cream to light gray brown limestone, which can be identified by a decrease
in gamma. Limestone intervals at the base of the Mission Canyon have spotty dark brown
to black oil staining. Gas shows (C1-C2) within the Mission Canyon reached 56 units and
background gases were within 22 to 38 units.
The Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] was penetrated at 7,080'
TVD (-4,931'), and is 16' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-
102. The top of the Lodgepole was marked by a transition from argillaceous limestone to
limestone. Limestone intervals at the top of the Lodgepole have spotty dark brown to black
oil staining and argillaceous limestone intervals at the Lodgepole 1 marker have spotty
medium brown oil staining. Background gas readings (C1-C2) were 13 to 54 units and gas
shows (C1-C3) are as high as 283 units, and connection/survey gas measurements (C1-C2)
reached 113 units. Trip gas measurements (C1) reached 100 units.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was penetrated at 7,789' TVD (-5,640'), which
is 15' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. The top of the
Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample returns of dark brown to black,
carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Survey gas shows (C1-C3) were recorded
up to 260 units. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to 565 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian-Devonian] was penetrated at 7,796' TVD
(-5,647'), and is 12' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102.
The Middle Bakken top was marked by a large decrease in gamma and rate of penetration
as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. The siltstone was
described as light gray to light gray brown, sub blocky, and calcite cemented. Silty
sandstone was described as light gray brown and cream to light brown in color, very fine
grained and calcite cemented. Also, trace poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty
light brown oil stain were present. Gas shows (C1-C3) within this interval reached 149
units, and background gas (C1-C2) was within 54 to 66 units. Connection gas shows
reached 95 units.
The Lower Bakken Shale [Devonian] was penetrated at 7,855' TVD (-5,706'), which was
16' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. It was very similar
to the Upper Bakken Shale Member except for being slightly lighter in color. The Lower
Shale Member also has a sharp increase in gamma, up to 689 API. Recorded gas shows
(C1-C3) in the Lower Bakken Shale reached 383 units, and connection gas shows reached
400 units.
The Three Forks Formation [Devonian] was drilled at 7,878' TVD (-5,729') and was 18'
low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102. The upper 5' of the
Three Forks was a dolomitic silty sandstone (Figure 3). The silty sandstone was cream to
light gray or peach in color and composed of very fine subrounded grains and trace to
occasional amounts of disseminated pyrite. Trace poor intercrystalline porosity and trace
spotty light brown oil stain was also present.
Sandstone samples were cut with
trichloroethylene (Carbo-Sol) and examined with a fluoroscope. The cut samples showed
weak pale yellow, slow to fast, diffuse and streaming cut fluorescence.

An approximately 7' thick interval of shale (Figure 4) lies beneath the sandstone. It was
pale gray to pale green, locally sandy and/or silty, soft to firm with an earthy texture,
contains occasional to common amounts of disseminated and nodular pyrite, and localized
sandstone stringers. It also displays no visible porosity and minor oil staining in coarser

The greatest hydrocarbon shows (C1-C4) recorded in the Three Forks occurred in the
dolomite cemented sandstone facies, including gas shows as high as 1,032 units at 11,756'
MD, and background gas ranged from 70 to 268 units. Connection/survey gas shows
within the sandstone reached 1,011 units at 11,763' MD. Hydrocarbon shows in the shale
are generally lower than in the sandstone, but localized zones rich in either very sandy
shale or sandstone produced significant shows. Gas shows reached 462 units at 17,169'
MD. Connection/survey gases reached 546 units at 17,134' MD. Background gas ranged
from 13 to 100 units.
GEO-STEERING
The target interval within the Three Forks was 8' thick, beginning approximately 3' below
the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD gamma
ray log of the offset control well, American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-102
(Figure 5). Gamma ray readings within the upper 3' of the target zone displayed a gamma
signature of 36-69 API. The lower 5' displayed a gamma signature of 65-125 API. The
contact of the higher gamma in the bottom of the target and the cool gamma marker at the
top of the target was used for dip calculations. The well plan called for a landing 10' below
the top of the Three Forks and landing depths were adjusted as formation markers were
traversed. Calculations from markers within the lower Lodgepole and Bakken were
adjusted with the Three Forks top and provided a target landing depth of 7,888' TVD (10'
below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale), after an original landing depth of 7,891' TVD
was proposed while drilling in the upper Lodgepole Formation. The directional drillers
were able to land at 7,888' TVD, which was flat to the planned target depth. MWD-gamma
ray correlated adequately with the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Ella 4-15-163-
102, which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. Geo-steering efforts were
made to stay within 3' above and 5' below the base of the cool gamma marker per direction
of the operations geologist. The steering team was successful in maintaining 65% of the
exposed 9,668' lateral wellbore in target, and about 71% within 1' of target window. Based
on intersections with the cool gamma marker, an overall apparent dip was estimated at
0.62° down (Figure 6).

SUMMARY
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Skjermo 2-14-163-102 was spud on July 25th,
2014 by Nabors #49. It is a two section Three Forks horizontal well within the Skjermo
Field of Divide County, North Dakota. 65% of the lateral was drilled in the ideal 8' primary
target interval. A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.3-9.6 ppg) during the vertical
and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.1-9.4 ppg) during the lateral was utilized. The
target zone was an 8' thick sandstone and shale interval which included an upper
approximately 3' thick zone of cool gamma (sandstone), with the desired target being near
the base of the cool gamma (36-69 API). The sandstone displayed poor intergranular
porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil staining. The shale displayed no visible
porosity and minor spotty very light brown oil staining within coarser zones. Background
gas (C1-C4) ranged from approximately 70 to 268 units within the sandstone, and 13 units
to 100 units within the shale. The Skjermo 2-14-163-102 awaits completion to determine
its full production potential.

Lithology
Rig crews caught lagged samples in 30' intervals under the supervision of Sunburst Consulting, Inc.,
well site geologists. A detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page.
Gamma ray marker tops have been inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples
were examined wet and dry under a stereo zoom trinocular microscope. Sample cuts were examined
with Carbo-sol (trichloroethylene) under UV light. The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface
casing to an intermediate casing point of 8,152' MD, and saltwater brine was used throughout the
lateral: from intermediate casing to a total depth of 17,820' MD. Samples were collected from 5,550' to
17,820' MD (TD). One set of dried samples was boxed and distributed to the state of North Dakota.
