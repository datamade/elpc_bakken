Introduction
XTO Energy Inc. Moses 21X-9B [NE NW Section 9, T151N, R98W] is located approximately 8
miles north of Watford City, North Dakota. The Moses 21X-9B is a single-section horizontal Middle
Bakken within the Garden Field of McKenzie County, North Dakota. The well will serve to develop
Section 9 with a southerly transecting lateral. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole Formation
and landed within the Middle Bakken drilling target. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will
then be used in tandem to maintain maximum exposure to a 20' target window.

Geology
Vertical Gas Shows & Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Tyler Formation. Formations with notable oil
staining and gas shows include the Mission Canyon, Upper Bakken Shale, and Middle Bakken. Of
notable significance was the elevated gas logged in all vertical drilling logged by well-site geology
including a trip gas of 4,272u observed when tripping for low ROP at 8,545' near the top of the Kibbey
lime.
The Mission Canyon was drilled at a MD of 9,638' and a TVD of 9,637', which was 1' low to prognosis
and 15' low to Lundeen 31X-9C. The Mission Canyon consisted of primarily limestone, grading to
argillaceous limestone with occasional dolomitic limestone and anhydrite in its upper sections. The
Mission Canyon had significant background gas and multiple gas shows. Background gas over 200 units
was common with large gas shows reaching 700 and a possible fracture gas of 1,054 units at 9,750'.
Trace to rare spotty brown dead oil staining was seen throughout the Mission Canyon.
The Upper Bakken Shale (UBS) was drilled at a MD of 11,158' and a TVD of 11,011', which was 2'
low to prognosis and even to Lundeen 31X-9C. The UBS was composed of black, carbonaceous and
petroliferous shale, characterized by very high gamma ray values (>300API). Background gas within the
UBS was commonly over 4,300 units with shows reaching 4,700 units.
The Middle Bakken was logged at a MD of 11,226' and a TVD of 11,032', which was 14' low to
prognosis and even to Lundeen 31X-9C. The Middle Bakken consisted of light to medium gray-brown
with varying amounts of white to cream and light to medium gray, very fine grained, well to very well
sorted, and moderately, calcite-cemented silty sandstone. The 20' target zone of the Middle Bakken
exhibited trace to occasional oil staining and trace intergranular porosity.
Geo-steering
Using control well logs, XTO geologists chose a 20' target window within a 45'-thick Middle Bakken.
The top of the target window was located 14' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale.
The initial landing target of 11,042' TVD was lowered to 11,047' TVD after offset wells indicated an
adjustment was necessary. The well was successfully landed at 11,050' TVD, 3' below the new target,
placing the bit 18' below the top of the Middle Bakken (Figure 2). The isopach map and offset profiles of
the Middle Bakken, provided by XTO Energy, Inc., projected the apparent formation dip to be a slight up-
dip from the curve landing until completion of the lateral. The observed final gross overall TVD change
from curve landing to the end of the lateral was estimated at 11.32 over 4,432', which gave an overall dip
of 0.15° up (Figure 3).

Lateral Gas & Oil Shows
During the lateral, background gas values ranged from 125 to 8,715 units (Figure 4). The highest gas
show was logged at 12,936' MD and reached 8,515 units against 10.00 ppg mud. Flow diversion through
the gas buster began at 11,676' MD causing gas values to slightly decrease. Throughout the lateral, trace
to occasional, medium brown spotty oil staining was seen in collected samples.

Bottom-Hole Location
Total depth resulted in a closure azimuth of 172.72° and a closure distance of 4,798.36'. The bottom-hole
location is projected to be 4,759.71' south and 607.83' east of the surface location or approximately
248.19' FSL and 1,982.83' FWL, SE SW Section 9, T151N, R98W. The hole was prepared for liner and
multi-stage fracture stimulation directly after drilling operations concluded.
Summary
The Moses 21X-9B efficiently reached TD after 17 days of drilling, on December 19, 2014. MWD
gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were successfully utilized to expose 100% of the lateral
borehole to the Middle Bakken and 100% within the targeted zone. The Moses 21X-9B awaits
completion operations to maximize its production potential.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
