Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Pipeline Notification
This well will be connected to Hiland's low pressure gathering system in Dunn County, North
Dakota. Oxy's acreage that comprises the spacing unit (SU) for the Kelsey Elizabeth 1-7-6H-
141-95 is dedicated to Hiland. Under terms of the contract, Hiland is obligated to install the
facilities that are required to connect these wells to its gathering system. Oxy provides (on a
monthly basis) to Hiland a drilling, completion and estimated first production date for all wells
that are scheduled to be drilled in the foreseeable future. In addition, Oxy and Hiland have
conference calls to discuss connection status of wells and changes to Oxy's drilling and
completion schedules when needed.
Gathering System
Gas from this pad will be gathered on Hiland's low pressure WC-Manning Gathering System
and processed at Hiland's Watford City Processing Plant located in Section 6, T151N, R102W,
McKenzie County, North Dakota.
In addition to Hiland, ONEOK owns and operates a low pressure gathering line located on the
same pad. ONEOK's lines, along with Hiland's, are shown on the attached maps.
The production facilities for the Kelsey Elizabeth 1-7-6H-141-95 well will be located adjacent to
Oxy's Wolberg 21-18H production facilities which are connected to ONEOK's low pressure
gathering system. It will require approximately two and one-half miles of pipe to connect the
Kelsey Elizabeth 1-7-6H-141-95 well to Hilands's low pressure gathering system.
Existing Lines
At the terminus of the metering and associated piping at the well's production facilities, Hiland
will construct approximately two and one-half miles of 6.625" outside diameter (OD) gathering
line. This 6.625" OD gathering line will connect to Hiland's 10.75" OD gathering line. The
10.75" OD gathering line traverses approximately two miles and then connects to a 12.75" OD
gathering line that traverses approximately six miles and connects to the inlet header at Hiland's
Manning Compressor Station located in Section 29, T142N, 96W, Dunn County, ND. The table
below shows the estimated capacities and current flows of the gathering lines connecting this
pad.
Hiland stated in a recent meeting that their gathering system in this area had sufficient capacity
to gather gas from wells Oxy currently plans on drilling on the dedicated acreage. Hiland further
stated that the Manning compressor station was designed to allow for the installation of
additional compressors when required as volume increases on the gathering system.
Flowback Strategy
After the fracture treatment, this well will be flowed through a choke to test production tanks.
Choke size will depend on final shut in pressure post frac and well pressure prior to opening the
choke. The choke will be adjusted up or down based on flowing pressure, liquid rates and sand
returns, to prevent clogging manifold chokes. During flowback, the water chlorides and sand
content will be monitored. When the water chlorides are greater than 100,000 ppm and the
produced fluids contain no sand, the well will be turned to production facilities. It is anticipated
that Hiland will have its gathering line extension constructed and Oxy will have its production
facilities constructed before flowback starts, and gas sales should start as soon as the well starts
flowing through the production facilities.

Plants are expensive
Uneconomical to operate when volume declines
Residue gas is still flared

Savings if current generation's fuel supply is diesel
Only a portion of gas is consumed operating the generator, remainder of gas will
