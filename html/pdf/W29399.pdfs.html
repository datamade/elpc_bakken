INTRODUCTION
The Oxy USA, Inc. State Gresz 3-26-35H-143-98
[NENW Sec 26, T143N, R98W] is located
approximately 6 miles northeast of Fairfield, North Dakota. The subject well was drilled in Billings County,
ND by the Nabors X-18 rig in the Hungry Man Butte Field of the Bakken pool. The subject well was
planned with one approximately 9,187' [Intermediate casing to projected total depth (TD)] long southerly
lateral bore with intent to intersect regional hydraulic fracture trends that might enhance reservoir quality
within the Middle Bakken. OXY USA intends to develop oil production in the late Devonian Middle Bakken
target porosity zone with a stratigraphy consisting of siltstone, silty sandstone and limestone with
depositional lithofacies ranging from onshore terrestrial to offshore marine deposits.
ENGINEERING
Surface, Vertical & Curve Operations:
A 13 1/2" surface hole was drilled on October 16, 2014 by Nabors X-18. Surface casing was set
at 2,543'. The vertical hole operations commenced on November 1, 2014 with Baker Hughes providing
equipment and personnel for MWD and directional drilling services.
The first vertical Bottom Hole Assembly (BHA), consisting of MWD tools, 1.50º bend motor and
MR616MCB PDC bit, drilled to a depth of 8,530'. At this depth, a trip was made due to a low rate of
penetration (ROP). Upon pulling this assembly, a new PDC bit (Smith MMDi516) and new set of MWD
tools were picked up. This BHA drilled to 8,754' before being pulled due to a MWD failure. The next BHA
consisted of the same bit and motor, along with a new set of MWD tools. It drilled to 9,953' and was
pulled for a low ROP.
At this time, the first curve BHA was picked up. It consisted of a Reed E1202-A11B bit and Ultra
X-treme Curve 2.40º mud motor which drilled to a depth of 9,987' and was pulled for a motor failure. A
second Ultra X-treme Curve 2.40º mud motor was picked up for the final curve assembly, which drilled to
a curve TD of 11,440' MD (11,024.5' TVD). This placed the wellbore 9.5' into the 19' thick (shale to shale)
Middle Bakken and approximately 1' into the silty sandstone target interval. The curve was drilled in a
total of 29.4 hours. No E-logs were run due to the close proximity of the State Gresz 2-26-35H-143-98
well. Directional tools were then pulled out of the hole and a string of 7" intermediate casing was run, set,
and cemented at a depth of 11,421' MD. After casing operations, a gyro was run to ensure separation
between offset wells, thus minimizing the ellipse of uncertainty. Once finished, true lateral operations
commenced.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was used throughout the vertical hole and curve build sections
maintaining a 9.5-10.0 ppg mud weight for stable downhole conditions.
Lateral Operations:
Leam provided equipment and personnel for MWD and directional drilling services throughout the
lateral section of the well. The directional drillers and Payzone geologists worked closely together
throughout the project to make steering decisions in order to maximize the amount of borehole exposure
to the pay zone.
A five bladed 6" bit (Security MMD54), 1.50º Gyro Data mud motor, two stabilizers, and one MWD
tool comprised the first lateral BHA (7th BHA overall) and was run in the hole on 4" drill pipe starting at
11,440' MD. This BHA drilled to a depth of 18,155' MD where it was pulled due to a motor failure. The
Security bit drilled 6,715' in 74.52 hours of total drilling time. The eighth BHA, consisting of a new Security
MMD54 bit and a Hunting 5/6 8.3 mud motor, drilled to a TD of 20,590' in 23.92 hours. The lateral was
drilled in 98.44 hours of drilling time. Moreover, spud to TD was a total of 21 days.
The lateral resulted in a TD of 20,590' MD (10,980.99' TVD) at a final azimuth of 182.09º,
resulting in a closure vertical section of 9,949.21'. The BHL is located 9,942.61' South & 362.29' West of
surface location or approximately 267.39' FSL & 2,022.29' FWL, SW SW Section 35, T143N, R98W
(Figure 2).
Salt water drilling fluid was used from the intermediate casing to TD with mud weights throughout
the lateral varying from 9.8-10.1 ppg.

GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Methods:
Geologic supervision of the well was provided by Payzone Consulting, Inc. with four well site geologists.
Laura Godowski and Bob Troutman drilled the vertical and curve. Mike Williamson and Kevin Jacks
drilled the horizontal sections. A wireless Totco Gas WatchTM gas chromatograph was interfaced with a
Rigwatch electronic data recorder system and provided total gas, C1, and C3 information for viewing
around location through the vertical and curve. Lagged samples were caught in 30' intervals from 8,000'
to 11,440' MD and 100' intervals from 11,440' to 20,590' MD (TD). Cuttings were examined under a
binocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are provided in the lithology document within
this evaluation. Two sets of dry cuttings were collected, with one set sent to the North Dakota Geological
Survey Core Library and the second set sent to Ed Schirm, the project geologist overseeing operations.

LATERAL GEOLOGY
Gamma Signature:
Within the Middle Bakken silty sandstone/siltstone target interval, gamma counts varied between 58 and
148 API. The packstone was not encountered throughout the lateral section of the well. The center of
the target zone depicted high 50's to mid 70's API. Above center, the gamma signature was extremely
ratty, fluctuating between low 70's and upwards of 115 API. Below center, the gamma signature was
much tighter, averaging between 90 and 110 API. Three hard streaks were encountered in this targeted
interval and were located near the top, middle and bottom of the target zone.
The wellbore tracked particularly well in the upper half of the zone, between the upper and middle hard
streaks. Very few steering adjustments were needed while the wellbore was in this part of the zone.
Below the center of zone, the wellbore tended to deflect downward, aggressively, making steering more
difficult. When the distinctive "high and tight" gamma signature of the lower zone was encountered,
directional was advised to slide up, as we have no exact confirmation on the thickness to the Lower
Bakken Shale. An inclination of 91.5 degrees or higher was needed in order to get above the center of
zone.
The entire well bore was drilled in between the 19' projected shale to shale zone with excellent gamma
markers and trends that represented the stratigraphy within the lateral. These markers and trends aided
geo-steering tremendously. The wellbore was in the Middle Bakken throughout the entire lateral and
79.4% within the 6' targeted interval.
These gamma markers were used for determining formation dip and depth within the target interval and
permitted efficient geosteering. They are plotted on the Wellsite horizontal log (Figure 6) and dip profile
(Figure 7).

Geo-steering:
The target zone within the Middle Bakken was established based on the best drill rates, gamma markers
and gas concentrations. A 6' zone of >6% porosity was expected to be encountered in the targeted
interval. Samples from the target zone varied in porosity, oil staining, color, cementation, and pyrite
content. Stratigraphic locations in the target zone were based on drill rates, gas shows, gamma ray
values and sample observations.
The Middle Bakken was penetrated at 11,313' MD; 11,015' TVD and consisted of siltstone and silty
sandstone when landing the curve. A 6' target window in an approximately 19' thick Middle Bakken was
selected, leaving 8' of TVD above the target window, and 5' of TVD below.
The wellbore stayed within the 6' targeted interval for approximately 7,258' of 9,150' or 79.4% (rat hole
out of intermediate casing to TD). The well was drilled in between the 19' shale to shale target zone for
100% of the lateral. Using the provided offset log information and the structural data provided from Oxy
USA Inc., Payzone geologists were able to anticipate and interpret the dip rate for the well. The TVD of
the formation decreased 43.53' over 9,150' (first survey out of intermediate casing to TD projection at bit)
resulting in an overall up dip of 0.31º or 90.31°.
A total depth of 20,590' was reached on January 4, 2015 at 04:00 MST. Total depth resulted in 100%
overall exposure within the Middle Bakken Member of the Bakken Formation and 79.4% within the
defined 6' targeted interval. (Figure 7)

Summary

The Nabors X-18 drilling rig successfully drilled a two-section horizontal wellbore within the
Middle Bakken Member of the Bakken Formation at the OXY USA, Inc. State Gresz 3-26-35H-
143-98.

A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.5-10.0 ppg) was used from spud to curve landing
with salt water drilling fluid used from the intermediate casing to 20,590' MD (TD). Salt water
mud (9.8-10.1 ppg) was efficient for maintaining stable down hole conditions and permitted
adequate analysis of gas concentrations in the lateral. Background gas concentrations ranged
from 50-300 units with connections as high as 291 units.
These elevated gas levels were
encountered in the first 4,000' of the lateral. There was no discernable correlation between gas
levels and depth in zone.

Steering was accomplished by sample evaluation and by following correlative drilling rate and
MWD gamma ray markers; making dip estimations based on intersections of these markers with
the well bore. Projections of dip were successfully used to maintain the well bore within the
Middle Bakken. A net of 7,258' (79.4%) of gross 9,150' of the lateral was drilled in the ideal 6'
thick Middle Bakken target with 100% exposure within the 19' shale to shale Middle Bakken
Member of the Bakken Formation.

Samples from the target consisted of white to off-white, light brown, trace light gray, and light gray
to dark gray siltstone/silty sandstone. Traces of intergranular porosity were seen throughout the
lateral. Samples from the ideal zone contained a spotty to even brown oil stain.

The transitional packstone was not present when landing the curve and was not encountered
throughout the lateral. Three hard streaks were present: one at the top of the 6' target zone, one
in the middle of the target zone and the other approximately 1' above the bottom of the targeted
interval. The wellbore tracked nicely between the upper and middle hard streaks.

The State Gresz 3-26-35H-143-98 was a successful drilling effort as measured by gross
exposure to the target. The well will be fitted with a 4 ½" production liner and swell packers in
preparation for a fracture stimulation that will determine the true commercial value of the well.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
