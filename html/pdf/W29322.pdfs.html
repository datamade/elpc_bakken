INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 6T3 [Lot 3, Section 3,
T153N, R93W] is located approximately 13 miles north of the town of New Town in
Mountrail County, ND. It is one of three wells on the Langved 5393 12-3 pad. The Langved
5393 12-3 6T3 is a horizontal Three Forks third bench well within the Williston Basin
consisting of one 9,507' lateral drilled toward the south. Geologically, the Williston Basin is
an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Three Forks Formation (Late Devonian) deposited
dolomitic mudstones interbedded with light colored shale and claystone. Advances in drilling
techniques and well stimulation have made multiple zones viable as an oil and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the
`Structure' section appended to this report. Offset well control is essential in curve building
operations, in order to successfully land within a targeted zone of the Three Forks Formation.
Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray signatures in these wells were compared to
gamma data of offset wells in order to successfully land the curve. The target landing true
vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to ensure accurate landing of the curve. The
offset wells used for the Langved 5393 12-3 6T3 were the Langved 5393 11-3H, Mangum
5493 44-7 T3, and the Flavin 5393 14-3T.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 12-3 6T3 was reentered on October 5, 2014 by Xtreme 21. Drilling fluid
was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt-water mud for the lateral. Two trips
were made during the vertical at 6,519' and 8,189'. Both were due to low ROP. The curve
was landed at 11,183' MD (10,817' TVD) with an inclination of 91.12°. Following the
landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 11,163' MD. To start the lateral, a 6
Varel PDC bit, MWD tool, and a 1.5° fixed mud motor were used. The BHA
accomplishments are tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached
total depth (TD) at 20,670' MD at 1500 hrs CDT on November 22, 2014, generating an
exposure of 9,507 linear feet of 6 hole through the Three Forks Third Bench target interval.
The bottom hole location (BHL) lies 10,067.39' south & 28.03' west of surface location or
approximately 234.49' FSL and 2,221.97' FWL, SE SW Sec. 10, T153N, R100W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 7,980' MD where the observed lithology consists of primarily shale
with thin interbedded sandstone units. The main zone of interest drilled at the subject well
was the Three Forks third bench.
The Three Forks third bench member of the Three Forks Formation (Late Devonian) was
entered at 10,787' TVD (-8,529'), 15' low to the Mangum 5493 44-7T3. Representative
samples consist of a light brown ­ medium brown with occasional light grey dolomite with
interbedded mint green to light blue green shale and intercrystalline porosity and a trace
light-brown oil stain. Samples may exhibit a very slow streaming cut fluorescence. Low
hydrocarbon show and heavy gases (C1-C2) were observed in this interval.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 20-211 units
above a background of ~40 units against an average mud weight of 10.1lb/gal diesel-invert.
Methane and Ethane (C1-C2) were observed throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than C2
were not observed. After kicking off the curve, shows were similar with respect to the
vertical. Shows remained low from kick-off through entry of the False Bakken interval of the
lower Lodgepole. Gas shows were consistently high from this interval through curve landing
in the targeted Three Forks pay zone. The highest show in the curve was a 5,953 unit gas
show in the Middle Bakken.
Shows from the False Bakken through curve landing from units (C1-C4) over a background of
200 units against a 10.1 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight.
The lateral began with low gas shows. At ~ 17,300' MD shows increased greatly and ranged
from 346-1,250 units (C1-C3) over a background of 260 units against a 9.75 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight. Background gas continued to rise for the remainder of the lateral. No free oil
was observed over the shakers.
GEOSTEERING
The ideal target zone of the Langved 5393 12-3 6T3 was chosen from core data from the
Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 (Figure 3). This zone is an experimental new target. It was imperative
that the well bore not go above the H marker to avoid contamination from the higher third
bench target. The ideal target is set from the lower inflection point of the F marker down 16'.
Figure 3. Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 TVD gamma log of Three Forks third bench with ideal target zone
highlighted in green (Curtis Johnson, Oasis Geologist).
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 10,820' TVD at 89.8°. The Base of Last Salt
top indicated that the target was 5' high of prognosis. Mission Canyon was 18' high to
prognosis. The decision was made to move the target up 18'. The curve was landed at a TVD
of 10,817' at 90.49°, about 30' TVD into the Three Forks Third Bench, 3' into the ideal target
zone.
The most consistent marker used to steer was the change between the high gamma E and the
low gamma D with the goal of never past the "red beds".

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 6T3 was drilled by Xtreme
21 from reentry to TD in 13 days. The Langved reached a total depth of 20,670' MD on
November 22, 2014. Good shows were recorded at the end of the lateral. Samples from the

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
