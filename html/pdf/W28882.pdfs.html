INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 5T [SE SW Section 10,
T153N, R93W] is located approximately 11 miles northwest of New Town in Mountrail
County, ND. The Langved 5393 42-10 5T is a horizontal first bench Three Forks well within
the Williston Basin consisting of one 9,559' lateral drilled toward the north. Geologically, the
Williston Basin is an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western
edge of the Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Three Forks Formation (Devonian)
consists of interbedded carbonate and clastic lithologies, which unconformably underlies the
Pronghorn Member of the Bakken Formation (formerly recognized as the Sanish Sand).
Advances in drilling and well stimulation have made the Three Forks Formation a viable oil
and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the
`Structure' section appended to this report. The Langved 5393 42-10 3T2, located 333 feet
west of the subject well, proved to be the most reliable and consistent of the primary offsets
used for depth correlation of formations above the Lodgepole and during the curve landing.
Offset well control was essential in curve operations, to successfully land within the first
bench target zone. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray signatures in these wells
were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations in order to successfully
land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to
ensure accurate landing of the curve.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 42-10 5T was re-entered on October 30, 2014 by Nabors 460. Drilling
fluid was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. Two
trips were made during the vertical. At 8,315', a trip was required for a new bit due to a
decline in ROP, and the new BHA was used to reach the kick off point at 10,145'. The
second trip in the vertical was made to pick up the curve assembly. The curve was initially
drilled with an 8 ¾" Security MMD55M PDC bit utilizing a 2.38° Predator mud motor. The
motor was replaced at 10,512' MD with a 2.45° Cavare motor due to low build rates. The
original bit was re-run in the hole. The curve required 26 hours and 831' of drilling and was
landed at 10,976' MD (10,685' TVD) with an estimated inclination of 90.9°. Following the
landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 10,962' MD.
To start the lateral, a 6 Reed SKH1613M-J2 PDC bit, MWD tool, and a 1.5° Baker mud
motor were used. This BHA drilled 2,804' in 60.5 hours to 13,780' MD where the first trip in
the lateral was made due to slow ROP. The bit was replaced with a Baker Hughes T406X
PDC bit and the motor was replaced with an identical Baker model. This assembly drilled
6,755' in 90 hours to a TD of 20,535' MD.
The BHA accomplishments are tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The
well reached total depth (TD) at 20,535' MD at 1900 hrs CDT on November 16, 2014,
generating an exposure of 9,573 linear feet of 6 hole through the Three Forks target interval.
The bottom hole location (BHL) lies 10,059.99' north & 11.81' west of surface location or
approximately 258.11 FNL and 1,864.19 FWL, Lot 3 Sec. 3, T153N, R93W.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 10-31 units
above a background of ~8 units against a 9.55-9.95 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight. Methane
(C1) was observed throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than C1 were not observed. After
kicking off the curve, gas levels remained low with one show of 23 units with background
ranging from 11-20 units, until reaching the Upper Bakken. Gas shows were elevated in
respect to the vertical from this interval through the entrance of the Three Forks Formation
first bench. Shows from the Upper Bakken through first bench ranged from 60-981 units (C1-
C4) over a background ranging from 37- 96 units against a diesel-invert mud weight of 10.2
lb/gal. Upon drilling the first bench of the Three Forks to the curve landing, background gas
slowly declined to ~42 units, with shows ranging from 73-123 units (C1-C2).
The lateral began with gas shows ranging from 84-313 units (C1-C3) over a background
ranging from 90-180 units against a 9.65 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas levels decreased
from ~ 11,550' - 11,900' MD, with shows from 109-251 units (C1-C3) over a background of
70-130 units against a 9.65 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. These levels were observed for
much of the lateral while the borehole remained in the middle of the target zone. Gas levels
increased when the clean dolomite (low gamma signature) was encountered. The clean
dolomite was encountered from ~ 11,900' - 12,420' MD, where gas shows ranged from 245-
382 units (C1-C3) over a background of 166-256 units against a 9.65 lb/gal salt-water mud
weight. The clean dolomite was encountered again from ~ 12,750' - 12,800' MD, where gas
shows ranged from 217-383 units (C1-C3) over a background of 166-256 units against a 9.65
lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas was elevated from ~ 12,900' - 12,980' MD, where gas
shows ranged from 325-412 units (C1-C3) over a background of 150-180 units against a 9.65
lb/gal salt-water mud weight.
Gas levels decreased significantly upon entering the claystone at 12,980', where background
levels ranged from 29-42 units with no gas shows. Gas levels increased after the borehole
dropped several feet into the claystone from 13,050' ­ 14,272', with shows ranging from 171-
296 units, over a background ranging from 58-110 units against a 9.65 lb/gal salt-water mud
weight. Gas levels were maintained at this magnitude from 14,272' ­ 16,292' while the
borehole remained positioned along the claystone interface and in the lower to mid portion of
the target zone. Significant gas increases were observed from 16,292' ­ 17,380' (C1-C4)
while the borehole was positioned along the claystone interface, with shows ranging from
287-2008 units, over a background ranging from 106-386 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight.
Gas levels decreased from 17,380' - 17,980' (C1-C4) while the borehole was tracking two to
three feet above the claystone, with shows ranging from 204 - 1310 units, over a background
ranging from 118-219 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas levels gradually
increased as the borehole rose toward the middle and upper sections of the target zone from
17,980' - 18,810' (C1-C4), with shows ranging from 380 - 1699 units, over a background
ranging from 158 - 409 units against a 9.75 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas levels stayed
in this range through the remainder of the well.
GEOSTEERING
The potential pay zone of the Langved 5393 42-10 5T was identified by evaluating electric
log data from the Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 (Figure 6).
Figure 6. Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 Type log. Ideal target zone highlighted in green.
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 10,695' TVD at 90.45°. The Lodgepole top
indicated that the target was approximately ten feet higher than prognosis based on the two
closest wells (Langved 5393 42-10 3T2 and Langved 5393 42-10 2T). The decision was
made to raise the target to 10,685'. The lower markers and formation tops supported this
decision. The wellbore was landed at a TVD of 10,685' at 89.9°, in the middle of the target
zone in the first bench Three Forks target interval.
Drilling ahead out of the casing at a depth of 10,962' MD, the inclination averaged 90.5° to
keep the borehole positioned in the center of the target zone. Just below the mid point in the
target interval, the gamma signature was characterized by large erratic swings ranging from
45 ­ 160 API from 10,950' ­ 11,580'. Slightly above the midpoint in the target zone, from
11,580' ­ 12,600', the gamma signature was more stable ranging from 110 ­ 130 API.
Several attempts were made to decrease the inclination in order to tag the claystone;
however, a hard streak in the lower dolomite bounced the well bore back up into section.
Several aggressive slides at 12,600' MD allowed the borehole to penetrate the hard streak,
but resulted in the inclination dropping to 85.9°. The borehole dropped several feet into the
claystone as a result. The gamma signature while in the claystone ranged from 140 ­ 180
API. The borehole remained in the claystone until 14,300'. Several attempts to slide up in
section were unsuccessful as the borehole dropped significantly during rotation. Structural
dip also increased while in the claystone ranging from 0.45° to 1.2° up which required
greater builds than initially expected. The gamma signature at the claystone interface served
as a good marker for calculating dip for much of the well.
The borehole remained at or near the base of target from 14,300' ­ 17,150', occasionally
entering the claystone from 15,250' - 15,450' and again briefly at 16,390'. Several attempts
were made to raise the borehole up into the target zone, however the hard streak observed
near the center of the target zone caused the borehole to bounce and significantly lowered the
inclination. This occurred at 15,100', 16,150', and 16,950'. The gamma signature just below
this hard streak ranged from 70 ­ 110 API. At 17,800', the borehole was gradually raised in
section reaching close to the top of target by 18,600', where the gamma readings ranged from
110 ­ 130 API. The borehole remained in the upper portion of the target zone for the
remainder of the well. The gross apparent dip for the well was 0.41° up. The dip profile is
presented below in Figure 7.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 5T was drilled by Nabors
460 from reentry to TD in 18 days. The well reached a total depth of 20,535' MD on
November 16, 2014. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination
obtained during the Langved 5393 42-10 5T indicate hydrocarbon presence in the Three
Forks first bench Target. Samples from the target zone consist of light gray to off white, pink
to very light orange-red crystalline carbonate, microsucrosic dolomite with intercrystalline
porosity, which yielded slow to moderate pale yellow to green diffuse and streaming cut
fluorescence. Interbedded with the aforementioned dolomite was light green, pale blue gray
shale with disseminated pyrite. Lateral operations were successful in exposing 9,573' of
potentially productive Three Forks target zone rock to the wellbore.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
