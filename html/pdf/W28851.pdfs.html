INTRODUCTION
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Byron 4-4-163-101 [Lot 4 Section 4, T163N,
R101W] is located approximately five miles northwest of the town of Fortuna in Divide
County, North Dakota. The subject well was designed to drill south at a heading of
179.38°, from Lot 4 [465' FNL and 1,150' FWL] Section 4, T163N, R101W by the Nabors
#49 drilling rig (Figure 1). The Byron 4-4-163-101 is intended to develop oil production
from the Mississippian-Devonian Middle Member of the Bakken Formation in the Colgan
Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Byron 4-4-163-101 offset control was provided by the American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101 [Lot 3 Section 4, T163N, R101W], the American Eagle
Energy Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-163-101 [Lot 3 Section 4, T163N, R101W], the
American Eagle Energy Corporation, Lynda 15-32-164-101 [SW SE, Section 32, T164N,
R101W] and the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Muzzy 15-33S-164-101 [SW SE
Section 33, T164N, R101W] (Figure 2). The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton
3-4-163-101 is located 535' east of the Byron 4-4-163-101, and was drilled in June 2012
in the Bakken pool, and has produced over 112,000 barrels of oil over the life of the well.
The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-163-101 is located 635' east of
the Byron 4-4-163-101, and was drilled in August of 2012 in the Bakken pool, and has
produced over 91,000 barrels of oil over the life of the well. The American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Lynda 15-32-164-101 is located approximately 0.61 miles northwest of the
Byron 4-4-163-101, and was drilled in November of 2013 in the Bakken pool, and has

Byron 4-4-163-101, and was drilled in October of 2012 in the Bakken pool, and has

wells were used in forecasting formation tops and the landing target for the Byron 4-4-163-
101. The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101 was used to provide log
character for the Middle Bakken.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On September 16th, 2014 Nabors #49 spud the Byron 4-4-163-101. The surface hole was
drilled to 1,635' MD and isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55) to 1,626' MD. The
vertical BHA included an 8 ¾" PDC bit and 1.83° motor which was used to 7,337' MD
This was 84' high to the planned kick-off point (KOP). At KOP the rig crew picked up the
curve BHA which included an 8 ¾" PDC bit and a 2.12° motor. The curve BHA was
drilled to a depth of 8,250' MD, which is TD for the curve. Diesel invert drilling fluid was
used through the vertical hole and curve build sections (1,635' to 8,250' MD) maintaining
a 9.4-9.6 ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions. Millennium Directional Service
provided directional drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys
from the vertical hole provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore for the curve build and
lateral. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided well-site geology consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# P-110, 32# HCL-80 and 32# P-110) was
set and cemented to 8,228' MD. Drilling fluid was changed out to saltwater ranging
between 9.5-9.7 ppg. The lateral BHA included a 6" PDC bit, a 1.5° fixed mud motor, and
MWD survey and gamma tools. Lateral operations began at 1743 hrs CDT, on September
23rd, 2014. The lateral BHA was used from 8,250' to 8,679' MD, then removed due to a
broken MWD tool. Because of low inclination caused by the broken MWD tool, the well
was sidetracked at 8,389' MD. A new BHA with the previous 6" PDC bit and 1.5° fixed
mud motor and MWD tools was used to a depth of 13,748' MD where it was removed due
to declining ROP. A new BHA with a new 6" PDC bit, 1.5° fixed mud motor and MWD
tools was then used to 17,510' MD.
The Byron 4-4-163-101 was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 17,510' MD, 7,992.57' true
vertical depth (TVD), at 22:17 CDT September 29th, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom
hole coordinates are 9,815.68' south and 136.98' east of surface location or approximately
254.20' FSL and 1,286.98' FWL SW SW Section 9, T163N, R101W. Projected to TD, the
wellbore has a closure distance of 9,816.64' at a closure direction of 179.20°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Byron 4-4-163-
101. The geology well-site team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and geo-steered.
Logging samples and gas monitoring started at 5,700' MD. The rig crews caught drill
cuttings in 30' lagged intervals per instruction from well-site geologists. Drill cuttings
were examined under a trinocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are
provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were collected and sent to the North
Dakota Geological Survey. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. TGC (total gas with
chromatograph) gas detector was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder (EDR).
The Pason system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump rates to the gas detector and
distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total gas was recorded in units
(100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air). In addition to cuttings and gas measurements
geological data such as gamma and ROP was interpreted for the use of geo-steering
throughout the well.

Sample examination began in the Piper Formation [Jurassic] above the Spearfish
Formation [Triassic/Permian]. The Spearfish Formation was penetrated at 5,748' TVD
(-3,476'), which is 3' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101.
The Spearfish is composed of red siltstone and silty sandstone in this area. Background gas
(C1) ranged from 8 to 10 units with no gas shows.
The Kibbey Formation of the Big Snowy Group [Mississippian] was penetrated at 5,972'
TVD (-3,700'), which is 16' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-
163-101. The Kibbey consists of siltstone, silty sandstone and anhydrite. Background gas
(C1) was 8 to 12 units with no shows.
The Charles Formation [Mississippian] was penetrated at 6,162' TVD (-3,890') and is
18' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101. The upper Poplar
interval of the Charles consists of salt, anhydrite, dolomitic limestone, argillaceous
limestone and limestone. Salt intervals were marked by an increase in rate of penetration
along with a decrease in weight on bit. Background gas (C1) within the Poplar was 6 to 15
units and gas shows up to 18 units were recorded. The Ratcliffe interval of the lower
Charles Formation consists of limestone, argillaceous limestone, argillaceous dolomite,
and anhydrite. The top of the Ratcliffe was penetrated at 6,550' TVD (-4,278') and is 6'
high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101. The Ratcliffe top is
within a dolomite facies below an anhydrite unit. Gas shows within the Ratcliffe reached
as high as 16 units, and background gases were within 12 to 13 units. The Base Last Salt
interval was not present in this area. The Midale interval of the lowermost Charles
Formation consists of argillaceous limestone and limestone. The top of the Midale was
penetrated at 6,717' TVD (-4,445') and is 17' high to the American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101. Gas shows within the Midale reached as high as 25 units,
and background gases were within 10 to 18 units.
The Mission Canyon Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] consists of argillaceous
limestone and limestone. The Mission Canyon top was penetrated at 6,835' TVD
(-4,563'), and is 11' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101.
The top of the Mission Canyon was marked by a transition from argillaceous limestone to
a cream to light gray brown limestone, which can be identified by a decrease in gamma.
Limestone intervals at the base of the Mission Canyon have spotty dark brown to black oil
staining. Gas shows (C1) within the Mission Canyon reached 22 units and background
gases were within 8 to 13 units.
The Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] was penetrated at 7,163'
TVD (-4,891'), and is 4' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-
101. The top of the Lodgepole was marked by a transition from argillaceous limestone to
limestone. Limestone intervals at the top of the Lodgepole have spotty dark brown to black
oil staining and argillaceous limestone intervals at the Lodgepole 1 marker have spotty
medium brown oil staining. Background gas readings (C1-C2) were 10 to 280 units and gas
shows are as high as 1,668 units, and connection/survey gas measurements 1,732 units.
Trip gas measurements (C1) reached 26 units.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was penetrated at 7,867' TVD (-5,595'), which
is 3' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101. The top of the
Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample returns of dark brown to black,
carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Gas shows (C1-C2) were as high as 597
units with survey gas show of 1,630 units. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to
511 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian-Devonian] was penetrated at 7,877' TVD
(-5,605'), and is 1' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101.
The Middle Bakken top was marked by a large decrease in gamma and rate of penetration
as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. The siltstone was
described as light gray to light gray brown, sub blocky and calcite cemented. Silty
sandstone was described as light gray brown and cream to light brown in color, very fine
grained and calcite cemented (Figure 3). Also, trace poor intergranular porosity and trace
spotty light brown oil stain were present. Samples were cut with trichloroethylene (Carbo-
Sol) and examined with a fluoroscope. The cut samples showed weak pale yellow, slow to
fast, diffuse and streaming cut fluorescence.

The greatest hydrocarbon shows (C1-C4) recorded in the Middle Bakken were as high as

GEO-STEERING
The target interval within the Middle Bakken was 10' thick, beginning approximately 24'
below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD
gamma ray log of the offset control well, American Eagle Energy Corporation, Anton 3-4-
163-101 (Figure 4). Gamma ray readings within the 10' of the target zone displayed a
gamma signature of 58-92 API. The top of the target was marked by a decrease in gamma
signature to 25-35 API. The contact of the cool gamma at the top of the target was used for
dip calculations. The well plan called for a landing 30' below the top of the Middle Bakken
and landing depths were adjusted as formation markers were traversed. Calculations from
markers within the Lodgepole were adjusted with the Middle Bakken top and provided a
target landing depth of 7,906' TVD (30' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale). The
directional drillers were able to land at 7,903' TVD, which was 3' high to the planned target
depth. MWD gamma ray correlated adequately with the American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Anton 3-4-163-101, which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-
steering. The steering team was successful in maintaining 90% of the exposed 9,260'
lateral wellbore in target, and about 94% within 2' of target window. Based on intersections
with the cool gamma marker, an overall apparent dip was estimated at 0.53° down (Figure
5).

SUMMARY
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Byron 4-4-163-101 was spud on September
16th, 2014 by Nabors #49. It is a two section Middle Bakken horizontal well within the
Colgan Field of Divide County, North Dakota. 90% of the lateral was drilled in the ideal
10' primary target interval. A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.4-9.6 ppg) during
the vertical and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.5-9.7 ppg) during the lateral was
utilized. The target zone was 10' thick silty sandstone interval (58-92 API). The silty
sandstone displayed trace intergranular porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil
staining. Background gas (C1-C4) ranged from approximately 6 to 260 units within the
target interval. The Byron 4-4-163-101 awaits completion to determine its full production
potential.

Lithology
Rig crews caught lagged samples in 30' intervals under the supervision of Sunburst Consulting, Inc., well-
site geologists. A detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma
ray marker tops have been inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined
wet and dry under a stereo zoom trinocular microscope. Sample cuts were examined with Carbo-sol
(trichloroethylene) under ultra violet light. The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface casing to an
intermediate casing point of 8,250' MD, and saltwater brine was used throughout the lateral: from
intermediate casing to a total depth of 17,510' MD. Samples were collected from 5,700' to 17,510' MD
(TD). One set of dried samples was boxed and distributed to the state of North Dakota.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
