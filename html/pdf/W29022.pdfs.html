Synopsis
Slawson Exploration's Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H [NE SW Section 14, T154N, R99W] is located
~13 miles East of the city of Williston, North Dakota. The Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H is a production
well drilled in the North Stockyard Creek field of Williams County. A single lateral leg trending
257.78°, with a 3° per 100' turn from the beginning of the lateral resulting in an azimuth of
270.00, was proposed to be drilled from the NE SW corner of Section 14 to the NW SW corner
of Section 15. The lateral was targeting the porous crystalline dolomite of the Three Forks 2nd
Bench.
Control Wells
There were many control wells utilized as offsets for the Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H, including the
Rodney 1-14H and the Sail & Anchor 4-13-14. The main completed well used as a control offset
to Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H was the Everett 1-15H.
The Everett 1-15H [SE SE Section 15, T154N, R99W] is a horizontal production well drilled by
Zavanna shortly before the subject well. Everett 1-15H is located approximately 3,000 East of
the Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H surface location. The Everett 1-15H lateral was drilled to the North
West. The subject well was drilled to the East so constant gamma correlation between markers
and usage of a structure map composed by Slawson Geology were pertinent tools during the
horizontal since all adjacent wells were drilled in a differing directions. During the curve, the
gamma ray that was produced was constantly compared to the Everett 1-15H. Gamma ray was
used to help determine a proper landing depth.
To assist in the choosing of an initial target, an isopach table (Table 1) was constructed to
measure the distance of formation tops to the target depth determined from the Everett 1-15H
offset well. Comparison of gamma ray markers showed the most consistent isopachs for up hole
formations to be the base of the Charles Salt.

Geologic Assessment
Methods
Geologic supervision of Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H was provided by two experienced RPM well site
geologists. Gas and chromatograph levels were measured using National Oilfield Varco real time
gas detector and chromatograph system. The NOV gas detection system uses non-dispersive
infrared and chemical sensor gas detection to evaluate gases liberated from the formation by the
drill bit and carried to the surface by the drilling fluid.
The NOV was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder system. Pason provided drill rate,
on-off bottom and pump strokes to the NOV and received total gas information from the
Bloodhound for viewing on location and remotely.
Geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals in the vertical hole, from 8,800' to 10,850'
MD; 30' intervals in the curve build from 10,850' to 11,663' MD; and 50' samples in the lateral
from 11,663' to TD at 18,340'.
Sampled drill cuttings were examined wet and dry under a binocular microscope using both plain
(broad spectrum) and transmitted light. Cuttings were evaluated for hydrocarbon cut during the
lateral utilizing Entron AE solution. 10% hydrochloric acid and alizarin red were used to
determine the calcareous and dolomitic content of rocks and cementing.
RPM Geologic, Inc. (RPM) well site geologists also closely examined MWD gamma-ray
information and penetration rates to aid in steering decisions and dip degree estimations.
Vertical Operations
Overview
The Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H was spud out of surface on 13th August, 2014 utilizing Frontier 24 as
the drilling contractor. Prior to commencement of RPM mud logging services, a 13 1/2" hole
was drilled with fresh water to depth of 2,436' and isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J-55 casing cemented
to surface.
RPM well site geologists began logging the vertical at 8,800' measured depth at 30' intervals.
Three PDC bits were used to drill out of surface casing to a Vertical TD depth of 10,850'. Bit #1
(8 ¾" Halliburton) PDC was used to drill from 2,436' to 8,827' and averaged an ROP of 155.9
ft/hr in 41 hours of use. Bit #2 (8 ¾" Security) PDC was used to drill from 8,827' to 10,397' and
averaged an ROP of 47.6 ft/hr in 33 hours of use. Bit #3 (8 ¾" Smith) PDC was used to drill
from 10,397' to 10,850' and averaged an ROP of 56.6 ft/hr in 8 hours of use to TD the vertical.
Diesel invert drilling fluid, with a mud weight ranging from 9.5 to 10.1 ppg, was used for the
vertical hole after surface casing. The vertical was drilled to a depth of 10,850' TVD, into the
Lodgepole formation.

Directional Operations
Schlumberger provided equipment and personnel for MWD services as well as equipment and
personnel for directional services. RPM geologists worked closely with Schlumberger directional
and Schlumberger MWD teams to formulate steering decisions to maximize the footage of
borehole in the pay zone.
Curve Build
Diesel invert drilling fluid, with a mud weight ranging from 10.0-10.5 ppg, was used for the
curve. Bit #4 was a 8 ¾" Security PDC used in front of a 2.25° fixed mud motor to drill the
curve from measured depth of 10,850' to 11,663' for 28.4 hours, averaging 28.6 ft/hr.
A Chart was constructed for the curve (Chart 1) that compared the isopach of certain easily
recognizable gamma markers from the Charles Salt to the Three Forks on the control well
Everett 1-15H to the Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H as marker picks were made. This chart shows the
"False Bakken" top being the closest in thickness to target based on the offset well. The "False
Bakken" marker was the main point used to make changes to the target landing depth during the
curve and should serve as a good reference point for future curve landings in the immediate area.
Chart 1: Comparing distances from gamma markers on the offset wells to gamma markers on the subject well.
The curve was completed at 90.0 degrees, a measured depth of 11,663' and a TVD of
11,335.32', ~ 17' into the Three Forks 2nd Bench and approximately 12' into the target
window.

Lateral
Overview
Drilling fluid consisting of salt water brine (9.8-10.0 ppg) was used while drilling the lateral
section up to the TD of 18,340'. One 6" PDC bit was used to drill the entire lateral.
Bit # 5 (6" Smith), was a PDC bit used to drill the lateral to a total depth of 18,340'. It drilled
6,677' and was used for 80 hours averaging 83.5 feet per hour.
Drilling operations were concluded at 15:00 CST on 29 August 2014 at a bottom hole location of
75.23' S & 7,224.27' W of surface location or approximately 1,384.77' FSL & 253.26' FWL, NW
SW Sec 15, T154 R99W.
Lithology
To aid in communications while drilling the lateral, a type log was provided by Slawson Geology
using the gamma signature from the electronic logs run in the North Plains Scanlan 3-5H NENW5-
153N-98W (Diagram 1). North Plains Scanlan 3-5H NENW5-153N-98W is located ~11 miles West of
the Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H in Section 5, T153N, R98W. The goal was to utilize the cooler
marker approximately 5' into the Second Bench of the Three Forks as an indicator of the top of
target and the cooler gamma marker at the bottom of zone for target indicators. While the target
was 18' thick efforts were made to stay in the lower half of the target window.

Gas and hydrocarbon Shows
It was found that the gas and hydrocarbon shows were a decent indicator of placement in target
throughout the lateral. Gas shows increased as the wellbore dropped lower in target zone and
decreased as it rose towards the Torquay Claystone. Background gas started around 500 units
and rose to approximately 2,500 units as more wellbore was exposed with connection peaks
exceeding 3,500 units.
The NOV gas chromatograph showed components of Methane and Propane present through the
entire lateral. Sample cuttings were very good throughout the lateral. The testing of cuts through
fluorocarbon methodology was used every 200' of the lateral and showed a consistent slight
streaming yellow cut regardless of placement within the target window.
Summary
1) The Bootleg 8-14-15 TF2H was initially spud for drilling at surface on 13 August, 2014
as a production well in the Stockyard Creek field of Williams County, North Dakota. The
well was spud by Frontier 24 and the vertical hole was drilled to a total depth of 10,850'.
2) A mud program consisted of diesel invert (9.5 ­ 10.5 ppg) was utilized to drill the vertical
past surface casing and curve build. Salt water Brine was utilized in the lateral averaging
9.8-10.0 ppg.
3) Six bits were utilized to drill the well to TD. One 13 ½" bit was used to drill surface. One
Smith 8 ¾" bit, one Security, and one Halliburton bit were used to drill the vertical. One
Security 8 ¾" bit was used to drill the curve. One Smith 6" bit was used to drill the lateral
to 18,340' (TD).
4) The lateral was targeting the Three Forks 2nd Bench porosity ranging from about 5'
below the Torquay Shale down to approximately 25' below the Torquay Shale. The top
of the target zone was marked by cool gamma reading 70-80 API and the bottom was
marked by cooler gamma reading 50-60 API.
5) Gas levels between 500 and 2,500 units were maintained through the lateral drilling
operations of the Three Forks 2nd Bench. Connection gases regularly exceeded 3,500
units.
6) The Three Forks 2nd Bench target consisted of one observed gross lithology. The target
Zone of the Three Forks 2nd Bench was observed to be crystalline dolomite with
apparent intergranular porosity which was capped off by much tighter siltstone and
interbedded claytone layers.
7) Moderate oil stain was present in sample during the entire lateral along with a moderate
blue to white diffuse cut and a slight streaming yellow cut.
8) Drilling operations were concluded at 15:00 CST on 29 August 2014 at a bottom hole
location of 75.23' S & 7,224.27' W of surface location or approximately 1,384.77' FSL &
253.26' FWL, NW SW Sec 15, T154 R99W.
9) Exposure to target zone was 100% of the lateral. The Slawson Exploration's Bootleg 8-
14-15 TF2H awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Slawson Pre-Frac Safety and Testing Procedure
7" casing testing procedure
1) Logging
a. Run bond log to top of L/H/P and ensure adequate cement coverage (at least 500' above top of zone to
be fractured)
b. Run casing inspection log in 7" casing to top of L/H/P or 100' beneath cement top
i. De-rate casing for any significant wear based on remaining wall thickness.
ii. Set maximum treating pressure at no more than 85% of the normal or de-rated pressure rating -
whichever applies.
2) Casing testing
a. RU wireline and set a test plug in the top of the Liner/Hanger/Packer.
b. Pressure test the 7" casing and wellhead as follows:
i. In new areas where treating pressures are unknown, test to 7500# for 30 minutes with less than
a 5% loss
ii. In areas with established operations, test to 500# above fracturing pressures seen in that area for
30 minutes with less than a 5% loss
iii. In the case of worn casing, if 85% of the de-rated pressure rating results in a maximum
allowable treating pressure that is less than 500# greater than the expected treating pressure ­
run a tie-back string
3) Pressure relief valve ­ an adequately sized pressure relief valve must be installed on the treating lines from
the pump to the wellhead and set to limit the line pressure to no more than the casing test pressure less 100#.
4) Surface casing valve ­ the surface casing valve must be left open and a diversion line run to a pit or
containment vessel; in order to enable relief of any pressure encountered in the surface casing/intermediate
casing annulus during fracture operations.
