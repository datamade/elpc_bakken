Well Summary
2.1. The Harley 41-2MBH well is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Middle Bakken Formation in
McKenzie County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled North to South crossing from 250' FNL & 1035' FEL of section 2,
T151N, R95W and into section 11, T151N, R95W. The well followed an approximate azimuth of 181.86 degrees until reaching
9010.95' FNL & 1276.71' FEL of section 11, T151N, R95W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately 20 feet thick zone.
The overall dip rate of this lateral was approximately 89.76 degrees or 0.418'/100'.

3.2. Hydrocarbon Analysis in the Vertical and Build Section
The first indication of hydrocarbons was observed at around 10558' lagged with a gas show from 29 units,
increasing to around 472 units. From that point on, we had consistent connection gases as well as down time gases
averaging 165 units. The Upper Bakken Shale produced average background gas of 339 units and a max of 489 at
10781'. The Middle Bakken Member produced a background gas averaging 149 units with a max of 278 at 10973'.
4.
Lateral Well
This well is comprised of an extended reach single lateral that extends from North to South from the casing point. The
well bore lateral was drilled from a 7" casing point at 11011' MD 10658.7' TVD and terminated at 19288' MD 10695.5' TVD,
giving a total lateral distance of 8277' MD, 100% of which was in the Middle Bakken Formation. The initial dip was set at an
89.7 but was adjusted slightly down to an 89.6 early in the lateral. At roughly 14200 we observed gamma signatures that
suggested a steeper down dip of 89.55. As we drilled out to approximately 14750, we observed gamma signatures that
suggested the dip had flattened back out to an 89.8. From that point on our dip remained an 89.8 until reaching 18100 where
the dip flattened out to an 89.9. We successfully drilled the entire lateral in two runs over 5 days.
4.1. Gas and Oil Shows
4.1.1.
The gas buster was not engaged during the lateral. Background gases averaged 58 units over the course of the
entire lateral. Connection gases averaged 115 units with a max of 761 units over the entire lateral portion of the well.
4.1.2.
OIL SHOWS- We had very light oil shows at the possum belly from the beginning of the lateral showing mostly
trace oil shows.

6.
Well Summary
The curve was landed roughly 4 feet below the middle of the target zone. Coming out of casing, we drilled into the middle
target at a projected inclination of 89.7 degrees. For this well we decided to drill the middle of the target zone where drilling
rates averaged 180 to 200 ROP and frequent sliding was not necessary. The DD's did an amazing job of staying in target and
avoiding dog legs early in the lateral. We were able to see decent gamma signatures when transitioning through the middle of
the target. The overall dip of the well averaged out to be an 89.76 degree dip rate with no significant structural variation. There
were no shale strikes during the entire lateral and we were able to complete the lateral in 5 days with one trip in the lateral.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
