Well Summary
2.1. The Harley 31-2TFH well is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Three Forks Formation in McKenzie
County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled North to South crossing from 250' FNL & 1080' FEL of section 2, T151N,
R95W and into section 11, T151N, R95W. The well followed an approximate azimuth of 185.87 degrees until reaching
9009.69' FNL & 1993.29' FEL of section 11, T151N, R95W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately 22 feet thick zone.
The overall dip rate of this lateral was approximately 89.55 degrees or 0.785'/100'.

3.2. Hydrocarbon Analysis in the Vertical and Build Section
The first indication of hydrocarbons was observed at around 10132' lagged with a gas show from 14 units,
increasing to around 758 units. From that point on, we had consistent connection gases as well as down time gases
averaging 297 units. The Upper Bakken Shale produced average background gas of 228 units and a max of 300 at
10755'. The Middle Bakken Member produced a background gas averaging 188 units with a max of 254 at 10771'. The
Lower Bakken Shale produced an average gas of 200 increasing to a max of 257 units. Once we entered the Three
Forks our gas averaged 147 units and had a max gas of 237 units.
4.
Lateral Well
This well is comprised of an extended reach single lateral that extends from North to South from the casing point. The
well bore lateral was drilled from a 7" casing point at 11120' MD 10730.94' TVD and terminated at 19454' MD 10799.11' TVD,
giving a total lateral distance of 8334' MD, 100% of which was in the Three Forks Formation. The initial dip was set at an 89.7
but once we reached a MD of approximately 12100', we observed a gamma signature that suggested a slight steeper down
dip of 89.5. As we drilled from 12100 out to 13000, the gamma signature suggested a steeper dip rate of 89.1. At 14324' MD
we observed gamma signatures that suggested the dip was an 89.3. From approximately 14700 out to 15900 the gamma
signature suggested the dip rate had flattened out to an 89.85. At approximately 17750' MD we observed the gamma
signature which suggested a slight down dip of 89.7 which was held out to TD. We successfully drilled the entire lateral in
three runs over 8 days.

6.
Well Summary
The curve was landed roughly 0 feet high of the middle of the target zone. Coming out of casing, we drilled into the middle
target at a projected inclination of 89.7 degrees. For this well we decided to drill the middle of the target zone where drilling
rates averaged 130 to 160 ROP and frequent sliding was not necessary. The DD's did an amazing job of staying in target and
avoiding dog legs early in the lateral. We were able to see decent gamma signatures when transitioning from roughly 3 feet
above to 5 feet below the middle of the target. The overall dip of the well averaged out to be an 89.55 degree dip rate with no
significant structural variation. There were no shale strikes during the entire lateral and we were able to complete the lateral in
8 days with two trips in the lateral.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
