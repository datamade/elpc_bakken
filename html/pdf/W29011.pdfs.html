Well Evaluation
Nelson 24X-11A [SE SW Section 11, T151N, R98W] is located approximately 8 miles
northeast of Watford City in McKenzie County, ND. The Nelson 24X-11A is a horizontal
Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled toward
the north. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Middle
Bakken. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will be used to maintain
maximum exposure to the target zone.

GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation in order to
log the Base Last Salt for curve landing purposes.
The Middle Bakken was logged at 11,078' TVD (-8,993' MSL). It consists primarily of
silty sandstone that is medium gray to light gray with occasional off white clasts and
contains trace to rare amounts of spotty, medium brown oil stain.
Gas Monitoring
During the vertical no fracture gases were evident. The highest gas observed while
drilling the vertical portion of the well was 139u at 10,385' MD. The highest gas value
recorded for the well was 5,775u. A 1-4' flare was observed for a short time once
circulating through the gas buster commenced at 13,568' MD and then subsided after
~14,000' MD. Background gas fluctuated between 50-3,500u throughout the lateral
section. The entire ideal target zone was observed to contain moderate gas concentrations
and consistent oil staining. The highest gas concentrations were seen in the upper 7 feet
of the ideal target zone (Figure 2).

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 20' interval, the top of which was initially set at
11,091' TVD, 34' below the top of the Upper Bakken Shale (Figure 1). The superposition
of a warm gamma marker reading 90-105 API ~5' into the Middle Bakken target zone,
and a natural deflection just above that warm gamma marker proved to be the most useful
for guides geo-steering. Offset wells indicated that the Middle Bakken would be up-
dipping approximately 0.05° from landing to TD. The final gross overall change in TVD
from landing to the end of the lateral was estimated at less than one foot over 9,737' (Flat
dip, Figure 3). The bottom-hole location is estimated to be 9,882.29' north & 1,002.68'
west of surface location or approx. 251.79' FNL & 667.32' FWL, Lot 4 Sec. 2, T151N,
R98W. Geo-steering techniques exposed 100% of uncased borehole to the 15' Middle
Bakken ideal target and the well reached a TD of 20,935' on October 3, 2014.

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Nelson 24X-11A was spud on September 13 and officially reached
TD in 21 total drilling days on October 3, 2014. Samples from the Middle Bakken silty
sandstone target contained oil staining that was described as trace to rare with visible
intergranular porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were
successfully utilized to expose 100% of the lateral borehole to the ideal Middle Bakken
target. The Nelson 24X-11A awaits completion operations to maximize its production
potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
