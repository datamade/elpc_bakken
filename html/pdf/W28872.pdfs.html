INTRODUCTION
Hess Bakken Investments II, LLC GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 (SE SE Section 31, T158N,
R97W), is located roughly 12 miles south of Wildrose, North Dakota in Williams County. GN-
Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 is a double-section horizontal Middle Bakken well located on a four
well pad; it consists of one 9,790'-long lateral drilled toward the north (354.21°). Drilling of the
vertical and curve portions of the well were completed by H&P #309. H&P #293 was moved
onto the pad to complete the lateral portions of the wells. 7" intermediate casing was set to
10,440' MD, leaving approximately 13' of exposed Upper Bakken Shale. During drilling of the
lateral portion of GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 directional drilling and geo-steering techniques
were employed to maintain maximum possible exposure to the target reservoir.
ENGINEERING
Lateral Operations
Drilling the lateral portion of GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 commenced on December 31, 2104.
Drilling began at a measured depth of 10,651' and was drilled in two runs totaling 93.4 drilling
hours. Salt water drilling fluid fluctuated in density between 9.9-10.05 ppg throughout the
lateral. The lateral was completed at a total depth (TD) of 20,000' MD on January 5, 2015 with a
closure azimuth of approximately 354.21° and a closure distance of approximately 9,840'. The
bottom-hole location (BHL) is estimated to be 9,790.28' north and 992.23' west of the surface
location or approximately 310.43' FNL and 1,465.23' FEL, NW NE Sec. 30, T158N, R97W in
Williams County, North Dakota.
GEOLOGY
Pay Zone
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,257' TVD (-8,006' MSL). The top of the ideal
Middle Bakken drilling target was encountered roughly 13' TVD below the base of the Upper
Shale Member. A layer of limestone deposited 9' TVD below the base of the Upper Shale
Member with a thickness of approximately 6' TVD comprised the upper 2'-3' of the targeted pay
zone. Sample returns from the lateral portion of the well consist of very fine-grained, slightly
pyritic, silty sandstone with dolomite and calcite cement with trace to common amounts of oil
staining (Figure 1). Sample cuts were examined throughout the lateral and displayed pale yellow
streaming and diffuse fluorescence with speeds varying from very slow to moderate. Due to 13'
of exposed shale from the curve build, samples were continuously monitored for shale content.
While drilling, shale percentages seen in sample never exceeded 20%.

Oil and Gas Show
Total gas was recorded as units (100 units = 1% methane) and chromatography of C1 ­ C4
components were recorded in parts-per-million. Gas shows were encountered throughout the
entire lateral. The largest gas show encountered, measuring 969 units, dominantly methane (C1)
with a mud weight of 10.05 ppg, at 19,927' MD, was seen when the wellbore was in the bottom
portion of the geologic window. Background gas varied throughout the lateral, ranging from
roughly 30 units to 300 units in some portions of the lateral. In general, the highest gas shows
were observed when the wellbore was in the lower half of the ideal geologic window (Figure 2).
There was no flare or live oil observed during the drilling of the GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2.

Geo-steering
Baker Hughes provided equipment and personnel for MWD and directional services for the
lateral part of the well. Directional drillers, MWD field engineers, Sunburst Consulting, Inc.
well-site geologists and the Hess operations geologist collaborated throughout the project to
make decisions regarding steering and exploration of formations for production of hydrocarbons.
The 10' thick target zone within the Middle Bakken was established by the Hess operations
geologist based on core analysis, electric logs from offsetting wells and seismic data of the area.
The upper 2'-3' of the targeted geologic window was characterized by cooler gamma signatures
(40-80 API) that are associated with the presence of a limestone layer. The center of the targeted
geologic window was characterized by a "hot" gamma spike (100-120 API). The bottom half of
the targeted geologic window was characterized by warmer, more ambiguous gamma signatures
(80-100 API). Overall gross apparent dip is calculated at 1.02° up (Figure 3). GN-Halseth-158-
97-3130H-2 reached TD (20,000' MD) while staying within the ideal geologic window for
roughly 61% of the well.

SUMMARY
1)
The Hess GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 lateral was successfully drilled as a Middle
Bakken lateral at New Home Field in 6 drilling days, reaching a TD of 20,000' on
January 5, 2015.
2)
Samples from this Middle Bakken lateral consisted mostly of very fine-grained silty
sandstone, varying in color from off white to light-medium gray to light brown, with
dolomite and calcite cement and traces of disseminated pyrite and trace to common oil
staining.
3)
The highest gas shows were observed in the bottom half of the ideal geologic window.
Background gas values varied throughout the lateral ranging from 30-300 units. No flare
was observed while drilling GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2.
4)
Overall apparent dip of GN-Halseth-158-97-3130H-2 was calculated at approximately
1.02° up. The steering team successfully opened ~9,840' of wellbore to the potentially
productive Middle Bakken reservoir rock while staying within the ideal geologic window
for 61% of the lateral.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
