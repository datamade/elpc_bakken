INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Fay 2-16HS [NW NE Section 16, T163N, R100W] is located
approximately 5.25 miles northeast of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota. The subject
well was designed to drill south at a heading of 182.39°, from [398' FNL and 2,310' FEL]
Section 16 to SE SW Section 21, T163N, R100W by the Nabors #687 drilling rig (Figure 1). The
Fay 2-16HS is intended to develop oil production from the Three Forks Formation in the West
Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Fay 2-16HS offset control was provided by the Kaiser-Francis Oil Company, Spyglass 3-8 [SW
NE Section 8, T163N, R100W], the SM Energy Company, Wolter 13-9H [SW SW Section 9,
T163N, R100W] and the Louisiana Land & Exploration Co. State Lacher 13-3 1 [NW SW
Section 3, T163N R100W] (Figure 2). The Spyglass 3-8 is located approximately 1.15 miles
northwest of the Fay 2-16HS, and was drilled in January of 2006. The Spyglass 3-8 is a
Duperow vertical well and has produced in excess of 29 thousand barrels of oil over the course
of its life. The Wolter 13-9H is located approximately 0.42 miles west-northwest of the Fay 2-
16HS, and was drilled in July of 2011. The Wolter 13-9H is a Three Forks horizontal well and to
date, has produced over 266 thousand barrels of oil. The State Lacher 13-3 1 is located 1.54
miles north-northeast of the Fay 2-16HS, and was drilled in September of 1985. It is currently a
non producing Duperow vertical well. Open-hole logs from these wells were used in forecasting
formation tops as well as the landing target for the Fay 2-16HS. The Wolter 13-9H was used to
provide the closest landing projection for the Three Forks target.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On December 17th, 2014, the Fay 2-16HS was re-entered at 1,562' measured depth (MD) by
Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55). The straight
hole was drilled to a KOP (kick off point) of 7,520' MD with a Smith 8 ¾" MDZi616 PDC bit
and a Hunting NBR 1.50º mud motor. The curve assembly, including a NOV 8 ¾" SK516D
PDC bit and a Sperry 2.25º motor, was tripped in at 7,520' MD and drilled to curve landing at
8,347' MD on December 21st, 2014. Diesel invert drilling fluid was used through the vertical
hole and curve build sections (1,562' to 8,347' MD) maintaining a 9.50-9.65 ppg mud weight for
stable hole conditions.
Sperry-Halliburton provided night tour directional drilling and
measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the vertical hole provided tie-in
orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and lateral drilling. Sunburst Consulting, Inc.
provided onsite geologic consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,274' MD. Drilling fluid was changed over to saltwater ranging between 9.55-9.70
ppg. Lateral operations began on December 22nd, 2014. The lateral BHA included a Varel 6"
6VM713DRU PDC bit, a Hunting HR 1.5° fixed mud motor, and Sperry MWD survey and
gamma tools. This BHA was used to drill the entire two section lateral to 17,575' MD.
The Fay 2-16HS was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 17,575' MD, 8,103' true vertical depth
(TVD), on December 27th, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole coordinates are 9,951.60'
south and 396.11' west of surface location or approximately 219.92' FSL and 2,577.21' FWL SE
SW Section 21, T163N, R100W. At TD the wellbore has a closure distance of 9,959.48' at a
closure direction of 182.28°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Fay 2-16HS. The
geology team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and provided onsite geo-steering consultation.
Sample logging and gas monitoring began at 7,520' MD. The wellsite geologists caught drill
cuttings in 30' lagged intervals through the curve and 100' intervals while drilling the lateral.
Drill cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions
are provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were organized and shipped to SM
Energy Company and the North Dakota Geological Survey. A Pason digital gas detector was
interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder (EDR). The Pason system distributed hole
depth, bit depth, pump rates and total gas readings across location. Total gas was recorded in
units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample logging began 26' into the Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group]. The
top is generally indicated by a transition from light brown limestone to a medium to dark brown
argillaceous limestone. The Lodgepole was penetrated at 7,456' TVD (-5,270'), and was 18'
high to prognosis. The Lodgepole top is further indicated by an increase in rate of penetration
and an increase in gamma. Limestone and argillaceous limestone compose the Lodgepole
Formation. Background gas readings within this interval were between 44 and 61 units, with
significant gas shows as high as 230 units.
Contact with the Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] occurred at 7,908' TVD (-5,722'), and
was 4' low to the prognosis. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample
returns of dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Gas shows were
as high as 434 units while gamma counts were recorded to 677 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian-Devonian] was entered at 7,919' TVD (-5,733'),
and was 2' low to prognosis. The Middle Bakken top was marked by an abrupt decrease in both
gamma API, and rate of penetration as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty
sandstone. This silty sandstone was logged as very light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite
cemented and containing trace amounts of disseminated pyrite. Additionally, trace poor
intergranular porosity and trace spotty light brown oil stain were present. Gas shows within this
interval reached 141 units, and background gas was within 52 to 86 units. Connection gas shows
were as high as 272 units.
The Lower Bakken Shale [Devonian] was penetrated at 7,979' TVD (-5,793'), which was 5'
low to prognosis. Lithology was very similar to the Upper Bakken Shale Member except for
being slightly lighter in color. The Lower Shale Member also has a sharp increase in gamma, up
to 923 API. Recorded gas shows in the Lower Bakken Shale reached 584 units, and significant
connection gas shows reached 514 units.
Contact with the Three Forks [Devonian] was drilled at 8,005' TVD (-5,819') and was 4' low to
prognosis. The upper bench of the Three Forks was a heavily dolomite cemented silty sandstone
with interbedded shale. An approximately 4' to 5' thick interval of dolomitic sandstone lies
around 4' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. Being distinct for its relatively cool
gamma signature (50-78 API) it was within this interval that the Fay 2-16HS drilling target was
set. This sandstone was described as cream to peach to off white in color (Figure 3). It was fine
to very fine grained and well to moderately sorted. Within this interval disseminated pyrite was
abundant though less common than in the green gray shale below. Spotty very light brown oil
stain was more prevalent in sandstone facies. Background gas with the preferred target interval
ranged from approximately 190 to about 360 units with shows as high as 659 units at 14,300'
MD.
Figure 3) Dolomite cemented sandstone intermixed with shale from within the low gamma
target interval, wet, 20X magnification (Andrew Kunz, Wellsite Geologist, Sunburst Consulting,
Inc.).
At the base of the drilling target an abrupt increase in gamma from 56 to 160 API was noted.
This interface between the sandstone target interval and the green gray shale below was
consistent and used as the primary steering marker throughout the lateral. This green shale was
described as pale gray to green, and soft to firm with an earthy texture. It is silty in part with
common to occasional disseminated and nodular pyrite, and displays no visible porosity with an
occasional very light brown oil stain.
Figure 4) Shale intermixed with dolomite cemented sandstone and nodular pyrite from below
the preferred target interval, wet, 20X magnification (Andrew Kunz, Wellsite Geologist,
Sunburst Consulting, Inc.).
GEO-STEERING
Figure 5) Type log of the Three Forks B1 gamma signatures as recorded in the SM Energy Company, Wolter 13-
9H MWD logs compared with the Fay 2-16HS (Andrew Kunz, Wellsite Geologist, Sunburst Consulting, Inc.).
Target interval identification was based on the TVD gamma ray log of the offset control well,
SM Energy Company, Wolter 13-9H (Figure 5). Landing depths were adjusted as formation
markers were traversed. Calculations from markers within the Lodgepole were confirmed with
the Bakken members and provided a target landing depth of 8,014' TVD. The directional
drillers landed the curve at 8,013' TVD within the cool gamma target interval. MWD-gamma
ray data correlated adequately with the Wolter 13-9H, which was also used as the most accurate
offset for geo-steering. The steering team maintained 64% of the exposed 9,228' lateral wellbore
within the target interval and 100% of the wellbore within 2' of the target interval (Figures 6 &
7). Based upon intersections with the cool to hot gamma transition at the lower target boundary,
an overall apparent dip was estimated at 0.53° down.

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Fay 2-16HS was re-entered on December 17th, 2014 by Nabors
#687. It is a two-section Three Forks horizontal well within the West Ambrose Field of Divide
County, North Dakota. 64% of the lateral was drilled within the ideal 4' to 5' primary target
interval, and 100% of the lateral was drilled within 2' of the target interval. A mud program
consisting of diesel invert (9.50-9.65 ppg) during the vertical and curve build sections, and
saltwater (9.55-9.70 ppg) during the lateral was employed. The target zone was a 4' to 5' thick
dolomitic sandstone interval that lies approximately 4' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken
Shale within the first bench of the Three Forks Formation. The sandstone displayed poor
intergranular porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil staining. Background gas ranged
from approximately 190 to about 360 units. The Fay 2-16HS was drilled to a total depth of
17,575' MD, 8,103' TVD, on December 27th, 2014 and currently awaits completion to determine
its full production potential.

Fay 2-16HS
Sunburst Geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals through curve and 100' intervals through the lateral. A
detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray marker tops have been
inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined wet and dry under a stereo zoom
Binocular microscope.
The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface casing to an intermediate casing point
of 8,347' MD and saltwater brine was used throughout the lateral to a total depth of 17,575' MD. Samples were
collected from 7,520' to 17,575' MD (TD). Two sets of dried samples were boxed and distributed: one to SM Energy
Company and one to the state of North Dakota.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
