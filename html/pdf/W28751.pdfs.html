GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Miramar 3-32H1 as a single lateral targeting the Upper
Devonian Three Forks formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The surface hole location
is in the Southwest one-quarter of the Southeast one-quarter of Section 32, Township 152 North,
Range 102 West (365' FSL & 1023' FEL). The well then traverses North to a final bottom hole
location 228.42' from the North section line and 1211.81' from the East section line of Section
29, Township 152 North, Range 102 West (228.42'FNL & 1211.81'FEL).
Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on October 7, 2014. Nine
and five eights inch diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2243' prior to the arrival
of H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8610' MD on October 9,
2014. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and chromatography
data were monitored and recorded using a Continental Laboratories total gas detector and
chromatograph Varian AGD 13. Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the
aid of lagged total gas data, lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and
gamma ray logging coupled with directional drilling survey data. Phoenix provided the
Directional Drilling (DD) and Measurement While Drilling (MWD) Services.
The build section began on the evening of October 12, 2014, at 10435'MD. Data from the Ladd
Cole 1-32 and Miramar 1-32AH, whose surface hole locations are situated Northeast and West,
respectively, of the Miramar 3-32H1 surface hole location, were used as offsets. The base of the
last Charles Salt and upper Lodgepole markers and formation thicknesses suggested the landing
TVD for the build section be raised 5' from prognosis, to 10948'TVD (22' into the Three Forks,
First Bench). This landing target held until Upper, Middle, and Lower Bakken markers indicated
the landing should be lowered to 10951'TVD, and the curve was landed at 11292'MD,
10951.56'TVD, (21' into the Three Forks B1). The 7" diameter intermediate casing was installed
and cemented in place at 11275' (11275' MD & 10951.32' TVD) on October 15, 2014. The well
path then proceeded along a general 0-degree North azimuth.
Drilling resumed on the Miramar 3-32H1 on November 1, 2014, after H&P 320 was skidded
back to the 3-32H1 from the Miramar 2-32H. On November 4, 2014, H&P 320 tripped for an
electrical issue that caused the mud pumps to shut down while drilling ahead. Drilling resumed
on the afternoon of November 5, 2014 after an electrician resolved the issue and a new BHA was
picked up.
Overall dip was projected to be ~90.05 degrees, based on offset wells in the area, including the
Miramar 2-32H. After drilling out of 7" casing, formation followed a similar trend to the
structure map, averaging a 90.15 degree dip until ~17200'MD. From this point, formation rolled
over, stair-stepping downward, but still following a general 89.8 degree dip to the well's total
depth (20705'MD, 10940.57'TVD). Overall dip was 89.97 degrees.

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
Samples throughout the course of the lateral remained fairly consistent, showing a light grey,
cream to tan, micro to cryptocrystalline, firm, moderately argillaceous Dolomite with
interbedded calcareous light blue green Shale, trace amounts of disseminated pyrite, and poor
intercrystalline porosity. The upper portion of zone is described as slightly coarser grained
(micro to cryptocrystalline) than the lower portion (cryptocrystalline) just above the Internal 1
Shale. Less pyrite was seen lower in our 8' target zone as well. No notable oil shows were seen
throughout the lateral. Background gas was very low, although did increase, eventually to around
500 units, as the lateral well progressed. Few prominent formation gasses were observed (see
total gas data).
A total depth of 20705' was reached at 21:35 CST on November 9, 2014. The bit was landed in
the lower 1' of the target zone, and was in zone 76% of the lateral portion of the well. NorAm
Wellsite Services was released following the bottoms up after running liner.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
