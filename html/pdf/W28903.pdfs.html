INTRODUCTION
Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 4B [Section 33, T156N R101W]
is located approximately 8 miles north of Williston in Williams County, North Dakota. The
Harbour 5601 42-33 4B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting
of one 9,365' long lateral drilled toward the north (1.19º). This well is an expansion of the
Tyrone Field. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,060' measured depth (MD). Plans
called for a 12º/100' build rate in the curve to ensure landing within the Middle Bakken silty
sandstone porosity. To isolate the targeted reservoir, intermediate casing was set after drilling to
11,034' MD. After intermediate casing had been set, directional drilling and geo-steering
techniques were employed to maintain maximum exposure to the target reservoir.
OFFSET WELLS
Similar offset wells were used as primary controls while drilling the Harbour 5601 42-33 4B.
The offsets utilized were the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 3T [SESW Section 33,
T156N, R101W], the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 2B [SESW Section 33, T156N,
R101W], and the Oasis Petroleum, Andre 5601 42-33 2B [SESW Section 33, T156N, R101W].
All of these wells were located on the same pad as the subject well (Figure 2). Data including
rate of penetration (ROP), lagged samples and measurement-while-drilling (MWD) gamma ray
data were used to determine marker and formation tops during the vertical hole. MWD gamma
ray data was also utilized during the curve build and lateral sections. During both the vertical and
curve-build sections, tops were compared to primary offset wells to ensure successful landing
within the target zone. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing target was calculated by
assuming comparable formation thicknesses to the offset wells.
Target was adjusted as
necessary until a successful landing was achieved.

ENGINEERING
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 4B was reentered on October 3,
2014 by Nabors #110. Drilling progressed from that point until kick-off point (KOP), at 10,060'
MD. The curve was landed with a projected inclination of 90.0°. The original wellbore
successfully penetrated the Middle Bakken at 10,784' MD (10,564' TVD, -8,369' MSL). Diesel
invert drilling fluid used during both the vertical and curve ranged in value from 9.5-9.9 parts per
gallon (ppg). The 7" intermediate casing consisted of 78 joints of 32# P-110 and 180 joints of
29# P-110 set to 11,034' MD. Casing operations were performed by DC Powertongs. Cementing
operations were performed by Schlumberger. After intermediate casing procedures were
completed, 4" drill pipe was picked up, diesel invert drilling fluid was replaced with salt water
drilling fluid. A mud motor failure necessitated a trip out of the hole at 19,324' MD to replace
the BHA. The MWD tool was also replaced at this time. TD was reached at 19:59 CDT on
November 6, 2014 to a depth of 20,415' MD (10,491.06' TVD) with an inclination of 90.77º,
closure angle of 1.19º and closure distance of 10,020.97'. The bottom hole location (BHL) is
estimated to be 10,018.81' N & 207.97' E of surface location or approximately 255.27' FNL &
2,442.97' FWL, NE NW Sec. 28, T156N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Two wellsite geologists Sunburst Consulting Inc. employees conducted the geologic analysis for
Harbour 5601 42-33 4B. Sunburst installed and monitored a digital gas detector and
chromatograph interfaced with a Rig Watch Electronic Data Recording System (EDR). The EDR
provided depth, drilling rate and pump stroke data to the M-Controller gas-monitoring software
which returned gas data in the form of total gas units and hydrocarbon constituent (methane,
ethane, propane, butane) concentrations expressed in parts-per-million. Gas samples were pulled
through ¼" polyflow tubing after agitation in Sunburst's gas trap installed in the possum belly.
Rig crews caught lagged samples under the supervision of wellsite geologists in 30' intervals
from 8,150' MD to MD. Wet and dry cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope.

Lithology
Sample analysis began at 8,150' MD in interbedded sandstone, shale and siltstone lithologies
with a persistent limestone bed located in the roughly the middle of the unit. One prospective pay
zone was recorded while drilling the subject well, the Mission Canyon.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississipian-Madison Group) is typically comprised of light to
medium brown fossiliferous limestone and dolomite.
The initial contact with the Mission
Canyon was at 9,295' MD 9,294' TVD (-7,099') MSL, 1' low to the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour
5601 42-33 3T offset. Traces of algal laminations were recorded. Samples exhibited pinpoint
porosity. Hydrocarbon shows and the presence of heavy gas (ethane) was recorded through upper
portion of this interval.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,784' MD,
10,564' TVD (-8,369') MSL, 2' high to the Oasis Petroleum,
Harbour 5601 42-33 3T offset. Representative samples of
the Middle Bakken consist of fine grained light gray brown
calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular porosity and
trace brown oil stain. Good hydrocarbon shows and heavy
gases (C1 ­C4) were recorded through this interval.

Hydrocarbon Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 ­C4) are expressed in parts-per million (ppm) concentrations.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was utilized in the vertical and curve. Salt water drilling fluid was
employed during the lateral. Background gases recorded throughout the vertical were ~10-15
units, reaching a peak value of 74 units while drilling in the Upper Mission Canyon. Methane
and Ethane gases (C1 ­C2) were recorded from through a small portion of the Upper Mission
Canyon only. Gases heavier than C2 were not observed during vertical drilling. While drilling
the curve background and peak gas shows began to increase when drilling in the False Bakken
Shale and the Lower Bakken Shale, reaching a peak value of 228 units when drilling in the
Upper Bakken Shale. While drilling the curve, peak values ranged from 16 units to 519 units in
the both while drilling in the Lodgepole. At the beginning of the lateral while circulating through
the flow line, background gases were noticeably low at ~50-150 units with gas shows reaching a
peak value of 642 units at 13,565' MD. Around 14,865' MD, as the wellbore gained more
exposure to the highly productive upper portion of the target zone, the decision was made to
circulate through the gas buster. For the remainder of the lateral background gases increased to
~500-1,100 units. The highest peak value recorded during the lateral was a show of 3,991 units
observed at 14,842' MD, just before diverting flow through the gas buster. A flare of 5'-10' was
observed for the majority of the second half of the lateral. Oil shows were described as trace
light brown spotty oil stain throughout the target zone along the length of the lateral. When
immersed in Carbo-Sol sample cuts typically displayed moderate faintly streaming blue white
fluorescence.
Geo-steering
RPM Consulting provided well site supervision. Gyrodata was contracted for MWD and
directional services. Oasis geologist in Houston, directional drillers and Sunburst geologists
worked closely throughout the well to make steering decisions to maximize the amount of
borehole exposure to the pay zone. The Middle Bakken member of the Harbour 5601 42-33 4B
is estimated to be ~43' thick. The top of the 12' target interval chosen for this well is located 10'
below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale and was logged initially at 10,564' TVD. The top of
target was bounded by a gamma low located ~2' above the zone of best porosity as indicated by
offset well logs. The clean gamma provided the most reliable steering guide throughout the
lateral. A strategy of contacting the clean gamma at least every 2,000' of MD was employed to
ensure the most accurate apparent dip calculations. Stratigraphic location within the target zone
was based on gamma ray values, sample observations, gas shows, and drill rates. API gamma
counts throughout target ranged from the low 20's to the high 90's. The overall formation dip of
0.53° up resulted in a structural position at TD that was ~90' higher in TVD than initial contact
with the target zone.
SUMMARY
1) The Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 4B is a continuation
of exploratory efforts in Williams County, North Dakota targeting the Middle Bakken.
2) The well was reentered on 3 October, 2014. The vertical hole was drilled to a kick-off
point of 10,060' on 7 October, 2014. Intermediate casing was set at 11,034' MD in
preparation for lateral operations. Lateral drilling operations concluded on 6 November,
2014.
3) The lateral was drilled in 104 hours with two 6" PDC bits.
4) A diesel invert mud program used through the vertical and curve ranged from (9.5-10.7
ppg). The salt water drilling fluid ranged from (9.3-9.4 ppg), to maintain hydrostatic
balance.
5) Samples from the Middle Bakken target porosity zone consisted of silty sandstone.
6) Hydrocarbon shows in the Middle Bakken were trace. Gas shows reached a peak value of
3,991 units at 14,842' MD.
7) Geo-steering was accomplished via sample evaluation and gamma markers. Dip
projections were successfully utilized to maintain contact between the well bore and the
Middle Bakken Member, with 96% exposure to the targeted porosity zone.
8) The Harbour 5601 42-33 4B awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate
production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
