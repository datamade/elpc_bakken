INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Beverly 2-16HN [NW NE Section 16, T163N, R100W] is located
approximately 5.25 miles northeast of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota. The subject
well was designed to drill north at a heading of 358.21°, from [363' FNL and 2,345' FEL]
Section 16 to Lot 3 Section 4, T163N, R100W by the Nabors #687 drilling rig (Figure 1). The
Beverly 2-16HN is intended to develop oil production from the Three Forks Formation in the
West Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Beverly 2-16HN offset control was provided by the Kaiser-Francis Oil Company, Spyglass 3-8
[SW NE Section 8, T163N, R100W], the SM Energy Company, Wolter 13-9H [SW SW Section
9, T163N, R100W] and the Louisiana Land & Exploration Co. State Lacher 13-3 1 [NW SW
Section 3, T163N R100W] (Figure 2). The Spyglass 3-8 is located approximately 1.15 miles
northwest of the Beverly 2-16HN, and was drilled in January of 2006. The Spyglass 3-8 is a
Duperow vertical well and has produced in excess of 29 thousand barrels of oil over the course
of its life. The Wolter 13-9H is located approximately 0.42 miles northwest of the Beverly 2-
16HN, and was drilled in July of 2011. The Wolter 13-9H is a Three Forks horizontal well and
to date, has produced over 266 thousand barrels of oil. The State Lacher 13-3 1 is located 1.54
miles north-northeast of the Beverly 2-16HN, and was drilled in September of 1985. It is
currently a non producing Duperow vertical well. Open-hole logs from these wells were used in
forecasting formation tops as well as the landing target for the Beverly 2-16HN. The Wolter 13-
9H was used to provide the Three Forks landing projection.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On December 31st, 2014, the Beverly 2-16HN was re-entered at 1,558' measured depth (MD) by
Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55). The straight
hole was drilled to a KOP (kick off point) of 7,540' MD with a 8 ¾", HDBS, MM65D, PDC dill
bit and a Hunting NBR 1.50º mud motor. The curve assembly, including a 8 ¾", NOV,
SKH516D, PDC bit and a Sperry 2.25º motor, was tripped in at 7,540' MD and drilled to curve
landing at 8,312' MD on January 3rd, 2015. Diesel invert drilling fluid was used through the
vertical hole and curve build sections (1,558' to 8,312' MD) maintaining a 9.75-9.90 ppg. mud
weight for stable hole conditions. Sperry-Halliburton provided night tour directional drilling and
measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the vertical hole provided tie-in
orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and lateral drilling. Sunburst Consulting, Inc.
provided onsite geologic consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,289' MD. Drilling fluid was changed over to saltwater ranging between 9.55-9.85
ppg. Lateral operations began on January 5th, 2015. The initial lateral BHA included a 6",
Varel, 6V713DRU, PDC bit, a Hunting HR 1.5° fixed mud motor, and Sperry MWD survey and
gamma tools. This BHA was used to drill to 16,125' MD where it was swapped out for a second
identical BHA due to low rate of penetration. The second lateral BHA drilled the remaining
lateral section to 18,331' MD.
The Beverly 2-16HN was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 18,331' MD, 7,874' true vertical depth
(TVD), on January 10th, 2015. Projected to TD, the bottom hole coordinates are 10,719.05'
north and 333.63' west of surface location or approximately 215.37' FNL and 2,600.21' FWL
Lot 3 Section 4, T163N, R100W. At TD, the wellbore has a closure distance of 10,724.24' at a
closure direction of 358.22°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Beverly 2-16HN. The
geology team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and provided onsite geo-steering consultation.
Sample logging and gas monitoring began at 7,540' MD. The wellsite geologists caught drill
cuttings at 30' time lagged intervals through the curve and 100' intervals while drilling the
lateral. Drill cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope, and complete lithologic
descriptions are provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were organized and
shipped to SM Energy Company and the North Dakota Geological Survey. A Pason digital gas
detector was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder (EDR). The Pason system
provided hole depth, bit depth, pump rates and total gas readings across location. Total gas was
recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample logging began in the Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group]. This top
is generally indicated by a transition from light brown limestone to a medium to dark brown
argillaceous limestone. The Lodgepole was penetrated at 7,451' TVD (-5,265'), and was 23'
high to prognosis. The Lodgepole top is further indicated by an increase in rate of penetration
and an increase in gamma. Limestone and argillaceous limestone compose the Lodgepole
Formation. Background gas readings within this interval were between 10 and 30 units, with no
significant gas shows.
Contact with the Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] occurred at 7,896' TVD (-5,710'), and
was 8' high to the prognosis. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample
returns of dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Gas shows were
as high as 168 units while gamma counts were recorded to 625 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was entered at 7,906' TVD (-5,720'),
and was 11' high to prognosis. The Middle Bakken top was marked by an abrupt decrease in
both gamma API and rate of penetration as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty
sandstone. This silty sandstone was logged as very light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite
cemented and containing trace amounts of disseminated pyrite (Figure 3). Additionally, trace
poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty light brown oil stain were present. Gas shows
within this interval reached 47 units, and back ground gas was within 22 to 31 units.
The Lower Bakken Shale [Devonian] was penetrated at 7,963' TVD (-5,777'), which was 11'
high to prognosis. Lithology was very similar to the Upper Bakken Shale Member except for
being slightly lighter in color. The Lower Shale Member also has a sharp increase in gamma, up
to 892 API. Recorded gas shows in the Lower Bakken Shale reached 368 units.
Contact with the Three Forks Formation [Devonian] occurred at 7,990' TVD (-5,804') and
was 11' high to prognosis. The upper bench of the Three Forks was a heavily dolomite
cemented sandstone with interbedded shale. An approximately 3' thick interval of dolomitic
sandstone lies around 3' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. Being distinct for its
relatively cool gamma signature (50 to 78 API) it was within this interval that the Beverly 2-
16HN drilling target was set. This sandstone was described as cream to peach to off white in
color (Figure 3). It was fine to very fine grained and well to moderately sorted. Within this
interval disseminated pyrite was abundant though less common than in the green gray shale
below. Spotty very light brown oil stain was more prevalent in the sandstone facies.
Background gas with the preferred target interval ranged from approximately 80 to about 290
units with shows as high as 433 units at 11,777' MD.

At the base of the drilling target an abrupt increase in gamma from roughly 56 to 160 API was
noted. This interface between the sandstone target interval and the green gray shale below was
consistent and used as the primary steering marker throughout the lateral. This green/blue shale
was described as pale gray to green to blue and soft to firm with an earthy texture (Figure 4). It
is silty in part with common to occasional disseminated and nodular pyrite, and displays no
visible porosity with an occasional very light brown oil stain.

GEO-STEERING
Target interval identification was based on the TVD gamma ray log of the offset control well,
SM Energy Company Wolter 13-9H (Figure 5). Landing depths were adjusted as formation
markers were traversed. Calculations from markers within the Lodgepole were refined with the
Bakken members and provided a target landing depth of 7,997' TVD. As the result of the curve
assembly yielding low build rates late in the curve, the directional drillers landed 13' below the
base of target at 8,010' TVD. MWD-gamma ray data correlated adequately with the Wolter 13-
9H, which was also used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. The steering team
maintained 49% of the exposed 10,042' wellbore within the target interval, with the vast
majority of the wellbore within 2' of the target interval (Figures 6 & 7). Based upon interactions
with the cool to hot gamma transition at the lower target boundary, an overall apparent dip was
estimated at 0.69° up.

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Beverly 2-16HN was re-entered on December 31st, 2014 by Nabors
#687. It is a two-section Three Forks horizontal well within the West Ambrose Field of Divide
County, North Dakota. 49% of the lateral was drilled within the ideal 3' primary target interval,
with the vast majority of the wellbore within 2' of the target interval. A mud program consisting
of diesel invert (9.75-9.90 ppg) during the vertical and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.55-
9.85 ppg) during the lateral was employed. The target zone was a 3' thick dolomitic sandstone
interval that lies approximately 3' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale within the first
bench of the Three Forks Formation. The sandstone displayed poor intergranular porosity and
trace spotty very light brown oil staining. Background gas ranged from approximately 80 to
about 290 units. The Beverly 2-16HN was drilled to total depth at 18,331' MD, 7,874' TVD, on
January 10th, 2015 and currently awaits completion to determine its full production potential.

Lithology
Sunburst geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals through the curve and 100' intervals through
the lateral. A detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray
marker tops have been inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined
wet and dry under a stereo zoom Binocular microscope. The drilling fluid was oil based invert from
surface casing to an intermediate casing point of 8,312' MD and saltwater brine was used throughout
the lateral to a total depth of 18,331' MD. Samples were collected from 7,540' to 18,331' MD (TD). Two
sets of dried samples were boxed and distributed: one to SM Energy Company and one to the state of
North Dakota.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
