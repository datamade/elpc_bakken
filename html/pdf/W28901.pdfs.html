INTRODUCTION
Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Andre 5601 42-33 2B [Section 33, T156N R101W] is
located approximately 12 miles north of Williston in Williams County, North Dakota. The Andre
5601 42-33 2B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one
~10,103' long lateral drilled towards the south (178.92º). This well is an expansion of the
Missouri Ridge Field. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,062' measured depth (MD).
Plans called for a 12º/100' build rate in the curve to ensure landing within the Middle Bakken
silty sandstone porosity. To isolate the targeted reservoir, intermediate casing was set after
drilling to 10,862' MD. After intermediate casing had been set, directional drilling and geo-
steering techniques were employed to maintain maximum exposure to the target reservoir.
OFFSET WELLS
Similar offset wells were used as primary controls while drilling the Andre 5601 42-33 2B. The
offsets utilized were the Oasis Petroleum, Andre 5501 13-4H [NWNE Section 4, T155N,
R101W], located 0.3 miles east of the subject well, the Oasis Petroleum, Holmes 5501 12-5 3B
[Lot 3 Section 5, T155N, R101W], located 1.03 miles west of the subject well, and the Oasis
Petroleum, Shepherd 5501 12-5 5T [Lot 3 Section 5, T155N-R101W], located 1.03 miles west of
the subject well (Figure 2). The above wells were selected based on their proximity to the subject
well. Data including rate of penetration (ROP), lagged samples and measurement-while-drilling
(MWD) gamma ray data were used to determine marker and formation tops during the vertical
hole. MWD gamma ray data was also utilized during the curve build and lateral sections. During
both the vertical and curve-build sections, tops were compared to primary offset wells to ensure
successful landing within the target zone. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing target was
calculated by assuming comparable formation thicknesses to the offset wells.
Target was
adjusted as necessary until a successful landing was achieved.

ENGINEERING
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Andre 5601 42-33 2B was reentered on August 29,
2014 by Nabors #110. Drilling progressed from that point until kick-off point (KOP), at 10,062'
MD. The curve was landed with a projected inclination of 90.0°. The original wellbore
successfully penetrated the Middle Bakken at 10,754' MD (10,567' TVD, -8,371' MSL). Diesel
invert drilling fluid used during both the vertical and curve ranged in value from 9.8-10.3 parts
per gallon (ppg). The 7" intermediate casing consisted of 78 joints of 32# P-110 and 181 joints of
29# P-110 set to 10,862' MD. Casing operations were performed by DC Powertongs. Cementing
operations were performed by Schlumberger. After intermediate casing procedures were
completed, 4" drill pipe was picked up, diesel invert drilling fluid was replaced with salt water
drilling fluid. At 11,325' MD ROP dropped off necessitating a trip to replace the BHA. TD was
reached at 07:00 CDT on September 11, 2014 to a depth of 20,993' MD (10,615.04' TVD) with
an inclination of 90.15º, closure angle of 178.92º and closure distance of 10,639.48'. The bottom
hole location (BHL) is estimated to be 10,637.58' south & 201.25' east of surface location or
approximately 260.59' FSL & 2,502.25' FWL, SE SW Sec. 9, T155N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Two wellsite geologists Sunburst Consulting Inc. employees conducted the geologic analysis for
Andre 5601 42-33 2B. Sunburst installed and monitored a digital gas detector and chromatograph
interfaced with a Rig Watch Electronic Data Recording System (EDR). The EDR provided
depth, drilling rate and pump stroke data to the M-Controller gas-monitoring software which
returned gas data in the form of total gas units and hydrocarbon constituent (methane, ethane,
propane, butane) concentrations expressed in parts-per-million. Gas samples were pulled through
¼" polyflow tubing after agitation in Sunburst's gas trap installed in the possum belly. Rig crews
caught lagged samples under the supervision of wellsite geologists in 30' intervals from 8,120'
MD to 20,993' MD. Wet and dry cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope.

Lithology
Sample analysis began at 8,120' MD in the Kibbey, consisting of interbedded sandstone, shale
and siltstone lithologies with a persistent limestone bed located in the roughly the middle of the
unit. One prospective pay zone was recorded while drilling the subject well, the Mission Canyon.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississipian-Madison Group) is typically comprised of light to
medium brown fossiliferous limestone and dolomite.
The initial contact with the Mission
Canyon was at 9,294' MD 9,294' TVD (-7,098') MSL, 5' low to the Oasis Petroleum, Andre
5501 13-4H offset. Traces of algal laminations were recorded. Samples exhibited pinpoint
porosity. Hydrocarbon shows and the presence of heavy gases (C1 ­C2) were recorded through
middle of this interval.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,754' MD, 10,567' TVD (-8,371') MSL, 1' low to
the Oasis Petroleum, Andre 5501 13-4H offset. Representative samples of the Middle Bakken
consist of fine grained light gray brown calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular porosity
and trace brown oil stain. Good hydrocarbon shows and heavy gases (C1 ­C4) were recorded
through this interval.

Hydrocarbon Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 ­C4) are expressed in parts-
per million (ppm) concentrations. Diesel invert drilling
fluid was utilized in the vertical and curve. Salt water
drilling fluid was employed during the lateral. Background
gases recorded throughout the vertical were ~10-15 units,
reaching a peak value of 44 units while drilling in the
Mission Canyon. Gases heavier than C2 were not observed
during vertical drilling.
While drilling the curve
background and peak gas shows began to increase when
drilling in the False Bakken Shale and the Upper Bakken Shale, reaching a peak value of 167
units when drilling in the Upper Bakken Shale. At the beginning of the lateral while circulating
through the flow line, background gases were noticeably low at ~10-50 units with gas shows
reaching a peak value of 368 units at 12,501' MD. Around 16,000' MD, as the wellbore rose to
the highly productive upper portion of the target zone, the decision was made to circulate
through the gas buster. Background gases increased to 150-275 units. The highest peak value
recorded during the second section of the lateral was a connection gas peak of 1,310 units at
17,003' MD.
When circulating through the gas buster an intermittent flare of 1'-8' was
observed. Oil shows were described as trace light brown spotty oil stain throughout the target
zone along the length of the lateral. When immersed in Carbo-Sol sample cuts typically
displayed slow faintly streaming blue white fluorescence.
Geo-steering
Gyrodata provided directional drilling services and MWD services. Oasis geologists in Houston,
directional drillers and Sunburst geologists worked closely throughout the well to make steering
decisions to maximize the amount of borehole exposure to the pay zone. The Middle Bakken
member of the Andre 5601 42-33 2B is estimated at ~42' thick. The top of the 11' target interval
chosen for this well is located 10' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale and was logged
initially at 10,585' TVD. The top of target was bounded by a gamma low located ~2' above the
zone of best porosity as indicated by offset well logs. The clean gamma provided the most
reliable steering guide as well. A strategy of contacting the clean gamma every 1,000' to 1,200'
of MD should be employed to ensure the most accurate apparent dip calculations. Stratigraphic
location within the target zone was based on gamma ray values, sample observations, gas shows,
and drill rates. API gamma counts throughout target ranged from the 20's to the 130's. The
overall formation dip of 0.17° down resulted in a structural position at TD that was ~30' lower in
TVD than initial contact with the target zone.
SUMMARY
1) The Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Andre 5601 42-33 2B is a continuation of
exploratory efforts in Williams County, North Dakota targeting the Middle Bakken zone.
2) The well was reentered on 29 August, 2014. The vertical hole was drilled to a kick-off
point of 10,062' on 1 September, 2014. Intermediate casing was set at 10,862' MD in
preparation for lateral operations. Lateral drilling operations concluded on 11 September,
2014.
3) The lateral was drilled in 92.5 hours with two 6" PDC bits.
4) A diesel invert mud program used through the vertical and curve ranged from (9.8-10.3
ppg). The salt water drilling fluid ranged from (9.5+-9.6+ ppg), to maintain hydrostatic
balance.
5) Samples from the Middle Bakken target porosity zone consisted of silty to very fine-
grained sandstone.
6) Hydrocarbon shows in the Middle Bakken were trace. Gas shows reached a peak value of
1,310 units at 17,010' MD.
7) Geo-steering was accomplished via sample evaluation and gamma markers. Dip
projections were successfully utilized to maintain contact between the well bore and the
targeted porosity zone, with 77% exposure to the Middle Bakken target zone.
8) The Andre 5601 42-33 2B awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate
production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
