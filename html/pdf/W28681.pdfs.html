INTRODUCTION
Leonard 4-12H is a North lateral Middle Bakken well located in Sec. 13, T159N, R96W,
Williams County, North Dakota. The objective was to steer the well within the 15' target
zone located 9.5' to 24.5' below the Top of the Middle Bakken formation. Directional and
MWD services were performed by Leam Drilling Systems, LLC. Solids Control services
were performed by Halliburton. Mud logging and geo-steering was performed by
Weatherford SLS.

SERVICES
Geological consulting and mud logging started on 11/8/2014. Services provided
included: morning reports, sample examination, vertical, curve and horizontal log, geo-
steering, and a final end of well report.
Samples were collected every 30' in the vertical hole and build section, and every 100'
while drilling the lateral. Descriptions included; mineralogy, color, firmness, argillaceous
content, structure, texture, allochems, porosity, oil stain, and hydrocarbon fluorescence.
Carbonate identification was determined with 10% dilute HCL- and Alizarin Red.
Hydrocarbon fluorescence was determined using a fluoroscope with a UV lamp.
Gas was logged using a Weatherford DAQ gas/chromatograph. The gas detection
system uses a hotwire to measure total gas and the chromatograph separates and
measures gases C1, C2, C3, and C4. Gas was recorded in units where 1 unit equals
100 ppm methane equivalent. Gas detection systems were checked daily using test gas
of a known unit value.
Lag time was approximated from a calculation of annular velocity was based on: pump
output, hole diameter, collar diameter, and drill pipe diameter. Connection gases were
monitored to confirm lag time calculations and thereby adjusted lag time when needed.

SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION
The majority of the well was drilled within the 15 foot target zone. 80% was drilled within
the target zone and 20% was drilled just a foot or two below the target zone. The
average formation angle was 90.63°.
Background gas was high for the first 1500'MD and the last 2500'MD of the
lateral. If production is conducted in 100' stages, I believe the first 15 and last 25
stages will yield the highest production.
The even moderate yellow fluorescence and bright milky bluish/white cut should
be an indicator to a good to moderate producing well.
Salt Water mud weights started out in the 10ppg to 10.1ppg range for the first
300'MD, then averaged 9.84PPG for the remainder of the well.
Spud to total depth was drilled in 64 days, on a three pad location, with three
trips. We tripped once at KOP to RIH with the curve assembly. After landing the curve,
we TOH to pick the lateral assembly and drilled all the way to TD with that package.
The well is now awaiting completion.

Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
