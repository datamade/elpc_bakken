Introduction
Continental Resources, Inc. (CRI) successfully drilled the Bud 3-30H1 horizontal well at
an average Azimuth of 1.64º and an average Inclination of 89.89°, targeting the first
bench of the Three Forks Formation from a surface hole location in Section 31, T153N,
R99W, (2143' FNL & 798' FWL) in Williams County, North Dakota, drilling north to the
northwest quarter of the northwest quarter of Section 19, T153N, R99W, with a spacing
unit consisting of 1,280 acres. The well is located in the Crazy Man Creek Field in the
West Central region of the Williston Basin, North Dakota. The main well used for
correlation was the Alfsvaag 1-31H drilled by CRI in Section 31, T153N, R99W,
Williams County, North Dakota. The second offset well was the Cornette POW 5300 34-
26 drilled by Oasis in Section 26, T153N, R99W, Williams County, North Dakota.
Operations Summary
Vertical Section
The Bud 3-30H1 was spud on August 18, 2014 and the surface casing
was drilled out on August 29, 2014. Brara Geologic Consulting arrived on site
August 31, 2014 and rigged up gas detection and geological equipment. Brara
Geologic Consulting started logging the Bud 3-30H1 on September 1, 2014 at
06:30 at a measured depth (MD) of 8400'. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. Redbox
gas detection system was used for analyzing and recording total gas and
chromatograph data. The Bud 3-30H1 had preset 9 5/8" surface casing at 1994'
MD. An invert mud system was used to drill out from surface through the curve
section. The first BHA used included (Bit #1) an 8 ¾'' Security MM65D, and a
Hunting 7/8 lobe, 3.5 Stage, 1.5° Fixed Bent Housing mud motor and a Scientific
MWD tool and was used to drill out from surface casing at 1994' MD. This
assembly drilled 8335' in 78.58 bit hours to the kick-off point at 10329' MD,
10328' TVD, (-8384' SS). The kick-off point (KOP) was reached at 14:00 on
September 3, 2014, and a trip was made to pick up the curve assembly.
Curve Section
Drilling operations resumed on September 4, 2014 at 22:20 with the curve
assembly. This BHA included (Bit #2) an 8 ¾'' Security MMD55M PDC, a Leam
4/5 Lobe, 7.0 Stage, with 2.5º Fixed Bent Housing and 4.56' Bit to Bend mud
motor, and a Scientific MWD tool. This assembly drilled 865' in 16.09 bit hours to
the landing of the curve at 11194' MD, 10827' TVD, (-8883' SS). The curve was
landed at 23:30 on September 5, 2014 at an Inclination of 88.5° and an Azimuth
of 20.12° in Section 31, T153N, R99W (1589' FNL & 1037' FWL). Seven inch (7")
Intermediate casing was set at 11165' MD on September 7 and 8, 2014 and
cemented.

Differential Pressure and gamma at 9162' MD, 9161' TVD, (-7217' SS), 31 feet high to
the estimated prognosis. Formation gas averaged 36 units (u) and ranged between 15u

Base of Last Charles Salt Formation Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught from the Base of the Last Salt
Formation at 9162' MD to 9382' MD (Mission Canyon top), every 30'.
Samples high in the section contained abundant amounts of halite that
was off-white to cream, semi-translucent to translucent and frosty, with coarse
grain.
Dolomite was light gray to gray, tan, light brown, sub-blocky to blocky,
firm, and brittle. Texturally, most of the dolomite was microcrystalline and
mudstone. There was generally no to very poor inter-crystalline porosity
throughout the formation. Sample fluorescence was dull yellow/orange when
observed under a fluoroscope, though fluorescence is most likely due to residual
invert mud in the cuttings. Faint milky cuts that were observed were also
attributed to the presence of invert residue in the samples.
Limestone in the samples was gray to dark gray, and tan. Samples were
mostly microcrystalline and mudstone. Cuttings were predominately sub-blocky,
firm, with no to poor visible inter-granular and inter-crystalline porosity. Traces of
anhydrite were observed in some samples. Limestone samples fluoresced dull
yellow/orange when observed under a fluoroscope, though fluorescence is most
likely due to residual invert mud in the cuttings. Faint milky cuts that were
observed were also attributed to the presence of invert residue in the samples.

Three Forks Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught in 100' intervals through the Three Forks
Formation from 11194' MD to total depth at 23110' MD/TD.
The Three Forks dolomite samples were medium brown to tan, medium to
light gray, buff to tan and occasionally off white. Cuttings were blocky to sub-
platy, soft to firm, and microcrystalline to fine crystalline with a chalky matrix.
Samples were frequently argillaceous to silty, occasionally grading to sandy.
Cuttings were tight, exhibiting fair visible porosity. Even dull yellow/green
fluorescence was observed in nearly all samples when examined under a
fluoroscope. Immediate to fast milky blue/white cuts were noted throughout the
Three Forks. Throughout the lateral there was trace amounts of blue-green
marine shale. This shale was blocky, firm and waxy. There was more of the
marine shale above and below the cool gamma zone located within the target
zone. There was also a slight trace of disseminated pyrite found throughout the
target zone of the Bud 3-30H1 Three Forks lateral. Some cuts and fluorescence
from samples taken while rotating/sliding were contaminated by the petroleum-
based lube sweeps used during slides.

Closing Summary
The Bud 3-30H1 was drilled from South to North in the Three Forks Member with
the target consisting of twelve vertical feet beginning fifteen feet below the Lower
Bakken Shale, ending five feet above the Internal 1 Shale. The well bore is estimated to
have been in target zone 84% of the lateral. The overall dip was 89.89°. Ninety foot
(90') surveys were taken throughout the turn in the lateral to total depth (TD).
Formation dip for the lateral had an average inclination of 89.89°. There were
very slight ascends and descends throughout the lateral. Throughout the lateral there
was an average of 10.2% C3, 3.6% C4 and 1.5% C5 gases with background gas
averaging approximately 859u. The wellbore ranged in the upper, middle and lower
sections of the target zone throughout the lateral of the Bud 3-30H1. There was no
significant difference in oil or gas shows in relation to wellbore position in zone.
Hydrocarbon shows dropped off significantly when the Internal 1 Shale was
encountered during drilling of the lateral.
The wellbore entered the Internal 1 Shale on one occasion in the lateral section
of the Bud 3-30H1 at approximately 12030' MD. The Internal 1 Shale was exited at
12350' MD, an overall exposure of 320'.
Nearly all samples exhibited good even yellow/green fluorescence when
examined under a fluoroscope. Oil cuts were commonly immediate, diffuse/flash milky
blue/white in color, leaving residual rings. A majority of samples were contaminated with
petroleum-based lubricant, which was used to achieve adequate bit weight to drill and to
slide. This contamination masked some fluorescence and oil cuts in some of the
samples.
Throughout the Bud 3-30H1 lateral, gas readings were considered fair to good
with a max trip gas for the lateral observed at 22845' MD with 3159u of total units. The
maximum gas show in the lateral was 4832u. The most consistent gamma marker for
this lateral was found five feet below the top of the target zone and was approximately
six feet in thickness. This cool gamma marker read in the high 30's (API) to the low 50's
(API). After total depth was reached at 23110' MD on October 14, 2014, Brara Geologic
Consulting remained onsite while rig crews spotted lube and began the trip out of the
hole in preparation to skid the rig over to the Alfsvaag well location.

Conclusions
1.) The Continental Resources, Inc. Bud 3-30H1 was a successfully drilled four-run
lateral well in the Three Forks Formation from a surface location in Section 31,
T153N, R99W (2143' FNL & 798' FWL) to a total depth (TD) of 23110' MD,

drilled from the intermediate casing shoe to TD with three trips. The first trip was
made for a motor at 17760' MD, on October 5, 2014. Drilling resumed on October
6, 2014. The second trip was made at 21561' MD on October 9, 2014 for the
BHA. Drilling resumed after the trip was completed on October 10, 2014. The
third trip was made for a bit at 22845' on October 13, 2014. Drilling resumed after
the trip was completed on October 13, 2014. A total of 11916' MD was made in
the lateral, from casing point to TD. Total depth was reached at 00:25 hours on
October 14, 2014. The Bud 3-30H1 required 21 total days of drilling and setting
intermediate casing.
2.) Ultraviolet sample shows in this lateral would be rated as fair to good with
slow to moderately fast milky cuts with residual rings. Total gas shows, although
intermittent at times, and overall porosity would also be rated as fair to good.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
