INTRODUCTION
Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 2B [SE SW Section 33, T156N
R101W] is located approximately 8 miles north of Williston in Williams County, North Dakota
(Figure 1). The Harbour 5601 42-33 2B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston
Basin consisting of one 9,744' long lateral drilled towards the north (354.72º). This well is an
expansion of the Tyrone Field. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,065' measured
depth (MD). Plans called for a 12º/100' build rate in the curve to ensure landing within the
Middle Bakken silty sandstone porosity. To isolate the targeted reservoir, intermediate casing
was set after drilling to 10,847' MD. After intermediate casing had been set, directional drilling
and geo-steering techniques were employed to maintain maximum exposure to the target
reservoir.
OFFSET WELLS
Similar offset wells were used as primary controls while drilling the Harbour 5601 42-33 2B.
The offsets utilized were the Oasis Petroleum, Andre 5501 13-4H [NWNE Section 4, T155N,
R101W], located 0.3 miles east of the subject well, the Oasis Petroleum, Holmes 5501 12-5 5T
[Lot 3 Section 5, T155N, R101W], located 1.03 miles west of the subject well, and the Oasis
Petroleum, Andre 5601 42-33 2B [SESW Section 33, T156N-R101W], located 132 feet east of
the subject well (Figure 2). The above wells were selected based on their proximity to the subject
well. Data including rate of penetration (ROP), lagged samples and measurement-while-drilling
(MWD) gamma ray data were used to determine marker and formation tops during the vertical
hole. MWD gamma ray data was also utilized during the curve build and lateral sections. During
both the vertical and curve-build sections, tops were compared to primary offset wells to ensure
successful landing within the target zone. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing target was
calculated by assuming comparable formation thicknesses to the offset wells.
Target was
adjusted as necessary until a successful landing was achieved.

ENGINEERING
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 2B was reentered on September
15, 2014 by Nabors 110. Drilling progressed from that point until kick-off point (KOP) of
10,065' MD, where a trip was made to pick up the curve assembly. The curve was landed with a
projected inclination of 90.0°. The original wellbore successfully penetrated the Middle Bakken
at 10,733' MD (10,563' TVD, -8,369' MSL). Diesel invert drilling fluid used during both the
vertical and curve ranged in value from 9.8-10.3 lbs. per gallon (ppg). The 7" intermediate
casing consisted of 78 joints of 32# P-110 and 180 joints of 29# P-110 set to 10,847' MD.
Casing operations were performed by DC Powertongs. Cementing operations were performed by
Schlumberger. After intermediate casing procedures were completed, 4" drill pipe was picked
up, diesel invert drilling fluid was replaced with salt water drilling fluid. At 12,121' MD the
ROP dropped off necessitating a trip to replace the BHA. At 15,881' MD a second trip was
executed to replace the MWD tool and motor due to a failure. TD was reached at 07:10 CST on
November 24, 2014 to a depth of 20,604' MD (10,493.04' TVD) with an inclination of 89.05º,
closure angle of 354.70º and closure distance of 10,062.68'. The bottom hole location (BHL) is
estimated to be 10,019.73' N & 928.74' W of surface location or approximately 254.43' FNL &
1240.26' FWL, NW NW Sec. 28, T156N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Two wellsite geologists Sunburst Consulting Inc. employees conducted the geologic analysis for
Harbour 5601 42-33 2B. Sunburst installed and monitored a digital gas detector and
chromatograph interfaced with a Rig Watch Electronic Data Recording System (EDR). The EDR
provided depth, drilling rate and pump stroke data to the M-Controller gas-monitoring software
which returned gas data in the form of total gas units and hydrocarbon constituent (methane,
ethane, propane, butane) concentrations expressed in parts-per-million. Gas samples were pulled
through ¼" polyflow tubing after agitation in Sunburst's gas trap installed in the possum belly.
Rig crews caught lagged samples under the supervision of wellsite geologists in 30' intervals
from 8,120' MD to 20,604' MD. Wet and dry cuttings were examined under a binocular
microscope. Cuttings were also evaluated for hydrocarbon "cut" by immersion in Carbo-Sol
(trichloroethylene) and inspected under a UV fluoroscope. One set of dry cuttings was collected
and sent to the North Dakota Geological Survey and Core Library. Complete lithologic
descriptions are provided in a lithology document as an appendix in the full report.
Lithology
Sample analysis began at 8,120' MD in interbedded sandstone, shale and siltstone lithologies
with a persistent limestone bed located in the roughly the middle of the unit. One prospective pay
zone was recorded while drilling the subject well, the Mission Canyon.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississipian-Madison Group) is typically comprised of light to
medium brown fossiliferous limestone and dolomite.
The initial contact with the Mission
Canyon was at 9,294' MD 9,293' TVD (-7,099') MSL, 1' low to the Oasis Petroleum, Andre
5601 42-33 2B offset. Traces of algal laminations were recorded. Samples exhibited pinpoint
porosity. Hydrocarbon shows and the presence of methane (C1) was recorded through middle of
this interval.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,733' MD, 10,563' TVD (-8,369') MSL, 2' high to
the Oasis Petroleum, Andre 5601 42-33 2B offset. Representative samples of the Middle Bakken
consist of fine grained light gray brown calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular porosity
and trace brown oil stain (Figure 3). Good hydrocarbon shows and heavy gases (C1 ­C4) were
recorded through this interval.
Figure 3. Middle Bakken silty sandstone (Kyle Eno, Sunburst wellsite geologist).
Hydrocarbon Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 ­C4) are expressed in parts-per million (ppm) concentrations.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was utilized in the vertical and curve. Salt water drilling fluid was
used during the lateral. Background gases recorded throughout the vertical were ~20-30 units,
reaching a peak value of 81 units while drilling in the lower Mission Canyon. While drilling the
curve background and peak gas shows remained very low (~10-15 units) in the Lodgepole, but
began to increase when drilling in the False Bakken Shale and the Upper Bakken Shale, reaching
a peak value of 321 units when drilling in the False Bakken and a peak value of 63 units in the
Upper Bakken Shale. Early on in the lateral major issues with the gas trap and polyflow line
occurred but Sunburst geologist were able to remedy the issue, after which background gas
levels of ~400-500 units were observed with shows peaking at 1,317 units at 11,688' MD. After
the trip at 12,121' MD, resulting in a 1,800 unit trip gas, the decision to circulate through the gas
buster for the remainder of the well was made. The highest gas show seen after this was at
15,543' with a peak of 1,548 total units accompanied by a ~8' flare. Background gases averaged
between 170-400 units. When circulating through the gas buster an intermittent flare of 1-5' was
observed. Oil shows were described as trace light brown spotty oil stain throughout the target
zone along the length of the lateral. When immersed in Carbo-Sol sample cuts typically
displayed slow to moderate, faintly streaming blue white fluorescence.
Geo-steering
GYRODATA provided directional drilling services and was contracted for MWD services. Oasis
geologist in Houston, directional drillers and Sunburst geologists worked closely throughout the
well to make steering decisions to maximize the amount of borehole exposure to the pay zone.
The Middle Bakken member of the Harbour 5601 42-33 2B is estimated at ~42' thick. The top of
the 12' target interval chosen for this well is located 10' below the base of the Upper Bakken
Shale and was logged initially at 10,573' TVD. The top of target was bounded by a low gamma
(~20-30 API) located ~2' above the zone of best porosity as indicated by offset well logs. This
zone of improved porosity was marked by higher gamma counts of ~100 API. The clean gamma
provided the most reliable steering guide as well. A strategy of contacting the clean gamma
every 1,000' to 1,200' of MD should be employed to ensure the most accurate apparent dip
calculations. Stratigraphic location within the target zone was based on gamma ray values,
sample observations, gas shows, and drill rates. API gamma counts throughout target ranged
from the 20's to the 110's. The overall formation dip of 0.49° up resulted in a structural position
at TD that was ~85' higher in TVD than initial contact with the target zone (Figure 4).

SUMMARY
1) The Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 2B is a continuation
of exploratory efforts in Williams County, North Dakota targeting the Middle Bakken.
2) The well was reentered on September 14, 2014. The vertical hole was drilled to a kick-
off point of 10,065' on September 21, 2014. Intermediate casing was set at 10,847' MD
in preparation for lateral operations. Lateral drilling operations concluded on November
24, 2014.
3) The lateral was drilled in 116 hours with two 6" PDC bits.
4) A diesel invert mud program used through the vertical and curve ranged from (9.8-10.3
ppg). The salt water drilling fluid ranged from (9.4-9.6 ppg), to maintain hydrostatic
balance.
5) Samples from the Middle Bakken target porosity zone consisted of a fine-grained, light to
medium gray silty sandstone with trace to rare light brown oil stain.
6) Hydrocarbon shows in the Middle Bakken were trace. Gas shows reached a peak value of
1,548 units at 15,543' MD.
7) Geo-steering was accomplished via sample evaluation and gamma markers. Dip
projections were successfully utilized to maintain contact between the well bore and the
targeted porosity zone, with 90% exposure to the Middle Bakken target zone.
8) The Harbour 5601 42-33 2B awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate
production potential.

placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
