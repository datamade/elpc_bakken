GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
The Continental Resources, Inc. Boise 3-24H1, 325' FSL, 1135' FEL, S24-T155N-R98W is
located approximately 9 miles southwest of Ray, North Dakota. Boise 3-24H1 is along the
southeasterly boundary of the Brooklyn Field, approximately 13 miles west of the Nesson
Anticline. Boise 3-24H1 is the 2nd northerly lateral originating along the southern boundary of
Section 24 and terminating near the northern boundary of Section 13. The well was planned
for one 9600' long northerly transect in the first bench of the Three Forks Formation. The
initial structural interpretation assumed a consistent up dip of approximately 90.36°, with the
terminus of the lateral bore expected to be 60' higher than the landing point of the curve. The
intent was to remain in a 10' foot thick zone in the Three Forks Formation. This horizon has
consistently fewer shaley laminations than the upper horizons of the first bench. Shaley
dolomite interbeds are currently believed to be a contributing factor to lower EUR's from
laterals with extensive exposure in those upper horizons of the First Bench of the Three Forks
Formation. The Target Zone is stratigraphically situated between 15'and 25' beneath the base
of the Lower Bakken Shale. The drilling plan included a vertical hole to be drilled to a kick-off
point at MD 10764' in the Lodgepole Formation. Directional tools were utilized to build the
curve and land within the Three Forks Formation. 7" intermediate casing was run to MD
11566', TVD 11206'. A single 6" horizontal lateral was then drilled along a 348º azimuth to MD
11908', then 360º terminating at MD 20975', TVD 11150', 236' FNL & 1324' FEL S13-T155N-
R98W Williams County, North Dakota.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Nabors Rig 784 spudded the Boise 3-24H1 September 6, 2014. Prior to the Nabors rig
moving onto the hole, a spudder rig drilled a 13 1/2" hole with fresh water to 2241'. The
surface hole was isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J55 STC casing and cemented to surface. Diesel
invert mud was used while drilling from the surface casing shoe to intermediate casing point at
MD 11556'. An 8 ¾" PDC bit and mud motor was used to drill from surface casing to MD
10066' where a top drive failure ceased drilling operations and was replaced. A trip out of hole
ensued, and a new 8 ¾" PDC bit using the same motor drilled to the kick-off point at MD
10710'. Directional tools employing a 2.38 degree bend motor was then used to build the
curve and land at MD 11439' TVD 11207' with a projected inclination of 88.4 degrees. 7", 32#
P110 IC, LTC intermediate casing was run to MD 11566' and cemented.

LITHOLOGY AND HYDROCARBON SHOWS
Vertical and Curve
Geologic evaluation began at 8900' in the lower Charles Formation. The Base of the Last
Charles Salt was encountered at TVD 9545' (SS TVD -7149). No significant hydrocarbon
shows were observed while drilling through the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at TVD 9757' (SS TVD -7361).
No hydrocarbon shows were observed while drilling through the Mission Canyon Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole "X" Marker was observed at TVD 10296' (SS TVD -7900). The
well was "kicked­off" at MD 10710'. Four gamma markers were selected and used to
calculate the curve landing point while drilling through the Lodgepole. No significant shows
were observed while drilling through the Lodgepole.
The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11176' TVD 11093' (SS TVD ­
8697), 4' structurally high to offset well Boise 1-24H. Samples consisted of carbonaceous
shale, very dark brown to black-brown, firm, blocky, slightly siltaceous, non-calcareous, non-
fluorescent with immediate milky, streaming yellow green cut. Total gas was fairly stable
ranging from 1650 units to a maximum of 3638 units while the shale was penetrated.
However, a rapid increase from 265 units of gas to 3181u was observed drilling through the
False Bakken at MD 11155' TVD 11080' (SS TVD ­8684).
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was recorded at MD 11208', TVD 11111' (SS
TVD -8715), 4' structurally high to Boise 1-24H. Samples were predominantly siltaceous to
slightly arenaceous dolomitic argillaceous mudstone, firm, tight, spotty medium to dark brown
oil stain, even dull yellow fluorescence and immediate dull pale milky yellow green cut. Total
gas units were volatile while drilling through the Middle Bakken and ranged from 200u to
2660u.
The Bakken Formation Lower Shale was encountered at MD 11304' TVD 11158' (SS TVD -
8762). Samples consisted of carbonaceous shale, very dark gray to black-brown, firm, blocky,
slightly siltaceous in part, non-calcareous, non-fluorescent with immediate milky and slow
streaming yellow green cut. While drilling the curve, total gas increased from 200 units to
2900 units while the lower shale was penetrated.
7
The Three Forks Formation was observed at MD 11391' TVD 11187' (SS TVD -8791).
Samples observed while drilling downward through the 15' section above the top of the Target
Zone consisted of argillaceous dolomite, tan to cream, some light and medium gray brown,
mottled, microsucrosic, slightly siltaceous, tight, scattered dark brown stain, spotty dull yellow
fluorescence and immediate pale yellow green milky cut, very trace pyrite. Sucrosic dolomite
interbeds were laminated with firm, light gray green waxy dolomitic, micro-pyritic shale. Total
gas was not stable and ranged from 500 to 2800 units while approaching the top of the Three
Forks Target Zone. While drilling the curve, the Target Zone top was first encountered at MD
11489' TVD 11202'. Samples consisted of argillaceous to arenaceous dolomite, tan to cream,
some light brown, firm to sub-hard, brittle, very fine arenaceous, slightly calcareous with light
gray green micro-pyritic laminae, predominantly tight to trace intercrystalline porosity, spotty
medium to dark brown oil stain, even dull yellow fluorescence immediate and slow pale yellow
green milky cut, trace pyrite. Total gas was static, excluding connection gas of 2046 units, with
readings from 200 units to 460 units. Gamma counts rose from 90 to 140 and back to 90 upon
entering the zone.
Lateral Sample Shows
Samples observed along the course of the lateral, when the well path was in the Primary
Target, varied between arenaceous dolomite, siltaceous to arenaceous dolomite, and
dolomite. Early in the lateral, samples collected from the casing point of MD 11566' to MD
11800' consisted of arenaceous dolomite, cream and tan to light brown, some light gray, firm
to sub-hard, brittle, very fine arenaceous, slightly calcareous, very fine sucrosic, trace to poor
intercrystalline porosity, spotty medium to dark brown stain, even faint yellow green
fluorescence, faint yellow green cut and very trace pyrite. Formation gas from 60u to 275u was
measured. Additional arenaceous dolomite was examined and found to contain slightly gray
green waxy shaley micro pyritic dolomite from MD 14200' to 14600' where gas shows ranged
from 110u to 490u. Arenaceous dolomite, not containing shaley dolomite interbeds, was
collected from MD 14600' to 15200'. These samples increased in oil stain from trace pinpoint
medium brown to common spotty medium brown stain in the MD 15000'-15100' sample.
Spotty bright pale yellow green fluorescence was also observed in this sample (the strongest
oil shows of any sample) and gas ranged from 200u to 1200u. Siltaceous to arenaceous
dolomite was intermittently observed in the upper horizon of the target zone from MD 11800' to
MD 16200'. These samples were cream and tan to light brown, some light gray , firm to sub-
hard, brittle, very fine arenaceous, slightly calcareous, micro to fine sucrosic, trace to poor
intercrystalline porosity, trace medium to dark brown oil stain, even faint yellow green
fluorescence, immediate faint yellow green cut and trace pyrite. Formation gas from this
lithology ranged from 100 units to 1950 units. The dolomite samples were slightly siltaceous to
arenaceous, tan to light brown with some cream and light gray, firm to sub-hard, very fine
sucrosic, trace to poor intercrystalline porosity, slightly calcareous, scattered medium to dark
brown stain, spotty pale yellow white fluorescence, faint pale yellow white cut, some light
green, waxy, micro pyritic dolomitic shale and some pyrite. Total gas produced by this
lithology ranged from 200u to 3570u and was present from MD 15200' to MD 20816'.
Above the Target Zone, cuttings were found to be predominately siltaceous dolomite, tan to
light brown, cream, some medium brown and light gray, slightly gray green, waxy, shaley
micro pyritic, firm slightly, siltaceous, very slightly calcareous and arenaceous, trace very fine
sucrosic with trace scattered medium to dark brown stain, spotty faint dull yellow fluorescence,
milky yellow green cut and trace pyrite. Formation gas above zone ranged 70u to 1840u.

Samples below the base of the Target Zone, while the well bore was in the Internal 1,
consisted of dolomite, light to medium gray to gray brown, firm to sub-hard, very fine sucrosic,
slightly siltaceous, slightly calcareous, gray green waxy laminae, scattered medium brown
stain, faint pale yellow white fluorescence and slow faint pale milky yellow white cut. Some of
these samples contained trace to some pyrite. Gas below zone ranged from 150u to 1900u.
Gas in the lateral of Boise 3-24H1 averaged 919u. No visible oil was observed in the mud
system or pits throughout the course of drilling the lateral.
TARGET ZONE IDENTIFICATION IN THE LATERAL
The Target Zone within the Three Forks consisted of a single nine foot thick Primary Target
beginning 16' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. Lithology in the target band was
observed to be arenaceous dolomite, siltaceous to arenaceous dolomite, and dolomite as
described above. A sharp drop in gamma from a high count of 125-140 API to 90 API was
observed while drilling the curve and denoted the top of the Target Zone. The lithologic
identifier of the zone top was the change from siltaceous to arenaceous dolomite, cream and
tan to light brown, at the top of zone to siltaceous dolomite, light to medium brown with gray
green waxy shaley micro pyritic dolomite, above zone. API gamma counts from 90 to 140
reflected the thinly bedded nature of the horizons above the target zone. Gamma within the
target was evidenced by API gamma counts from 45 to 80, with an intermittent stringer near
the upper boundary emitting 95 to 115 count gamma. Near the lower boundary of the Target
Zone, the lithology encountered was arenaceous dolomite, tan to some medium brown and
dolomite, slightly siltaceous to arenaceous, tan to light brown and some light gray brown.
Below the target zone, in the Internal 1, API gamma counts sharply increased from 90 to 130.
Samples were observed to be dolomite light gray to gray brown with light gray green waxy
micro pyritic dolomitic shale.
LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of Boise 3-24H1 was
replaced 9.75 to 10.5# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. The
landing point of the curve was approximately 19 vertical feet below the top of the Three Forks
Formation. 7" intermediate casing was set in the Three Forks Formation at MD 11566' TVD
11206' with a projected inclination of 88.40 degrees. A 6" PDC bit and 1.5 Deg. steerable
motor was used to drill out of the casing shoe and begin the lateral section. Upon exiting the
7" casing shoe, the BHA was rotated with inclinations approaching 90.30°, similar to the
formation dip of adjacent Middle Bakken well Boise 2-24H and other surrounding offset wells.
Gamma was also interpreted as riding the upper boundary of the Target Zone. This was not
the case however, and the base of the Lower Bakken Shale was encountered at MD 14741'.
Formation dip calculated at this depth was 90.06°. The subsequent dip calculation proved the
actual dip to be 90.02°. A decision was made to pull back to MD 13978' and begin time drilling
a sidetrack. The hole was time drilled to 14009' resulting in a successful sidetrack. Formation
dip was calculated along the length of the lateral as new recognizable gamma signatures were
encountered. The resulting structural interpretation shows a relatively flat (90.02°-90.06°)
formation dip existing from the casing shoe to approximately MD 16120' followed by a dip
increase of 90.49° to approximately MD 16500'. The formation dip continued to increase to
91.07° at MD 18540'. From MD 18770' to 19730', the formation dip appeared to have
reversed and was calculated to be from 89.89°, reversing again to 90.79° from MD 19769' to
20292'.
9
Slow ROP was experienced and a trip out of hole for a second 6" PDC bit and 1.50 degree
mud motor was required at MD 14814'. This BHA drilled to TD at MD 20975'. The 6" bore
exposed 9409 linear feet of hole through the Three Forks Formation First Bench. Rotational
drilling accounted for 9048' (89.2%) of the lateral and slide drilling 1100' (10.8%). Including the
sidetrack, the well bore remained in the lateral target zone approximately 3390' (36%), below
the target zone 2610' (28%) and above the target zone 3409' (36%).
CONCLUSIONS
Gas and oil shows observed while drilling the lateral of Boise 3-24H1 were fairly impressive,
particularly gas with an average over 900 units. Oil stain, fluorescence and cut were displayed
in all samples, not only in the Target Zone but both above and below zone. The strongest
shows were observed in the lower horizon of the Target Zone, one to two feet above the
Internal 1. However, oil shows in the mud at the shale shakers were non-existent throughout
the lateral. Subsurface structural control, utilizing data from adjacent Boise 2-24H and Boise
1-24H, both Middle Bakken wells parallel to Boise 3-2H1, displayed formation dips that varied
significantly from Boise 3-2H1 in the initial 3500' of the lateral. Boise 2-24H had a dip of
90.29° over the first 1400' increasing to 90.50° over the next 2500'. Boise 1-24H exhibited a
90.30° formation dip over its beginning 5000'. Contrarily, Boise 3-24H1 had dips of 89.88°
over the first 500' and 90.02° during the next 2200'. Overall, the formation dip was 90.06° in
the initial 3300'. Beyond that point, unique sample and gamma signatures from the beds
directly adjacent to the Target Zone significantly aided geological interpretation. Steering to
correct for inclination or azimuth was adequate until the final 300' to TD. Boise 3 -24H1 will be
completed with a multi-stage fracture stimulation after which its production potential will be
revealed. Samples and surveys will be delivered to the North Dakota Industrial Commission.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
