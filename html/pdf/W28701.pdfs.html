INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum, North America, Cook 5300 41-12 10BX [SW SW Section 12, T153N,
R100W] is located approximately 6.5 miles southeast of Williston in Williams County, North
Dakota. The Cook 5300 41-12 10BX is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston
Basin consisting of one 9,677' lateral drilled toward the south. Geologically, the Williston
Basin is an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Bakken Formation (Late Devonian to early
Mississippian) consists of four members: the basal Pronghorn Member (formerly recognized
as the Sanish Siltstone) which underlies lower and upper organic-rich shale members deposited
above and below the clastic and coastal-carbonate middle member which consists of calcareous
and dolomitic sandstone and siltstone shore facies.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the `Structure'
section appended to this report. Offset well control was essential in curve operations to
successfully land within the Middle Bakken. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray
signatures in these wells were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations in
order to successfully land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was updated
twice in the Bakken formation to ensure accurate landing of the curve.
ENGINEERING
The Cook 5300 41-12 10BX was spud on January 21, 2015 by Xtreme 21. Drilling fluid was
diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. The curve was
kicked off at a depth of 10,480'. The new curve was landed 8' low of the ideal position at
11,290' MD (TVD of 10,977' with an inclination of 90°). Following the landing, a 505' tangent
was drilled to cross into the drilling corridor. After completing the tangent, 7" intermediate
casing was set and cemented to 11,598' MD. To start the lateral, a 6 Baker Hughes PDC bit,
Ryan Directional Services MWD tool, and a 1.5° adjustable Baker Hughes mud motor were
used. The complete BHA accomplishments are tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this
report. The well reached total depth (TD) at 21,275' MD at 03:30 hrs CST on February 27,
2015, generating an exposure of 9,677' linear feet of 6 hole through the Middle Bakken target
interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies 10,463.05' south & 610.68' west of surface
location or approximately 310.31' FSL & 12.15' FEL, SE SE Sec. 23, T153N, R100W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 8,350' MD within the Otter. The targeted zone to be drilled is the
Bakken Formation.
The Mission Canyon was logged at a depth of 9,611', 9,610' TVD (-7,431'). Sunburst
geologists observed cuttings that consisted of a clean limestone that were cream and light gray
in color. This limestone was friable to firm and had an earthy texture. Occasionally a darker
gray of light gray brown, argillaceous limestone was seen in samples throughout this interval.
Samples were described as microcrystalline, banded limestone are rarely displayed. Sunburst
geologists observed trace medium brown dead oil staining throughout the Mission Canyon.
The Lodgepole Formation top was logged at a measured depth of 10,182', 10,181' TVD,
(-8,002'). The Lodgepole consisted of a medium gray argillaceous limestone. Cuttings were
comprised of both mudstone and wackestone that had an earthy texture. There were no
significant oil staining or visible porosity throughout the Lodgepole Formation.
The Upper Bakken Shale was drilled at 11,061' MD, 10,928' TVD (-8,749'). The Upper
Bakken Shale was composed of black to very dark brown, carbonaceous and petroliferous
shale, characterized by very high gamma ray (>500api).

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, unusually high gas shows ranged from
14-264 units above a background of ~14-170 units against a 9.5-9.8 lb./gal diesel-invert mud
weight. Methane (C1) was observed, starting in the Tyler Formation. Gases heavier than C1
were not observed until the Charles Formation. Methane, ethane, and propane (C1-C3) were
logged from the Charles Base Last Salt to the Lodgepole. The most promising hydrocarbon
shows of the vertical were observed in the lower portion of the Mission Canyon. While drilling
this portion hydrocarbon shows regularly exceeded 250 units. While drilling the Lodgepole
shows averaged 120 units with a background of 100 units. While drilling the Upper Bakken
Shale a gas shows of 969 units and a connection gas of 810 units were observed. The Upper
member displayed a dark brown to black even oil stain.
Hydrocarbon shows at the beginning of the lateral were relatively poor. Although one would
expect gas shows to be promising while drilling over a slight anticlinal feature, shows were
relatively low. These low shows (background gasses of ~100-200 units) continued until a depth
of 16,300'. Fluorescent cuts up to this point were less than favorable also. Cuts observed were
often slow or very slow, with a pale yellow diffuse. The first promising hydrocarbon were
logged from 16,300'-19,300'. During this period of drilling background gasses ranged from
250-600 units with connection/survey gasses regularly exceeding 1,000 units. Unfortunately,
due to heavy contamination from drill lube there were no fluorescent cuts performed at this
time. The most favorable hydrocarbon shows were observed above the target interval from
19,300'-19,860', and also from 20,450-21,275'(TD). While drilling this higher porosity
interval background gasses ranged from 1,000-1,500 units, while connection gasses would
occasionally exceed 2,000 units. The interval just above the target zone used for the Cook 5300
41-12 10BX should be considered while determining future target zones for Middle Bakken
wells in the immediate area.
GEOSTEERING
The potential pay zone of the Cook 5300 41-12 10BX was identified by evaluating the gamma
ray data collected while drilling the Oasis Petroleum, Cook 5300 41-12 11T, and the steering
practices of the Oasis Petroleum, Cook 5300 42-12H, and Oasis Petroleum, Hanover Federal
5300 13-14H. The target was determined to be 10' thick, be 17' below the Upper Bakken Shale
and 14' above the Lower Bakken Shale (Figure 3). The target interval was not based on high
porosity but rather creating a safe interval for drilling a lateral with minimal risks of contacting
the shale.

Structure maps provided by Oasis Petroleum geologists indicated that for the first ~2,000' of
the lateral the structure would dip upward at a rate ranging from .3°-.7°, before rolling over
and remaining a constant .3-.5° down for the remainder of the well. Since the wellbore was
slightly below target upon reaching casing point. The plan for drilling forward, out of casing
was to slowly rise back into the target and maintain a very low position within the target
interval. In doing this, geologists would be able to locate the roll over as soon as possible and
be more likely in remaining within the target zone. The roll over occurred at a measured depth
of ~12,900' (2,100' vertical section). Because of the inconsistencies of the gamma throughout
the target interval, the roll over was not noticed until the warm gamma associated with the top
of target was identified. The plan for drilling forward at this point was to maintain a position
between the warm gamma observed at the top of the target zone and the warm gamma
associated with the base of target. As was projected by Oasis Petroleum geologists, the
structure continued down at a rate of .2°-.4° for the remainder of the project. For future
reference, the most consistent gamma marker seen over the course of the lateral was at the top
of the target (120-130 API). This interval also displayed the most favorable drill rates and
hydrocarbon shows. This marker could be used as a base of target, and could result in a quicker
drilling well and even better hydrocarbon shows.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Cook 5300 41-12 10BX was drilled by Xtreme
21 from spud to TD in 17 days. The Cook 5300 41-12 10BX reached a total depth of 21,275'
MD on February 27, 2015. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample
examination obtained during the Cook 5300 41-12 10BX indicate an encouraging hydrocarbon
system in the Middle Bakken. Positive connection and survey shows were recorded throughout
the lateral. Samples from the target zone consisted of cream-light gray, silty sandstone and a
trace, light brown oil stain which yielded a faint, white, poor, diffuse to streaming cut
fluorescence. Lateral operations were successful in exposing 9,677' of potentially productive
Middle Bakken target zone rock to the wellbore. The well was within the targeted zone for
74% of the lateral.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
