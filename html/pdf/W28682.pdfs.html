INTRODUCTION
Leonard 3-12H1 is a North lateral, Three Forks well, located in Sec. 13, T159N, R96W,
Williams County, North Dakota. The objective was to steer the well within the 10' target
zone located 8' to 18' below the Top of the Three Forks formation. Directional and MWD
services were performed by Leam Drilling Systems, LLC. Solids Control services were
performed by Halliburton, Baroid. Mud logging and geo-steering was performed by
Weatherford SLS.

SERVICES
Geological consulting and mud logging started on 11/1/2014. Services provided
included: morning reports, sample examination, vertical, curve and horizontal logs, geo-
steering, and a final end of well report.
Samples were collected every 30' in the vertical hole and curve section, and every 100'
while drilling the lateral. Descriptions included; mineralogy, color, firmness, argillaceous
content, structure, texture, allochems, porosity, oil stain, and hydrocarbon fluorescence.
Carbonate identification was determined with 10% dilute HCL- and Alizarin Red.
Hydrocarbon fluorescence was determined using a fluoroscope with a UV lamp.
Gas was logged using a Weatherford DAQ gas/chromatograph. The gas detection
system uses a hotwire to measure total gas and the chromatograph separates and
measures gases C1, C2, C3, and C4. Gas was recorded in units where 1 unit equals
100 ppm methane equivalent. Gas detection systems were checked daily using test
gas of a known unit value.
Lag time was approximated from a calculation of annular velocity and was based on:
pump output, hole diameter, collar diameter, and drill pipe diameter. Connection gases
were monitored to confirm lag time calculations and thereby adjusted lag time when
needed.

describe.

SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION
The majority of the well was drilled within the 10 foot target zone. 92% was drilled
within the target zone and 8% was drilled out of target, 3% above the target zone and
5% below the target zone. The average formation angle was 90.61°.
Background gas averaged ~200 units for the first ~800'MD and ~191 units for the
last ~4000'MD of the lateral. Background gas averaged ~800 units for the 5500'MD of
the middle section. The gas buster was turned on for the last 4000'MD.
The even to patchy bright yellow fluorescence and immediate very bright milky
bluish/white instant diffused and fast streaming cut should be an indicator to a good to
moderate producing well.
Salt Water mud weights started out in the 9.75ppg to 10.4ppg range for the
lateral which averaged 9.93PPG.
Spud to total depth was drilled in 74 days, on a three pad location, with six trips.
We tripped once at KOP for the curve assembly. After landing the curve we TOH for
lateral assembly. At 9713'MD and 14847'MD we tripped for gamma tool failures. At
14838'MD and 15976'MD we tripped for slow ROP. The well is now awaiting
completion.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
