SUMMARY
This report summarizes the drilling of EOG Resources Parshall 27-3332H. Surface location is: 300' FSL &
1650' FWL sesw Sec. 33-T152N-R90W Latitude: 47° 56' 06.857", Longitude: -102° 12'28.184" Mountrail
County, ND
9-5/8" surface casing was set at 2010' TVD. Spud occurred on December 29, 2014. The path was to the
northwest with a planned course line of 318°. Sample evaluation and gas monitoring began above the
Charles at 7350' MD.
Kick-off point for the build section was reached at 8936' MD 07:40 hours CST January 1, 2015.
The Curve began at 20:20 HRS CST January 1, 2015.
The Middle Bakken was entered 9810' MD; 9433' TVD; -7430' SS. Curve TD was reached at 9807' MD
04:00 hours CST January 3, 2015
Intermediate Casing Shoe is set at 9803' MD; 9432' TVD; -7429' SS Location: 949' FSL & 986' FWL Sec
33 Coordinates: 662' N & 676' W
The lateral began at Begin Lateral 01:45 hours CST January 5, 2015. The Gamma was intercepted twice
during the lateral. Please refer to "Formation Tops" page for depths
Total Depth of 15233' MD was reached on January 7, 2014 at 03:30 hours CST.
Projection to bit is 15233' MD; 87°INC; 319.5°AZM; 9464' TVD; 6334' VS -7461' SS
Bottom Hole Location is 252' FNL & 2699' FWL nenw Sec. 32-T152N-R90W (Coordinates: 4717 N & 4227'
W) Mountrail County, North Dakota.
A total of 5212' of lateral was drilled, of which 5212' occurred in the Middle Bakken. In-zone calculation is
100%.
The overall dip of the drilled formation is interpreted to be: 89.91°Inc; -0.09°; -0.15'/100'.
Maximum recorded bottom hole temperature was 207°Fahrenheit.
Total Formation Mud Losses in Lateral: 1965 bbls.
Production Liner is Hung at 8915' MD and Set at 15229' MD
The Pason Gas Analyzer was used for gas detection. Maximum Total Gas from Kick-off point through the
curve measured 912 units. Average Total Gas was 135 units. Maximum Total Gas in the lateral was 2129
units. Maximum trip gas was 2101 units at 11,759' MD with a 6' to 8' flare observed. Average Total Gas in
lateral was 322 units.
Samples were collected and examined at 30' intervals in Vertical and Curve, and 100' intervals in Lateral,
and washed with dish detergent when drilling with invert OBM in vertical and curve. Salt water was used in
the lateral. Sample color was determined by examining wet cuttings and evaluating exterior surfaces as
well as fresh surfaces. Samples were evaluated for Fluorescence and Cut. Accurate evaluation may have
been affected by invert diesel contamination in the vertical and curve and mud additives and drilling lube in
the lateral.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
