Introduction
Emerald Oil, Inc. successfully drilled the Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H horizontal well
at an average Azimuth of 179.96º and an average Inclination of 89.99°, targeting the
Middle Bakken Formation beginning in Section 32, T 149 N, R 102 W, (370' FSL &
1300' FEL) in McKenzie County, North Dakota, drilling south to the southeast quarter of
the southeast quarter of Section 11, T 149 N, R 102 W, with a spacing unit consisting of
1,280 acres. The well is located in the Sheep Butte Field in the southwest portion of the
Williston Basin, North Dakota. Main wells used for correlation were the vertical and
curve sections of the Dudley Dawson 7-2-11H and Davis-Choctaw, in Section 32 T 149
N, R 102 W, and the Joel Goodsen 6-32-29H and Joel Goodsen 7-32-29H, in Sections
32, T 149 N, R 102 W.
Operations Summary
Vertical Section
The Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H was spud on November 12, 2014. Brara
Geologic Consulting arrived on site November 15, 2014 and began well site
operations on November 16, 2014. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. Redbox gas
detection system was used for analyzing and recording total gas and
chromatograph data. Surface drilling was completed on November 13, 2014 and
set 9 5/8" surface casing at 2176' (MD). An invert mud system was used to drill
out from surface throughout the landing of the curve.
The first bottom-hole assembly (BHA) used in the vertical was (Bit #2)
an 8 ¾'' Security PDC MM65D, a Hunting 1.5° mud motor, and a HDS-1 MWD
tool, which drill out of surface casing at 2176' (MD). This assembly drilled 7989'
in 86 hours to the kick-off point (KOP) at 10165' MD, 10154' TVD, (-7731' SS).
The KOP was reached at 19:39 on November 18, 2014, and a trip was made to
pick up the curve assembly.
Curve Section
Drilling operations resumed on November 19, 2014 at 13:32 with the
curve assembly. This BHA included (Bit #3), an 8.75'' Smith MDi516, paired with
a Pathfinder 2.5° bend mud motor and HDS-1 MWD/gamma tool. This assembly
drilled 1165' in 39.5 hours to 11330' (MD), where a short trip was made back to
(KOP) 10154' MD due to insufficient build rates in the latter part of the curve on
November 21, 2014.
While washing back to bottom during the short trip, the wellbore became
stuck at 10875' (MD). After numerous attempts to free the BHA, it was concluded
that a Free Point was necessary to free the assembly. After the Free Point was
released, a separation from the wellbore occurred approximately 125' from the
bit. A trip was then made and a fishing crew was called out to retrieve the lost
BHA. Fishing was successful on the first attempt.

Base of Last Charles Salt
The Base of Last Charles Salt top was picked from samples, and ROP at 9446'
MD, 9438' TVD, (-7015' SS). This top came in five feet shallower than prognosis.
Base of Last Charles Salt Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught through the Base of the Last Charles
Salt from 9446' (MD) to 9618' (MD) (Mission Canyon top) every 30' by manually
catching rock cuttings in samples.
Samples in the base of section contained abundant amounts of halite that
was off-white, semi-translucent to translucent and frosty, with coarse grains.
The Limestone/Dolomite in the samples were mudstone, light to medium
gray, very fine crystalline, slightly soft to occasionally firm, and occasionally
silted. Anhydrite in the formation remained predominantly white to off white with
occasional graying, microcrystalline, very soft but occasionally firm, amorphic
and massive.
Mission Canyon Formation
The Mission Canyon formation top was picked from samples at 9618' MD, 9608'
TVD, (-7185' SS). Formation background gas through the Mission Canyon varied from
40 to 70 units and a high of 95 units. Slow, blue/white diffuse cuts with blue residual ring
were observed, but were attributed to the presence of invert residue in the samples. No
DST was recommended or conducted.
Mission Canyon Formation Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught in 30' intervals from 9618' (MD) to
10145' (MD) (Lodgepole Formation Top) through the Mission Canyon formation
by manually catching rock cuttings in samples bags.
The Mission Canyon is comprised of mainly limestone, which are light to
dark gray, gray/brown, cream, sub-blocky, soft firm, argillaceous mudstone to
wackestone in part, with occasional trace microcrystalline limestone. Visible inter-
granular porosity was absent to very poor. No oil staining was observed.
The samples had moderate light green mineral fluorescence when
observed under a fluoroscope, though fluorescence is most likely due to residual
invert mud in the cuttings. Slow blue/white diffuse cuts with blue residual ring that
were observed were also attributed to the presence of invert residue in the
samples.

Lodgepole Formation
The Lodgepole top was picked from samples at 10145' MD, 10135' TVD, (-7712'
SS), 19 feet higher than prognosis. The kick-off point was reaches at 10165' MD,
10154' TVD, (-7731' SS) on November 18, 2014. A trip was made to pick up the curve
BHA with a trip gas of 161 units. Slow white/blue cuts with blue residual ring were
observed in the Lodgepole, due to residual invert fluid. Background gas was between
35 to 55 units.
Lodgepole Formation Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught in 30' intervals from 10145' (MD) to
11067' (MD) (False Bakken Top) in the Lodgepole formation 30' by manually
catching rock cuttings in samples.
The Lodgepole is predominately comprised of argillaceous limestone that
ranged from being a mudstone to a wackestone, shades of gray, mainly very fine
crystalline, firm-hard, occasional laminations, very earthy textures, and no visible
porosity with differing amounts of silt through the formation. Slow white/blue cuts
with blue residual ring were observed but most likely due to residual invert drilling
fluid.
False Bakken Shale
The False Bakken top was picked from samples, Gamma Ray, gas shows and
penetration rate at 11067' MD, 10909' TVD, (-8486' SS), nine feet higher than
prognosis. Dull, even yellow/orange fluorescence was observed in the samples when
viewed under a fluoroscope. Cuts in the False Bakken were moderately fast milky
blue/white but were heavily contaminated by invert drilling fluid.
False Bakken Shale Lithology Descriptions
Samples were caught from 11067' (MD) to 11198' (MD) (Upper Bakken
Top) in 10' intervals through the False Bakken. Samples were comprised of shale
and limestone.
Shale in the False Bakken was mostly black, fissile in part, hard to brittle,
blocky-sub blocky, earthy textured with occasionally waxy surfaces. Limestone
returns were mudstone-wackestone with various amounts of argillaceous,
shades of gray ranging from light to dark, occasional laminations but
predominantly massive, platy-to sub platy, very earthy textured with no visible
porosity.

Upper Bakken Shale Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught from 11198' (MD) to 11226' (MD)

The Middle Bakken top was picked from sample lithology, Gamma and

Middle Bakken Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught in 100' intervals through the Middle
Bakken from 11226' (MD) to 20865' (MD/TD) by manually catching cuttings from
the shakers. The Middle Bakken samples were light gray to tan with some light-
med brown grading to gray brown limey, argillaceous dolostone. There is usually
white with varying opacity mottling due to micro calcite inclusions. White and light
brown micro laminations are present. The shape of the cuttings are sub blocky to
sub platy to very platy. The texture is dominantly a grain stone varying from
microcrystalline to very fine grain crystalline. Visible porosity is generally poor.
Trace to small amounts of disseminated pyrite with occasional observed. Overall
cementing is dolomitic and calcite and remained true in our sample descriptions
of being a dolomitic limey siltstone.
Overall, vertical wellbore position in formation gave no overly inclusive
rule to composition of color, accessory minerals, or availability of visual porosity.
Texture ranged from being a mudstone to a wackestone, wackestone texture
prevailed higher in zone near packstone lithology, samples ranged from shades
of gray-brown and off whites, microcrystalline to very fine crystalline with varying
amounts of siltiness, overall firm-hard firmness, angular-sub angular grain
shapes, occasional sucrose texture, trace disseminated amounts of pyrite, with
blue-white fluorescent shows when cut.

Well Summary
The Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H was drilled from north to south in the Middle
Bakken formation. The target consisted of 13 vertical feet, from the bottom of the
packstone to approximately three feet above the Lower Bakken Shale. The well bore is
estimated to have been in target zone 97.4% of the lateral. The overall dip was 89.99°
Formation started at 90.05º to 90.1° in the beginning of the lateral between the
intermediate casing shoe and 13450' (MD). This section had an average of 0 to 6% C3
and 0 to 9% C4 with background gas averaging from 550 to 850 units. At 13300' (MD)
formation gas depleted to averages around 100 to 300 units.
From 13450' (MD) to 15400' (MD) a localized dip of 89.7° was observed. Gas
through this section of the well was relatively low compared to the beginning of the
lateral, averaging between 300 and 400 units with 2 to 5% C3 and 0 to 3% C4. Between
15719' (MD) and 15966' (MD) a Lower Bakken Shale strike occurred, and 247' of
exposure was put through the wellbore. Once we came out of the shale we observed
the upper third of the target was showing the highest gas readings with higher C3 and
C4.
At 16500' (MD) formation dip began to drop to 89.75° for 1400' (MD) to 17900'
(MD). Formation gases were averaging between 400 and 500 units with 0 to 6% C3 and
0 to 1% C4. Formation dip rose to 90.15° for the next 1100' between 18400' (MD) to
19500' (MD).
At 19500' (MD) the formation dip dropped to 90° and remained flat for the next
1365' to 20865 (MD/TD). Formation gas averaged 450 units between 19500' (MD) and
20865' (MD/TD).
Most of the samples exhibited trace to moderate amounts of light yellow to white
mineral fluorescence when examined under a fluoroscope. Small amounts of light
yellow oil fluorescents were encountered higher in zone. Oil cuts were commonly slow
to moderately fast, diffuse milky blue to yellow in color, trace slow yellow streaming
cuts, leaving spotty yellow to blue residual rings. Some samples were contaminated
with petroleum-based lubricant, which was used to achieve adequate bit weight to drill
and to slide. This contamination masked some fluorescence and oil cuts in some of the
samples.
After total depth was reached on December 7, 2014, Brara Geologic Consulting
was released from the Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H on December 8, 2014 while the rig was
preparing to run production casing.

Conclusions
1.) The Emerald Oil, Inc. Dudley Dawson 6-2-11H was a successfully drilled
eight-run lateral well in the Middle Bakken Formation from a surface location in
Section 32, T 149 N, R 102 W (370' FSL & 1300' FEL) to a total depth at 20865'
MD, 10950.68' TVD, (-8527.68' SS), with a bottom hole location in Section 11, T
148 N, R 103 W (274.88' FSL & 1254.74' FEL) in McKenzie County, North
Dakota. The well was drilled from the intermediate casing shoe to TD with in four
runs. Total depth was reached at 00:21 on December 7, 2014. The Dudley
Dawson 6-2-11H required 28 total days of drilling and setting intermediate
casing.
2.) Ultraviolet sample shows in this lateral would be rated as poor to fair with slow
to moderately fast diffuse cuts to slow streaming cuts with spotty to yellow/blue
residual rings. Total gas shows would also be rated as poor to fair. Overall
porosity was poor.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
