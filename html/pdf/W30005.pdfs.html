Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.

RE:
Oasis Petroleum Well Pad Sites
REMARKS
The five proposed well pads have been reviewed for airspace purposes in consideration of the
close proximity of the well pads to the Alternative 2 airport site for Williston Airport. The well
pads that have been reviewed are as follows:
Harbour Andre ­ Two Well
Harbour Andre ­ Five Well
Harbour McCauley ­ Four Well
Shepherd Holmes ­ Two Well
Shepherd Holmes ­ Four Well
The following are the facts as they have been determined:
-
The Ultimate Runway End Elevation 2,210' MSL (48° 17' 23.23"N 103° 41' 47.25"W) [This
was based on the submitted FAA 7480 Form designating a proposed airport coordinates and
elevations]
-
Approach slope 50:1 beginning 200' from the runway threshold
-
Permanent structures will exist on the well pads up to 40' AGL
-
Drilling Rig 185' AGL with work expected to begin as early as March 2014 and end no later
than March 2016 (actual expectation from Oasis is by December 2014)
-
Work Over Rigs 104' AGL will operate on the well pads through the life of the well on a
temporary basis.
Based on this information the following was determined:
-
The Well Pads are outside of the planned Runway Protection Zones
-
Due to the close proximity of the well pads an FAA Form 7460 should be filed for the
permanent structures up to 40' AGL
-
The 7460 forms for the well pads permanent structures were filed with the FAA on
December 18, 2013 with the following Obstruction Evaluation numbers (4 OEs for each
well pad to portray the four corners of each well pad):
2013-AGL-12592-OE
2013-AGL-12593-OE
Harbour Andre ­ Two Well
2013-AGL-12594-OE
2013-AGL-12595-OE
2013-AGL-12616-OE
2013-AGL-12617-OE
Harbour Andre ­ Five Well
2013-AGL-12618-OE
2013-AGL-12619-OE
2013-AGL-12620-OE
2013-AGL-12621-OE
Harbour McCauley ­ Four Well
2013-AGL-12622-OE
2013-AGL-12623-OE
2013-AGL-12624-OE
2013-AGL-12625-OE
Shepherd Holmes ­ Two Well
2013-AGL-12626-OE
2013-AGL-12627-OE
2013-AGL-12628-OE
2013-AGL-12629-OE
Shepherd Holmes ­ Four Well
2013-AGL-12630-OE
2013-AGL-12631-OE
-
The drilling rigs will complete their work prior to construction of the airport and according
to the FAA there is no need to file a 7460 for these drilling rigs per discussion with Fred
Souchet, FAA Air Traffic Specialist
-
Through the life of the wells the work over rigs and their 104' AGL height will require a
7460 form to be filed prior to authorization to erect the work over rigs
-
Given all that has been examined, there are no apparent obstructions to Williston Site 2's
protected airspace if the proposed well pads were to be developed.
The determination by the FAA will take 60-90 days and is expected to be favorable. It is
recommended that Oasis Petroleum proceed with all other necessary permitting and it is expected
that the FAA determination will be concluded prior to construction of the well pads.
As noted above the well operator will be required to complete a FAA Form 7460 in the future prior
to erection of a work over rigs on any of the proposed well locations. Conditions such as
obstruction lighting on the rig would likely be required.
If you have any other questions, please don't hesitate to let us know.
