2. Well Summary
The Hammerhead 31-26-2MBH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Middle Bakken
Member Formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled North to South,
crossing from the SHL at 428' FNL & 2275' FEL of section 26, T153N, R97W, into section 35, T153N,
R97W and followed an approximate azimuth of 181.07 degrees until reaching total depth BHL at 365.99'
FSL & 2342.34' FEL of section 35, T153N, R97W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately a 20
feet thick zone (~12' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale). The overall dip rate of this lateral was
approximately -0.27' per 100' or 89.84 degree down dip.

4. Gas and Oil Shows
4.1. Uphole and Build Shows Table:
The first noteworthy gas show appeared while drilling the curve portion of the well in the
Lodgepole Formation. It first showed up as a 910 unit gas show at approximately 10180' MD. After
this peak, gas leveled out to 150 units until we reached the Carrington Shale formation at 10700' MD
with a gas show of 874 units. The gas leveled off from there to around 150 units until we saw the Upper
Bakken Shale at 10750' MD where we saw an increase to 1743 units of gas until approximately 10950'
MD where the gas leveled off again to around 488 units. After this there were no more notable gas shows
as we drilled and TD'd the curve at 10965' MD.
