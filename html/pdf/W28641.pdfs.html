INTRODUCTION
The Continental Resources, Inc. Boise 2-24H, 325' FSL, 1090' FEL, S24-T155N-R98W is
located approximately 9 miles southwest of Ray, North Dakota. The Boise 2-24H is along the
southeasterly boundary of the Brooklyn Field, approximately 13 miles west of the Nesson
Anticline. Boise 2-24H is the 3rd northerly lateral originating along the southern boundary of
Section 24 and terminating near the northern boundary of Section 13. The well was planned
for one 9676' long northerly transect in the Middle Bakken Formation. The initial structural
interpretation assumed a consistent up dip of approximately 90.35°, with the terminus of the
lateral bore expected to be 40' higher than the landing point of the curve. The intent was to
remain in a 10' thick zone in the Middle Bakken Formation. The target zone is stratigraphically
situated between 12' and 22' beneath the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. The drilling plan
included a vertical hole to be drilled to a kick-off point at MD 10685' in the Lodgepole
Formation. Directional tools were utilized to build the curve and land within the Middle Bakken
Formation. 7" intermediate casing was run to MD 11415', TVD 11123'. A single 6" horizontal
lateral was then drilled along a 27.92º azimuth to MD 12355', then 0º azimuth terminating at
MD 21005', TVD 11049', 235' FNL & 949' FEL S13-T155N-R98W Williams County, North
Dakota.

The top of the Lodgepole "X" Marker was observed at TVD 10295' (SS TVD -7899). The well

The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11198' TVD 11087' (SS TVD -

to a maximum of 3760 units while the shale was penetrated.
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was encountered at MD 11264', TVD 11108'
(SS TVD -8712), 3' structurally high to Boise 3-24H1. Samples were varied and observed to
consist of argillaceous to siltaceous slightly arenaceous dolomitic mudstone, firm, very slightly
sucrosic, spotty medium to dark brown stain, even dull yellow fluorescence and slowly
streaming pale milky yellow green cut.

TARGET ZONE IDENTIFICATION IN THE LATERAL
The Primary Target Zone consists of a 10' thick segment of the Middle Bakken Formation
beginning 12' beneath the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. API gamma counts from 80 to 125
reflected the thinly bedded nature of the horizons in the target zone. Lithology in the target
was observed to be dolomitic siltstone to mudstone with a definite lithological change to
arenaceous pelletal wackestone at the upper boundary of the zone. This was accompanied by
a sharp drop in gamma count from 80 to 40 when drilling through the top of the Target Zone.
Identification of the lower boundary of the zone was much more subtle. The base of the zone
was evidenced by API gamma counts from 80 to 105.
Lithology both in and below zone
consisted of dolomitic siltstone to mudstone, with cuttings from below zone being slightly more
arenaceous and slightly less argillaceous. The curve was landed 3.5' into the top of the target
zone with a projected inclination of 88.90 degrees.
LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical curve portions of the hole was replaced 9.6#
to 10.0# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. A single 6" PDC bit and
1.5 degree steerable motor was used to drill the lateral section. Formation dip was calculated
along the length of the lateral by periodically allowing the wellbore to drill above the target zone
where consistent low gamma signatures were encountered.
The resulting structural
interpretation showed a 90.29°formation dip existing from the casing shoe at MD11415' to MD
12722'.
The Base of the Target Zone was encountered at MD 13323' with the wellbore
vacillating in and out of zone to MD 15128' producing a gradual dip increase to 90.50°. The
formation dip continued to increase to 90.82° at MD 18449' before flattening at MD 19278' to
90.36°. The final dip calculation of 90.11° from MD 19278' to MD 20384' displayed continued
flattening. A formation dip of 90.45°was calculated for the entire lateral. The 6" bore exposed
9590 linear feet of hole through the Middle Bakken Formation. Rotational drilling of 8641'
(90.3%) and directional slides of 925' (9.7%) comprised drilling of the lateral. The wellbore
remained in the target zone approximately 5934' (62%), below the target zone 1684' (17%)
and above the target zone 1972' (21%).

CONCLUSIONS
Oil shows observed while drilling the Boise 2-24H were fair with relatively strong gas shows.
Oil shows in samples were consistent although weak throughout the lateral. Subsurface
structural control, utilizing data from Boise 1-24H, was adequate for the entire lateral. Unique
sample and gamma signatures from the bed directly above the Target Zone significantly aided
geological interpretation. Steering to correct for inclination or azimuth was responsive until MD
20570' where dulling of the only bit used to drill the lateral necessitated longer slides to be
executed. Average rotating ROP of 162'/Hr. and sliding ROP of 36'/Hr., along with zero trips
out of hole resulted in the lateral to be drilled in four days and 10 hours. Boise2-24H will be
completed with a multi-stage fracture stimulation after which its production potential will be
revealed. Samples and surveys will be delivered to the North Dakota Industrial Commission.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
