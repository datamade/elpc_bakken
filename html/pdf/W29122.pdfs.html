GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
The objective of the Miles 3-6H oil well borehole was to drill a single, South-bearing horizontal
lateral, across the Middle Bakken Formation. This is the first of four wells to be drilled on this
pad. The well's surface location is 355' from the North line (FNL) & 1090' from the West line
(FWL), Section 6, T 150 N, R 96 W, McKenzie County, North Dakota. The engineering oil-well
plan sets the seven-inch intermediate casing at 11,107' MD, 791' FNL & 866' FWL, Section 6, T
150 N, R 96 W. The location at total depth (TD) is 234' FSL & 635' FWL, Section 7, T 150 N,
R 96 W.
Vertical and Build Sections
The surface hole was pre-drilled and cased to 2050' MD prior to the rig mobe. At 20:30 on
October 7, 2014, Cyclone Rig #34 drilled out from the 9 5/8-inch surface casing, float and shoe
at 2056 feet measure depth (MD). The vertical and curve portions of the wellbore were drilled
with diesel-based, oil invert mud (OBM). The operations company-men, Derek Tangsrud and
Keith Sproales provided supervision across the oil well drilling operations. Geologic supervision
was performed by Kim Roccoforte of NorAm Wellsite Services.
NorAm Wellsite personnel rigged up and started monitoring total gas on October 9, 2014. The
mud logging operations commenced at 8430' MD on October 10, 2014 just above the top of the
Charles Formation. The mud logging software utilized the vertical strip log and horizontal log
versions from WellSight Systems, Incorporated. The borehole's geologic information came
from; the time lagged drill cuttings, time lagged total gas unit values data, rate of drilling
penetration data and real time down hole API gamma ray data. The drilling rate of penetration
(ROP) data was imported from the Pason monitoring system (EDR) through NorAm's MiniPac
2300 total gas detector and the Varian 614 Chromates, gas detection system. The total gas unit
values were determined from NorAm's MiniPac 2300 hotwire total gas detector, which is
calibrated at the standard of 100 units equals 1% methane in atm.
As per the North Dakota Oil Commissions requirements, the mudloggers caught the borehole's
30-foot sample drill cuttings intervals for geology samples. Sample intervals changed to 100-
foot samples during drilling of the lateral portion. The geology samples were then cleaned, dried
and inventoried in order for library inspection.
Mudlogging and sample collection began at 8430' MD on October 10, 2014 at the top of the
Charles Formation. The top of the Charles was picked at 8495' TVD (15' high to the prognosis).
The Base of the Last Charles Salt came in at 9151' MD, 9150' TVD (20' high to the prognosis).
The Mission Canyon came in at 9361' MD, 9360' TVD (20' high to the prognosis). The
Lodgepole came in at 9940' MD, 9939' TVD (29' high to the prognosis). The vertical section of
the borehole was completed when the Kick-Off-Point (KOP) was reached at 10,335' MD at
02:00 on October 13, 2014.
Kick Off Point for the build section commenced at 10,335' MD using a 8 ¾ inch bit with a 5.0
stage mud motor with a 1.5 degree bend. The well plan was designed to require motor yields and
dogleg of 13 degrees per 100 feet. Based on the MS Guidance Services design plan, from the
KOP at 10,335' MD to the landing point at 11,124' MD, the bit was aimed to 207.37 azimuth to
avoid other proposed wells on the same pad. Drilling proceeded effectively without event.

The build section was reached at a total depth of 11,124' MD, 10,822.2' TVD approximately 22

horizontal wellbore landing TD, was completed at an inclination of 89.9 degrees, near the base of
Continentals' predetermined target zone stratum. The location of the 7 inch casing point was 791'
FNL & 866' FWL, Section 6, T 150 N, R 96 W.
Crews landed the curve at 11,124' MD at 01:00 on October 15, 2014. After setting intermediate
casing to 11,107' MD, crews finished cementing casing and setting the pack-off tool at 15:00
October 16, 2014. The BOP was nippled down and the well was then paused. Crews then skid
the rig to Miles 4-6H1.
Lateral Horizontal Section
Drilling ahead commenced on the South-bearing horizontal borehole on November 7, 2014 at
04:00 using a 6 inch SEC MM64 bit. Drilling started low in target at 89.9 inclination.
Directional drillers' worked on kicking out toward the Southwest right out of casing and the bit
remained low in target to 11,500' MD. Drilling proceeded to 11,800' MD turning toward the
Southwest with a Formation dip of 89.5 degrees. When drilling continued Southward the dip
changed to 90.15 to 13,000'. The bit rose on rotation to 11,900' with slides being ineffective at
controlling the bit. On November 8, a wiper trip was completed from 12,030' to 12,927' and
drill rate improved.
Formation gas, monitored as background and connection total gas unit values, remained low to
12,800' MD. Gas readings increased from 12,800' MD from apparent Formation gas with
frequent increases over 3000 units through the gas buster. Formation gas remained up for the
remainder of drilling with some increases to 10,000 units.
Miles 3-6H's, predetermined drill target zone was estimated at 11 feet thick. During drilling the
target appeared to be 14' thick. The top of the target zone is approximately 10 feet into the
Middle Bakken Formation.
Drilling proceeded from 13,000' MD to 18,000' MD with the Formation remaining relatively flat
(89.99 to 90.05 degrees). From approximately 18,000' to 18,500' the Formtion dip changed
from 90.05 degrees to approximately 89.75 degrees. The Formation came up from 18,500' to
18,750' MD at 90.02 degrees. The Formation dip changed again at 18,750' MD, dropping at
89.8 degrees to approximately 19,000' MD before flattening to 89.99 to 90.0 degrees to TD.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
