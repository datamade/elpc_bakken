Pressure Control Equipment
Blowout preventer (BOP) and related equipment (BOPE) shall be installed, used,
maintained and tested in manner necessary to assure well control. The BOP and
related control equipment shall be suitable for operations in those areas which are
subject to subfreezing conditions. The BOPE shall be based on known or anticipated
sub-surface pressures, geologic conditions, accepted engineering practice and surface
environment. The working pressure of all BOPE shall exceed the anticipated surface
pressure to which it may be subjected, assuming a partially evacuated hole with a
pressure gradient of 0.22 psi/ft. A well control device shall be installed at the surface
that is capable of complete closure of the wellbore. This device shall be closed
whenever the well is unattended. If repair or replacement of the BOPE is required
after testing, this work shall be performed prior to drilling out the casing shoe. When
the BOPE cannot function to secure the hole, the hole shall be secured using cement,
retrievable packer, bridge plug packer, bridge plug, or other acceptable approved
method to assure safe well conditions. A schematic of the proposed pressure control
equipment is attached.
a.Type: Eleven (11) inch (Nabors Rigs) double gate hydraulic BOP with eleven (11)
inch annular preventer. The blow-out preventer will be equipped as follows:
i. Annular preventer.
ii. Two sets of pipe rams, and a blind ram.

inch minimum, kill side shall be at least 3-inch diameter).
iv. 3 inch diameter choke line.
v. 2 choke line valves (3 inch minimum).
vi. Kill line (3 inch minimum).
vii. 2 chokes with 1 remotely controlled from rig floor (diagram
attached).
viii. 2 kill line valves and a check valve (2 inch minimum).
ix. Upper kelly cock valve with handle available.
x. When the expected pressures approach working pressure of the
system, 1 remote kill line tested to stack pressure (which shall run
to the outer edge of the substructure and be unobstructed).
xi. Lower kelly cock valve with handle available.
xii. Safety valve & subs to fit all drill string connections in use.

Blow-Out Preventer
At a minimum, the BOP, choke manifold, and related equipment will be high
pressure tested to MASP or to the approved working pressure of the BOP
stack/wellhead (if isolated from the surface casing by a test plug) or to 80% of the
internal yield strength of the surface casing (if the BOP is not isolated from the
surface casing by a test plug) and low pressure tested to 250 psi. Pressure will be
maintained for a period of at least ten (10) minutes or until the requirements of the
test are met, whichever is longer. At a minimum, the above pressure test will be
performed:
1. When the BOP is initially installed;
2. Whenever any seal subject to test pressure is broken;
3. Following related repairs; and
4. At thirty (21) day intervals.
In addition, the pipe and blind rams will be activated each trip, but not more than
once each day. All BOP drills and tests will be recorded in the IADC driller's
log.
e. Choke Manifold Equipment:
All choke lines will be straight lines unless turns use tee blocks or are targeted with
running tees, and will be anchored to prevent whip and vibration. All valves (except
chokes) in the kill line choke manifold and choke line shall be a type that does not
restrict the flow (full opening) and that allows a straight through flow. Pressure
gauges in the well control system shall be a type designed for drilling fluid service.
f.
Accumulator:
The accumulator will have sufficient capacity to open the hydraulically-controlled
choke line valve (if so equipped), close all rams plus the annular preventer, and retain a
minimum of 200 psi above pre-charge on the closing manifold without the use of the
closing unit pumps. The fluid reservoir capacity will be double the usable fluid volume
of the accumulator system capacity and the fluid level of the reservoir will be
maintained at the manufacturer's recommendations.
The BOP system will have two (2) independent power sources to close the preventers.
Nitrogen bottles (3 minimum) will be one of these independent power sources and
will maintain a charge equal to the manufacturer's specifications. The accumulator
pre-charge pressure test will be conducted prior to connecting the closing unit to the
BOP stack and at least once every six (6) months thereafter. The accumulator
pressure will be corrected if the measured pre-charge pressure is found to be above or
below the maximum or minimum limits.
A manual locking device (i.e., hand wheels) or automatic locking device will be
installed on all systems of 2M or greater. A valve will be installed in the closing line
as close as possible to the annular preventer to act as a locking device. This valve
will be maintained in the open position and will be closed only when the power
source for the accumulator system is inoperative.
Remote controls shall be readily accessible to the driller. Remote controls for all 3M
or greater systems will be capable of closing all preventers. Remote controls for 5M
or greater systems will be capable of both opening and closing all preventers. Master
controls will be at the accumulator and will be capable of opening and closing all
preventers and choke line valve (if so equipped).
g. Miscellaneous Information:
The choke manifold and BOP extension rods with hand wheels will be located
outside the rig sub-structure. The hydraulic BOP closing unit will be located at least
twenty-five (25) feet from the well head but readily accessible to the driller. Exact
locations and configurations of the hydraulic BOP closing unit will depend upon the
particular rig contracted to drill this hole. A flare line will be installed after the choke
manifold extending 125 feet (minimum) form the center of the drill hole to a separate

Surface Casing
Burst Design: The surface casing is designed to withstand having the casing exposed full evacuation
while drilling the 8 ¾" hole due to a kick; with a fracture gradient of 16.0ppg (verified through LOT), and
no support by cement. At 2,200ft TVD the expected surface pressure is 1,830psi, the burst of the surface
casing is 3,950psi. Therefore ADF = 2.158. During a 2000psi pressure test the casing is withstanding
50.6% of its burst pressure yielding a burst ADF = 1.975.
Collapse Design: The surface casing is designed to withstand full evacuation of the 8 ¾" hole with no
support from cement. The shoe will break at 1,830psi (16ppg) yielding an ADF = 1.404.
Tensile Design: The surface casing is designed to withstand 100,000lb over-pull while being run in
8.33lb/gal freshwater. The buoyed weight with 100,000lb over-pull is 176,808lb yielding an ADF =
2.545.
Intermediate Casing
Burst Design: Burst for the 7" intermediate casing is calculated twice, once for drilling operations, and
again for completions operations. In the drilling phase the casing is designed to withstand being fully
exposed to reservoir pressure (10.2ppg) while being fully evacuated to gas at 0.1psi/ft as the shoe is set
inside the reservoir. This gives maximum expected pressure of 4,519psi at surface. With no external
support the casing the minimum ADF = 2.755 for the 32lb/ft casing for drilling operations. During
completions phase the 7" casing is exposed to a maximum casing test pressure of 9,000psi. For
completions operations the minimum ADF = 1.383.
Collapse Design: The 7" intermediate casing is designed to contain plastic deformation of salt formations
in the last 1,500ft of the vertical hole. Overburden is assumed to be 1.0psi/ft TVD. Areas where the
35lb/ft casing is run this formation goes to 9,750ft TVD, and in all other areas where the 32lb/ft is run this
salt formation goes to 9,000ft TVD. Therefore the design factors for the 35lb/ft and 32lb/ft casings are
ADF = 1.198 and 1.336 respectively.
Tensile Design: The intermediate casing is designed to withstand 100,000lb over-pull while being run in
9.8ppg mud. The buoyed weight of 11,000ft (10,500ft TVD) of 32lb/ft casing with 100,000lb over-pull is
399,334lb yielding a minimum of ADF = 2.243.
Production Liner
Burst Design: The 11.6 and 13.5 lb/ft liner is designed to operate below 80% burst rating during the
completions phase pressure test of 9,000psi. The 11.6 is run in the Bakken laterals and is specifically
ordered to have a minimum yield strength of 125ksi in the P-110 grade and the 13.5 is run in the Three
Forks laterals. At 9,000psi the 11.6 is operating at 74% of Burst or ADF = 1.35 and the 13.5 is operating
at 72% of Burst or ADF = 1.38.
Collapse Design: Both liner weights are designed to sustan full evacuation while being exposed to a
wellbore with 3,000ft TVD of 11.7lb/gal CaCl and 7,500ft of 10.2lb/gal saltwater, yielding a net pressure
of 5,803psi. Under these conditions the 11.6 ADF = 1.526 and the 13.5 ADF = 1.842.
Tensile Design: Both liner weights are designed to withstand 100,000lb of over-pull while being run in
9.6lb/gal saltwater. The buoyed weitght of 11,000ft of 11.6 liner with 100,000lb over-pull is 208,898lb
yielding ADF = 2.011; and the 13.5 ADF = 1.861.
Centralization & Cementing Program
Surface Casing: The surface casing will have three centralizers on the bottom three joints, then one every
three joints back to surface. Cement top will be at surface and topped-off if the cement should drop.
Surface cement is approx. 367sx of lead cement at 11.5ppg and 2.9ft3 /sx, and 284sx of tail cement at
15.8ppg and 1.16 ft3 /sx.
Intermediate Casing: Intermediate casing will have one stand-off band every joint through the curve,
then one centralizer every three joints into the salts, then one every joint 100ft+/- the salt formations, then
one every joint to 1,500ft below the surface shoe. The cement top is designed to be 1,500ft below the
surface shoe. Pipe rotation and reciprocation during cement displacement is standard practice.
Intermediate cement is approx. 284sx of cement at 11.8ppg and 2.21 ft3 /sx; 527sx of cement at 15.8ppg
and 1.54 ft3 /sx. Tail cement is designed to cover the salt formations.
Production Casing: Currently (1Q2014) standard practice is un-cemented liner with swell packers.
However we are moving to cemented liners. A cemented liner will have one solid body 5.75"OD
centralizer per joint throughout the lateral. Cement will be a 15.8ppg circulated to the liner top. Pipe
rotation will be standard practice.
