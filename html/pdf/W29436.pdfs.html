Introduction
XTO Energy, Inc. Sorenson 14X-33EXH [SW SW Section 33, T150N, R98W] is located approximately
2.5 miles South of Watford City, in the Siverston Field of McKenzie County, North Dakota. The
Sorenson 14X-33EXH is designed as a horizontal Three Forks well. The curve would be kicked off in the
Lodgepole Formation and landed within the Three Forks Formation. Directional drilling and geosteering
techniques will then be applied to maintain maximum exposure of a 20' target zone over the course of one
approximately 9,825'-long lateral drilled to the north.
The Williston Basin is a structural and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the Canadian
Shield. Within the basin, the first bench of the Three Forks Formation (Devonian) underlies gray siltstone
of the Upper Pronghorn Member of the Bakken Formation and extends down to the light brown shale of
the top of the second Three Forks "bench." Through exploration and study, the Three Forks first bench,
which consists of interbedded dolomite and shale, has been developed as an extremely viable oil and
natural gas resource by way of horizontal drilling and hydraulic fracturing technology.

Engineering
The Sorenson 14X-33EXH was spud on November 5, 2014 by a surface hole rig. Fresh water drilling
fluid was used in the surface hole, diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt-water fluid for the
lateral.
The 2 vertical BHAs consisted of PDC bits and mud motors with a 1.5° bend. One trip was made during
the vertical at 8,902' due to low ROP, the second for completion of the vertical. The curve was
accomplished in 3 runs. The first consisted of a PDC bit and 2.38° motor, the second included a 2.75º
motor and a tri-cone bit, and the third consisted of a 3.0º motor with a mill bit. Due to poor motor yields
the curve was landed at 11,711' MD / 11,463' TVD, approximately 9' into the Three Forks second bench
with a projected inclination of 94.73°. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and
cemented to 11,686' measured depth.
The lateral was completed using one BHA including a PDC bit and 1.5º motor. A total depth of 21,325'
MD was reached on February 27, 2015. The bottom hole location lies 9,810.99' north & 1,083.73' west of
surface location, or approximately 249.59' FNL & 32.18' FWL, NW NW, Section 28, T150N, R98W.
Geology
Sunburst well site geologists caught lagged samples at 30' intervals beginning at 9,710' MD, just prior to
the Base of Last Salt, to TD. Rock sample analysis was performed via binocular microscopy. Formation
tops were picked using gamma data throughout the well. Further accuracy was achieved using ROP and
drilling parameters in the vertical.
Hydrocarbon components, methane (C1), ethane (C2), propane(C3), and butane (C4), measured in parts-
per-million (ppm) concentrations, were monitored by Sunburst Geologists using the Mudlogging
Systems, Inc. chromatograph to record possible viable plays throughout the vertical and curve.
Throughout the vertical, background gas averaged around 50 units. While drilling the Mission Canyon,
gas shows reached 344 units against 9.7 ppg diesel-invert mud. Gas shows in the curve within the
Lodgepole and Middle Bakken formations reached 4,487 units (u) and 5,802u against 10.0 ppg diesel-
invert mud.
Target Zone
A 20' target zone was chosen for the XTO Sorenson 14X-33EXH by XTO that began 23' below the base
of the Lower Bakken Shale and ended 5' from the top of the 2nd bench. The thickness was estimated using
control well logs, structure maps, and isopach maps to determine a potential pay zone for the extent of the
lateral--which would, in turn, help decide the curve landing target. The Sorenson 14X-33EXH curve
landing TVD gamma log was used as a guide for expected gamma ray markers while drilling the lateral
because of its complete record of the local Three Forks gamma signatures (Figure 2). The target zone was
characterized by alternating hot gamma beds (approximately 100-165 API) and low gamma beds
(approximately 50-70 API).

Target Zone Lithology
The targeted Three Forks Formation (Devonian) was penetrated at 11,416' TVD (-9,056' MSL). The
general lithology of the formation consisted of light tan to cream colored, white, sucrosic dolomite (3a)
interbedded with pale green or blue grey shale (3b). The dolomite, which was the primary lithology
witnessed, displayed trace to rare intercrystalline porosity and trace to occasional light brown spotty oil
stain (Figures 3a & b).

Target Zone Oil and Gas Show
During the course of the lateral, background gas ranged from 500 units to 1,200 units. The highest show
was a trip gas of 5,802 units at 11,439' MD against 10.15 ppg diesel mud. Higher gas shows were
generally associated with the target zone (Figure 4). Trace to occasional, light brown, spotty oil staining
was observed in dolomite samples, with hydrocarbon cut fluorescence primarily being pale yellow, slow-
fast diffuse and streaming when observed. No free oil was observed at shakers.

Geosteering
The original curve landing target of 11,438' TVD was calculated by assuming comparable formation
thicknesses with offset wells extending from the Lodgepole to the landing target gamma signature. The
False Bakken top indicated a higher landing target was necessary and the landing target was raised to
11,435' TVD. No further adjustments were made to the landing target, and the wellbore was landed at a
TVD of 11,463' within the Three Forks second bench due to poor motor yields.
The original prognosis dip profile was developed using subsurface maps of the region, as well as the full
dip profile from XTO Sorenson 14X-33A and 14X-33F wells. These resources indicated that formation
would initially have a slight down dip, followed by a flattening out. Structurally, the formation dropped
an estimated 26' over the course of the lateral. This resulted in an overall apparent down dip of 0.15°
(Figure 5).

Summary
The XTO Sorenson 14X-33EXH was drilled by H&P 456 from spud to total depth in 20 days. Geologic
data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination indicate a hydrocarbon rich system in the
Three Forks target. Shows within the target zone reached 2,844 units within the center of zone. Samples
from the target zone primarily consisted of light cream to white dolomite with trace to occasional
intercrystalline porosity and trace to occasional brown spotty oil staining. Also pale green or light to
medium grey blue shale interbeds were also present, most notably in areas that displayed hot gamma
signatures. Geosteering techniques exposed 96% of uncased borehole to the 20' Three Forks target zone.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
