ENGINEERING:
The Syverson 150-99-36-25-10H was drilled with Patterson-UTI #344. Pad
height of the Syverson 150-99-36-25-10H was 2102.6' above mean sea level with the
Kelly bushing located at 2,127.6' above mean sea level, 25' above ground level.
The Syverson 150-99-36-25-10H was drilled with fresh water from surface to
2,142' MD. A switch was then made, and oil base mud was used from 2,142' MD to
11,417' MD. Once horizontal the OBM was replaced by Brine and it was used from
11,417' MD to TD at the end of the well at 20,881' MD (see mud report summary).
Initially, the Syverson 150-99-36-25-10H was drilled as a 13 1/2" hole to 2,142'
MD, and 9 5/8" 36# J-55 casing was set to a depth of 2,136' through the Fox Hills
formation. The Syverson 150-99-36-25-10H then progressed as an 8 ¾" hole from the
base of the Fox Hills formation until KOP. Kick off point was reached in the base of the
Lodgepole Formation on July 19, 2014 at a measured depth of 10,665'. At KOP a trip
was made to pick up a mud motor with a 2.2° fixed bend and a new bit. Directional work
from here was performed by Chijioke Onyekwere and Kyle Blair, from Baker Hughes
Directional Services. The curve from vertical to horizontal was then drilled starting from
10,665' MD and landing horizontal at 11,417' MD (11,143' TVD). The curve was drilled
holding an average build rate of 12.0° and was landed on July 22, 2014. Once landed,
7" 29# HPL-80 intermediate casing was set to a depth of 11,394', through the salts and
curve to horizontal and then cemented in place.
