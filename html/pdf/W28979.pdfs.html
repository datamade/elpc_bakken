Synopsis
Slawson Exploration's Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH [NE SW Section 14, T154N, R99W] is located
~13 miles East of the city of Williston, North Dakota. The Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH is a production
well drilled in the North Stockyard Creek field of Williams County. A single lateral leg trending
61.87°, with a 3° per 100' turn from the beginning of the lateral resulting in an azimuth of 90.00,
was proposed to be drilled from the NE SW corner of Section 14 to the NE NE corner of Section
13. The lateral was targeting the porous crystalline dolomite of the Three Forks 1st Bench.
Control Wells
There were many control wells utilized as offsets for the Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH, including the
Ironbank 5-14-13 TFH. The main completed well used as a control offset to Ironbank 6-14-13
TFH was the Everett 1-15H.
The Everett 1-15H [SE SE Section 15, T154N, R99W] is a horizontal production well drilled by
Zavanna shortly before the subject well. Everett 1-15H is located approximately 3,000 East of
the Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH surface location. The Everett 1-15H lateral was drilled to the North
West. The subject well was drilled to the East so constant gamma correlation between markers
and usage of a structure map composed by Slawson Geology were pertinent tools during the
horizontal since all adjacent wells were drilled in a differing directions, with the exception of the
Ironbank 4-14-13 TFH and Ironbank 5-14-13 TFH which were 2,500' to the North. During the
curve, the gamma ray that was produced was constantly compared to the Everett 1-15H,
Ironbank 5-14-13TFH and Ironbank 4-14-13 TFH wells. Gamma ray was used to help determine
a proper landing depth.
To assist in the choosing of an initial target, an isopach table (Table 1) was constructed to
measure the distance of formation tops to the target depth determined from the Everett 1-15H
offset well. Comparison of gamma ray markers showed the most consistent isopachs for up hole
formations to be the base of the Charles Salt.

Geologic Assessment
Methods
Geologic supervision of Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH was provided by two experienced RPM well site
geologists. Gas and chromatograph levels were measured using National Oilfield Varco real time
gas detector and chromatograph system. The NOV gas detection system uses non-dispersive
infrared and chemical sensor gas detection to evaluate gases liberated from the formation by the
drill bit and carried to the surface by the drilling fluid.
The NOV was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder system. Pason provided drill rate,
on-off bottom and pump strokes to the NOV and received total gas information from the
Bloodhound for viewing on location and remotely.
Geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals in the vertical hole, from 8,800' to 10,729'
MD; 30' intervals in the curve build from 10,729' to 11,754' MD; and 50' samples in the lateral
from 11,754' to TD at 19,216'.
Sampled drill cuttings were examined wet and dry under a binocular microscope using both plain
(broad spectrum) and transmitted light. Cuttings were evaluated for hydrocarbon cut during the
lateral utilizing Entron AE solution. 10% hydrochloric acid and alizarin red were used to
determine the calcareous and dolomitic content of rocks and cementing.
RPM Geologic, Inc. (RPM) well site geologists also closely examined MWD gamma-ray
information and penetration rates to aid in steering decisions and dip degree estimations.
Vertical Operations
Overview
The Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH was spud out of surface on 12nd November, 2014 utilizing Frontier
24 as the drilling contractor. Prior to commencement of RPM mud logging services, a 13 1/2"
hole was drilled with fresh water to depth of 2,412' and isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J-55 casing
cemented to surface.
RPM well site geologists began logging the vertical at 8,800' measured depth at 30' intervals.
Two PDC bits were used to drill out of surface casing to a Vertical TD depth of 10,720'. Bit #1
(8 ¾" Smith) PDC was used to drill from 2,412' to 5,908' and averaged an ROP of 241.1 ft/hr in
26.5 hours of use. Bit #2 (8 ¾" Smith) PDC was used to drill from 5,908' to 10,719' and
averaged an ROP of 67.5 ft/hr in 71.3 hours of use to TD the vertical.
Diesel invert drilling fluid, with a mud weight ranging from 9.6 to 9.9 ppg, was used for the
vertical hole after surface casing. The vertical was drilled to a depth of 10,720' TVD, into the
Lodgepole formation.

Schlumberger provided equipment and personnel for MWD services as well as equipment and

depth during the curve and should serve as a good reference point for future curve landings in the
immediate area.

Lateral
Overview
Drilling fluid consisting of salt water brine (9.8-10.8 ppg) was used while drilling the lateral
section up to the TD of 19,216'. Four 6" PDC bits were used to drill the lateral.
Bit # 4 (6" Hughes), was a PDC bit used to drill the lateral to a depth of 14,043'. It was used for
34.8 hours and averaged 65.8 feet per hour and footage of 2,289'. Bit # 5 (6" Security), was a
PDC bit used to drill the lateral to a total depth of 16,509'. It was used for 34.5 hours and
averaged 71.5 feet per hour and footage of 2,466'. Bit # 6 (6" Security), was a PDC bit used to
drill the lateral to a depth of 17,243'. It was used for 12 hours and averaged 61.2 feet per hour
and footage of 734'. Bit # 7 (6" Hughes), was a PDC bit used to drill the lateral to a total depth
of 19,216'. It was used for 26.3 hours and averaged 75.0 feet per hour and footage of 1,973'.
Drilling operations were concluded at 17:50 CST on 15 December 2014 at a bottom hole
location of 580.51' N & 8099.13' E of surface location or approx. 1955.22' FSL & 250.73' FEL
NE SE Sec 13 T154 R99W.

Gas and hydrocarbon Shows
It was found that the gas and hydrocarbon shows were excellent indicator of placement in target
throughout the lateral. Gas shows decreased as the wellbore dropped towards the claystone and
increased as it rose towards the Lower Bakken Shale. Background gas started around 450 units
and rose to approximately 3,500 units as more wellbore was exposed with connection peaks
exceeding 5,000 units.
The NOV gas chromatograph showed components of Methane and Propane present through the
entire lateral. Sample cuttings were very good throughout the lateral. The testing of cuts through
fluorocarbon methodology was used every 200' of the lateral and showed a consistent slight
streaming yellow cut regardless of placement within the target window.
Summary
1) The Ironbank 6-14-13 TFH was initially spud for drilling at surface on 12 November,
2014 as a production well in the Stockyard Creek field of Williams County, North
Dakota. The well was spud by Frontier 24 and the vertical hole was drilled to a total
depth of 10,720'.
2) A mud program consisted of diesel invert (9.6 ­ 10.8 ppg) was utilized to drill the vertical
past surface casing and curve build. Salt water Brine was utilized in the lateral averaging
9.8-10.1 ppg.
3) Seven bits were utilized to drill the well to TD. One 13 ½" bit was used to drill surface.
Two Smith 8 ¾" bits were used to drill the vertical. One T505 8 ¾" bit was used to drill
the curve. Two Security 6" bit and two Hughes were used to drill the lateral to 19,216'
(TD).
4) The lateral was targeting the Three Forks porosity ranging from about 20' below the
Lower Bakken Shale down to approximately 31' below the Lower Bakken Shale or
approximately 1' above the Torquay Claystone. The top of the target zone was marked
by cool gamma reading 60-80 API and the bottom was marked by warmer gamma
reading over 120 API.
5) Gas levels between 3,500 and 4,500 units were maintained through the lateral drilling
operations of the Three Forks. Connection and trip gases regularly exceeded 5,000 units.
6) The Three Forks target consisted of one observed gross lithology. The target Zone of the
Three Forks was observed to be crystalline dolomite with apparent intergranular
porosity which was capped off by much tighter siltstone and interbedded claytone layers.
7) Moderate oil stain was present in sample during the entire lateral along with a moderate
blue to white diffuse cut and a slight streaming yellow cut.
8) Drilling operations were concluded at 17:50 CST on 15 December 2014 at a bottom hole
location of 580.51' N & 8099.13' E of surface location or approx. 1955.22' FSL &
250.73' FEL, NE SE Sec 13, T154, R99W.
9) Exposure to target zone was 100% of the lateral. The Slawson Exploration's Ironbank 6-
14-13 TFH awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate production potential.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

7" casing testing procedure
1) Logging
a. Run bond log to top of L/H/P and ensure adequate cement coverage (at least 500' above top of zone to
be fractured)
b. Run casing inspection log in 7" casing to top of L/H/P or 100' beneath cement top
i. De-rate casing for any significant wear based on remaining wall thickness.
ii. Set maximum treating pressure at no more than 85% of the normal or de-rated pressure rating -
whichever applies.
2) Casing testing
a. RU wireline and set a test plug in the top of the Liner/Hanger/Packer.
b. Pressure test the 7" casing and wellhead as follows:
i. In new areas where treating pressures are unknown, test to 7500# for 30 minutes with less than
a 5% loss
ii. In areas with established operations, test to 500# above fracturing pressures seen in that area for
30 minutes with less than a 5% loss
iii. In the case of worn casing, if 85% of the de-rated pressure rating results in a maximum
allowable treating pressure that is less than 500# greater than the expected treating pressure ­
run a tie-back string
3) Pressure relief valve ­ an adequately sized pressure relief valve must be installed on the treating lines from
the pump to the wellhead and set to limit the line pressure to no more than the casing test pressure less 100#.
4) Surface casing valve ­ the surface casing valve must be left open and a diversion line run to a pit or
containment vessel; in order to enable relief of any pressure encountered in the surface casing/intermediate
casing annulus during fracture operations.
