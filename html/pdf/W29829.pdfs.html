INTRODUCTION
The Thompson 6-29-32BHD is a horizontal well targeting the Middle Bakken. The surface
location of the well is in the northwest quarter of the northeast quarter of Section 29, Township
150 North, Range 95 West, in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Lateral Section of the well
enters the objective Middle Bakken at 10,883' MD, 10,708.97' TVD (-8,422.97' SS) in the
northwest quarter of the northeast quarter of Section 29 and reaches its total depth of 20,513'
MD, 10,826.15' TVD (-8540.15' SS) in the southwest quarter of the southeast quarter of Section
32 Township 150 North, Range 95 West. The total length of the Lateral Section is 10,074' MD.
Starting at the casing point of 10,970' MD to 20,513' MD, 100% (9,543' MD) of the borehole is
in the objective Middle Bakken formation. The overall dip was approximately 89.37º and its
thickness is approximately 38' based on measured interval thicknesses within the formation.
The path in the Build Section of the Thompson 6-29-32BHD well is north heading at a target
azimuth of 191º overall from the surface location in section 29, to the casing point located at the
northwest quarter of the northeast quarter of section 29. The well path continues in a southern
direction to the well's total depth of 20,513' MD in the southwest quarter of the southeast quarter
of Section 32 at a point 9,641.16' South and 178.18' West of the 0' N/S, 0' E/W surface location.

Mudlogging for the Thompson 6-29-32BHD provided by Weatherford Surface Logging Systems,
Dickinson, ND and commenced at 6,000' MD. Samples of drill cuttings were collected at 30'
intervals off the shaker table from this point to the landing depth of the vertical and curve section.

fluorescence and cut. Carbonate identification was determined with 10% dilute HCL and alizarin
red. Hydrocarbon fluorescence was determined using a fluoroscope with a UV lamp.

6.0 SAMPLE DESCRIPTIONS
The vertical well and curve were drilled in oil-based inverted mud, all drill cutting
samples were thoroughly washed with diesel, degreaser, soap and hot water prior to
examination. Despite these efforts, sample shows may not accurately represent the rocks
"in situ" since it is very difficult to remove all of the inverted mud from the cuttings. The
horizontal well was drilled in salt water and washed with fresh water, allowing for a
better assessment of the hydrocarbon shows.
Detailed descriptions of samples logged are provided in the mud log and horizontal log.
A typical description of select sections of the Middle Bakken is provided below:
Directly Below Shoal (top half of target window): Dolomite- predominantly light to
medium gray with scattered light and dark brown, tanish gray scattered black, soft to
moderately firm, very fine microcrystalline grain size, very fine granular texture,
common earthy texture, very argillaceous, very calcitic, silty-sand, scattered trace pyrite,
scattered light-dark limestone, trace platy shale, spoty to even dull yellow fluourescence,
fast bright bluish white steaming/diffusing cut.
Ten to Twenty Feet Below Shoal (lower half of target window): Dolomite- tan to light
gray, medium gray, light brown, white to cream, scattered dark gray, soft to slightly firm,
microcrystalline grain size, earthy to very fine granular texture, sub round to sub angular,
silty, sandy, slightly argillaceous, very calcitic, good intercrystalline porosity, even dull
yellow fluourescence, faint to moderately bright bluish white milky to slow streaming
cut.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,

NOTES ON 9-5/8" SURFACE CASING DESIGN:
The key point in designing surface casing is to extend the shoe of the surface casing beyond the top of
the Pierre Shale. This ensures that all potable or usable water zones are covered. At this point in the well
design, the drilling fluid is changed to oil base mud to contend with salt sections that occur at
approximately 6,670' TVD and 9,050' TVD. The 36 ppf J55 casing accomplishes these criteria easily.
A higher burst rating is not necessary based on industry standards throughout the Williston Basin.
In the event the surface casing burst rating was increased, the normally pressured and sub normally
pressured zones all the way thru the Madison Group at approximately 8,400' TVD has the potential to
break down. Therefore the increased pressure rating of surface casing is not warranted. It is not feasible
to set the surface casing point at a greater depth because the shut in surface pressures would not
appreciably increase. The Dakota formation (5,000' TVD) is frequently weak. Typically, we drill the
intermediate section with 10.5 ppg OBM to the 7" casing point. In the event we lost circulation in the
exposed intermediate interval, the wellbore will not be entirely evacuated and the surface casing can be
shut in without compromising the pressure rating of the 36 ppf J-55.

Additional Information For Oil Base Mud
A. Oil-base mud will be mixed in the closed circulating system and transferred to 500-bbl tanks on
location for storage prior to and after drilling operations. Drip pans will be installed below the
rotary beams on the substructure and can be viewed on site from the cellar area. As the intermediate
and production section of the hole is drilled, the cuttings transported to the surface with the drilling
fluid will be mechanically separated from the drilling fluid as waste by shale-shakers and then
cleaned/dried via a mud cleaner and/or centrifuge. These separated cuttings will be transferred to a
container, dried and stabilized, and then transported to an approved disposal site or, if a cuttings pit
is approved, the solidified and dried cuttings will be stored in the pit.
B. Plastic material will underlay the rig, oil base mud/diesel storage tanks and mud pits. All tanks on
location will be placed inside of berms. Any oily waste fluids and sediments generated at the work
site during drilling operations or when cleaning the fluid containment system after drilling will also
be stabilized and then transported to an approved disposal site or, if a cuttings pit is approved, it will
be solidified and stored in the cuttings pit.
C. All rig ditches will be lined and directed to a lined sump for fluid recovery. A drip pan will be
installed on the BOP stack, a mud bucket will be utilized as needed on connections and a vacuum
system will be used on the rig floor for fluid recovery in those areas.
