Well Evaluation
XTO David Federal 21X-20A
INTRODUCTION
The David Federal 21X-20A is a horizontal well, targeting the Middle Bakken Member, operated
by XTO Energy, Inc. in the Lost Bridge Field. It is located approximately 25 miles northwest of
Killdeer in Dunn County, North Dakota [NE NW Section 20, T148N, R96W]. The vertical and
curve will be drilled using a diesel invert mud system. Plans call for a kick-off of 10,265' in the
Lodgepole and for the curve to be built at 14°/100' until reaching an approximate 90° inclination
in the Middle Bakken. Seven-inch intermediate casing would then be set to isolate the target
reservoir. After casing, a 6" horizontal wellbore will be drilled into Section 29 using salt water
drilling fluid.
OFFSET INFORMATION
Three surrounding wells were used as offset control in development of the prognosis. Electric
logs and geologic data from these offset wells provided control for formation tops and assistance
with interpretation of possible structure and gamma trends for the David Federal 21X-20A target
zone. The closest offset well, the XTO Energy, Inc., David Federal 21X-20B (#29007) [NE NW
Sec. 20, T148N, R96W] is located approximately 60 feet southeast of the subject well. The XTO
Energy, Inc., David Federal 21X-20F (#29008) [NE NW Sec. 20, T148N, R96W] is located
approximately 90 feet southeast of the subject well. The Denbury Onshore, LLC, Lost Bridge
State 1-16 (#08448) [NE NE Sec. 16, T148N, R96W] is located approximately 1.9 miles
northeast of the subject well.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
To gather information for possible future development, lithologic descriptions and sample
analysis began in the Charles Formation.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,692' TVD (-8,735 MSL). It consists primarily of
silty sandstone and contains trace amounts of spotty, light brown oil staining. The Middle
Bakken was the target reservoir for the lateral portion of the well.
Gas Monitoring
The target zone was observed to contain moderate amounts of gas throughout the lateral. Gas
fluctuated between 15-1,269 units with connection gases reaching as high as 911 units (Figure

GEOSTEERING
Comparing gamma signatures from offsetting wells helped predict formation tops and accurately
land the curve. Offset well data is included as an appendix to this report and contains a detailed
summary of offset tops. The curve was landed at 10,709' TVD.
Lateral
The lateral section of this well was drilled toward the south with two PDC bits in three runs. The
well-bore reached total distance depth (TD) of 20,070' MD on December 17, 2014 with a closure
azimuth of 184.44º and a closure distance of 9,558.14'. The bottom-hole location (BHL) is
estimated to be 9,529.51' south and 739.19' west of surface location or approximately 246.17
FSL & 672.81' FWL, SW SW Sec. 29, T148N, R97W.
Steering decisions were made through the XTO Energy, Inc. geologist Rebecca Harrington. The
plan for steering was to drill out at an 89.4° inclination to come down into the center. At, 11,500'
MD it was decided to drill flat. At 12,781' MD we encountered the clean gamma above our
target zone. We dropped our target to 10,722' TVD by a MD of 14,000' to get under the clean
gamma and back into center of target. For the remainder of the well we kept inclination around
89.7° to follow formation (Figure 2).

SUMMARY
1) The XTO Energy, Inc., David Federal 21X-20A is a horizontal, double-section unit, Middle
Bakken well within the Lost Bridge Field in Dunn County, North Dakota. The well was spud
September 16, 2014 and was drilled to a total depth of 20,070' in 19 drilling days on
December 17, 2014.
2) Throughout the vertical and curve, gas ranged from 15-157 units until the False Bakken was
intersected. While drilling the Middle Bakken, background gas values ranged from 15 to
1,269 units with connection gas reaching as high as 911 units.
3) Samples from the Middle Bakken target zone consisted of silty sandstone and contains trace
amounts of spotty, light brown oil staining. Porosity was poor in cuttings.
4) The well-bore was drilled within the Middle Bakken for 100% of the lateral. Structurally, the
Middle Bakken Member descended an estimated 43' in TVD throughout the lateral, resulting
in an apparent overall down dip of 0.27°.
5) The David Federal 21X-20A awaits completion operations to determine its production
potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.

states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Logging, DST and Coring Program
1. A mud log will be run from Base of Last Salt to KOP and on all laterals: Mudlog to include: total gas chromatograph and sample cuttings -
30' sample intervals in vertical hole & 30' intervals in laterals. A CBL/GR log will be run from deepest free-fall depth in 7 inch casing to
surface. A MWD GR/ROP log will also be run from KOP (where the CBL will tie into) to TD of lateral.
2. Open hole logs are NOT planned at this time.
3. DSTs are NOT planned at this time.
H2S
Minor H2S shows may be present from Base Last Salt to KOP. If H2S is noted, rig up safety trailer and stay rigged up to the 7" casing point.
Maximum Formation Pressure and Temp
1. Normal formation pressure gradient is expected (up to 0.5 psi/ft or 9.6 ppg) from surface to the Bakken Shale. The Bakken Shale and Bakken
Middle Member may be over pressured up to as much as 0.65 psi/ft (12.5 ppg).
2. The maximum anticipated BHT is 260 degrees F. or less.
BOP Equipment Requirements
See attached diagram detailing BOPE specifications.
1. Rig will be equipped with upper and lower kelly cocks with handles available.
2. Inside BOP and TIW valves will be available to use on all sizes and threads of DP used on well.
3. BOP accumulator will have enough capacity to close HCR valve, close all rams and annular preventer, and retain minimum of 200 psi
above precharge on the closing manifold without the use of closing pumps. The fluid reservoir capacity shall be at least double the usable
fluid volume of the accumulator system capacity & the fluid level shall be maintained at manufacturer's recommendation. There will be 2
additional sources of power for the closing pumps (electric and air). Sufficient N2 bottles will be available and will be recharged when
pressure falls below manufacturer's minimum
4. BOP ram preventers will be tested to 5,000 psi for 10 minutes using a test plug (a) when initially installed, (b) after 7 inch casing is nippled
up, (c) whenever any seal is broken or repaired, (d) if the time since the previous test exceeds 30 days. Additionally, BOP and casing strings
will be tested to .22 psi/ft (or 1,500 psi minimum) for 30 minutes, prior to drilling out 9-5/8" and 7" casing. Rams and hydraulically operated
remote choke line valve will be function tested daily at a minimum (preferably test at every crew change).

Drilling Fluid and Related Equipment
1. Pumps shall be equipped with stroke counters with displays located in dog house. Slow pump speed shall be recorded on drilling report
at least daily after mudding up (preferably at every crew change).
2. A Pit Volume Totalizer will be installed and the readout will be displayed in the dog house.

Lines to the flare pit will be straight runs (staked down) and turns will utilize targeted tees. Flare pit will be located down wind as much as
