SUMMARY
This report summarizes the drilling of EOG Resources Shell 15-2819H. Surface location is: 472' FSL &
1300' FWL SE/SW Sec. 28-T153N-R89W; Latitude: 48° 02' 19.97", Longitude: -102° 07' 56.04" W,
Mountrail County, North Dakota.
9-5/8" surface casing was set at 2182' TVD. Spud occurred on March 13, 2015. The path was to the
northwest with a planned course line of 318.52°. Sample evaluation and gas monitoring began above the
Charles at 7100' MD.
Kick-off point for the build section was reached at 8558' MD at 20:10 hours CST on March 14, 2015.
The Curve began at 4:25 hours CST on March 15, 2015.
The Middle Bakken was entered at 9368' MD; 9081' TVD; -7036 ' SS. Curve TD was reached at 9373' MD
at 04:10 hours CST on March 16, 2015
Intermediate Casing Shoe is set at 9369' MD; 9081' TVD; -7036' SS; Location: 818' FSL & 928' FWL Sec
28; Coordinates: 346' N & 373' W.
The lateral began at 11:15 hours CST on March 17, 2015. The Gamma Marker and Limestone Shoals were
intercepted during the lateral. Please refer to "Formation Tops" page for depths
Total Depth of 21963' MD was reached on March 21, 2015 at 19:00 hours CST.
Projection to bit is 21963' MD; 89.00°INC; 319.40°AZM; 9155.56' TVD; 13057.8' VS; -7111' SS.
Bottom Hole Location is 265' FNL & 2024' FEL nwne Sec. 19-T153N-R89W; (Coordinates: 9823' N & 8607'
W) Mountrail County, NDW. Mountrail County, North Dakota
A total of 12590' of lateral was drilled, of which 12590' occurred in the Middle Bakken. In-zone calculation
is 100%.
The overall dip of the drilled formation is interpreted to be 89.67°Inc; -0.33°; -0.58'/100'.
Maximum recorded bottom hole temperature was 203°Fahrenheit.
Total Formation Mud Losses in Lateral: 4243 bbls.
Production Liner is Hung at 8450' MD and Set at 21963' MD
The Pason Gas Analyzer was used for gas detection. Maximum Total Gas from Kick-off point through the
curve measured 708 units. Average Total Gas was 93 units. Maximum Total Gas in the lateral was 1614
units at 21121' MD. Average Total Gas in lateral was 291 units. The Gas Buster was not used during this
lateral, hence no flares were observed. Low gas in various parts of the Lateral relative to offset wells is
attributed to formation losses.
Samples were collected and examined at 30' intervals in Vertical and Curve, and 100' intervals in Lateral,
and washed with dish detergent when drilling with invert OBM in vertical and curve. Salt water was used in
the lateral. Sample color was determined by examining wet cuttings and evaluating exterior surfaces as
well as fresh surfaces. Samples were evaluated for Fluorescence and Cut. Accurate evaluation may have
been affected by invert diesel contamination in the vertical and curve and mud additives and drilling lube in
the lateral. Samples towards the end of the lateral were obscured by Drilling Lube, making accurate
description impossible.
