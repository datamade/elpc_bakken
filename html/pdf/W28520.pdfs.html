INTRODUCTION
The North Plains Energy, LLC, Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H [250' FSL & 2,235' FWL; SE
SW Section 11, T160N, R100W] lies within Smokey Butte Field and is located approximately 40
miles north of Williston, North Dakota in Divide County (Figure 1). The well follows several
other Three Forks horizontal, dual-section wells designed to develop oil production in this area.
The surface will be drilled with freshwater, while the vertical hole and curve build section will
be drilled using a diesel invert mud system. Directional plans called for the vertical section to be
drilled to a kick-off point of 8,598' measured depth (MD), build a curve at 12°/100' and land in
the center of the Three Forks target interval. After casing the vertical and curve, the lateral will
be drilled using a produced salt-water mud system. The lateral was planned to drill at an azimuth
of 0.09°, from the southeast octant of the southwest quadrant of Section 11 [T160N, R100W] to
Lot 2 of Section 2 [T160N, R100W]. Upon anticipated completion of the well, the hole will be
prepared for casing and production evaluation using multi-stage fracture stimulation with
packers and sleeves as needed.
OFFSET WELLS
Four primary offset wells were used to develop a well-rounded prognosis of formation tops to be
used as control for the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H (Figure 2). These wells include:
-The Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-1H [SE SW Sec. 11, T160N, R100W] which is
located on the same pad, 30 feet east of the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H in Smokey
Butte Field. Drilled in September of 2014 by North Plains Energy, LLC, it is a horizontal
double-section unit, Three Forks well that is awaiting completion.
-The Roy Hagen 1 [SW NE Sec. 12, T160N, R100W] which is located approximately 1.5
miles northeast of the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H in Smokey Butte Field. Drilled in
1969-1970 by Miami Oil Producers, Inc., it is a vertical Red River well that is currently
inactive.
-The State 160-100-3-10-36-15E-1H [SE SW Sec. 36, T161N, R100W] which is located
approximately 2.5 miles northwest of the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H in Smokey Butte
Field. Drilled in September of 2013 by North Plains Energy, LLC, it is a horizontal
double-section unit, Three Forks well that is currently active.
-The Knudsvig 12-1-160-100H 1PB [SW SE Sec. 12, T160N, R100W] which is
approximately 1.2 miles east of the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H in Smokey Butte Field.
Drilled in August of 2011 by Baytex Energy USA, LLC, it is a horizontal double-section
unit, Three Forks well that is currently active.
Gamma signatures from the Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-1H were also used to provide log
character for the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H target interval. Complete formation tops are
included as an appendix to this report.

ENGINEERING
The Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H well was spud on September 27, 2014 by Ensign rig #118.
A 13 ½" hole was drilled using freshwater, with one tri-cone bit in 16.5 drilling hours to 1,708'
MD, at which point a casing string consisting of 9 " (36#) J-55 casing joints were set and
cemented to surface. After casing, a diesel invert mud system was used with mud weights
ranging from 9.70-9.90 pounds/gallon and associated viscosities ranging from 60-68
seconds/quart. The hole was then drilled to a kick-off point (KOP) of 8,570' MD, in just under
75 drilling hours with one 8 ¾" PDC bit over one bottom-hole assembly (BHA) run. A kick-off
point of 8,570' MD was reached on October 2, 2014, after which the hole was circulated in
preparation to start the curve.
The curve started on October 3, 2014 with an 8 ¾" PDC bit in front of a 2.38° fixed motor. The
833' curve was drilled, using one bit over one BHA assembly, to 9,403' MD in 18.2 drilling
hours, landing on October 4, 2014. A 7" casing string, consisting of 7" (32#) HCL-80 casing
joints, was then set and cemented to 9,384' MD.
The lateral portion of the well began on October 6, 2014, using a produced brine mud system
with mud weight ranging from 9.40 ­ 9.70 pounds/gallon and viscosities ranging from 27-28
seconds/quart. The lateral BHA consisted of a 6" PDC bit, 1.5° fixed mud motor, MWD survey
tool, and MWD gamma tool. The entire lateral was drilled using one bit over one BHA
assembly, drilling the entire lateral in 76.8 drilling hours. The Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H
reached a total depth (TD) of 18,824' MD on October 13, 2014 at 19:55 CDT, after 16 days from
spud. A total of four drilling bits were used to drill the well from surface to total depth in 169.2
total drilling hours. The bottom-hole coordinates are estimated to be 10,009.89' north and 65.53'
east of the surface location or approximately 250.80' FNL and 2,300.53' FWL, Lot 3 Section 2,
T160N, R100W. Projected to total depth, the well-bore is at an inclination of 89.93°, a closure
azimuth of 359.42° and a true vertical depth (TVD) of 8,953.24', resulting in a closure distance
of 10,010.11'.
GEOLOGICAL EVALUATION
Methods
A two-person geology team logged drill cuttings, monitored mud gas and provided guidance for
steering. Sample and gas evaluation began in the Kibbey Formation at 6,900' MD. Rig crew
members caught 30' lagged samples under direction of the well-site geologists. Wet and dry
cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope. Cuttings were also examined for
hydrocarbon `cut' fluorescence by immersion in Entron solvent under ultraviolet light. One set
each of dry cuttings were collected and sent to North Plains Energy, LLC and the North Dakota
Geological Survey. A digital gas detector was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder
(EDR). The Pason provided hole depth, bit depth and pump stroke information to the gas
detection program (MController) and distributed the detector's total gas information for viewing
across location. Total mud gas was recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air)
and chromatography of C1 ­ C4 components were calculated in parts-per-million.
Lithology of Prospective Formations & Hydrocarbon Shows
Note: There were no electric logs run for the Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H; therefore, all depths while drilling the
vertical and curve are referenced to driller's depth.
Sample logging began in the Kibbey Formation, right above the Charles Formation. Samples
from the Charles consisted of limestone (primarily mudstone) and anhydrite, along with varying
amounts of argillaceous and dolomitic limestone, as well as beds of argillaceous dolomite.
Background gas remained slightly elevated, between 10 and 20 units, with no significant gas
shows. Trace to rare spotty and dead black oil staining and traces of intercrystalline porosity
were visible within the upper Charles Formation. The Base of Last Salt, an interval of the
Charles, was encountered at 7,573' MD [7,572' TVD (-5,378' subsea)], and was used for landing
depth estimations.
The Mission Canyon Formation was marked by a transition from argillaceous and dolomitic
limestone to a clean limestone at 7,849' MD [7,848' TVD (-5,654' subsea)]. Samples consisted
of limestone, primarily mudstone with occasional wackestone, and slightly dolomitic and
argillaceous limestone. Some of the samples throughout this formation contained traces of fossil
fragments, algae material and were mottled in part. No oil stain was observed within the upper
part, but a trace of light brown spotty oil staining was observed within the center and lower half
of this formation, with traces of intercrystalline porosity throughout. Background gas ranged
from 20 and 30 units, increasing to 40 units, with connection gases up to 38 and 40 units and gas
shows reaching 47 and 51 units.
The Lodgepole Formation was encountered at 8,546' MD [8,545' TVD (-6,351' subsea)]. The
Lodgepole Formation top was indicated by a slight increase in gamma readings in reaction to the
transition from limestone to predominantly argillaceous limestone. The Lodgepole was
approximately 421' thick and consisted of variably argillaceous, lime mudstone. While drilling
the Lodgepole Formation, background gas stayed slightly elevated at 20 and 30 units.
Connection and slide gases reached 42 and 46 units. The basal ~10' of the Lodgepole is
comprised of the informal "False Bakken" and underlying Scallion subintervals. The "False
Bakken" is ~5' of medium to dark gray-brown, calcareous shale while the Scallion is ~5' of
gray-brown, fossiliferous lime mudstone and partial wackestone. Background gas was elevated
to 25 units in the False Bakken and over 50 units in the Scallion, with connection gas of 26 units
in the False Bakken.
The Bakken Formation, Upper Bakken Shale Member [Mississippian] was encountered at
9,048' MD [8,966' TVD (-6,772' subsea)].
This member consists of ~12' of black,
carbonaceous and petroliferous shale with anomalously high gamma-ray log response (>300-400
API) and increased rate of penetration (ROP). Background gas in the upper Bakken shale
increased between 300 and 350 units, with a connection gas reaching 393 units and a gas show
reaching 378 units. The Middle Member [Devonian-Mississippian] was encountered at 9,072'
MD [8,978' TVD (-6,784' subsea)]. Decreased penetration rates and siltstone in sample returns
indicated entry into this interval. Three distinct lithology zones were recognized in cuttings
while drilling through the Middle Bakken: siltstone, limestone and silty sandstone. The siltstone
comprises the upper ~13' of the middle member. Underlying the siltstone is a ~3' thick
limestone bed classified as oolitic packstone. The limestone generally contains sparry calcite
and micrite, but contains no visible porosity. Beneath the limestone is ~39' of fine-grained and
moderately dolomite and calcite-cemented silty sandstone. Small amounts of pyrite, in
disseminated and nodular form, were also observed as an accessory mineral throughout the
middle member, with more nodular pyrite towards the lower section of the member. A trace of
spotty light brown oil stain was also present in the upper and lower halves of the silty sandstone
interval. While drilling the curve, background gas measured 80 units in the siltstone, 25 units in
the limestone, and 100 units in the silty sandstone. The Lower Bakken Shale Member
[Devonian] was encountered at 9,194' MD [9,032' TVD (-6,838' subsea)]. This member
consists of ~20' of black, carbonaceous and petroliferous shale similar to the upper shale with
anomalously high gamma ray log response (>600-700 API). Background gas in the lower shale
varied between 250 and 300 units, with a connection gas of 495 units and a slide gas of 461
units.
The Three Forks Formation [Devonian] was encountered at 9,258' MD [9,052' TVD (-6,858'
subsea)]. This formation consists of interbedded silty dolomite, dolomite-cemented silty
sandstone and shale. A trace of intergranular and intercrystalline porosity was observed in this
interval. The pale green shale presented no visible porosity. Pyrite (disseminated, nodular and
euhedral form) was also a fairly common accessory mineral in this section of formation. The

Geo-steering
Independent consultants provided well-site supervision, while Weatherford provided directional
drilling and MWD services. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided well-site geology consulting.
Drilling fluids were provided for and analyzed daily by MiSwaco.
The well plan called for a landing of ~9-10' into the Three Forks Formation, at the top of the 15'
target interval, at a projected TVD of 9,067' and an inclination of approximately 89.5° to 90°.
While drilling the vertical, the landing target was kept at 9,067' TVD after correlating real time
gamma-ray from the tops of the Lodgepole markers and the False Bakken, in comparison to the
previously drilled Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-1H and the Knudsvig 12-1-160-100H 1PB
offset wells. The actual landing was at 9,067' TVD, putting the well-bore 6' below the top of the
15' target, entering the target zone at 9,309' MD. The top of the 15' target came in at 9,061'
TVD and the bottom of the target came in at 9,076' TVD.
The target zone is a 15' thick interval, approximately 9' below the base of the Bakken
Formation. The target interval was based on gamma-ray markers from all three offset wells and
the Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-1H MWD curve landing gamma log. Gamma-ray readings
crossing through the top of the 15' target (marker D) were typically around 105 (API) counts,
30-130 (API) counts through the center (marker F), and 65-120 (API) counts through the base
(marker H) of the 15' target zone. The majority of the lateral was drilled within the center of the
target zone with gamma-ray readings of 30-125 (API) counts. Real time MWD gamma-ray
signatures correlated very well with the gamma data from the Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-
1H curve landing (Figure 4).
Figure 4.) Type log of gamma signatures recorded on the Stevens 160-100-14-23-11-14A-1H curve landing

Transitions from high to low gamma-ray markers, and amplitude of gamma counts provided
comparative points from which dip estimates and steering decisions were made. Gas readings
and sample observations also helped make dip estimates by determining the position of the well-
bore within the stratigraphic interval. Offset wells also contributed to excellent structure control,
since the State 160-100-3-10-36-15E-1H horizontal Three Forks well lies directly to the west, the
Knudsvig 12-1-160-100H 1PB horizontal Three Forks well lies directly to the east, and both the
previously drilled Amundson 160-100-22-15-13B-1H Bakken well and the Stevens 160-100-14-
23-11-14A-1H Three Forks well lie directly south.
Structure trends were typically upwards ranging from 0.50º - 0.80º throughout the entire lateral.
A gross apparent up-dip of 0.70° was observed upon completion of the lateral. The majority of
the lateral was drilled within the upper half and the center (between markers "E" and "F") of the
15' target interval. Steering was successful in maintaining the well-bore in the desired target
interval for approximately 100% of the 9,515' lateral section (Figure 5).

Hydrocarbons
Average background gas in the lateral portion of the well was approximately 60 to 250 units
against heavy salt water drilling fluid, weighing 9.40 to 9.70 pound/gallon. Gas components (C1-
C4) were observed throughout the course of the lateral. Upon exiting the shoe, background gas
averaged between 60 and 150 units, with connection and slide gases reaching up to 220 units.
As the well progressed, background gas increased upwards between 100 and 250 units with
connection gases reaching upwards of around 350 and 480 units (Figure 6). Background gas
decreased to 20 and 40 units while the well-bore was right below the "F" marker, between
11,430' and 12,900' MD. The rig also experienced some down time resulting in a down time gas
of 1,247 units at 15,085' MD. No flare was observed throughout the lateral and flow was never
vented nor circulated through the gas buster/flare system. No associated free oil was observed at
the shakers or in samples throughout the lateral. No back-pressure was held while drilling the
Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H.
Total gas readings were fairly similar, if not slightly lower, than the previously drilled State 160-
100-3-10-36-15H and Knudsvig 12-1-160-100H 1PB Three Forks wells, that lie directly west
and east respectively, which had an average background gas of 160 to 600 units with mud
weights ranging 9.30-9.50 pounds/gallon. No back pressure was held during either well but the
gas buster/flare system was utilized for a majority of each lateral.

SUMMARY DISCUSSION
The North Plains Energy, LLC, Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H is a double-section, horizontal
Three Forks well within Smokey Butte Field in Divide County, North Dakota. The well spud on
September 27, 2014 and was drilled to a depth of 8,570' MD for kick-off point. The curve
entered the top of the target interval at 9,309' MD, and was landed at 9,067' TVD, putting the
well-bore 6' below the top of the 15' target. Intermediate 7" casing was run into the Three Forks
target interval reaching over the legal hard-line boundaries. The well was later maintained
within the target for the remaining 100% of the lateral. Samples from the target zone consisted
of interbedded silty dolomite, dolomite-cemented silty sandstone and shale, with fair
intergranular and intercrystalline porosity and trace spotty light brown oil staining. After
penetrating the Three Forks Member, gas values ranged from 60 to 250 units with no associated
free oil in samples or over the shakers. The single lateral well reached a total depth of 18,824'
MD on October 13, 2014, after 16 drilling days from spud. The North Plains Energy, LLC,
Stevens 160-100-11-2-14B-1H awaits completion to determine its full production potential.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
