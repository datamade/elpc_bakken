INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 2T[SE SW Section 10,
T153N, R93W] is located approximately 11 miles northwest of New Town in Mountrail
County, ND. The Langved 5393 42-10 2T is a horizontal first bench Three Forks well within
the Williston Basin consisting of one 9,482' lateral drilled toward the north. Geologically, the
Williston Basin is an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western
edge of the Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Three Forks Formation (Devonian)
consists of interbedded carbonate and clastic lithologies, which unconformably underlies the
Pronghorn Member of the Bakken Formation (formerly recognized as the Sanish Sand).
Advances in drilling and well stimulation have made the Three Forks Formation a viable oil
and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the
`Structure' section appended to this report. The Curren 11-14 TFH, located 0.8 miles
southeast of the subject well, proved to be the most reliable and consistent of the primary
offsets used for depth correlation of formations above the Lodgepole and during the curve
landing. Offset well control was essential in curve operations, to successfully land within the
first bench target zone. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray signatures in these
wells were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations to land the curve.
The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to ensure accurate
landing of the curve.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 42-10 2T was re-entered on September 17, 2014 by Nabors 460. Drilling
fluid was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. Two
trips were made during the vertical. The first trip was at 8,258' for a new bit due to slow
ROP. This BHA was used to reach the Kick off point at 10,210'. The second trip in the
vertical was made to pick up the curve assembly. The curve was drilled with an 8 ¾"
Halliburton PDC bit utilizing a 2.38° NOV mud motor. The curve required 22.5 hours and
793' of drilling and was landed at 11,003' MD (10,691' TVD) with an inclination of 91.4°.
Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 10,985' MD. To start
the lateral, a 6 Smith PDC bit, MWD tool, and a 1.5° fixed Baker mud motor were used.
This BHA drilled 2,205' in 33.5 hours to 13,208' MD where the first trip in the lateral was
made due to gamma tool failure. The motor was replaced with a 1.5° fixed Predator motor
and the bit was rerun in the hole. This assembly drilled 4,569' in 73.5 hours to 17,777' MD
where the second trip in the lateral was made due slow ROP. The bit was replaced with a 6
Baker Hughes bit and the motor was replaced with a 1.5° fixed Baker motor. This new
assembly drilled 2,708' in 50.5 hours to a TD of 20,485' MD. The BHA accomplishments are
tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached total depth (TD) at
20,485' MD at 0720 hrs CDT on October 4, 2014, generating an exposure of 9,482 linear feet
of 6 hole through the Three Forks target interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies
9,942.53' north & 571.59' west of surface location or approximately 375.57' FNL and 938.41'
FWL, NW NW Sec. 3, T153N, R93W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 7,830' MD where the observed lithology consists of primarily shale
with thin interbedded sandstone units. The main zones of interest drilled at the subject well
include the Ratcliffe, Mission Canyon, Middle Member Bakken, and first bench interval of
the Three Forks formations.

from the Ratcliffe range from a tan to light gray, medium
brown, microcrystalline limestone (Figure 2). Samples
have no visual porosity and no oil staining was observed.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 14-63 units
above a background of ~20 units against a 9.65-10.0 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight.
Methane (C1) was observed throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than C1 were not
observed. After kicking off the curve, shows ranged from 43-73 units with background
ranging from 30-38 units. Shows remained low from kick-off through entry of the Upper
Bakken. Gas shows were elevated in respect to the vertical from this interval through the
entrance of the Three Forks Formation first bench. Shows from the Upper Bakken through
first bench ranged from 343-396 units (C1-C4) over a background of ~160 units against a
diesel-invert mud weight of 10.0 lb/gal.
The lateral began with gas shows ranging from 42-121 units over a background ranging from
20-40 units against a 9.4 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas levels increased from ~ 11,450' -
12,650' MD, with shows from 81-426 units (C1-C2) over a background of 40-120 units
against a 9.4 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. From ~ 12,650' - 13,000' MD, gas levels
decreased to a background level of 13 units with no gas shows against a 9.4 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight. This decrease was concurrent with the borehole being positioned in the top of
the claystone. From ~13,000' - 16,500' gas levels progressively increased, with a maximum
gas show of 1839 units (C1-C4) over a background level of 1,100 units, against a 9.5 lb/gal
salt-water mud weight. From ~16,500' - 18,000' gas levels progressively decreased as the
borehole approached the claystone layer, with gas shows ranging from a high of 1732 units
(C1-C4) to a low of 460 units API (C1-C4), with background levels falling from 1325 units to
337 units API, against a 9.5 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Just prior to entering maximum gas
show of 1839 units over a background level of 1,100 units, against a 9.52 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight. Trip gas following a bit change at 17,777' was measured at 5,393 units and
resulted in the gas being flared. Also just before the borehole entered the claystone gas
levels were elevated from 17,800' - 17,900', with a maximum show of 1966 units (C1-C4),
over a background level of 555 units, against a 9.52 lb/gal salt-water mud weight.
While the borehole was in the claystone from 17,936' - 19,131' MD, gas levels again
decreased, with shows ranging from 284-141 units (C1-C3), over a background level of 74
units, against a 9.5 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Once the borehole was back in the target
zone, gas levels steadily increased until reaching TD at 20,485' MD, with a maximum gas
show of 365 units (C1-C4) over a background of ~350 units against a 9.5 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight.
GEOSTEERING
The potential pay zone of the Langved 5393 42-10 2T was identified by evaluating electric
log data from the Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 (Figure 6).
Figure 6. Mangum 5493 44-7 T3 Type log. Ideal target zone highlighted in green.
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 10,689' TVD at 90.45°. The Lodgepole top
indicated that the target was approximately eight feet higher than prognosis based on the
three closest wells (Curren 11-14 TFH, Flavin 5393 14-3T, and Gloria 5393 44-4B). The
decision was made to raise the target to 10,679'. The lower markers and formation tops
supported the decision to lower the target landing by 5 feet to 10,684' TVD, prior to entering
the Three Forks. As the Three Forks was encountered, the Lower Bakken Shale was
observed to be 6 feet thicker than the adjacent wells, causing the target to be dropped to
10,690'. At this late stage, minimal corrections could be made. The wellbore was landed at a
TVD of 10,686' at 89.8°, about 1' below the top of the target zone in the first bench Three
Forks target interval.
Drilling ahead out of the casing at a depth of 10,985' MD, the inclination was adjusted
downward to get the borehole from the upper part of the target zone down to the claystone.
The well bore also needed to make a right turn to change the azimuth from ~336° to 360°.
The claystone was encountered at 12,425' MD. During this drop through the target zone,
gamma gradually dropped from 150 API near the top of section to ~ 70 to 80 API near the
bottom, with erratic swings as much as 50 API near the bottom of the target zone. The
borehole penetrated the claystone as much as 4 feet at the lowest point and was back in the
Three Forks dolomite at 12,919' MD, a total of 494' of exposure to the claystone. While in
the claystone, the structural dip changed from 0.05° up to 0.45° up, which resulted in the
borehole going a bit deeper into the claystone than desired. Gamma values ranged from
~110 to 185 API while in the claystone. From 12,919' to ~ 13,850', the borehole steadily
climbed back to the top of the target zone, with gamma readings climbing back to ~140 API
as seen earlier near the top of section.
There was some difficulty trying to get the borehole back in the lower part of the section, as
the build rates were rather high while rotating the remainder of the stand, which negated the
gains from sliding. There appeared to be a hard section in formation causing the well to
build. Several aggressive slides were implemented to get through this section, resulting in a
steep inclination of 86.9°. The borehole quickly approached the claystone, but was leveled
off just before encountering the claystone. The wellbore followed the interpreted gamma
interface between the Three Forks and the claystone for the next 1,350 feet from ~14,100'
MD to 15,450' MD. During this time, the gamma exhibited continuous erratic swings
ranging from 50 API to 170 API, and did not allow for any solid dip calculations. Regional
dip was estimated at 0.1 during much of this interval since the claystone was never
encountered while maintaining inclinations near 90°.
From 15,450' MD to 15,850' MD the wellbore gradually climbed toward the upper part of the
zone. The gamma signature during this climb matched the gamma seen earlier throughout
the middle section of the zone, gradually climbing to a high of 120 API near the upper
section. The wellbore remained near the top of the target zone from 15,850' to 16,300'.
From 16,300' to 16,800' MD the wellbore slowly dropped to the bottom of the target zone.
Near the middle of zone, a new marker appeared at 16,511', characterized as a short duration
spike reaching 170 API. This marker was used to calculate dip upon reaching the middle of
zone again at 17,350'. The borehole stayed near the middle of the target zone through
~17,800'. Two 20' slides were implemented back to back from 17,760' ­ 17,800', one before
tripping out of the hole, and one immediately after tripping back in hole. As a result,
inclination fell to 87.3° and the borehole entered the claystone at 17,936' at a relatively sharp
angle. The borehole penetrated the claystone as much as 5 feet at the lowest point and was
back in the Three Forks dolomite at 19,139' MD, a total of 1,203' of exposure to the
claystone. The borehole remained in the lower half of target zone for the remainder of the
well. High gamma markers a few feet above the claystone allowed for good dip calculations
during the remainder of the well. The overall dip for the lateral was 0.35° up (Figure 7).

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 2T was drilled by Nabors
460 from reentry to TD in 18 days. The well reached a total depth of 20,485' MD on October
4, 2014. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination obtained
during the Langved 5393 42-10 2T indicate hydrocarbon presence in the Three Forks first
bench Target. Samples from the target zone consist of light gray to off white, pink to very
light orange-red crystalline carbonate, microsucrosic dolomite with intercrystalline porosity,
which yielded slow to moderate pale yellow to green diffuse and streaming cut fluorescence.
Lateral operations were successful in exposing 9,482' of potentially productive Three Forks
target zone rock to the wellbore.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
