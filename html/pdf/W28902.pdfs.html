INTRODUCTION
Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 5T [SE SW Section 33, T156N
R101W] is located approximately 8 miles north of Williston in Williams County, North Dakota.
The Harbour 5601 42-33 5T is a horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin
consisting of one ~9,465' long lateral drilled towards the north (5.04º). This well is an expansion
of the Tyrone Field. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,125' measured depth (MD).
Plans called for a 12º/100' build rate in the curve to ensure landing within the Three Forks
sucrosic dolomite porosity. To isolate the targeted reservoir, intermediate casing was set to
11,133' MD. After intermediate casing had been set, directional drilling and geo-steering
techniques were employed to maintain maximum exposure to the target reservoir.
OFFSET WELLS
Similar offset wells were used as primary controls while drilling the Harbour 5601 42-33 5T.
The offsets utilized were the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 3T [SESW Section 33,
T156N, R101W], located on the same pad as the subject well, the Harbour 5601 42-33 4B
[SESW Section 33, T156N, R101W], located on the same pad as the subject well, and the Oasis
Petroleum, Holmes 5501 12-5 5T [Lot 3 Section 5, T155N-R101W], located 1.03 miles west of
the subject well (Figure 2). The above wells were selected based on their proximity to the subject
well. Data including rate of penetration (ROP), lagged samples and measurement-while-drilling
(MWD) gamma ray data were used to determine marker and formation tops during the vertical
hole. MWD gamma ray data was also utilized during the curve build and lateral sections. During
both the vertical and curve-build sections, tops were compared to primary offset wells to ensure
successful landing within the target zone. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing target was
calculated by assuming comparable formation thicknesses to the offset wells.

adjusted as necessary until a successful landing was achieved.

ENGINEERING
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 5T was reentered on October 14,
2014 by Nabors #110. Drilling progressed from that point until kick-off point (KOP), at 10,125'
MD. Drilling proceeded from this point until curve landing was reached. The curve was landed
with a projected inclination of 89.23°. The original wellbore successfully penetrated the Three
Forks at 10,896' MD (10,627' TVD, -8,431' MSL). Diesel invert drilling fluid used during both
the vertical and curve ranged in value from 9.6+-10.4 parts per gallon (ppg). The 7" intermediate
casing consisted of 81 joints of 32# P-110 and 181 joints of 29# P-110 set to 11,133' MD.
Casing operations were performed by DC Powertongs. Cementing operations were performed by
Schlumberger. After intermediate casing procedures were completed, 4" drill pipe was picked
up, diesel invert drilling fluid was replaced with salt water drilling fluid. At 18,931' MD the mud
motor failed necessitating a trip to replace the BHA. TD was reached at 09:26 CDT on October
29, 2014 to a depth of 20,605' MD (10,556.25' TVD) with an inclination of 90.68º, closure angle
of 5.04º and closure distance of 10,056.87'. The bottom hole location (BHL) is estimated to be
10,017.94' N & 884.10' E of surface location or approximately 256.22' FNL & 2,129.78' FEL
NW NE Sec. 28, T156N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Two well site geologists Sunburst Consulting Inc. employees conducted the geologic analysis for
Harbour 5601 42-33 5T. Sunburst installed and monitored a digital gas detector and
chromatograph interfaced with a Rig Watch Electronic Data Recording System (EDR). The EDR
provided depth, drilling rate and pump stroke data to the M-Controller gas-monitoring software
which returned gas data in the form of total gas units and hydrocarbon constituent (methane,
ethane, propane, butane) concentrations expressed in parts-per-million. Gas samples were pulled
through ¼" polyflow tubing after agitation in Sunburst's gas trap installed in the possum belly.
Rig crews caught lagged samples under the supervision of wellsite geologists in 30' intervals
from 8,120' MD to 20,605' MD. Wet and dry cuttings were examined under a binocular
microscope. One set of dry cuttings was collected and sent to the North Dakota Geological
Survey and Core Library. Complete lithologic descriptions are provided in a lithology document
as an appendix in the full report.
Lithology
Sample analysis began at 8,120' MD in interbedded sandstone, shale and siltstone lithologies
with a persistent limestone bed located in the roughly the middle of the unit. One prospective pay
zone was recorded while drilling the subject well, the Mission Canyon.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississipian-Madison Group) is typically comprised of light to
medium brown fossiliferous limestone and dolomite.
The initial contact with the Mission
Canyon was at 9,296' MD 9,295' TVD (-7,099') MSL, 1' low to the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour
5601 42-33 3T offset. Traces of algal laminations were recorded. Samples exhibited pinpoint
porosity. Hydrocarbon shows and the presence of heavy gases (C2) were recorded through
middle of this interval.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 10,663' MD, 10,566' TVD (-8,370') MSL, 1' high to
the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 3T offset. Representative samples of the Middle
Bakken consist of fine grained light gray brown calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular
porosity and trace brown oil stain. Good hydrocarbon shows and heavy gases (C1 ­C4) were
recorded through upper portion of this interval.
The Three Forks Formation (Devonian) top was encountered at 10,896' MD 10,627' TVD,
(-8,431') MSL, 2' high to the Oasis Petroleum, Harbour 5601 42-33 3T. The formation is
comprised of interbedded dolomite, shale and claystone. Representative samples consist of light
pink to tan dolomite and pale green to blue green shale with intercrystalline porosity and traces
of light brown spotty oil stain. Hydrocarbons show and heavy gases (C1 ­C4) were recorded
through this interval.

Hydrocarbon Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 ­C4) are expressed in parts-per million (ppm) concentrations.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was utilized in the vertical and curve. Salt water drilling fluid was
employed during the lateral. Background gases recorded throughout the vertical were ~10-15
units, reaching a peak value of 40 units while drilling in the Upper Mission Canyon. Methane
and Ethane gases (C1 ­C2) were recorded from through a small portion of the Upper Mission
Canyon and the Lower Mission Canyon. While drilling the curve background and peak gas
shows began to increase when drilling in the Upper Bakken Shale down to the Lower Bakken
Shale, reaching a peak value of 210 units when drilling in the Lower Bakken Shale. Once the
wellbore was established in the First Bench of the Three Forks, background gases were observed
between ~200-500 units while circulating through the gas buster. Around 15,600' MD the
decision was made to circulate through the flow line. Background gases increased to 400-900
units. The highest peak value recorded during the second section of the lateral was a show of
2,963 units at 20,452' MD while drilling near the top of the target zone. Oil shows were
described as trace light brown spotty oil stain throughout the target zone along the length of the
lateral.
Geo-steering
RPM Consulting provided well site supervision. Gyrodata was contracted for MWD and
directional services. Oasis geologist in Houston, directional drillers and Sunburst geologists
worked closely throughout the well to make steering decisions to maximize the amount of
borehole exposure to the pay zone. The Three Forks member of the Harbour 5601 42-33 5T is
estimated at ~17' thick. The top of the 5' target interval chosen for this well is located 7' above
the claystone and was logged initially at 10,635' TVD. The top of target was chosen 8' below the
Pronghorn member where the highest porosity is observed. This target interval was also chosen
to minimize chances of contacting the Pronghorn siltstone member.
Due to a lower than expected curve landing the lateral commenced deep in the claystone.
Misinterpretation of samples in combination with a structural map indicating a slight down dip
and a new gamma tool in the hole led the geology team to believe the wellbore was above the
claystone and the decision was made to descend in section in search of the claystone. It was later
discovered after descending approximately 30' of TVD with no recognizable gamma markers
that the wellbore had in fact made its way into the Second Bench of the Three Forks formation.
Upon this discovery the directional team facilitated a rapid ascent and the wellbore was returned
to the First Bench target zone by ~14,400' MD. Numerous interbedded shale layers provided
helpful geosteering markers throughout the remainder of the lateral, but a strategy of contacting
the Claystone every few thousand feet was employed to ensure position in zone, as the
interbedded shale layers were observed to be intermittent at times. Stratigraphic location within
the target zone was based on gamma ray values, sample observations, gas shows, and drill rates.
API gamma counts throughout target ranged from the mid 20's to the low 150's. The overall
formation dip of 0.45° up resulted in a structural position at TD that was ~77' higher in TVD
than initial contact with the target zone.
SUMMARY
1) The Oasis Petroleum North America LLC Harbour 5601 42-33 5T is a continuation
of exploratory efforts in Williams County, North Dakota targeting the Upper Three Forks
Formation.
2) The well was reentered on 14 October, 2014. The vertical hole was drilled to a kick-off
point of 10,125' on 17 October, 2014. Intermediate casing was set at 11,133' MD in
preparation for lateral operations. Lateral drilling operations concluded on 29 October,
2014.
3) The lateral was drilled in 162 hours with two 6" PDC bits.
4) A diesel invert mud program used through the vertical and curve ranged from (9.6+-10.4
ppg). The salt water drilling fluid ranged from (9.3-9.55 ppg), to maintain hydrostatic
balance.
5) Samples from the Three Forks target porosity zone consisted of interbedded light pink to
tan dolomite and pale green to blue green shale with intercrystalline porosity and a trace,
light-brown oil stain.
6) Gas shows reached a peak value of 2,963 units at 20,452' MD.
7) Geo-steering was accomplished via sample evaluation and gamma markers. Dip
projections were successfully utilized to maintain contact between the well bore and the
targeted porosity zone, with 48% exposure to the Three Forks Formation target zone.
8) The Harbour 5601 42-33 5T awaits completion operations to determine its ultimate
production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
