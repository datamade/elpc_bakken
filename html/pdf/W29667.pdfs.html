Introduction
Continental Resources, Inc. (CRI) successfully drilled the Thronson Federal 9-21H
horizontal well at an average Azimuth of 176.18º and an average Inclination of 89.64°,
targeting the Middle Bakken Formation from a surface hole location of Section 21,
T154N, R94W, (280' FNL & 1365' FWL) in Mountrail County, North Dakota, drilling
south to the south side of southwest quarter of Section 33, T154N, R94W, with a
spacing unit consisting of 1,280 acres. The well is located in the Alkali Creek Field in the
central region of the Williston Basin, North Dakota. The main well used for correlation
was the Thronson Federal 7-21H, and the Sorenson 6-16H2, operated by CRI.
Operations Summary
Vertical Section
The Thronson Federal 9-21H was spud on October 27th, 2014 and the surface
casing was drilled out on February 27th, 2014. Geo-Link Inc., started logging the
Thronson Federal 9-21H on March 1st, 2015 at 23:05 at a measured depth (MD) of
8900'. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. Redbox gas detection system was used for
analyzing and recording total gas and chromatograph data. The Thronson Federal 9-
21H had preset 9 5/8" surface casing at 1864' MD. An invert mud system was used to
drill out from surface throughout the landing of the build section. The first BHA used
included (Bit #1) an 8 ¾'' Smith MDZi616, a Hunting 7/8 stage, 5.7 lobe,1.5° Fixed Bent
Housing (FBH) mud motor, and a Multi Shot serial MWD tool. This BHA was used to
drill out from surface casing at 1864' MD. There was a trip at 8315' MD for a BHA prior
to reaching the salt formations. This assembly drilled 6451' in 35.40 bit hours. The
second BHA used included (Bit #2) an 8 ¾'' Smith MDSi516, a Hunting 7/8 stage, 5.7
lobe,1.5° Fixed Bent Housing (FBH) mud motor, and a Multi Shot serial MWD tool. This
assembly drilled 1860' to the kick-off point at 10175' MD in 20.59 bit hours. The kick-off
point (KOP) was reached at 15:00 on March 2nd, 2015 at 10175' MD, 10174' TVD (-
8082' SS). The trip out of the hole (TOOH) for the build assembly began at 15:30 on
March 2nd, 2014.
Build Section
Drilling operations resumed on March 4th, 2014 at 04:00 with the Build assembly.
This BHA included (Bit #3) an 8 ¾'' Security MMD55M PDC, a Mpact 4/5 Lobe, 7.0
Stage, with 2.38º FBH and 4.18' Bit to Bend mud motor and a Multi Shot serial MWD
tool. This assembly drilled the build of 704' MD in 11.30 bit hours to the landing of the
build section at 10879' MD, 10613' TVD (-8521' SS). The build was landed at 22.30 on
March 3rd, 20145 at an inclination of 88.6° and an Azimuth of 139.40° in Section 21,
T154N, R94W (611' FNL & 1714' FWL). Seven inch (7") Intermediate casing was set at
10864' MD on March 5th, 2015 and cemented.
Lateral Section
The cement, the shoe and the float were drilled out on March 6th, 2015 at 21:65
hours, with a new BHA and a 9.0-10.1 lb/gal saltwater mud system. The new BHA
included (Bit #4) a 6" Security MMD64, paired with a Mpact motor 6/7 Lobe, 8.0 stage,
1.50º FBH and 3.75' bit to bend motor and Multi Shot serial MWD tool. This assembly
drilled a total of 10498' in 37.0 bit hours to 21377' MD, where there was a trip on March
11th, 2015 at 04:30 for a BHA. On March 12th, 2015 the trip back in the hole with the
new BHA was completed and drilling resumed at 00:20. This BHA included (Bit #5) a 6"
Security MMD64, paired with a Discovery Superhawk 7/8 Lobe, 8.2 stage, 1.50º FBH
and 4.55' bit to bend motor and a Multi Shot serial MWD tool. This assembly drilled 660'
MD to 22037' MD in 7.1 bit hours. A trip was made at 22037' MD for a MWD on March
12th, 2015 at 09:25. On March 13th, 2014 the trip back in the hole with the new BHA
was completed and drilling resumed at 02:55. This BHA included (Bit #5 which was re-
ran from the previous BHA) paired with a Discovery Superhawk (also re-ran from the
previous BHA) 7/8 Lobe, 8.2 stage, 1.50º FBH and 4.55' bit to bend motor and a Multi
Shot serial MWD tool. This assembly drilled 4845' in 37.0 bit hours to total depth (TD) of
26222' MD, 10716' TVD (-8624' SS) was reached at 15:40 hours on March 15th, 2015
with an Inclination of 90.5° and an Azimuth of 180.5° and a bottom hole location in
Section 33, T154N, R94W (86' FSL & 2420' FWL). After TD was reached Geo-Link Inc.,
remained on location to monitor gas until production liner was set.
Conclusions
1.) The Continental Resources, Inc. Thronson Federal 9-21H was a successfully
drilled two-run lateral well in the Middle Bakken Formation from a surface
location in Section 21, T154N, R94W (280' FNL & 1365' FWL) to a total depth
(TD) of 26222' MD, 10716' TVD (-8624' SS), with a bottom hole location in
Section 33, T154N, R94W (86' FSL & 2420' FWL) in McKenzie County, North
Dakota. The well was drilled from the intermediate casing shoe to TD with two
trips. A total of 15343' MD was made in the lateral, from casing point to TD. Total
depth was reached at 15:40 hours on March 15th, 2015. The Thronson Federal
9-2IH required seventeen total days of drilling and setting intermediate casing.
2.) Ultraviolet sample shows in this lateral would be rated as fair to good with
moderately fast to immediate milky cuts with residual rings. Total gas shows and
overall porosity would also be rated as fair to good. Wellbore placement was
within the target zone for approximately 100% of the total 15343' of lateral
section, which should allow for excellent production opportunities.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
