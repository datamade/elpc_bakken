Well Evaluation
Introduction
Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-21-28-2TFH [SW SW
Section 16, T158N, R95W] is located approximately 7.3 miles northwest of Tioga, in the
McGregor Field of Williams County, North Dakota. The ND State 158-95-21-28-2TFH is
designed as a horizontal Three Forks well situated along the western flank of the Nesson anticline
of the Williston Basin. The curve would be kicked off in the Lodgepole Formation and landed
within the Three Forks Formation. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will then be
applied to maintain maximum exposure to a 7' target zone over the course of one approximately
10,355' long lateral drilled to the south (179.24°).
The Williston Basin is a structural and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the first bench of the Three Forks Formation (Devonian)
underlies the Bakken Formation's grey siltstone Upper Pronghorn Member and extends down to
the light brown shale of the top of the second Three Forks "bench." Through exploration and study,
the Three Forks First Bench, which consists of interbedded dolomite and shale, has been developed
as an extremely viable oil and natural gas resource by way of horizontal drilling and hydraulic
fracturing technology.
Offset Wells
The following existing offset wells provided stratigraphic information to optimize planning for
curve landing. Geological tops and landing true vertical depth (TVD) of these wells were used as
control data against the corresponding geological tops identified in the vertical and curve of the
ND State 158-95-21-28-TFH. This allowed for target landing TVD to be periodically updated to
ensure accuracy in the landing target (Figure 1).

Engineering
The ND State 158-95-21-28-2TFH was spud on September 22, 2014 by a surface hole rig.
Freshwater drilling fluid was used in the surface hole, diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve,
and saltwater fluid for the lateral.
The vertical BHAs consisted of a PDC bit and a fixed mud motor with 1.5° bend. One trip was
made during the vertical at 8,757' MD due to low ROP. The curve was accomplished in two runs.
The first BHA included of a PDC bit and a fixed mud motor with 2.38° bend, the second
incorporated a 2.5° fixed mud motor. The curve was landed at 9,952' MD, or 9,665' TVD with a
projected inclination of 90.0°. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented
to 9,946' MD.
The lateral was completed using three BHAs, all including PDC bits and 1.5º bend motors. Trips
for new BHA were made at 11,810' MD for low ROP, 12,085' for a sidetrack, and 16,015' for bit
failure. A total depth of 20,165' MD was reached on January 12, 2015 at 18:25 hours CST. The
bottom hole location lies 10,861.06' south and 133.04' east of surface location, or approximately
226.94' FSL and 1,244.04' FWL SW SW, Section 28, T158N, R95W.
Geology
Sunburst Consulting, Inc. well-site geologists and rig hands caught lagged samples at 30' intervals
beginning at 8,060' MD, just prior to the Base of Last Salt. Beginning within the Lower Bakken
Shale, samples were caught at 10' intervals until curve landing. Throughout the lateral samples
were caught at 50' intervals to total depth. Rock sample analysis was performed via binocular
microscopy. Formation tops were picked using gamma data throughout the well. Further accuracy
was achieved using ROP and drilling parameters in the vertical.
Hydrocarbon components, methane (C1), ethane (C2), propane(C3) and butane (C4) were measured
in parts-per-million (ppm) concentrations and were monitored by well-site geologists using the
Mudlogging Systems, Inc. chromatograph to record possible viable plays throughout the vertical
and curve. Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) was also monitored for safety and geologic records. Throughout
the vertical, background gas (C1) averaged around 30 units. While drilling the Ratcliffe, gas shows
(C1-C2) reached 197 units against 9.65 ppg diesel-invert mud. Gas shows in the curve within the
Lower Bakken reached 120 units against 9.85 ppg diesel-invert mud.
Target Zone
A 7' target zone was chosen for the ND State 158-95-21-28-2TFH by Liberty Resources
Management Company, LLC geologists that began 13' below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale
and ended 5' from the top of the 2nd bench. The thickness was estimated using control well logs,
structure maps and isopach maps to determine a potential pay zone for the extent of the lateral,
which would, in turn, help to determine the curve landing target. Type logs from the NDCA 4 were
used as a guide for expected gamma ray markers while drilling the lateral because of their complete
record of the local Three Forks gamma signatures (Figure 2). The target zone was characterized
by alternating hot gamma beds (approximately 90-135 API) and low gamma bed (approximately
30-60 API). It should be noted that the hot gamma marker at the base of zone became less
pronounced as we drilled south.

Target Zone Lithology
The targeted Three Forks Formation (Devonian) was drilled at 9,656' TVD (-7,171' MSL). The
general lithology of the formation consisted of light tan to cream colored, light white, very fine
crystalline to sucrosic dolomite interbedded with pale green or blue gray shale (Figure 3). The
dolomite, which was the primary lithology witnessed, displayed trace to rare intercrystalline
porosity and trace to occasional light brown spotty oil stain.

Target Zone Oil and Gas Show
During the course of the lateral, background gas (C1-C4) ranged from 60 to 275 units (Figure 4).
The highest show was a connection gas of 1,142 units at 19,916' MD against 10.0 ppg saltwater
mud. Higher gas shows were generally associated with the target zone. Trace to occasional, light
brown, spotty oil staining was observed in dolomite samples, with hydrocarbon cut fluorescence
primarily being pale yellow, slow-fast diffuse and streaming when observed. No free oil was
observed at shakers.

Geo-Steering
The original curve landing target of 9,662' TVD at 90° was calculated by assuming comparable
formation thicknesses with offset wells extending from the Lodgepole to the landing target gamma
signature. The Lodgepole "X" top indicated a lower landing target was necessary and the landing
target was adjusted to 9,666' TVD. No further adjustments were made to the landing target, and
the wellbore was landed at 9,665' TVD within the upper portion of the target zone.
The original prognosis dip profile was developed using subsurface maps of the region, as well as
the full dip profile from the Hove 11-21H-2128-15895-TF. These resources indicated that
formation would initially drop in dip, followed by a small up dip before flattening out after which
the dip would drop again downward. Based on the offset wells used for geo-steering the initial
down dips of 1.2º and 1.8º down were unexpected and led to contact with the Lower Bakken Shale
at 12,005' MD (Figure 5). The wellbore was successfully sidetracked at 11,730' MD and no
further Lower Bakken Shale strikes occurred.

65' over the total length of the lateral. This resulted in an overall apparent dip of 0.36° down.

Summary
The Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-21-28-2TFH was drilled by
Patterson-UTI 286 from spud to total depth in 21 days. The well reached a total depth of 20,165'
MD on January 12, 2015. Geologic data such as hydrocarbon gas measurements and sample
examination obtained while drilling the ND State 158-95-16-9-2TFH indicate a hydrocarbon rich
system in the Three Forks target. Shows (C1-C4) within the target zone reached 1,142 units within
the center of zone. Samples from the target zone primarily consisted of light cream to white
dolomite with trace to occasional intercrystalline porosity and trace to occasional brown spotty
oil staining. Pale green or light to medium gray blue shale interbeds were also present, most
notably in areas that displayed hot gamma markers. Geo-steering techniques exposed 64% of
uncased borehole to the 7' Three Forks target.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
