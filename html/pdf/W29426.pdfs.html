2. Well Summary
The Teton 5-1-3TFSH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Three Forks
Formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota. It also included a coring job/pilot hole (Teton 5-1-
3TFSH-P) from ~ 20' above the Carrington Shale to ~ 20' into the Birdbear formation. This operation
extracted two core samples that were ~ 180' in length. The lateral well was drilled north to south, starting
from the Surface Hole Location at 1204' FSL & 2475' FEL of SWSE Section 34, T151N, R96W and into
section 3, T151N, R96W. It was drilled following an approximate azimuth of 180.27° until reaching total
depth Bottom Hole Location at 222.83' FSL & 2537.1' FEL of SWSE section 10, T151N, R96W. The
drilling target pay zone was approximately a 24 feet thick zone (~32'- 56' below the base of the
LOWER BAKKEN SHALE). The overall dip rate of this lateral was approximately .40'/100' or 89.77
degree down dip.

3. Vertical & Build Curve Well Section
The vertical was drilled with a slight tangent of 5°. The build started at 2,433' MD to 3248' MD
and back to 0°. Before continuing with the curve from the intended KOP, a separate coring job/pilot
hole (Teton 5-1-3TFSH-P) was drilled to 11210' MD, 11204.53' TVD. The Lodgepole was 7' low to
the well prognosis (10-31-14) and required that the core point be dropped from 10671' to 10681'
TVD. This portion of the well produced a separate package of data that is addressed in section 3.1.
In the vertical portion of the well (Teton 5-1-3TFSH) from the Kibby Lime to the Lodgepole,
tops came in from 3' high to 8' low of the well prognosis dated 10/31/2014, (see chart 3.2). A whip-
stock was utilized at a KOP of 10,370' MD for Teton 5-1-3TFSH, to initiate a planned curve of 12.0°
per hundred feet, for a total of 476.5 feet of vertical section. (Wire-line data from the pilot hole
correlated with Carrington Shale - Upper Bakken tops was used to produce a revised geo-prog dated
11-7-2014). The Carrington Shale top came in at 10,701.85' TVD; this was 3' low to the geo-prog
dated 11-7-14. The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,715.71' TVD; this was also 3' low to the geo-
prog dated 11-7-14. The landing target was adjusted and finalized down 3' from 10,844' TVD to
10,847' TVD. The Landing was made at 11,195' MD, 10,859.83' TVD and 7" casing was set at
11,183' MD, 10856.46' TVD, (-8451' SS), at Coordinates: 564.27 S, 85.9 W, Legal: 639.73' FSL,
2560.9' FEL, SWSE Section 34 T151N-R96W.

5. Lithology
Samples: See mud log and horizontal logs for full sample descriptions. All samples were taken at
30' lagged intervals from the shakers, cleaned, labeled, and bagged for shipment to the state of North
Dakota. The samples collected with the best quality were within the first 1000' of the lateral just outside
the casing shoe. After this, samples were returned ground up into fine sand and were extremely fine
grained at the tail end of the lateral.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
