GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
INTRODUCTION
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Frisco 3-31H as a two-section lateral targeting the Middle
Member of the Bakken Formation, a fine grained, calcareous siltstone sourced by the organic-
rich Upper and Lower Bakken Shales, in McKenzie County, North Dakota within the Williston
Field. This is the first vertical and curve drilled of a two well pad, and the last (second) lateral to
be completed. The surface hole location is in the Southeast one-quarter of the Southeast one-
quarter of Section 31, Township 152 North, Range 102 West (395' FSL & 853' FEL). The well
then traverses North to a final bottom hole location 231.15' from the North section line and
634.61' from the East section line of Section 30, Township 152 North, Range 102 West
(231.15'FNL & 34.61'FEL).
PLAN
The vertical portion of the Frisco 3-31H was planned to be drilled to a kick-off point of
10298'MD, within the Mississippian Lodgepole Formation. Build rates of 12°/100' were required
to land the curve with the bit inclined approximately 90° within the 10' targeted interval, 10'
below the top of the Middle Bakken, according to prognosis. Intermediate casing would then be
set and cemented at 11138'MD (10792 'TVD), prior to drilling the lateral portion of the well. The
horizontal well would be drilled to a planned total depth of 20592' MD, within the Northeast
one-quarter of the Northeast one-quarter of Section 30, Township 152 North, Range 102 West
(200' FNL & 660' FEL).
OFFSET WELLS
The primary offset used was the vertical Iverson 1-31H, situated northeast of the Frisco 3-31H
surface hole location. The Iverson 1-31H gamma ray type log data in addition to several other
offset wells (Frisco 2-31H1, Frisco 1-31H, Miramar 2-31H, Miramar 3-31H1) were used to
facilitate formation thicknesses in the area as well as lithology, estimated zones of porosity, and
an accurate projection of a curve landing target depth throughout the vertical and build sections
of the Frisco 3-31H. These data, in conjunction Upper Bakken structural isopach maps, were
utilized to predict a local structure expected to be encountered during the lateral section of the
Frisco 3-31H, aided in geosteering decisions. The Frisco 1-31H was a north-bearing lateral well
drilled parallel to the Frisco 3-31H within the Middle Bakken pool. Its completed geosteering
cross section, interpreted from gamma ray and borehole data during the lateral portion of the
Frisco 1-31H, were used as an additional type log for geosteering purposes.

ENGINEERING OPERATIONS
Vertical
Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on November 21, 2014. 36
weight BTC 9.625" diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2192' prior to the arrival of
H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8490' MD on November
23, 2014. Vertical operations were completed with 2 8.75" bits at a total depth of 10270'MD on
November 26, 2014. Diesel based drilling mud weighing 9.6-9.9 ppg was used throughout the
vertical section.
Curve Build
The build section began early on the morning of November 27, 2014 at 10270'MD with a fixed
2.38° mud motor and 8.75" PDC bit. The curve was drilled to a measured depth of 11169', a total
of 899' in 16 hours. Necessary build rates were achieved and the bit was landed at 10791.4' TVD,
10.4' into the targeted Mississippian Middle Bakken. 32 weight BTC 7" diameter intermediate
casing was installed and cemented in place at 11167' MD (10790.4' TVD) on November 29,
2014.
Lateral
The active drilling fluid system was switched to brine-based mud for the lateral portion of the
Frisco 3-31H, with a weight 9.7-9.9 ppg. Lateral drilling resumed along a general 0-degree North
azimuth on December 19, 2014, after drilling out of the 7" intermediate casing shoe. The north-
bearing Frisco 3-31H lateral then drilled to a depth of 17871' MD (10802.1' TVD), at which
point gamma began to indicate a shale strike. Bottom's up was circulated and the suspicion was
confirmed. We then tripped out of the hole for a new BHA, as the present assembly was
suspiciously weak. We returned to bottom and sidetracked at 16395' MD. Drilling then
proceeded well until MWD malfunctions necessitated a trip at 19370' MD. After this, the well
was drilled with one near-Lower Bakken Shale strike to TD. Total depth was reached on
December 26, 2014 with a final projection to bit at 20525'MD, 89.6° INC, 359.1° AZ,
10791.26'TVD, concluding the 14th day. The bottom hole location (BHL) is approximately 231'
from the North line (FNL) and 635' from the East line (FEL) of Section 30, Township 152 North,
Range 102 West.

GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Methods
NorAm Wellsite Services provided wellsite geologic supervision for the Frisco 3-31H. A two-
person team of geologists provided geosteering services while logging drill cuttings and
monitoring gas from 8490'MD until the well was drilled to completion and production liner was
set. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and chromatography
data were monitored and recorded using a NorAm Wellsite Services Advanced Gas Detector
(AGD 08), including a Continental Laboratories total gas detector and chromatograph Varian.
Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data,
lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with
directional drilling survey data. Rock cuttings were collected by H&P 320 rig crews in 30'
intervals throughout the vertical and build sections, and 100' intervals throughout the lateral,
under the supervision of the wellsite geologists. The cuttings were then examined both wet and
dried using a 10x-25x zoom binocular stereo microscope with 10x oculars. Examination of
hydrocarbon cut fluorescence was conducted until the completion of the lateral well, and was
described as a slow, weak, yellow green diffuse cut for the majority of the lateral, although many
sections of the targeted zone showed no hydrocarbon cut fluorescence. These data in conjunction
with gamma ray data provided by Phoenix Technology Services MWD hands and real-time
drilling parameters from the RigSense EDR workstation were used to provide Phoenix
Technology Services directional drillers with optimal TVD targets ensuring a wellbore within the
10' targeted Middle Bakken drilling interval.

Please see the Total Gas Data section for hydrocarbon shows throughout the vertical and build

Hydrocarbon shows consistently increased throughout the duration of the lateral portion of the

GEOSTEERING
Directional and MWD equipment and personnel were provided by Phoenix Technology Services
for the duration of the Frisco 3-31H. NorAm Wellsite Services wellsite geologists worked
together with Phoenix and the Continental Resources, Inc. operations geologist to make
decisions ensuring maximum wellbore exposure within the targeted zone.
The potential pay zone for the Frisco 3-31H was determined by evaluating electric logs from
offset wells in the area, including neutron density logs from the Iverson 1-31H. It was
determined the ideal target would be approximately 10' thick, and begin 10' into the Middle
Member of the Bakken Formation and extend to 6' above the Lower Bakken Shale. The 90°
landing target was projected to be 10' below the top of the Middle Bakken, at the top of the target
zone.

more reliably differentiated, as well as providing some structural control in the form of hard

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
SUMMARY
1. The Frisco 3-31H is a Middle Bakken horizontal well spudded with H&P320 on November
21, 2014, and was successfully drilled in the Williston Field in 14 days, reaching a total depth of
20525'MD at 20:16 CST on December 26, 2014.
2. The wellbore was within the targeted zone 92% of the 9356' lateral footage, exposing a total of
8607' of open hole within the prospective pay zone.
3. Samples throughout the course of the lateral varied from a cream to light brown, very
calcareous to dolomitic siltstone to a more brown to brown-grey hue, and in the lower most
portion of zone to a more grey brown to grey hue. Shows were best in the upper 5' of zone
overall.
4. Diesel based invert drilling fluid during the vertical and curve build sections, and brine-based
mud during the lateral, maintained stable hole conditions and permitted adequate analysis of gas
concentrations.
4. Background gas values averaged 350 units throughout the lateral and connection gas values
averaged around 500 units. The highest gas show observed was 774 units at 18400'MD.
5. Geosteering and borehole guidance were accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data,
lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with
directional drilling survey data from Phoenix Technology Services. The overall apparent dip of
the Frisco 3-31H was approximately 90.03°.
6. The Frisco 3-31H is awaiting completion operations to maximize production.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
