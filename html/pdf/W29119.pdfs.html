GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
XTO Energy, Inc.'s Eide 31X-29G (250' FNL; 2449' FEL NWNE Sec 29, T158N, R95W, Williams Co.,
ND) is located approximately 6 miles northwest of Tioga, in Williams Co. ND. The well is east of the
Nesson anticline and part of a 3 well pad program designed to develop oil production in a unit
composed of parts of Sections 29 and 32. The Eide 31X-29G well was drilled to 1,915' and 9-
5/8"surface casing set to a depth of 1,910' MD. The small drilling rig drilling the pad's surface holes was
then moved off the well. On October 29, 2014 the Nabors B11 rig was walked to the well and rigged up.
It then drilled to 10095' and 7" intermediate casing was set to a depth of 10070'. The lateral was then
drilled to 19733' and a production liner was run to 19683'. The well is to be a Three Forks 1rst Bench
well. The intent was to remain in a 19' thick Target Zone of the 1rst Bench, stratigraphically situated
from 7' to 24' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. Through geosteering and directional drilling
techniques the wellbore was drilled to maximize its extent in the target zone to the Total Depth of
19733'.
EQUIPMENT AND SAMPLING
Gas detection and wellsite geological supervision commenced at 7,900', in the Charles Formation.
Geologic analysis for Eide 31X-29G was provided by a Decollement Consulting geology team.
Information was networked through a Pason electronic data recorder system. This equipment provided
depth, ROPs, pump strokes, total gas units, and other drilling parameters to monitors across the drilling
location and internet.
Sample collection began above the Charles Formation and continued with 30' interval samples until
total depth was reached. Rock cuttings were analyzed in wet and dry conditions under a binocular
microscope and also with a fluoroscope to analyze fluorescence and solvent cuts. Detailed lithologic
descriptions are included in the lithology portion to this report. Sample quality was good for most of the
well. Cuttings will be sent to the North Dakota Geologic Survey and to XTO Energy, Inc.
OFFSET DATA
The primary offset wells used for structural correlation during vertical and curve operations were the
XTO Energy, Inc. Eide 31X-29D, and the Petro-Hunt Pederson AI 1 wells. These wells were useful in
the vertical hole and during curve drilling operations while landing the well.
DRILLING THE VERTICAL AND CURVE HOLE SECTIONS
The Eide 31X-29G spudded October 14, 2014 and reached a surface casing depth of 1915' and was
cased and cemented to 1910'. The intermediate and curve hole section was then drilled to 10095' and
7" casing was run to 10070' and cemented in preparation for the drilling of the lateral in the 1rst bench
of the Three Forks.
Gas detection and wellsite geological supervision commenced at 7,900', in the Charles Formation.
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in part-per-million concentrations. Sampled
gas was pulled from Pason's agitator in the possum belly and analyzed by Pason's Gas Analyzer to
deliver the compositional data.
5
A successful Three Forks landing was accomplished by comparing gamma signatures from the offsets
to the MWD TVD gamma ray log produced during the drilling of the Eide 31X-29C. The landing target
true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to ensure accurate landing of the curve within the
Three Forks as near the target window as possible.
Logging began in the Charles Formation, a series of salts, anhydrites, limestones and shales. The
Base of the Last Salt of the Charles Formation was encountered at a depth of 8,133' MD (TVD
8,131'). At MD 8,180' (TVD 8,178') the Ratcliffe was entered, and was a light to medium grey algal
limestone (mudstone) was drilled. Some stringers of anhydrite were also noted. The Mission Canyon
formation (a gray dense to finely crystalline mudstone) was then drilled into at MD 8,338' (TVD 8,336').
The Lodgepole formation came in at 8,941' MD (8,938' TVD). Samples showed a medium to dark grey
mudstone. Drilling then continued through the Lodgepole limestone to the KOP at 9,225' MD. The BHA
was changed out to a curve building assembly, the BHA was then tripped in and the curve was drilled
to 10,094' MD. Background gas varied from 71 units to 130 units during the curve. C1 through C4
gases were also present in proportionate quantities. Sliding continued through the Lodgepole formation
to the top of the Upper Bakken Shale, which gamma showed coming in at MD 9,652' (TVD 9,572'), a
13' zone of black carbonaceous shale with some pyrite. The highest gas reading recorded was 797
units. The Middle Bakken Dolomite was encountered at MD 9,676' (TVD 9,584'), with gamma and
samples confirming the light gray, very silty dolomite. Some dull yellow fluorescence was observed that
had a good flush cut when exposed to solvent. Total gas units ranged from 94 to 684 units with
proportionate C1 through C4's. The Lower Bakken Shale was drilled at MD 9801' (TVD 9641') and
was composed of black carbonaceous shale with gas readings reaching a maximum of 758 units. The
Pronghorn was encountered at MD 9903' (TVD 9668') and was composed of a light grey to light brown
sandy dolomite. The target formation, the Three Forks Formation was drilled at MD 9930' (TVD 9673')
and was composed of light grey to light brown silty dolomite with a dull yellow fluorescence that
produced a good flush cut when exposed to solvent. The landed well was drilled to a projected 90
degrees at which point the well was rotated for 61 feet then TD'ed for 7-inch casing.
GEOLOGIC INTERPRETATION OF THE THREE FORKS TARGET SECTION
For geosteering and evaluation purposes a TVD log of the well's target zone was created from the
MWD data recorded during the drilling of the curve of the Petro-Hunt Pederson AI 1, which was used as
a reference during the drilling of the lateral by the wellsite team.
Figure 1. Log of the Petro-Hunt Pederson AI 1 Three Forks section.
From the base of the Lower Bakken Shale to the base of the Three Forks 1st Bench section was 26 feet
thick, including 5 feet of the Pronghorn. Most of the samples of this clean dolomitic section contained
substantial proportions of quartz silt and extremely fine quartz sand and could also be termed a
dolomitic siltstone. Most of the cuttings observed had a dull yellow hydrocarbon fluorescence that gave
6
a fair to good cut with organic solvent. Cuttings in the 2nd Bench could be classified as dirty dolomitic
siltstone, with light to dark greyish brown cuttings, trace to abundant anhydrite, and usually no visible to
very little dull yellow fluorescence that gave a fair to good cut with organic solvent.
The drilled cuttings in the 2nd Bench observed during the lateral were mostly composed of silty
dolomites and light colored shales. The dolomite colors were mostly light brown to medium greyish
brown with some light to medium grey cuttings. The cuttings ranged from firm to soft, were silty to very
silty, and were slightly to moderately argillaceous throughout the target section. Minor constituents were
mostly of anhydrite, with trace amounts of pyrite found, especially in the top of the 2nd Bench.
DRILLING AND GEOSTEERING OF THE THREE FORKS LATERAL
The geosteering of the lateral was done by the XTO Operations Geologist with input from the wellsite
geologic team, with a goal of maximizing the wellbore contact with the best reservoir rock available
while minimizing dogleg severity and drilling as fast as conditions permitted.
