GENERAL:
On November 13, 2014 Continental Resource s Raleigh 7-20H was spudded as a Middle
Bakken formation, 2560 spaced, lateral well. The subject well is being drilled in the Dollar Joe field,
located about 10 miles south of Ray, in Williams County in North Dakota. Ray is located in the north
central portion of the Williston Basin. Surface location of the Raleigh 7-20H is 310 FSL & 1075
FEL, SESE of Section 20, T155N - R97W. The Raleigh 7-20H was planned with an ultimate goal of
oil production in the Middle Bakken member of the Bakken formation. The horizontal well-path was
planned to follow structure with an estimated 90.22° average formation dip to total depth at 21,529
MD. The well bore was planned to be drilled to the north across sections 20 and section 17 following
an azimuth of 179.09°. The Raleigh 7-20H well is located on the same pad as the Annapolis 6-29H1,
Annapolis 7-29H and Raleigh 6-20H1 wells.

ENGINEERING:
The Raleigh 7-20H surface hole was pre-spudded with a smaller surface rig prior to Cyclone 10
moving on location. Pad height of the Raleigh 7-20H was 2,405 above mean sea level with the
Cyclone #10 kelly bushing located at 2,425 above mean sea level, 20 above ground level.
Raleigh 7-20H was drilled with fresh water from surface to 2,260 MD. From surface casing,
the Raleigh 7-20H was drilled with oil based mud. Oil based mud was used from 2,260 MD to
11,338 MD. A switch was then made to brine mud from 11,338 MD to a total depth of 20,914 MD.
Initially, the Raleigh 7-20H was drilled as a 13 ½ hole to 2,260 MD, and 9 5/8 36# J-55
casing was set to a measured depth of 2,260 through the Fox Hills formation into the Pierre formation.
The Raleigh 7-20H then progressed as an 8 ¾ hole from the upper part of the Pierre formation until
landing of the curve in the Middle Bakken member. Kick off point (KOP) was reached in the
Lodgepole formation on November 16, 2014, 1815 hrs at 10,546 MD (10,545.11 TVD). At KOP, a
trip was made to pick up a LEAM mud motor with a 2.5° fixed bend and a new PDC bit. Directional
work during the curve was performed by Robert Horton and Nick Edwards from LEAM directional
services. The curve from vertical to horizontal began on November 17, 2014 at 1057 hrs and was
drilled starting from 10,572 MD (10,571.11 TVD) and landed horizontal (92.91°) at 11,338 MD
(11,048.64 TVD). The curve was drilled holding an average build rate of 12.13° per 100 ft and was
landed on November 18, 2014 at 1715 hrs. One mechanical problem was encountered while drilling
the curve, a trip was made at 11,041 MD to pick up a new BHA due to lack of build rates.

Once landed, 7 32# P-110 casing was set from surface to a measured depth of 11,313 through
the Charles Salt and curve into the Middle Bakken member of the Bakken formation. Then, cementing
of the 7 casing was performed by Halliburton.
Drilling of the 6 lateral portion of the well began on December 7, 2014 at 1901 hrs. The lateral
was within the 13 marker window for 84% or 8,044 measured depth of the 9,576 measured depth
drilled. The lateral was within the Middle Bakken member for 100% of the time. The lateral was TD d
at 20,914 MD (10,998.29 TVD) on December 12, 2014 at 1902 hrs.
The lateral had a net rise of 34.8 throughout 9,576.28 of vertical section drilled from the
casing shoe to TD. Regional dip throughout the lateral was 90.21° (see attached structure sheet for
calculated target).
Two mechanical problems were encountered while drilling the Raleigh 7-20H lateral. Trips
were made at 15,687 MD and 17,019 MD to replace the MWD tool.
The Raleigh 7-20H was completed with seven BHA s, and took a total of 123.0 hours to
complete, for an average penetration rate of 131.4 feet per hour (see attached BHA record summary).
Once TD d, cement bond logs were run by Schlumberger. Then, a 4 ½ 11.6# liner was run
from ~10,500 MD to ~20,775 MD.
GEOLOGICAL EVALUATION:
Geologic supervision of the Continental Resource s Raleigh 7-20H well was provided by Geo-
Link, Inc with a two-man mudlogging / geo-steering consulting service. Geologic evaluation
began just above the Charles formation and continued until TD at the end of the lateral in the
Middle Bakken member. MSI s Red Box (chromatograph) was used to record gas levels at the
drilling rig s shakers. The Red Box gas data was integrated into Pason s EDR (Electronic Data
Recorder) system. Also integrated into Pason s EDR system was gamma ray data provided by
Phoenix Technologies MWD services. Pason s EDR provided drill rate, Red Box gas data,
gamma and circulation data for viewing on location and remote via Pason s Datahub. Lagged
samples for the well were caught in 30 intervals from 8,750 measured depth until total depth
was reached at 20,914 measured depth. Cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope
and UV fluoroscope. Cuttings were tested with hydrochloric acid and alizarin to distinguish
between carbonates. Acetone was used to examine oil cut from the samples. The samples were
logged in MPlot, MSI s mudlogging program. After sample evaluation, one set of dried
cuttings was saved for the State of North Dakota.
Sample evaluation for the Raleigh 7-20H began just above the Charles Salt formation at a
measured depth of 8,750 . The Charles Salt came in at 8,767 measured depth, 8,766 total
vertical depth and -6,341 mean sea level. The Charles Salt consisted mostly of salts. Salts from
the Charles formation were typically clear to translucent to milky to translucent red in color,

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
