Well Evaluation
Johnson 24X-31F [SE SW Section 31, T150N, R97W] is located approximately 6 miles
southeast of Watford City in Siverston field of McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Johnson
24X-31F is a horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin, consisting of one lateral
drilled toward the north. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Three
Forks. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will then be used to ensure maximum
exposure to the Three Forks target zone.
Vertical Offset Wells
· XTO Energy, Cherry Creek State 44-36H [SE SE Sec. 36, T150N, R98W] is located 0.58
miles west of the subject well.
· XTO Energy, Johnson 24-31NEH [SE SW Sec. 31, T150N, R97W] shares the pad with
the subject well.
· XTO Energy, Lucy 14X-32F [SW SW Sec. 32, T150N, R97W] is located 0.73 miles east
of the subject well.
Figure 1. Map of subject well and correlating offset wells (Source: NDIC website).
Geological Methodology
Sunburst provided the geological analysis for the XTO Johnson 24X-31F well. Lagged samples
at 30' intervals were caught by Sunburst well site geologists beginning at 9,250' MD, in the
Charles Salt. Geological analysis consisted of rock sampling, gas detection, rate of penetration
(ROP) and gamma ray data, which in collaboration, aided in formation identification,
geosteering and dip calculations.
Prospective Lithology
The Lodgepole Formation was drilled at 10,190' MD/ 10,186' TVD, 2' high to prognosis and
3' high to the Johnson 24-31NEH. The Lodgepole consists primarily of grey to brown
argillaceous mudstone. Trace amounts of disseminated pyrite were observed in samples. Thin
layers of clean, microcrystalline limestone were also observed in the Lodgepole. Gas shows in
the Lodgepole read from 30 to 168 units against 9.8 ppg drilling mud.
The Bakken Formation has two members of black, pyritic shale, known as the Upper Bakken
Shale and Lower Bakken Shale. Layered between the Upper Bakken and Lower Bakken Shale is
the Middle Bakken Member. The Middle Bakken consists primarily of silty sandstone which is
fine grained, moderately cemented and light to medium brown in color. The Bakken Formation
is particularly rich with organics, making it the primary oil source rock.
The Upper Bakken Shale was drilled at 11,080' MD/ 11,019' TVD, 1' high to prognosis and 2'
high to the Johnson 24-31NEH. Gas shows topped 2,234 units against 10.2 ppg drilling mud.
The Middle Bakken was drilled at 11,112' MD/ 11,039' TVD, 1' high to prognosis and even to
the Johnson 24-31NEH. Gas shows ranged primarily from 785-1,583 units, against 10.2 ppg
drilling mud. The Lower Bakken Shale was drilled at 11,188' MD/ 11,084' TVD, 1' low to
prognosis and 5' low to the Johnson 24-31NEH. The Lower Bakken Shale formation top was
critical in determining an accurate, viable landing depth to our Three Forks target.
The Three Forks Formation (Picture 1) was the target reservoir for the XTO Johnson 24X-31F.
The Three Forks was drilled at 11,265' MD/ 11,119' TVD, 5' low to prognosis and 4' low to the
Johnson 24-31NEH. The Three Forks consists of cream to light-medium brown colored
dolomite, microsucrosic in texture, with primarily intercrystalline porosity. Trace amounts of
light mint green shale and pyrite were commonly observed. Oil shows consisted of spotty light-
medium brown staining. Gas shows reached upwards of 600 units on multiple occasions against
9.85-9.9 ppg drilling mud. The Three Forks target zone entry was drilled at 11,289' MD/
11,127.24' TVD. Target zone landing occurred at 11,421' MD/ 11,142.47' TVD, with a target
zone top of 11,127.75' TVD and target zone bottom of 11,147.75' TVD.
Picture 1. Three Forks Formation: dolomite & shale.
Target Zone Gas and Oil Shows
Three Forks background gas shows in the Johnson 24X-31F typically ranged from 200-500 units
while drilling the lateral (Figure 2). The highest gas shows observed reached slightly over 1,000
units, on rare occasion. Peak gas shows, including the two highest observed, were generally in
the upper and lower 3' of the target zone. High rates of drilling penetration (ROP) often
correlated with increased gas shows in the upper target zone. Gas was seldom high enough to
produce a flare, although the gas was being vented through the gas buster frequently.

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 20' interval in the first bench of the Three Forks
Formation (Figure 3). The top of the target zone was set 21' below the base of the Lower
Bakken Shale. The most significant markers used to geo-steer in the target zone consisted of the
highest and lowest gamma markers, particularly in the lower half of the zone. The highest
gamma (units of API) marker was observed approximately 5' above the bottom of the target
zone, reaching upwards of 125 API, with the lowest gamma observed 2' and 9' above the bottom
of our target window, reading 45-65 API. The majority of the Johnson 24X-31F was drilled
within the lower half of the target zone, providing ample signatures for accurate geo-steering.
Offset wells indicated that the Three Forks Formation would likely be down-dipping overall
from landing to TD. The gross overall target formation drop in TVD from start to finish in the
Johnson 24X-31F lateral was 26.23' (0.16° down; see Figure 4). The bottom hole location is
estimated to be 9,929.18' N & 636.94' E of surface location or approximately 249.86' FNL &
2,631.09' FEL, NW NE Sec. 30, T150N, R97W. Geo-steering methods exposed 94% of uncased
borehole to the 20' Three Forks target zone. The well reached a TD of 20,793' on November 1,
2014 (Figure 4).

Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
