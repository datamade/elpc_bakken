Well Evaluation
Johnson Federal 11X-5A [Lot 4 Section 5, T149N, R97W] is located approximately 8 miles
southeast of Watford City in McKenzie County, ND. The Johnson Federal 11X-5A is a
horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled toward
the south. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Middle Bakken.
Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques were then used to maintain maximum exposure
to the target zone.

GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation at 9,270' MD to log
the Base Last Salt for landing target purposes.
The Middle Bakken Member was logged at 11,128' TVD (-8,835' MSL). It consists primarily of
silty sandstone and contains trace to rare amounts of spotty, medium brown oil stain. The Middle
Bakken was the target reservoir for the lateral portion of the well.
Gas Monitoring
While drilling the lateral, background gas values initially fluctuated between 150-350u and
elevated to 400-700u when drilling in the upper half of zone. Periods where gas was vented
through the gas buster dropped gas values to 200u-600u. Drilling fluid was circulated through
the gas buster starting around 13,620' MD, and continued throughout the lateral producing an
intermittent flare that ranged from 2' to 7'. The highest gas values recorded for the well was a
show of 3,989u while drilling the curve and a trip gas of 2,386u. The upper half of the target
zone was observed to contain the highest gas concentrations (Figure 2).

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 20' interval, the top of which was initially set at 10,950'
TVD, 28' below the top of the Upper Bakken Shale (Figure 1). The cool gamma markers reading
70-85 API at the center and lower half of our zone were used to effectively geo-steer our
wellbore. Offset wells indicated that the Middle Bakken would be initially up-dipping
approximately 0.18° from landing to around 16,500' MD then turn down dip approximately
0.20° to TD. The bottom-hole location is estimated to be 9,600.04' south & 442.50' west of
surface location or approximately 248.62 FSL & 697.50' FWL, SW SW Sec. 8, T149N, R97W.
Geo-steering techniques exposed 98% of uncased borehole to the 20' Middle Bakken ideal target
and the well reached a TD of 20,320' on January 16, 2015.

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Johnson Federal 11X-5A was spud on October 20, 2014 by a work-over
rig and officially reached TD in 20 total drilling days January 16, 2015. Samples from the
Middle Bakken silty sandstone target contained oil staining that was described as trace to rare
with visible intergranular porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were
successfully utilized to expose 98% of the lateral borehole to the ideal Middle Bakken target.
The Johnson Federal 11X-5A awaits completion operations to maximize its production potential.
