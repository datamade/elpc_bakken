Well Synopsis
Mildred 5-19H1 Cyclone 2
Three Forks
Well Plan:
The Mildred 5-19H1 was spud on September 25th, 2014 at 18:00 hours, with a surface hole
location of 355 FSL and 2000 FWL in the SESW corner of Section 19 - Township 155 North
and Range 98 West in Williams Co., North Dakota. This well was operated by Continental
Resources Incorporated with the objective target of the late Devonian Three Forks. The well
plan showed a build section with a kickoff point of 10698 MD in the Mississippian Lodgepole
with a 13°/100 build rate to the landing point of 11391 MD; 11139 TVD, approximately 20
below the Three Forks Formation. The plan was to drill lateral for an estimated 9651 to the
hardline in the NWNE of section 18 - Township 155 North and Range 98 West in Williams Co.,
North Dakota, following the estimated dip of 90.05°. The projected well path is to the North
with a proposed azimuth of 2.47°.
The offsets provided were the Mildred 1-4-19H Wells all operated by Continental Resources
Inc.
Gas logged in the vertical and lateral sections were monitored using Mud Logging Systems
Mlogger & Mcontrol. (Primary Logger: ML-369 Backup Logger ML-098)
M-Logger CC & TC filaments calibrated with 1% and 100% test gas Chromatograph
calibrated with 1% test gas (gas units).
Build Section:
The build section was kicked off @ 10705 MD on September 30th, 2014 at 03:00 hours, In the
Lodgepole formation. The up hole markers logged were the Base of the Last Salt (9497 TVD (-
7200 ss)), Mission Canyon (9708 TVD (-7411 ss)), and the Lodgepole (10333 TVD (-8036
ss)). The build section started on September 30th, 2014 at 19:40 hours, w/ a (BHA) bottom
hole assembly consisting of a 6.75 Mpact XXL Performance motor with a 2.5° fxd bend and a
Security 8 3/4 MMD55M PDC Bit. This assembly able to generate sufficient enough build rates
to land in the approximate target zone. The down hole markers below kick off point consisted
of the False Bakken (11020 TVD (-8723 ss)), Upper Bakken Shale (11034 TVD (-8737 ss)),
Middle Bakken Member (11052 TVD (-8755 ss)), Lower Bakken Shale (11095 TVD (-8798 ss))
& Three Forks Formation (11121 TVD (-8824 ss)). These markers came in within a few feet of
the prognosis and offset logs. The estimated landing was calculated @ 11139 TVD, which was
never adjusted through the build section. The landing for 7 intermediate casing was
completed on October 1st, 2014 at 11:39 hours, about 15 into the Three Forks Formation,
with a landing at 11460 MD; 11136.15 TVD. The 7 casing was set @ 11444 MD; 11135.94
TVD, with a location of: 812 FSL & 2244 FWL SESW Section 19: T155N-R98W Williams Co.,
ND. The build section consisted of one assembly, 755 total footage drilled w/ 603 sliding
(9.67 hrs) 152 rotating (3.33 hrs). (See Mildred 5-19H1 Build, TVD log & Formation Tops for
more detailed information)
Gas observed in the build section showed gas ranging 23-505 units through the Lodgepole
formation, averaging 99 units with no oil shows with faint to good milky-cloudy invert cut.
The Upper & Lower Bakken Shale s showed moderate amounts of gas resulting in 1686-1822
units. The Middle Bakken Member ranged from 114-904 units, averaging 438 units. The Three
Forks formation displayed 179-1246 units, averaging 589 units and 650-2890 units while
circulating out trip gas after the landing. (See Mildred 5-19H1 Build log for more detailed gas
data)
Lateral Section:
Penetration of the lateral section started on October 3rd, 2014 at 13:58 hrs, with a depth of
11460 MD, drilling with a 6 Baker Hughes T406x PDC bit and 4.75, 5/6 lobe 8.3 stage 1.5°
bend RPG 1.04 Mpact XXL Performance mud motor. The plan was to drill in the target Zone
and follow the 10-11 zone of interest between the Internal Shale and a hot gamma marker
13 from the top of the Three Forks. The lateral section was drilled from 11460 MD to 20995
MD for a lateral length of 9535 , with a bottom hole location: 231 FNL & 2418 FWL NWNE
Sec 18, T155N-R98W, Williams Co., ND completed on October 10th, 2014 09:39 hours.
(20995 MD; 11099.12 TVD). The lateral section consisted of 9535 total footage drilled w/
906 sliding (35.83 hrs) 8629 rotating (86.34 hrs). The well was drilled entirely in the Three
Forks Formation with one complete trip for a bit (BHA) @ 17953 MD; 11104.4 TVD.
Samples collected in the lateral section mostly consisted of light-medium brown, light-
medium tan/brown, buff tan, mottled brown/tan, dark gray/brown Dolomite/Limestone
consisting of abundant to trace amounts of Pyrite, mainly in our upper and lower target zone.
Samples also consisted of blue/green, gray/green shale, predominately in the upper target
zone and internal shale. The middle-lower zone displayed predominately more tan/brown
clean Dolomite, above the Internal 1 Shale, consisting of fair to good porosity. This targeted
zone displayed 5-20%, light-medium pale greenish/yellow & light-medium brown oil on
shakers and fair bright green/yellow fluorescence with fair cloudy dull blooming/streaming
blue/white cut.
Gas averaged 1452 units and ranged from 240 units to 8022 units of gas. Connection gasses
ranged from 1001 units to 5478 units and trip gas ranged from 1600 units to 4677 units. Gas
in the clean Dolomite zone produced more gas & oil shows versus drilling the bottom Internal
Shale & upper target zone. (For more detailed sample descriptions & gas data see Mildred
Federal 5-19H1 Horizontal Log)
The formation dip was calculated using several significant gamma markers throughout the
target zone. The most consistent markers observed were in the clean Dolomite zone,
between the orange marker line and bottom target zone and the Internal Shale. The upper
target zone marker (orange dashed-line) ranged from 110 125 (API) and was a significant
hard spot, which resulted in severe deflections of over 2-3 degrees. The lower-middle target
zone displayed the coolest gamma, which ranged from 42-82 (API). This zone was relatively
easy to drill in with good to very good well control, often allowing 3-4 stands of rotation
without sliding. The clean Dolomite zone was the main target and proved to be the best
overall drilling plan, since it was tracking consistently better than the upper & lower zone.
The lower target zone and Internal Shale displayed more Pyrite and Blue-green Shales giving
more erratic gamma counts ranging from 115-144 (API), whereas the middle target zone
displayed less Pyrite in the clean tan-brown Dolomite giving us lower gamma counts. Utilizing
these distinct zones it was possible to calculate an accurate formation dip rate. The
Formation climbed 60 from the start of the lateral to total well depth (90.23° Dip). (For more
detailed gamma signatures and structure please see Mildred 5-19H1 cross section)

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
