INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 7B [Lot 3, Section 3,
T153N, R93W] is located approximately 13 miles north of the town of New Town in
Mountrail County, ND. It is the second of three wells on the Langved 5393 12-3 pad. The
Langved 5393 12-3 7B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin
consisting of one 9,536' lateral drilled toward the south. Geologically, the Williston Basin is
an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the area of interest, the Bakken Formation (Late Devonian) consists
of four members: the basal pronghorn member, the organic rich lower shale member, the
coastal-carbonate middle member which consists of calcareous silty sandstone and the upper
shale. The two shales act as traps and sources rocks and the Middle Bakken Member acts as a
reservoir. Advances in drilling and well stimulation have made the Bakken Formation an
extremely viable oil and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found `Structure'
section appended to this report. Offset well control was essential in curve operations, to
successfully land within the Middle Bakken. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray
signatures in these wells were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations
in order to successfully land the curve. There was no need to update the target landing true
vertical depth (TVD) for this well.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 12-3 7B was reentered on October 13th, 2013 by Xtreme 21. Drilling fluid
was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. One trip
was made during the vertical at 8,179' for low ROP. The curve was landed at 10,922' MD
(10,567 TVD) with an inclination of 91.95°. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing
was set and cemented to 10,904' MD. To start the lateral, a 6 Varel PDC bit, MWD tool, and
a 1.5° fixed mud motor were used. The BHA accomplishments are tabulated in an appendix
(BHA Record) to this report. The well reached total depth (TD) at 20,458' MD at ~ 0950 hrs
CST on November 14, 2014, generating an exposure of 9,536 linear feet of 6 hole through
the Middle Bakken target interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies 10,060.34' south &
266.62' east of surface location or approximately 234.43' FSL and 2,549.62 FWL, SE SW
Sec. 10, T153N, R93W.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 25-136 units
above a background of ~36 units against a 9.9-10 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight. Methane
(C1) was observed in the Charles Formation and throughout the vertical. Gases heavier than
C1 were not observed. After kicking off the curve, shows were similar with respect the
vertical. Shows remained low from kick-off through entry of the False Bakken interval of the
lower Lodgepole. Gas shows were consistently high from this interval through curve landing
in the targeted Middle Bakken pay zone.
Shows from the False Bakken through curve landing ranged from 410-6426 units (C1-C4)
over a background of 365 units against a 10.3 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight.
The lateral began with gas shows ranging from 2,000-3,000 units. Shows were highest in the
ideal targets unit At ~ 13,100', MD shows increased greatly and ranged from 1983-5834 units
(C1-C4) over a background of over 1000 units against a 9.8 lb/gal salt-water mud weight.
Below the ideal target zone shows dropped to 383-757 units. A 2-3' flare was observed while
drilling the lateral. Free oil was not observed over the shakers.
GEOSTEERING
Due to the existing well bore of the Langved 11-
3H in the Middle Bakken ideal target zone two
target zones were used for geosteering. The first,
referred to as the ideal target zone, was a 15'
target set between the MBB top and 4' above the
MBA top (Figure 3). At 12,500' MD we
changed zones to what was referred to as the
anti-collision zone, which is set between Hard
Topline and Hard Baseline (Figure 3). Once in
the anti-collision zone the low gamma marker
labeled "d" on the type gamma log was used to
steer, and the wellbore was kept beneath it. At
19,200' MD we were allowed to return the ideal
target zone. The decision was made to stay near
the bottom of the ideal target zone for the
remainder of the lateral, continuing to use the
"d" marker to steer.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 12-3 7B was drilled by Xtreme
21 from reentry to TD in 14 days. The Langved 5393 12-3 7B reached a total depth of
20,458' MD on November 14, 2013. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and
sample examination obtained during the Langved 5393 12-3 7B indicate a hydrocarbon rich
system in the Middle Bakken target. Significant shows were recorded throughout the lateral.
Samples from the target zone consist of off white to light gray with occasional light-brown,
calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular porosity and a rare light-brown oil stain,
which yielded a faint, pale-yellow, good, blooming to streaming cut fluorescence. Lateral
operations were successful in exposing 9,536' of potentially productive Middle Bakken Sand
target zone rock to the wellbore. The well was within the targeted zones for 100% of the
9,536' of lateral.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
