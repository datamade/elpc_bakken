GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
Continental Resources, Inc. Stangeland ­Hayes EcoPad is a four well location
approximately 6 miles southeast of Williston, North Dakota and within the boundaries of the
Crazy Man Creek Field. The Stangeland ­Hayes EcoPad location and drilling procedures
were designed to allow efficient extraction of hydrocarbon resources from the Bakken and
Three Forks Formations lying beneath sections 6, 7 and 18 of T153N, R99W and section 31
of T154N R99W. Hayes 3-6H1 was the third lateral drilled from the EcoPad and targeted the
First Bench of the Three Forks Formation. Surface location coordinates are 300' FSL &
1035' FWL, S6-T153N R99W, Williams County, North Dakota. The Stangeland ­Hayes
drilling unit consists the aforementioned four sections. The Stangeland 2-7H and 3-7H1 are
southerly transects and the Hayes 2-6H and 3-6H1 are northerly transects. All holes are
planned as ~10,000' laterals. The completed wells will utilize hydraulic fracturing technology
to enhance their petroleum yields from the Middle member of the Bakken and First Bench of
the Three Forks Formations. Drilling the Hayes 3-6H1 began September November 5, 2014
and the hole reached total depth of MD 21057' TVD 11186' on November 26, 2014.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Cyclone Rig 33 spudded the Hayes 3-6H1 on September 22, 2014. Prior to the Cyclone rig
moving onto the hole, a spudding rig drilled a 12-1/4" hole with fresh water to 2356'. The

point at MD 10760' in the Lodgepole Formation. Directional tools employing a 2.38°degree
bent assembly were used to land the build curve at MD 11523' with a projected TVD of
11223' and inclination of 89.7°. 7", 32# P110 LTC intermediate casing was then run to MD
11515', TVD 11230' and cemented. The Intermediate casing shoe location is 795.7' FSL
1101' FWL, S6-T153N-R99W MD 11514' TVD 11230', Williams County, North Dakota
Lateral
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
with 9.8# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. Four 6" PDC bits and
1.5° steerable motors were used to drill the lateral section. A single 9543' long northerly
transect was drilled in the First Bench of the Three Forks Formation on a 0° azimuth to MD
21057', TVD 11231', terminating 33' south of the unit boundary hard-line near the north end
of Section 31, T154N R99W. Initial structural interpretation assumed relatively consistent
formation dip of 90.4° to approximately MD 14500 and then flattening to approximately 90°
from MD 14500 to MD 18000' and finally dipping upward at approximately 90.5° from MD
18000 to TD. Over the course of the lateral, the formation dipped 40' upward resulting in an
average formation dip of 90.24°.

LITHOLOGY AND HYDROCARBON SHOWS
Vertical and Curve
Geologic evaluation of Hayes 3-6H1 began at MD 8950' in the Charles Formation. The
Base of the Last Charles Salt was encountered at MD 9577' TVD 9577' (SS TVD -7251), 4'
structurally high to Hayes 2-6H and 25' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H. The Ratcliffe and
Midale intervals are composed of compact wackestone to mudstone with interbeds of
massive anhydrite. No significant hydrocarbon shows were observed while drilling through
the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at MD 9789' TVD 9788', (SS
TVD -7462),
4' structurally high to Hayes 2-6H and 6' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H.
Samples from the Rival interval (9791' to 9805') consisted of light gray to light gray brown
microcrystalline wackestone with poor to fair anhydrite occluded pinpoint vuggy porosity a
trace of dark brown oil stain, dull yellow fluorescence and faint milky pale yellow cut. Total
gas increased from 220 units to 400 units and then declined rapidly to 230 units while the
Rival was penetrated. With the lack of significant shows observed in the samples and the
rapid decline of the gas liberated while drilling through and beyond the Rival, it is unlikely that
commercial volumes of hydrocarbons are present at this location.
No other significant
porous zones or gas shows were observed while drilling through the Mission Canyon
Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole Formation was encountered at MD 10380' TVD 10379' (SS TVD
-8053), ), 1' structurally high to Hayes 2-6H and 10' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H. The
curve was "kicked­off" at MD 10762'. Four gamma markers were selected and used to
calculate the curve landing point while drilling through the Lodgepole. Lodgepole samples
consisted of compact ARGILLACEOUS MUDSTONE with minor carbonaceous shale present
at the False Bakken Marker. A significant gas show from a background of 170 units to 3370
units was observed while drilling through the Scallion Interval From MD 11170' to the top of
the Upper Bakken Shale at 11189'. Samples from the Scallion consisted of very light gray
mudstone, sub hard brittle lithographic with rare fossil fragments no visible porosity or oil
show. It is likely that fractures emanating vertically from the Bakken shale are the source of
this hydrocarbon show.
The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11189' TVD 11124' (SS TVD
-8798'), ), 1' structurally low to Hayes 2-6H and 3 structurally low to Hayes 1-6H.
Samples

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
