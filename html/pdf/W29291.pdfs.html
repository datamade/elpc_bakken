GENERAL:
On October 12, 2014 Continental Resource's Annapolis 7-29H was "spudded" as a Middle
Bakken formation, 2560 spaced, lateral well. The subject well is being drilled in the Dollar Joe field,
located about 10 miles south of Ray, in Williams County in North Dakota. Ray is located in the north
central portion of the Williston Basin. Surface location of the Annapolis 7-29H is 310' FSL & 830'
FEL, SESE of Section 20, T155N - R97W. The Annapolis 7-29H was planned with an ultimate goal of
oil production in the Middle Bakken member of the Bakken formation. The horizontal well-path was
planned to follow structure with an estimated 89.63° average formation dip to total depth at 21,529'
MD. The well bore was planned to be drilled to the south across the southern part of section 20 and
across sections 29 and section 32 following an azimuth of 179.09°. The Annapolis 7-29H well is
located on the same pad as the Annapolis 6-29H1, Raleigh 7-20H and Raleigh 6-20H1 wells.
OFFSET INFORMATION:
The main offset well for drilling Continental Resource's Annapolis 7-29H well was provided
from Continental's ­ Madison 1-28H well, API# 33-105-02197. The Madison 1-28H well is located in
Section 28, T155N ­ R97W.
ENGINEERING:
The Annapolis 7-29H surface hole was pre-spudded with a smaller surface rig prior to Cyclone
10 moving on location. Pad height of the Annapolis 7-29H was 2,405' above mean sea level with the
Cyclone #10 kelly bushing located at 2,425' above mean sea level, 20' above ground level.
Annapolis 7-29H was drilled with fresh water from surface to 2,281' MD. From surface casing,
the Annapolis 7-29H was drilled with oil based mud. Oil based mud was used from 2,281' MD to
11,310' MD. A switch was then made to brine mud from 11,310' MD to a total depth of 21,505' MD.
Initially, the Annapolis 7-29H was drilled as a 13 ½" hole to 2,281' MD, and 9 5/8" 36# J-55
casing was set to a measured depth of 2,281' through the Fox Hills formation into the Pierre formation.
The Annapolis 7-29H then progressed as an 8 ¾" hole from the upper part of the Pierre formation until
landing of the curve in the Middle Bakken member. Kick off point (KOP) was reached in the
Lodgepole formation on October 15, 2014, 1646 hrs at 10,553' MD (10,551.32' TVD). At KOP, a trip
was made to pick up a LEAM mud motor with a 2.5° fixed bend and a new PDC bit. Directional work
during the curve was performed by Nick Davis and Nick Edwards from LEAM directional services.
The curve from vertical to horizontal began on October 16, 2014 at 0654 hrs and was drilled starting
from 10,553' MD (10,551.32' TVD) and landed horizontal (84.34°) at 11,288' MD (11,044.40' TVD).
The curve was drilled holding an average build rate of 11.47° per 100 ft and was landed on October 16,
2014 at 2256 hrs. Rat hole was drilled from 11,288' MD to 11310' MD. No mechanical problems were
encountered while drilling the curve.

Once TD'd, cement bond logs were run by Schlumberger. Then, a 4 ½" 11.6# liner was run
from ~10,500' MD to ~20,350' MD.
GEOLOGICAL EVALUATION:
Geologic supervision of the Continental Resource's Annapolis 7-29H well was provided by
Geo-Link, Inc with a two-man mudlogging / geo-steering consulting service. Geologic
evaluation began just above the Charles formation and continued until TD at the end of the
lateral in the Middle Bakken member. MSI's Red Box (chromatograph) was used to record gas
levels at the drilling rig's shakers. The Red Box gas data was integrated into Pason's EDR
(Electronic Data Recorder) system. Also integrated into Pason's EDR system was gamma ray
data provided by Phoenix Technologies MWD services. Pason's EDR provided drill rate, Red
Box gas data, gamma and circulation data for viewing on location and remote via Pason's
Datahub. Lagged samples for the well were caught in 30' intervals from 8,750' measured depth
until total depth was reached at 21,505' measured depth. Cuttings were examined under a
binocular microscope and UV fluoroscope. Cuttings were tested with hydrochloric acid and
alizarin to distinguish between carbonates. Acetone was used to examine oil cut from the
samples. The samples were logged in MPlot, MSI's mudlogging program. After sample
evaluation, one set of dried cuttings was saved for the State of North Dakota.

measured depth of 8,750'. The Charles Salt came in at 8,768' measured depth, 8,767' total
vertical depth and -6,342' mean sea level. The Charles Salt consisted mostly of salts. Salts from
the Charles formation were typically clear to translucent to milky to translucent red in color,

Bakken shale member.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
