Well Evaluation
XTO David Federal 21X-20B
INTRODUCTION
The XTO Energy, Inc. David Federal 21X-20B is a horizontal well targeting the Middle Member
of the Bakken Formation in the Lost Bridge Field. It is located approximately 25 miles northwest
of Killdeer in Dunn County, North Dakota [NE NW Section 20, T148N, R96W]. The vertical and
curve will be drilled using a diesel invert mud system. Plans call for a kick-off of 10,270' in the
Lodgepole Formation and for the curve to be built at 14°/100' until reaching an approximate 90°
inclination in the Middle Bakken. Seven-inch intermediate casing would then be set to isolate the
target reservoir. After casing, a 6" horizontal wellbore will be drilled into Section 29 using
saltwater drilling fluid.
OFFSET INFORMATION
Three surrounding wells were used as offset control in development of the prognosis. Electric logs
and geologic data from these offset wells provided control for formation tops and assistance with
interpretation of possible structure and gamma trends for the David Federal 21X-20B target zone.
The closest offset well, the XTO Energy, Inc., David Federal 21X-20F (#29008) [NE NW Section
20, T148N, R96W] is located 19' south and 23' east of the subject well. The XTO Energy, Inc.,
Fenton 27-1 WIW (#9682) [NW SW Section 27, T148N, R96W] is located approximately 2.45
miles northeast of the subject well. The Denbury Onshore, LLC, Lost Bridge State 1-16 (#8448)
[NE NE Section 16, T148N, R96W] is located approximately 1.88 miles northeast of the subject
well.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
To gather information for possible future development, lithologic descriptions and sample analysis
began in the Charles Formation.
The Middle Bakken was logged at 10,692' TVD (-8,735 MSL). It consists primarily of silty
sandstone that contains trace amounts of spotty, light brown oil staining. Porosity was poor in

SUMMARY
1) The XTO Energy, Inc., David Federal 21X-20B is a two section Middle Bakken horizontal
well within the Lost Bridge Field in Dunn County, North Dakota. The well was spud
September 12, 2014 and was drilled to a total depth of 19,796' MD in 18 drilling days on
January 2, 2015.
2) Throughout the vertical and curve, gas (C1-C2) ranged from 15 to 110 units until the False
Bakken was intersected. While drilling the Middle Bakken gas values (C1-C4) ranged from 15
to 944 units with connection gas reaching as high as 1,425 units.
3) Samples from the Middle Bakken target zone consist of silty sandstone that contains trace
amounts of spotty, light brown oil staining. Porosity was poor in cuttings.
4) The well-bore was drilled within the Middle Bakken for 98% of the lateral. Structurally, the
Middle Bakken Member descended an estimated 40' in TVD throughout the lateral, resulting
in an apparent overall down dip of 0.26°.
5) The David Federal 21X-20B awaits completion operations to determine its production
potential.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
