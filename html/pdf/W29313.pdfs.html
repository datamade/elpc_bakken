Well Evaluation
Introduction
Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH [SW SW
Section 16, T158N, R95W] is located approximately 7.3 miles north of Tioga, in the McGregor
Field of Williams County, North Dakota. The ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH is designed as a
horizontal Middle Bakken well situated along the western flank of the Nesson anticline of the
Williston Basin. The curve would be kicked off in the Lodgepole Formation and landed within the
Middle Bakken. Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will then be applied to maintain
maximum exposure to a 22' target zone over the course of one approximately 10,249' long lateral
drilled to the south (180.9°).
The Williston Basin is a structural and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Bakken Formation (Late Devonian ­ Early Mississippian)
consists of four members: the Upper Bakken Shale, the Middle Bakken, the Lower Bakken and
the Pronghorn. The Middle Bakken sits between the pyritic, organic-rich Upper and Lower Bakken
shale members. The Pronghorn Member, a gray siltstone, sits below the three `Bakken' members.
Through exploration and study, the Middle Bakken, which consists of fine grained, silty sandstone,
has been developed as an extremely viable oil and natural gas resource by way of horizontal
drilling and hydraulic fracturing technology.
Offset Wells
The following existing offset wells provided stratigraphic information to optimize planning for
curve landing (Figure 1). Geological tops and landing true vertical depth (TVD) of these wells
were used as control data against the corresponding geological tops identified in the vertical and
curve of the ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH. This allowed for target landing TVD to be periodically
updated to ensure accuracy in the landing target.
· Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-16-9-2MBH [SW SW
Section 16, T158N, R95W] is located approximately 200' south and 53' east of the subject
well, making it the closest offset.
· Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-16-9-2TFH [SW SW
Section 16, T158N, R95W] is located approximately 200' south and 88' east of the subject
well.
· Enduro Operating, LLC NDCA 4 [NW NW Section 21, T158N, R95W] is located
approximately 0.28 miles south of the subject well.

Engineering
The ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH was spud on September 23, 2014 by Patterson-UTI 286.
Freshwater drilling fluid was used in the surface hole, diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve,
and saltwater fluid for the lateral.
Vertical BHAs included PDC bits and fixed mud motors with 1.5° bends. One sidetrack was
necessary in the vertical after the BHA was lost down hole at 2,810' MD. The sidetracked vertical
was accomplished in one run. The curve was accomplished in one run with a PDC bit and a fixed
mud motor with 2.38° bend. The curve was landed at 9,956' MD, or 9,586' TVD with a projected
inclination of 89.5°. Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 9,950'
MD.
The lateral was completed in one run, using a PDC bit and a fixed mud motor with a 1.5° bend.
Total depth was reached at 20,054' MD on January 21, 2015 at 09:00 hours CDT, producing an
exposure of 10,098' linear feet of 6" hole through the Middle Bakken. The bottom hole location
lies 10,865.40' south and 147.87' west of surface location, or approximately 222.60' FSL and
928.13' FWL SW SW, Section 28, T158N, R95W.
Geology
Sunburst Consulting, Inc. well-site geologists and rig hands caught lagged samples at 30' intervals
beginning at 7,350' MD, just prior to the Kibbey "Lime". From the False Bakken to curve landing,
samples were caught at 10' intervals, and throughout the lateral they were caught at 50' intervals
to total depth. Rock sample analysis was performed via binocular microscopy. Formation tops
were picked using gamma data throughout the well. Further accuracy was achieved using ROP
and drilling parameters in the vertical.
Hydrocarbon components, methane (C1), ethane (C2), propane(C3) and butane (C4), measured in
parts-per-million (ppm) concentrations, were monitored by well-site geologists using the
Mudlogging Systems, Inc. chromatograph to record possible viable plays throughout the vertical
and curve. Hydrogen sulfide (H2S) was also monitored for safety and geologic records. While
drilling the vertical, gas shows (C1-C2) reached 119 units in the Ratcliffe and 150 units in the
Mission canyon, both against 9.70 ppg diesel-invert mud. A gas show of 643 units was measured
in the Upper Bakken Shale during the curve against 10.50 ppg diesel-invert mud. H2S was detected
during the lateral starting at 9,986' MD, with concentrations ranging from 0 to 100 ppm. Higher
readings are likely a result of the specific H2S sensor reading other component gases. It was
believed that H2S was leaching from nearby wells that had been previously fracked.
Target Zone
A 22' target zone was chosen for the ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH by Liberty Resources
Management Company, LLC geologists that began 10' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale
and ended 25' from the top of the Lower Bakken Shale. The thickness was estimated using control
well logs, structure maps and isopach maps to determine a potential pay zone for the extent of the
lateral, which would, in turn, help to determine the curve landing target. The ND State 158-95-16-
9-2TFH type log was used as a guide for expected gamma ray markers while drilling the lateral
because of its complete record of the Middle Bakken gamma signature (Figure 2). The target
interval is comprised of distinct gamma zones. The top portion of target zone was characterized
by a low gamma bed (approximately 35-55 API). The lower portion of the zone by a warmer
gamma bed (80-115 API).
Figure 2. Type log of Middle Bakken in ND State 158-95-16-9-2TFH (Adam Collar, Lead
Well-site Geologist & Geo-steering Consultant Sunburst Consulting, Inc.).

MSL). The general lithology of the formation consisted of off-white, light gray-tan, fine grained
silty sandstone superimposed by an off-white to light tan lime packstone (Figure 3).
Both
lithologies showed porosity and trace light brown-brown spotty oil staining.

Target Zone Oil and Gas Show
During the course of the lateral, gas readings (C1-C4) ranged from 120 to 1,100 units (Figure 4).
The highest show was 1,054 units at 13,870' MD against 9.90 ppg saltwater mud while drilling
through the base of zone. Higher gas shows were generally associated within the target zone. Trace
to occasional, light brown, spotty oil staining was observed in silty sandstone samples, with
hydrocarbon cut fluorescence primarily being yellow or milky white, slow to fast diffuse and
streaming when observed. No free oil was observed at shakers.

Geo-Steering
The original curve landing target of 9,584' TVD at 90° was calculated by assuming comparable
formation thicknesses with offset wells extending from the Lodgepole to the landing target gamma
signature. The Lodgepole "X" top indicated a lower landing target was necessary and the landing
target was lowered to 9,592' TVD. The False Bakken and Scallion markers came in low to both
the prognosis and the closest offset well, and the target was lowered to 9,594' TVD. No further
adjustments were made to the landing target, and the wellbore was landed at a TVD of 9,586'.
After landing just above the top of the target zone, the wellbore was redirected downwards into
zone.
The original prognosis dip profile was developed using subsurface maps of the region, as well as
full dip profiles from previous Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC wells. These
resources projected that formation dip would drop moderately and steeply southward towards the
center of the basin until formation flattened. Structurally, the formation dropped an estimated 21'
TVD until 13,100' MD where it was discovered that dip, instead of continuing to drop, began to
climb up and flatten over a horst feature (Figure 5). At 16,180' MD, a moderate to steep down dip
recommenced, continuing until total depth. This resulted in an overall apparent dip of 0.30° down.
Figure 5. Cross sectional representation of lateral target zone targets as drilled (Adam Collar, Lead Well-site Geologist
& Geo-steering Consultant Sunburst Consulting, Inc.).
Summary
The Liberty Resources Management Company, LLC ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH was drilled
by Patterson-UTI 286 from spud to total depth in 26 days. The well reached a total depth of 20,054'
MD on January 21, 2015. Geologic data such as hydrocarbon gas measurements and sample
examination obtained while drilling the ND State 158-95-21-28-1MBH indicate a hydrocarbon rich
system in the Middle Bakken target. Shows (C1-C4) within the target zone reached 1,054 units.
Samples from the target zone primarily consisted of off-white, light gray-tan, fine grained silty
sandstone superimposed by an off-white to light tan lime packstone. The well was within the
targeted zone for 71% of the lateral, and lateral operations were successful in exposing 10,098' of
6" wellbore to potentially productive Middle Bakken rock.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
