INTRODUCTION
The Oxy USA, Inc. State Gresz 2-26-35H-143-98
[NENW Sec 26, T143N, R98W] is located
approximately 6 miles northeast of Fairfield, North Dakota. The subject well was drilled in Billings County,
ND by the Nabors X-18 rig in the Hungry Man Butte Field of the Bakken pool. The subject well was
planned with one approximately 9,817' [Intermediate casing to projected total depth (TD)] long southerly
lateral bore with intent to intersect regional hydraulic fracture trends that might enhance reservoir quality
within the Middle Bakken. OXY USA intends to develop oil production in the late Devonian Middle Bakken
target porosity zone with a stratigraphy consisting of siltstone, silty sandstone and limestone with
depositional lithofacies ranging from onshore terrestrial to offshore marine deposits.
ENGINEERING
Surface, Vertical & Curve Operations:
A 13 1/2" surface hole was drilled on October 18, 2014 by Nabors X-18 and surface casing was
set at 2,533'. The vertical hole operations commenced on October 20, 2014 with Baker Hughes providing
equipment and personnel for MWD and directional drilling services.
The first vertical Bottom Hole Assembly (BHA), consisting of MWD tools, 1.50° bend motor and
Tercel MR616MCB PDC bit, drilled to a depth of 2,615'. At this depth, a trip was made due to a MWD
failure. Upon pulling this assembly, a new tool was used with the same bit and motor for the second
vertical BHA. This BHA drilled to 8,617' when the third trip was made due to low ROP. A new PDC bit
(Smith MDSi516) and 1.50° bend motor made up the third vertical BHA. This BHA drilled to the planned
kick off point (KOP) of 10,559'.
Wire lines (quad combo) were run by Baker Hughes after reaching KOP and measured neutron
density, bulk density, acoustic and corrected porosity values in the vertical hole. Wire lines were then
pulled out of the hole and curve operations commenced. The first curve assembly (5th BHA overall)
consisted of a Reed KHI516D-P2 bit with 5x18 jets and an Ultra X-treme 2.40º bend mud motor. This
BHA drilled to 10,848' MD when it was pulled due to low build rates.
The second curve assembly,
consisting of a Smith F30T bit with 3x20 jets and new Ultra X-treme motor drilled to a curve TD of 11,286'.
This placed the well bore approximately 13.75' into a 19' thick (shale to shale) Middle Bakken zone and 5'
into the 6' thick silty sandstone targeted interval. This 12º/100' curve was drilled in a total of 32.6 hours.
Directional tools were then pulled out of the hole and a string of 7" intermediate casing was run,
set, and cemented at a depth of 11,271' MD.
After casing operations, a gyro was run to ensure
separation between off-set wells, thus minimizing the ellipse of uncertainty. Once finished, true lateral
operations commenced.
Diesel invert drilling fluid was used throughout the vertical hole and curve build sections
maintaining a 9.5-10.3 ppg mud weight for stable downhole conditions.
Lateral Operations:
Leam provided equipment and personnel for MWD and directional drilling services for the lateral
section of the well. The directional drillers and Payzone geologists worked closely together throughout the
project to make steering decisions in order to maximize the amount of borehole exposure to the pay zone.
A five bladed 6" bit (Security MMD54), 1.50º Hunting mud motor, and MWD tools comprised the
first lateral BHA (7th BHA overall) and was run in the hole on 4" drill pipe starting at 11,286' MD. This
BHA drilled to a depth of 14,020' MD and was pulled due to motor failure. The second lateral BHA,
consisting of a five bladed 6" bit (Smith MDi513), new Hunting 1.50º bend mud motor, and MWD tools
drilled to 20,996' MD where TD was reached. The lateral was drilled in 6.5 days in total with one trip.
Moreover, spud to TD was a total of 22 days.
The lateral resulted in a TD of 20,996' MD (10,968.23' TVD) at a final azimuth of 186.04º,
resulting in a closure vertical section of 9,926.05'. The BHL is located 9,870.94' South & 1,044.49' West
of surface location or approx. 339.06' FSL & 575.51' FWL, SW SW Section 35, T143N, R98W (Figure 2).
Salt water drilling fluid was used from the intermediate casing to TD with mud weights throughout
the lateral varying from 9.9-10.1 ppg.

GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Methods:
Geologic supervision of the well was provided by Payzone Consulting, Inc. with four well site geologists.
Laura Godowski, and Bob Troutman drilled the vertical and curve. Mike Williamson and Kevin Jacks
drilled the horizontal sections. A wireless Totco Gas WatchTM gas chromatograph was interfaced with a
Rigwatch electronic data recorder system and provided total gas, C1, and C3 information for viewing
around location through the vertical, curve and lateral portions of the well. Lagged samples were caught
in 30' intervals from 8,000' to 11,300' MD and 100' intervals from 11,300' to 20,996' MD (TD). Cuttings
were examined under a binocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are provided in the
lithology document within this evaluation. Two sets of dry cuttings were collected, with one set sent to the
North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library and the second set sent to Ed Schirm, the project geologist
overseeing operations.

the Amsden Formation [Pennsylvanian; Minnelusa Group]. The first top that was used for landing was

LATERAL GEOLOGY
Gamma Signature:
Within the Middle Bakken silty sandstone/siltstone/limestone target interval, gamma counts varied
between 23 and 134 API. The packstone (23-43 API) was present when landing the curve, but pinched
out by 13,100' MD. A hotter, dirtier silty sandstone/siltstone (95-134 API) replaced the limestone until
13,850' MD. At this depth, the transitional limestone was encountered when coming down, thus having
pinched back in. The center of target zone depicted low 60's to mid 70's gamma counts. The upper half
of zone was characterized by a gamma signature ranging between 85 and 110 API, while gamma counts
in the lower half of zone showed less variation, typically ranging between 90 and 100 API. Two hard
streaks were encountered in the targeted interval and were located near the top and middle of zone.
These hard streaks were intermittent, making steering decisions difficult, as it was uncertain whether the
wellbore would deflect when passing through them.
As the lateral commenced after casing operations, there was excellent control for geo-steering based on
the gamma signature. The packstone allowed geologists to infer where the wellbore was within the target
zone and once encountered, directional was advised to slide down. A hard streak at the base of the
packstone would occasionally deflect the wellbore downward if the inclination was not too severe. Below
the packstone, a silty sandstone consisted of low 100's count gamma which trended into low 60's gamma
in the center of zone. A hard steak was present at the center of zone which would occasionally deflect the
wellbore downward when approached from the bottom. However, as previously stated, the hard streak
was intermittent, thus it could not be relied on for this deflection. Below this hard streak, the gamma
signature was much "tighter" (90-100 API) than above, and was a clear indication as to the wellbore's
location in zone. At the base of the target zone, gamma counts approaching 110 API were encountered
and the formation had a tendency to pull the wellbore down. With no exact indication of the thickness to
the Lower Bakken Shale below this marker, directional was advised to slide up to avoid any potential
shale strikes.
The entire well bore was drilled in between the 20' projected shale to shale zone with excellent gamma
markers and trends that represented the stratigraphy within the lateral. These markers and trends aided
geo-steering tremendously. The wellbore was in Middle Bakken throughout the entire lateral and 77.1%
within the 6' targeted interval.
These gamma markers were used for determining formation dip and depth within the target interval and
permitted efficient geosteering. They are plotted on the Wellsite horizontal log (Figure 6) and dip profile
(Figure 7).

Geo-steering:
The target zone within the Middle Bakken was established based on the best drill rates, gamma markers
and gas concentrations. A 6' zone of >6% porosity was expected to be encountered in the targeted
interval. Samples from the target zone varied in porosity, oil staining, color, cementation, and pyrite
content. Stratigraphic locations in the target zone were based on drill rates, gas shows, gamma ray
values and sample observations.
The Middle Bakken was penetrated at 11,153' MD; 11,021' TVD and consisted of siltstone, limestone and
silty sandstone when landing the curve. A 6' target window in an approximately 20' thick Middle Bakken
was selected, leaving 9' of pay zone above the targeted interval, and 5' below.
The wellbore stayed within the 6' targeted interval for approximately 7,496' of 9,725' or 77.1% (rat hole
out of intermediate casing to TD). The well was drilled within the 20' shale to shale target zone for 100%
of the lateral. Using the provided offset log information and the structural data provided from Oxy USA
Inc., Payzone geologists were able to anticipate and interpret the dip rate for the well. The TVD of the
formation decreased 66.50' over 9,696' (first survey out of intermediate casing to TD projection at bit)
resulting in an overall up dip of 0.32º or 90.32°.
A total depth of 20,996' MD was reached on January 15, 2015 at 05:30 MST. Total depth resulted in
100% overall exposure within the Middle Bakken Member of the Bakken Formation and 77.1% within the
defined 6' targeted interval. (Figure 7)

Hydrocarbons:
During the drilling of the lateral, gas readings were recorded, imported/exported, and analyzed via a
Totco gas detection system. The line was hooked up to the gas sniffer and hard-lined into the base of the
possum belly. There was little variation in background gas concentrations, (14-35 units) however, values
did increase steadily throughout the lateral. Peaks were scattered minimally throughout the target zone
and reached as high as 48 units.
The highest gas values were encountered when the well bore
approached the top of target zone below the packstone. These elevated backgrounds gases (30-35 units)
produced intermittent flares that ranged from 1-3' in height. Connection gases reached as high as 83
units with an average of 35 units. Oil show in samples consisted of even to spotty golden brown oil stain.

Summary

The Nabors X-18 drilling rig successfully drilled a two-section horizontal wellbore within the
Middle Bakken Member of the Bakken Formation at the OXY USA, Inc. State Gresz 2-26-35H-
143-98.

A mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.5-10.3 ppg) was used from spud to curve landing
with salt water drilling fluid used from the intermediate casing to 20,996' MD (TD). Salt water
mud (9.9-10.1 ppg) was efficient for maintaining stable down hole conditions and permitted
adequate analysis of gas concentrations in the lateral. Background gas concentrations ranged
from 14-45+ units with connections as high as 83 units. These elevated gas levels increased as
the lateral progressed, and the highest concentrations were recorded at the top of the targeted
interval.

Steering was accomplished by sample evaluation and by following correlative drilling rate and
MWD gamma ray markers; making dip estimations based on intersections of these markers with
the well bore. Projections of dip were successfully used to maintain the well bore within the
Middle Bakken. A net of 7,496' (77.1%) of gross 9,725' of the lateral was drilled in the ideal 6'
thick Middle Bakken target with 100% exposure within the 20' shale to shale Middle Bakken
Member of the Bakken Formation.

Samples from the target consisted of white to off-white, light brown, trace light gray, and light gray
to dark gray siltstone/silty sandstone. Traces of intergranular porosity were seen throughout the
lateral. Samples from the ideal zone contained a spotty to even brown oil stain.

The transitional packstone was present when landing the curve but had pinched out by 13,100'
MD. A hotter, dirtier silty sandstone/siltstone replaced the limestone until 13,850' MD when it was
encountered again. Two hard streaks were present: one under the packstone, and the other near
the center of zone. These hard streaks were intermittent, making steering decisions difficult, as it
was uncertain whether the wellbore would deflect when passing through them.

The State Gresz 2-26-35H-143-98 was a successful drilling effort as measured by gross
exposure to the target. The well will be fitted with a 4 ½" production liner and swell packers in
preparation for a fracture stimulation that will determine the true commercial value of the well.

State Gresz 2-26-35H-143-96 Lithology
Rig crews caught samples in 30' intervals from 8,000' to 11,300' and 100' intervals from
11,300' to 20,996' (TD). Formation tops and lithologic markers have been inserted into
the sample descriptions below for reference.
Sample descriptions began in the
Minnelusa Formation. Samples were examined wet and dry under a binocular
microscope and were checked for hydrocarbon cut fluorescence during the lateral.
Hydrocarbon cut was masked by invert mud through 11,271' (intermediate casing) where
the invert mud was exchanged to a saltwater mud for the duration of the lateral.
Quantifiers in order of increasing abundance are trace, rare, occasional, common and
abundant. Subsea is delineated as the negative numbers within the parentheses located
next to the measured depth or true vertical depth. Sample quality was excellent to good.
Overall, the samples within the Middle Bakken target consisted of silty sandstone with
grains that varied from off-white, white, light to medium brown with traces of tan to light
cream, and light grey in color. Siltstone and limestone were present above the top of the
target window. Variances occurred throughout the lateral and are noted below.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
