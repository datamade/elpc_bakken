Geologic Summary
The Van Hook 25-1319H was drilled as a single vertical and horizontal wellbore, targeting the Middle
Member of the Bakken Formation. The surface location of the well is in Section 13, T152, R91, of
Mountrail County, North Dakota. The vertical whole location was Two-thousand-three-hundred feet
(2300') from the south section line, and four-hundred-fifty feet (450') from the west section line.
The Van Hook 25-1319H spud on Nov 09, 2014, and reached a total measured depth of 18588' (feet) on
November 22, 2014, 13 days after spud.
Fresh water was used to drill from the surface to 2018' measured depth in the vertical hole. Invert mud
was used to drill from 2018' measured depth until we set casing at 9611' MD. Salt water was used to
drill the horizontal hole from 9611' MD to TD at 18588' MD.
Well site geological supervision began Nov 11, 2014. Logging began above the Base of Last Salt
formation Top at a measured depth of 7860'. Brad Hazel and Brandi Ray logged vertical and horizontal
hole. Thirty foot-lagged samples were caught during the vertical and curve sections of the well. One-
hundred (100) foot lagged samples were caught during the lateral section.
The 3rd Shale Marker and False Bakken member of the Lodgepole formation appear to be relatively
reliable markers for isopach thickness to target. The Upper Bakken shale came in at 9474.19' true
vertical depth. The Middle Bakken dolomite came in at 9491.97' true vertical depth.
We encountered the gamma ray marker several times allowing for good control throughout the lateral
section of the well.
A total depth of 18588' (feet) was reached on November 22, 2014 at 19:36 HRS.
