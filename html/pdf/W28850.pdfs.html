INTRODUCTION
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 [Lot 4 Section 4,
T163N, R101W] is located approximately five miles northwest of the town of Fortuna in
Divide County, North Dakota. The subject well was designed to drill north at a heading of
0.35°, from Lot 4 [465' FNL and 1,250' FWL] Section 4, T163N, R101W by the Nabors
#49 drilling rig (Figure 1). The Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 is intended to develop oil
production from the Mississippian-Devonian Middle Member of the Bakken Formation in
Colgan Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 offset control was provided by the American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101 [Lot 4 Section 4, T163N, R101W], the American Eagle
Energy Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-163-101 [Lot 3 Section 4, T163N, R101W], the
American Eagle Energy Corporation, Lynda 15-32-164-101 [SW SE Section 32, T164N,
R101W], and the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Christianson Brothers 15-33-164-
101 [SW SE Section 33, T164N, R101W] (Figure 2). The American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101 is located 100' east of the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101,
and was drilled in September 2014 in the Bakken pool, and is currently awaiting
completion. The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-163-101 is located
535' east of the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101, and was drilled in August of 2012 in the
Bakken pool, and has produced over 91,000 barrels of oil over the life of the well. The
American Eagle Energy Corporation, Lynda 15-32-164-101 is located approximately 0.63
miles northwest of the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101, and was drilled in November of 2013
in the Bakken pool, and has produced over 44,000 barrels of oil over the life of the well.
The American Eagle Energy Corporation, Christianson Brothers 15-33-164-101 is located
approximately 0.40 miles northeast of the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101, and was drilled in
December of 2012 in the Bakken pool, and has produced over 99,000 barrels of oil over
the life of the well. Open-hole logs from these wells were used in forecasting formation
tops and the landing target for the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101. The American Eagle Energy
Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-163-101 was used to provide log character for the Middle
Bakken.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On October 5th, 2014 Nabors #49 spud the Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101. The surface hole
was drilled to 1,622' MD and isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55) to 1,614' MD.
The vertical BHA included an 8 ¾" PDC bit and 1.83° motor which was used to 6,925'
MD, where it was then removed due to low ROP. A new BHA with a new 8 ¾" PDC bit
and 1.50° motor was used to 7,301' MD, which was 25' high to the planned kick-off point
(KOP). At KOP the rig crew picked up the curve BHA which included an 8 ¾" PDC bit
and a 2.12° motor. The curve BHA was drilled to a landing depth of 8,312' MD. Diesel
invert drilling fluid was used through the vertical hole and curve build sections (1,622' to
8,312' MD) maintaining a 9.4-9.7 ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions. Millennium
Directional Service provided directional drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD)
services. Surveys from the vertical hole provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore for the
curve build and lateral. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided well site geology consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# P-110, 32# HCL-80 and 32# P-110) was
set and cemented to 8,295' MD. Drilling fluid was changed out to saltwater ranging in
weight between 9.3-9.6 ppg. The lateral BHA included a 6" PDC bit, a 1.5° fixed mud
motor, and MWD survey and gamma tools. Lateral operations began at 700 hrs CDT, on
October 13th, 2014. The lateral BHA was used from 8,312' to 14,426' MD.
The Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 14,426' MD, 7,819.87'
true vertical depth (TVD), at 20:01 CDT October 15th, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom
hole coordinates are 6,800.27' north and 39.49' east of surface location or approximately
1,048.03' FSL and 1,289.49' FWL Lot 3 Section 28, T164N, R101W. Projected to TD,
the wellbore has a closure distance of 6,800.38' at a closure direction of 0.33°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Shelley Lynn 4-
4N-163-101. The geology well-site team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and geo-
steered. Logging samples and gas monitoring started at 5,700' MD. The rig crews caught
drill cuttings in 30' lagged intervals per instruction from well-site geologists. Drill cuttings
were examined under a trinocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are
provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were collected and sent to the North
Dakota Geological Survey. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. TGC (total gas with
chromatograph) gas detector was interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder (EDR).
The Pason system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump rates to the gas detector and
distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total gas was recorded in units
(100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air). In addition to cuttings and gas measurements
geological data such as gamma and ROP was interpreted for the use of geo-steering
throughout the well.
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began in the Piper Formation [Jurassic] above the Spearfish
Formation [Permian-Triassic]. The Spearfish Formation was penetrated at 5,747' TVD
(-3,475'), which is 1' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101.
The Spearfish is composed of red siltstone and silty sandstone in this area. Background gas
(C1) ranged from 6 to 12 units with no gas shows.
The Kibbey Formation of the Big Snowy Group [Mississippian] was penetrated at 5,972'
TVD (-3,700'), which is flat to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-
101. The Kibbey consists of siltstone, silty sandstone and anhydrite. Background gas (C1)
was 9 to 12 units with no shows.
The Charles Formation [Mississippian] was penetrated at 6,163' TVD (-3,891') and is 1'
low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101. The upper Poplar
interval of the Charles consists of salt, anhydrite, dolomitic limestone, argillaceous
limestone and limestone. Salt intervals were marked by an increase in rate of penetration
along with a decrease in weight on bit. Background gas (C1) within the Poplar was 10 to
29 units and gas shows up to 33 units were recorded. The Ratcliffe interval of the lower
Charles Formation consists of limestone, argillaceous limestone, argillaceous dolomite,
and anhydrite. The top of the Ratcliffe was penetrated at 6,550' TVD (-4,278') and is flat
to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101. The Ratcliffe top is within
a dolomite facies below an anhydrite unit. No gas shows were recorded within the Ratcliffe
and background gases were within 13 to 20 units. The Base Last Salt interval was not
present in this area. The Midale interval of the lower Charles Formation consists of
argillaceous limestone and limestone. The top of the Midale was penetrated at 6,718' TVD
(-4,446') and is 1' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101.
Gas shows within the Midale reached as high as 22 units, and background gases were
within 12 to 17 units.
The Mission Canyon Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] consists of argillaceous
limestone and limestone. The Mission Canyon top was penetrated at 6,837' TVD
(-4,565'), and is 2' low to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101.
The top of the Mission Canyon was marked by a transition from argillaceous limestone to
a cream to light gray brown limestone, which can be identified by a decrease in gamma.
Limestone intervals at the base of the Mission Canyon have spotty dark brown to black oil
staining. Gas shows were not present within the Mission Canyon and background gases
(C1) were within 10 to 20 units.
The Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] was penetrated at 7,159'
TVD (-4,887'), and is 4' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-
101. The top of the Lodgepole was marked by a transition from argillaceous limestone to
limestone. Limestone intervals at the top of the Lodgepole have spotty dark brown to black
oil staining and argillaceous limestone intervals at the Lodgepole 1 marker have spotty
medium brown oil staining. Background gas readings (C1-C3) were 10 to 226 units and gas
shows (C1-C4) are as high as 1,460 units, and connection/survey gas measurements reached
2,554 units. Trip gas measurements (C1) reached 28 units.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was penetrated at 7,857' TVD (-5,585'), which
is 10' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101. The top of the
Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample returns of dark brown to black,
carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Gas shows (C1-C2) were as high as 1,221
units with survey gas show of 2,102 units. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to
581 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was penetrated at 7,867' TVD
(-5,595'), and is 10' high to the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Byron 4-4-163-101.
The Middle Bakken top was marked by a large decrease in gamma and rate of penetration
as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. The siltstone was
described as light gray to light gray brown, sub blocky, and calcite cemented. Silty
sandstone was described as light gray brown and cream to light brown in color, very fine
grained and calcite cemented (Figure 3). Also, trace poor intergranular porosity and trace
spotty light brown oil stain were present. Samples were cut with trichloroethylene (Carbo-
Sol) and examined with a fluoroscope. The cut samples showed very weak to moderately
strong pale yellow, slow to fast, diffuse and streaming cut fluorescence.

The greatest hydrocarbon shows (C1-C4) recorded in the Middle Bakken were as high as
257 units at 9,327' MD, and background gas ranged from 6 to 100 units.
Connection/survey gas shows reached 314 units at 9,998' MD.
GEO-STEERING
The target interval within the Middle Bakken was 10' thick, beginning approximately 21'
below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD
gamma ray log of the offset control well, American Eagle Energy Corporation, Elizabeth
3-4N-163-101 (Figure 4). Gamma ray readings within the 10' of the target zone displayed
a gamma signature of 67-80 API. The top of the target was marked by a decrease in gamma
signature to 24-38 API. The contact of the cool gamma at the top of the target was used for
dip calculations, along with hotter gamma at the base of the target. The well plan called for
a landing 32' below the top of the Middle Bakken and landing depths were adjusted as
formation markers were traversed. Calculations from markers within the Lodgepole were
adjusted with the Middle Bakken top and provided a target landing depth of 7,899' TVD
(32' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale). The landing target was adjusted based on
the Upper Bakken Formation, and was moved to 7,897' TVD. The directional drillers were
able to land at 7,896' TVD, which was 1' high to the planned target depth. MWD-gamma
ray correlated adequately with the American Eagle Energy Corporation, Elizabeth 3-4N-
163-101, which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. The steering team
was successful in maintaining 82% of the exposed 6,114' lateral wellbore in target, and
about 90% within 2' of target window. Based on intersections with the cool gamma
marker, an overall apparent dip was estimated at 0.57° up (Figure 5).

SUMMARY
The American Eagle Energy Corporation Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 was spud on
October 5th, 2014 by Nabors #49. It is a one and quarter section Middle Bakken horizontal
well within the Colgan Field of Divide County, North Dakota. 82% of the lateral was
drilled in the ideal 10' primary target interval. A mud program consisting of diesel invert
(9.4-9.7 ppg) during the vertical and curve build sections, and saltwater (9.3-9.6 ppg)
during the lateral was utilized. The target zone was 10' thick silty sandstone interval (67-
80 API). The silty sandstone displayed trace intergranular porosity and trace spotty very
light brown oil staining. Background gas ranged from approximately 6 to 100 units (C1-
C4) within the target interval. The Shelley Lynn 4-4N-163-101 awaits completion to
determine its full production potential.

Rig crews caught lagged samples in 30' intervals under the supervision of Sunburst Consulting, Inc., well-site
geologists. A detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray marker tops
have been inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined wet and dry under a stereo
zoom trinocular microscope. Sample cuts were examined with Carbo-sol (trichloroethylene) under ultraviolet light.
The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface casing to an intermediate casing point of 8,312' MD, and saltwater
brine was used throughout the lateral: from intermediate casing to a total depth of 14,426' MD. Samples were
collected from 5,700' to 14,426' MD (TD). One set of dried samples was boxed and distributed to the state of North
Dakota.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
