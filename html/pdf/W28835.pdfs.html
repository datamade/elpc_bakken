INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HN [NE NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] is
located approximately 10 miles east of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota. The
subject well was designed to drill north at a heading of 1.78°, from [250' FNL and 2,325'
FWL] Section 28 to NW NE Section 16, T163N, R99W by the Nabors #687 drilling rig
(Figure 1). The Waters 3-28HN is intended to develop oil production from the Devonian
Three Forks Formation in the Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Waters 3-28HN offset control was provided by the SM Energy Company Waters 4-28H
[NW NW Section 28, T163N, R99W], the SM Energy Company Hauge 13-21H [SW SW
Section 21, T163N, R99W] and the SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS [NE NW
Section 28, T163N, R99W]. The Waters 4-28H is located approximately 0.22 miles west
of the Waters 3-28HN and was drilled in November of 2011. The Waters 4-28H was
drilled in the Three Forks Formation, and has produced over 136 thousand barrels over
the life of the well. The Hauge 13-21H is located approximately 0.23 miles northwest of
the Waters 3-28HN, and was drilled in October of 2011. The Hauge 13-21H was drilled
in the Three Forks Formation, and has produced approximately 204 thousand barrels over
the life of the well. Waters 3-28HS is located 50' east of the Waters 3-28HN, and was
drilled in September of 2014 in the Three Forks Formation. According to the NDIC the
Waters 3-28HS is currently on confidential status. Open-hole logs from these wells were
used in forecasting formation tops and the landing target for the Waters 3-28HN. The
Waters 3-28HS was used to provide log character for the upper Three Forks.
Figure 2) Map showing the location of the SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HN and
the three wells used for offset control (NDIC Oil & Gas Division, Arc IMS online).
ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On September 29, 2014, the Waters 3-28HN was re-entered at 1,476' measured depth
(MD) by Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-
55). The well was drilled to 7,659' MD, where the rig crew tripped out for a curve
assembly which included an 8 ¾" Hughes PDC bit in front of a 2.25° Sperry mud motor.
The new BHA drilled 111' to KOP at 7,770' MD and a curve was then drilled to 8,528'
MD and landed at 8,210' TVD on October 5th, 2014. Diesel invert drilling fluid was used
through the vertical hole and curve build sections (1,476' to 8,528' MD) maintaining a
9.60-9.85 ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions. Sperry-Halliburton provided night
tour directional drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from
the vertical hole provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and
lateral drilling. Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided wellsite geology consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,508' MD. Drilling fluid was changed over to saltwater ranging from 9.65
to 9.95 ppg. The lateral BHA included a Varel 6" PDC bit, a Hunting 1.5° fixed mud
motor, and MWD survey and gamma tools. Lateral operations began at 2100 hrs CDT,
on October 6th, 2014. Despite striking the Lower Bakken Shale on two separate
occasions (the first at 13,542' MD and the second at 17,022' MD) resulting in two
sidetracks, the original BHA was used through almost the entire lateral from 8,525' to
18,338' MD. The two sidetrack points were kicked off at 13,200' and 16,620' MD. Loss
of communication with the MWD tool caused a trip at 18,338' MD and a second identical
BHA which included the original bit, was used to drill the final 220' to 18,558' MD.
The Waters 3-28HN was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 18,558' MD, 8,080.61' true
TVD, on October 14, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole coordinates are 10,604.2''
north and 300.31' east of surface location or approximately 215.14' FNL and 2,625.31'
FWL NE NW Section 16, T163N, R99W. Projected to TD, the wellbore has a closure
distance of 10,609.07' at a closure direction of 1.62°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Waters 4-28HN.
The geology wellsite team logged drill cuttings and provided geo-steering consult.
Sample logging and gas monitoring began at 7,770' MD. The wellsite geologists caught
drill cuttings at 30' time lagged intervals through the curve, and 100' intervals through
the lateral. Drill cuttings were examined under a binocular microscope, and complete
lithologic descriptions are provided in the appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were
collected and shipped to SM Energy Company and the North Dakota Geological Survey.
A Pason digital gas detector was interfaced with PASON, an electronic data recorder
(EDR). The PASON system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump rates to the gas
detector and distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total gas was
recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began at 7,770' MD within the Lodgepole Formation
[Mississippian, Madison Group]. The Lodgepole was drilled at 7,701' TVD (-5,453')
which is 1' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The Lodgepole top was indicated by an increase
in rate of penetration and an increase in gamma API. Limestone and argillaceous
limestone compose the Lodgepole Formation, and background gas readings within this
interval were between 70 to 110 units, with no significant gas shows.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was contacted at 8,100' TVD (-5,852') which
is 3' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by
sample returns of dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale.
Gas shows were as high as 453 units and there were no connection gas shows within in
this interval. Additionally, gamma counts were recorded to 650 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was drilled starting at 8,113'
TVD (-5,865') and is 3' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The Middle Bakken top was marked
by an abrupt decrease in both gamma measurements and rate of penetration as the
wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty sandstone. This silty sandstone was
logged as very light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite cemented and containing trace
amounts of disseminated pyrite. Additionally, trace poor intergranular porosity and
trace spotty light brown oil stain were present. Gas shows within this interval reached
231 units and background gas was within 105 to 140 units. There were no significant
connection gas shows within this interval.
The Lower Bakken Shale [Devonian] was penetrated at 8,172' TVD (-5,924') which is
6' high to the Waters 3-28HS. It was very similar to the Upper Bakken Shale Member
except for being slightly lighter in color. The Lower Shale Member also has a sharp
increase in gamma, up to 887 API. Recorded gas shows in the Lower Bakken Shale
reached 834 units, and significant connection gas shows reached 418 units.
The Three Forks Target Formation [Devonian] was drilled into at 8,201' TVD
(-5,953') and is 6' high to the Waters 3-28HS. The upper part of the Three Forks is a
heavily dolomite cemented sandstone with interbedded shale (Figure 3). Silty sandstone
was described as cream to peach to off white in color. It is fine to very fine grained and
well to moderately sorted. Within this interval disseminated pyrite is less common.
Spotty very light brown oil stain is more prevalent in this facies. Sandstone samples were
cut with trichloroethylene (Carbo-Sol) and examined with a fluoroscope. The cut samples
showed slow, blooming, pale yellow cut florescence.

The target interval within the Three Forks was 8' thick, beginning approximately 4'
below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD

landing 10' below the top of the Three Forks Formation. Landing depths were adjusted

addition to the Bakken member tops to determine a landing depth of 8,210' TVD.

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HN was re-entered on September 29, 2014 by
Nabors #687. It is a two-section Three Forks horizontal well within the Ambrose Field in
Divide County, North Dakota. 61% of the lateral was drilled in the ideal 8' primary target
interval and 100% within 2' of the target subsequent to sidetracks. A mud program
consisting of diesel invert (9.60 to 9.85 ppg) during the vertical and curve build sections,
and saltwater (9.65 to 9.95 ppg) during the lateral was employed. The target zone was an
8' thick interval that lies approximately 4' below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale
Member. Sandstone of the target zone displayed poor intergranular porosity and trace
spotty very light brown oil staining. Background gas within the sandstone ranged from
approximately 55 to 420 units. The Waters 3-28HN awaits completion to determine its
full production potential.

Sunburst geologists caught lagged samples in 30' intervals through curve and 100' intervals through the
lateral. A detailed list of sampling intervals is included in the Well Data Summary page. Gamma ray
marker tops have been inserted in the sample descriptions below for reference. Samples were examined wet
and dry under a stereo zoom trinocular microscope. Sample cuts were examined with Carbo-sol
(trichloroethylene) under UV light. The drilling fluid was oil based invert from surface casing to an
intermediate casing point of 8,528' MD, and saltwater brine was used throughout the lateral: from
intermediate casing to a total depth of 18,558' MD. Samples were collected from 7,770' to 18,558' MD.
Two sets of dried samples were boxed and distributed: one to SM Energy Company and one to the state of
North Dakota.

Drilling pit
NDAC 43-02-03-19.4 states that "a pit may be utilized to bury drill cuttings and solids generated during well
drilling and completion operations, providing the pit can be constructed, used and reclaimed in a manner that will
prevent pollution of the land surface and freshwaters. Reserve and circulation of mud system through earthen pits
are prohibited. All pits shall be inspected by an authorized representative of the director prior to lining and use.
Drill cuttings and solids must be stabilized in a manner approved by the director prior to placement in a cuttings
pit."

Survey Requirements for Horizontal, Horizontal Re-entry, and Directional Wells
NDAC Section 43-02-03-25 (Deviation Tests and Directional Surveys) states in part (that) the survey
contractor shall file a certified copy of all surveys with the director free of charge within thirty days of completion.
Surveys must be submitted as one electronic copy, or in a form approved by the director. However, the director
may require the directional survey to be filed immediately after completion if the survey is needed to conduct the
operation of the director's office in a timely manner. Certified surveys must be submitted via email in one adobe
document, with a certification cover page to certsurvey@nd.gov.
Survey points shall be of such frequency to accurately determine the entire location of the well bore.
Specifically, the Horizontal and Directional well survey frequency is 100 feet in the vertical, 30 feet in the curve
(or when sliding) and 90 feet in the lateral.

Surface casing cement

hours, and tail cement utilized on production casing must have a minimum compressive strength of 500 psi
before drilling the plug or initiating tests.
