GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Uhlman 1-7H as a single lateral targeting the Upper
Devonian-Lower Mississippian Middle Bakken formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota.
The surface hole location is in the Southwest one-quarter of the Southeast one-quarter of Section
7, Township 152 North, Range 99 West (345' FSL & 1640' FEL). The well then traverses North
to a final bottom hole location 210.5' from the North section line and 2003.9' from the East
section line of Section 6, Township 152 North, Range 99 West (210.5'FNL & 2003.9'FEL).
Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on September 10, 2014.
Nine and five eights inch diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2073' prior to the
arrival of H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8700' MD on
September 13, 2014. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and
chromatography data were monitored and recorded using a Continental Laboratories total gas
detector and chromatograph Varian AGD 13. Geosteering and borehole guidance were
accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data, lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR
Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with directional drilling survey data. Phoenix
provided the Directional Drilling (DD) and Measurement While Drilling (MWD) Services.
The build section began on the evening of September 15, 2014, at 10460'MD. Data from the
Jamestown Federal 3/14H1, whose surface hole location is situated Southeast of the Uhlman 1-
7H surface hole location, were used as an offset. The base of the last Charles Salt and upper
Lodgepole markers and formation thicknesses suggested the landing TVD for the build section
should remain the prognosed 11040'TVD (15' in to the Middle Bakken). This landing target held
until lower Lodgepole markers indicated the landing should be lowered to 11048'TVD. On
September 16, 2014, H&P 320 tripped for a motor failure after stator rubber detached from the
motor, and was observed coming over the shakers. Drilling resumed the evening of September
16, 2014, and the Middle Bakken was intercepted at 11021.7' TVD. The curve landing target was
adjusted accordingly to 11037' TVD, and the bit was landed at 11039.8' TVD, 17' into the
Middle Bakken formation. The 7" diameter intermediate casing was installed and cemented in
place at 11386' (11386' MD & 11039.9' TVD) on September 19, 2014. The well path then
proceeded along a general 0-degree North azimuth.
On September 23, 2014, H&P 320 tripped for an MWD tool failure. Drilling resumed early on
the morning of September 24, 2014.
Overall dip was projected to be ~89.8 degrees, based on offset wells in the area, including the
Jamestown Federal 2, 3, 6, and 7 (south-bearing wells east of Uhlman 1-7H), and the Kuhn 1, 2,
and 3 (north-bearing wells west of Uhlman 1-7H). After drilling out of 7" casing, formation was
flatter than the structure map projected, holding close to 90 degrees. Structure followed a general
~90 degree dip out of the intermediate casing shoe until ~14500'MD, where it rolled up and
followed an ~ 90.4 degree dip until 15500'MD. Formation then briefly flattened out and turned
over at ~16300'MD to follow a ~89.4 degree dip until ~18000'MD, from which point formation
leveled off and remained fairly flat until the well's total depth (21823'MD, 11040.9'TVD).
Overall dip was 89.95 degrees.

GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS (CONT.)
Samples throughout the course of the lateral remained consistent, showing a light to medium
grey to grey brown, microcrystalline, firm, argillaceous, calcareous to dolomitic Siltstone with
poor intercrystalline porosity. No notable oil shows were seen until lube sweeps were being
pumped consistently every hour, contaminating the integrity of the cuts. Background gas
averaged around 400 units throughout the lateral portion of the Uhlman 1-7H, with few
prominent formation gasses (see total gas data).
A total depth of 21823' was reached at 22:32 CST on September 25, 2014. The bit was landed in
the upper 1' of the target zone, and was in zone 100% of the lateral portion of the well. NorAm
Wellsite Services was released following the bottoms up after running liner.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
