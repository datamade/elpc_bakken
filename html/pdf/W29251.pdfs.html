Vertical & Curve Well Evaluation
1.0) Well Introduction
1.1.1) Well Overview
Zavanna, LLC Sigurd1 5-8 7H [Lot 1 Section 5, T153N, R99W] is located approximately 12
miles southeast of Williston in Williams County, ND. The Sigurd 5-8 7H is a horizontal Middle
Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled towards the south.
Wellsite geologists began logging during vertical hole drilling, just above the Base Last Salt
marker in order to gather data to ensure a curve landing within the desired target. The curve was
kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Middle Bakken. Seven-inch intermediate casing
was then set to isolate the target reservoir, after which directional drilling and geo-steering
techniques will be used to maintain maximum exposure to the target zone.
2.0) Drilling operations
2.1.1) Drilling Plan
The kick off point was planned at 10,806' MD, and called for a 12° per 100' build throughout
the curve. The geologic prognosis estimated the ideal target zone to be 31' below the top of the
Upper Bakken Shale. The single lateral will commence after intermediate casing is set, and the
three other wells to the west are drilled to completion.
2.2.1) Engineering
The surface hole was spud on November 13, 2014, and drilled to 2,480' MD, with surface casing
set to 2,474' MD. Ensign #162 commenced drilling the vertical hole on January 6, 2015, and
drilled to 10,750' MD before tripping out of the hole to pick up the curve assembly. The curve
assembly, comprised of a 2.38° mud motor, drilled out to the kick-off point of 10,800'MD, and
completed the curve in 11.1 drilling hours at 11,611' MD.

The closest offset well, Bruce 5-8 1H, is located approximately 0.5 mile to the west, and was
used to provide the geologic prognosis tops, with the exception of the Lodgepole A-G, False
Bakken and Scallion tops. These tops were calculated by assuming comparable formation
thicknesses (isopach) between the subject well and all three offset wells from the Lodgepole A
marker to the top of the Scallion marker. Both the Niels 32-29 1H and the Bills 32-29 4TFH
offset wells provided gamma data consistent with the gamma data of the Bruce 5-8 1H, making
the determination of formation tops for the subject well relatively easy.
3.2.1) Curve Landing
The geologic prognosis estimated the landing target to be located at 11,283' TVD, which
represents the top of the ideal target zone. The Base Last Salt through the Lodgepole markers
averaged ~4'TVD high to prognosis, and ~2' TVD high to the Bruce 5-8 1H. Due to these
markers trending high there was an adjustment of the landing target to 11,280' TVD. However,
as drilling progressed, the later Lodgepole markers and False Bakken trended low causing a
second landing adjustment to 11,282' TVD.
The Middle Bakken marker came in at 11,274' TVD, and the curve was subsequently landed ~3'
TVD above the ideal target zone at 11,281' TVD (Figure 3). The Zavanna operations geologist
defined the ideal target zone as a 4' thick zone approximately 12' TVD below the base of the
Upper Bakken Shale (Figure 2). Although the landing was high and the formation is expected the
have a structural down dip, entry into the ideal target zone should be easily attained as the
projection to bit inclination was 88.14°.

4.0) Geologic Evaluation
4.1.1) Stratigraphy & Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation, in order to log the
Base Last Salt for target landing purposes, and continued through to the Devonian-Mississippian
Bakken Formation. The two upper members of the Bakken Formation will be discussed in detail
here, complete sample descriptions are in the addendum of this report.
The Upper Bakken Member is known as organic-rich brownish black to black shales. This
member is described as firm pyritic, carbonaceous, petroliferous with earthy, blocky texture. The
upper member is believed to have been deposited in a chemically reducing and anoxic deep
marine environment, and is one the source rocks for the targeted reservoir. The Upper Bakken
Shale was drilled at 11,428' MD (11,254' TVD), and was picked based on the high gamma
signature observed as well as confirmed with sample analysis.
The Middle Bakken Member has varying lithofacies, and has locally been logged to include the
following: 1) off white to medium gray siltstone located just below the Upper Bakken Member
2) off white to cream lime packstone located just above the ideal target zone 3) off white to
cream sucrosic dolomite 4) off white to tanish gray silty sandstone. It is worth noting the three
offset wells are lacking the low gamma signature that indicates the presence of the lime
packstone. This is of importance because the ideal target zone is typically defined as beginning at
the base of the lime packstone, and extending ~4' TVD below this lithofacies. The ideal target is
expected to include the sucrosic dolomite, and the silty sandstone lithofacies. The Middle
Bakken Member was observed at 11,528' MD (11,274' TVD) due to the significant decrease in
the gamma signature.
4.2.1) Gas and Oil Shows Within the Vertical and Curve Drilling
The vertical hole of the Sigurd 5-8 7H yielded background gas of 24 units, and had gas shows
ranging from 15-84 units. The largest gas show of the vertical hole was observed at 10,787' MD
within the Lodgepole Formation, reading 84 units. It is worth noting a higher background gas of
41 units was observed while drilling the Lodgepole. This was significantly higher than the
overall vertical hole background gas of 24 units. This is probably due to the presence of fracture
porosity.
The curve build yielded a background gas of 232 units, with gas shows ranging form 59-2,129
units. While drilling the False Bakken through Upper Bakken interval background gas averaged
925 units, and several significant fracture gas shows were observed (Figure 3). They ranged from
1,172-2,129 units, and were the largest gas shows while drilling the vertical and curve segments
of the well.

geologist)
5.0) Summary
The curve of the Zavanna, LLC Sigurd 5-8 7H was landed at 11,611' MD (11,281' TVD), approximately
3' TVD above the ideal target zone within the Middle Bakken. The proximity of the offset wells, as
well as consistency of gamma data of all three offset wells provided excellent control for
determining the location of the ideal target zone.
A structural down dip yielding an approximate 20' drop in TVD over the course of the lateral is
expected. Although the curve landing was above ideal target zone, the projected low inclination
at the bit (88.14°) coupled with the absence of the lime packstone just above target zone, should
allow for a relatively easy entry into the ideal target zone when drilling resumes.

1.0) Well Overview
Zavanna, LLC Sigurd 5-8 7H [Lot 1 Section 5, T153N, R99W] is located approximately 14
miles southeast of Williston in Williams County, ND. The Sigurd 5-8 7H is a horizontal Middle
Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled towards the south.
2.0) Wellsite Geology observations and Geo-steering
2.1.1) Lithology
The Devonian-Mississippian Bakken Formation is comprised of three members. The upper and
lower Bakken members are organic-rich brownish black to black shales. These members are
described as firm pyritic, carbonaceous, petroliferous with earthy, blocky texture. Both the upper
and lower Bakken members are believed to have been deposited in a chemically reducing and
anoxic deep marine environment, and are the source rock for the petroleum system. The Middle
Bakken Member has varying lithofacies, and this well consisted of slightly different lithology
than other wells locally mapped. The lithology observed within the ideal target zone for the
Sigurd 5-8 7H consisted of four lithofacies in the following stratigraphic order: 1) off white to
light gray silty sandstone with trace to rare light brown spotty oil staining 2) transparent to tan
colored sucrosic dolomite with common light to medium brown spotty oil staining 3) off white
lime packstone located in the center of the ideal target zone 4) cream to tanish gray silty
sandstone.
2.2.1) Gas Monitoring
There was an obvious correlation of gas trends throughout the drilling of the Sigurd 5-8 7H. As
more formation was exposed to the wellbore there was a pronounced increase in both
background gas levels and shows. The highest interval of background gas was observed during
the middle portion of the lateral, while located just adjacent or within the ideal target zone. This
background gas averaged 1,000 units, with gas shows ranging from approximately 1,100-1,777
units. The remainder of the lateral was run through the gas buster with an average background
gas of 820 units, with gas shows ranging from approximately 806-4,074 units. The largest gas
reading was observed while rotating out below the ideal target zone. The gas show read at 4,074
units at 20,856'MD, and is interpreted as a fracture gas show (Figure 1).

2.3.1) Geo-steering by Gamma Signature
The Middle Bakken of Sigurd 5-8 3H is estimated to be 41' in thickness. The target zone was defined by
Sunburst well-site geologists and the Zavanna prospect geologist as a 4'-thick, and is noted below in
figure 2. The target interval was based primarily on a slightly higher porosity zone and the ease of geo-
steering.
Figure 2. Offset gamma signatures from the Sigurd 5-8 5TFH for the Sigurd 5-8 7H (Jenn Torres, Sunburst well site geologist)
As drilling progressed it became apparent the Middle Bakken contained interbedded silty
sandstone, dolomite and lime packstone both within and below the ideal target zone. It is also
important to note two observations made during the course of the well. First, the warmer gamma
peak bounding the bottom of the ideal target zone pinched out by ~14,000' MD. Second, the
cooler gamma packstone not observed in landing was present by ~16,250' MD. These factors
presented a unique challenge for geo-steering the well. It was determined the drilling was ideal
near the top to ~2' TVD above the ideal target zone; therefore, it was agreed upon to drill in this
ideal drilling zone and use the warmer top of zone gamma signature to continually confirm
stratigraphic location for the duration of the lateral drilling operations.

Offset wells indicated that the Middle Bakken structure should drop ~20' from curve landing to
17,000' then come back up 30'. However, gamma signatures and sample analysis suggest that
the formation had an initial structural drop of 22' TVD over the first 4,464' MD, yielding a down

being within the ideal target zone. The well reached a TD of 20,800' on March 30, 2015.
2.3.2) Geo-steering by Sample Observation
As mentioned earlier in this report, this Middle Bakken reservoir had some unique lithology
variations in comparison to local offset wells. Alternating interbedded silty sandstone, sucrosic
dolomite, and lime packstone were observed within the ideal target zone as well as below the
ideal target zone. The most helpful of characteristic of sample analysis observed within the
Middle Bakken was the higher level of oil staining (occasional to common) of the dolomite when
drilling within the ideal target zone, along with the increased presence of disseminated and
nodular pyrite (Picture 2-4). The other helpful characteristic observed was the presence of an
intermittent off white to medium gray siltstone stringer within the silty sandstone located below
the ideal target zone (Picture 5).

3.0) Summary
The Zavanna, LLC Sigurd 5-8 7H was spud on November 13, 2014, re-entered on January 3,
2015 and again on March 26, 2015, reaching TD in 20 total drilling days on March 30, 2015.
Samples from the Middle Bakken target indicated interbedded silty sandstone, sucrosic dolomite
and lime packstone. The oil staining was described as spotty trace to common light to medium
brown, and in some cases contained visible intercrystalline and intergranular porosity. MWD
gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were successfully utilized to expose 55% of the
lateral borehole to ideal target zone. The Sigurd 5-8 7H awaits completion operations to
maximize its production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
