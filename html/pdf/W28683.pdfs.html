INTRODUCTION
Leonard 2-12H is a North lateral, Middle Bakken well, located in Sec. 13, T159N,
R96W, Williams County, North Dakota. The objective was to steer the well within the 15'
target zone located 9.5' to 24.5' below the Top of the Middle Bakken formation.
Directional and MWD services were performed by Leam Drilling Systems, LLC. Solids
Control services were performed by Halliburton, Baroid. Mud logging and Geo-steering
was performed by Weatherford SLS.

SERVICES
Geological consulting and mud logging started on 10/24/2014. Services provided
included: morning reports, sample examination, vertical, curve and horizontal logs, geo-
steering, and a final end of well report.
Samples were collected every 30'MD in the vertical hole and curve section, and every
100'MD while drilling the lateral. Descriptions included; mineralogy, color, firmness,
argillaceous content, structure, texture, allochems, porosity, oil stain, and hydrocarbon
fluorescence. Carbonate identification was determined with 10% dilute HCL- and Alizarin
Red. Hydrocarbon fluorescence was determined using a fluoroscope with a UV lamp.
Gas was logged using a Weatherford DAQ gas/chromatograph. The gas detection
system uses a hotwire to measure total gas and the chromatograph separates and
measures gases C1, C2, C3, and C4. Gas was recorded in units where 1 unit equals
100 ppm methane equivalent. Gas detection systems were checked daily using test
gas of a known unit value.
Lag time was approximated from a calculation of annular velocity and was based on:
pump output, hole diameter, collar diameter, and drill pipe diameter. Connection gases
were monitored to confirm lag time calculations and thereby adjusted lag time when
needed.

Throughout the first half of this well, ~11200'MD through ~16750'MD samples that were
collected were slightly contaminated with lube. We were able to see individual grains
and make an educated guess as to their true color. After 16750'MD the samples were
highly contaminated with lube sweep and their color was dark gray from the lube.
The target zone portion of the formation consisted of the following:
"Upper Middle Bakken", 9.5' to 17' below the Top of the Middle Bakken, Gamma
readings averaged 113 API's, minimum 92 API to 144 API maximum.
Dolomite - medium- light gray, trace brown, cream, microcrystalline Mudstone, slightly
firm, sub blocky to subrounded, silty to sandy, scattered carbonaceous material, trace
Pyrite, even bright yellow/green fluorescence, instant bright milky blue/white streaming
cut. Sample contaminated with lube.
"Lower Middle Bakken", 17' to 24.5' below the Top of the Middle Bakken, Gamma
readings averaged 148 API's, minimum of 126 API to 165 API maximum
Dolomite: medium Grey - light Grey - moderate Brown, sub-blocky to sub-round,
microcrystalline framework, free Pyrite, tight porosity, even light yellow mineral
fluorescence, flash.to streaming cut
Above target zone, ~0' to 9.5' below the Top of the Middle Bakken, from 18560'MD to
18845'MD (total of 285'MD). Gamma readings averaged 113 API's, minimum of 57 API
to 157 API maximum.
Dolomite - dark brown/gray, microcrystalline Mudstone, flakey, subrounded, moderately
argillaceous, very calcareous, silty, scattered carbonaceous material, trace Pyrite, even
dull yellow/green fluorescence, Quick bright milky blue/white streaming cut. Sample
contaminated with lube.
Below target zone, 24.5' to ~50' below the Top of the Middle Bakken, from 19751'MD to
19834'MD (total of 83'MD). Gamma readings were un-available due to tool failure.
Samples highly contaminated with lube.
Dolomite - dark gray/brown, microcrystalline Mudstone, flakey, subrounded, moderately
argillaceous, very calcareous, silty, scattered carbonaceous material, trace Pyrite, even
dull yellow/green fluorescence, fast bright milky blue/white streaming cut. Sample
contaminated with lube.

SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION
The majority of the well was drilled within the 15 foot target zone. 96.42% was
drilled within the target zone and 3.58% was drilled out of target, 2.77% above the
target zone and 0.81% below the target zone. The average formation angle was
90.67°.
Background gas averaged ~111 units with a minimum of 1 unit and a maximum
of 934 units. The gas buster was turned on for the trip at 18600 but was turned off
before drilling commenced.
The even to patchy bright yellow fluorescence and immediate very bright milky
bluish/white instant diffused and fast streaming cut should be an indicator to a good to
moderate producing well.
Salt Water mud weights started out in the 9.10ppg to 10.15ppg range for the
lateral which averaged 9.725PPG.
Spud to total depth was drilled in 89 days, on a three pad location, with five trips.
We tripped once at KOP for the curve assembly. After landing the curve we TOH for
lateral assembly. At 18600'MD we tripped for slow ROP. The well is now awaiting
completion.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
