Figure 1. The H&P Rig 456 drilling the XTO Energy, Inc. Ranger 21X-18C well in November 2014.
INTRODUCTION
The XTO Energy, Inc. Ranger 21X-18C (NE NW Section 18, T151N, R99W) is located about
13 miles northwest of the town of Watford City in Mckenzie County, North Dakota. The Ranger
21X-18C is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin (Tobacco Garden Field),
consisting of one lateral drilled toward the south-southeast. The curve will be kicked off in the
Lodgepole and landed in the Middle Bakken. Directional drilling technologies and geo-steering
techniques will be used to land in the Middle Bakken and maintain exposure to the ideal target
rock.
OFFSET WELLS & CURVE LANDING TARGET
Offset well data was used for depth correlation when landing the curve and can be found in the
`Control Data' section attached to this report. Offset well data was imperative in landing the
curve successfully within the Middle Bakken. The formation thicknesses from the offset well
data were compared to the thicknesses obtained, through the gamma markers, during drilling
operations in order to successfully land the curve. The true vertical depth (TVD) of the landing
target was adjusted accordingly throughout the drilling of the curve in order to ensure an
accurate landing.
GEOLOGY
The Mission Canyon Formation was drilled at 9,779' MD/ 9,776' TVD, 5' low to prognosis
and 7' low to the Ranger 21X-18G. The Mission Canyon Formation consisted of a lime
mudstone that was described as light-medium gray, occasional off white, occasional tan-light
brown, microcrystalline, friable-firm, earthy, trace disseminated pyrite, trace calcite, trace spotty
dead oil stain. Also present was an argillaceous lime mudstone that was described as medium
gray, occasional light gray, microcrystalline, friable-firm, dense, earthy texture, with trace

GEO-STEERING
Baker Hughes, Inteq provided personnel and equipment for measurement-while-drilling (MWD)
services and the directional drilling. The Baker Hughes directional drillers, MWD, XTO
geologist and Sunburst Consulting personnel worked together during the duration of the well to
evaluate well data and make geo-steering decisions to maximize the amount of borehole in the
target zone.
The Ranger 21X-18C well had a 20' target zone, which was chosen by XTO Energy geologist.
The top of the target zone was 10' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale and the bottom of
the target was 10' above the top of the Lower Bakken Shale (Figure 3). The thickness of this
target zone was estimated using data collected from the offset wells. The closest offset well, the
Ranger 21X-18G, provided the gamma markers needed in order to determine the stratigraphic
position of the well bore while drilling in the target zone. The top of the target zone contained
relatively high gamma peaks up to 110 API and readings as low as 44 API. The middle of the
target zone contained more consistent gamma ranging from mid 90s to the mid 80s API, with
low stringers of calcite mixed in throughout. The bottom of the target zone contained ratty
gamma between 100 and 50 API (Figure 3).
Figure 3. Gamma type log of the Middle Bakken from the Ranger 21X-18G well; green is Upper and Lower

The entire well bore was drilled in the target zone for the entire length of the lateral. The well
bore started out near the bottom of the target zone and moved up to the top of target around
15,000' MD, where a hard streak was encountered which knocked the bit down twice sending it
back to the middle of the target zone. The rest of the well was drilled consistently in the middle
of the target until the end of the well, where the bottom of the target zone was seen briefly before
rising up to the middle again at TD (Figure 4).
Structurally, the Middle Bakken appeared to drop approximately 23' during the course of the
lateral resulting in an overall apparent down dip of 0.13°. The well reached a TD of 21,220' on
November 27, 2014.

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Ranger 21X-18C was spud on July 30, 2014 and re-entered for vertical
and curve drilling on November 13, 2014. The well reached TD in 19 total drilling days on
November 27, 2014. Samples caught from the Middle Bakken target zone consisted primarily of
a silty sandstone, with fair intergranular porosity and occasional light brown spotty oil staining.
The well site team worked together to maintain the well bore in the desired target interval for
100% of the lateral, opening 9,836' of potentially productive reservoir rock. The XTO Energy,
Inc. Ranger 21X-18C well awaits completion operations to determine its production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
