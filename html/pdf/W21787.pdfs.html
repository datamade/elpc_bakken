Penetration of the lateral section started on December 13, 2014, at 10:20 hrs. at a depth of
10,630 MD . We started drilling with a 6 Ulterra U613S 6X18, Geo Force XL motor
1.50 deg. Bend. We then proceeded to drill our well deviation turn before we could
proceed to the north.
The well plan was to drill out of casing and verify landing inclination from the vertical
build then build at a dip of 90.25 deg. in the Middle Bakken C reservoir. Our first point
of reference in regard to the target line and the wellbore taking us down to the top of the
Middle Bakken D zone established a new dip of 90.55 deg. At this point we made the
decision to come up through the Middle Bakken to the top of the C and the base of the
B formations establishing a new dip of 90.62 deg. We drilled ahead at this dip for a
total of 3,000 of formation. We drilled back down through the middle Bakken member
and drilled along the D top for 2000 of lateral before coming back up at approx.
16,400 MD and crossed the top of the C porosity zone and the base of the B again
and established a new dip of 90.00 deg..We continued to drill ahead in section following
above and below the target line at a dip of approximately 90.0 to 89.9basedonthedata
gathered from the TOBACCO and PUMPKIN well borehole eventually ending the lateral
at 19,700 MD at a dip of 89.8 deg.. Based on the structure map and the control wells,
we expected the formation to be less steep at the beginning of the hole and then take a
downward turn the last half mile of the second section. The downward turn eventually
presented itself almost right at the expected position of 16,500 MD in the borehole. The
hotter and cooler gamma were seen perfectly the same each time we crossed the markers
needed to verify dip rates respectively. We drilled the entire Middle Bakken zone from
just above the basal B where we saw the very cooler Gamma (25cts) to the top of the
D zone twice during the course of the lateral, never drilling out of zone at any time
during the course of the lateral. The entire cross section of this lateral relies on good
gamma information acquired. There were three trips during the course of the lateral. The
trips were due to a loss of signal from the MWD tool. This was caused by the tool being
packed off with fine sediment being present in the mud. There is much speculation as to
the content of the material and how to avoid this situation in future drilling operations.
Nobody had the clear answer to this problem at the time the well was finished. The lateral
ended at 19,700 MD, 10,238.73 TVD, on December 24, 2014 at 12:20 hrs.

Filter Socks and Other Filter Media

Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
