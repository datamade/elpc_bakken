Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

OneOk Rockies Midstream, LLC (ORM) will gather and process North Plains Energy's (NPE) gas production
from its operated wells in the Smoky Butte Field. Gas will be gathered into ORM's Crosby (Divide) and
Alamo (Divide/Williams) systems and processed at ORM's Stateline Plant in Williams County, S21T155N
103W. ORM installed LP gas gathering lines in the eastern standup units of Smoky Butte Field in 2013 to
gather and process gas from Baytexoperated wells. No other midstream gas gathering lines exist in the
immediate area at present other than ORM's. ROW acquisition is in progress for buildout of the Smoky
Butte Field gas gathering system. ORM projects tieing in NPE's Smoky Butte producing wells starting in
November and December 2014 with anticipated completion in first quarter 2015. The State 1601002116
1H pad location is located along OneOk's gathering line ROW and will commence gas sales upon its
completion in 2015.

ORM's nearest gas plant and compressor station design capacities and current throughput volumes as of
October 2014 are described below. ORM's Crosby Station will handle 20 MMcfd and have spare capacity by
the end of 4Q14. Their Crosby and Alamo gathering systems are projected to handle 100% of NPE's gas
production with new initial rates projected at 375 Mcfd peak production per well.

NPE is scheduled to drill the State 16010021161H location in 2Q2015. NPE completed the Amundson
well (File No. 23707) in Sept2014 and is currently drilling the 2well Stevens pad (File Nos. 28519, 28520)
with scheduled completion in Nov/Dec2014. Well flowback commences during lateral cleanout, and the
wells will be put on ESP after cleanout operations. Anticipated average IP90 rates are 350 BOPD and 300
Mcfd. NPE utilizes wellhead gas for infield electric power generation for artificial lift and SWD pump
operations in Smoky Butte Field.

NPE currently utilizes 20% of its produced gas in North Dakota for electric power generation for its 7
producing wells and 3 SWD wells. NPE presently produces ~800 Mcfd in total and flares approximately 80%
of its gas production on a statewide basis. All NPE wells are 1st wells in spacing units. NPE's flare % ratio is
projected to decline to <50% by 12/15/2014 and <20% by 4/1/2015 with ORM's gas gathering installation in
the T160/161R100 area completed through the 1st quarter of 2015. NPE currently operates 4 wells in the
Smoky Butte Field with a flare rate of 80%. All of NPE's Smoky Butte wells are scheduled to be tied into
ORM's sales line within the next 23 months, with the two new 4Q14 wells to be tied in upon
commencement of production. No other alternatives to gas flaring (than current power generation use of
gas) are considered economic at this time, given the forecast of gas sales commencing in 23 months.
