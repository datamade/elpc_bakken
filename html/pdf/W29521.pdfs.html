INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum, North America, Twobins 5501 41-20 7B [SW SW Section 20, T155N,
R101W] is located approximately 5 miles northwest of Williston in Williams County, North
Dakota. The Twobins 5501 41-20 7B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston
Basin consisting of one 9,360' lateral drilled toward the north. Geologically, the Williston
Basin is an intracratonic, structural, and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the
Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Bakken Formation (Late Devonian to early
Mississippian) consists of four members: the basal pronghorn member (formerly recognized
as the Sanish Siltstone) which underlies lower and upper organic-rich shale members deposited
above and below the clastic and coastal-carbonate middle member which consists of calcareous
and dolomitic sandstone and siltstone shore facies.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the `Structure'
section appended to this report. Offset well control was essential in curve operations to
successfully land within the Middle Bakken. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray
signatures in these wells were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations in
order to successfully land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was updated
twice in the Bakken formation to ensure accurate landing of the curve.
ENGINEERING
The Twobins 5501 41-20 7B was spud on December 15, 2014 by Nabors 169. Drilling fluid
was diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. Due to an
issue with the MWD mule shoe sleeve rotating, the assembly was not steered in the directional
driller's desired direction. The decision was made at 10,448' to cement back the existing
wellbore and kick off the curve a second time. After all of these processes the new curve was
kicked off of the existing wellbore at a depth of 10,190' on December 26, 2014'. The new
curve was landed in an ideal position at 11,116' MD (TVD of 10,769' with an inclination of
90.2°). Following the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 11,105' MD. To
start the lateral, a 6 Baker Hughes PDC bit, Ryan Directional Services MWD tool, and a 1.5°
adjustable Baker Hughes mud motor were used. The complete BHA accomplishments are
tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached total depth (TD) at
20,465' MD at 17:30 hrs CST on January 05, 2015, generating an exposure of 9,360' linear
feet of 6 hole through the Middle Bakken target interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies
9,866.02' North, 486.59' East of surface location or approximately 317.48' FNL, 1,234.59'
FWL, NW NW Sec. 17, T155N, R101W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 8,300' MD within the Kibbey. The main zones of interest drilled at
the subject well include the Ratcliffe, Mission Canyon, Lodgepole and Bakken Formations.
The Ratcliffe interval of the Charles Formation (Mississippian - Madison Group) was drilled
at 9,293' TVD (-6,998'), 4' high to the Twobins 5501 41-20 6T. Samples observed during this
interval of drilling consisted of argillaceous limestone and occasionally clean limestone,
varying in texture from mudstone to wackestone. The argillaceous limestone was a medium to
dark gray, light gray and occasionally light brown. The argillaceous limestone was
microcrystalline and firm to friable. Occasionally, throughout this interval was a clean
limestone that was cream, tan, and light gray in color. The cleaner limestone was more
commonly friable than firm and had an earthy texture. While drilling this portion a medium
brown dead oil staining was from time to time observed in samples.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississippian -Madison Group) was drilled at 9,486' TVD
(-7,191'), 4' high to the Twobins 5501 41-20 6T. Sunburst geologists observed cuttings that
consisted of a clean limestone that was cream, dark to light gray, and light brown in color. This
limestone was friable to firm with an earthy texture and exhibited trace to rare fossil fragments.
Samples were described as microcrystalline, rarely displaying trace light-medium brown dead
oil staining.
The Lodgepole Formation (Mississippian -Madison Group) was drilled at a TVD of 10,064',
(-7,769'), 5' high to the Twobins 5501 41-20 6T. The Lodgepole consisted of a medium gray
argillaceous limestone. Cuttings were comprised of both mudstone and wackestone that had
an earthy texture. There were no promising oil staining or visible porosity throughout the
Lodgepole Formation.
While drilling the second curve the Upper Bakken Shale was drilled at 10,925' MD, 10,744'
TVD (-8,449'), 2' high to the Twobins 5501 41-20 6T. The Upper Bakken Shale was composed
of black to very dark brown, carbonaceous and petroliferous shale, characterized by very high
gamma ray (>500api).
Target Middle Bakken Member
This member was drilled at 10,975' MD, 10,759' TVD (-8,464'), 2' high to the Twobins 5501
41-20 6T. The Middle Bakken displayed gamma counts ranging from 60-150 API. The middle
member was composed of a light brown gray to off white, silty sandstone. The silty sandstone
was fine grained, moderately cemented with calcite and on rare occasion dolomite. The Middle
Bakken displayed a trace spotty and occasionally even, light brown oil stain and trace
intergranular porosity. Samples throughout this interval contained rare amounts of
disseminated and nodular pyrite.
Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm)
concentrations. Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water
mud was used to drill the lateral. During the vertical, unusually high gas shows ranged from
50-215 units above a background of ~40-210 units against a 9.5-10.5 lb./gal diesel-invert mud
weight. Methane (C1) was observed throughout the vertical. Methane, Ethane, and Propane
(C1-C3) were logged from the Charles Base Last Salt to the Lodgepole. While drilling in the
Ratcliff interval shows ranged from 50-152 units. Occasionally, samples throughout the
Ratcliffe displayed a medium to light brown dead oil staining. The most promising
hydrocarbon shows of the vertical were observed in the middle portion of the Charles. While
drilling this portion hydrocarbon shows regularly exceeded 200 units. While drilling the
Lodgepole shows ranged from 20-57 units. Through the Scallion gas shows did not pick up as
anticipated due to gas line problems. While drilling the Upper Bakken Shale a gas shows of
271 units and a connection gas of 196 units were observed. The Upper member displayed a
dark brown to black even oil stain.
Over the course of the lateral hydrocarbon shows were fairly promising. Background gasses
peaked at 5,000 units, but were more often near 1,500 units while drilling the lateral portion.
Connection and survey gasses regularly exceeded 3000 units regularly regardless of the
wellbores position in the target interval. The consistent oil staining was described as being
light brown and spotty, however hydrocarbon shows decreased while drilling in the lower
target zone. Fluorescent cuts were poor to moderate throughout the lateral. While the most
promising fluorescent cuts that were observed being described slow, white to cream streaming,
becoming diffuse.
GEOSTEERING
The potential pay zone of the Twobins 5501 41-20 7B was identified by evaluating the electric
log of the Sonat Exploration Co., Glen 1-30, and the lateral logs of the Oasis Petroleum,
Twobins 5501 42-20H, and Oasis Petroleum, O M Erickson 5501 42-19H. The target was
determined to be 10' thick, and the top of the target started 8' below the Upper Bakken Shale
(Figure 2). The plan for steering the lateral portion was to stay as near as possible to the cool
gamma signature near the top of zone. While steering off of this interface more favorable
hydrocarbon shows would be observed in comparison to the bottom of the zone.
Figure 2. Top, Sonat Exploration Co., Glen 1-30 neutron density e-log. Ideal target zone highlighted in
green, while the Upper and Lower shales are highlighted in red.
Structure maps provided by Oasis Petroleum geologists indicated that over the course of the
structure would trend upward at a steady inclination of 0.5° up. Since the wellbore was near
the top of the target interval upon reaching casing point the plan for drilling forward was to
slowly descend down to the bottom of the target interval. In doing this, gamma markers
throughout the target interval would be cataloged. The bottom of the zone was identified by a
gamma count from 125 to 130. After seeing this, the plan for drilling forward was to drill
within the cool gamma near the top of the target zone. The gamma markers were not as
consistent as we would of liked throughout the course of the lateral, but the geologist were able
to make minor dip calculations through the course of the lateral to help steer the lateral to total
depth. This steering strategy worked remarkably well throughout the lateral.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Twobins 5501 41-20 7B was drilled by Nabors
169 from spud to TD in 22 days. The Twobins 5501 41-20 7B reached a total depth of 20,465'
MD on January 5, 2014. Geologic data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample
examination obtained during the Twobins 5501 41-20 7B indicate an encouraging hydrocarbon
system in the Middle Bakken. Positive connection and survey shows were recorded throughout
the lateral. Samples from the target zone consisted of cream-light gray, silty sandstone and a
trace, light brown oil stain which yielded a faint, white, poor, diffuse to streaming cut
fluorescence. Lateral operations were successful in exposing 9,360' of potentially productive
Middle Bakken Sand target zone rock to the wellbore. The well was within the targeted zone
for 84% of the lateral.
