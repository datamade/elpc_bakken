Well Evaluation
Johnson Federal 11X-5E [Lot 4 Section 5, T149N, R97W] is located approximately 8 miles
southeast of Watford City in McKenzie County, ND. The Johnson Federal 11X-5E is a
horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin consisting of one lateral drilled toward
the south. The curve will be kicked off in the Lodgepole and landed in the Three Forks.
Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will then be used to maintain maximum
exposure to the target zone.

XTO Energy, Inc., Johnson 24X-31F [SE SW Sec. 31, T150N, R97W] located 0.9 miles
west-northwest of the subject well.

XTO Energy, Inc., Lucy 14X-32F [SW SW Sec. 32, T150N R97W], located 0.29 miles

While drilling the curve the landing target shifted down 2' to 11,041' TVD based on the depth
the Upper Bakken Shale and Middle Bakken markers were logged. Landing was projected at
90.2° although surveys showed the landing inclination at 90.0° when drilling out of the casing
shoe.

GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation in order to log the
Base Last Salt for curve landing purposes.
The Three Forks Formation was logged at 11,020' TVD (-8,913' MSL). It consists primarily of
dolomite with laminated intervals of pale green shale and contains trace to rare amounts of
spotty, medium brown oil stain. The Three Forks was the target reservoir for the lateral portion of
the well.
Gas Monitoring
During the vertical a gas show of 465u was recorded at 10,050' MD which was the highest gas
observed while drilling the vertical portion of the well. The highest gas value recorded for the
well was a survey gas of 2,107u observed while drilling the Lower Bakken Shale in the curve.
Background gas fluctuated between 100-500u throughout the lateral. The entire ideal target zone
was observed to contain relatively low to moderate gas concentrations and consistent oil
staining. The highest gas concentrations were seen in the upper half of our target zone,
specifically ~2-4' from the top of our target zone (Figure 2).

Geo-steering
The ideal target zone was defined as a 22' interval, the top of which was initially set at 11,028'
TVD, 55' below the top of the Lower Bakken Shale (Figure 1). The superposition of a warm
gamma marker reading 115-125 API, 3-4' above a cool gamma marker reading 75-85 API in the
lower half of the target zone proved to be the most useful for geo-steering. Offset wells indicated
that the Three Forks would be initially up-dipping approximately 0.20° from landing to ~
16,000' MD then turn down dip approximately 0.30° to TD. The bottom-hole location is
estimated to be 9,597.68' south & 170.12' east of surface location or approx. 250.50' FSL &
1,340.12' FWL, SE SW Sec. 8, T149N, R97W. Geo-steering techniques exposed 92% of uncased
borehole to the 22' Three Forks ideal target and the well reached a TD of 20,401' on January 8,

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Johnson Federal 11X-5E was spud on October 18 and officially reached
TD in 19 total drilling days on January 8, 2015. Samples from the Three Forks dolomite and
shale target contained oil staining that was described as trace to rare with visible intercrystalline
porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and ROP were successfully utilized to
expose 92% of the lateral borehole to the ideal Three Forks target. The Johnson Federal 11X-5E
awaits completion operations to maximize its production potential.

binocular microscope. Sample collection began in the Charles Formation at 30' intervals to the Three

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
