Well Synopsis
Rennerfeldt 4-30H Cyclone 2
Middle Bakken Dolomite
Well Plan:
The Rennerfeldt 4-30H was spud on October 15th, 2014 with a surface location of 355 FSL
and 2200 FWL in the SESW corner of Section 19 - Township 155 North and Range 98 West in
Williams Co., North Dakota. This well was operated by Continental Resources Incorporated
with the objective target of the late Mississippian - early Devonian Middle Bakken Dolomite.
The well plan showed a build section with a kickoff point of 10631 MD in the Mississippian
Lodgepole with a 13°/100 build rate to the landing point of 11321 MD; 11072 TVD,
approximately 20 below the Middle Bakken Dolomite Formation. The plan was to drill lateral
for an estimated 10325 to the hardline in the SESW of section 31 - Township 155 North and
Range 98 West in Williams Co., North Dakota, following the estimated dip of 89.72°. The
projected well path is to the South with a proposed azimuth of 181.66°.
The offsets provided were the Mildred 4-19H, Mildred 5-19H1 & Rennerfeldt 3-30H1 all
operated by Continental Resources Inc.
Gas logged in the vertical and lateral sections were monitored using Mud Logging Systems
Mlogger & Mcontrol. (Primary Logger: ML-369 Backup Logger ML-098)
M-Logger CC & TC filaments calibrated with 1% and 100% test gas Chromatograph
calibrated with 1% test gas (gas units).
Build Section:
The build section was kicked off @ 10638 MD on October 19th, 2014 at 22:30 hours. In the
Lodgepole formation. The up hole markers logged were the Base of the Last Salt (9501 TVD (-
7204 ss)), Mission Canyon (9714 TVD (-7417 ss)), and the Lodgepole (10335 TVD (-8038
ss)). The build section started on October 20th, 2014 at 12:24 hours. w/ a (BHA) bottom hole
assembly consisting of a 6.75 Mpact XXL Performance motor with a 2.5° bend and a Security 8
3/4 MMD55M PDC Bit. This assembly was able to generate sufficient enough build rates to
land in the approximate target zone. The down hole markers below kick off point consisted of
the False Bakken (11023 TVD (-8726 ss)), Upper Bakken Shale (11035 TVD (-8738 ss)) &
Middle Bakken Member (11053 TVD (-8756 ss)). The up hole & down hole markers logged
came in 2-4 lower versus the prognosis, the Landing target was only adjusted 2 lower from
11071 to 11073 TVD. The landing for 7 intermediate casing was completed on October 21st,
2014 at 05:13 hours, 21.6 into the Middle Bakken Formation, with a landing at 11328 MD;
11074.6 TVD. The 7 casing was set @ 11307 MD; 11073.91 TVD, with a location of: 48 FNL
& 2032 FWL NENW Section 30: T155N-R98W Williams Co., ND. The build section consisted
of one assembly, 690 total footage drilled w/ 558 sliding (9.83 hrs) 132 rotating (2.58 hrs).
(See Rennerfeldt 4-30H Build and TVD log for more information)
Gas observed in the build section showed gas ranged from 22-360 units through the
Lodgepole formation, averaging 76 units with no background oil shows and a slight milky
blue/white invert cut. The Upper Bakken Shale showed moderate amounts of gas resulting in
120-2677 units, averaging 499 units. The Middle Bakken Member ranged from 42-1116 units,
averaging 455 units with connection gasses up to 554units. Circulating trip gas after the
landing resulted in 262-3650 units. (See Rennerfeldt 4-30H Build log for more detailed gas
data)
Lateral Section:
Penetration of the lateral section started on October 23rd, 2014 at 04:45 hrs, with a depth of
11328 MD, drilling with a 6 Smith MDi613 Bit and Mpact XXL Performance 4.75, 5/6 lobe 8.3
stage 1.5° bend .039cl 1.04rpg mud motor w/ fit test completed @ 11348 MD: 05:07 hrs. The
lateral section was drilled from 11328 MD to 21600 MD for a lateral length of 10272 , with a
bottom hole location: 231 FSL & 1901 FWL SESW Sec 31, T155N-R98W, Williams Co., ND
completed on October 29th, 2014 09:33 hours. The lateral section was drilled entirely in the
Middle Bakken Dolomite with one complete trip @ 17733 MD; 11079.8 TVD, for a BHA.
Samples collected were primarily light-medium gray, dark gray, medium-dark brown, mottled
med gray/brown and off-white, slight traces of light tan-tan/brown; slight-moderately firm,
brittle in part, moderately calcareous, microsucrosic, moderately argillaceous, very sandy,
moderately silty, limy, flaky in part, sub-angular-platy, fine-very fine grained, fair
intercrystalline-pin point porosity & had slight traces of Pyrite in the samples. Samples taken
had poor to fair moderately bright yellow/green fluorescence with poor-fair immediate
bright even cloudy blue/white streaming/blooming diffused cut. The upper zone above the
upper target displayed more oil shows, cut & drilling gas versus the middle and lower target
zones.
Gas averaged 1267 units and ranged from 389 units to 6280 units of gas. Connection gasses
ranged from 334 units to 6844 units with 6727 units of trip gas. Gas monitored in the lateral
section was primarily on the low side while drilling in the lower target zone, but greatly
increased in the upper target zone and increased to about a 3000 unit average for the last
500-600 of the lateral section. (For more detailed sample descriptions & gas data see
Rennerfeldt 4-30H Horizontal Log & Data Plots)
The gamma markers observed in the lateral section consisted of several distinct cool & hot
zone that were present in the middle & upper target zones. The cooler counts ranged from
56-83 API and plotted like erratic scribble and were more prominent in the upper target zone,
but the hotter counts also in the upper target zone ranged from 90-118 API, as seen on the
cross section one prominent spot where the gamma heated up to 118 API. Utilizing these
points and the cooler trending gamma above the target zone & marker 1 line, it was possible
to formulate an accurate formation dip rate and keep the wellbore mostly under control from
erratic build rates. The Formation remained flat until about 16200 MD (89.99° Dip) where it
descended about 51 to total well depth (89.47° Dip). The lateral section consisted of 10272
(111.66 Hrs) total footage drilled w/ 552 sliding (25.0 hrs) 9720 rotating (86.66 hrs). (For
more detailed gamma signatures and structure please see Rennerfeldt 4-30H cross section)

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
