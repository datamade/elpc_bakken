Lithology of Bakken
The Hunt Exploration team identifies two main sequences within the Middle Bakken in the Sioux Trail
160-101-9-4H-1. The first is identified as the "Upper Middle Bakken" (top of Middle Bakken to the
gamma ray marker). The second sequence is identified as the "Lower Middle Bakken" (base of
gamma marker to top Lower Bakken Shale).
The "Upper Middle Bakken" lithology was represented in this well as
DOL/SLTST(100%) light tan/gray, light-dark gray, medium tan/gray, firm, sub-platy, earthy,
cryptocrystalline-microcrystalline Wackestone, composed of well sorted fine-very fine sub-
angular Quartzose grains, slightly sandy, moderately-very argillaceous, scattered
carbonaceous material, uneven weak yellow/white fluorescence, slow fair blue/yellow diffuse
cloudy cut.
The Lower Middle Bakken lithology was represented in this well as
DOL/SLTST(100%) light-medium tan/gray, light-dark gray, firm, sub-platy, earthy,
cryptocrystalline-microcrystalline Wackestone, composed of well sorted fine-very fine sub-
angular Quartzose grains, moderately argillaceous, scattered carbonaceous material, uneven
weak yellow/white fluorescence, very slow to fast weak to good blue/yellow diffuse/streaming
cloudy cut. Trace nodular & disseminated Pyrite.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container

placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
