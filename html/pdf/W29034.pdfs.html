Continental Resources, Inc. Stangeland ­Hayes ecopad is a four well location approximately
6 miles southeast of Williston, North Dakota and within the boundaries of the Crazy Man
Creek Field. The Stangeland ­Hayes ecopad location and drilling procedures were
designed to allow efficient extraction of hydrocarbon resources from the Bakken and Three
Forks Formations lying beneath sections 6,7 and 18 of T153N, R99 and section 31 of T154N
R99W. Hayes 2-6H was the fifth lateral drilled in the unit and targeted the Middle Member of
the Bakken Formation. Surface location coordinates are 300' FSL & 990' FWL, S6-T153N
R99W, . The Stangeland ­Hayes drilling unit consists the aforementioned four sections. The
Stangeland 2-7H and 3-7H1 are southerly transects and the Hayes 2-6H and 3-6H1 are
northerly transects. All holes are planned as ~10,000' laterals. The completed wells will
utilize hydraulic fracturing technology to enhance their petroleum yields from the Middle
member of the Bakken and First Bench of the Three Forks Formations. Drilling the Hayes 2-
6H began October 28, 2014 and the hole reached total depth of MD 21027' TVD 11107' on
December 10, 2014.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Cyclone Rig 33 spudded the Hayes 2-6H on October 28, 2014. Prior to the Cyclone rig
moving onto the hole, a spudding rig drilled a 12-1/4" hole with fresh water to 2368'. The
surface hole was isolated with 9 5/8" 36# J55 STC casing and cemented to surface. A 8 ¾"
PDC bit, 1.83° mud motor and diesel invert mud were used to drill the vertical hole to kick off
point at MD 10716' in the Lodgepole Formation. Directional tools employing a 2.38° degree
bent assembly were used to land the build curve at MD 11438' with a projected TVD of
11161' and projected inclination of 93.2°. 7", 32# P110 LTC intermediate casing was then
run to MD 11424', TVD 11162' and cemented. The Intermediate casing shoe location is 748'
FSL & 811' FWL, S6-T153N-R99W, Williams County, North Dakota
Lateral
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
with 9.6# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. Three 6" PDC bits
and 1.5° steerable motors were used to drill the lateral section. A single 9603' long southerly
transect was drilled in the Middle Member of the Bakken formation on a 358.2° azimuth to
MD 21027', TVD 11107', terminating 241' south of the north line of Section 31. Initial
structural interpretation assumed relatively consistent formation dip of 90.4° to approximately
MD 14500 and then flattening to approximately 90° from MD 14500 to MD 18000' and finally
dipping upward at approximately 90.5° from MD 18000 to TD. Over the course of the lateral,
the formation dipped 50' upward, resulting in an average formation dip of 90.29°.

Geologic evaluation of Hayes 2-6H began at MD 9500' in the Charles Formation. The
Base of the Last Charles Salt was encountered at MD 9581' TVD 9588' (SS TVD -7255'), 4'

wackestone to mudstone with interbeds of massive anhydrite. No significant hydrocarbon
shows were observed while drilling through the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at MD 9792' TVD 9792', (SS
TVD -7466), ), 4' structurally low to Hayes 3-6H1. Samples from the Rival interval (9792' to
9818') consisted of light gray to medium graybrown microcrystalline wackestone to
packstone, tight with a trace of dark brown to black hydrocarbon residue, dull yellow
fluorescence and faint milky pale yellow cut. Total gas increased from 200 units to 250 units
and then declined rapidly to 200 units while the Rival was penetrated. With the lack of
significant shows or visible porosity and the rapid decline of the gas liberated while drilling
through and beyond the Rival, it is unlikely that commercial volumes of hydrocarbons are
present at this location. No other significant porous zones or gas shows were observed while
drilling through the Mission Canyon Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole Formation was encountered at MD 10380' TVD 10380' (SS TVD
-8054), 1' structurally low to Hayes 3-6H1. The curve was "kicked­off" at MD 10716'. Four
gamma markers were selected and used to calculate the curve landing point while drilling
through the Lodgepole.

MUDSTONE with minor carbonaceous shale present at the False Bakken Marker. A
significant gas show from a background of 360 units to 2850 units was observed while

11222'. Samples from the Scallion consisted Mudstone light to medium gray firm to subhard
micro to cryptocrystalline tight even dull yellow fluorescence no shows. It is likely that
fractures emanating vertically from the Bakken Shale are the source of this hydrocarbon
show.

The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was encountered at MD 11263', TVD 11139'

The Target Zone within the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation consists of a 11' thick
zone beginning 13' beneath the base of the Lower Bakken Shale. Lithology within the target
was consistent over the course of the lateral, when quality samples were available. Target
lithology was observed to be Siltaceous to slightly very fine arenaceous slightly dolomitic
mudstone, tight, predominantly non fluorescent. Cuttings from samples associated with

subtly lower gamma count, slightly more arenaceous marker beds displayed even dull yellow

LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS AND GEOSTEERING
Three trips were required during the drilling of the lateral. The first trip at MD 14597 for a
failed motor. The second trip ocurred at MD 15504 for a failed motor noted by a loss of 400#
of standpipe pressure. Upon completion of the trip out for a third failed motor at MD 20094, it
was noted that the motor lower bearing package, the bit box and the bit had twisted off. The
hole was successfully sidetracked at 19120 and drilled to TD without further problems. Upon
exiting the 7" casing shoe at MD 11424', steering procedures were executed to evaluate all of
the discrete lithologic horizons in the target zone of the Middle Member of the Bakken
Formation. No single horizon within the target appeared to display a favorable advantage with
respect to oil shows or rate of penetration. Steering response was adequate to achieve
targets for the entire length of the lateral. Structural interpretation indicates a nearly flat
formation dip of 89.9° from the 7" casing shoe to approximately MD 12000'. From MD 12000'
to approximately MD 16000' the formation rose upward approximately 23 vertical feet
resulting in a average local dip of 90.3°. From MD 16000' to MD 18500' formation dip
remained very flat with and average local dip of less than 90.1º. From MD 18500' to TD the
formation dipped upward significantly climbing 24', yielding an average local dip of 90.55º
over the final 2500' of lateral. Over the course of the lateral, the formation climbed 50'
upward resulting in an average formation dip of 90.29°. The 6" bore exposed 9603 lineal feet
of hole through the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation. While drilling the lateral, the
BHA was slid 760' (7.4%). The bore remained in the target zone for 9275' (96%) of the
lateral.

CONCLUSIONS
Oil shows observed while drilling Hayes 2-6H were weak with a spotty oil stain and dull
yellow fluorescence present throughout the course of the lateral. Mud gas shows climbed
steadily from 1500 to 2300 units in the early portion of the lateral (to MD 13000'), after which

with a 3 BHA's. Hayes 2-6H will be completed with a multi-stage fracture stimulation after
which its production potential will be revealed.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
