Bakken Shale. The drilling plan included a vertical hole to be drilled to a kick-off point at MD

Vertical and Curve
Nabors Rig 784 spudded Boston 2-25H October 25, 2014. Prior to the Nabors rig moving onto
the hole, a spudder rig drilled a 13 1/2" hole with fresh water to MD 2220'. The surface hole

An 8 ¾" PDC bit and 1.5 degree mud motor was used to drill from surface casing to MD

and 2.38 degree mud motor was deployed to drill to the kick off point at MD 10660' and
continue to drill the curve, planned to land at MD 11376'. However, slow ROP was again

curve. The curve was landed at MD 11451' TVD 11131', four feet below the Target Zone, with

LITHOLOGY AND HYDROCARBON SHOWS
Vertical and Curve
Geologic evaluation began at 9500' in the lower Charles Formation. The Base of the Last
Charles Salt was encountered at TVD 9537' (SS TVD -7141). No significant hydrocarbon
shows were observed while drilling through the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at TVD 9753' (SS TVD -7357).
No hydrocarbon shows were observed while drilling through the Mission Canyon Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole "X" Marker was observed at TVD 10295' (SS TVD -7899). The well
was "kicked­off" at MD 10654'. Four gamma markers were selected and used to calculate the
curve landing point while drilling through the Lodgepole. No significant shows were observed
while drilling through the Lodgepole.
The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11228' TVD 11083' (SS TVD -
8687), 5' structurally high to offset well Boston 3-25H1. Samples contained carbonaceous
shale, black, some very dark brown, blocky, non-calcareous and non-fluorescent with
immediate milky pale yellow slowly streaming cut. Total gas increased rapidly from 200 units
to a maximum of 1801 units while the shale was penetrated.
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was encountered at MD 11284', TVD 11104'
(SS TVD -8708), 4' structurally high to Boston 3-25H1. Samples were varied and observed to
be argillaceous to siltaceous slightly arenaceous dolomitic mudstone, firm, very slightly
sucrosic, microcrystalline to poor intercrystalline porosity, spotty medium to dark brown stain,
spotty dull yellow fluorescence and slow to slowly streaming dull pale milky yellow green cut.
Total gas was fairly stable while drilling through the Middle Bakken and ranged from 200 units
to 400 units with occasional sharp peaks of formation gas of 1000 to 2000 units and
connection gas of 1926 units.

TARGET ZONE IDENTIFICATION IN THE LATERAL
The Primary Target Zone was a 10' thick segment of the Middle Bakken Formation beginning
12' beneath the base of the Upper Bakken Shale. API gamma counts from 75 to 105 reflected
the thinly bedded nature of the horizons in the Target Zone. There was also a high gamma
(120-135 API) marker three feet beneath the top of the Target Zone which greatly enhanced
zone identification. Lithology in the target was observed to be siltaceous calcareous dolomite
with a definite lithological change to arenaceous dolomitic wackestone, some pellatal, at the
upper boundary of the zone. This was accompanied by a sharp drop in gamma count from 80
to 40 when drilling through the top of the Target Zone. Identification of the lower boundary of
the zone was much more subtle. The base of the zone was evidenced by API gamma counts
from 80 to 110. Lithology below zone consisted of dolomitic siltstone to mudstone, with
cuttings being slightly more argillaceous and less calcareous than cuttings from the target
zone.
LATERAL DRILLING OBSERVATIONS
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical curve portions of the hole was replaced 9.8#
to 9.85# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. Three 6" PDC bits, two
1.5 degree steerable motors, one 1.83 degree steerable motor and four MWD tools were used
to drill the lateral section. The first survey upon exiting the casing shoe revealed an inclination
of 86.70 degrees, and with the wellbore having landed below zone, directional slides were
required. These slides were successful and the Target Zone was re-entered at MD 12,169'
resulting in an initial formation dip of 89.81°. Additional formation dips were calculated along
the length of the lateral where consistent gamma signatures were encountered. The structural
interpretation showed three dips ranging from 89.69° to 89.90° to approximately MD 15,000'.
From this point to MD 20,600' the dip was fairly consistent and was calculated at 89.67°. Two
more short footage dips of 89.39° and 89.71° were calculated before TD of MD 21542'. A
formation dip of 90.70° was calculated for the entire lateral. The 6" bore exposed 10,111 linear
feet of hole through the Middle Bakken Formation. Rotational drilling of 9532' (93.4%) and
directional slides of 675' (96.6%) comprised drilling of the lateral. Average rotating ROP was
177'/Hr. and sliding ROP was 37'/Hr. The wellbore remained in the Target Zone approximately
8717' (86%), below the target zone 870' (9%) and above the target zone 524' (5%).

SUMMARY
The Boston 2-25H was drilled in the Middle Bakken Formation and oil shows observed were
fair with relatively strong gas shows. Oil shows in samples were consistent displaying trace to
some medium brown stain and spotty to even dull yellow fluorescence. Subsurface structural
control, utilizing data from Boston 1-25H, cuttings evaluation and MWD gamma was sufficient
to allow drilling the hole with low potential for stratigraphic positional misinterpretation.
Steering to correct for inclination or azimuth was responsive until MD 21050' where dulling of
the bit necessitated longer slides to be executed. Boston 2-25H will be completed with a multi-
stage fracture stimulation after which its production potential will be revealed. Samples and
surveys will be delivered to the North Dakota Industrial Commission.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
