GEOLOGIST'S SUMMARY
INTRODUCTION
Continental Resources, Inc. Stangeland ­Hayes ecopad is a four well location approximately
6 miles southeast of Williston, North Dakota and within the boundaries of the Crazy Man
Creek Field. The Stangeland ­Hayes ecopad location and drilling procedures were
designed to allow efficient extraction of hydrocarbon resources from the Bakken and Three
Forks Formations lying beneath sections 6,7 and 18 of T153N, R99 and section 31 of T154N
R99W. Stangeland 2-7H was the second lateral drilled in the unit and targeted the Middle
Member of the Bakken Formation. Surface location coordinates are 300' FSL & 745' FWL,
S6-T153N R99W, Williams County, North Dakota. The Stangeland ­Hayes drilling unit
consists the aforementioned four sections. The Stangeland 2-7H and 3-7H1 are southerly
transects and the Hayes 2-6H and 3-6H1 are northerly transects. All holes are planned as
~10,000' laterals. The completed wells will utilize hydraulic fracturing technology to enhance
their petroleum yields from the Middle member of the Bakken and First Bench of the Three
Forks Formations. Drilling the Stangeland 2-7H began September 13, 2014 and the hole
reached total depth of MD 21598, TVD 11153' on October 21, 2014.

DRILLING OPERATIONS
Vertical and Curve
Cyclone Rig 33 spudded the Stangeland 2-7H on September 13, 2014. Prior to the Cyclone
rig moving onto the hole, a spudding rig drilled a 12-1/4" hole with fresh water to 2423'. The

point at MD 10695' in the Lodgepole Formation. Directional tools employing a 2.38°degree
bent assembly were used to land the build curve at MD 11416' with a projected TVD of
11158' and inclination of 89.8°. 7", 32# P110 LTC intermediate casing was then run to MD
11401', TVD 11157' and cemented. The Intermediate casing shoe location is 116' FNL 651'
FWL, S7-T153N-R99W, Williams County, North Dakota
Lateral
Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical and curve portions of the hole was replaced
with 9.8# sodium chloride brine for the remainder of drilling operations. Two 6" PDC bits and
1.5° steerable motors were used to drill the lateral section. A single 10197' long southerly
transect was drilled in the Middle Member of the Bakken formation on a 180° azimuth to MD
21598', TVD 11153', terminating 231' north of the unit boundary hard-line near the south end
of Section18. The initial structural interpretation assumed relatively flat formation dip to MD
16500 followed by crossing perpendicular to the axis of a subtle eastward trending local high
from MD 16500 to MD 19500. After crossing the subtle structural high, the formation dip was
anticipated to remain essentially flat to TD. Over the course of the lateral, the formation fell 4'
downward resulting in an average formation dip of 89.8°.

LITHOLOGY AND HYDROCARBON SHOWS
Vertical and Curve
Geologic evaluation of Stangeland 2-7H began at MD 8950' in the Charles Formation. The
Base of the Last Charles Salt was encountered at MD 9577' TVD 9577' (SS TVD -7251), 2'
structurally low to Stangeland 3-7H1 and 25' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H. The Ratcliffe
and Midale intervals are composed of compact wackestone to mudstone with interbeds of
massive anhydrite. No significant hydrocarbon shows were observed while drilling through
the Ratcliffe and Midale intervals.
The top of the Mission Canyon Formation was encountered at MD 9799' TVD 9789', (SS
TVD -7463), 2' structurally high to Stangeland 3-7H1 and 5' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H.
Samples from the Rival interval (9789' to 9810') consisted of light gray to light gray brown
microcrystalline wackestone with poor to fair pinpoint vuggy porosity a trace of dark brown oil
stain, dull yellow fluorescence and faint milky pale yellow cut. Total gas increased from 320
units to 1237 units and then declined rapidly to 320 units while the Rival was penetrated.
The Rival interval is commercially productive from this interval in many vertical "legacy" wells
locally.
It is possible the Rival interval could be commercially productive at, or nearby this
location.
No other significant porous zones or gas shows were observed while drilling
through the Mission Canyon Formation.
The top of the Lodgepole Formation was encountered at MD 10385' TVD 10384.5' (SS
TVD -8059), 2' structurally low to Stangeland 3-7H1 and 4' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H.
The curve was "kicked­off" at MD 10698'. Four gamma markers were selected and used to
calculate the curve landing point while drilling through the Lodgepole. Lodgepole samples
consisted of compact ARGILLACEOUS MUDSTONE with minor carbonaceous shale present
at the False Bakken Marker. A significant gas show from a background of 350 units to 2350
units was observed while drilling through the Scallion Interval From MD 11217' to MD 11229'.
Samples from the Scallion consisted tan to cream and light gray brown siltaceous to slightly
argillaceous microcrystalline wackestone with no visible porosity, even pale yellow
fluorescence and a weak pale yellow milky cut. It is likely that fractures emanating vertically
from the Bakken shale are the source of this hydrocarbon show.
The Bakken Formation Upper Shale was encountered at MD 11231' TVD 11119' (SS TVD
-8793'),
6' structurally high to Stangeland 3-7H1 and 2' structurally high to Hayes 1-6H.
Samples consisted of CARBONACEOUS SHALE, black, firm blocky, non-calcareous, slightly
siltaceous in part, non-fluorescent, weak milky pale yellow cut. Total gas at the top of the
shale was 2300 units and decreased slightly to 2100 units while the shale bed was
penetrated.
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation was encountered at MD 11280', TVD 11138'
(SS TVD -8812'), 5' structurally high to Stangeland 3-7H1 and structurally flat to Hayes 1-6H.
Samples observed while drilling through the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation were
very consistent and consisted of argillaceous to siltaceous and slightly arenaceous slightly
dolomitic mudstone. Weak shows with a trace of dark brown stain pale yellow fluorescence
and immediate pale yellow milky cut were persistent in all samples. No significant high or low
gamma horizons were obvious with gamma counts ranging from 75 to 90 API. Total gas was
static and ranged from 2300 to 2500 units while the Middle Member of the Bakken was
penetrated.
7" intermediate casing was set in the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation
at MD 11401', TVD 11157', (SSTVD -8831)' with a projected inclination of 89.8°.

Oil and gas shows observed in the samples when the well path was in the target zone were
weak, yet consistent over the course of the lateral. Fluorescence was absent in all samples

There were no obvious shows of oil in the drilling mud during the lateral. 1' to 6' gas flares
were first observed at MD 15700' and appear to be most closely related to the sub interval of
the lateral that crossed the low relief structural high between MD 16000' and MD 20100'.
Mud gas was noted to slowly increase as the hole VS increased with peak values peak

Diesel invert mud used while drilling the vertical curve portions of the hole was displaced with

retrieval of a failed MWD tool. The second trip at MD 12851 for a new bit and motor. Upon
exiting the 7" casing shoe at MD 11401', steering procedures were executed to evaluate all of
the discrete lithologic horizons in the target zone of the Middle Member of the Bakken
Formation. No single horizon within the target appeared to display a favorable advantage with
respect to oil shows or rate of penetration. Steering response was adequate to achieve
targets for the entire length of the lateral.

fell 11' vertically resulting in a local dip of 89.8°. TD. Over the course of the lateral, the
formation fell 4' downward resulting in an average formation dip of 89.8°.

CONCLUSIONS
Oil shows observed while drilling Stangeland 2-7H were weak with a spotty oil stain and dull
yellow fluorescence present only in samples that could be directly correlated to the slightly
more arenaceous horizons evidenced by gamma counts less than 70 API. Mud gas shows
climbed steadily as the laterals vertical section increased, indicative of the very low
permeability of the formation. Subsurface structural control, utilizing data from the adjacent
Stangeland 3-7H1 Three Forks lateral was adequate to land the curve and geosteer the hole.
The lateral was drilled with a 2 BHA's. Trip gas of 4172 units after running the 4 ½" liner is a
positive indication of hydrocarbons. Stangeland 2-7H will be completed with a multi-stage
fracture stimulation after which its production potential will be revealed.

Filter Socks and Other Filter Media

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
