Gust 2-11 3TFH
Vertical & Curve Evaluation
Gust 2-11 3TFH [Lot 4 Section 2, T153N, R99W] is located approximately 8 miles southeast
of Williston, in Williams County, North Dakota. The Gust 2-11 3TFH is set to be a horizontal
Three Forks well within the Williston Basin, drilled toward the south. The vertical hole was
drilled to a depth of 10,900' MD and the curve was landed at a depth of 11,685' MD and 11,362'
TVD in the Three Forks first bench, 11' TVD below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale.
Directional drilling and geo-steering techniques will be used to promote maximum exposure to
the Middle Bakken target zone.
Engineering Plan
The curve was planned to kick off at 10,883' MD in the Lodgepole Formation, with ~10° per
100' build until the Three Forks first bench target is landed at ~90° inclination. Engineering
made slight adjustments to land the Three Forks first bench target (10,900' MD KOP). Offset
wells were used to correlate formation tops to assist an accurate target landing depth.
Offset Wells
· Zavanna, LLC, Nelson 3-10 3TFH [Lot 2, Sec. 3, T153N, R99W] is located 0.4 miles
west of the subject well (primary offset-fig 1).
· Zavanna, LLC, Simmental 2-11 4TFH [SW SE Sec. 35, T154N, R99W] is located 0.57
miles east northeast of the subject well (fig. 1).
· Zavanna, LLC. Angus 34-27 4TFH [Lot 4, Sec. 3, T153N, R99W] is located 0.93 miles
west of the subject well (fig. 1).
Figure 1. Map of subject well and correlating offset wells (Source: NDIC website).
Geological Methodology
Sunburst Consulting, Incorporated, provided the geological analysis for the Zavanna, LLC, Gust
2-11 3TFH. Lagged samples at 30' intervals were caught by Sunburst well site geologists
beginning at 9,650' MD, in the Charles Formation. Geological analysis consisted of rock
sampling, gas detection, rate of penetration (ROP) and gamma ray data, which in collaboration,
aided in formation top identification, providing ample information for determining target landing
depth.
Prospective Lithology
The Lodgepole Formation was drilled at 10,518' MD/ 10,518' TVD, 1' high to prognosis and
2' low to the Nelson 3-10 3TFH. The Lodgepole consists primarily of gray to brown argillaceous
mudstone. Trace amounts of disseminated pyrite were observed in samples. Thin layers of clean,
microcrystalline limestone were also observed in the Lodgepole. Gas shows in the Lodgepole
topped 35 units against 10.05 ppg drilling mud.
The Bakken Formation has two members of black, pyritic shale, known as the Upper Bakken
Shale and Lower Bakken Shale. Layered between the Upper Bakken and Lower Bakken Shale is
the Middle Bakken Member. The Gust 2-11 1H and Gust 2-11 2H are Middle Bakken targets,
rich with organics, making it one of the primary oil extraction formations in the Williston Basin.
The Upper Bakken Shale was drilled at 11,350' MD/11,267' TVD, 3' low to prognosis and 3'
high to the Nelson 3-10 3TFH. Gas shows topped 851 units against 9.95 ppg drilling mud. The
Middle Bakken was drilled at 11,386' MD/11,287' TVD, 4' low to prognosis and 1' high to the
Nelson 3-10 3TFH. Gas shows topped 300 units, against 9.95 ppg drilling mud. The Lower
Bakken Shale was drilled at 11,474' MD/11,326' TVD, 3' low to prognosis and even to the
Nelson 3-10 3TFH. Gas shows in the Lower Bakken Shale topped 341 units against 10.55 ppg
drilling mud.
The Three Forks Formation wad drilled at 11,565' MD/11,351' TVD, 6' low to prognosis and
even to the Nelson 3-10 3TFH. The Three Forks consists of cream to light and medium brown
colored dolomite, microsucrosic in texture, with primarily intercrystalline porosity. Trace
amounts of light mint green shale and pyrite were commonly observed. Oil shows consisted of
spotty light-medium brown staining. Gas shows reached upwards of 200 units on multiple
occasions against 10.55 ppg drilling mud.
Curve & Target Zone Landing
The curve was kicked off at 10,900' MD and reached an inclination of 90° at landing. The Three
Forks target was landed at 11,685' MD/ 11,362' TVD, 1' low to prognosis. The wellbore
finished an estimated 1' above the Three Forks first bench target (target zone 6' thick, as shown
in fig. 2) and was determined using the target reference TVD gamma log (fig. 3).

Summary
The Zavanna Gust 2-11 3TFH was spud on September 20, 2014, re-entered for vertical and curve
drilling on December 3, 2014. The Nelson 3-10 3TFH was used at the primary offset log for
target zone landing. Gamma log and formation tops from the Nelson 3-10 3TFH were accurate
and key in predicting a landing target for the Gust 2-11 3TFH.
The Gust 2-11 3TFH lateral is predicted to have an overall down-dip of 18' TVD over the course
of an estimated 9,589' lateral. Sample analysis, MWD gamma ray markers, ROP and formation
tops were successfully analyzed to land the Three Forks target approximately 1' above target top,
in a 6' target window. Moving forward, the acquired curve TVD gamma plot will be used to
assist geosteering in the Zavanna Gust 2-11 3TFH lateral.

Lateral Evaluation
The Gust 2-11 3TFH is a horizontal Three Forks well within the Williston Basin, consisting
of one ~9,575' lateral drilled toward the south. The vertical hole was drilled to a depth of 10,900'
MD and the curve was landed at a depth of 11,685' MD and 11,362' TVD in the Three Forks
first bench, 12' TVD below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale.
GEOLOGY
The Three Forks Formation wad drilled at 11,565' MD/11,351' TVD, 6' low to prognosis and
even to the Nelson 3-10 3TFH. The Three Forks consists of cream to light and medium brown
colored dolomite, microsucrosic in texture, with primarily intercrystalline porosity interbedded
with variable amounts of light mint green shale. Oil shows consisted of spotty to even light-
medium brown staining (Figure 1).
Figure 1: Wet cutting of the Three Forks target zone.
Lateral Gas and Oil Shows
The background gas for the Gust 2-11 3TFH ranged from 30 to 200 units during the course of the
lateral and generally averaged between 70 and 100 units. The highest gas show was 1,603 units
at 18,800' in 9.8 ppg mud weight. A trip gas at 19,330' measured 1,897 total units. Gas was not
circulated through the gas buster. There was a slight upward trend across the lateral for
hydrocarbon shows.
Geo-steering
A 6' target zone was chosen for the Gust 2-11 3TFH by Zavanna geologists. The primary offset
for establishing the target zone was the Zavanna Nelson 3-10 3TFH well which was drilled 0.4
miles west of the Gust location, as well the Zavanna Simmental 2-11 4TFH and the Angus 34-27
4TFH wells. The target zone was bounded by a distinct high gamma marker at the top (~115
API) and another high gamma marker below (~100 API), with a low gamma marker (~45 API)
separating the peaks (Figure 2). Beneath the established target, gamma readings drop down to the
lowest marker (~40 API) in the first bench of the Three Forks. Below that gamma increases to
120+ API which indicates the presence of the "claystone" in the second bench (Figure 2).
Figure 2. Gamma type log of the Three Forks from the curve landing from the Angus 34-27 4TFH well.
The Zavanna Simmental 2-11 4TFH and Gust 2-11 1H wells were utilized as the most reliable
prognosis for the lateral. Based off these wells it was expected that formation would have a
general down dip somewhere between 0.02º-0.12º. A general up dip ranging from 0.08º to 0.84º
was observed during the lateral until ~16,400 MD when gamma markers indicated a down dip
peaking at 0.40º. This trend appeared to have continued until ~19,400'. After 19,400' typical
geosteering techniques were abandoned with the decision to drill out with no gamma and poor
sample quality. Overall, the Three Forks dropped structurally ~18' from curve landing to the end
of the lateral which gave an overall apparent down dip of 0.10° (Figure 3).

Bottom Hole Location
Total depth resulted in a closure azimuth of 177.35° and a closure distance of 10,023.76'. The
bottom-hole location is projected to be 10,013.06' south and 462.91' east of the surface location
or approximately 283.27' FSL and 1,155.91' FWL, SW SW Section 11, T153N, R99W.
SUMMARY
The Zavanna, LLC Gust 2-11 3TFH was spud on September 19, 2014 and re-entered by Nabors
rig #92 on December 3, 2014. The well efficiently reached TD in 17 total drilling days on
December 19, 2014. Samples from the Three Forks target contained oil staining that was
described as trace to occasional with visible intercrystalline porosity. MWD gamma ray markers,
sample analysis, and ROP were successfully utilized to expose 100% of the lateral borehole to
the Three Forks. The Gust 2-11 3TFH awaits completion operations to maximize its production
potential.

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
