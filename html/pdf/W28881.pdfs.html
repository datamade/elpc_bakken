INTRODUCTION
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 4B [SE SW Section 10, T153N,
R93W] is located approximately 11 miles northwest of New Town in Mountrail County, ND. The
Langved 5393 42-10 4B is a horizontal Middle Bakken well within the Williston Basin consisting of one
9,462' lateral drilled toward the north. Geologically, the Williston Basin is an intracratonic, structural,
and sedimentary basin located at the western edge of the Canadian Shield. Within the basin, the Bakken
Formation (Late Devonian to early Mississippian) consists of four members: the basal pronghorn
member (formerly recognized as the Sanish Sand) which underlies lower and upper organic-rich shale
members deposited above and below the clastic and coastal-carbonate middle member which consists of
calcareous sandstone and siltstone shore facies. Advances in drilling and well stimulation have made the
Bakken Formation an extremely viable oil and gas resource.
OFFSET WELLS
Offset well data used for depth correlation during curve operations are found in the `Structure' section
appended to this report. The Langved 5393 42-10 5T, located 33 feet east of the subject well, proved to
be the most reliable and consistent of the primary offsets used for depth correlation of formations above
the Lodgepole and during the curve landing. Offset well control was essential in curve operations, to
successfully land within the Middle Bakken target zone. Formation thicknesses expressed by gamma ray
signatures in these wells were compared to gamma data collected during drilling operations in order to
successfully land the curve. The target landing true vertical depth (TVD) was periodically updated to
ensure accurate landing of the curve.
ENGINEERING
The Langved 5393 42-10 4B was re-entered on November 20, 2014 by Nabors 460. Drilling fluid was
diesel invert mud for the vertical and curve, and salt water mud for the lateral. Two trips were made
during the vertical. The first trip was at 8,197' for a new bit due to a decline in ROP. The bit was
replaced with a Security FX65D PDC bit. The second trip in the vertical was made to pick up the curve
assembly after reaching kick off point at 10,090'. The curve was drilled with an 8 ¾" Security
MMD55M PDC bit utilizing a 2.38° Predator mud motor. The curve required 30 hours and 878' of
drilling and was landed at 10,968' MD (10,573' TVD) with an estimated inclination of 90.2°. Following
the landing, 7" intermediate casing was set and cemented to 10,958' MD.
To start the lateral, a 6 Security SK516M-A1 PDC bit, MWD tool, and a 1.5° Predator mud motor were
used. This assembly drilled 9,452' in 150 hours to a TD of 20,420' MD. The BHA accomplishments are
tabulated in an appendix (BHA Record) to this report. The well reached total depth (TD) at 20,420' MD
at 0728 hrs CDT on December 5, 2014, generating an exposure of 9,462 linear feet of 6 hole through
the Middle Bakken target interval. The bottom hole location (BHL) lies 10,045.93' north & 278.52' west
of surface location or approximately 272.07' FNL and 1,564.48' FWL, Lot 3 Sec. 3, T153N, R93W.
GEOLOGY
Lithology
Sample logging began at 7,970' MD where the observed lithology consists of primarily siltstone with
interbedded anhydrite units. The main zones of interest drilled at the subject well include the Ratcliffe,
Mission Canyon, and Middle Member Bakken.
The Ratcliffe interval of the Charles Formation (Mississippian -
Madison Group) was drilled at 8,997' TVD (-6,835'), 7' low to the
Langved 5393 42-10 5T. Samples from the Ratcliffe range from a tan to
light gray, medium brown, microcrystalline limestone (Figure 2).
Samples have no visual porosity and no visible oil staining was
observed. A slight gas show of 16 units was observed at 9,008'.
Figure 2. Cuttings of limestone from the Ratcliffe interval of the Charles
Formation.
The Mission Canyon Formation (Mississippian - Madison Group) was
drilled at 9,101' TVD (-6,939'), 8' low to the Langved 5393 42-10 5T.
Representative samples consist of a tan light gray off white to brown
microcrystalline limestone (Figure 3). The samples did not contain any
visual porosity and no visible oil staining was observed. Gas shows
ranging from 17 ­ 25 units were observed in the upper 300' of the
formation.
Figure 3. Cuttings of limestone from the Mission Canyon Formation.
The Middle Member of the Bakken Formation (Late Devonian-Early
Mississippian) was drilled at 10,540' TVD (-8,378'), 2' low to the
Langved 5393 42-10 5T. Representative samples consist of a light gray
to off white tan, calcareous silty sandstone with intergranular porosity
and a trace light-brown oil stain. (Figure 4). Strong hydrocarbon show
and heavy gases (C1-C4) were observed in this interval.

Gas and Oil Shows
Hydrocarbon constituents (C1 through C4) are expressed in parts-per-million (ppm) concentrations.
Diesel-invert mud was used to drill the vertical and curve, while salt-water mud was used to drill the
lateral. During the vertical, gas shows ranged from 14-25 units above a background of ~10 units against
a 9.7-10 lb/gal diesel-invert mud weight. Methane (C1) was observed throughout the vertical. Gases
heavier than C1 were not observed. After kicking off the curve, gas levels remained low with one show
of 53 units with background ranging from 14-21 units, until reaching the False Bakken. Gas shows were
elevated in respect to the vertical from this interval through the entrance of the Middle Bakken. Shows
from the False Bakken through Middle Bakken ranged from 249-1701 units (C1-C4) over a background
of 67 units against a diesel-invert mud weight of 10.2 lb/gal. Upon drilling the Middle Bakken to the
curve landing, background gas slowly declined to ~20 units, with shows ranging from 48-105 units (C1-
C4).
The lateral began with gas shows ranging from 51-105 units (C1-C4) over a background of 20 units
against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. These levels were observed for much of the well with
occasional increases, as described below. These increases do not appear to be associated with specific
zones within the Middle Bakken. Increased gas levels were observed from 11,150' - 11,350' MD, with
shows ranging from 142-241 units (C1-C4) over a background of 50 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight. Stronger gas shows were observed from 12,870' - 13,100' MD, with shows ranging from
160-300 units (C1-C4) over a background of 105 units against a 9.65 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. The
highest gas levels during the first half of the lateral were observed from 13,900' - 14,350' MD, with
shows ranging from 383-846 units (C1-C4) over a background of 175 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water
mud weight.
Gas levels were increased from 15,000' - 16,300' MD, with shows ranging from 160-500 units (C1-C4)
over a background of 105 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Another section of increased
gas levels was observed from 17,050' - 17,450' MD, with shows ranging from 273-704 units (C1-C4)
over a background of 190 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. The lowest gas levels in the
lateral were observed from 17,580' - 17,900' MD, with two shows of 41 and 56 units (C1-C4) over a
background ranging from 25-30 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. Gas levels increased
again from 17,950' - 18,150' MD, with shows ranging from 185-320 units (C1-C4) over a background of
160 units against a 9.7 lb/gal salt-water mud weight. At 18,500' MD gas levels began to steadily
increase with shows reaching as high as 1,997 units at 19,412' MD. Gas levels stayed elevated for the
remainder of the well, with shows ranging from 222-1997 units (C1-C4) over a background of 350-500
units against a 9.70 lb/gal salt-water mud weight.
GEOSTEERING
The potential pay zone of the Langved 5393 42-10 4B was identified by evaluating gamma ray log data
from the Langved 5393 42-10 5T (Figure 5).
Figure 5. Langved 5393 42-10 5T type log. Ideal target zone bound in green (Oasis Petroleum).
The original prognosis had the curve landing at 10,567' TVD at 90.45°. The Lodgepole top indicated that
the target was tracking well with the prognosis based on the two closest wells (Langved 5393 42-10 3T2
and Langved 5393 42-10 5T). The wellbore was landed at a TVD of 10,573' at 90.2°, in the middle of
the base of the primary target zone in the Middle Bakken.
Drilling ahead out of the casing at a depth of 10,962' MD, the inclination was reduced in order to
identify some of the markers lower in section. Two target zones were established for this lateral; the
upper primary target zone for most of the lateral, and a lower secondary zone to be used when
intersecting an existing well between 16,800' and 17,600'. The borehole was lowered to the top of the
secondary target zone at 11,400', where the D marker was observed, characterized by a lower gamma
signature (below 80 api). The borehole was then brought back up into the lower portion of the primary
target zone where it remained until 15,000'. The E marker was observed a few feet above the base of the
primary target. The D and E markers served as the primary markers for calculating dip.
At 15,000' the inclination was reduced to get the borehole down into the secondary target in preparation
for the intersection with the existing well which was installed in the upper 10-15 of the Middle Bakken.
The borehole approached the base of the secondary target at approximately 15,650' and remained near
the base until 16,850'. The gamma signature in the secondary target is nondescript ranging from 87-110
api. At approximately 17,000' MD the controlled ascent from the secondary target zone, back in to the
primary target zone was initiated. The well bore re-entered the primary target zone at ~17,900' clearing
the potential hazard margin from the previous Bakken lateral. After reentry in to the primary target zone
it became apparent the local formation dip rate had flattened from 0.40° to 0.10°.
Structural dip is interpreted to be flat at the beginning of the lateral, with an up dip ranging from 0.10° to
0.60° for the length of the lateral. The gross apparent dip for the well was 0.35° up. The dip profile is
presented below in Figure 6.

SUMMARY
The Oasis Petroleum North America, LLC Langved 5393 42-10 4B was drilled by Nabors 460 from
reentry to TD in 16 days. The well reached a total depth of 20,420' MD on December 5, 2014. Geologic
data, hydrocarbon gas measurements, and sample examination obtained during the Langved 5393 42-10
4B indicate hydrocarbon presence in the Middle Bakken Target. Samples from the target zone consist of
light to medium gray, off white, calcareous cemented silty sandstone. Disseminated pyrite was present
in varying degrees, trace intergranular porosity, trace spotty brown oil stain were also observed along
with fast streaming strong pale yellow fluorescence. Lateral operations were successful in exposing
9,462' of potentially productive Middle Bakken target zone rock to the wellbore.

RE: Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
