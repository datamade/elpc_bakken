INTRODUCTION
The SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS [NE NW Section 28, T163N, R99W] is located
approximately 10 miles east of Fortuna in Divide County, North Dakota. The subject well was
designed to drill south at a heading of 176.08°, from [250' FNL and 2,375' FEL] Section 28 to
SW SE Section 33, T163N, R99W by the Nabors #687 drilling rig (Figure 1). The Waters 3-
28HS is intended to develop oil production from the Devonian Three Forks Formation in the
Ambrose Field.
OFFSET WELL CONTROL
Waters 3-28HS offset control was provided by the SM Energy Company, Waters 4-28H [NW
NW Section 28, T163N, R99W], the SM Energy Company, Hauge 13-21H [SW SW Section 21,
T163N, R99W], and the EOG (New Mexico) Inc., Bublitz 1-22 [NW SW Section 22, T163N,
R99W] (Figure 2). The Waters 4-28H is located approximately 0.24 miles west of the Waters 3-
28HS, and was drilled in November of 2011. The Waters 4-28H was drilled in the Three Forks
Formation, and has produced over 136 thousand barrels over the life of the well. The Hauge 13-
21H is located approximately 0.24 miles northwest of the Waters 3-28HS, and was drilled in
October of 2011. The Hauge 13-21H was drilled in the Three Forks Formation, and has
produced approximately 204 thousand barrels over the life of the well. The Bublitz 1-22 is
located approximately 0.84 miles northeast of the Waters 3-28HS, and was drilled in October of
1992, as a dry vertical well in the Duperow Formation. Open-hole logs from these wells were
used in forecasting formation tops and the landing target for the Waters 3-28HS. The Waters 3-
28HS was used to provide log character for the upper Three Forks.

ENGINEERING & OPERATIONS
On September 15, 2014, the Waters 3-28HS was re-entered at 1,478' measured depth (MD) by
Nabors #687. The surface hole was isolated with 9 " surface casing (36# J-55). The well was
drilled to 7,604' where the decision was made to trip out of the hole due to low ROP. At 7,604',
the rig crew tripped out of the hole to pick up a new bottom hole assembly (BHA), which
included an 8 ¾" PDC bit and a 2.25° motor for the remaining vertical hole and curve build
section. The well was drilled to 7,792' MD, which was 24' high to the planned kick-off point
(KOP) and continued drilling to a landing depth of 8,502' MD. Diesel invert drilling fluid was
used through the vertical hole and curve build sections (1,478' to 8,502' MD) maintaining a 9.65
to 9.85 ppg mud weight for stable hole conditions. Sperry-Halliburton provided night-time
directional drilling and measurement-while drilling (MWD) services. Surveys from the vertical
hole provided tie-in orientation of the wellbore to begin the curve build and lateral drilling.
Sunburst Consulting, Inc. provided well-site geology consultation.
After curve landing, 7" intermediate casing (29# HCL-80 and 32# HCL-80) was set and
cemented to 8,487' MD. Drilling fluid was changed out to saltwater ranging between 9.65 to
9.80 ppg. The initial lateral BHA included a 6" PDC bit, a 1.5° fixed mud motor, and MWD
survey and gamma tools. Lateral operations began at 0500 hrs CDT, on September 21, 2014.
The original BHA was used through the entire lateral from 8,502' to 18,147' MD.
The Waters 3-28HS was drilled to a total depth (TD) of 18,147' MD, 8,303.82' true vertical
depth (TVD), on September 26, 2014. Projected to TD, the bottom hole coordinates are
10,100.80' south and 699.31' east of surface location or approximately 217.44' FSL and
2,216.25' FEL SW SE Section 33, T163N, R99W. Projected to TD, the wellbore has a closure
distance of 10,124.97' at a closure direction of 176.04°.
GEOLOGIC EVALUATION
Geologic supervision was provided by Sunburst Consulting, Inc. for the Waters 3-28HS. The
geology well-site team monitored gas, logged drill cuttings and geo-steered. Logging samples
and gas monitoring started at 6,700' MD. The well-site geologists caught drill cuttings in 30'
lagged intervals through the curve and 100' intervals through the lateral. Drill cuttings were
examined under a trinocular microscope and complete lithologic descriptions are provided in the
appendix of this report. Dried cuttings were collected and sent to SM Energy Company and the
North Dakota Geological Survey. A Pason digital gas detector was interfaced with Epoch, an
electronic data recorder (EDR). The Epoch system provided hole depth, bit depth and pump
rates to the gas detector and distributed the detector's total gas readings across location. Total
gas was recorded in units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air).
Lithology & Hydrocarbons
Sample examination began in the Charles Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] above
the last Charles salt. The Base of the Last Charles Salt was penetrated at 6,821' TVD
(-4,573'), which was 2' high to prognosis. Salt intervals were marked by an increase in rate of
penetration along with a decrease in weight on bit. Background gas data above the Base of the
Last Charles Salt ranged from 50 to 64 units, and had no gas shows.
The Ratcliffe interval of the lower Charles Formation consists of argillaceous limestone,
limestone, dolomitic limestone and anhydrite. The top of the Ratcliffe was penetrated at 6,895'
TVD (-4,647'), which was 6' high to prognosis. The Ratcliffe top is within a limestone facies
and below an anhydrite facies. Background gas within the Ratcliffe ranged from 40 to 50 units
with no shows.
The Mission Canyon Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group] consists of limestone,
argillaceous limestone and dolomitic limestone. The Mission Canyon top was penetrated at
7,072' TVD (-4,824'), and was 1' low to prognosis. The top of the Mission Canyon was marked
by a transition from argillaceous limestone to a light gray to light brown limestone, which can be
identified by a decrease in gamma. Limestone intervals throughout the Mission Canyon have
algal laminations. Gas shows within the Mission Canyon reached as high as 78 units, and
background gases were within 55 to 70 units.
A transition from light brown limestone to a medium to dark brown argillaceous limestone
marked the top of the Lodgepole Formation [Mississippian, Madison Group]. The Lodgepole
was penetrated at 7,696' TVD (-5,488'), and was 6' high to prognosis. The Lodgepole top is
further indicated by an increase in rate of penetration and an increase in gamma. Limestone and
argillaceous limestone compose the Lodgepole Formation. Background gas readings within this
interval were between 80 and 100 units, with no significant gas shows.
The Upper Bakken Shale [Mississippian] was penetrated at 8,103' TVD (-5,855'), which was
2' high to prognosis. The top of the Upper Bakken Shale was indicated by sample returns of
dark brown to black, carbonaceous, petroliferous and pyritic shale. Gas shows were as high as
136 units, and there were no connection gas shows within in this interval. Additionally, gamma
counts were recorded to 675 API.
The Middle Bakken Member [Mississippian/Devonian] was penetrated at 8,116' TVD
(-5,868'), and was 2' high to prognosis. The Middle Bakken top was marked by a large decrease
in gamma and rate of penetration as the wellbore passed from shale to siltstone and silty
sandstone. Silty sandstone was logged as very light gray to light gray tan in color, calcite
cemented and containing trace amounts of disseminated pyrite. Also, trace poor intergranular
porosity and trace spotty light brown oil stain were present. Gas shows within this interval
reached 155 units, and background gas was within 80 to 95 units. There were no significant
connection gas shows within this interval.
The Lower Bakken Shale [Devonian] was penetrated at 8,178' TVD (-5,930'), which was 1'
high to prognosis. It was very similar to the Upper Bakken Shale Member except for being
slightly lighter in color. The Lower Shale Member also has a sharp increase in gamma, up to
902 API. Background gas within the Lower Bakken Shale ranged from 75 to 95 units with no
significant connection or gas shows.
The Three Forks Formation [Devonian] was drilled at 8,207' TVD (-5,959') and was 3' low to
prognosis. The upper part of the Three Forks was a heavily dolomite cemented sandstone with
interbedded shale. Sandstone was described as cream to peach to off white in color (Figure 3).
It was fine to very fine grained and well to moderately sorted. Within this interval disseminated
pyrite was less common. Spotty very light brown oil stain was more prevalent in this facies.
Sandstone samples were cut with trichloroethylene (Carbo-Sol) and examined with a
fluoroscope. The cut samples showed pale yellow, slow, blooming cut.

An approximately 4' thick interval of silty shale (Figure 4) lies beneath the dolomite cemented
sandstone. It is described as pale gray to pale green and soft to firm with an earthy texture. It is
silty in part with occasional disseminated and nodular pyrite, and displays no visible porosity or
oil stain.
Figure 4) Shale intermixed with dolomite cemented sandstone and

Consulting, Inc.).
The greatest hydrocarbon shows recorded in the Three Forks occurred in the dolomite cemented
sandstone facies, including gas shows as high as 693 units at 10,381' MD, and background gas
ranged from 320 to 420 units. Connection gas shows within the sandstone reached 522 units at
16,505' MD. Hydrocarbon shows in the shale are slightly lower than in the sandstone. Gas
shows reached 420 units at 9,533' MD. Connection gases reached 158 units at 9,516' MD.
Background gas ranged from 80 to 140 units.
GEO-STEERING
Figure 5) Type log of the Three Forks gamma signatures as recorded in the SM Energy Company, Waters 4-28H
open-hole logs and used to provide log character, in comparison to TVD gamma ray (MWD) observed while
drilling the Waters 3-28HS (Alexa Guttormson, Well-site Geologist, Sunburst Consulting, Inc.).
The target interval within the Three Forks was 8' thick, beginning approximately 4' below the
base of the Lower Bakken Shale. The target interval was based on the TVD gamma ray log of
the offset control well, SM Energy Company, Waters 4-28H (Figure 5). Gamma ray readings
within the upper 3' of the target zone displayed a gamma signature of 60 to 110 API. The
middle 3' displayed a gamma signature of 40 to 60 API. The lower 2' displayed a gamma
signature of 90 to 160 API. The base of the cool gamma (40 to 60 API) marker was used for dip
calculations. The well plan called for a landing 10' below the top of the Three Forks Formation.
Landing depths were adjusted as formation markers were traversed. Calculations from markers
within the Lodgepole were confirmed within the Bakken members and provided a target landing
depth of 8,211' TVD. The directional drillers were able to land at 8,212' TVD, which was 1'
low to the planned landing depth. MWD-gamma ray correlated adequately with the Waters 4-
28H, which was used as the most accurate offset for geo-steering. Geo-steering efforts were
made to stay within the 8' thick, cool (40 to 110 API) and hot (90 to 160 API) gamma markers.
The steering team was successful in maintaining 98% of the exposed 9,645' lateral wellbore.
Based on intersections with the cool gamma marker, an overall apparent dip was estimated at
0.51° down (Figure 6).

SUMMARY
The SM Energy Company Waters 3-28HS was re-entered on September 15, 2014 by Nabors
#687. It is a two-section Three Forks horizontal well within the Ambrose Field in Divide
County, North Dakota. 98% of the lateral was drilled in the ideal 8' primary target interval. A
mud program consisting of diesel invert (9.65 to 9.85 ppg) during the vertical and curve build
sections, and saltwater (9.65 to 9.80 ppg) during the lateral was utilized. The target zone was an
8' thick interval that lies approximately 4' below the base of the Lower Bakken. Sandstone of the
target zone displayed poor intergranular porosity and trace spotty very light brown oil staining.
Background gas within the sandstone ranged from approximately 320 to 420 units. The Waters
3-28HS awaits completion to determine its full production potential.
