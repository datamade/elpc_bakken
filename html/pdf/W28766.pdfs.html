GENERAL:
Newfield Production Company's double section Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H
was "spudded" as a Three Forks Formation Double lateral well on September 20th, 2014. The
subject well is being drilled in the Westburg field, located about 10 miles North West of Keene
in McKenzie County, North Dakota. Surface location of the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-
10H is 368' FNL, 848' FEL, NENE Section 21-T153N-R96W. The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-
21-28-10H was planned with the ultimate goal of oil production in the dolomite porosity of the
upper bench of the Three Forks formation. The horizontal well-path was planned to follow
structure with an estimated 90° average formation dip throughout the lateral to total depth at
20,535' MD. The well bore was planned with one 20 degree turn and finished with drilling to the
South following an azimuth along 180.0°.
ENGINEERING:
The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H was drilled with Patterson UTI 346. Ground
level of the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H was 2,057' above mean sea level with the
Kelly bushing located at 2,082' above mean sea level, 25' above ground level.
The Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H was drilled with fresh water from surface to
1,703' MD. A switch was then made and oil base mud, and was used from 1,703' MD to
10,547'MD. Once horizontal, the well was switched over to salt brine from 10,547' MD to total
depth at 20,348' MD.
The main lateral of Newfield Production Company's Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-
10H began on November 14th, 2014. It was drilled from 10,547' MD (10,248' TVD) to a TD at
20,348' MD (10,331.32'TVD, est). At TD a 5" liner was run in the vertical and 4.5" liner was
ran through the lateral and fracture stimulation was planned to enhance the well.
The final lateral profile for the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H showed a
net drop of 72' TVD over a span of 9801' of vertical section drilled bringing the average dip for
the whole lateral to approximately 89.58 deg. The bottom hole location for the Sand Creek
Federal 153-96-21-28-10H is 252' FSL, 1101' FEL of SESE Sec 28 T153N R96W.

The Charles Salt came in at 7,847' MD 7,835' TVD -5,753' sub-sea-level. The Charles
Salt consisted predominately of salts, limestone, and anhydrite. Salts from the Charles formation
were typically clear to translucent to translucent red in color, hard, massive, cubic and euhedral
crystalline structure, vitreous to sub glassy textures. Lithology below the salts in the Charles
formation consisted of limestone and anhydrites. Limestone's above the Ratcliffe interval within
the Charles formation were typically light gray to medium gray to bluish gray in color, firm to
brittle to hard, predominantly coarse to poor crystalline, wackestone, argillaceous, with poor
visible fracture porosity, no stain, no fluorescence, no odor, or cut fluorescence. No oil or gas
shows were observed while drilling the Charles Formation. No problems were encountered while
drilling the Charles Formation. The Base of the last Charles Salt was observed at 8,570' MD,
8,558' TVD and -6,476' sub-sea level.
The Mission Canyon formation top came in at 8,762' MD, 8,750' TVD and -6,668' sub-
sea level. The Mission Canyon formation consisted of gray limestones, moderately hard to hard
in hardness, cryptocrystalline wackestone to packstone in structure. Common shale inter-bedding
was observed. Poor to moderate oil show accompanied it in samples. Occasional lost circulation
intervals are found in the Mission Canyon. No other problems were encountered while drilling
the Mission Canyon formation.
The Lodgepole Formation top was recorded and observed at 9,377' MD, 9,363' TVD and
-7,281' sub-sea level. The Lodgepole Formation consisted of medium gray to gray to dark brown
to dark gray brown limestone, hard and microcrystalline to cryptocrystalline packstone and
wackestone in structure. No problems were encountered while drilling the Lodgepole Formation.
The False Bakken top was recorded and observed at 10,173' MD, 10,123' TVD and -
8,041' sub-sea level. The False Bakken formation consisted of dark brown to dark gray limey
shale's. The shale's of the False Bakken were typically firm to brittle, sub-platy in structure, and
silty to earthy in texture. A 7,890 unit gas show was encountered while drilling the False
Bakken and had an oil show consisting of scattered faint yellow fluorescence with fast streaming
blue white cut fluorescence. No problems were encountered while drilling the False Bakken
formation.
The Upper Bakken Shale formation top was recorded and observed at 10,189' MD,
10,133' TVD and -8,051' sub-sea level. The Upper Bakken Shale's consisted of very dark gray
to black shale's, firm to soft to occasionally brittle in hardness, sub-blocky ­ blocky in structure,
carbonaceous to earthy in texture. Oil show was noted with drill cuttings of the Upper Bakken
Shale exhibiting a flash blue white cut fluorescence. A background gas increase was observed
while drilling the Upper Bakken Shale with a peak gas recorded of 3,692 units. No problems
were encountered with the Upper Bakken Shale.

The Middle Bakken dolomite top was recorded and observed at 10,219' MD, 10,155'
TVD and -8,073' sub-sea level. The Middle Bakken consisted of light gray to gray to tan to gray
brown dolomite. The Middle Bakken dolomite was typically soft to firm and occasionally brittle
in hardness, very fine crystalline packestone in structure, with crystalline to sub sucrosic
textures. Drill cuttings of the Middle Bakken showed good oil shows with even dull green to
yellow green fluorescence, faint to very strong hydrocarbon odors, moderately bright milky
bluish white flash cut with secondary multi-streaming cut fluorescence, and good visible
porosity. Gas levels while drilling the middle Bakken formation ranged from 1000 units to 2500
units recorded by the ML TGC gas detector. No problems were encountered while drilling the
Middle Bakken.
The Lower Bakken shale top was recorded and observed at 10,307' MD, 10,202' TVD
and -8,120' sub-sea level. The Lower Bakken consisted of black organic rich shale and silty
shale. The Lower Bakken shale was typically soft to very soft blocky carbonaceous shale, it is
earthy to silty in texture with strong hydrocarbon odor and traces of pyrite. The Lower Bakken
shale had dull yellow fluorescence, streaming yellow cut as well as bright yellow flash cut and a
bright yellow residual cut ring. A peak gas show of 1,752 units was recorded in the Lower
Bakken Shale. Gas was not run through the gas buster and no flare was observed while drilling
the Lower Bakken shale. No problems were encountered while drilling the Lower Bakken shale.
The Three Forks top was recorded and observed at 10,394 MD, 10,233' TVD and -8,151'
sub-sea level. The Three Forks consisted of light gray to gray to tan to gray brown dolomite. The
Three Forks dolomite was typically off white to gray to light gray to white, hard to firm and
occasionally soft in hardness, crystalline packestone in structure, with coarse to intermittently
coarse crystalline structure. Cuttings showed moderate to good visible porosity and showed
moderate to good oil shows with even dull yellow fluorescence, faint hydrocarbon odors,
moderately bright milky white to yellow flash cut with secondary slow streaming cut
fluorescence. The Three Forks in clay stone was light blue green to blue gray in color, firm to
hard, crypto crystalline in structure. Cuttings are very slightly calcareous have common
interstitial pyrite, no visible porosity or oil show. Gas levels while drilling the Three Forks
formation ranged from 1,000 units to 4,000 units recorded by the ML TGC gas detector. Gas was
run through the gas buster starting at 10,800 and we had an intermittent 2-10' flare for the entire
lateral while drilling the Sand Creek Federal 153-96-21-28-10H. The lateral was drilled 100% in
the Three Forks, 75% in zone and reached total depth of 20,348' MD on 11/24/2014 @ 15:14
hrs. Average dip for the lateral was 89.58°.
