GENERAL:
On August 30, 2014 Continental Resource's Margaurite 2-15H1 was "spudded" as a Three
Forks formation, 1280 spaced, lateral well. The subject well is being drilled in the Sanish field, located
about 10 miles north of New Town, in Mountrail County in North Dakota. New Town is located in the
east central portion of the Williston Basin. Surface location of the Margaurite 2-15H1 is 450' FNL &
955' FEL, NENE of Section 15, T153N - R93W. The Margaurite 2-15H1was planned with an ultimate
goal of oil production in the 1st Bench of the Three Forks formation. The horizontal well-path was
planned following structure with an estimated 89.7° average formation dip to total depth at 20,317'
MD. The well bore was planned to be drilled to the south across sections 15 and 22 following an
azimuth of 180.20°. The Margaurite 2-15H1well is located on the same pad as the Margaurite 1-15H,
Margaurite 3-15H wells.

ENGINEERING:
The Margaurite 2-15H1 surface hole was drilled to 2,051' MD with a smaller surface drilling
rig. Pad height of the Margaurite 2-15H1 was 2,072' above mean sea level with the Cyclone #10 kelly
bushing located at 2,092' above mean sea level, 20' above ground level.
Margaurite 2-15H1 was drilled with fresh water from surface to 2,051' MD. From surface
casing, the Margaurite 2-15H1 was drilled with oil based mud. Oil based mud was used from 2,051'
MD to 10,877' MD. A switch was then made to brine mud from 10,877' MD to a total depth of
20,300' MD.
Initially, the Margaurite 2-15H1 was drilled as a 13 ½" hole to 2,051' MD, and 9 5/8" 36# J-55
casing was set to a measured depth of 2,051' through the Fox Hills formation into the Pierre formation.
The Margaurite 2-15H1 then progressed as an 8 ¾" hole from the upper part of the Pierre formation
until landing of the curve in the Three Forks formation. Kick off point (KOP) was reached in the
Lodgepole formation on September 2, 2014, 1132 hrs at 10,095' MD (10,093.46' TVD). At KOP, a
trip was made to pick up a LEAM mud motor with a 2.5° fixed bend and a new PDC bit. Directional
work during the curve was performed by Robert Horton and Nick Edwards from LEAM directional
services. The curve from vertical to horizontal began on September 2, 2014 at 2236 hrs and was drilled
starting from 10,095' MD (10,093.46' TVD) and landed horizontal (90.38°) at 10,877' MD
(10,574.38' TVD). The curve was drilled holding an average build rate of 11.56° per 100 feet and was
landed on September 4, 2014 at 0625 hrs. One mechanical problem was encountered while drilling the
curve. A trip was made at 10,237' MD to replace the MWD tool.

Geologic supervision of the Continental Resource's Margaurite 2-15H1 well was provided by
Geo-Link, Inc with a two-man mudlogging / geo-steering consulting service. Geologic
evaluation began just above the Charles formation and continued until TD at the end of the
lateral in the Three Forks formation. MSI's Red Box (chromatograph) was used to record gas
levels at the drilling rig's shakers. The Red Box gas data was integrated into Pason's EDR
(Electronic Data Recorder) system. Also integrated into Pason's EDR system was gamma ray

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
