Well Synopsis
Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 Cyclone 2
Three Forks
Well Plan:
The Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 was spud on November 2nd, 2014 at 22:45 hours, with a surface hole
location of 355 FSL and 2245 FWL in the SESW corner of Section 19 - Township 155 North
and Range 98 West in Williams Co., North Dakota. This well was operated by Continental
Resources Incorporated with the objective target of the late Devonian Three Forks. The well
plan showed a build section with a kickoff point of 10698 MD in the Mississippian Lodgepole
with a 13°/100 build rate to the landing point of 11388 MD; 11139 TVD, approximately 20
below the Three Forks Formation. The plan was to drill lateral for an estimated 10303 to the
hardline in the SWSE, of section 31 - Township 155 North and Range 98 West in Williams Co.,
North Dakota, following the estimated dip of 89.72°. The projected well path is to the South
with a proposed azimuth of 178.83°.
The offsets provided were the Mildred 5-19H1 & Rennerfeldt 4-30H all operated by
Continental Resources Inc.
Gas logged in the vertical and lateral sections were monitored using Mud Logging Systems
Mlogger & Mcontrol. (Primary Logger: ML-369 Backup Logger ML-098)
M-Logger CC & TC filaments calibrated with 1% and 100% test gas Chromatograph
calibrated with 1% test gas (gas units).
Build Section:
The build section was kicked off @ 10705 MD on November 7th, 2014 at 03:35 hours, In the
Lodgepole formation. The up hole markers logged were the Base of the Last Salt (9500 TVD (-
7203 ss)), Mission Canyon (9711 TVD (-7414 ss)), and the Lodgepole (10337 TVD (-8040
ss)). The build section started on November 7th, 2014 at 16:55 hours, w/ a (BHA) bottom hole
assembly consisting of a 6.75 Mpact XXL Performance motor with a 2.5° fxd bend and a
Security 8 3/4 Smith MDi513 PDC Bit. This assembly able to generate sufficient enough build
rates to land in the approximate target zone. The down hole markers below kick off point
consisted of the False Bakken (11024 TVD (-8727 ss)), Upper Bakken Shale (11037 TVD (-
8740 ss)), Middle Bakken Member (11057 TVD (-8760 ss)), Lower Bakken Shale (11100 TVD
(-8803 ss)) & Three Forks Formation (11127 TVD (-8830 ss)). These markers came 3-8 low
versus the prognosis and offset logs. The estimated landing was calculated @ 11139 TVD,
which was adjusted to 11142-11144 TVD due to lower Prognosed formation tops. The
landing for 7 intermediate casing was completed on November 8th, 2014 at 10:25 hours,
about 10 into the Three Forks Formation, with a landing at 11458 MD; 11137 TVD. The 7
casing was set @ 11428 MD; 11136.96 TVD, with a location of: 120 FNL & 2372 FEL NWNE
Section 30: T155N-R98W Williams Co., ND. The build section consisted of one assembly,
753 total footage drilled w/ 599 sliding (12.25 hrs) 154 rotating (2.08 hrs). (See
Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 Build, TVD log & Formation Tops for more detailed information)
Gas observed in the build section showed gas ranging 23-325 units through the Lodgepole
formation, averaging 49 units with faint light brown/black oil stains with faint, moderately
good milky-cloudy invert cut. The Upper & Lower Bakken Shale s showed moderate amounts
of gas resulting in 396-3862 units. The Middle Bakken Member ranged from 224-1295 units,
averaging 572 units & displayed fair to good brown/black oil stains with fair-good milky-
cloudy invert cut. The Three Forks formation resulted 305-1644 units, averaging 805 units and
811-3125 units while circulating out trip gas after the landing. Three Forks samples also
included fair amounts of brown/black oil stains, with fair immediate moderately fast cloudy
blue/white Invert cut. (See Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 Build log for more detailed gas data)
Lateral Section:
Penetration of the lateral section started on November 10th, 2014 at 21:30 hrs, with a depth
of 11458 MD, drilling with a 6 Baker Hughes T406x PDC bit and 4.75, 5/6 lobe 8.3 stage 1.5°
bend RPG 1.04 Mpact XXL Performance mud motor. The plan was to drill in the target Zone
and follow the 10-13 zone of interest between the Internal Shale and a hot gamma marker
13 from the top of the Three Forks. The lateral section was drilled from 11458 MD to 21650
MD for a lateral length of 10192 , with a bottom hole location: 230 FSL & 2488 FEL SWSE
Sec 31, T155N-R98W, Williams Co., ND completed on November 20th, 2014 08:29 hours.
(21650 MD; 11205.37 TVD). The lateral section consisted of 10192 total footage drilled w/
946 sliding (46.25 hrs) 9246 rotating (99.0 hrs). The well was drilled entirely in the Three
Forks Formation with two complete trips for a motor (BHA). One @ 20852 MD; 11196 TVD
and @ 15298 MD; 11142.7 TVD and one wire-line attempt at this depth which failed due to
pipe scale packing off the MWD tool.
Samples collected in the lateral section mostly consisted of light-medium brown, light-
medium tan/brown, buff tan, mottled brown/tan, light-medium pink, dark gray/brown
Dolomite/Limestone consisting of abundant to trace amounts of Pyrite, mainly in our upper
and lower target zone. Samples also consisted of blue/green, gray/green shale,
predominately in the upper target zone and internal shale. The middle-lower zone displayed
predominately more tan/brown clean Dolomite, above the Internal 1 Shale, consisting of fair
to good porosity. This targeted zone displayed 5-15% light-medium pale greenish/yellow &
light-medium brown oil on shakers. Samples also displayed light-medium black/brown oil
stains and fair to bright green/yellow fluorescence with fair milky-cloudy dull
blooming/streaming blue/white cut.
Gas averaged 816 units and ranged from 240 units to 7984 units of gas. Connection gasses
ranged from 247 units to 5100 units and trip gas ranged from 650 units to 8000 units. Gas in
the clean Dolomite zone and upper target zone produced more gas & oil shows versus drilling
the bottom Internal Shale zone. (For more detailed sample descriptions & gas data see
Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 Horizontal Log)
The formation dip was calculated using several significant gamma markers throughout the
target zone. The most consistent markers observed were in the clean Dolomite zone,
between the orange marker line and bottom target zone and the Internal Shale. The upper
target zone marker (orange dashed-line) ranged from 110 125 (API) and was a significant
hard spot, which resulted in severe deflections of over 2-3 degrees. The lower-middle target
zone displayed the coolest gamma, which ranged from 42-75 (API). This zone was relatively
easy to drill in with good to very good well control, often allowing 3-4 stands of rotation
without sliding. The clean Dolomite zone was the main target and proved to be the best
overall drilling plan, since it was tracking consistently better than the upper & lower zone.
The lower target zone and Internal Shale displayed more Pyrite and Blue-green Shales giving
more erratic gamma counts ranging from 115-142 (API), whereas the middle target zone
displayed less Pyrite in the clean tan-brown Dolomite giving us lower gamma counts. The
Formation dropped about 67 from about 16300 MD to total well depth (89.27° Dip). (For
more detailed gamma signatures and structure please see Rennerfeldt 5-30H1 cross section)

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
