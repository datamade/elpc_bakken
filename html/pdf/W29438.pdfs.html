INTRODUCTION
XTO Energy, Inc. Sorenson 14X-33F [SW SW Section 33, T150N, R98W] is located approximately 2.5
miles south-southeast of Watford, in Siverston Field of McKenzie County, North Dakota (Figure 1). The
well was designed as a Three Forks horizontal well to develop Sections 33 and 28. Directional drilling
and geo-steering would be employed to land in the Three Forks reservoir and maintain maximum
exposure to a 20' target zone.
OFFSET WELLS
Three primary offset wells were used for structural correlation during curve operations. The closest at 0.3
miles west was the GV 44-32NEH [SE SE Section 32, T150N, R98W]. At 1.6 miles east of the subject
well, the Johnson 16-34H [SE SE Section 34, T150N, R98W] was the second closest, and at 1.9 miles
southeast of the subject well, the Johnsrud 1-3 [SE SE Section 3, T149N, R98W] was the furthest offset.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Geologic analysis for the Sorenson 14X-33F was provided by a Sunburst Consulting, Inc. geology team.
Sunburst well site geologists caught lagged samples at 30' intervals from 9,700' to TD (total depth). Rock
samples, rate of penetration and gamma ray data were used in geologic analysis to aid in geo-steering and
dip calculations.
Prospective Lithology
The Ratcliffe was drilled at 9,808' MD / 9,806' TVD, 4' high to prognosis and 4' low to GV 44-32NEH.
This interval was mainly lime mudstone to wackestone, which was cream to medium brown in color,
friable and with an earthy texture. It also consisted of anhydrite and dolomite. Gas shows ranged from 68
units to 101 units against a 9.7 ppg mud weight.
The Mission Canyon Formation was drilled at 9,952' MD / 9,950' TVD, 20' high to prognosis and 18'
low GV 44-32NEH. The Mission Canyon consists primarily of clean, argillaceous and dolomitic
limestone. The upper Mission Canyon is primarily friable to firm, light to medium gray argillaceous
limestone. While the lower lithology consisted primarily of medium to dark gray, microcrystalline lime
mudstone. Hydrocarbon shows ranged from 66 to 294 units in 9.8 ppg drilling mud.
The argillaceous limestone signifying the top of the Lodgepole Formation was penetrated at 10,511'
MD / 10,509' TVD which is 4' high to prognosis and 20' low to GV 44-32NEH. This slope marine
deposit consisted of mostly gray to brown argillaceous lime mudstone that was firm to hard,
microcrystalline and contained rare disseminated pyrite. Fracture gases ranged from 107 units to 436 units
against 9.85 to 10.00 ppg drilling mud.
The False Bakken shale was drilled at 11,360' MD / 11,307' TVD which was 2' low to prognosis and 1'
high to GV 44-32NEH. This relatively thin lithology consists of medium to dark gray brown shale. A
single gas show of 302 units was witnessed against a 10.1 ppg mud weight.
The Bakken Formation has four members, two of which are organic-rich pyritic shale, separated by a
silty sandstone middle member. The carbonaceous Upper Bakken Shale was penetrated at 11,377' MD /
11,317' TVD, 2' low to prognosis and 1' high to GV 44-32NEH. A single gas show of 1,730 units (C1
through C4) was witnessed before penetrating the Middle Bakken at 11,409' MD / 11,337' TVD, 3' low
to prognosis and 1' low to GV 44-32NEH. The Middle Member consisted of light gray to light brown
silty sandstone. Background gas here rose to 571 units against 10.15 ppg mud. Oil staining was common
along with disseminated pyrite. The Lower Bakken Shale came in at 11,488' MD / 11,379' TVD, 3' low
to prognosis and 2' low to GV 44-32NEH and was similar to the Upper Bakken Shale, with a slightly
browner color. Gas shows here got as high as 1,020 units (C1 through C4). The final member, The
Pronghorn, came in at 11,530' MD / 11,398' TVD, 5' low to prognosis and 4' low to GV 44-32NEH.
This member consists of a medium gray to brown siltstone.
The targeted Three Forks Formation was observed at 11,566' MD / 11,410' TVD, 3' low to prognosis
and 2' low to GV 44-32NEH. The observed lithology consisted of a cream to brown dolomite, which was
crystalline with an earthy texture. This facies also exhibited laminations and contained disseminated
pyrite. The targeted Three Forks lithology also contained a very light, mint green to pale blue shale that
was occasionally off white. This lithology was soft to friable and contained disseminated and nodular
pyrite. Spotty light brown oil staining and intercrystalline porosity were present throughout. The 20'
target zone was 13' below the top of the Three Forks Formation (Figure 2), and consisted of the same
argillaceous dolomite and shale. Gas shows ranged from 91 units to 1,762 units.

Lateral Gas and Oil Shows
The background gas for the Sorenson 14X-33F ranged from 47 to 1,323 units during the course of the
lateral. The highest gas show was 1,762 units at 14,566' MD against 10.10 ppg mud. Higher gas shows
were generally witnessed in the upper part of the target zone. The gas buster was venting gas throughout
the lateral. A 1' to 2' intermittent flare was witnessed at 14,600' MD.
Geo-steering
A 20' target zone was chosen for the Sorenson 14X-33F by XTO Energy geologists that began 13' below
the Pronghorn and ended 4' from the top of the Second Bench of the Three Forks. The thickness was
estimated using control well logs. A well drilled previously, 1.9 miles away, the Johnsrud 1-3, provided
guidance for expected gamma ray markers while drilling the lateral (Figure 3). The top of the target zone
had relatively low gamma reading around 80 to 95 API, just below this gamma marker was a higher
marker reading 120 API. Below this, gamma would continue to increase to 155 API. Below this high
gamma marker was a relatively thin bed of 80s gamma just before entering a low gamma marker reading
around 60 API. Gamma would then heat up slightly to 110 API then back down to 70 API before entering
a middle high gamma marker that read 160 API. Below this high gamma API would cool off with thin
beds reading between 80 and 115. Near the bottom of target zone a very low and relatively thick layer of
gamma was witnessed. Gamma would drop all the way to 50 API before entering a high marker at the
very bottom of target zone. This gamma would get as high as 168 API.

Bottom-Hole Location
Total depth resulted in a closure azimuth of 3.15° and a closure distance of 9,823.90'. The bottom-hole
location is projected to be 9,809.10' north and 539.16' east of the surface location or approximately
251.34' FNL & 1,664.16' FWL, NE NW Section 28, T150N, R98W. The hole was prepared for liner and
multi-stage fracture stimulation directly after drilling operations concluded.
SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Sorenson 14X-33F was spud on October 30, 2014 by Advanced Energy Rig #36
and re-entered by H&P 456 on December 31, 2014. The well efficiently reached TD in 20 total drilling
days on January 15, 2015. Samples from the Three Forks target contained oil staining that was described
as trace and spotty with visible intercrystalline porosity. MWD gamma ray markers, sample analysis, and
ROP were successfully utilized to expose 98% of the lateral borehole to the Three Forks target zone. The
Sorenson 14X-33F awaits completion operations to maximize its production potential.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
