2. Well Summary
The Kings Canyon 6-8-34UTFH is an extended reach single lateral production well in the Three Forks
Formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The lateral well was drilled from South to North, with the
surface location at 2326' FSL & 486' FEL of section 34, T151N, R96W, then moving into section 27,
T151N, R96W, where the well was TD'd. The wellbore was drilled on an approximate azimuth of 352.11
degrees relative to surface position until reaching total depth of 19100' at 238' FNL, 1606' FEL NWNE
S27, T151N, R96W. The drilling target pay zone was approximately a 22 feet thick zone (~18' ­ 40' TVD
below the base of the LOWER BAKKEN SHALE). The overall dip rate of this lateral was
approximately 0.55' per 100' or a 90.3 degree up dip.

Lodgepole Formation. Background gas increased from 29 units to a peak of 419 units from 10,772' MD

units at 11,000' MD (Middle Bakken) to 756 units at 11,100' MD (Lower Bakken), followed by a steady

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

The proposed ND 23 project is scheduled to start construction in 2015 which includes widening, super2 passing lanes,
and numerous turn lanes. The proposed pad is located on the west side of ND 23 just south of a ¼ line gravel road that
ties into ND 23 at the top of a vertical curve at near RP 19.8 to 19.9. The gravel road appears to have been improved in
2011 of which the well pad access will be obtained from. We are designing a super two passing lane in the northbound
direction at this location but will be located on the opposite side of the road as the proposed well pad. We are also
installing a NB left and a SB right to this gravel road. According to the preliminary plats submitted by our consultant we
will be needing temp construction easements along the ¼ line gravel road to the west in this location for the gravel road
approach tiein to ND 23. The NDDOT currently owns 100' of R/W from the centerline through this area. At this time we
are not proposing to acquire any property south of the ¼ line gravel road adjacent to where the well pad is being
proposed. The roadway in this area is definitely hilly terrain but fortunately the terrain from the centerline west to the
well pad is relatively flat in nature which is probably why it is being used as farm land.

One thing that I have failed to mention in past reviews which is especially important on this project is the fact that
numerous utilities are needing relocating and when these well pads go in they should take into account any additional
utilities being added should be placed as to not interfere with the roadway construction. This will help alleviate the
need for additional relocations during construction.
