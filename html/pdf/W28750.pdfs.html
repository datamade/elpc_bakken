GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY & CONCLUSIONS
Continental Resources, Inc. drilled the Miramar 2-32H as a single lateral targeting the Upper
Devonian/Lower Mississippian Middle Bakken formation in McKenzie County, North Dakota.
The surface hole location is in the Southwest one-quarter of the Southeast one-quarter of Section
32, Township 152 North, Range 102 West (365' FSL & 1078' FEL). The well then traverses
North to a final bottom hole location 230.81' from the North section line and 693.64 from the

Continental Resources began drilling operations using H&P rig 320 on October 7, 2014. Nine
and five eights inch diameter surface casing was cemented in place at 2200' prior to the arrival
of H&P 320. NorAm Wellsite Services began geological supervision at 8640' MD on October
18, 2014. Lagged total gas, calibrated to a one percent Methane in air standard, and
chromatography data were monitored and recorded using a Continental Laboratories total gas
detector and chromatograph Varian AGD 13. Geosteering and borehole guidance were
accomplished with the aid of lagged total gas data, lagged geological samples, a RigSense EDR
Workstation, and gamma ray logging coupled with directional drilling survey data. Phoenix
provided the Directional Drilling (DD) and Measurement While Drilling (MWD) Services.
The build section began on the morning of October 21, 2014, at 10400'MD. Data from the
Miramar 1-32AH, Miramar 3-32H1, and Ladd Cole 1-32, whose surface hole locations are
situated Southwest, West, and Northeast, respectively, of the Miramar 2-32H surface hole
location, were used as offsets. The base of the last Charles Salt and upper Lodgepole markers
and formation thicknesses suggested the landing TVD for the build section be set at 10896''TVD
(14' in to the Middle Bakken). This landing target held until the end of the curve and the bit was
landed at 10898' TVD, 16' into the Middle Bakken formation. The 7" diameter intermediate
casing was installed and cemented in place at 11192' (11192' MD & 10898.3' TVD) on October
23, 2014. The well path then proceeded along a general 0-degree North azimuth.
Overall dip was projected to be ~90.01 degrees, based on offset wells in the area, including the
Miramar 1-32AH (north bearing well west of Miramar 2-32H) and the Ladd Cole 1-32 (vertical
well northeast of Miramar 2-32H). After drilling out of 7" casing, formation briefly (11215' to
~14000'MD) followed closely (~90.0 degrees) to structural isopachs in the area, until it turned
more steeply upward around 14000'MD. Dip was then calculated at ~90.3 degrees between
14000' and ~16000'MD. Formation then flattened out to a general 90.0 degree dip until
18500'MD, at which point formation turned downward to ~89.85, where it remained until the
well's total depth (20628'MD, 10882.75'TVD). Overall dip was 90.02 degrees.
Samples throughout the course of the lateral remained consistent, showing a light to medium
grey, buff grey to grey brown, microcrystalline, slightly earthy to sucrosic, firm, argillaceous,
calcareous to dolomitic Siltstone with poor intercrystalline porosity. No notable oil shows were
seen until lube sweeps were being pumped frequently, compromising the integrity of the shows.
Background gas averaged around 400 units throughout the lateral portion of the Miramar 2-32H,
with few prominent formation gasses (see total gas data).

RE:
Filter Socks and Other Filter Media
Leakproof Container Required
Oil and Gas Wells
Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
