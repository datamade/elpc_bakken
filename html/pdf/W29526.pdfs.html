INTRODUCTION
This report summarizes the drilling of EOG Resources Parshall 90-3029H located in the Parshall Field.
This is a grass-roots horizontal on the pad targeting Middle Bakken Oil withdrawal.
Surface location is 450' FNL x 1600' FWL in the nenw of Section 30, Township 152N, Range 90W. The
projected path was to the Southeast with a proposed Vertical Section Direction of 131.0°extending into
the sesw of section 29.
9 5/8" Surface casing was set to 2016' TVD. Surface casing was drilled out October 06, 2014.
Samples were collected and gas monitored from above the Charles at 7300' MD. Intermediate 7"
casing was set to 9650' MD, 9373' TVD, 551' FNL, 2211' FWL nenw Section 30. The top of the
targeted Middle Bakken was encountered at 9631' MD; 9370' TVD; -7473'SS. Straight line projection
to the bit at 16035' FTD is 9370' TVD, -7473'SS, 6884' VS, 255' FSL x 1617' FEL sesw Section 29.
The lateral exposed 6385' of Middle Bakken. Production liner was hung at 8924' MD and set to 16031'
MD.
The dip from entry to TD calculates to 0.06°(1'/1000' updip)
The Pason Gas Analyzer was utilized for gas detection. Background gas was a steady 85-100 units
until entering the Scallion at which time a maximum of 2418 units was recorded while drilling with 10.6
lb/gal Invert drilling fluid. Invert was displace to Brine water after setting intermediate casing.
Background gas in the lateral averaged 500 units while rotating and 200 units while sliding. Subtle
connection spikes 40-50 units over background were common. Formation gas increased while drilling
in close proximity to the Gamma Marker. Maximum gas recorded was 3333 units from a depth of
15509.
Samples were collected at 30' intervals in the vertical section and curve. 100' samples were collected
in the lateral. Lithology in the targeted Middle Bakken was interpreted as Dolostone- very light to
medium gray, crypto to fine microcrystalline, subsucrosic/silty to subchalky, soft to semi firm. Finely
disseminated Carbonaceous flake and minor fine Pyrite are common to the entire Middle Bakken,
although increased within the Gamma Marker Silt to very fine Lower Sand rich laminae are observed
just below the Gamma Marker. The lower half of the Middle Bakken at this location was interpreted
very calcareous grading to dolomitic Limestone. No visible intercrystalline or intergranular Porosity was
observed at x20 magnification. The coarse silty to sandy laminae does appear to have slightly fair
surface relief inferring low matrix Porosity. The same lithology occasionally was interpreted to exhibit
questionable Oil stain. Fluorescence was generally pale yellow to blue/white mineral. Cuts were
observed as weak, although once lube was introduced to facilitate drilling the fat constituent yielded a
moderately strong white cut when introduced to Naptha.
Water losses while drilling the lateral totaled 900 barrels.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
