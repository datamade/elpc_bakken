Penetration of the lateral section started on January 22, 2015, at 23:40 hrs., at a depth of
10,783' MD'. We started drilling with a 6" Baker Hughes T406 6X18, Geo Force XL
motor 1.50 deg. Bend.
The well plan was to drill out of casing and verify landing inclination from the vertical
build then drop at a dip of 89.6 deg. in the Three Forks "D" reservoir. Our first point of
reference the TF "A" marker gave us a dip rate of 89.68 deg. When we drilled into
Section 21, we continued to drill above and below the target line seeing a continued
repeat of the gamma pattern the whole length of the lateral. For future reference on this
well we used the control points, wellbore, and Dip Rates based off the previous Bakken
Well the TIPI. We continued to drill ahead in section following below the target line at a
dip of approximately 89.6basedonthedatagatheredfromtheTIPIwellborehole
eventually ending the lateral at 20.730' MD at a dip of 89.60 deg... Based on the
structure map and the control wells, we expected the formation to be as expected the
entire length of the lateral. The hotter and cooler gamma were seen evenly across the
whole length of the lateral however the cooler gamma noted below the TF"A" marker
was present exactly as needed to verify our position in the structure respectively. We
drilled the entire Three Forks wellbore from just above the target zone at 12,000' to
14,000, and then between the desired target zone and the TF"A" marker. The entire cross
section of this lateral relies on good gamma information acquired. There were two trips
during the course of the lateral. The first trip to the shoe to repair the swivel packer on the
top drive assembly, and the second for a new BHA due to poor ROP rates. The lateral
ended at 20,730' MD, 10,454.32' TVD, on January 30, 2015 at 11:43 hrs.
The entire well was drilled with invert/diesel drilling fluid. The starting mud weight in
the beginning of the lateral was at 10.3 lbs. The mud was built up during the course of the
lateral from 10.3 to 10.6. Gas averaged at the beginning of the lateral of approximately
200-400 units, connection gases were on the average between 300 units up to 800 units
from the beginning and built to 1200-2500 units mid hole, building to over 4000 units by
the end of the well.
There was a presence of 10% up to 50% light brown to brown/black oil staining observed
throughout the lateral and fluorescence of a slight to spotty dull, even dull to bright
yellowgold to yellowgreen to bright yellowgold, with milky white to blue white, slow to
fast streaming, diffusing to blooming cuts observed in the cuttings throughout the lateral
section. The cuttings were washed very well with Citrasolv solution, water, and/or a good
detergent to remove as much of the diesel invert as possible. Mineral fluorescence was
taken into consideration when describing the sample lithology. Both wet and dry cuts
were observed and photos were taken every 200' of lateral and also upon achieving
formation tops in the vertical portion of the well.
