Introduction
Continental Resources, Inc. (CRI) successfully drilled the Alfsvaag 3-31H1 horizontal
well at an average Azimuth of 181.47º and an average Inclination of 89.86°, targeting
the Three Forks Formation from a surface hole location Section 31, T153N, R99W,
(2051' FNL & 1024' FWL) in Williams County, North Dakota, drilling south to the south
side of northwest quarter of Section 10, T152N, R100W, with a spacing unit consisting
of 2,880 acres. The well is located in the Crazy Man Creek Field in the central region of
the Williston Basin, North Dakota. The main well used for correlation was the Alfsvaag
1-31H, operated by CRI. Other nearby offsets included the Cornette POW 5300 34-26,
operated by Oasis PET NO America, and the Bud 2-30H and the Bud 3-30H1, both

The Alfsvaag 3-31H1 was spud on August 19, 2014 and the surface
casing was drilled out on October 27, 2014. Brara Geologic Consulting arrived on
site October 30, 2014 and first began analysis on the Alfsvaag 3-31H1 at 08:55
and at a measured depth (MD) of 9550'. A Mudlogging Systems, Inc. Redbox
gas detection system was used for analyzing and recording total gas and

Base of Last Charles Salt Formation Lithology Descriptions
Composite samples were caught from the Base of the Last Salt
Formation at 9165' MD to 9387' MD (Mission Canyon top), every 30'.

was off-white to cream, semi-translucent to translucent and frosty, with coarse
grain.

Closing Summary
The Alfsvaag 3-31H1 was drilled from North to South in the Three Forks Member
with the target consisting of ten vertical feet from seventeen feet below the Lower
Bakken Shale to four feet above the Internal 1 Shale. The well bore is estimated to have
been in target zone 85% of the lateral. The overall dip was 89.86°. Ninety foot (90')
surveys were taken throughout the turn in the lateral to total depth (TD).
Formation dip for the lateral had an average inclination of 89.86°. There were
very slight ascends and descends throughout the lateral. Throughout the lateral there
was an average of 11.6% C3, 4.2% C4 and 1.4% C5 gases with background gas
averaging approximately 1373u. The wellbore was drilled in the upper, middle and lower
sections of the target zone throughout the lateral of the Alfsvvag 3-31H1. There was no
significant difference in oil or gas shows in relation to where the wellbore was drilled in
formation. The one exception was when the wellbore was in the Internal 1 Shale, which
showed a decrease in oil shows and a slight decrease in gas.

The wellbore entered the Internal 1 Shale on one occasion in the lateral section
of the Alfsvaag 3-31H1. This happened at about 15600' MD and remained in the
Internal 1 Shale until approximately 16045' MD for a total of about 445' MD.
Nearly all samples exhibited good even yellow/green fluorescence when
examined under a fluoroscope. Oil cuts were commonly immediate, diffuse/flash milky
blue in color, leaving residual rings. A majority of samples were contaminated with
petroleum-based lubricant, which was used to achieve adequate bit weight to drill and to
slide. This contamination masked some fluorescence and oil cuts in some of the
samples.
Throughout the Alfsvaag 3-31H1 the gas readings were considered fair to good
with a max trip gas for the lateral came at 14285' MD with 8260u of total gas. The max
gas for the lateral of 8260u came after drilling resumed after the first trip. The most
consistent gamma marker for this lateral was found six feet below the top of the target
zone and was approximately four feet in thickness. This cool gamma marker read in the
High 30's (API) to the high 50's (API). After total depth was reached at 22444' MD on
November 15, 2014, Brara Geologic Consulting remained onsite while the rig skid back
to the Alfsvaag 2-31H to drill the lateral section.
Conclusions
1.) The Continental Resources, Inc. Alfsvaag 3-31H1 was a successfully drilled
three-run lateral well in the Three Forks Formation from a surface location in
Section 31, T153N, R99W (2051' FNL & 1024' FWL) to a total depth (TD) of
22444' MD, 10865' TVD (-8921' SS), with a bottom hole location in Section 10,
T152N, R100W (239' FSL & 1324' FWL) in Williams County, North Dakota. The
well was drilled from the intermediate casing shoe to TD with three trips. The first
trip was made for a MWD at 14285' MD, on November 6, 2014. The second trip
at 15151' MD was made for MWD on November 8, 2014. The third trip was made
for a motor at 15529' MD on November 9, 2014. A total of 11297' MD was made
in the lateral, from casing point to TD. Total depth was reached at 17:35 hours on
November 15, 2014. The Alfsvaag 3-31H1 required twenty total days of drilling
and setting intermediate casing.
2.) Ultraviolet sample shows in this lateral would be rated as fair to good with
moderately fast to immediate milky cuts with residual rings. Total gas shows and
overall porosity would also be rated as fair to good. Wellbore placement was
within the target zone for approximately 85% of the total 11297' of lateral section,
which should allow for excellent production opportunities.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
Thank you for your cooperation.
