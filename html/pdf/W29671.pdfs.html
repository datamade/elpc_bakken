Well Synopsis
Thronson Federal 6-21H2 Cyclone 2
Three Forks B2
Well Plan:
The Thronson Federal 6-21H2 was spud on December 1st, 2014 at 08:30 hours, with a surface
hole location of 280 FNL and 1185 FWL in the NWNW corner of Section 21 - Township 154
North and Range 94 West in Mountrail Co., North Dakota. This well was operated by
Continental Resources Incorporated with the objective target of the late Devonian Three
Forks B2. The well plan showed a build section with a kickoff point of 10348 MD in the
Mississippian Lodgepole with a 13°/100 build rate to the landing point of 11037 MD; 10789
TVD, approximately 80 below the Three Forks Formation. The plan was to drill lateral for an
estimated 15065 to the hardline in the SWSW, of section 33 - Township 154 North and Range
94 West in McKenzie Co., North Dakota, following the estimated dip of 89.62°. The projected
well path is to the South with a proposed azimuth of 179.94°.
The offsets provided were the Thronson 2-28AH & Sorenson 2-21AH all operated by
Continental Resources Inc.
Gas logged in the vertical and lateral sections were monitored using Mud Logging Systems
Mlogger & Mcontrol. (Primary Logger: ML-098 Backup Logger ML-070)
M-Logger CC & TC filaments calibrated with 1% and 100% test gas Chromatograph
calibrated with 1% test gas (gas units).
Build Section:
The build section was kicked off @ 10366 MD on December 5th, 2014 at 21:30 hours, In the
Lodgepole formation. The up hole markers logged were the Base of the Last Salt (8940 TVD (-
6848 ss)), Mission Canyon (9139 TVD (-7047 ss)), and the Lodgepole (9808 TVD (-7716 ss)).
The build section started on December 6th, 2014 at 08:37 hours, w/ a (BHA) bottom hole
assembly consisting of a 6.75 Mpact XXL Performance motor with a 2.5° fxd bend and a
Security 8 3/4 Smith MMD55M PDC Bit. This assembly able to generate sufficient enough
build rates to land in the approximate target zone. The down hole markers below kick off
point consisted of the False Bakken (10555 TVD (-8463 ss)), Upper Bakken Shale (10569 TVD
(-8477 ss)), Middle Bakken Member (10593 TVD (-8501 ss)), Lower Bakken Shale (10658
TVD (-8566 ss)), Three Forks Formation (10716 TVD (-8624 ss)) & Three Forks Internal 1/B2
(10760 TVD (-8668 ss)). These down hole markers came 3-8 low versus the prognosis and
offset logs. The estimated landing was calculated @ 10789 TVD, 81 into Three Forks
Formation, which was adjusted to 10785 TVD due to a shallower landing zone putting the
landing roughly 65 into the Three Forks versus 81 . (23.5 below Three Fks Int 1/B2) The
landing for 7 intermediate casing was completed on December 7th, 2014 at 01:00 hours,
about 67.5 into the Three Forks Formation, with a landing at 11047 MD; 10783.52 TVD. The
7 casing was set @ 11038 MD; 10783.29 TVD, with a location of: 711 FNL & 1194 FWL
NWNW Section 21: T154N-R94W Mountrail Co., ND. The build section consisted of one
assembly, 681 total footage drilled w/ 545 sliding (9.83 hrs) 136 rotating (2.0 hrs). (See
Thronson Federal 6-21H2 Build, TVD log & Formation Tops for more detailed information)
Gas observed in the build section showed gas ranging 15-402 units through the Lodgepole
formation, averaging 72 units with faint light brown/black oil stains with faint, moderately
good milky-cloudy invert cut. The Upper & Lower Bakken Shale s showed moderate amounts
of gas resulting in 185-2049 units. The Middle Bakken Member ranged from 69-481 units,
averaging 322 units & displayed fair to good brown/black oil stains with fair-good milky-
cloudy invert cut. The Three Forks formation resulted 106-1168 units, averaging 513 units and
485-2890 units while circulating out trip gas after the landing. Three Forks samples also
included poor-fair amounts of brown/black oil stains, with fair immediate moderately fast
cloudy blue/white Invert cut. (See Thronson Federal 6-21H2 Build log for more detailed gas
data)
Lateral Section:
Penetration of the lateral section started on December 9th, 2014 at 07:00 hrs, with a depth of
11047 MD, drilling with a 6 Security MM64 PDC bit and 4.75, 5/6 lobe 8.3 stage 1.5° bend
RPG 1.04 Mpact XXL Performance mud motor. The plan was to drill in the target Zone and
follow the 15-20 zone of interest below the Three Fks Internal 1/B2, locating the best sample
oil & sample shows. The lateral section was drilled from 11047 MD to 26084 MD for a lateral
length of 15037 , with a bottom hole location: 79 FSL & 1199 FWL SWSW Sec 33, T154N-
R94W, McKenzie Co., ND completed on December 20th, 2014 20:16 hours. (26084 MD;
10854.65 TVD). The lateral section consisted of 15037 (192.42 hrs) total footage drilled w/
853 sliding (56.25 hrs) 14184 rotating (136.17 hrs), 790 of the lateral was drilled out of
zone into the Internal 1/B2 upper section top. The well consisted of one complete trip for a
BHA (21009 MD; 10858.6 TVD) & two successful wire-line attempts: (13509 MD; 10803.8
TVD), (15420 MD; 10825 TVD)
Samples collected in the lateral section mostly consisted of light-medium brown, abundant
dark brown, light-medium tan/brown, buff tan, mottled brown/tan, light-medium pink, dark
gray/brown, off white Dolomite/Limestone consisting of trace amounts of Pyrite, mainly in
our upper target zone. Samples also consisted of blue/green, gray/green shale,
predominately in the upper target zone and internal 1 shale. The middle-lower zone
displayed predominately darker brown, tan/brown clean Dolomite, below the Internal 1
Shale, consisting of fair to good porosity. This targeted zone displayed 5-15% light-medium
pale greenish/yellow & light-medium brown oil on shakers. Samples also displayed light-
medium black/brown oil stains and fair to bright green/yellow fluorescence with fair milky-
cloudy dull blooming/streaming blue/white cut.
Gas averaged 1130 units and ranged from 112 units to 6836 units of gas. Connection gasses
ranged from 724 units to 5434 units and trip gas ranged from 713 units to 9550 units with 20-
30 gas flare. Gas in the clean Dolomite zone above the Internal 2/B3 produced more gas & oil
shows versus drilling the upper target zone and close to the Internal 1/B2 zone. Oil shows
were pretty consistent throughout the lateral section, but displayed more in the lower drilling
zones. (For more detailed sample descriptions & gas data see Thronson Federal 6-21H2
Horizontal Log)
The formation structure was calculated using distinct cool zones, one zone located 2-3 above
the Internal 2/B3, displaying gamma 55-70 (API). This was the cleanest zone in the drilling
target and also resulted in better gas & sample shows. The middle drilling zone consisted of
clean gamma ranging from 72-85 (API). The two distinct hot gamma zones were present
above the upper target towards the Internal 1/B2 ranging from 115-162 (API) and the Internal
2/B3 which ranged from 110-121 (API). Both hot gamma zones displayed reasonable amounts
of gas and shows but mainly resulted in erratic build rates of 1.5 degrees per connection. The
drilling was best in the cleaner gamma zones where the wellbore tracked really well resulting
in 4 to 5 stands of rotation per slide. The Formation dropped about 73 from start to about
22400 MD (89.63°) and then climbed 12 to total depth of 26084 MD (90.17°). (89.77°
Average Lateral Dip Angle). (For more detailed gamma signatures and structure please see
Thronson Federal 6-21H2 cross section)

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
