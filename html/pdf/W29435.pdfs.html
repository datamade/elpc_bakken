WELL EVALUATION
View of Cyclone 5 while drilling in lateral Vixen Federal 1 SLH in Mountrail County, North Dakota

Introduction
The Slawson Exploration Company, Inc. Vixen Federal 1 SLH [Lot 4, Section 18, T152N,
R91W] is located 4 miles east of New Town in Mountrail County, North Dakota. The location
lies within the Big Bend field of the Williston Basin, where Slawson Exploration Company, Inc.
is producing from the Middle Member of the Bakken Formation and using hydraulic fracturing
techniques to create new fractures and enhance natural fractures to draw from the pool.
The Vixen Federal 1 SLH was planned as a two section lateral extending 10,113' at a heading of
185.18° from the SW quarter of the SW quadrant of Section 18, T152N, R92W to the SW quarter
of the SW quadrant of Section 30, T152N, R91W.
OFFSET WELLS
The primary offset well used to develop a prognosis of formation tops was the Slawson Exploration
Company, Inc. Vixen Federal 2-19-30H, which is 50 feet west of the subject well. The Slawson
Exploration Company, Inc., Phoenix 1-18H (0.5 miles east) and the Loon Federal 1-24-25H, (0.75
miles southwest) were also used as offset wells. Each of these wells played an intricate part in
landing of the build section for lateral operations.
ENGINEERING
The surface hole was spud October 21, 2014 and drilled to a depth of 1,820' MD with two 13 ½"
PDC bits in a time of 15.5 hours. Surface casing was set to 1,788' MD and consisted of 39 joints
of 9 5/8" 36# J-55. The vertical hole was drilled with two 8 ¾" bits to a KOP depth of 9,300' MD.
Weatherford provided directional drilling and MWD personnel, tools, and equipment. MWD
surveys from the vertical hole provided survey tie-in data for curve and lateral surveys. BHA #5
with a fixed 2.38° fixed motor drilled 1,385' of curve in 45.2 hours with a landing 30' below the
base of the Upper Bakken Shale in the Middle Bakken Formation. The curve was landed at a
measured depth of 10,685' at approximately 9,887' TVD with a projected inclination of 89.72°.
An intermediate casing string consisting of 249 joints of 7" 32# HCP-110 was then set to 10,622'
MD and cemented.
The lateral began with BHA #6, drilling 7,030' in 102 hours before being pulled for high torque.
This assembly was replaced by BHA #7, which drilled the remainder of the lateral (3,002' in 48
hours). Total depth of 20,717' MD was reached at 14:30 CT on November 21, 2014. The bottom
hole location is 263.29' FSL and 13.68' FEL, SE SE Section 25, T152N, R91W. Projected to TD,
the wellbore is 10,701.55' south and 984.68' west of the surface location. Freshwater based drilling
fluid was used while drilling the surface hole, oil-based invert drilling fluid was utilized for the
vertical and curve, while both saltwater and oil-based drilling fluid were used for the lateral
portion.
GEOLOGY
Methods
Geologic supervision was provided by a Sunburst Consulting, Inc. geology team, which logged
drill cuttings, monitored mud gas, and provided on-site geo-steering consultation.
Geologic
evaluation began at 7,200' MD and continued until the well reached a total depth of 20,717' MD.
Rig crew members caught 30' lagged samples under direction of the well site geologists. Cuttings
were examined under a binocular microscope, while cut fluorescence was observed under UV light
with trichloroethylene (Carbo-Sol) cutting agent. Lithologic descriptions are provided as an
appendix to this report. A digital gas detector, (MLogger total gas with chromatograph), was
interfaced with a Pason electronic data recorder system. Pason provided depth, drill rates, and
pump information to the gas detection computer and received total gas information for viewing
around location. Total gas was recorded as units (100 units = 1% methane equivalent in air) and
chromatography of C1­C4 components were calculated in parts-per-million.

formation was identified by a white to off white, cream, microcrystalline, soft, earthy limestone,
and an off-white to white, trace light gray, microcrystalline, firm, massive, earthy anhydrite
(Figure 1).

The Upper Bakken Shale was drilled at 10,049' MD, 9,846' TVD (-7,880') and consisted of a

The Middle Bakken was penetrated at 10,120' MD, 9,865' TVD (-7,899'). This Bakken member

Gas shows (C1-C4) in the target zone were low throughout the lateral, with background gases
slowly rising and/or falling depending upon position within the target interval. Background gases
were consistently around 300 to 400 units early in the lateral rising to 600-800 units later in the
lateral. However, when the wellbore exited the base of the target interval, background gases
dropped into the 300-400 unit range until reaching total depth (Figure 8). These gas levels were
recorded against 9.7 to 10.2 lb/gal mud. Traces of light brown spotty oil staining were observed in
samples throughout the lateral.

Geo-steering
The target zone was defined by the Slawson Exploration Company, Inc. project geologist as a 12'
thick interval beginning approximately 10' below the base of the Lower Bakken Shale in an area
of increased porosity. A TVD type log, created by the Weatherford MWD (Figure 9), was utilized
by Sunburst Consulting geologists to correlate the gamma ray signature of formation markers
during the steering of the lateral in order to determine stratigraphic position and local apparent dip.
In addition, samples, drilling parameters, type logs, and gas shows were utilized as tools to aid in
the wellbore's stratigraphic position.
Figure 9. Vixen Federal 1 SLH target interval (Modified by Gerald Jett, Sunburst Consulting, Inc. geologist).
The drilling plan was to land 26' below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale within the area of
higher porosity. The wellbore was actually landed 4' lower. From the target entry to TD a gross
apparent dip of 0.10° down was interpreted (Figure 10). On four occasions the wellbore exited the
base of zone; the first at 11,113' MD where the wellbore exited the base of the target and remained
out of zone until 11,747' MD. The second occurred at 13,001' MD and remained out of zone until
13,612' MD; and the third exit at 15,058' MD before re-entering at 16,240' MD. The final exit out
the bottom of the target zone occurred at 17,638' MD, where it remained until reaching total depth
at 20,717' MD.
Figure 10. Cross sectional interpretation of dip illustrating gas, MWD gamma ray and ROP (Gerald Jett, Sunburst
Consulting, Inc.).
SUMMARY
1)
Vixen Federal 1 SLH was drilled to 20,717' MD with a lateral exposure of 10,095'.
2)
The well spud on October 21, 2014 and consisted of the surface, vertical, and curve
operations. The well was drilled from surface to total depth utilizing seven bits in 314.7
drilling hours. The total depth was reached after 32 drilling days on November 21, 2014.
3)
Diesel-based drilling fluid (8.50 to 11.00 lb/gal) was utilized from surface casing to a curve
landing of 10,685' MD. Both saltwater based and oil-base drilling fluid (9.60 to 10.20
lb/gal) were successfully utilized throughout lateral portion of the well.
4)
The wellbore exited the base of the target interval on four separate occasions. The wellbore
remained within the target zone ~36% of the lateral. Overall apparent dip of the target zone
was estimated at 0.10° down.
5)
Background gases (C1-C4) were consistently around 200 to 300 units through the early part
of the lateral, rising to 600-800 units later in the lateral. Background gases declined to 200-
400 units when the well bore was below the target zone. This pattern remained consistent
to TD.

Dear Operator,
North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all
applicable local, state, and federal laws and regulations.
Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.
Effective June 1, 2014, a container must be maintained on each well drilled in North Dakota beginning when the
well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container
If the operator will not utilize a filtration system, a waiver to the container requirement will be considered, but
only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.
As previously stated in our March 13, 2014 letter, North Dakota Administrative Code Section 33-20-02.1-01
states in part that every person who transports solid waste (which includes oil and gas exploration and production
wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Pre-Frac Testing Procedure
7" casing testing procedure
a. Run bond log to top of L/H/P and ensure adequate cement coverage.
b. Run casing inspection log in 7" casing from top of liner hanger to surface. De-rate casing pressure
rating as needed based on minimum wall thickness.
c. RU wireline and set a test plug in the top of the Liner/Hanger/Packer.
d. Pressure test the 7" casing and wellhead as follows:
i. Pressure test 7" casing, liner hanger, and wellhead to anticipated frac pressure for 30 minutes
with less than a 5% loss. Do not exceed 85% of the API pressure rating nor the de-rated
pressure rating of the 7" casing.
ii. If anticipated frac pressure exceeds the de-rated pressure rating then run a 4-1/2" frac string and
land into PBR. Pressure test 4-1/2" x 7" annulus to 1500 psi after landing frac string.
