Well Plan:
The Sorenson 6-16H2 was spud on January 19th, 2015 at 07:49 hours, with a surface hole
location of 280 FNL and 1275 FWL in the NWNW corner of Section 21 - Township 154 North
and Range 94 West in Mountrail Co., North Dakota. This well was operated by Continental
Resources Incorporated with the objective target of the late Devonian Three Forks B2. The
well plan showed a build section with a kickoff point of 10348 MD in the Mississippian
Lodgepole with a 13°/100 build rate to the landing point of 11044 MD; 10789 TVD,
approximately 80 below the Three Forks Formation. The well plan was later changed to land
64 into the Three Forks 22 below the Three Forks Internal 1/B2. This changed the
estimated KOP to 10310, requiring 12°/100 . The plan was to drill lateral for an estimated
4925 to the hardline in the NWNW, of section 16 - Township 154 North and Range 94 West in
Mountrail Co., North Dakota, following the estimated dip of 90.47°. The projected well path is
to the North with a proposed azimuth of 359.2°.
The offsets provided were the Thronson Federal 6-21H2 & Thronson Federal 7-21H both
operated by Continental Resources Inc.
Gas logged in the vertical and lateral sections were monitored using Mud Logging Systems
Mlogger & Mcontrol. (Primary Logger: ML-098 Backup Logger ML-070)
M-Logger CC & TC filaments calibrated with 1% and 100% test gas Chromatograph
calibrated with 1% test gas (gas units).
Build Section:
The build section was kicked off @ 10310 MD on January 22nd, 2015 at 20:25 hours, In the
Lodgepole formation. The up hole markers logged were the Base of the Last Salt
(8936 TVD (-6844 ss)), Mission Canyon (9134 TVD (-7042 ss)), and the Lodgepole (9798 TVD
(-7706 ss)). The BLS & Mission Canyon came within a few feet from the Thronson 6 & 7, but
the Lodgepole marker had a variation of 4 to 10 feet high versus these wells. The landing
target was left @ 10780 TVD. The build section started on January 23rd, 2015 at 17:20 hours,
w/ a (BHA) bottom hole assembly consisting of a 6.75 Mpact XXL Performance motor with a
2.38° fxd bend and a Security 8 3/4 Smith MMD55D PDC Bit. This assembly able to generate
sufficient enough build rates to land in the approximate target zone. The down hole markers
below kick off point consisted of the False Bakken (10550 TVD (-8458 ss)), Upper Bakken
Shale (10564 TVD (-8472 ss)), Middle Bakken Member (10589 TVD (-8497 ss)), Lower
Bakken Shale (10650 TVD (-8558 ss)), Three Forks Formation (10707 TVD (-8615 ss)) &
Three Forks Internal 1/B2 (10749 TVD (-8657 ss)). These down hole markers came 2-10 high
versus the prognosis and offset logs. The estimated landing was calculated @ 10780 TVD, 64
into Three Forks Formation, which was adjusted to 10776 TVD due to higher formation
markers. The landing for 7 intermediate casing was completed on January 24th, 2015 at
10:30 hours, about 69 into the Three Forks Formation, with a landing at 11100 MD;
10776.01 TVD. The 7 casing was set @ 11086 MD; 10775.55 TVD, with a location of: 239
FSL & 1215 FWL SWSW Section 16: T154N-R94W Mountrail Co., ND. The build section
consisted of one assembly, 790 (12.67 hrs) total footage drilled w/ 667 sliding (10.25 hrs)
123 rotating (2.42 hrs). (See Sorenson 6-16H2 Build, TVD log & Formation Tops for more
detailed information)
Gas observed in the build section showed gas ranging 14-487 units through the Lodgepole
formation, averaging 71 units with faint light brown/black oil stains with faint, moderately
good milky-cloudy invert cut. The Upper & Lower Bakken Shale s showed moderate amounts
of gas resulting in 157-2846 units. The Middle Bakken Member ranged from 99-638 units,
averaging 301 units & displayed fair to good brown/black oil stains with fair-good milky-
cloudy invert cut. The Three Forks formation resulted 201-1340 units, averaging 650 units and
360-3204 units while circulating a bottoms up after the landing, connection Gasses
throughout the landing ranged from 147-2341 units. Three Forks samples also included poor-
fair amounts of brown/black oil stains, with fair immediate moderately fast cloudy
blue/white Invert cut. (See Sorenson 6-16H2 Build log for more detailed gas data & sample
shows)
Lateral Section:
Penetration of the lateral section started on January 26th, 2015 at 11:20 hrs, with a depth of
11100 MD, drilling with a 6 REED SKH711M PDC bit and 5 , 7/8 lobe 8.2 stage 1.5° FXD
Discovery SuperHawk 500 mud motor. The plan was to drill in the target Zone and follow the
13 zone of interest below the Three Fks Internal 1/B2, locating the best sample oil & sample
shows. The lateral section was drilled from 11100 MD to 15900 MD for a lateral length of
4800 , with a bottom hole location: 237 FNL & 1180 FWL NWNW Sec 16, T154N-R94W,
Mountrail Co., ND completed on January 29th, 2015 06:21 hours. (15900 MD; 10743.98
TVD). The lateral section consisted of 4800 (45.25 hrs) total footage drilled w/ 341 sliding
(14.5 hrs) 4459 rotating (30.75 hrs). The well consisted of one complete trip for a BHA
(13764 MD; 10770 TVD).
Samples collected in the lateral section mostly consisted of light-medium brown, abundant
dark brown, light-medium tan/brown, buff tan, mottled brown/tan, light-medium pink, dark
gray/brown, off white Dolomite/Limestone consisting of trace amounts of Pyrite, mainly in
our upper target zone. Samples also consisted of blue/green, gray/green shale & slight traces
of light orange/amber Chert, predominately in the lower target zone. The middle-lower zone
displayed predominately darker brown, tan/brown clean Dolomite, below the Internal 1
Shale, consisting of fair to good porosity. This targeted zone displayed 5% light-medium pale
greenish/yellow & light-medium brown oil on shakers. Samples also displayed light-medium
black/brown oil stains and poor dull yellow/green fluorescence with moderately fair milky-
cloudy dull streaming blue/white cut.
Gas averaged 599 units and ranged from 109 units to 3590 units of gas. Connection gasses
ranged from 147 units to 3209 units and trip gas ranged from 207 units to 6108 units with 10-
20 gas flare. Oil shows were pretty consistent throughout the lateral section, but displayed
more in the lower drilling zones. (For more detailed sample descriptions & gas data see
Sorenson 6-16H2 Horizontal Log)
The formation structure was calculated using distinct cool zones, one zone located 2-3 above
the Internal 2/B3, displaying gamma 47-56 (API). This was the cleanest zone in the drilling
target and also resulted in better gas & sample shows. The middle drilling zone consisted of
clean gamma ranging from 72-85 (API) and was very erratic. The two distinct hot gamma
zones were present below the lower target above the clean gamma zone and a distinct hot
zone roughly 2 above the lower target zone. Both hot gamma zones displayed small amounts
of gas and shows, and also resulted in harder drilling with a more consolidated formation.
These zones ranged from 75-117 (API) and also displayed erratic gamma counts. The drilling
was best just above the cleaner gamma zones w here the wellbore tracked really well
resulting in 4 to 5 stands of rotation per slide. The Formation ascended about 41 throughout
the lateral. (90.42°). (For more detailed gamma signatures and structure please see Sorenson
6-16H2 cross section)

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with
exploration or production of oil and gas must be properly disposed of in an authorized facility in accord with all

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very
concerned about the proper disposal of used filters (including filter socks) used by the oil and gas industry.

well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations

leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.

Continental Resources standard CCL, CBL, 4-1/2" liner running and testing procedures
Continental Resources' standard practice for running the cement bond log and casing
caliper log is to run both logs immediately after coming out of the hole after TD, prior to running
the 4-1/2" liner, to the deepest depth obtainable; however, if there are well control concerns that
require us to run the liner sooner, only the CBL will be run and the CCL will be run after setting
the liner.
Based on the CCL results, we determine the actual API minimum burst allowance for the 7"
casing. If the downgraded API burst pressure is below our minimum required frac pressures, we
will run a 4-1/2" frac string; if severe wear or holes are found in the casing, we will run a 5"
cemented, to surface, tie back string.
The CBL log is run in order to determine the top of cement, as required by the NDIC.
Our current 4-1/2" liner program for a 1280 unit is 30, evenly spaced, stages with 29 swellable
packers. The liner shoe is set approximately 180 feet off bottom. The shoe stage below the last
packer has 2 joints, a double valved float, one joint, and a ported guide shoe ­ appx 130 ft. The
liner is run using a running tool on the end of 4" DP. The 7" packer/hanger is set about 40 ft
above KOP between two casing collars but conditions occasionally occur that require setting
higher, either through unexpected failure or in order to isolate casing wear close to KOP.
Recently we have tried 40 stage liners and the trend to explore the optimum stage count will
continue.. Once the liner is at depth, a ball is dropped through the DP, the ball is pressured up
against the setting tool to approximately 2500 psi, and the 7" packer/hanger is set.
A push pull test is done to confirm the hanger has set. Then, a 4500 psi pressure test is
completed on the back side of the 4" DP to confirm the packer has set. The setting tool is then
backed off and the 4" DP/running tool is laid down.
Immediately after the rotary rig has been moved off the well location, the 7" csg and liner
packer/ hanger are tested to the frac pressure. The testers will rig up and test the tubing head to
5000 psi. Next a test plug will be run and set, using wire line, in the top of the 7" packer/hanger.
Testers will pressure up to our frac pressure, typically 8500 psi, to confirm the 7" is ready for
completion.
