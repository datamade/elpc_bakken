1.0 Introduction
The P Levang 14-13-23-24LL is a horizontal well targeting the Three Forks. The surface location
of the well is in the southeast quarter of the southeast quarter of Section 11, Township 150 North,
Range 95 West, in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Lateral Section of the well enters the
objective Three Forks at 11109' MD, 10,858.3' TVD (-8,584.3' Subsea) in the southeast quarter of
the southeast quarter of Section 11 and reaches its total depth of 21,155' MD, 10,893.85' TVD (-
8,619.8' Subsea) in the southwest quarter of the southwest quarter of 24, Township 150 North,
Range 95 West. The total length of the Lateral Section, starting at the casing point of 11,222' MD,
is 9,933' of which 9,619' (96.8%) is in the objective Three Forks formation. The overall dip was
approximately 89.87º and its thickness is approximately 47' based on measured interval
thicknesses within the formation.
The path in the Build Section of the P Levang 14-13-23-24LL well is southeast for a short
distance before heading south, targeting an azimuth of 180.04º overall from the surface location
in section 11. The well path continues in a southern direction to the well's total depth of 21,155'
in the southwest quarter of the southwest quarter of Section 24, targeting an azimuth of 179.80º.

NOTES ON 9-5/8" SURFACE CASING DESIGN:
The key point in designing surface casing is to extend the shoe of the surface casing beyond the top of
the Pierre Shale. This ensures that all potable or usable water zones are covered. At this point in the well
design, the drilling fluid is changed to oil base mud to contend with salt sections that occur at
approximately 6,700' TVD and 9,050' TVD. The 36 ppf J55 casing accomplishes these criteria easily.
A higher burst rating is not necessary based on industry standards throughout the Williston Basin.
In the event the surface casing burst rating was increased, the normally pressured and sub normally
pressured zones all the way thru the Madison Group at approximately 8,400' TVD has the potential to
break down. Therefore the increased pressure rating of surface casing is not warranted. It is not feasible
to set the surface casing point at a greater depth because the shut in surface pressures would not
appreciably increase. The Dakota formation (5,000' TVD) is frequently weak. Typically, we drill the
intermediate section with 10.5 ppg OBM to the 7" casing point. In the event we lost circulation in the
exposed intermediate interval, the wellbore will not be entirely evacuated and the surface casing can be
shut in without compromising the pressure rating of the 36 ppf J-55.
