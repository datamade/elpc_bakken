GEOLOGY
Lithology
Lithologic descriptions and sample analysis began in the Charles Formation at 9,340' MD to log
the Base Last Salt for curve landing purposes.
The Middle Bakken was logged at 11,040' TVD (-8,851' MSL). It consists primarily of silty
sandstone and contains trace to rare amounts of spotty, medium brown oil stain. The Middle
Bakken was the target reservoir for the lateral portion of the well.
Gas Monitoring
While drilling the lateral, background gas values initially fluctuated between 400u and 800u and
dropped to 200u-400u while drilling in the lower half of zone. When venting through the gas
buster, gas values ranged from 200u to 600u. Drilling fluid was circulated through the gas buster
starting at 13,588' MD, and continued throughout the lateral producing an intermittent flare that
ranged from 4' to 8'. The highest gas values recorded for the well were a trip gas of 5,350u, and
a connection gas of 2,071u. The upper half of the ideal target zone was observed to contain the
highest gas concentrations (Figure 2).

SUMMARY
The XTO Energy, Inc. Johnson 24X-31A was spud on July 21, 2014 by a work over rig, and
officially reached TD in 19 total drilling days October 15, 2014. The well was called TD short of
plan due to motor failure. Samples from the Middle Bakken silty sandstone target contained oil
staining that was described as trace to rare with visible intergranular porosity. MWD gamma ray
markers, sample analysis, and ROP were successfully utilized to expose 88% of the lateral
borehole to the ideal Middle Bakken target. The Johnson 24X-31A awaits completion operations
to maximize its production potential.

North Dakota Administrative Code Section 43-02-03-19.2 states in part that all waste material associated with

Filtration systems are commonly used during oil and gas operations in North Dakota. The Commission is very

well is spud and must remain on-site during clean-out, completion, and flow-back whenever filtration operations
are conducted. The on-site container must be used to store filters until they can be properly disposed of in an
authorized facility. Such containers must be:
leakproof to prevent any fluids from escaping the container
covered to prevent precipitation from entering the container
placard to indicate only filters are to be placed in the container

only upon the operator submitting a Sundry Notice (Form 4) justifying their request.

wastes) is required to have a valid permit issued by the North Dakota Department of Health, Division of Waste
Management. Please contact the Division of Waste Management at (701) 328-5166 with any questions on the
solid waste program. Note oil and gas exploration and production wastes include produced water, drilling mud,
invert mud, tank bottom sediment, pipe scale, filters, and fly ash.
