Curve 10,450' —— 11,293 ’ MD
The curve for the South lateral was started in the Lodgepole Formation at a measured depth of 10,450' at 11:00 am. CST on November 2, 2014. An 8 3/4” Hughes bit and 2.25 degree ﬁxed mud motor were used to build the curve. The landing target for the Skedsvold #21-4H at the beginning of the curve was set at 10,949’ @ 90 deg. based oﬁ‘ the Lodgepole top and the ﬁrst LP marker. The Skedsvold # 150-101-4B-9—1H and the Skedsvold #21—4—3H were used for correlation purposes through the curve. Penetration rates at the beginning of the curve averaged around 70 - 80 ﬁ/hr while sliding. Background gases through the Lodgepole formation averaged around 85 — 100 units. The lithology of the Lodgepole exhibited primarily a cream gray tan/light to medium gray limestone with occasional anhydrite and shale present. Gamma markers began to show that the lower portion of the Lodgepole had thickened slightly. After seeing this, the TVD landing target was lowered to a TVD of 10,950’. The remainder of the Lodgepole formation was drilled out and the False Bakken was encountered.
