Base of Last Salt 8,436 ’ MD 8,434 ’ T VD -6,528’ SS
The depth of the Base of the Last Salt (BLS) was encountered at a true vertical depth (TVD) of 8,434’. This depth was determined by drilling signatures and analysis of samples. Above the Last Salt, the Greenpoint Anhydrite showed distinctive slower drilling which was useful in determining stratigraphic position. ROP through the last salt bench averaged lOOfph.
The kick off point (KOP) for the curve and a preliminary target for the curve landing were calculated using the depth of the BLS. An estimated isopach thickness of 1,642’ from the BLS to the expected Middle Bakken target provided a preliminary target of 10,07 6’ TVD and the planned curve radius of 477’ provided a KOP of 9,5 99’ TVD (actual KOP was last kelly down before the calculated depth). The calculations were based on landing the curve at the target base.
Mission Canyon 8,646 ’ MD 8,644 ’ T VD -6, 753’ SS
The Mission Canyon Formation was seen at 8,646’ MD. Its depth was estimated from drilling signatures. Subtle changes in lithology were also useﬁil in determining the depth of the Mission Canyon. ROP in the formation averaged 69fph while formation gas averaged 160 units. The highest gas readings were detected high in the formation directly below the impermeable anhydrite units. Near the top of the formation, samples were less carbonaceous and signiﬁcantly more anhydritic than underlying stratum. The rate of penetration was slightly slower in the anhydrite units. Samples showed subtle changes in clay and carbon content several times before reaching the top of the Lodgepole Formation.
Lodgepole 9,226 ’ MD 9,224 ’ T VD -7,318 ’ SS

The depth of the Lodgepole Formation was encountered at a MD of 9226’. As the Lodgepole

was encountered, a 11thology change trom a brown carbonaceous limestone to a predominantly gray crystalline and argillaceous limestone was identiﬁed. It was not only the subtle changes in lithology and drilling rates that deﬁned the formation but also differential pressure, rotary, torque and weight on bit. Drill rates through the formation in the vertical segment averaged 70fph while gas averaged 140units with a range from 107 units to 205 units. streaming cuts. Shortly after encountering the Lodgepole, KOP was reached at 11:00 am CDT on August 31, 2014 at a measured depth of 9,581’.
