THE CURVE
The Hansen 44-28—3H curve was drilled with a Security MMDSSM 8.75” bit along with a Baker Hughes mud motor set to 2.4 degrees and an invert mud system. There was a 30’ rotation made from this point before the sliding for the curve commenced at the kick off point of 9,870’ MD.
FALSE BAKKEN 10,400’ MD 10,309’ T VD -8,115’ SS

At 10,400’ MD the False Bakken came in at 10,309’ TVD, 1’ high to prognosis. The gamma reached 145.88 API units while in the False Bakken. The samples for the False Bakken consisted of cream gray to gray tan, with blocky to platy limestone. The samples had weak milky yellow cuts with a peak of 180 units of gas seen in this interval.
SCALLION 10,407’ 1WD 10,312’ TVD -8,118’ SS
The Scallion was the next formation encountered. The scallion came in at 10,407 ’ MD and 10,3 12’ TVD, 1’ high to prognosis The gamma cooled back down to 43.77 API units in the Scallion. The Scallion consisted of mostly cream buﬁ‘ Limestone with slow dull milky yellow cuts. Gas peaked at 847 units of gas along with fair milky cuts.
UPPER BAKKEN 10,432’ MD 10,324’ TVD -8,130’ SS
The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,432’ MD, and 10,324’ TVD, this was 3’ high to the prognosis. The gamma reached 617.54 units at this point. Background gas averaged 300.59 units, peaking 673 units. Samples in the Upper Bakken were black, carbonaceous blocky shale, with instant milky yellow cuts.

MDDLE BAKKEN

The Middle Bakken was encountered at a MD of 10,483’ MD and 10,346’ TVD, 1’ low to the prognosis. The Middle Bakken was picked based on the change in samples and a decrease in gamma from 617.54 units down to 61.11 units API. The Middle Bakken samples consisted mostly of a light to dark cream gray tan platy siltstone. Gas peaked at 7296 units of gas along with fair milky cuts. The curve was landed at a total depth of 10,725’ MD at 10,385’ TVD on Sunday, October 12, 2014 at 1:30 PM CST. 7” intermediate casing was set to 10, 708’ MD.
