Middle Bakken Dolomite
The Middle Bakken top was picked from sample lithology, Gamma, and

penetration rate at 10777’ MD, 10560’ TVD, (-8359’ SS).

Middle Bakken Lithology Descriptions Composite samples were caught in 100’ intervals through the Middle
Bakken from 10777' (MD) to 20566’ (MDfl‘ D) by manually catching cuttings from the shakers.
The middle Bakken member was dominantly light gray to tan with some light to med brown grading to gray/brown limey dolostone. White with varying opacity mottling due to calcite inclusions was observed. White and light brown micro laminations were also present. The shapes of the cuttings were largely blocky to sub-blocky and occasionally sub-platy. The texture was dominantly a grainstone, varying from silty to very ﬁne grain crystalline. Visible porosity was generally very poor to poor. Trace to small amounts of disseminated pyrite with occasional nodules was observed. Overall, calcareous/dolomitic cementing in parts was observed.

Vertical wellbore position in formation gave no overly inclusive rule to

composition of color, accessory minerals or availability of visual porosity. Texture ranged from being a mudstone to a wackestone, wackestone texture prevailed higher in zone near packstone lithology. Samples ranged from shades of gray/browns to off whites to tans.
