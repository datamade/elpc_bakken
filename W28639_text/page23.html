Way Point 4 was then adjusted upward from 14,650 — 15,146’ MD, with a target window of 9,322 — 9,332’ TVD between these two points. This was done to attempt to encounter the GR Marker once again to determine formation dip, but the marker was not witnessed again before TD was reached just beyond Way Point 4, at 15,231’ MD. All Way Points were published by Tony Rios of ECG Resources.

The Middle Bakken dolomite throughout the entire horizontal section was typically light bluish gray to medium bluish gray to gray, spotted very light beige brown, dark gray in part, microcrystalline, argillaceous, and minimally carbonaceous. The dolomite was also generally moderately ﬁrm, with fair to good intergranular porosity and light green to dark yellow to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with mostly fast streaming cuts. ROP in the horizontal ranged from 0.20 to 4.90 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranged from 27 to 6,742 units, with max gas value within range observed at 14,907’ MD.
Conclusion
EOG Resources Parshall 92-28H reached a total depth of 15,231’ MD at 3:30 PM CST on October 2, 2014 in the Middle Bakken, and is now awaiting completion.
