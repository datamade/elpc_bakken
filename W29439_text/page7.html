The lithology at the top of the Three Forks Formation was observed to be a Dolomite (97%) that was noted to be light tan, gray-tan, light orange to peach, cream, and beige with abundant black argillaceous laminations with 3% Mudstone that was described as light sea-foam to mint green, and Pyritic. Gas shows varied but were generally considered to be poor to fair throughout most of the Three Forks Formation with values for total gas ranging from 7 to 1,237 units of total gas. The overall average gas value for the Three Forks Formation was only 404 units. The average rate of penetration through the Three Forks Formation was 0.62 minutes per foot. Oil shows were
generally good with many hazy, cloudy and some milky blooming cuts being described throughout the formation.
