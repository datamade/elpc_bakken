GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

WPX Energy’s Roggenbuck 4-9HY was drilled as a single horizontal well, targeting the upper section of the Three Forks formation. The surface location of this well is located on the SW SW corner of Section 33 Township 151N Range 92W, in Mountrail County, North Dakota. The bottom-hole location is in the SW SE comer of Section 9 Township 150N Range 92W, 249.96’ ﬁom the South line (F SL), and 1,862.64’ from the East line (FEL).
The Roggenbuck 4-9HY spud on September 4th, 2014. Neset Consulting Service, Inc. provided services that began on September 14‘“, 2014, which included mud log creation, sample descriptions, gas monitoring, and geologic steering. Gas monitoring equipment was provided and maintained by Pason Systems Corporation. Lagged samples were caught through the vertical and curve sections at 30’ intervals, and the lateral section at 100' intervals. Reached a Target Depth of 21,375’ MD on September 24th, 2014 at 2:30 PM CST.

Upper Bakken Shale 10,890’ MD 10,238’ TVD -8,186’ ss
The Upper Bakken Shale was entered at a depth of 10,890’ MD, 10,238’ TVD, which correlated to a landing of 10,383’ TVD. The Upper Bakken Shale was identiﬁed by observing a rapid and extreme increase in gamma API, going from 20 API to over 400 API, along with a slight ROP increase ﬁom 1.5 to 0.5 minutes per foot. The Shale formation was conﬁrmed by collecting and identifying samples consisting of earthy, blocky, carbonaceous dark brown to black Shale, with trace light gray interbedded Limestone, no ﬂuorescence, and an instant thick streaming milky cut.
