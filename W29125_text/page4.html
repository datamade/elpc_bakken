Dear Mr. Richard Suggs,
I appreciate your time on the phone recently discussing our request for an openhole log waiver on the permitted Bair Farm B—3526-6491 well (NDIC Permit #29125).
Cornerstone controls significant leasehold offsetting the proposed Bair Farm well, including leases in Section 25/36 164N-91W, 30/31-164N-90W and 27/34-164N-91W. Our intent is to continue to evaluate and prove the economics of
developing the Bakken reservoir in these northern-most Townships of Burke County.
With one Bakken well currently completed (Carpentier 3-34-27H in 27/34-164N-90W), the Bair Farm well is planned as a reservoir confirmation well. It is highly likely we will continue development along the US / Canada border, and Cornerstone will commit to providing an openhole logging suite in a currently non-logged area (specifically within a future Unit well in either 25/36-164N-91W or 30/31-164N-90W - whichever is drilled first). Both the aforementioned Units are well beyond a 1 mile radius of existing openhole log suites and potentially offer a more meaningful dataset, than one obtained from the proposed Bair Farm well (see attached map).

Again, I am happy to discuss further at your convenience. However as previously mentioned, Cornerstone remains in a "proof of economics mode” in this portion of Burke County. Presently our economics remain sensitive to overall well costs. We anticipate future wells will have more margin to bear additional costs associated with an openhole log suite as development and well results are refined. We therefore based on the commitments outlined above, request a waiver to the openhole logging requirement on the subject well. A Sundry request is attached.
