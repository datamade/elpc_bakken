Curve 9,810’ — 10,555’ MD
The Iverson 31-14TFHH curve section began on January 20, 2015 at 8:00pm with a KOP of 9,810’ MD. Diilling times in the Lodgepole below KOP were between 1-5 minutes sliding. Gas varied between 11-17 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the Lodgepole to correlate to the Iverson 41- 14H hole. Target stayed at
10,279’ based on Lodgepole correlations.

Middle Bakken 10,212 ’ 1WD 10,162 ’ TVD -7, 751 ’ SS
At 10,162’ TVD the Middle Bakken was encountered. This top was picked from both lithology and gamma changes. The sample lithology of the Middle Bakken consisted of sandstone. A typical sample exhibited medium to light gray sandstone, light to medium grayish brown, with beige to oﬁlwhite inclusions, very ﬁne grained, subrounded, calcareous, limey cementation, moderate sorting, poor intergranular porosity, scattered yellow ﬂuorescence, cloudy bright yellow green cut.

Three Forks 10,368’ MD 1 0,25 7’ T VD -7,846 ’ SS

At 10,257’ TVD the Three Forks was entered. The top was picked from both lithology and gamma changes. The typical samples consisted of siltstone that was medium grayish brown, light grayish brown, trace 03‘ white, arenaceous, argillaceous, dolomitic, with poor induration, Lithology also included trace to moderate shale that was medium bluish gray, waxy, platy, blocky, with moderate induration. Samples exhibited poor intergranular porosity, light brown oil staining, even to scattered dull to pale yellow green ﬂuorescence, with generally instant bright yellow to yellow-green cuts.
