Scallion 9, 820’ MD 9,384 ’ TVD -7,380’ SS Upper Bakken Shale 9,889 ’ MD 9,399 ’ TVD - 7,395 ’ SS
The Upper Bakken Shale (UBS) was encountered at 9,889’ MD/-7,395’ SS, which was initially determined from an increase in ROP when transitioning out of harder limestone into softer shale, and ﬁnally determined when gamma values dramatically increased as the gamma tool entered the UBS, with the gamma tool being 52 feet behind the drill bit. Here shale samples were generally dull black to very dark gray and very dark brown, blocky, ﬁssile, carbonaceous, spotted dark yellow to dark gray to black ﬂuorescence, with instant to fast blooming milky cuts.
The curve section landed in the Middle Bakken at an 82‘23° angle at 10,018' MD/9,419.67’ TVD on December 30, 2014 at 9:00 AM CST. The ﬁnal curve TD depth of 10,018’ MD was conﬁrmed when the bottoms up sample at this depth contained light bluish gray Middle Bakken dolomite with some very dark gray to black shale slough oﬁ‘.

Horizontal Section 1 0, 01 8 ’ — 1 6, 02 7’ MD
After the intermediate casing shoe was set to 10,015’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 8:30 AM CST on January 1, 2015. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 10,011’ MD/-7,413’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned from the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The wellbore was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near measured depth of 10,700’ MD. The formation dip angle from 10,000 to 11,000’ MD averaged 89.5 degrees, and from 11,000’ MD through the rest of the horizontal section, the dip angle ranged from 89.7 to 89.9 degrees.
