During the stabilization process we monitored moisture content and compaction with a nuclear density gauge to determine that the stabilization contractor had achieved satisfactory compaction of the soil/cement blend. We estimate that the compacted soil ranged from approximately 96 to 101 percent of the Target Density at moisture contents ranging from 12.1% to 20.4%. Upon completion of the compaction process, we tested the stabilized soils with a dynamic cone penetrometer (DCP). The DCP is used to estimate an in-situ California Bearing Ratio (CBR) of the stabilized soil, a measurement of soil strength, which can be used to determine the required
aggregate surfacing for the well pad to withstand projected future trafﬁc.

DCP readings were recorded and CBR values were estimated at approximately 24-hours and 48hours after stabilization. The results of the ﬁnal readings are attached on the Soil/Cement DCP Test Results. Review of the test results at the well pad indicates an average CBR at 48-hours was 31.1 with a standard deviation of 8.6, giving a design value of 22.4 (average minus one standard
deviation). We typically like to achieve ﬁnal CBR values of 20.0 or greater at 72-hours.

DCP readings were also performed on the soils below the soil/cement section near the lanes for the walking rig at four locations. The results of these tests are shown on the attached Subgrade
DCP Testing form.

It should be noted that the soils at the well pad were ﬁlled on top of within 72-hours of stabilization with heavy construction equipment. Scoria gravel was also being placed within this time—frame after ﬁnal stabilization of the subgrade soils. We typically recommend that the soils
be allowed cure for at least 72—hours before loading to allow the cement/soil bond to mature
