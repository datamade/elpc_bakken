Existing Lines
At the terminus of the metering, associated piping at the well’s production facilities and the 500’ of 4.50” OD low pressure gathering line noted above, a connection will be made to an existing 8.625” OD gathering line. This 8.625” OD gathering line extends approximately 2.25 miles and then connects to a 16” OD gathering line. The 16” OD gathering line extends approximately 24 miles and then connects to the inlet station piping at ONEOK’s Lodgepole compressor station. This will be the primary ﬂow path of gas delivered to ONEOK from this pad. As noted under “Gathering System”, above, the gas will have secondary ﬂow paths due to the dynamics of ONEOK’s gathering system. ONEOK is in the process of adding compressors at its Lost Bridge compressor station in Section 14, T147N, R96W, Dunn County and installing a 16” OD low pressure gathering line that will start in T145N, R94W, Dunn County (Dunn Center) and end at the inlet of the Lost Bridge compressor station. ONEOK is also planning on constructing a new discharge line from its Lost Bridge compressor station that will deliver some of the gas currently processed at ONEOK’s Grasslands Processing Plant to ONEOK’S Garden Creek Processing Plant(s).
Flowback Strategy
After the fracture treatment, these wells will be ﬂowed through a choke to test production tanks. Choke size will depend on ﬁnal shut in pressure post frac and well pressure prior to opening the choke. The choke will be adjusted up or down based on ﬂowing pressure, liquid rates and sand returns, to prevent clogging manifold chokes. During ﬂowback, the water chlorides and sand content will be monitored. When the water chlorides are greater than 100,000 ppm and the produced ﬂuids contain no sand, the wells will be turned to production facilities.
