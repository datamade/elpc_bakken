At 11,570’ measured depth, the clean gamma was seen indicating the wellbore crossed the set line. The wellbore continued to drill in the clean gamma which is located in the bottom 8’ of the target window until 11,845’ measured depth. This gamma contact point was used to estimate the dip to be 89.89 degrees. Between 11,571’ to 11,845’ the samples were generally described as a light tan sandstone with varying amounts of shale and siltstone. The overall porosity was fair to good with a milky yellow streaming cut. Drilling rates ranged from 0.23 to 1.6 minutes per foot and averaged 0.54 minutes per foot. Gamma ranged from 39 to 78 API and averaged 59 API. Total gas ranged from 180 to 466 units and averaged 369 units.
At 11,845’ measured depth, the set line was crossed and the hot ratty gamma was encountered. Drilling continued in the hotter gamma until 12,313’. Here is clean gamma was seen and used to estimate the dip to be 90.17 degrees. The wellbore continued to drill in the lower part of the target zone until 13,515’ measured depth. The interval of 12,313’ to 13,515’ rate of penetration ranged from 0.23 to 1.95 minutes per foot and averaged 0.40 minutes per foot. Total gas averaged 364 units. Gamma ranged from 41 to 106 API and averaged 67 API. The samples were generally described as sandstone: cream, cream tan, translucent, very ﬁne, subrounded, well to moderately sorted, poorly cemented, dolomitic cement; 5% shale, light blue green; scattered siltstone; rare pyrite; good porosity; scattered green ﬂuorescence, cloudy green faint streaming cut.
At 13,515’ measured depth, the wellbore crossed the set line and the hot ratty gamma was encountered. The wellbore continued to rise in true vertical depth and went out the top. Sliding succeed in bringing the wellbore back into the target window. The wellbore continued to drop in true vertical depth and crossed the set line at 13.848’ measured depth. Here is clean gamma was encountered and used to estimate the dip to be 90.31 degrees. Samples in the bottom target window were generally described as a cream sandstone with varying percentages of shale and siltstone. Overall with a good porosity and a fair streaming cut. 44 to 83 API and averaged 60 API. Total gas ranged from 152 to 466 units. Rate of penetration ranged from 0.30 to 1.57 minutes per foot and averaged 0.45 minutes per foot.
