GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation’s Solberg 41-2TFX is located in the NWNE of Section 2 T156N R97W in Williams County, North Dakota. The Solberg 41— 2TFX was drilled on a multi well pad with the Solberg 41-2H, Solberg 31-2WH, Solberg 31-2TFH, and Solberg 21—2TFH. The Solberg 41-2TFX is a horizontal well with the two mile lateral drilled south along the section line between sections 2 and 11, targeting the Three Forks. Bottom hole location is in the SESE of Section 11 T156N R97W. The Solberg 41-2TFX was spudded August 21, 2014 at 8:00 am and reached a total measured depth of 21,525’ on October 1, 2014 at 9:30 am, 18 days from spud. Wellsite geological services provided by Jessica Stark and Jen Meidinger of Neset Consulting Service began at 7,200’. A Total Gas Detection system was utilized for gas analysis. Samples were caught every thirty feet throughout the vertical and curve and every ﬁfty feet throughout the lateral, as well as intermediate samples when necessary. One set of samples was washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.

Vertical

Tyler 7,239’ MD 7,228 ’ TVD -4, 94 7’ SS
The top of the Tyler was picked using gamma. Initially samples from the Tyler consisted of orange red siltstone with scattered amounts of anhydrite and beds of purplish red shale. As drilling continued samples transitioned to medium to light gray siltstone. At the base of the Tyler samples were majority dolomite, and varying shades of red. Overall porosity in the Tyler was poor and oil shows were minimal. Rate of penetration ranged from 0.17 to 4.33 minutes per foot and averaged 0.71 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 22 to 157 units and averaged 58 units.
Kibbey Lime 7, 782’ MD 7, 770’ TVD -5,490’ SS
The Kibbey Lime marker was picked using gamma and sample. Gamma dropped considerably and samples changed to gray tan to light gray limestone.

Charles

The top of the Charles was picked by using sample and gamma. Samples changed from red orange siltstone to salt. A total of 3 salt beds, along with beds of dolomite, and anhydrite were encountered in the Charles formation. Rate of penetration in the Charles ranged from 0.17 to 0.93 minutes per foot and averaged 0.36 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 22 to 140 units and averaged 52 units.
