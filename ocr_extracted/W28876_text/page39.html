Middle Bakken

The top of the Middle Bakken was picked at a decrease in rate of penetration and gamma. The ﬁrst sample caught in the Middle Bakken was described as cream dolomite with good to fair porosity and a slow milky green cut. The low gamma values correlated with the sample collected as being in the clean “B” facies. Samples transitioned to light to medium gray siltstone with varying amounts of dolomite and sandstOne present. Porosity was fair overall with a milky pale yellow cut. Rate of penetration through the entire Middle Bakken ranged from 0.47 to 1.20 minutes per foot and averaged 0.62 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 129 to 876 units and averaged 437 units. Lower Bakken

The top of the Lower Bakken Shale was picked at an increase in rate of penetration and gamma. Sample also changed from siltstone to black shale. Gamma peaked at 920 API and averaged 696 API. Rate of penetration ranged from 0.20 to 1.10 minutes per foot and averaged 0.41 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 147 to 1757
units and averaged 951 units. The top of the Lower Bakken shale again conﬁrmed the landing TVD of 10,188’ TVD.
Three Forks 10,410’ MD 10,1 79’ TVD -7,887’ SS

The top of the Three Forks was picked at a decrease in rate of penetration and gamma, as well as a change in sample from black shale to cream gray sandstone. Rate of penetration in the Three Forks while drilling the curve ranged from 0.47 to 3.90 minutes per foot and averaged 0.77 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 213 to 971 units and averaged 679 units. Gamma ranged from 82 to 162 API and averaged 103 API. Overall samples were light to medium gray, gray brown and cream gray sandstone with poor porosity. Fluorescence was patchy pinpoint yellow with a milky green cut and slow yellow streaming.
