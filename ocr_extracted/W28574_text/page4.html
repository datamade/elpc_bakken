False Bakken 10,555’ MD 10,486” TVD -8,II6" SS
The False Bakken is a shale streak that occurs just above the upper bakken shale (usually 15-25 ft.) and is just used primarily as a marker when drilling before the bakken is encountered.
The False Bakken is a small thin member of shale interbedded in the bottom of the lodgepole limestone.
Upper Bakken 10,574 ’ MD 10,500’ TVD -8,128 ’ SS

The Upper Bakken Shale was encountered at 10,500’ TVD; -8, 128’ sub-sea level. The Upper Bakken was picked by change in rate ofpenetration, gamma data and lithology identiﬁcation. A typical sample was as follows; SHALE: Black, very ﬁne grained, platy to blocky, ﬁrm non-calcareous, carbonaceous, ﬁssile, brittle, poor interparticle porosity, spotty dull yellow ﬂuorescence, thick streaming bright yellow cut. Drill rates ranged from 0.3-1.6 minutes per foot. Gas ranged between 13-1319 units.
