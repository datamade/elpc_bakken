RE: AndersMadson 5201 42-24 pad

Dear Mr. Holweger:

Per your request, the following letter provides additional detail regarding Oasis Petroleum’s plan for the AndersMadson well pad.
Location of the pad: Oasis has investigated the possibility of drilling these wells from an already constructed pad, namely the Leanne pad to the west. While the company aims to minimize its footprint and consolidate wells on a single pad whenever possible, in this particular instance it simply was not a feasible option. In addition to the financial costs, there are environmental and engineering challenges with this location, outlined below:
1. Engineering Risk: Moving the SHL to the west would generate additional mechanical risk, as it would increase the stepout for at least one well beyond our tolerance threshold. Oasis aims for stepouts of 1000’ or less. The current location already yields a maximum stepout of ~1058’, and moving the location to the west would increase this distance by a substantial margin. With the additional stepout comes added costs and anti-collision concerns.
2. Environmental Risk: The SWSW of Section 24 would also be affected by the location of the acquifer. In addition, there is a creek adjacent to the east side of this pad. Expanding the pad to the east would disrupt the drainage pattern. Finally, the downward slope of the terrain from west to east (towards the creek) is steep as one moves away from the pad. Were Oasis to attempt to expand it, a substantial amount of fill would be required.
