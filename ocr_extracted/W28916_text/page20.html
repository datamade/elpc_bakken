Vertical Wellbore
Wellsite geological services began at 8,100' MD just above the Kibbey Lime formation. Multiple formations would be recorded before reaching KOP including the Charles, Ratcliffe, Rival, and Lodgepole formation. The Kibbey Lime was recorded off of a gamma marker decrease and lithology change from tan to brown sandstone to light brown to tan limestone. The Kibbey Lime top was recorded at 8,196' TVD and would be drilled through at 8,346‘ TVD where the top of the Charles formation was recorded. The top of the Charles formation mainly consists of large sections of hard salt. Drill rates would increase in these sections as well as gamma count decreases in order to locate most salt sections. Sections of limestone interbedded with gummy anhydrite sections would make up the remainder of the Charles. At 8,952’ TVD the Charles ended and the Last Salt formation began, which is mainly an extension of the Charles Salts but serves as an indicator of the last salt marker throughout the remaining formations to be drilled. The Last Salt formation is used as an early landing marker for the build curve in this area of North Dakota as well as Lodgepole markers. The Last Salt formation was 42' thick and the Base of the Last Salt was recorded at 8,994' TVD. A slight transition section between the Base of the Last Salt of 46' was drilled before reaching the Ratcliffe formation at 9,040’ TVD, and was mainly made up of dark gray to light brown limestone with moderate amounts of anhydrite beds included through the formation. The Ratcliffe formation was drilled over 140' and the top of the Rival formation was reached at 9,180' TVD. Rival formation lithology continued with limestone, cream to light gray brown to tan in color, with dolomite and anhydrite layers.
