Horizontal Section
Aﬁer tripping in the hole with a new drilling assembly, drilling began in the horizontal section at 4:00 AM on September 18, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 9,553’ MD/-7,378’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned from the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near survey depth of 10,587’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 10,382’ MD/-7,398’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,600’ — 12,000’ MD averaged 89.80 degrees, and then ﬂattened to ~90 degrees from 12,000’ — 12,900’ MD. The formation dip angle then averaged 89.95 degees from 12,900 to TD of 14,707’ MD.

The Middle Bakken dolomite throughout the entire horizontal section was typically light bluish gray to medium bluish gray to gray, spotted very light beige brown, dark gray in part, microcrystalline, argillaceous, and minimally carbonaceous. The dolomite was also generally moderately ﬁrm, with fair to good intergranular porosity and light green to dark yellow to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with mostly fast streaming cuts. ROP in the horizontal ranged from 0.20 to 13.70 minutes per foot, and
gas measurements ranged from 22 to 1,769 units, with max gas value within range observed at 10,437’ MD.

The ﬁrst section of the horizontal for Parshall 91-28H was drilled to Way Point 1, set at 11,000’ MD, with a target window of 9,350 — 9,362’ TVD. The second section of the horizontal was drilled to Way Point 2, set at 12,000’ MD, with a target window of 9,354 — 9,366’ TVD. Within Way Point 2, drilling involved sliding to make an azimuth turn from approximately 165 degrees to approximately 136 degrees, which was accomplished by ~11,300’ MD. Also within Way Point 2, the GR Marker was encountered at 10,999’ MD/-7,401’ SS.
