Mission Canyon 8,802’ MD 8, 795’ T VD ~6,531 ’ SS
The top of Mission Canyon Formation was reached at 8,802’ MD, corresponding to a true vertical depth of 8,795’, 17 feet higher than the prognosis. The top was chosen by a distinct change in gamma ray response where the values quickly drop from 59 API to 17 API and remain fairly low for the next 25 feet. The average gamma ray response for the formation is 24 API, but this is slightly skewed by the lower 194 feet of the formation that averaged 14.5 API, and the formation had a maximum value of 61 API. ROP varied throughout the section and signiﬁcantly slowed down towards the end. The average ROP was 40 feet per hour. The lithology of the Mission Canyon remained fairly consistent throughout, typically a light gray to light tan, slightly dolomitic, limestone.
