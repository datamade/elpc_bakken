Geologic Summary

The Bergstrom 11-13H is a Middle Bakken well laterally drilled by Unit 120 for Whiting Oil and Gas Corp. (API# 33-105-03745) and is located at Section 12 T156N R97W, 630’ FSL & 745’ FWL. The bottom hole location is Section 24 T156N R97W 240.9 FSL and 694.21’ FWL. The well was spudded on January 16, 2015. A total measured depth of 20,507’ MD was reached at 4: 15 am on March 3, 2015. All data is logged by either Jonathan Stoltman, Arin Marchand, or Anthony Samoski, of Neset Consulting Services. Well site geological services began at 7,225’ MD, above the Tyler Formation, on February 3, 2015 at 2: 15 am CST. Thirty foot lagged samples were collected. All samples were washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota. Gas monitoring equipment was provided by Neset Consulting Service, using a Total Gas system.

Vertical

Mudlogging service began above the Tyler Formation on February 3, 2015 at 2:15 am CST at 7,225' MD. The entire vertical section was drilled with invert mud, and aﬁer intermediate casing the drilling ﬂuid was Brine. Rates of penetration for the vertical section averaged 38 feet/hour with a maximum of 400 feet/hour. The vertical ended at the KOP of 9,560’ MD on February 6, 2015.

The Tyler formation was reached shortly aﬁer logging began. It came in at measured depth of 7,244’ and a true vertical depth of 7,23 7’. The top of the Tyler is identiﬁed by a sharp increase in the gamma ray response, followed by a sudden decrease. Typical samples were red, reddish brown, tan, very fine grained, silty shale to siltstone. These samples included trace amounts of anhydrite and light gray limestone. Gamma ranged between 16 and 200 API and the rate of penetration averaged 76 feet per hour through the formation.

Kibbey Lime

The Kibbey Lime came in at 7,757’ TVD, at a measured depth of 7,764’. A sudden decrease in gamma ray response is the main identiﬁer for the Kibbey Lime, dropping from 50-60 API to under 10 API in a few feet. API values staying fairly consistent and rose to 20 API over the next 30 feet. The Kibbey Lime consists of varying amounts of light to medium gray limestone, reddish brown siltstone, and off white and pasty anhydrite.
