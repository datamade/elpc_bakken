DETAILS OF WORK (Continued)

Upon completion of drilling activities, the stabilized area will be reclaimed back to its original condition.
Existing aggregate will be stripped and transported offsite for use at another location. The stabilized soil pH will be tested and 5 lbs. of ammonium sulfate fertilizer will be added per 1,000 square feet of area for every 0.4 points the pH is over the prestabilization pH value. Equipment will be used to pulverize the stabilized area to a depth of 12 inches or the bottom depth of the soil-cement layer, whichever is deeper, and the fertilizer will be thoroughly mixed into this material. The stabilized soil will also be sampled for EC, CEO, and SAR during reclamation activities. If the results of any of these analyses exceed NDIC allowable levels, appropriate remedial alternatives will be considered. These remedial alternatives may include but are not limited to flushing with water, the addition of gypsum, or excavation and transport for offsite disposal. Analytical results and any proposed remedial alternatives will be provided to NDIC in a Form 4 Sundry Notice.
Finally, the stockpiled topsoil will be spread over the reclaimed area and returned to original contours.
