Lateral

Drilling of the lateral began on November 15‘“, 2014 at 3 :27 PM CDT. The wellbore came out of the shoe 15.84’ feet into the Middle Bakken member, and approximately 4.16 feet above the target of 11,094’ TVD. The casing was set at 11,196’ MD, and at 11,063’ TVD, approximately 341’ before the EOC depth of 11,404’ MD, which resulted in exposed wellbore within the Upper Bakken Shale. As a result, invert drilling ﬂuid was used in the lateral section to stabilize the exposed shale. Initial dip for the 10’ target window was set at 90.0 degrees.
As drilling commenced in the lateral section, signiﬁcant gamma markers in the upper half of the target window led to an alteration of the original 900 dip to 89.88.
Throughout the lateral section, samples were mostly a silty and sandy marlstone which was medium to dark gray brown, light to medium gray, tan gray, sandy, slightly argillaceous, dolomitic, moderate to highly calcareous marlstone, moderately indurated, fair to poor intergranular porosity, even dull yellow green ﬂuorescence, and with instant milky to cloudy cuts.
Oil shows were questionable due to the oil—based drilling ﬂuid used during the lateral, but were generally even ﬂuorescence, bright to dull yellow to yellow green with thin to thick broad streaming. During drilling the lateral section, the rate of penetration averaged 89 feet per hour and gaseous hydrocarbons averaged 209 with ever increasing background gas and connection gas. During several trips we had over 10,000 units of trip gas, with a 20-30 foot ﬂare the last trip at 21,020 feet.
Conclusion

The Rennerfeldt 7-30H reached a total measured depth of 21,522’ MD on December 13th, 2014, at 2:32 AM CST. This well is currently awaiting completion.
