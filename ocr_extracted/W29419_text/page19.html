GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation’s State 31-3-3H is a single lateral horizontal oil well targeting the Middle Bakken and is located in NWNE Section 3 T150N R104W, in McKenzie County, North Dakota. It was spudded on 22 Nov. 2014. Total depth of 20,300' MD; 10,366.7' TVD; 10,058.38' VS reached at 1530 hrs MST 04
Dec. 2014.
Meredith Remmich, Alan Chappell of Neset Consulting Service, Inc. provided geological services from a measure depth of 7,450’. One set of samples was collected in thirty foot intervals for the vertical and curve, and in one hundred foot intervals during the lateral. All samples were washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey core library in Grand Forks, North Dakota. Pason Gas
Detection was utilized for gas detection.

VERTICAL / CURVE WELLBORES

Drilling

"An oil—based mud system was used in the vertical and curve wellbores. Logging began at 1520 hrs MST 26 Nov 2014 ﬁom 7.450’ MD in the Amsden

Formation. TOOH due to low ROP from 7,958' MD at 2130 hrs MST 26 Nov 2014; resumed drilling vertical wellbore at 0430 hrs MST 27 Nov 2014. TOOH for curve assembly from 9,800' MD at 0125 hrs MST 28 Nov 2014; resumed drilling at 1230 hrs MST 28 Nov 2014. Began building curve from 9,810’ MD; 9,807' TVD. The target was set at 10,3 82’ TVD, 10’ into the Middle Bakken. Curve ROP averaged 1.0 min/Pt (60 feet per hour) and total gas averaged 15 units with a peak recorded reading of 18 units. The Middle Bakken TVD target window was established as an 8’ window, with the target top 5’ below the Upper Bakken Shale and the target bottom 7’above the Lower Bakken Shale, with a total Middle Bakken Member thickness estimated at 20’.
7” casing was set at 10,691 .2’ MD and cemented.
F ORIMA T IONS

Tyler

The Tyler Formation was observed at 7,525’ MD (7,522’ TVD; —5,602’ SS), 20’ higher than the prognosis, The Tyler top was identiﬁed by GR signature and rate of penetration (ROP). ROP averaged 0.6 minutes per foot (100 feet/hour). Background gas averaged 40 units and a peak reading of 6 units was recorded. No unexpected hydrocarbon shows were observed in the Tyler Formation.
