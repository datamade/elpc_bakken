GEOLOGIC SUNIIVIARY

The Triangle USA Petroleum Corporation Britt 151-102-4-9-4H was the 1St vertical and curve and the 4‘11 lateral of 4 wells located on Section 4 T151N R102W in McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Britt 151—102-4-9-4H was spudded on July 18, 2014 with surface drilled and cased to be resumed at a later date. Patterson 320 spudded the Britt 151-102-4-9-4H on August 3, 2014 and reached a total depth of 20,761 ’ MD on October 2, 2014. Wellsite geological services began at 8,200 on August 5, 2014 at 10:35 PM MST. The initial phase of the Britt 151-102-4—9-4H is a vertical and curve landing in the Middle Bakken Formation on August 9 2014. The single lateral well targeting the Middle Bakken Formation approximately 11’—1 5’ below the base of the Upper Bakken Shale was started on September 25, 2014. Thirty-foot samples were collected throughout the vertical and curve with ﬁfty—foot samples collected in the lateral. All samples were washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.

Vertical

Logging services began at 8,200’ MD in the Heath formation on August 5, 2014 at 10:35 PM MST. Gas detection system was tested before logging depth with system ﬁinctioning correctly. Drill rates before encountering the Kibbey Lime formation ranged from 0.18-5.35 minutes per foot and averaging 1.31 minutes per foot. Gas ranged between 17—152 units averaging 110 units. A typical sample was: SILTSTONE: Rusty brown, reddish brown, dark brown, reddish orange, gritty, argillaceous, chalky, slightly calcareous, 10% anhydrite, cream tan, off—white, soft to pasty, trace limestone, light gray to gray, even dull yellow green ﬂuorescence, good fast milky cut.

The Kibbey Lime was picked 3 feet lower than prognosis, based on sample changes, ROP, and differential pressure. Drill rates in the Kibbey Lime formation ranged from 0.8—5.2 minutes per foot with average penetration rate of 1.66 minutes per foot. Gas ranged between 112—202 units averaging 142 units. A typical sample was: LIMESTONE: Light gray to gray, wackestone, argillaceous, dolomitic in part, poor induration, no intercrystalline porosity, 40 % anhydrite, white to cream tan, soft to pasty, even yellow green ﬂuorescence, slow milky cut.
