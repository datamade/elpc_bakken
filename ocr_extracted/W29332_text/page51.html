GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

The Triangle Petroleum Corporation Wisness 150—100-23-14—12TFH well, is located in the SE/ SW quarter of Section 23 T1 SON RIOOW and ends in the NE/NE quarter of Section 14 T1 SON RIOOW in McKenzie County, North Dakota.
The Wisness 150-100-23-14-12TFH, a horizontal well, was drilled by H&P 255 in the Three Forks targeting the ﬁrst bench 17 to 27 feet into the Three Forks formation. The Wisness 150-100-23-14-12'I'FH well spudded December 2nd, 2014. Total depth of 20,932’ MD was reached on December 18, 2014.

Wellsite geological services began at 8,800 feet in the Kibbey Formation. The gas detection system used was Neset Total Gas. Thirty foot lagged samples were collected in the vertical section and curve. Fifty foot samples were collected in the horizontal. All lagged samples were washed, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey core library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.

Logging services began at 8,800 feet measured depth in the Kibbey Formation on December 5th, 2014. Drill rates ranged from 0.4 to 7.9 minutes per foot. Gas ranged from 11 to 342 units.
Charles 8,85 7’ MD 8,853’ T VD -6,534’SS
The Charles formation was encountered at 8,857 ’ MD; 8,857’ TVD and was picked due to an increase in ROP related to the Charles Salt, mixed with anhydrite. Samples consisted of a mix of Salt, Anhydrite, and Limestone. Sample characteristics showed clear, angular, crystalline, translucent, moderate anhydrite, rusty brown to orange, off white to cream white, trace limestone, white mineral to dull yellow ﬂuorescence with no cut.

Base Last Salt

The Base of the Last Salt was encountered at 9,595’ MD; -7,244’ SS, and was picked by the last sample with salt present, as well as an increase in the rate of penetration of the last salt bed. Samples then became beds of limestone and anhydrite, until the Mission Canyon.
