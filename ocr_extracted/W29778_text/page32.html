Day 4 18,905’ —20,510’ MD
Drilling continued with the wellbore sitting about 5 feet below the base of the Middle Bakken target interval. Slides were done throughout the day to work on keeping the wellbore pointed up to work on brining the wellbore up in TVD towards the Middle Bakken target interval. As the base of the target window was entered the wellbore rolled over and began heading down in TVD at around 89 — 88 degrees. Starting at a MD of 19,600’ the MWD tool began to read erratically ranging from 80 — 140 counts. Aﬂer seeing the slides were done to keep the wellbore at or near dip to TD of the S lateral. Penetration rates throughout the day averaged around 250 - 300 ﬁ/hr while rotating and around 60 - 100 ﬁ/hr while sliding. Background gases averaged around 350 — 800 units with connection gases reaching as high as 1,442 units. The samples seen in the lower portion of the formation continued to exhibit a light to medium tan/cream brown dolomite with traces of limestone and siltstone present and an even dull yellow green ﬂuorescence and a slow faint streaming to cloudy yellow green cut.
Total depth of the South lateral was called at 20,510’ MD for the Arnegard #2126-2H with the wellbore ending in the upper portion of the Middle Bakken. The formation throughout the lateral had an overall estimated dip rate of 90.08 degrees. Total depth of the South Lateral was reached at 20,510’ MD at 4: 15 pm.
