LODGEPOLE

The Lodgepole formation came in at 10,137’ MD and TVD of 10,135’. The Lodgepole was encountered 13’ low to the prognosis, and picked off of differential pressure and torque, and conﬁrmed with a sudden increase in gamma. Gamma increased up from 14.27 API units to 30.67 API units to mark the top of the Lodgepole. Samples in the Lodgepole were mostly light to medium gray tan to gray brown Limestone with minimal ﬂuorescence and dull weak to slow milky yellow cuts.

FALSE BAKKEN 10,928 ’ MD 10,8 77’ TVD -8,812 ’ SS
A 10,928’ MD the False Bakken came in at 10,877’ TVD, 10’ low to prognosis. The gamma reached 135.68 API units while in the False Bakken. The samples for the
False Bakken consisted of cream gray to gray tan, with blocky to platy limestone. The samples had weak milky yellow cuts with a peak of 466 units of gas seen in this interval.
SCALLION 10,933 ’ MD 10,881 ’ T VD -8,816’
The Scallion was the next formation encountered. The scallion came in at 10,933’ MD and 10,881 ’ TVD, 10’ low to prognosis. The gamma cooled back down to 39.4 APl units in the Scallion. The Scallion consisted of mostly cream buff Limestone with slow dull milky yellow cuts. Gas peaked at 3098 units of gas along with fair milky
cuts.

UPPER BAKKEN

The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,948’ MD, and 10,890’ TVD, this was 10’ low to the prognosis. The gamma reached 568.9 units at this point. Background gas averaged 716 units, peaking 3091 units. Samples in the Upper Bakken were black, carbonaceous blocky shale, with instant milky yellow cuts.

MIDDLE BAKKEN 10,982’ MD 10,909’ T VD -8,844 ’SS
The Middle Bakken was encountered at a MD of 10,982’ and 10,909’ TVD, 10’ low to the prognosis. The Middle Bakken was picked based on the change in samples and a decrease in gamma from 568.9 units down to 41.64 units API. The Middle Bakken
samples consisted mostly of a light to dark cream gray tan platy siltstone. Gas peaked at 1666 units of gas along with fair milky cuts.
