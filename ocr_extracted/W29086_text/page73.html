Base 0fLast Salt 9,1 74' MD 9,1 71 ' T VD -6, 527 'SS
The Base of Last Salt was encountered at a measured depth of 9,174 feet, which was 5 feet high to the prognosis. The Base of Last Salt was picked off of correlating drill rates to that of an off-set log, changes in gamma, and a change in lithology from salt to a light to medium tan, gray brown, limestone. The lithology through this formation consisted of a light to medium tan, gray brown limestone.
Mission Canyon 9,365 ' 1WD 9,362' T VD -6, 718' SS
The Mission Canyon formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,365 feet, 1 foot low to the prognosis. The top was picked primarily off of correlating drill breaks with those of the off-set wells and conﬁrmed by gamma. Samples in the upper portion of the Mission Canyon exhibited a light to medium tan, light gray, ﬁnely crystalline limestone with occasional interbedded anhydrite present.
Lodgepole 9,856 ' MD 9,853' T VD -7,209' SS
The Lodgepole Formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,856 feet. The top of the Lodgepole was initially evaluated using a correlation between gamma ray in this well and the Cymbaluk #21—15PH. There was also a change in samples from a pale to light tan limestone at the base of the Mission Canyon formation, to a light to medium gray limestone in the top of the Lodgepole formation.
A KOP of 10,160’ MD was reached at 3:00 AM MST on December 30th, 2014. Using the Lodgepole top, and several gamma markers before KOP as a reference, the landing target for the curve was set at 10,632’ TVD, roughly 10 feet into the Pronghorn formation.
After tripping out of the hole, the rig crew picked up the curve assembly, tripped in the hole, and started to build the curve at 2:00 PM MST on December 30th, 2014.
The directional team was not getting the builds that were needed to land in the Pronghorn formation. As a result the decision was made to trip out of the hole to pick up a 275 degree motor. At 10,582’ MD the rig crew started tripping out of the hole at 11:00 PM MST on December 30th, 2014. After picking up a higher bend motor and tripping back to bottom, they resumed building the curve at 8:30 AM MST on December 313‘, 2014.
