False Bakken Scallion

The False Bakken was observed at a measured depth of 9,3 85’ MD (9,270’ TVD) near an inclination of 63 degrees The False Bakken was identiﬁed with samples changing over from a gray dolomitic limestone to a black to dark brown grease noncalcareous shale with blooming yellow green cuts and dull orange brown ﬂuorescence. Gas at this time rose to 185 units from 61 units. Gamma conﬁrmed the False Bakken with a rise in API from 34 to a peak of 106 API. The False Bakken Measured twelve feet thick and was exited at 9,41 S’MD (9,282’ TVD) with an inclination of 64 degrees.

The Scallion was noted at 9,415’ MD (TVD 9,282) as samples changed over to a tan gray to tan dolomitic limestone. Gamma recorded a drop from 109 API to a low of 29 API. The Scallion recorded a total thickness of ten feet and was exited at an inclination of 64 degrees.
NOTE: Prior to entering the Upper Bakken shale a trip out of the hole was made due to a motor failure at a measured depth of 9,400’ MD. The build rates needed to complete the curve was 8 degrees per on—hundred. The TOOH occurred from 11:30 am on 10/20/14 and returned to bottom on 10/20/14 at 8:00 PM with a re—run of the Hughes T505 and a new Phoenix motor 675”, 7.8 lobe, HR stage.

An exit of the shale was made at 9,490’ MD with a TVD of 9,311’ at 68 degrees for a wellbore exposure of 48 feet and a total thickness of 18-20 feet. During the landing the Upper Bakken Shale came in roughly 2 feet high to the prognosis. Build rates prior to landing fell short with a 3 and 4 degree build rate consecutively.
