GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation’s Tiisto 43 -7-2H is a grassroots well located in the NE of the SE Sec 7-T154N-R91W in Mountrail County, North Dakota. It is a single lateral horizontal well targeting the lower portion of the Middle Bakken siltstone as well as tan sandstone, drilled on a 2560-acre spacing unit. The well was spudded on November 23, 2014 and reached a total depth of 19,632 at 11:00 PM CDT on December 21, 2014. Clint Wold and Nevan Larson were onsite for well site supervision, along with Scott Strahm and Stephanie Connole representing Neset Consulting Service, Inc for wellsite geological services.
Well site services began at the logging depth of 7,500’ MD, at 10:30 PM CDT, on November 26, 2014. Two man geological services were used for the vertical, curve and lateral. A Total Gas detector was utilized for gas analysis. Samples were caught every thirty feet throughout the vertical section and curve. Samples were caught every ﬁfty feet
through the lateral. All samples caught were saved to be archived at the ND Core and Sample Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.

LODGEPOLE 9,209’ 1WD 9,200’ TVD -6,846’ SS
The Lodgepole formation came in at 9209’ MD and TVD of 9,200’. The Lodgepole was encountered 13’ high to the prognosis, and picked off of differential pressure and torque, and conﬁrmed with a sudden increase in gamma. Gamma increased up from 13.24 API units to 33.37 API units to mark the top of the Lodgepole. Samples in the Lodgepole were mostly light to medium gray tan to gray brown limestone with minimal ﬂuorescence and dull weak to slow milky yellow cuts.
