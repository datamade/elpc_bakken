THE CURVE
The Tiisto 43-7—2H curve was drilled with a BHI T505 8.75” bit along with a Baker Hughes mud motor set to 2.4 degrees and an invert mud system. There was a 30’
rotation made from this point before the sliding for the curve commenced at the kick off point of 9,500’ MD.
FALSE BAKKEN 10,053’ MD 9,940’ TVD -7,586’ SS
At 10,053’ MD the False Bakken came in at 9,940’ TVD, 8’ low to prognosis. The gamma reached 141.01 API units while in the False Bakken. The samples for the False Bakken consisted of cream gray to gray tan, with blocky to platy limestone. The samples had weak milky yellow cuts with a peak of 11 units of gas seen in this interval.

S CALLION

The Scallion was the next formation encountered. The scallion came in at 10,061’ MD and 9,945’ TVD, 9’ low to prognosis. The gamma cooled back down to 65.09 API units in the Scallion. The Scallion consisted of mostly cream buff Limestone with slow dull milky yellow cuts. Gas peaked at 11 units of gas along with fair milky cuts.

UPPER BAKKEN 10,103 ’ 1WD 9,963 ’ TVD - 7, 609’ SS
The Upper Bakken Shale came in at 10,103’ MD, and 9,963’ TVD, this was 10’ low to the prognosis. The gamma reached 611.39 units at this point. Background gas
averaged 154.48 units, peaking 612 units. Samples in the Upper Bakken were black, carbonaceous blocky shale, with instant milky yellow cuts.
