Flowback Strategy
After the fracture treatment, these wells will be ﬂowed through a choke to test production tanks. Choke size will depend on ﬁnal shut in pressure post frac and well pressure prior to opening the choke. The choke will be adjusted up or down based on ﬂowing pressure, liquid rates and sand returns, to prevent clogging manifold chokes. During ﬂowback, the water chlorides and sand content will be monitored. When the water chlorides are greater than 100,000 ppm and the produced ﬂuids contain no sand, the wells will be turned to production facilities. Since the pipeline is already at the location of the pad and Oxy will have its production facilities constructed before ﬂowback starts, and gas sales should start as soon as the well start ﬂowing through the production facilities, unless there are capacity issues on ONEOK’S system at that time. Based on current information, it is our belief the system can take this gas upon completion of the four wells.
This well will initially flow 445 barrels of oil and 160 thousand cubic feet of gas per day based on Oxy’s production type curve of this area. It is anticipated this well will decline sharply after initial production as do the majority of horizontal wells in the Williston Basin.
