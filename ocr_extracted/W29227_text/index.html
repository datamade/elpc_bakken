DETAILS OF PROCEDURE
Per a conference call with Richard Suggs and John Axtman, the following procedure will be used to plug the P Jackman 156-100-2-18-6-2H3 in preparation for a sidetrack to the present wellbore:
1.Set a CIBP at 9,900’ — 9,950’ on tubing and pump 50 sxs of cement on top.
2.Perforate 6 squeeze shots at 7,100’...set a retainer on drill pipe and tubing...inject into formation and attempt to pump 90 sxs of cement through perfs and leave 10 sxs on retainer top...100 sxs total.
3.If squeeze holes will not take the 90 sxs, then close retainer and leave remainder of cement on retainer top.
4.Cut and pull 7” casing at 4,600’ (@ 100’ above Mowry top).
5.Set a cement plug from 50’ inside the 7” casing top and back to 200’ above the cut off 7”.
6.Proceed to drill out of 9-5/8” casing with a new hole and sidetrack around the abandoned ﬁrst hole.
7.Drilling will proceed in normal manner of setting the 7” casing and then drilling a lateral to original BHL and running a liner.
Let me know if this is acceptable to everyone involved.
Jim McCormick Senior Drilling Engineer
