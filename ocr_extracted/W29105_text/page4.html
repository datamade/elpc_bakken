Upper Bakken 9,599 ’MD 9,289 ’TVD - 7,266 ’SS
Entering the Upper Bakken Shale at 74 degrees at 9,599’ MD with a TVD of 9,289’ Gamma rose to peak of 342 API from 33 API. Gas increased to 200 units. Samples in the Upper Bakken consisted of greasy black non calcareous brittle shale with oil shows consisting of even dull yellow brown ﬂuorescence and instant milky yellow brown cuts. Gas averaged 200 units as the Upper Bakken was penetrated. An exit of the shale was made at 9,721 ’ MD with a TVD of 9,3 16’ at 82 degrees for a wellbore exposure of 134 feet and a total thickness of 27 feet. During the landing the Upper Bakken Shale came in roughly 3 feet low to the prognosis. Due to the large build rates experienced during the upper portion of the curve the lower portion of the well was mostly rotated to ensure the proper build rates needed to land.
