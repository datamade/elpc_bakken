Enelplus Earth Lodge 1 48-92-22A-21H Exploratory Well -— Mud and Cementing Plan

2. Proposed Casing and Cementing Programs
Casing Program
The surface casing shall consist of new pipe that has been previously tested to 1,000 psi. The surface casing shall be set and cemented at a point not less than 50 feet (15.24 meters) below the base of the Fox Hills Formation. Production or intermediate casing strings shall consist of new pipe that has been previously tested to 2,000 psi. Production liner top would be planned for 100 feet above the KOP. It would be set using a hydraulically-activated liner hanger/packer assembly. Aﬁer the liner hanger is set and the hanger packer is energized, they
would be pressure—tested to 3,000 psi for 30 minutes prior to removal of the setting tool. A frac string will be set.

Cementing Program
Surface Casing: 9 %—inch surface pipe would be cemented back to the surface with 25% excess using:
0 Lead Slurry: 347 sacks of Control Set ‘C’ + 1.0% CaCl + 1.0% SMS + 1.0% OGC-60 + 0.125 lb/sk Poly-E-Flake (lost circulation additive). Yield: 2.85, Density: 11.5.
0 Tail Slurry: 210 sacks of 0:1:0 ‘Type 111’ + 1.0% CaClz + 0.125 lb/sk Poly-E-Flake. Yield 1.46, Density: 14.2.
Wait-on-Cement (WOC) time minimum of 12 hours or until stabilized. Surface casing should be allowed to stand under pressure until the tail cement has reached a compressive strength of
500 psi.
Intermediate Casing: 7-inch intermediate casing would be cemented back to 500 feet above the top of the Dakota formation, with 30% excess on the lead slurry and 20% on the tail
slurry, using:

Lead Slurry: 112 sacks of 1:2:6 ‘PozszGel’ + 3.0% KCl (%bwow) + 0.50% CFL-4 +0.25% FWCA—H + 0.60% LTR + 0.4% DF-7011 + 0.125 lb/sk Poly-E-Flake. Yield: 2.76, Density: 11.6.
