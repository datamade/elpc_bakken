At 11,500’ measured depth, the wellbore went out of the base of the target. The wellbore dropped in true vertical depth and brieﬂy encountered the Aquitard at 12,015’. Gamma spiked to 154 API. Sliding succeeded in bringing the wellbore higher in true vertical depth. The wellbore encountered the base of the target zone at 12,180’. The wellbore continued to drill in the lower part of the target zone. At 12,393’ measured depth, the wellbore deﬂected off the set line and the hot gamma was seen. Between 12,180’ to 12,393’ the samples were generally described as sandstone: translucent, cream gray, off white, very ﬁne, subrounded to subangular, moderately sorted, moderately cemented, dolomitic cement; 25% siltstone, light gray, platy, laminated; 15% shale, light blue green, light gray, pyritic, ﬁssile; 1% pyrite; poor porosity; scattered dull green ﬂuorescence, cloudy green cut. Total gas ranged from 146 to 881 units and averaged 652 units. Gamma ranged from 44 to 112 API and averaged 66 API.
The wellbore continued to drill in the lower part of the target zone until 13,279’ measured depth. Here the hot gamma was encountered and the wellbore crossed the set line. This contact point was used to estimate the dip to be 90 degrees. At 13,306’ measured depth the wellbore went out the top of the target zone. Sliding succeed in bring the wellbore down in true vertical depth and entered the top of the target zone at 13,600’ measured depth.
