Geologic Summary

Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation’s Knife River State #41-16TFH is a horizontal well targeting the Three Forks formation, to be drilled in a south southeast direction at a vertical section azimuth of 177.18 degrees for approximately 8,965 feet vertical section. The well is located in the NE V4, NE ‘/4, Section 16, Township 153N, Range 92W of Mountrail County, northwest of New Town, North Dakota.

Began logging in the Charles Formation
Logging services began at 8,450 feet measured depth in the Charles formation at 6:00 AM CST on November 9TH, 2014. Charles consisted primarily of anhydrite, limestone, and interlarninated dolomitic lenses, along with trace to moderate reddish orange shale. Drill rates ranged from one to two minutes per foot. Background gas averaged 14 units. Base of the last salt was picked at 8,652 feet measured depth, 8,650 feet true vertical depth, with a drill break of .4 to 1.5 minutes per foot. Samples following consisted primarily of limestone, anhydrite, and minor dolomite, with a varying presence of shale through the Mission Canyon.
Mission Canyon 8,826’ [MB 8,824’ T VD -6, 604’ SS
Mission Canyon was picked at 8,826 feet measured depth, 8,824 feet true vertical depth The Mission Canyon consisted of predominantly light to medium cream gray, microcrystalline, fairly argillaoeous limestone, off white to cream white anhydrite and light tan, micro to cryptocrystalline dolomite. Drill rates averaged l to 1.5 minutes per foot through the Mission Canyon, with 14 to 35 units of average background gas. Even white mineral ﬂuorescence and slow, pinpoint streaming cut present in majority of samples caught. A trip was performed at 9,143 feet measured depth within the Mission Canyon due to MWD tool failure shortly before 10:30 PM CST on November 9m. Drilling resumed shortly after 9:00 AM CST on November 10TH. Due to low gas readings, a bump test was performed at the shakers and conﬁrmed correct operation of gas equipment.
