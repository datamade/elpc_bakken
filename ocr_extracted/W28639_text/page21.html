Upper Bakken Shale 9,523’MD 9,310’ TVD -7,357’ss
The Upper Bakken Shale (UBS) was encountered at 9,523’ MD/-7,357’ SS, which was initially determined from an increase in ROP when transitioning out of harder limestone into softer shale, and ﬁnally determined when gamma values dramatically increased as the gamma tool entered the UBS, with the gamma tool being 52 feet behind the drill bit. ROP in the UBS ranged from 0.50 to 1.30 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, with gas measurements ranging from 332 to 1,452 units. Here shale samples were generally dull black to very dark gray and very dark brown, blocky, ﬁssile, carbonaceous, spotted dark yellow to dark green to black ﬂuorescence, with instant to fast blooming milky cuts.
The curve section landed in the Middle Bakken at an 84.70° angle at 9,640' MD/9,330.47’ TVD on September 28, 2014 at 2:15 PM. The ﬁnal curve TD depth of 9,640’ MD was conﬁrmed when the bottoms up sample at this depth contained bluish gray Middle Bakken dolomite with trace amounts of very dark gray to black shale.
Horizontal Section
Aﬂer the intermediate (7”) casing shoe was set to 9,637’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 1:15 AM on September 30, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 9,638’ MD/-7,378’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned ﬁ'om the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near survey depth of 9,830’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 9,737’ MD/-7,388’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,600’ - 10,200’ MD averaged 90 degrees, and then angled upward at an average of ~90.15 degrees from 10,200’ — 15,231’ MD.
