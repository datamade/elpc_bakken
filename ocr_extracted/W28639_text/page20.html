Mssion Canyon

The Mission Canyon formation top was picked at 7,946’ MD/-5,990’ SS, and was picked from lithology change and a temporary increase in ROP when transitioning from the Charles formation limestone into Mission Canyon limestone. ROP in the Mission Canyon ranged from 0.20 to 1.20 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranging from 41 to 757 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were medium to light and dark gray, medium to light and dark brown, packstone to wackestone, moderately argillaceous, moderately carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair intergranular porosity, light to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with slow to fast streaming cuts.
Lodgepole 8,542’ MD 8,539’ TVD -6, 586’ SS
The Lodgepole formation top was picked at 8,542’ MD/—6,586’ SS, where samples changed ﬁom a light to medium brown and gray limestone to a medium to dark gray limestone. ROP in the Lodgepole ranged from 0.30 to 4.50 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, with gas measurements ranging from 31 to 1,381 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone which were medium to dark and light gray, brown to light brown in part, argillaceous, wackestone, silty, slightly carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair to good intergranular porosity, light green to dark green/gray ﬂuorescence, with moderately fast streaming cuts.
Curve Section 8,815 ’ — 9, 640’ MD
The curve section of Parshall 92—28H began 273’ MD into the Lodgepole formation at a KOP of 8,815’ MD. Directional tools were picked up on September 27, 2014, and the curve was started at 7:00 PM on September 27, 2014. Drilling rates in the curve ranged from 0.50 to 4.50 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, and gas
measurements ranged from 51 to 1,433 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the curve to correlate Parshall 92-28H with Parshall 91-28H.
