Dear Mr. Helms,
As a follow—up to our recent visit on April 20, 2012, please accept this detailed
explanation for changes to drill well locations as originally proposed for the Corral Creek-Bakken Unit (CCU).
As discussed during the referenced visit, the original well locations were planned during the unitization process, and had not been ﬁeld—veriﬁed prior to implementation. As our Construction Supervisor began staking potential locations with the survey crew, it was determined that several areas would need to be modiﬁed, due to topographic issues within CCU. Two speciﬁc Drill Spacing Units (DSU) located in the northwest portion of CCU are affected by this limitation, as will be other preliminary locations within the Unit. Please see the attached plat for illustration.

The two referenced DSUs, located in sections 6 and 7, and sections 5 and 8, both located in Tl47N R95W, have extremely rough topography, making accessibility very difﬁcult. It would be exceedingly complicated to build roadways into these areas, lay flowlines or gathering lines, bring in electrical power, or build drilling/producing locations capable of conducting operations. In addition, because they are located immediately adjacent to the Little Missouri River, there are elevated environmental risks. The closest area to develop these sections from would be the southern portion of sections 18 and 17, immediately north of Hwy 22. Although this will require drilling extended laterals to reach the northern extent of these DSUs, and cause development expenses to be increased, we feel this is the only option available to exploit this area of the Unit and prevent stranded acreage within CCU. For reference please see the attached map.
