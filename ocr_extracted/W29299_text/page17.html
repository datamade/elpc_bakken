GEOLOGIC SUMMARY
EOG Resources Parshall 97-3332H is a horizontal well located in SW/ SE Section 33 T152N R90W in Mountrail County, North Dakota, and was drilled by H&P 316, with surface and Kelly bushing elevations of 1,980’ and 2,004’ respectively. The three phases of Parshall 97-3332H were vertical, curve, and horizontal sections, with the horizontal section targeting the Middle Bakken formation. Parshall 97-3332H had a spud date of December 11, 2014, with geological logging and gas detection services beginning in the Kibbey formation. Lagged thirty foot samples (with occasional lagged ten foot samples to determine formation tops in all sections) were collected in the vertical and curve sections, and lagged one hundred foot samples were collected in the horizontal section. All samples were washed, dried, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core and Sample Library, located on the campus of the University of North Dakota in the city of Grand Forks. Parshall 97—3332H reached TD of 15,656’ MD at 2:00 PM CST on December 22, 2014 in NE/NE Section 32 T152N R90W.
Vertical Section 7,340’ — 8,900’ MD
Logging services began at 7,340’ MD in the bottom of the Kibbey formation on December 13, 2014. The gas detection system was tested before reaching logging depth to ensure that the system was ﬁinctioning correctly. The logged vertical section was drilled through the Charles and Mission Canyon formations, and into the Lodgepole formation, where rate of penetration (ROP) ranged from 0.10 to 530 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranged from 8 to 193 units. Kick off point (KOP) was reached within the Lodgepole formation at 6:20 AM CST on December 14, 2014 at 8,900’ MD.
Charles 7,407’ MD 7,403’ TVD -5,399’ SS
The Charles formation top was picked at 7,407’ MD/-5,399’ SS. ROP in the Charles formation ranged from 0.10 to 1.80 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranged from 10 to 96 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were light gray to light brown and light reddish brown, gray, dark gray in part, argillaceous to carbonaceous, wackestone to packstone, with moderately poor induration, fair to good intergranular porosity, dark green to dark gray ﬂuorescence, and a slow streaming cut.
Base of the Last Salt 7,840’ [MB 7,836 ’ TVD -5,832’ SS
The Base of the Last Salt was encountered within the Charles formation at 7,840’ MD/-5,832’ SS.
