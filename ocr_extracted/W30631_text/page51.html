Curve

The curve was started at 11:00 AM CST on March 2, 2015 with an 8 3%. inch BHI curve bit and a NOV 2.38 degree mud motor targeting the Middle Bakken Formation with an initial target TVD of 11,263 feet and 15 feet into the Middle Bakken Formation. The Sondrol 11-3HU gamma markers were correlated to the Sondrol 11-3TFH and Sondrol 11-3—2H gamma data. Baker Hughes directional team started the curve with an average built rate of 11.5 degrees per 100 feet needed. The decision was made to TOOH for insufﬁcient build rates at 4:20 PM CST on March 2, 2015 at 11126' MD. Drilling resumed at 6:45 AM CST on March 3, 2015 with a Security bit and 2.5 degree mud motor. The decision was made to TOOH for insufﬁcient build rates at 9:00 AM CST on March 3, 2015 at 11212‘ MD. Drilling resumed at 10:40 PM CST on March 3, 2015 with the same Security bit as the previous run, teamed with a 2.75 degree mud motor. The decision was made to TOOH for insufficient build rates at 1:50 AM CST on March 4, 2015 at 11308' MD. Drilling resumed at 1:30 PM CST on March 4, 2015 with a Smith tri-cone bit and a 2.75 degree mud motor. While correlating gamma signatures between Sondrol 11—3TFH and Sondrol 11—3-2H, the ideal landing target ﬂuctuated between 11,264 feet and 11,277 feet TVD throughout the seven Lodgepole Formation gamma markers. With the vast variation in recommended landing targets while correlating gamma signatures, the target was changed to 11,271 feet TVD aﬁer Lodgepole gamma marker number 6 and remained the ideal target for the remainder of the build section.
