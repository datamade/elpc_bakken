DETAILS OF WORK Kodiak respectfully requests approval to strip NGLs from eight wells: P Wood 154-98-15-23-15-3H (File No. 27930), P Wood 154-98-15-23-15-2HA (File No. 27931), P Wood 154-98-15-23-15-2H (File No. 27932), P Wood 154-98-15-23-3515H3A (File No. 27933), P Wood 154-98-15-23-35-15HA (File No. 27934), P Wood 154-98-15-23-35-15H3 (File No. 27935),.P Wood 154-98-15-23-35-15H (File No. 27936), and P Wood 154-98-15-23-15-3HA (File No. 28884). Gas from each well is measured through a meter on the heater treater outlet piping before being combined into one stream to the NGL recovery equipment. Each meter consists of a Sr Orifice Meter with an Electronic Flow Meter. The meters will be calibrated per NDIC regulation NDAC rule 43-02-03-142. The revenues from the NGL recovery equipment will be allocated on the ratio of each well's production to the total of eight wells. The residue gas, or the gas remaining after NGL extraction, will continue to be flared until a gathering pipeline can be constructed and all of the gas is gathered. The ROW easement is in negotiations. The NGL recovery processing skid, Unit TES-R008, is provided and operated by Torrent Energy Services for Kodiak Oil and Gas, while Kodiak remains the operator. The recovered NGLs will be stored in a separate pressurized tank. As such Kodiak plans to file a Form 53 once NGLs are recovered.
