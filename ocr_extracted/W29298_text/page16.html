Mission Canyon 8,010’ MD 8, 006’ T VD -6, 002’ SS

The Mission Canyon formation top was picked at 8,010’ MD/-6,002’ SS, and was picked from lithology change and a temporary increase in ROP when transitioning from the Charles formation limestone into Mission Canyon limestone. ROP in the Mission Canyon ranged from 0.20 to 8.90 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranging from 7 to 213 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were light to medium gray and dark gray in part, medium to light brown and dark brown in part, packstone to wackestone, moderately argillaceous, moderately carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair intergranular porosity, light to dark green and dark yellow and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with moderately fast streaming cuts.
Lodgepole 8, 607’ MD 8, 603’ T VD -6, 599’ SS
The Lodgepole formation top was picked at 8,607’ MD/-6,599’ SS, where samples changed from a light to medium brown and gray limestone to a medium to dark gray limestone. ROP in the Lodgepole ranged from 0.20 to 8.90 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, with gas measurements ranging from 8 to 220 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were medium gray to dark and light gray, brown to light brown in part, argillaceous, slightly carbonaceous, packstone to wackestone, silty, slightly carbonaceous, moderate to well indurated, with fair to good intergranular porosity and light green to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with mostly fast streaming cuts.
Curve Section 8, 724’ — 1 0, 018’ MD
The curve section of Parshall 96—3332H began 117’ MD into the Lodgepole formation at a KOP of 8,724’ MD. Directional tools were picked up on December 27, 2014, and the curve was started at 12:00 PM CST on December 27, 2014. On the evening of December 27, 2014, a trip out of the hole at 8,980’ MD occurred due to poor build rates, with resumption of drilling occurring at mid-morning of December 28, 2014. On the late morning of December 29, 2014, a trip out of the hole at 9,652” MD occurred due to poor rate of penetration, with resumption of drilling occurring in the late evening of December 29, 2014. Drilling rates in the curve ranged from 0.10 to 8.90 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, and gas measurements ranged from 16 to 1,004 units.
Gamma markers for Parshall 96—3332H listed below were utilized throughout the curve to correlate Parshall 96-3332H with Parshall 97-3332H.
