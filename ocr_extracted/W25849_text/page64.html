Began logging in the Charles Formation

Logging services began at 8,550 feet measured depth in the Charles formation at 8:30 AM CST on January 8”, 2015. Charles consisted primarily of anhydrite, limestone and interlaminated dolomitic lenses, along with trace to moderate reddish orange shale. Drill rates ranged from one to two minutes per foot. Background gas averaged 11 to 15 units. Base of the last salt was picked at 8673 feet measured depth, 8,672 feet true vertical depth, with no signiﬁcant drill break, but conﬁrmed by gamma. Samples following consisted primarily of limestone. anhydrite, and minor dolomite. with a varying presence of shale through the Mission Canyon.
Mission Canyon 8,848’ MD 8,84 7’ TVD -6, 762’ SS
Mission Canyon was picked at 8,848 feet measured depth, 8847 feet true vertical depth. The Mission Canyon consisted of predominantly light to medium cream gray. rnicrocrystalline, fairly argillaceous limestone. off white to cream white anhydrite and light tan micro to cryptocrystalline dolomite. Drill rates averaged .5 to 1.1 minutes per foot through the Mission Canyon, with 11 to 15 units of average background gas. Even white mineral ﬂuorescence and slow, pinpoint streaming cut present in majority of samples caught. Due to low gas readings, a bump test was performed at the shakers and confirmed correct operation of gas equipment.
Lodgepole 9,524 ’ MD 9,523 ’ TVD -7,438’ SS
Lodgepole was picked at 9,524 feet measured depth, 9,523 feet true vertical depth using MWD gamma data. Drill rates averaged .8 minutes per foot in the initial portion of the Lodgepole. Background gas continued to average 11 to 15 units through the Lodgepole. An additional bump gas test was successfully performed on the evening of January 8 after background gas continued to average 11 units.
