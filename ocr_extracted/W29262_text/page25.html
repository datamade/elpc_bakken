Mission Canyon 7,951 ’ MD 7,949’ TVD -6,045 ’SS
The Mission Canyon formation top was picked at 7,951’ MD/-6,045’ SS, and was picked from lithology change and a temporary increase in ROP when transitioning from the Charles formation limestone into Mission Canyon limestone. ROP in the Mission Canyon ranged from 0.30 to 1.80 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranging from 10 to 22 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were medium to light and dark gray, medium to light and dark brown, packstone to wackestone, moderately argillaceous, moderately carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair intergranular porosity, light to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with slow to fast streaming cuts.

Lodgepole

The Lodgepole formation top was picked at 8,543’ MD/-6,636’ SS, where samples changed from a light to medium brown and gray limestone to a medium to dark gray limestone. ROP in the Lodgepole ranged from 0.20 to 6.60 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, with gas measurements ranging from 10 to 1,.363 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone which were medium to dark and light gray, brown to light brown in part, argillaceous, wackestone, silty, slightly carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair to good intergranular porosity, light green to dark green/ gray ﬂuorescence, with moderately fast streaming cuts.
Curve Section 8,850’ — 9,689 ’ MD

Drilling rates in the curve ranged from 0.20 to 6.60 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, and gas measurements ranged from 10 to 1,363 units.
