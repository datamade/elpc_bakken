Upper Bakken 11,270’ MD 11,142’ TVD -8,814’ SS

The Bakken formation was penetrated at 11,270 feet measured depth and 11,142 feet true vertical depth. The upper shale was initially identiﬁed with an increased penetration rate followed by a sharp increase in gamma values and eventually veriﬁed in sample. The cuttings of the upper shale were a very dark brown to black, slightly calcareous shale with no indication of porosity or permeability. Distinct hydrocarbon shows, primarily at its top and bottom contacts were present in the form of ﬂuorescence and cut. Gas readings in the shale were the strongest recorded in the vertical/curve section of the well, peaking at just over 3,300 total units of gas.

Entry into the middle Bakken came at measured depth of 11,330 feet and true vertical depth 11,159 feet. Initial samples of the middle member began with Tan to pale brown, translucent dolomite with rare inclusions of detrital sediment. a ﬁinction of the increasing amount of clastic inclusions and bioturbation in the dolomitic Bakken member.

On December 8*, 2014 at 7:00 am CT the end of curve was reached at a measured depth of 11,485 feet, and a projected true vertical depth of 11,173 feet, slightly high to center within the middle Bakken. The curve was completed after 7.7 hours of “on bottom/bit” time. At 11 vertical feet below the upper Bakken shale the wellbore was positioned at the top of an 8 foot target window that would be applied for the lateral leg of the well.
