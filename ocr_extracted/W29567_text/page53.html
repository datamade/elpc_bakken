GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY

The Java 24-30H well is located in Section 30 T150N R90W in McLean County, North Dakota. It is a horizontal well targeting the 2'Ill Bench of The Three Forks Formation with surface spud on October 23rd and intermediate being spud on November 5‘“, 2014. The Java reached a total depth of 26,715’ MD on November 28‘”, 2014. 9 5/8” surface casing was set to 1,940’ MD. 7” casing was set at 11,362’ MD. The mud system was invert for the vertical and curve portion and salt water for the horizontal. Trevor and Kathleen Meyers of Neset ConSulting Service Inc. provided mudlogging and geological services for the vertical and curve. Dan and Lynda Griffin were additionally brought on during the horizontal section. M9 gas detector services were utilized throughout this well, along with Total Gas detection in the horizontal to conﬁrm gas readings. Samples were caught and saved for archive at 30’ intervals during the vertical and curve sections starting at 7,500 MD. 100’ samples were caught throughout the horizontal section. Sample quality was good in the vertical and curve. At times samples were poor in the lateral due to lube sweeps.
