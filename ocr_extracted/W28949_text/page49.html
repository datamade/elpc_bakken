Dear Mr. Robinette:
This letter is to conﬁrm that American Engineering Testing, Inc. (AET) has completed the laboratory work for the soil chemistry and physical properties of the soil for the soil cement stabilization performed at this well site. The soil has been stabilized with cement to improve the stability and load carrying capacity of the subgrade, allowing a thinner layer of aggregate surfacing to be placed in the working and driving areas and reducing future maintenance time
and costs.

Sampling and Testing
Soil samples were obtained from the site by representatives of Samson Resources Company on August 15, 2014. The samples were then delivered to the Dickinson, ND ofﬁce for speciﬁc testing.

The chemical laboratory test results were performed by American Engineering Testing. The material was tested for sodium absorption ratio (SAR), cation exchange capacity (CBC), electric conductance (EC) and pH. The physical properties of the soil were determined by AET and included hydraulic conductivity as well as Atterberg Limits, wash 200 sieve analysis, and
standard Proctor moisture-density relationship.
