At 13,229’ MD it became apparent that the MWD tool was not reading the correct inclination values, as a result the TVD values were incorrect. The directional driller was sliding down trying to bring the well back into the target window. As he was doing this the inclination and TV was rising, with an inclination of 91 .8° at 13,128’ MD. Gamma ray response values and samples did not match these readings. Gamma was consistently between 70 API and 80 API, which matches up with the lower part of the target window. The samples were also a light to medium cream gray siltstone, not the very light tan siltstone found above the target window. After some trouble shooting, it was decided to TOOH and replace the MWD tool. This occurred at 13,229’ MD, at 12:30 pm CST on December 18, 2014. Drilling operations resumed 15 hours later at 3:30 pm CST on December 19, 2014.
Surveys were reshot at measured depths of 12,950’; 13,042’; and 13,134’. Before the well bore could be turned around it reached a maximum depth of 10,138.6’ TVD at 13,727’ MD.
