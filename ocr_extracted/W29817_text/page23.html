SC Norma 154-98-0706—H-8

Summary of events:

On December 26th at approximate 21:30 hours while running 7” casing, we encountered tight hole at a depth of 11,600' MD in the Middle Bakken formation
Attempts to establish circulation were unsuccessful and the casing could not be pulled more than 18’ back up the hole.
Over the next 9 hours, the casing was successfully worked down the hole to the intended landing point at 11,768’ MD, but circulation could not be established.
Field inspector Tammy Cook was notified of the plans to perforate per the depths outlined in the table below.
The table below also shows that all hardline requirements have been met. The casing was perforated with 2 spf from 11,324’ to 11,328’
Circulation was established staging up to 5 BPM.
The 7" casing was cemented as follows:

127 bbls 11.8 ppg EconoCem lead cement. 168 bbls of 14 ppg ExtendaCem Tail Cement.

The cement was displaced with 398 bbls SW leaving 10 bbls of cement in casing above perfs, There were full returns throughout the cement job. Calculated tops are as follows:
0 876' for lead slurry
o 5456’ for tail slurry

Plan forward:

Tag cement top and record in daily drilling reports
Before drilling out, test casing to 1500 psi and chart for 30 minutes.
Ram test pressure up to 3500 psi for ten minutes.
Drill out perforations.
Test perforations to 1500 psi for 30 minutes — note and record any pressure loss.
Notify NDIC field inspector and drilling engineer of test results.
Obtain permission from NDIC field inspector prior to drilling out cement and drilling ahead. Drill lateral as per original well plan.
After TD is reached, we will run a CBL and report TOC.
From there, we will run in hole with the completion liner. The liner hanger will be set above the perforations at approximately 100 ft above KOP 10,778’ (MD)
