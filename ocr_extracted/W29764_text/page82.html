The ﬁrst sample caught in the Middle Bakken was described as dolomite; gray, light gray, cream gray, microcrystalline, sucrosic, argillaceous, poorly indurated; 10% shale, black, ﬁssile, blocky, carbonaceous, 15% siltstone, light gray, medium gray, argillaceous, poorly indurated; fair to good porosity; even dull green ﬂuorescence, bright cloudy blue cut with faint yellow streamers. Three Forks 10,299’ 1WD 10,144’ T VD -7,909’ SS

The top of the Three Forks was picked at a decrease in rate of penetration and

gamma, as well as a change in sample from black shale to gray tan sandstone. Rate of penetration in the Three Forks while drilling the curve ranged from 0.33 to 1.27 minutes per foot and averaged 0.60 minutes per foot. Total gas ranged from 22 to 499 units and averaged 295 units Gamma ranged from 35 to 119 API and averaged 76 API. Initially samples were sandstone; gray tan, light gray, tan, very ﬁne, subrounded, well sorted, argillaceous, silty, poorly cemented, dolomitic cement; 10% shale, light gray green, pyritic, blocky; scattered siltstone, medium gray; poor porosity; scattered dull green ﬂuorescence, milky green yellow cut. Samples transitioned to a majority tan sandstone with fair porosity. Intermediate casing point was reached at 10,382’ measured depth on December 7, 2014 at 5:45 pm. Projection to bit put the wellbore at a TVD of 10,157’ at 85.35 degrees, approximately 5’ above the top of the target window.
