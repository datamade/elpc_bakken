Lodgepole 10,464’MD 10,461' TVD -8,025’SS
Continuing to drill ahead, penetration rates averaged 35 - 70 ft/hr while samples showed mostly gray to gray brown dolomitic limestone. The top of the Lodgepole was picked at 10,461’ TVD using a slight change in drill rates and gamma to conﬁrm the Lodgepole. Drill rates began to drop off slightly once several feet into the Lodgepole formation. KOP was reached at 10,754’ MD and a trip out of the hole was made to pick up curve tools.
Beginning the build portion, drill rates averaged 40 — 6O ft/hr while sliding 9O — 100 %. Samples continued to show gray argillaceous dolomitic limestone. Gamma markers in the upper portion of the formation were surprisingly good, each generating consistent landing targets. The upper to middle Lodgepole markers suggested the Middle Bakken was coming in slightly lower than anticipated, but no changes to target were made.
Markers through the middle and lower portions of the Lodgepole showed the distance between markers was slightly thicker than the ﬁrst half of the formation. This suggested the Middle Bakken would come in on target. No changes to target were made at the time since current target was within a few feet of the ideal landing target.
Upper Bakken 11,451 ’ MD 11,260’ TVD -8,824’ SS
The top of the Upper Bakken was reached at approximately 11,451’ MD / 11,260’ TVD, 5’ lower than originally predicted on the prognosis. The formation top was ﬁrst estimated in real time with drill rates then reﬁned by the gamma. Samples transitioned to black carbonaceous shale with total gas increasing to average 250 — 300 units and over 400 units at connection. After reaching the base of the shale the total thickness was estimated to be 18’, 2’ thinner than
predicted on the prognosis.

Middle Bakken 11,508’ MD 11,2 78' T VD -8,842 ’ SS
At 1 1,508' MD weight on bit increased as rates of penetration decreased, gamma began dropping below 150 API to conﬁrm the top of the Middle Bakken, 3’ lower than the original estimated top. This suggested a ﬁnal landing target of 11,298‘ TVD at 20 feet below the upper shale, 3’ lower than ﬁnal landing target. Samples transitioned to gray argillaceous siltstone with drill rates averaging 20 — 40 ft/hr, total gas averaging 100 — 250 units. Continuing to drill ahead, wellbore dropped in structure slightly as gamma ﬂuctuated around 80 — 90 API. Total depth of the curve was reached at 11,616’ MD on December 18, 2014 at 10:45 am CST.
