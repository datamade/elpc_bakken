Logging services began at 7,340’ MD in the bottom of the Kibbey formation on December 3, 2014. The gas detection system was tested before reaching logging depth to ensure that the system was functioning correctly. The logged vertical section was drilled through the Charles and Mission Canyon formations, and into the Lodgepole formation, where rate of penetration (ROP) ranged from 0.20 to 1.50 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranged from 4 to 112 units. GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

EOG Resources Parshall 98-3 3H is a horizontal well located in SW/SE Section 33 T152N R9OW in Mountrail County, North Dakota, and was drilled by H&P 316, with surface and Kelly bushing elevations of 1,980’ and 2,004’ respectively. The three phases of Parshall 98-33H were vertical, curve, and horizontal sections, with the horizontal section targeting the Middle Bakken formation. Parshall 98-33H had a spud date of December 1, 2014, with geological logging and gas detection services beginning in the Kibbey formation. Lagged thirty foot samples (with occasional lagged ten foot samples to determine formation tops in all sections) were collected in the vertical and curve sections, and lagged one hundred foot samples were collected in the horizontal section. All samples were washed, dried, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core and Sample Library, located on the campus of the University of North Dakota in the city of Grand Forks. Parshall 98-33H reached TD of 14,610’ MD at 7:20 PM CST on December 8, 2014 in NW/NW Section 33 T152N R9OW.
