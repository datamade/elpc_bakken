Charles

The Charles Formation was encountered at 7,918’ MD and 7,916’ TVD. The Charles was picked using both a change in the gamma ray response and a change in lithology. Above the contact gamma values average in the mid 40’s API and an average ROP of 99 ft/hr. Below the contact gamma value quickly decrease to below 20 API and ROP almost doubles to about 185 feet per hour. This low gamma and quick ROP remains fairly consistent for the next 200’.
Shortly after the top of the Charles Formation is crossed the samples switch from a reddish brown siltstone to a translucent salt, with typical samples being: SALT: Clear, white, transparent, blocky, frosty, hard, no porosity, no ﬂuorescence, weak cloudy cut.
Towards the bottom the salt become less of the majority and samples become a light gray limestone. They are most commonly described as: LIMESTONE: Light to medium gray, gray tan, light brown, light cream gray, mudstone to wackestone, cryptocrystalline, argillaceous, poor to moderate induration, trace anhydrite, cream, soﬁ, pasty, poor intergranular porosity, scattered dull yellow ﬂuorescence only, instant weak hazy cut. Minor and trace amounts of salt are still found. As stated above, ROP for roughly 200’ of the formation was 185 ft/hr and the average ROP for the entire formation was 81 feet per hour. Gamma ray response values ranged from 1 API to 81 API, with an average of 20 API.
