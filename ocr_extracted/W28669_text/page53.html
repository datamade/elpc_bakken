Lodgepole 9, 846’ MD 9,843’ TVD -7,13 7’ SS
The Lodgepole Formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,846 feet. The top of the Lodgepole was initially evaluated using a correlation between gamma ray in this well and the Kessel #41-26PH. There was also a change in samples from a pale to light tan limestone at the base of the Mission Canyon formation, to a light to medium gray limestone in the top of the Lodgepole formation.
A KOP of 10,060’ MD was reached at 9:30 PM MDT on July 28th, 2014. Using the Lodgepole top, and several gamma markers before KOP as a reference, the landing target for the curve was set at 10,628’ TVD, roughly 10 feet into the Pronghorn formation.
Aﬁer tripping out of the hole, picking up the curve assembly, and tripping back to bottom, they started building the curve at 8:00 AM MDT on July 29th, 2014.
