Curve 9,828’ — 10,654’ MD
The Barb W. l 1-6TFH curve section began with a KOP of 9,828’ MD. Drilling times in the Lodgepole below KOP were between 1-5 minutes sliding. Gas varied between 9-317 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the Lodgepole to correlate to the JB 11-6TFH hole. Target stayed at 10,3 59’ based on Lodgepole correlations.
Upper Bakken Shale 10,234’MD 10,201’ TVD -8,129’SS
The Upper Bakken Shale was entered at 10,201’ TVD. Background gas was from 36 to 317 units in the shale. A typical sample was Shale: Black, very dark gray,
carbonaceous, calcareous, pyritic, gritty, blocky, even pale yellow ﬂuorescence, instant bright green milky cut.
Middle Bakken 10,269’ 1WD 10,221 ’ TVD -8,149’ SS
At 10,221’ TVD the Middle Bakken was encountered. This top was picked from both lithology and gamma changes. The sample lithology of the Middle Bakken consisted of sandstone. A typical sample exhibited medium to light gray sandstone, light to medium grayish brown, with beige to off-white inclusions, very ﬁne grained, subrounded, calcareous, limey cementation, moderate sorting, poor intergranular porosity, scattered yellow ﬂuorescence, cloudy bright yellow green cut.
Lower Bakken Shale 10,350’ MD 1 0,28 7’ T VD -8,215 ’ SS
The Lower Bakken Shale was entered at 10,287’ TVD. Background gas was from 124 to 286 units in the shale. A typical sample was Shale: Medium gray, grayish brown, trace dark gray, some mottled, carbonaceous, pyritic, gritty, blocky, even pale yellow green ﬂuorescence, instant bright cloudy yellow green cut.
Three Forks 10,42 7’ MD 10,333 ’ T VD -8,261 ’ SS
At 10,333’ TVD the Three Forks was entered. The top was picked from both lithology and gamma changes. The typical samples consisted of siltstone that was medium grayish brown, light grayish brown, trace off white, arenaceous, argillaceous, dolomitic, with poor induration. Lithology also included trace to moderate shale that was medium bluish gray, waxy, platy, blocky, with moderate induration. Samples exhibited poor intergranular porosity, light brown oil staining, even to scattered dull to pale yellow green ﬂuorescence, with generally instant bright yellow to yellow-green cuts.
When we reached 10,654' MD at 2:45pm on November 9, 2014 the decision was made to cement back and re drill the curve due to not getting build rates.
