RE: Drummond 5501 42-21 wells

Dear Mr. Holweger:

I have discussed options for noise reduction and security with our construction and drilling teams. Per our conversation, the following letter provides additional detail regarding Oasis Petroleum’s plan for noise reduction and security at the Drummond 5501 42-21 well pad.
The rig that will be used to drill the well uses a disc brake system, which is known to be significantly quieter than rigs that use drum brakes. A sound muffler will also be used on the sound suppresser.
The top soil stockpile will be located along the east side of the pad, which should help act as both a visual and sound buffer for residents. The dirt excavated for the cuttings pit will also be located along the east and northern sides of the pad, continuing the buffer. Although close in proximity, the dirt from the two stockpiles will not be commingled.
There will be no access road on the east side ofthe pad. The only access will come from the south side.
A fence will surround the perimeter of the pad after drilling operations have concluded and interim reclamation has occurred. Signs will also be posted to discourage trespassers. As with the Harrier pad, Keith Oyloe and Vern Barman will be available 24/7 for any emergencies. Their contact information is 701-577—1613 and 701-577-1646, respectively.
