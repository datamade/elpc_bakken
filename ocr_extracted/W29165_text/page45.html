Geologic Summary

The SC-Norma-LE-l 54-98-0705H-2 well was drilled to be a single lateral, targeting the Three Forks production potential in Williams County, North Dakota. The surface location for the well is located 321 ‘ FEL & 525’ FSL in Section 7 T154N R98W. The well was drilled from south to north ﬁnishing at approximately 303.67‘ FNL & 192.01 ‘ FWL in Section 5 T154N R98W of Williams County. Kelly bushing elevation was estimated at 2,436’. This well was drilled using an invert (oil based) mud system during the straight hole and build portion and salt water throughout the lateral. Jared Bryl and Thomas Osadchuk of Neset Consulting Service Inc., provided wellsite mudlogging services beginning at a depth of 8,800’ MD through the end of the Three Forks lateral, which reached a total measured depth of 20,679” MD on February 25, 2015 at 9:10 pm CST. Two sets of samples were saved to be archived at both the Hess Corporation in Houston, Texas, as well as the North Dakota Geological Survey in Grand Forks,
North Dakota.

The top of the Charles was reached at approximately 8,996' TVD as drill rates nearly doubled to 90 — 120 ft/hr with salt observed in samples. Top of Charles was 31’ higher than originally expected, but was within several feet of the other wells. Drill rates continued to average 90 — 120 ft/hr while drilling in the salt, picking up off bottom occasionally to avoid getting stuck. An abundant amount of salt was observed in all samples with varying amounts of gray to gray brown dolomitic limestone. Total gas continued to average around 10 — 30 units.
