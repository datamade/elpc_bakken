Over the next 1,000’ from 19,000” MD to 20,000‘ wellbore remained predominantly in the upper half of the target window and appeared to be following dip of formation. Correlations continued to show a consistent up — dip, but were not real strong as several different interpretations were plausible resulting in uncertainty of wellbore’s position in zone. The target was lowered 4’ TVD and inclination changed to 90.2 degrees. This change in targets took place at approximately 19,932” MD, or 9,000‘ VS. Samples were of little help as there was little to no change in the overall consistency of the samples. Penetration rates continued to average 75 ~ 140 ft/hr. Gamma was from 65 to 125 API units. Total gas was from 190 — 400 units with increases at connections. Continuing to drill past 20,000” MD to 20679 wellbore continued to act as if it were high in zone, but correlations were still poor. Wellbore eventually bounced off a hard steak in formation causing well to quickly move up in formation before dropping back down. This generated a good correlation and conﬁrmed well was higher in formation than originally thought. This also suggested that formation dip was toe down in the ﬁnal few hundred feet.
