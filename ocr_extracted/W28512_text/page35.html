Lodgepole

The top of the Lodgepole was encountered at 10,071’ MD, 10,064” TVD picked 1 foot lower than prognosis, based on sample changes, ROP, and differential pressure. Drill rates in the Lodgepole above KOP of 10,400’ MD ranged from 0.3—2.8 minutes per foot with average penetration rate of 0.81 minutes per foot, Gas ranged between 84-168 units averaging 113 units. Typical samples were: LIMESTONE: Gray to dark gray, light gray brown to gray brown, light gray tan to light gray, cryptocrystalline, argillaceous, dolomitic in part, fair to good induration, poor to no intergranular porosity, trace anhydrite, white to cream tan, soft to pasty, patchy dull yellow green ﬂorescence, weak
milky cut. Curve tools were picked up once kick off point of 10,400’ MD was reached at 10:30 AM MST on August 15, 2014.
Curve 10,400’ — 11,292’ MD
Drilling of the curve began at 12:15 AM MST on August 16, 2014, An initial landing target of 10,879’ TVD was set before curve was started. Drilling rates in the Lodgepole below KOP ranged from 03—338 minutes per foot with an average of 0.85 minutes per foot. Gas in the Lodgepole below KOP ranged from 62-7964 units with average of 552 units. Gamma ranged from 18-82 API with an average of 34 API. Typical samples were: LIMESTONE: Gray to dark gray, light gray to light gray brown, cryptocrystalline, argillaceous, fair to good induration, poor to no intergranular porosity, even dull blue green ﬂorescence, weak milky cut. Gamma markers throughout the Lodgepole and formation tops of the False Bakken, Upper Bakken Shale, and Middle Bakken were used to correlate with the Britt 151-102—4-9—4H and Wahlstrom 3-10H showing a landing target around 10,879’ TVD, the same as the original well prognosis landing target of 10,879’ TVD.
