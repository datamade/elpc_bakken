Mission Canyon

The Mission Canyon formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9, 809’, true vertical depth of 9,808’. The top of the Mission Canyon was picked off an increase in penetration rates as well as comparisons to the offsetting wells porosity benches throughout the Mission Canyon. Penetration rates throughout the majority of the Mission Canyon averaged around 120 - 140 ft/hr with increases up to 160 ft/hr seen throughout the different porosity benches. The samples throughout the upper 100' of the formation exhibited primarily a gray brown/gray tan limestone with trace to moderate anhydrite present and occasional dolomite mixed in. The remainder of the formation exhibited primarily limestone with trace amounts of cream to off-white anhydrite present. Background gases averaged around 100 — 160 units.

Curve 10,635 ' — 11,460’ MD
The curve for the South lateral was started in the Lodgepole Formation at a measured depth of 10,635' at 11:20 pm. CST on December 3, 2014. An 8 3/4” Security bit and 2.38 degree ﬁxed mud motor were used to build the curve. The landing target for the Amegard #21-26-3H at the beginning of the curve was set at 11,154’ @ 90 deg. based off the Lodgepole top and the ﬁrst and second LP markers. The Amegard #21-26H and the H] Rogness ET AL 1-26 were used for correlation purposes through the curve. Penetration rates at the beginning of the curve averaged around 100 - 120 ﬁ/hr while sliding. Background gases through the Lodgepole formation averaged around 200 — 500 units. The lithology of the Lodgepole exhibited primarily a cream gray tan/light to medium gray limestone with occasional anhydrite and shale present. Gamma markers began to show that the lower portion of the Lodgepole had thickened slightly. Aﬁer seeing this, the TVD landing target was lowered to a TVD of 11,158’. The remainder of the Lodgepole formation was drilled out and the False Bakken was encountered.
