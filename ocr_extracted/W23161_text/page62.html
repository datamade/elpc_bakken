Separation, Measurement and Allocation
Each well will continuously produce into a dedicated 2—phase separator. Oil and water will be continuously measured in a combined liquid phase via a Coriolis meter and NOC (net oil computer). Gas will be measured continuously via an orifice-plate or cone meter. After the individual well liquid volumes have been separately measured, liquid from each 2—phase separator will dump into a bulk three phase separator. Oil will dump from the 3—phase separator into tanks or to a pipeline LACT (lease automated custody transfer). Water will dump from the 3—phase separator to tanks or a pipeline LACT
The oil sales volume per well will be calculated as follows:
1.
The meter factor will represent the most recent valid meter factor based on quarterly master meter proving. The meter factor will include a correction to standard conditions based on proving data, measured fluid temperatures and pressures.
Measured daily well volume will be the measured oil volume as recorded by the coriolis meter.
The measured daily well volume will be corrected to standard conditions via the net oil computer. Measurement will be done continuously. Triangle personnel will verify the NOC (net
oil computer) measurement daily. Measured meter data will be stored and archived.
Measured monthly well volumes will represent the sum ofthe prior months measured daily volumes times the meter factor.
The measured monthly pad volume will represent the sum of the measured monthly well volumes from each well. The custody transfer pad volume will represent the actual volume sold through a LACT or tank
gauges and trucks. The monthly well sales volume will be calculated by multiplying the custody transfer pad volume
times the measured monthly well volume divided by the monthly measured pad volume. Well revenue will be calculated based on the monthly well sales volume.

The water volume per well will be calculated as follows:
1. The meter factor will represent the most recent valid meter factor based on quarterly master
meter or proving. 2. Measured daily well volume will be the measured water volume as recorded by the coriolis
meter. Measurement will be done continuously. Triangle personnel will verify the NOC (net oil computer) measurement daily. Measured meter data will be stored and archived.
3. Measured monthly well volume will represent the sum of the prior months measured daily volumes times the meter factor.
4. The measured monthly pad volume will represent the sum of the measured montth well
volumes from each well. 5. The custody transfer pad volume will represent the actual volume delivered through a LACT or tank gauges and trucks.
