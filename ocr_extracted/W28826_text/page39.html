GEOLOGICAL SUMMARY

The Whiting Oil and Gas Corporation Skedsvold #21-4H was drilled as a single lateral horizontal with the primary objective being in the Middle Bakken formation. This well is located in the NENW Section 4 T1 SON R101W of McKenzie County, North Dakota. The Skedsvold #21-4H was spud on October 27, 2014 and reached a total depth for the vertical & curve at 11,293’ MD on November 3, 2014, seven days from spud. Total depth of the South Lateral was reached at 20,940’ MD on November 12, 2014, sixteen days from spud. This well was drilled using an invert based mud system for the vertical & curve and Brine for the lateral. Shawn Morrow and Freddy Roberts of Neset Consulting Service, Inc. provided wellsite mudlogging services, beginning at a depth of 9,200’ in the Charles Formation, through total depth in the Middle Bakken target. A NCS Total Gas detector was utilized for monitoring gas. Thirty foot lagged samples in the vertical & curve and ﬁfty foot lagged samples in the South Lateral were caught and saved to be archived at the North Dakota Geological Survey Core Library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.

Vertical

Mudlogging began in the Charles Formation at a measured depth of 9,200’. Penetration rates throughout the Charles averaged around 150 - 200 ﬁ/hr through the salt benches and around 100 — 120 ft/hr throughout the limestone and anhydrite benches. Background gases in the Charles averaged around 50 - 75 units with connection gases reaching as high as 90 units. The samples throughout the Charles exhibited alternating benches of anhydrite and limestone with occasional shale and dolomite present with the
salt benches showing primarily a clear angular salt. The Charles was evaluated while drilling ahead.

The Base of the Last salt was encountered at a measured depth of 9,444’, true vertical depth of 9,423’, four foot low to prognosis. Penetration rates through the last salt averaged around 150 - 180 ft/hr with 5K weight on bit and penetration rates through the remainder of the Charles averaged around 120 - 140 ﬁ/hr with 30K weight on bit. The samples throughout the lower portion of the Charles exhibited primarily anhydrite and limestone with traces of cream tan dolomite and gray shale present. The remainder of the Charles was drilled out and the Ratcliﬂ‘e and Midale formations were encountered.
