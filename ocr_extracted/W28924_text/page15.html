GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

Slawson Exploration Company’s Howo 5-33-4TFH, NW NW Section 33 T152N R92W is a single Three Forks horizontal well located 2 miles south of New Town in Mountrail County, North Dakota. The drilling rig, CanElson #41 , began drilling on September 18, 2014 and ceased drilling aﬁer the lateral reached a total depth of 20,035’ on October 6, 2014. The bottom hole location is located in the SE% SW% of Section 4 T151N R92W.
Howo 5-33-4TFH landed the curve and drilled laterally within the First Bench of the Three Forks Formation (Late Devonian) within the Williston Basin. The vertical and curve segments were drilled with an 8%” bit and cased with 7” intermediate casing to a measured depth (MD) of 10,488’. The lateral extended another 9,547’ to a total depth of 20,035’. It was drilled with a 6” bit and cased with 4 U2” liner. The drilling ﬂuid was oil based mud for the vertical and curve segments but during the lateral it was substituted with brine.
Wellsite geological services began within the Kibbey Formation at a MD of 7,800’. Thirty foot lagged samples were collected during the vertical and curve, and ﬁﬁy foot lagged samples were collected in the lateral. Samples were cleaned, catalogued, and saved for archive at the North Dakota Geological Survey core library in Grand Forks, North Dakota.
Vertical 7,800 ’ - 9,682 ’ MD
Logging services began in the Kibbey Formation at a measured depth of 7,800’ at 8:15 pm CDT on September 23, 2014 aﬁer tripping into the hole with a new bit. The vertical segment was drilled with a Reed DS616M-01 type bit which was added at 7,719’ MD. The primary off-set well was Holst 2-33H as it was drilled on the same pad as Howo 5-33-4TFH. The samples collected within the Kibbey Formation were generally described as a light brown to red brown, slightly calcareous, dolomitic shaly siltstone. The ﬁrst sample from the Kibbey Formation had traces of the Kibbey Lime Marker which was described as a vuggy grainy limestone with traces of anhydrite. Formation gas in the Kibbey Formation averaged 65 units with the highest reading of 138 units detected from the Kibbey Lime. A Pason chromatograph gas detection system was used to monitor gas. logging services began. Charles 7,904’ MD 7,903’ TVD -5,997’ SS
The top of the Charles Formation was encountered at 7,904’ measured depth (MD) when a relatively massive salt unit, indicative of the Charles top, was encountered. Lithology identiﬁed in the Charles included salt, anhydrite, mudstone, crystalline limestone, and dolomitic limestone. Fast and consistent penetration rates deﬁned the Charles’ salt benches fairly well. Along with gamma data, drilling signatures such as differential pressure, weight on bit, rotary and torque were also useful in determining the depths of the salts with indications of easy drilling. Penetration rates averaged 82fph throughout the Charles Formation but up to 115fph in the salts. Formation gas readings were fairly consistent at about 94 units, however, surges in gas were at times detected directly below impermeable evaporate layers. The highest readings were detected at the base of the formation in sandier, more permeable layers which gave readings near 140 units. The heavier gases were also more prevalent near the Charles base.

Base of Last Salt 8,435 ’ MD 8,434 ’ TVD -6,528’ SS
The depth of the Base of the Last Salt (BLS) was encountered where expected at a true vertical depth (TVD) of 8,434’. This depth was determined by drilling signatures and analysis of samples.
