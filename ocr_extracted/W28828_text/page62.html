The top of the Mission Canyon was picked oﬂ‘ an increase in penetration rates as well as comparisons to the offsetting wells porosity benches throughout the Mission Canyon. Curve 10,490' - 11,293’ MD
The curve for the South lateral was started in the Lodgepole Formation at a measured depth of 10,490' at 3:40 am. CDT on September 7, 2014. An 8 3/4” Hughes bit and 2.38 degree ﬁxed mud motor were used to build the curve. The landing target for the Skedsvold #21-4-4H at the beginning of the curve was set at 10,945’ @ 90 deg. based off the Lodgepole top and the ﬁrst two LP markers. The Skedsvold #21-4-5H and the Skedsvold #150—101-4B-9-1H were used for correlation purposes through the curve. Penetration rates at the beginning of the curve averaged around 80 - 90 ﬁ/hr while sliding. Background gases through the Lodgepole formation averaged around 150 ~ 300 units. The lithology of the Lodgepole exhibited primarily a cream gray tan/light to medium gray limestone with occasional anhydrite and shale present. Gamma markers began to show that the lower portion of the Lodgepole had thickened slightly. After seeing this, the TVD landing target was lowered to a TVD of 10,948’.
