4. THE PROPOSED CASING AND CEMENTING PROGRAM
Whiting Oil & Gas Corporation (Whiting) proposes to drill a vertical hole in the SWSE (452' FSL, 1868' FEL) of Section 19 in Township 147 North, Range 91 West to an approximate TVD of 9701' (KOP) at which time directional drilling operations will be initiated on an azimuth direction of 70° with a 12°/ 100 build and will end the build at 10288' TVD, 10804' MD and an inclination of 90° located 991' FSL and 1361' FEL of Section 19 in Township 147 North, Range 91 West, at which point the 7” intermediate casing will land. The horizontal lateral azimuth will be held at 360° thru the Three Forks Pay with a hold of 90° inclination. The projected bottom hole target location of 559' FNL and 1362' FEL of Section 18 in Township 147 North, Range 91 West. Total length of lateral is approximately 8999'.

The 7” intermediate casing will he landed in the horizontal section of the Bakken Formation before commencement of drilling the lateral.
Whiting may elect to have the liner isolated with swell packers or equivalent.

The surface casing will have centralizers on the bottom three (3) joints with a stop ring utilized midway on the shoe joint. Then every third joint for a total of 17 centralizers.
Casing string(s) will be pressure tested to 0.22 psi/foot of casing string length or 1,500 psi, whichever is greater (not to exceed 70% of the internal yield strength of the casing), after cementing and prior to drilling out from under the casing shoe.
The 7” intermediate casing string has been designed for service in a sour gas (H23) and salt environment. The intermediate casing will have turbolizers on the bottom three (3) joints, then every other joint to 45 degrees. Centralizers will be used every other jt from 45 degrees to KOP, then every fourth joint to the top of the Dakota sands.
The 4%“ liner will be centralized with standoff bands on every other joint.
