Conclusion

The SC-Norma-154-98-0706H-7 was drilled as a north Middle Bakken lateral in the Truax ﬁeld. As the structure map indicated and past wells in the area had shown, formation dip was expected to change several times throughout the entire lateral. Dip was expected to be mostly ﬂat through the beginning portion of the lateral and then transition to a toe-up through the middle portion, while the last few thousand feet was expected to once again be ﬂat. Average dip rate was expected to be around 90.27 degrees over the entire lateral.
Gamma signature and correlations were weak to begin the lateral as wellbore started extremely ﬂat, covering very little formation. Formation dip was estimated at 89.95 — 90.05 degrees. Correlations remained difficult for much of the well due to lack of contact with clean gamma marker at the top of the geologic zone. Even when contact with the clean maker was made, correlations remained incredibly difﬁcult due to a somewhat anomalous gamma signature afterwards. Formation dip for the ﬁnal 1,500’ was estimated at 90.4 degrees, similar to the way other wells on the pad had ﬁnished. Even though formation dip changed several times throughout the lateral, overall wellbore position was estimated to be near the center of the geologic zone of interest for the majority of the lateral.
Lithology made several color transitions as wellbore moved down, through the zone and then back up into geologic zone. Samples typically showed light gray to light gray brown dolomitic siltstone with dull blue yellow ﬂuorescence and a slow weak cut. Through most of the lateral, total gas typically averaged around 500 — 1,200 units with slight increases at connections. Well was drilled at an average target inclination of 90.2 degrees with average formation dip estimated at 90.25 degrees for the entire lateral. Average formation dip was 0.02 degrees below the original anticipated well dip of 90.27 degrees. Upon reaching total depth of the lateral it was estimated well was drilled 94% in zone.
