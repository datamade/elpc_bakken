B. Reserve Pits

32. Reserve Pits: Closed systems are preferable to reserve pits. However, when used, reserve pits will be located in cut material.
Liner(s): A reserve pit liner will be required. The liner will have minimum burst strength of 300 pounds per square inch (psi). lfthe reserve pit is excavated through sand, coal or clinker/scoria, the pit will be covered (lined) with six (6) inches of clay before the liner is installed. This may require the resloping of the pit walls.
Fencing: The reserve pit will be fenced on three (3) non-working sides during drilling. After drilling is completed, the fourth side of the pit will be fenced until the pit is reclaimed. Fences will be constructed and maintained as specified.
Drilling Fluid Levels: All drilling fluids shall be contained in the reserve pit. Fluid levels will be no closer than one (1) foot from the top ofthe liner at all times. Excess fluids shall be hauled off to a proper disposal facility. Upon completion of the drilling of the well, the Operator shall remove and properly dispose of as much ofthe pit fluids as possible priorto closing the pit. Frozen pits must be treated or ice removed prior to closure.
Trenching: "Trenching" of the reserve pit during the pit reclamation phase is prohibited.
Muds and Cuttings: All muds and cuttings must remain in the pit. Reference reclamation of reserve pits for closing requirements.
Additional Reserve Pits: When an additional pit is permitted at a later date, it shall meet all the requirements within this COA. If the new pit has to be dug into the first or older pit, the material from the older pit shall be segregated and placed upon a liner and diked as specified. Upon completion of use of the new pit, the older materials shall be placed within the new pit and both the new material and older material shall be solidified in place or removed to an acceptable disposal site.
Solidification: "Solidification" ofthe reserve pit during the pit reclamation phase shall be as specified under Reclamation, COA 24.0.

C. Production Pits: Production pits are prohibited.
D. Fresh Water Pits: If approved, any fresh water pit will be constructed, lined, fenced, drained, and closed to the same standards as a Reserve Pit. Drilling fluids and/or other production fluids will not be contained in the fresh water pit.
