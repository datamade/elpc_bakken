GEOLOGIC SUMMARY

The Hess Comoration, LK-A QTR CIR-147-96-1807H-4 was drilled as a single lateral

horizontal well targeting the Middle Bakken Formation. The target zone was a ten foot thick window existing approximately ﬁfteen feet below the Upper Bakken Shale. The ground elevation is 2,627’ above sea level with a Kelly Bushing of 26’ to make the total KB at 2,653”. This well is located in Section 18 T147N R96W Dunn County, North Dakota. The LK-A QTR CIR-147-96-1807H-4 was spudded on January 5, 2015 and reached a total depth for the vertical hole and curve at 1 1,710 feet measured depth and 11,417’ true vertical depth on January 10, 2015, 6 days from spud.
Total depth of the LK-A QTR CIR—147-96-1807H-4 lateral was reached at 21,805’ measured depth on January 20, 2015 at 2:30 P.M. CDT. The bottom hole location is 384.54’ FNL and 1,725.81 ’ FWL, Section 7 T147N R96W. This well was drilled using an invert based mud system for the vertical / curve, and saline-rich salt water for the lateral. Tim Purdy and Devin Pays of Neset Consulting Service, Inc. provided the Well Site Geo-steering, Mud Logging and Gas Detection Services beginning at a depth of 9,030’ in the Kibbey Lime through the total depth in the Three Forks Formation. The services were provided through the curve and lateral portion of the Three Forks Fonnation. Samples were caught from lagged depths at intervals
between 10 — 100 feet.
Kibbey Lime 9,058 ’ MD 9,04 7’ TVD —6,394 ’ SS

The Kibbey Lime Formation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,058’ and a true

vertical depth of 9,047’, which was two feet higher than the prognosis. A typical sample was as follows LIMESTONE: Gray, brown, red, rust, tan, wackestone, argillaceous, trace carbonaceous, poorly to moderately indurated, scattered dull yellow white ﬂuorescence, slow pinpoint streaming to cloudy yellow cut.

Charles 9,201 ’ MD 9,190’ TVD -6,53 7’ SS
The Madison Fonnation was encountered at a measured depth of 9,201’ and a true vertical depth of 9,190’, which was one foot higher than the prognosis. A typical sample was as follows; ANHYDRITE: Gray, buff, light tan, pasty, very soft, ﬁne grained, poor porosity, trace limestone, Halite 35%, clear frosted, rounded to sub rounded, trace red brown siltstone, faint cloudy to thick streaming cut.
Base ofLast Salt 9, 790’ MD 9, 779’ TVD - 7,126 ’ SS
The Base of Last the Salt was encountered at a measured depth of 9,790’ and a true vertical depth of 9,779’, which was sixteen feet higher than the prognosis. A typical sample was as follows; HALITE: Translucent to white, light brown, light gray, 30% anhydrite, light gray, tan, off white, angular to subangular, blocky, poor induration, no visible porosity, even dull yellow-white ﬂuorescence, no cut.
