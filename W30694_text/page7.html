ABNORMAL CONDITIONS
No abnormal temperatures or pressures are anticipated. Hydrogen sulﬁde (HZS) may be encountered in the Mission Canyon Formation at a TVD of approximately 8940'. Safety equipment will be in operation at approximately 8440'.
A Hydrogen Sulﬁde (HZS) Contingency Plan is being prepared and will be submitted under separate cover for review and approval. As a safety precaution, the rig sub-structure will remain open during drilling operations to allow natural convection to dissipate any accumulations of hydrogen sulﬁde gas. Should HZS concentrations exceed 20 ppm, a “bug blower” fan will be activated at that position in order to dissipate the concentration of H28
gas to acceptable levels.
Maximum anticipated bottom hole pressure equals approximately 5729psi (calculated at 0.55 psi/foot at Bakken target depth, 10417’) and maximum anticipated surface pressure equals approximately 3438psi (bottom hole pressure minus the pressure of a partially evacuated hole calculated at 0.22 psi/foot at Bakken target depth). The intermediate design casing will be run and cemented before drilling the target lateral.
Reservoir temperature is anticipated to be 244°F.
