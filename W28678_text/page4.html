Sidetrack #1 12,940’ MD — 15,800’ MD
Sidetrack I began with time drilling from 12,940’ to 12,955’ MD. After successfully sidetracking, the bit came off the original wellbore and in to the clean gamma. As drilling continued the bit followed dip, remaining in the clean gamma, and indicated a change in dip had occurred through this part of the well, showing a hard dip of 88.1° — 88.6°. From 13,700’ to 13,860’ MD the bit scraped slightly below the clean and did so again from 14,420’ to 14,690’ MD. This was still in the target zone and gave a good dip calculation, verifying a continuing hard down dip. The bit remained in the clean portion of target zone until reaching the end of this section and showing the bit in the middle portion of the clean gamma.
This section showed an immediate increase in gas and oil shows as it separated from the original wellbore with background gas up to 1,500 units and connection gases averaging over 2,000 units.
