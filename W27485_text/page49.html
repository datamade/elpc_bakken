Accumulator—for actuation of chokes and BOP’s:
Accumulator will have sufficient capacity to open hydraulically-controlled choke line valve (if so equipped). close all rams plus annular preventer, and retain a minimum of 200 psi above precharge on the closing manifold without the use of closing unit pumps. The fluid reserve capacity will be double accumulator capacity and the fluid level of reservoir will be maintained at manufacturer’s recommendations.
BOP System will have two independent power sources to close the preventers, including nitrogen bottles that would maintain a charge equal to the manufacturer’s specifications. The Accumulator pre-charge pressure test will be conducted prior to connecting the closing unit to the BOP stack and at least every six months after that. The accumulator pressure would be corrected if the measured pre-charge pressure is found to be above or below the minimum or maximum limits specified by the Bureau of Land Management (BLM) Onshore Oil and Gas Order No. 2.
A manual or automatic locking device would be installed on all systems with pressure ratings of 2,000 psi or more. A valve would be installed in the closing line as close as possible to the annular preventer to act as a locking device. This valve would be maintained in the open position and wold be closed only when the power source for the accumulator system is inoperative. Remote controls shall be readily accessible to the driller. Remote controls for all 3,000 psi or greater systems would be capable of closing all preventers. Remote controls for 5,000 psi or greater systems would be capable of both opening and closing all preventers. Master controls would be at the accumulator and would be capable of opening and closing all preventers and choke line valve.
