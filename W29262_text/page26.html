The curve section was landed in the Middle Bakken at an 84.50O angle at 9,689' MD/9,364.54’ TVD on November 7, 2014 at 8:00 PM CST. The ﬁnal curve TD depth of 9,689’ MD was conﬁrmed when the bottoms up sample at this depth contained light bluish gray Middle Bakken dolomite with trace amounts of very dark brown/ gray to dull black shale slough off.
Horizontal Section
After the intermediate casing shoe was set to 9,689’ MD, drilling began in the horizontal section at 6:30 AM CST on November 9, 2014. The Middle Bakken top was ofﬁcially determined at 9,665’ MD/—7,458’ SS from a decrease in gamma values as the gamma tool transitioned from the UBS into the Middle Bakken dolomite. The curve was generally leveled off within the drilling window at a ~90° angle near measured depth of 10,000’ MD, and the GR Marker was ﬁrst encountered at 9,769” MD/—7,47l’ SS. The formation dip angle from 9,600’ — 10,700’ MD averaged 90.3 degrees, and from 10,700’ - 11,900’ averaged slightly greater than 90 degrees. The formation dip angle from 1 1,900’ — 15,000’ then angled up again at an average of 90.2 degrees, and then ﬂattened out from 15,000’ MD to TD of 16,090’ MD with a dip angle of 90 to 90.1 degrees.
