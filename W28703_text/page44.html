Procedure for Production Allocation
a.
Each well will be tested for a minimum of three days per quarter with an approximate 24hour test duration. Exact time of test start and test end will be adjusted to yield true 24 hour tests Each well’s downtime will be monitored to determining actual producing time for each well Daily production per well is calculated as follows: i. For Oil— Daily calculated volume equals well test oil rate times producing hours during each day ii. For Water- Daily calculated volume equals well test water rate times producing hours during each day iii. For Gas- Daily calculated volume equals established well GOR times daily calculated oil volume The daily calculated volume from each well is totaled for all wells to determine the calculated total daily volume, and a correction factor is determined by dividing the actual total daily volume by the calculated total daily volume. Each well’s allocated daily production is calculated by multiplying its individual daily calculated oil, water and gas volumes by the correction factor.

Measurement

A minimum of one vertical heater treater will be designated as the “test” treater and will have the following meters installed. i. Oil — 1.5” Turbines Inc. TM0200 Turbine Meter with a Cameron MCIII EXP Turbine Meter Flow Analyzer ii. Water — 1.5” Turbines Inc. TM0200 Turbine Meter with 21 Cameron MCIII EXP Turbine Meter Flow Analyzer iii. Gas — 3” Fox Thermal Mass Meter, Model FT3

Notes a.
A treater inlet manifold will be constructed to switch the wells between the “test” and the “bulk” treaters.
All recycle will go to the bulk treater.
Liquids collected from the scrubber(s) on the sales gas lines will be drained into the oil production line downstream of the allocation meters. Drained liquid from the scrubbers will be allocated based on the oil allocation percentages determined by the above procedure for total site oil production allocation.
