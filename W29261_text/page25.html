Mission Canyon

The Mission Canyon formation top was picked at 7,951’ MD/-6,045’ SS, and was picked from lithology change and a temporary increase in ROP when transitioning from the Charles formation limestone into Mission Canyon limestone. ROP in the Mission Canyon ranged from 0.30 to 5.60 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, and gas measurements ranging from 6 to 81 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone, which were medium to light and dark gray, medium to light and dark brown, packstone to wackestone, moderately argillaceous, moderately carbonaceous, moderately well indurated, fair intergranular porosity, light to dark green and dark gray ﬂuorescence, with slow to fast streaming cuts.
Lodgepole 8,542’ MD 8,540’ TVD -6,636 ’ SS
The Lodgepole formation top was picked at 8,542’ MD/—6,636’ SS, where samples changed from a light to medium brown and gray limestone to a medium to dark gray limestone. ROP in the Lodgepole ranged from 0.30 to 3.00 minutes per foot while rotating and sliding, with gas measurements ranging from 4 to 1,018 units. Samples generally consisted of limestone which were medium to dark and light gray, brown to light brown in part, argillaceous, wackestone, silty, slightly carbonaceous, were moderately well indurated, with fair to good intergranular porosity and light green to dark green/gray ﬂuorescence, with moderately fast streaming cuts.

Curve Section

The curve section of Parshall 88-3029H began 308’ MD into the Lodgepole formation at a KOP of 8,850’ MD. Directional tools were picked up on November 18, 2014, and the curve was started at 6:15 AM CST on November 18, 2014. Drilling rates in the curve ranged from 0.30 to 3.00 minutes per foot while sliding and rotating, and gas measurements ranged from 7 to 1,018 units. Gamma markers were utilized throughout the curve to correlate Parshall 88-3029H
with Parshall 26-3029H.
