Cuttings and Oil Show Analysis
Cuttings lithology throughout the lateral generally presented as arenaceous, silty dolostone with varying shades of brown and grays. The D facies presented as grayish brown to gray with grayish tan platiness and poor intercrystalline porosity. Tray ﬂuorescence was pale and solvent cut ﬂuorescence presented as slow, clear, and pale yellow to pale greenish yellow. The C facies presented as a lighter brown to brownish gray and also some platiness, however the plates in the C facies tended to be lighter with darker staining than those observed in the D facies. Porosity was also poor. Tray ﬂuorescence was scattered and dull greenish yellow, and solvent cut ﬂuorescence presented as moderately rapid to rapid, cloudy, and pale to dull greenish yellow. The A facies presented as light to medium browns and brownish grays, with some scattered unconsolidated quartz sand. Tray ﬂuorescence was even and dull greenish yellow. Solvent cut ﬂuorescence was rapid, streaming, cloudy dull greenish yellow.

Formation Dip/Inclination

Based on offset wellbores, the formation had the potential to come up 30’ TVD

while making the directional turn, drop ~15’ TVD over the next 3,000’, and then come up ~10’ throughout the remainder of the lateral, for an average dip of 90.05. As expected, the formation came up very quickly initially, coming up 30’ of TVD in the ﬁrst 2,500’ of measure dept while making the directional turn, averaging a dip of 90.5 degrees. The formation turned over at 13,300’ MD. The formation continue to move downward until 18,900’ Nﬂ), dropping 26’ of TVD, with the dip averaging 89.5 degrees in that interval. At 18,900’ MD, the formation turned back up and began its predicted upward movement, coming up ~6’ TVD until TD at 21,775’ MD, averaging a dip of 90.4 degrees in that interval.
